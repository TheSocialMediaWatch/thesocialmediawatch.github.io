The Best Crafting Blogs to Follow for Inspiration


Whether you’re upcycling a thrift store find, learning a new fiber art, or simply keeping your hands busy on a rainy day, the world of crafting blogs is a treasure trove waiting to be explored. Blogs have long been the soul of the crafting community—offering accessible tutorials, creative spark, and the warmth of shared passion. And in the overflowing universe of craft tutorials, patterns, and DIY guides, certain blogs stand out as veritable lighthouses, guiding makers of every skill level to new heights.

Grab a mug of tea, clear off your workspace, and let’s take a deep dive into the brightest and most inspiring crafting blogs worth bookmarking this year.

1. A Beautiful Mess

A Beautiful Mess has become synonymous with lively, approachable home décor projects and joyful DIY ideas. Sisters Elsie Larson and Emma Chapman curate their blog with an infectious playfulness—think bold wall art, inventive furniture makeovers, and seasonal-themed crafts. The tutorials are digestible, accompanied by step-by-step photos that make daunting projects feel attainable for beginners and satisfying for seasoned crafters. From resin jewelry to painted flower pots, the variety compels you to try something new every single week.

Why Follow?

  • Incredibly friendly, detailed instructions
  • Stylish, on-trend projects for home and wardrobe
  • Equally inspiring recipes, cocktail how-tos, and lifestyle content sprinkled throughout

2. Craftsy Blog (formerly Bluprint)

If you crave variety, Craftsy’s blog (now part of the TN Marketing family) offers a mixed palette—sewing, knitting, quilting, cake decorating, embroidery, painting, and more. Their posts dig deeper into technique, providing troubleshooting guides, pattern recommendations, and clever hacks that make a tangible difference for makers. Paid classes are available, but the blog itself is a goldmine of free advice, ranging from beginner-friendly scarf patterns to advanced quilt piecing tricks.

Why Follow?

  • Deep technical dive into traditional and modern crafts
  • Free resources and tutorials for every level
  • Access to a vast community of makers for group projects or challenges

3. Paper & Stitch

The charming universe of Paper & Stitch beckons with creative, modern projects for the design lover. Blogger Brittni Mehlhoff inspires with paper crafts, unique gift wrap ideas, easy DIY wall art, and floral arrangements that double as room accessories. What sets this blog apart is its chic, minimalist aesthetic—every project could be featured in a magazine spread. If you’re eager to put a contemporary spin on classic techniques, this is your new creative headquarters.

Why Follow?

  • Magazine-worthy styling on real-world budgets
  • On-trend color palettes and seasonal project ideas
  • Simple instruction that maximizes visual impact

4. Honestly WTF

Few sites have blurred the boundaries between fashion, jewelry, and artful crafting quite like Honestly WTF. Creator Erica Chan Coffman leads with adventurous DIYs—friendship bracelets get a luxe rebirth, basic sandals become runway-ready, and home décor leans bohemian with unexpected touches. If you want to recreate high-design accessories without breaking the bank or losing your weekend, this blog’s visual stories and video guides have you covered.

Why Follow?

  • Fresh, fashion-forward crafting inspiration
  • Step-by-step video guidance for complex projects
  • Occasional artist features and travel stories ignite deeper curiosity

5. The House That Lars Built

Whimsy jumps off every page at The House That Lars Built. Brittany Jepsen weaves Scandinavian-inspired motifs with festive party décor, clever upcycled crafts, and an endless supply of printable templates. This blog feels like a celebration of color and creativity—hand-stitched felt flowers, papercraft crowns, and imaginative holiday décor are regular features. Downloadable patterns and artist collaborations ensure you’ll never run out of weekend projects or inspiration.

Why Follow?

  • Printable patterns and projects for all ages
  • Bright, playful photography that makes crafting contagious
  • Plenty of kid-friendly and party-perfect ideas

6. Purl Soho Blog

For many crafters, Purl Soho is pure fiber heaven. Their blog is an extension of Purl Soho’s iconic NYC store, marrying clean photos with meticulously detailed sewing, knitting, crochet, and embroidery tutorials. Patterns are thoughtfully designed and free to download, with a focus on heirloom-quality projects—think linen aprons, hand-knit blankets, and timeless accessories. Even if you’re an enthusiastic beginner, the clear breakdowns and Q&A in the comments go a long way to help.

Why Follow?

  • Professional-quality patterns that remain beginner-accessible
  • Step-by-step guides with clear photos for every stage
  • Beautiful, minimalist color palettes and textures

7. Craft Passion

If your Pinterest boards are bursting with sewing patterns, plush toys, and quirky accessories, Craft Passion will feel like coming home. Joanne L. crafts a sprawling library of project ideas, with a special knack for plushies, amigurumi, quilting, knitting, and crochet. She also dives into seasonal decoration and upcycling challenges. Her patterns and guides are downloadable, and the blog’s community regularly shares user-completed projects for inspiration.

Why Follow?

  • Extensive pattern library, especially plush toys and amigurumi
  • Reusability and eco-conscious themes (think upcycling and scrap projects)
  • Interactive community and troubleshooting in comment threads

8. Makezine (Make:)

Need an adventurous twist on crafting? Makezine (Make:) bridges the gap between tech tinkering, DIY, and traditional crafts in a way that feels utterly fresh. The blog covers everything from paper circuits and leatherworking to 3D printing and robotics. This makes it especially appealing if you want to blend your crafting with a hint of STEM. If just gluing glitter to everything has lost its charm, Make: will have you building gadgets, coding soft electronics, and pushing into new creative frontiers.

Why Follow?

  • Tech-infused traditional and digital crafts
  • Project ideas suitable for adults and motivated kids alike
  • Projects that range from beginner to near-expert

9. Red Ted Art

Red Ted Art is a family-friendly blog that puts creativity right into little hands. Maggy Woodley brings hundreds of imaginative kids’ craft ideas—from simple origami and egg carton animals to handprint art and holiday-themed crafts. Her step-by-step instructions often include helpful videos geared towards parents, teachers, and anyone hoping to spark that first artistic spark in kids of all ages.

Why Follow?

  • Huge library of crafts for kids, sorted by age and season
  • Crafts using everyday household materials
  • Ideal for parents, childminders, and educators

10. Damask Love

At the crossroads of color, celebration, and serious creativity stands Damask Love. Amber Kemp-Gerstel’s approach is cheerful and energetic—her blog explodes with paper crafts, bold and simple home décor, creative storage solutions, and DIY gifts for all occasions. Her writing makes even the most complex project approachable (and fun), with plenty of downloadable templates and video walk-throughs. If you love your crafts with a dash of puns and a whole lot of personality, Damask Love will be your favorite bookmark.

Why Follow?

  • Inclusive, welcoming writing and project range
  • Crafts are approachable but never dull
  • Tons of printables for parties and celebrations

Tips on Finding Your Own Crafting Muse

Scrolling endless feeds and photos can feel overwhelming without a bit of guidance. Here are some pointers for finding the blogs that’ll best match your crafting aspirations:

  • Focus on Your Interest Area: Whether it’s sewing, upcycling, scrapbooking, or mixed media, seek out blogs with a deep niche focus or those that offer project roundups and resources in your specific interest area.
  • Community Matters: Comment sections, social media groups, and forum activity on a blog can be an underrated gem—interactive communities provide feedback, troubleshooting, and even new friendships.
  • Printable Patterns and Templates: Sometimes a free downloadable template makes all the difference. Blogs that offer these boost your project success rate, especially for beginners who want to minimize trial-and-error.
  • Skill-Building Content: Look for blogs that not only show projects, but actually teach techniques. Tutorials that break down stitches, cuts, measurements, or painting technique will make your crafting experience smoother.

Why These Blogs Stand Out

The crafting blogosphere is vast, but certain characteristics separate memorable sites from the crowd. The blogs above represent a thoughtful curation of accessible instruction, visual inspiration, trend-setting ideas, and unique project voices. What truly makes them shine is their dedication to fostering creativity at every level:

  • Inspiration Without Intimidation: These blogs share gorgeous results but invite real people to try, fail, tweak, and succeed at their own pace.
  • Diversity of Projects: Rather than repeating tired DIYs, the best blogs continually innovate—remixing traditional crafts, experimenting with materials, and encouraging readers to personalize every step.
  • Quality Over Quantity: With so many aggregate “top 100” lists and content farms flooding search results, actual crafters who carefully document and photograph real projects foster trust and a sense of community.

A Call to Creativity

It’s easy to fall into the rut of scrolling beautiful finished creations and never picking up scissors or glue. These blogs make it easy to shift from inspiration to action, serving as catalysts for your own crafty adventures. Whether you’re sprucing up your home, learning needlework, organizing a craft night with friends, or making something special for someone you love, there’s a wealth of ideas waiting at your digital doorstep.

In a world of mass-produced objects, making something yourself is an act of joy and defiance. Pick a blog that speaks to your sense of style, sign up for their newsletter, and let your imagination (and workspace) run wild. Who knows—the next viral craft project might start at your kitchen table, inspired by the bloggers designing extraordinary projects for ordinary days. Happy making!
