Top Animated TV Shows You Can't Miss


Animation is no longer just for children—it’s an art form that has produced some of the most innovative storytelling, dazzling visuals, and unforgettable characters in television history. From satirical comedies to intense dramas, animated TV shows capture what live-action often can’t, transporting viewers to imaginative worlds and tackling complex themes with heart, humor, and sophistication. Whether you’re a lifelong fan or just dipping your toes into the genre, these animated series are essential viewing for anyone who loves great television.

1. BoJack Horseman

Netflix’s “BoJack Horseman” is a prime example of how animation can navigate mature themes with nuance. The series centers on BoJack, a washed-up ’90s sitcom star (who also happens to be a horse), as he grapples with depression, addiction, and the pressures of celebrity. What sets “BoJack Horseman” apart is its ability to blend caustic satire with gut-punch emotion. It explores Hollywood’s superficiality, the complexities of personal growth, and the messiness of relationships. The show’s unique visual style combines anthropomorphic animals and humans in an absurd, yet oddly believable, world. Every season stretches the boundaries of storytelling—don’t miss the brilliantly experimental episodes like “Fish Out of Water” or “The View from Halfway Down.”

2. Avatar: The Last Airbender

If epic world-building and intricate storytelling are your ideals, “Avatar: The Last Airbender” deserves a place on your must-watch list. Set in a world divided by elemental nations, the show follows Aang, the last Airbender and the Avatar destined to bring balance. Every episode is a masterclass in character growth, with heroes and villains alike evolving in meaningful, surprising ways. The series doesn’t shy from serious themes—war, imperialism, loss—and tempers them with humor and delight. Outstanding animation, a diverse cast, and a seamlessly woven plot deliver a resonant, timeless saga. This is all-ages animation at its absolute best.

3. Rick and Morty

Few animated comedies have exploded like “Rick and Morty.” On the surface, it’s an irreverent sci-fi adventure following cynical scientist Rick Sanchez and his neurotic grandson Morty. Dig deeper, and you’ll find biting commentary on science, existence, and family dysfunction, all wrapped up in a whirlwind of interdimensional chaos. The show is notorious for its wild imagination—alternate universes, mind-bending timelines, and more creative sci-fi concepts than you can count. “Rick and Morty” is unpredictable, hilarious, and wickedly smart, making it a must-watch for anyone who enjoys comedic animation with real intellectual muscle.

4. Archer

Part spy spoof, part dysfunctional family comedy, “Archer” blends stunning visual style with razor-sharp wit. The show parodies classic espionage tropes by following the egotistical Sterling Archer and his coworkers at the world’s most inept spy agency. The dialogue is quick, clever, and packed with running gags that reward long-term viewers. Over its run, “Archer” has transformed its format with themed seasons, from gumshoe noir to Miami Vice homage, keeping things fresh while retaining its trademark humor. A blend of smart writing, edgy voice acting (especially from H. Jon Benjamin), and endless pop-culture references keep “Archer” squarely in the animation hall of fame.

5. Gravity Falls

A modern classic for fans of the mysterious and magical, “Gravity Falls” is as heartfelt as it is hilarious. The series revolves around twins Dipper and Mabel Pines, who spend the summer with their great-uncle Stan in the enigmatic town of Gravity Falls, Oregon. Behind the vibrant colors and zany humor lurk cryptic secrets and intricate conspiracies. The show is loaded with clever clues, ciphers, and Easter eggs—rewarding careful viewers. Its two-season run is tightly crafted, with no filler episodes, making it a perfect candidate for binge-watching. Memorable characters, moving arcs, and a satisfying conclusion make “Gravity Falls” a must for anyone who loves supernatural mysteries.

6. Samurai Jack

“Samurai Jack” is an epic saga blending cinematic visuals with minimalist storytelling. Created by Genndy Tartakovsky, the show chronicles the journey of Jack, a noble samurai sent to a dystopian future ruled by the evil Aku. Each episode is a visual feast: gorgeous backgrounds, inventive action sequences, and a remarkable use of silence and timing. The storytelling is concise yet profound, meditating on themes of perseverance, honor, and loneliness. Its revival with a fifth season gave closure to Jack’s quest, delivering a poignant finale that resonates with both nostalgia and new depth.

7. Futurama

From the creator of “The Simpsons,” “Futurama” pushes the boundaries of what science fiction and comedy can accomplish. Set in the 31st century, the series follows Fry, a pizza delivery boy cryogenically frozen for 1,000 years, as he awakens in a wildly imaginative future. The show’s genius lies in its mix of zany humor, sharp social satire, and surprisingly touching moments. Futurama is also known for its labyrinthine sci-fi plots and clever nods to classic literature, mathematics, and techno-babble. Episode standouts like “Jurassic Bark” and “The Luck of the Fryrish” prove that animated shows can deliver both hearty laughs and true emotional heft.

8. The Simpsons

No list of top animated TV shows feels complete without “The Simpsons.” Since its debut in 1989, it’s become a cultural phenomenon, influencing countless shows that followed. Chronicling the misadventures of the Simpson family and the citizens of Springfield, each episode blends satirical observations of American life with absurdist comedy and moments of genuine warmth. The early seasons are widely celebrated for their perfect balance of heart and humor—think “Marge vs. the Monorail” or “Lisa’s Substitute.” Despite a massive episode count, the show’s best years cement its place as required viewing for any animation fan.

9. Adventure Time

“Adventure Time” is a fever dream of fantasy, whimsy, surreal humor, and underlying poignancy. Centered on Finn, a boy adventurer, and his magical dog Jake, the show explores the post-apocalyptic Land of Ooo with a style that’s charmingly weird yet deeply emotional. What begins as goofy, playful escapades grows into one of the most sophisticated serialized stories in animation. “Adventure Time” is renowned for its inventive world-building, quirky cast, and episodes that explore themes of love, loss, identity, and hope. Its creative risks and emotional resonance garnered a passionate following, and its influence still ripples through animated TV today.

10. Bob’s Burgers

“Bob’s Burgers” has steadily built a reputation as one of the most delightful animated comedies around. Centered on the Belcher family’s burger joint, the show’s appeal comes from its offbeat charm and relatable, lovable characters. Unlike many other sitcoms, “Bob’s Burgers” finds humor in domestic squabbles, puns, and musical numbers, rather than cynicism or shock value. The writing is witty, the jokes land firmly, and the family’s supportive dynamic brings a warmth rarely seen in animated sitcoms. It’s an animation comfort food that keeps getting better with each season.

11. Invincible

For those craving something darker and more intense, “Invincible” brings superhero animation to a whole new level. Based on the acclaimed comic by Robert Kirkman, the show follows Mark Grayson, a teenager learning to use his powers under the shadow of his seemingly heroic father, Omni-Man. The series is brutal and not for the faint of heart, but its violence is matched by complex characters, moral questions, and gripping plot twists. “Invincible” has garnered praise for its earnest characters and willingness to flip superhero conventions on their head.

12. Castlevania

Based on the classic video game series, “Castlevania” is a gothic horror fantasy bursting with intricate lore and breathtaking animation. It chronicles the struggle of Trevor Belmont, Sypha Belnades, and Alucard against the vengeful Dracula. Known for its fluid action, mature themes, and rich characterization, “Castlevania” stands as a rare example of video game adaptation done right. The series mixes horror, dark humor, and grand battles, serving up a feast for fans of serialized fantasy and stylish storytelling.

13. Over the Garden Wall

“Over the Garden Wall” is a breathtakingly atmospheric miniseries that takes viewers on a surreal journey through the unknown. The plot follows half-brothers Wirt and Greg, lost in a mysterious forest filled with peculiar characters and haunting secrets. The show’s autumnal visuals, quirky humor, and poetic storytelling make it feel like a lost fairy tale. Each episode builds on a sense of nostalgia and quiet wonder, concluding with a deeply poignant finale. Perfect for a cozy night, this short series feels like a hidden gem.

14. The Venture Bros.

Mixing sci-fi, superhero, and pop culture parody, “The Venture Bros.” is one of the sharpest, most original adult animated series ever made. Chronicling the exploits of the Venture family and their bizarre adversaries, every episode is packed with jokes, stunning references, and surprisingly deep existential musings. Its sprawling mythology and loyal fan base are testaments to its quality and wit. If you love layered storytelling and absurd humor served with deadpan delivery, “The Venture Bros.” is a series worth savoring.

15. Steven Universe

“Steven Universe” brings heart and inclusivity to center stage, telling the story of young Steven and his adventures with the Crystal Gems in the town of Beach City. The series excels at blending slice-of-life warmth with epic intergalactic conflict, exploring themes such as identity, compassion, and acceptance. The show shines thanks to its catchy music, vibrant color palette, and deeply human storytelling. “Steven Universe” has resonated with a broad audience, offering comfort, representation, and affirmation through its heartfelt messages.

16. Attack on Titan

No conversation about game-changing animation is complete without “Attack on Titan.” This anime juggernaut delivers an adrenaline-fueled story of humanity’s struggle for survival within walled cities under threat from giant, man-eating Titans. Combining stunning action sequences, intricate lore, and relentless plot twists, the story rapidly deepens into a dark meditation on morality, freedom, and the cyclical nature of violence. “Attack on Titan” is a global hit for good reason—every season escalates the stakes, offering revelations that redefine everything viewers thought they knew.

17. South Park

For decades, “South Park” has pushed the envelope with unapologetic social satire and subversive humor. Matt Stone and Trey Parker’s animated series lampoons contemporary issues and taboos faster than any other show on television. Its crude animation style is its own kind of genius, enabling the creators to churn out topical episodes at lightning speed. Behind the irreverence, “South Park” often delivers surprisingly pointed commentary on politics, society, and human nature.

18. Big Mouth

Puberty gets the animated treatment in “Big Mouth,” a brash comedy that exaggerates the awkwardness, confusion, and chaos of adolescence. Following a group of middle schoolers and their Hormone Monsters, the series pulls no punches, diving into taboos of sexuality, changing bodies, and social anxiety. The show’s unique blend of raunchy humor and genuine empathy has won over viewers who appreciate both laugh-out-loud moments and honest conversations about growing up.

19. Cowboy Bebop

“Cowboy Bebop” remains a gateway anime for good reason. With its genre-blending mix of space-western, film noir, and jazz-inspired style, it’s a visually and sonically stunning masterpiece. Following a crew of bounty hunters drifting through the solar system, the episodic adventures quietly unfold deep themes of loss, regret, and the search for meaning. Every frame pulses with atmosphere, and the soundtrack, scored by Yoko Kanno, has achieved legendary status. “Cowboy Bebop” is often cited as one of the best anime series of all time, and its influence can be felt across animation years later.

20. King of the Hill

Last but certainly not least, “King of the Hill” earns its spot as a subtle, hilarious slice of American life. Mike Judge’s series follows the quiet but dignified Hank Hill and his eccentric family and friends in the fictional Texas suburb of Arlen. What distinguishes “King of the Hill” is its commitment to realism; its grounded humor and affection for its characters make the show a loving portrait of ordinary people navigating life’s struggles with understated wisdom.

Animation’s power lies in its versatility—from wild comedies and heartwarming family sagas to genre-defining epics and brutal deconstructions. Each of these top animated television series has left an indelible mark on viewers and the medium itself. Whether you’re seeking new worlds, sharp satire, or poignant storytelling, these shows deliver countless hours of entertainment, laughter, and reflection. So settle in, hit play, and let these series remind you why animation is one of the most compelling forms of storytelling on television today.
