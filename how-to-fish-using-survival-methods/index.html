How to Fish Using Survival Methods


Fishing is one of the most essential survival skills you can learn. Whether you are stranded in the wilderness or trying to make it through a challenging outdoor adventure, knowing how to fish using survival methods can be the difference between life and death. In this article, we’ll cover various techniques to catch fish without the use of modern fishing gear, from crafting improvised fishing tools to using nature to your advantage.

1. Understanding Survival Fishing

Before you dive into the techniques, it’s important to understand that survival fishing isn’t about catching fish for sport—it’s about survival. When you’re in a survival situation, you need to focus on using the resources around you to your advantage. You won’t have access to fishing rods, reels, or store-bought baits, but with some knowledge and ingenuity, you can still catch fish.

Key Survival Fishing Principles:

  • Improvise with available materials: Use what you have on hand to create fishing tools.
  • Understand fish behavior: Knowing where fish tend to hide and how they react to your bait is crucial.
  • Choose the right fishing location: Fish are often found in deeper, slower-moving parts of rivers or lakes.
  • Patience and persistence: Survival fishing is often about trial and error. Stay persistent and be patient.

2. Basic Survival Fishing Gear

Even in a survival situation, you might have a few tools at your disposal. However, if you don’t have a typical fishing kit, you can create makeshift tools using materials from the environment.

Improvised Fishing Rods

A fishing rod doesn’t need to be a store-bought item. You can use natural materials to create your own rod. The key is to find a strong, flexible branch that is about 4-6 feet in length. Ideally, the branch should have a slight curve to give it natural flexibility.

How to Make an Improvised Fishing Rod:

  • Step 1: Find a long, strong branch with some flexibility. A sapling or young tree often works well.
  • Step 2: Remove all branches and leaves, leaving a smooth, clean shaft.
  • Step 3: Secure a piece of string or cord to the tip of the branch. You can use anything from paracord to shoelaces or even thin strips of plant fibers.
  • Step 4: If you have access to any hooks, use them. If not, create a simple hook by bending a small piece of wire, bone, or even a sharp piece of wood into a hook shape.

Creating Fishing Line

If you have no fishing line, there are several natural materials you can use to create a strong line. The trick is to find something durable, such as plant fibers, animal sinew, or even thin strips of leather. Here are some options:

  • Plant Fibers: Many plants have strong fibers that can be twisted into makeshift fishing lines. Look for long, fibrous plants such as hemp, nettles, or even certain grasses.
  • Sinew: The tendons of animals, particularly large ones like deer or elk, make for very strong lines. If you’ve harvested any small game, look for sinew on the animal’s legs or back.
  • Leather Strips: Thin, flexible strips of leather can be twisted into durable lines. If you’re near a hide or animal skin, consider using these materials to create strong lines.

Crafting Fish Hooks

A fish hook is arguably the most critical piece of survival fishing equipment. In the wild, you can create hooks out of natural materials, such as bones, sharp stones, or even bent branches. Here are some ideas:

Bone Hooks:

  • Find a bone from a small animal.
  • Sharpen one end to create a hook-like shape.
  • You can attach it to your line using sinew or plant fiber for a strong connection.

Wooden Hooks:

  • Cut a piece of wood into a small, sharp point.
  • Carve the other end into a small hole or notch to secure the line.
  • You can then attach the hook by tying it tightly with your makeshift fishing line.

3. Finding a Fishing Spot

In a survival situation, choosing the right location for fishing is just as important as your fishing technique. Fish tend to congregate in specific areas of bodies of water, and knowing where to look will increase your chances of success.

Rivers and Streams

Fish in rivers and streams generally prefer calm, deep areas. Look for places where the water flows more slowly, such as near the banks, behind rocks, or in eddies. Fish are also drawn to places where they can easily find food, such as near submerged vegetation, fallen trees, or other debris in the water.

Lakes and Ponds

If you’re near a lake or pond, focus on the edges where the water is deeper. Fish tend to stay close to structures like submerged rocks or sunken trees, as these areas provide shelter and attract prey. Look for areas with cooler water, as fish tend to congregate there during the warmer months.

Saltwater Fishing

If you find yourself near the ocean or a saltwater estuary, look for areas with shallow water near the shore. Tide pools, sandbars, and areas where the water is calmer can be prime fishing spots. In saltwater fishing, you can also try using baitfish that are easy to catch in shallow water, such as small crustaceans or mollusks.

4. Baiting Your Hook

Baiting your hook properly is critical for attracting fish. While you can use store-bought bait if you happen to have it with you, in a survival situation, you’ll need to use whatever you can find in nature. Here are some common survival bait options:

Insects and Worms

Insects, particularly worms and grubs, are one of the most effective baits for fish. Worms are common and can be found almost everywhere, especially in moist environments. You can find them under rocks, decaying logs, or in the soil near water.

Small Fish

If you’re near a body of water with smaller fish, use these as bait for larger fish. You can catch small fish with your improvised fishing rod or traps and use them as live bait for bigger fish.

Plant-Based Bait

Some fish are attracted to plant-based food. You can use plant matter such as seeds, berries, or even small pieces of leaves. Some plants, like cattails or water lilies, also provide edible roots that can serve as a good bait source.

5. Fishing Techniques

Now that you have your fishing rod, line, hook, and bait ready, you can start fishing using various techniques. These techniques will help you catch fish effectively, even without modern fishing gear.

Hand Fishing or Noodling

In some survival situations, hand fishing can be a highly effective way to catch fish. Known as noodling, this technique involves using your hands to grab fish directly from the water. This method is typically used to catch catfish, which often hide in underwater crevices.

To noodle:

  1. Look for underwater holes or crevices where fish are likely hiding.
  2. Reach into the hole, feeling around for the fish. You may need to work quickly and carefully, as fish can be fast.
  3. Once you make contact, grab the fish by the gills or body, and pull it out.

Spearfishing

Spearfishing is another ancient technique that can be incredibly effective in survival situations. If you don’t have a spear, you can create one using a sharp branch or piece of bone. Spearfishing requires patience and skill, but it’s very effective in shallow waters.

To spear fish:

  1. Use a long, strong stick or branch to fashion a spear.
  2. Aim for the fish’s head or upper body.
  3. Strike swiftly to ensure the fish is impaled and cannot escape.

Trapping Fish

Another fishing method is setting traps in the water. Fish traps can be constructed using branches, vines, or other materials. The basic idea is to create a structure that lets fish swim into a confined space but not escape.

Fishing with Nets

If you are near a river or lake, you might be able to fashion a makeshift fishing net from plant fibers. A net can be placed in the water, trapping fish as they swim through it. This method works well if you know the migration paths of local fish species.

6. Preparing Your Catch

Once you’ve successfully caught a fish, the next challenge is preparing it for consumption. Depending on the size and type of fish, you’ll want to clean and cook it properly to avoid illness.

Cleaning the Fish

To clean your fish:

  • Scale: Use a sharp object, such as a rock or knife, to scrape off the scales, starting at the tail and moving toward the head.
  • Gut: Make a small incision along the belly, and remove the internal organs. Be sure to discard everything except the edible parts.
  • Filleting: If you need to fillet the fish, make careful cuts along the sides of the bones to remove the meat.

Cooking the Fish

Cooking fish is an important part of survival. The simplest method is to roast it over an open fire. You can also boil or fry the fish if you have access to a cooking vessel.

7. Conclusion

Survival fishing is a vital skill that can save your life in the wild. By learning how to use natural materials to create fishing tools, finding the right fishing spots, and using the correct bait and techniques, you can increase your chances of survival. While it may take time and patience, fishing with survival methods is a highly effective way to sustain yourself in nature.

If you’re serious about wilderness survival, it’s a good idea to practice these techniques before you actually need them. By familiarizing yourself with survival fishing methods, you’ll be prepared for any outdoor emergency, ensuring that you have the skills and knowledge to survive in the wild.
