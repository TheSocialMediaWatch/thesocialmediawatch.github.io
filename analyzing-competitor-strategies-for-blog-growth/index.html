Analyzing Competitor Strategies for Blog Growth


When it comes to growing your blog, taking a peek at your competitors’ strategies can be a valuable exercise. Learning from other bloggers’ experiences and observing their tactics can help you better understand your audience, improve your content, and boost your outreach. But, how do you go about doing this? Let’s dive into a step-by-step process for analyzing competitor strategies for your blog’s growth.

First, Identify Your Competitors

Start by making a list of blogs that are similar in nature to yours—in terms of niche, target audience, and content quality. They might not necessarily be direct competitors, but they should have enough overlap that you can learn from them. To find such blogs, you can:

  1. Use search engines: Type in keywords related to your niche and see what comes up. You’ll probably discover a few blogs that are already ranking for those terms.
  2. Browse social media: Look for accounts that share content similar to yours and follow them. Often, you’ll find blogs associated with these accounts.
  3. Engage with your audience: Ask your readers for suggestions on blogs they follow and enjoy. They’ll be happy to help and might introduce you to new resources.

Once you’ve made a list of competitors, it’s time to analyze their strategies.

Step 1: Assess Their Content Marketing Strategy

Take a look at your competitors’ blogs and assess their content marketing strategy. Consider:

  1. Topic selection: What types of content are they producing? How often do they publish? Are they covering topics that interest your audience?
  2. Content format: Do they primarily use text, visuals, or a mix? How do they structure their posts?
  3. Content length: What’s the average word count? Do they have long-form or short-form content?
  4. Content promotion: How do they share their content on social media? Do they use email newsletters? Any guest posting?
  5. Content engagement: Do their posts receive comments, shares, and likes? What sort of engagement do they foster with their audience?

Step 2: Study Their SEO Techniques

Search engine optimization (SEO) is crucial for driving organic traffic to your blog. Examine your competitors’ SEO tactics:

  1. Keyword usage: What words and phrases do they target? Are they covering the same keywords as you?
  2. On-page SEO: How do they structure their blog posts for SEO? Do they use subheadings, meta descriptions, and alt tags?
  3. Link building: Do they have backlinks from reputable sources? Are they actively pursuing link building opportunities?
  4. Technical aspects: Are their websites mobile-friendly? Do they have site speed and structured data issues?

Step 3: Review Their Outreach and Community Building

A strong community is essential for blog growth. Investigate how your competitors are building theirs:

  1. Social media presence: How active are their social media accounts? Do they interact with their followers regularly?
  2. Email marketing: Do they use email newsletters? What types of content do they send to their subscribers?
  3. Collaboration and partnerships: Are they collaborating with other bloggers or influencers? How do they engage with their readers and supporters?
  4. Audience interaction: Do they respond to comments on their blog? Is there an open dialogue with their readership?

Step 4: Analyze and Incorporate Your Findings

Now that you’ve gathered insights from your competitors, it’s time to analyze and incorporate them into your own strategies. Consider the following:

  1. Evaluate your strengths and weaknesses: Can you copy some tactics or improve on areas where you fall short?
  2. Determine what sets you apart: How can you differentiate your blog from your competitors? Focus on your unique angle or tone.
  3. Make necessary adjustments: Based on the lessons learned, make changes to your content, SEO, outreach, and community building efforts.
  4. Measure your progress: Keep an eye on your traffic, engagement, and follower growth to see if your new strategies are making a difference.

Blogging is a continuous learning process, and analyzing your competitors can provide valuable insights to enhance your efforts. So, go ahead, study your competition, gather the strategies that work best, and incorporate them into your growth plan. But remember to stay true to your unique voice and personally trademarked style to give your blog its distinct identity.
