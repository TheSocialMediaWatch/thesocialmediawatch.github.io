How to Make DIY Macrame Plant Hangers


Macrame plant hangers are a stylish, functional way to showcase your indoor plants while adding a touch of bohemian charm to any space. Whether you’re looking to add greenery to your home or want to create a beautiful, handmade gift for a loved one, learning how to make a macrame plant hanger is a rewarding and easy craft project.

This guide will walk you through the process of creating your very own macrame plant hangers from start to finish, including materials, knots, and techniques. Whether you’re a seasoned crafter or a beginner, this tutorial will equip you with all the necessary skills to make your own stunning macrame plant hangers.

Materials You’ll Need

Before you begin, gather the following materials. You can find most of them at craft stores, and they are all fairly inexpensive.

  • Cotton macrame cord: A 3-4mm thickness works well for beginners. You will need about 100 feet for each plant hanger, but this depends on the length and style of the design.
  • Wooden ring or metal ring: This is used as the base to hang your plant hanger from.
  • Scissors: For cutting the macrame cord to the desired length.
  • Measuring tape: For measuring the lengths of your cords.
  • A plant pot: To ensure your hanger fits, use a plant pot to measure the cord lengths. The pot size will determine how much cord you need for each knot.

Optional materials:

  • Beads or wooden dowels: To add a decorative touch.
  • Plant pot with drainage holes: Recommended for keeping plants healthy.

Now that you have everything you need, it’s time to get started!

Step-by-Step Guide to Creating Your Macrame Plant Hanger

Step 1: Preparing Your Cord

Start by cutting your macrame cords to the desired length. The length you need will depend on how long you want your plant hanger to be. A general rule is to cut around 8-10 strands of cord, each about 5-6 feet long, for a standard-sized plant hanger.

After cutting, fold each strand of cord in half, making a loop at the top. This loop will attach to your wooden or metal ring later on.

Step 2: Attaching the Cords to the Ring

Once you have all your cords cut and folded, it’s time to attach them to the ring. To do this, use a simple lark’s head knot. Here’s how:

  1. Take one of your folded cords and place it under the ring.
  2. Pull the two ends of the cord over the top of the ring.
  3. Then, pull those two ends through the loop created by the fold in the cord.
  4. Tighten the knot by pulling on the two ends of the cord.

Repeat this step with all the cords until you have them all attached to the ring. You should now have all your cords hanging down from the ring in a uniform pattern.

Step 3: Creating the First Knots

Now that the cords are attached, you can begin creating the knots that will form the structure of the plant hanger. The two most commonly used knots in macrame plant hangers are the square knot and the spiral knot.

Square Knot

The square knot is a fundamental knot used in macrame, and it is a great choice for creating sturdy, beautiful patterns. Here’s how to make a square knot:

  1. Take four cords—two from each side. These will be the working cords.
  2. Position the left cord over the two middle cords, forming a “4” shape.
  3. Now, bring the right cord over the left cord and under the two middle cords, pulling it up through the hole created by the “4”.
  4. Pull both the working cords to tighten the knot.
  5. Repeat the process to create another square knot.

Continue making square knots along the length of the cords, spacing them evenly. The more knots you make, the shorter the gaps between your knots will be, creating a dense pattern.

Spiral Knot

The spiral knot is an easy-to-make knot that adds a twisting, rope-like effect to your macrame plant hanger. Here’s how to make a spiral knot:

  1. Take two outer cords and leave the middle cords loose.
  2. Knot the first outer cord around the second one repeatedly.
  3. Repeat this process until you get a twisted effect, and the knot starts to spiral down the cords.

The spiral knot is perfect for adding texture to your hanger, and it works well for a more relaxed, casual look.

Step 4: Creating the Bottom Section

As you work your way down the plant hanger, you will want to create a larger loop at the bottom to hold the plant pot. This loop will be formed by gathering the cords and tying them together.

To make the loop:

  1. Gather the cords at the bottom, and bring them together.
  2. Tie them into a large knot, making sure it is tight enough to hold the weight of your plant pot.

Once you’ve completed the loop, it’s time to attach any decorative elements you’d like. Beads are a popular addition to macrame plant hangers, and they can be threaded onto the cords for a touch of personality. You can also attach a wooden dowel for a more geometric look.

Step 5: Adjusting the Length

Once you’ve finished creating the bottom section and any decorative touches, check the length of the hanger by placing your plant pot inside. The hanger should be long enough to hold the plant securely, with some room to spare at the bottom to accommodate any drainage from the pot.

If the hanger is too long, simply trim the cords to the desired length. Make sure all the cords are even for a neat finish.

Step 6: Final Touches

Once you’re happy with the design and the length of the macrame plant hanger, it’s time to give it a final check. Make sure the knots are secure and that the hanger is sturdy enough to hold the weight of the plant. If necessary, tighten the knots or adjust the cord length to balance the hanger.

Lastly, hang the plant hanger on a hook, a ceiling hook, or a wall bracket, and place your favorite plant inside the loop. You can also use multiple hangers in different sizes to create a dynamic, cascading display of plants.

Tips for Customizing Your Macrame Plant Hanger

While the basic steps for creating a macrame plant hanger are simple, there are plenty of ways to customize the design and make it uniquely your own. Here are some ideas:

Color Choices

Macrame cords come in a variety of colors, so don’t be afraid to experiment with different shades. You can choose neutral tones for a more minimalist look or go bold with bright colors to make a statement piece.

Adding Beads

For a personalized touch, thread wooden, ceramic, or metal beads onto your cords. This can add a decorative element and enhance the overall look of your hanger. You can place beads in between knots or even weave them into the knotting pattern itself.

Creating Different Knotting Patterns

You don’t have to stick with just square knots and spiral knots. Once you’re comfortable with basic macrame techniques, you can experiment with other knots such as the half hitch knot, the double half hitch knot, or even more intricate patterns like the chevron or diamond designs.

Using Different Rings

Instead of using a traditional wooden ring, you can also use a metal ring or even a sturdy branch for a rustic look. Different types of rings can drastically change the overall aesthetic of your macrame plant hanger.

Creating Multi-Tiered Hangers

If you have multiple plants or want a more dramatic look, consider creating a multi-tiered macrame plant hanger. This involves attaching multiple rings at different lengths and knotting them together to create cascading levels of plant hangers. This works well for showcasing a variety of plants in one piece.

Conclusion

Making your own DIY macrame plant hangers is not only a fun and relaxing craft project but also a great way to personalize your home decor. With a little practice and creativity, you can create beautiful plant hangers that perfectly match your style and enhance the beauty of your indoor garden.

By following this simple guide and adding your personal touch, you’ll be able to make macrame plant hangers that can hang proudly in any room of your house. Whether you’re hanging them in the living room, on the porch, or in the bathroom, these handmade plant hangers will give your space a cozy and bohemian vibe.

So, gather your materials, unleash your creativity, and start knotting your way to stunning plant hangers today!
