The Best Bike Trails to Explore Around the World


There’s nothing quite like the exhilaration of exploring breathtaking landscapes on two wheels. For cycling enthusiasts, discovering the best bike trails around the world isn’t just about exercising; it’s about immersing yourself in nature, embracing adventure, and forging unforgettable memories. From rugged mountain paths to serene seaside routes, the trails beckon riders of all skill levels, offering an eclectic mix of scenery, cultures, and challenges.

Here are some of the globe’s most remarkable bike trails that should top every cyclist’s bucket list.

1. The Great Divide Mountain Bike Route - USA

Stretching over 2,700 miles, the Great Divide Mountain Bike Route is renowned as the longest off-road cycling trail in the world. Traversing from Banff in Alberta, Canada, all the way to Antelope Wells in New Mexico, this trail takes riders through the heart of the Rocky Mountains. Along the way, cyclists can expect challenging climbs, rewarding descents, and unparalleled views of pristine wilderness.

The route meanders through remote areas, passing by alpine lakes, lush forests, and expansive plains. While it demands endurance and preparation, its beauty and tranquility provide the ultimate reward. Wildlife encounters are common, and the sense of solitude is something that makes this experience extraordinary.

2. Munda Biddi Trail - Australia

The Munda Biddi Trail, translating to “path through the forest” in the Noongar language, unfolds across Western Australia. This 1,000-kilometer trail starts in Perth and ends in Albany, offering cyclists an unparalleled opportunity to immerse themselves in the region’s diverse landscapes. From Eucalyptus forests to picturesque farmland, the scenery is as varied as it is stunning.

One of the highlights of this trail is its dedicated cycle path, designed to keep riders safe from motor vehicle traffic. Along the route, you’ll discover charming villages, indigenous wildlife, and historical points of interest. With plenty of accommodation options and rest stops, the Munda Biddi Trail caters to both seasoned cyclists and those seeking a leisurely ride through nature.

3. La Route Verte - Canada

Spanning more than 5,300 kilometers across Quebec, La Route Verte has been hailed as one of the best cycling networks in the world. Connecting urban cities, quaint towns, and rural landscapes, this network’s breadth provides a little something for everyone. Whether you’re pedaling along waterfronts, exploring forests, or admiring idyllic farmland, La Route Verte encapsulates the beauty of North America in every mile.

For those seeking a challenge, the trail offers sections with steep climbs and descents. However, there are also segments suited for families and casual riders. Take the time to explore Quebec’s rich cultural offerings as you pedal past wineries, museums, and historic sites that pepper the trail.

4. The Carretera Austral - Chile

Nestled in the rugged terrain of Patagonia, the Carretera Austral is a scenic road stretching approximately 1,240 kilometers from Puerto Montt to Villa O’Higgins. This bike route boasts a dazzling array of landscapes, including fjords, glaciers, and lush rainforests. Despite its remote location and challenging terrain, it’s widely regarded as one of the most picturesque rides in the world.

Cyclists can expect gravel roads and occasional rough patches, but the payoff is the untouched natural beauty that surrounds you. Many riders explore the villages along the way to experience authentic Chilean hospitality. Trekking through Patagonia is a bucket-list-worthy adventure, and cycling the Carretera Austral ensures you see it at a slower, more intimate pace.

5. EuroVelo Routes - Europe

Spanning 19 cycling routes that crisscross Europe, the EuroVelo network is a treasure trove for cycling enthusiasts. These trails interconnect countries, cities, and regions, allowing riders to explore the rich cultural heritage, history, and natural beauty Europe has to offer. Whether it’s the romantic landscapes of France, the dramatic coastlines of Norway, or the vineyards of Italy, there’s a EuroVelo route perfect for every type of cyclist.

One of the most popular trails is the EuroVelo 6, which stretches from the Atlantic Ocean to the Black Sea. Riders pass through iconic locations like the Loire Valley castles, the Rhine River, and the medieval towns of Eastern Europe. With well-maintained paths and opportunities for sightseeing at every turn, EuroVelo promises a truly enriching experience.

6. The Cape Town Cycle Route - South Africa

Cape Town offers some of the most stunning cycling trails in the world, with routes catered to every level of expertise. The Table Mountain route is a favorite for mountain biking enthusiasts, providing panoramic views of the city, coastline, and iconic landmarks. For cyclists seeking a longer journey, the Cape Point trail presents an enthralling mix of oceanside vistas and cultural discovery.

Another highlight is the Cape Town Cycle Tour route, which became globally famous thanks to its annual race attracting thousands of participants. Beyond its jaw-dropping scenery, Cape Town allows riders to experience South Africa’s unique biodiversity, with opportunities to spot local wildlife during your ride.

7. Otago Central Rail Trail - New Zealand

New Zealand’s Otago Central Rail Trail is an immensely popular cycling route that combines history with stunning scenery. Spanning 150 kilometers through New Zealand’s South Island, this trail takes riders along an old railway line, providing easy gradients and well-maintained pathways.

The Otago countryside is marked by rolling hills, towering mountains, and charming little towns that offer excellent breaks along the ride. For those interested in history, the trail features old railway bridges, tunnels, and relics from a bygone era. It’s perfect for families, solo adventurers, and anyone seeking a peaceful yet captivating ride.

8. The Friendship Highway - China and Nepal

For the more daring cyclist, the Friendship Highway offers an unparalleled sense of accomplishment and awe. Extending from Lhasa in Tibet to Kathmandu in Nepal, this 800-kilometer route boasts dizzyingly high altitude, snow-capped peaks, and jaw-dropping views of Everest. The road itself is challenging, with steep climbs and unpredictable weather, making it an adventure reserved for seasoned riders.

However, for those willing to take on the task, the rewards are extraordinary. The cultural immersion in Tibetan villages, combined with the sheer majesty of the Himalayan landscape, guarantees an unforgettable experience unlike any other.

9. The Danube Cycle Path - Austria, Germany, and Beyond

The Danube Cycle Path (Donauradweg) is one of Europe’s most iconic bike routes. Stretching across multiple countries along the Danube River, this trail is especially popular for its scenic sections in Austria and Germany. Riders enjoy smooth paths that wind through vineyards, riverside villages, and UNESCO World Heritage sites.

Ideal for families, the Danube Cycle Path offers relatively flat terrain, making it accessible to cyclists of all abilities. Along the way, you can sample regional cuisine, explore medieval castles, and relax amidst stunning natural beauty. It’s a perfect blend of comfort, adventure, and cultural discovery.

10. The Hebridean Way - Scotland

The Hebridean Way is a hidden gem for cyclists seeking a more off-the-beaten-path journey. This 185-mile trail takes riders through Scotland’s Outer Hebrides, a stunning archipelago off its west coast. The route treats cyclists to rugged coastlines, sandy beaches, and dramatic cliffs, all while offering unforgettable glimpses of Scotland’s rich heritage and wildlife.

Whether you’re braving the elements or soaking in the tranquility of this remote region, the Hebridean Way is an epic adventure for anyone looking to experience the raw beauty of Scotland.

Final Thoughts

Cycling is more than just a sport; it’s a way to connect with the world around you. Bike trails offer a unique perspective on landscapes, cultures, and histories that often go unnoticed from a car or on foot. Whether you’re chasing adrenaline on a challenging mountain route or indulging in quiet exploration through coastal paths, these trails promise unforgettable adventures.

So, wherever your next cycling destination may be, saddle up, embrace the open road, and let the journey shape your experience as much as the destination. Happy riding!
