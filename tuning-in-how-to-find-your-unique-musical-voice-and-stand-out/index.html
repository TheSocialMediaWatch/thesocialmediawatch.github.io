Tuning In: How to Find Your Unique Musical Voice and Stand Out


Have you ever been captivated by a song that just resonates with you, feeling that it was written just for you? That unmistakable feeling you get is the result of an artist having found and honed their unique musical voice. In this blog post, we will explore how you can also discover your distinct voice in the world of music and stand out from the crowd.

Step 1: Understanding Authenticity

A unique musical voice begins with authenticity. As the late musical legend John Lennon said, “Being honest with yourself is a good practice to be in.” When you are unapologetically yourself and embrace your true personality, your creativity, and originality can shine through in your music. Start by reflecting on your emotions, experiences, and the stories that inspire you. Tap into these deeply personal connections and let them influence your music.

Step 2: Stimulate Your Musical Imagination

The power of creativity can be harnessed to help you find your unique voice. Stimulate your mind by exposing yourself to a variety of artists, genres, and musical styles. You never know which unlikely influences may unconsciously inspire your creation. A popular exercise among songwriters is to mimic the sound of various musical artists and then incorporate elements from each one into your work. The more diverse your inspirations, the deeper your creative well will become.

Step 3: Experiment and Venture Beyond Your Comfort Zone

Challenge yourself to explore unfamiliar instruments, techniques, or material that aligns with your personal interests. Fearlessly push the boundaries that may be holding you back. Embrace that the only true wrong note is the one not played. If you’re not comfortable with a particular aspect of your music, that calls for more exploration, practice and experimentation before moving forward.

Step 4: Develop Your Own Writing Style

A writer’s pen and a musician’s voice are similar in many ways. Both help to communicate their ideas and emotions through their respective art forms. Develop your own writing style, honing the language with which you describe your emotions and stories. When creating lyrics, try to strike the perfect balance of imagery, rhyme, and rhythm that feels natural to you. This distinctive approach will make your music identity stand out.

Step 5: Network and Collaborate with Other Musicians

Collaboration can help you grow, both as a musician and as individuals. When you collaborate, you’re required to communicate and understand musical expressions of your peers, helping you learn more about harmony, melody, and rhythms, in turn shaping your unique voice. Engage with peers, join bands or invest in songwriting sessions, and your creativity will soar higher.

Step 6: Feedback and Self-Assessment

It’s essential to assume the role of both a creator and consumer when you’re receiving feedback on your music. Review the comments and critiques with an open mind and evaluate the suggestions that resonate with your musical vision. If the feedback doesn’t align with your musical intentions, reassess and find your unique voice in the reflection of their perspectives.

Step 7: Constantly Evolving and Revising

Lastly, embrace the understanding that music is a living, breathing entity. Always strive to improve, learn and evolve, not only to find but also to refine your unique voice. Perfection is a journey, not a destination. The progress you make in your music is what matters the most, as you’ll continue to learn and grow throughout your musical journey.

Conclusion

Finding a unique musical voice takes time, hard work, and introspection. As you navigate through this creative process, bear in mind the seven steps outlined above. In the words of composer and violinist Itzhak Perlman, “Music is your own experience, your thoughts, your wisdom.” So, trust in your abilities, be patient, and the world will inevitably tune in to your unique musical voice.
