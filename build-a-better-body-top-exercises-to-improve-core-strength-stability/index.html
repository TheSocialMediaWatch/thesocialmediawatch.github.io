Build a Better Body: Top Exercises to Improve Core Strength & Stability


Do you want to achieve a stronger core and improve your overall stability? While there are countless exercises out there, certain moves target and strengthen your core muscles more effectively than others. Here, we’ve assembled a list of some of the top exercises that will help you build a better body through core strength and stability.

Before we dive in, let’s first define what we mean by “core.” The core is a group of muscles that extend from your lower back, through your stomach, around to your pelvis, and through to the spine. This group of muscles is crucial for overall stability and balance, and plays a key role in movements like lifting, twisting, bending and even just standing upright.

Incorporating exercises that target these muscles can lead to improved posture, better balance, reduced risk of injury, and increased strength. Plus, strengthening your core can also improve your sports performance and enhance your day-to-day movements.

So, here are 5 of the top exercises to improve core strength and stability:

  1. Planks – Effective, simple, and with countless variations, planks can help you build ab strength, stability, and even endurance. Planks work your entire core, holding you in a straight, rigid line from your head to your heels. Aim for three sets of 30-60 seconds to start, working your way up.

  2. Bicycle crunches – One of the most effective ways to improve your core strength is by targeting both sets of your abs – the rectus abdominis, which are the muscles you target when you do crunches, and the obliques, the muscles that give your waist its definition. The bicycle crunch combines both by having you lift one elbow towards your opposite knee.

  3. Dead Bug – This exercise is great for stabilizing the core without involving the lower back. Lie flat on your back with your knees bent, lift your head, neck and shoulders off of the ground. Bring one knee towards your chest whilst extending the other leg and bending it at the knee. Make sure to keep your arms fully extended and hover your hands slightly above the ground. Alternate sides carefully.

  4. Superman – This exercise strengthens multiple muscles in the lower and upper back, core, and glutes all at once. Lay flat on your stomach, lift both arms and both legs up simultaneously and hold for a few seconds before slowly lowering them back. Repeat for 12 reps.

  5. Russian twists – This exercise targets the obliques and you can work with either a medicine ball or a dumbbell. Sit on the floor with your knees bent and your feet flat. Lean back twisting your hips so that your torso is at about a 45-degree angle from the floor. Hold the medicine ball, dumbbell or an empty water bottle, and twist from side to side. Make sure to keep a minimal base of support to increase the intensity and the challenge.

To start incorporating these exercises into your routine, try doing them at least 2-3 times a week. Don’t forget to warm up thoroughly and remember to work the muscles around your core too, including your back, hips, and legs. Remember, consistency is key!

As your core strength and stability improve, you’ll be amazed at the difference it makes in your overall strength, agility, posture and even your confidence. So, what are you waiting for? Keep that core strong and stable, and your body will thank you in the long run!
