How to Mix and Match Prints Like a Pro


Prints are a timeless fashion element that add excitement, vibrancy, and personality to any outfit. From bold florals to geometric patterns, stripes, and animal prints, these designs offer endless possibilities for creating unique and eye-catching looks. However, mastering the art of mixing and matching prints can feel like a daunting task, especially when it seems like everything in your wardrobe is competing for attention.

But fear not! Mixing prints like a pro is a skill that anyone can learn with a little guidance and practice. The key is to approach it with intention, confidence, and a few tried-and-true rules to create a harmonious and stylish outfit that works for you.

In this post, we’ll break down the essential tips and tricks for mixing prints, providing you with the tools to create chic and balanced looks every time you step out the door.

Understanding the Basics of Mixing Prints

Before diving into the how-tos, let’s start by understanding the basics of print mixing. Prints can be divided into several categories, including:

  1. Bold Prints: These are large, loud, and eye-catching designs, such as oversized florals, tropical patterns, and abstract graphics.
  2. Small Prints: These are finer, more delicate patterns, like polka dots, pinstripes, and small florals.
  3. Geometric Prints: These include designs like stripes, chevrons, and plaid.
  4. Textured Prints: This category includes prints that are not necessarily visible but add texture to fabrics, like brocade or jacquard.

The primary challenge in mixing prints is balancing the size, color, and intensity of different patterns so that they complement rather than clash. The goal is to create a visual interest that feels cohesive and well-thought-out, rather than chaotic and overwhelming.

Tip 1: Start with a Neutral Base

One of the easiest ways to introduce print mixing into your wardrobe is to start with a neutral base. Think of neutral tones like black, white, beige, navy, and gray as your foundation. A neutral base provides a solid backdrop that allows you to experiment with different prints without worrying about overwhelming the eye.

For instance, if you’re pairing a bold floral blouse with a striped skirt, make sure at least one of the patterns incorporates a neutral color. This can help tie the look together and prevent the outfit from feeling too busy.

Tip 2: Use the Same Color Palette

Color is a powerful tool when mixing prints, and one of the best ways to achieve a cohesive look is by sticking to a similar color palette. When prints share one or more common colors, they naturally blend together, creating a harmonious effect. This can apply to prints that may seem wildly different at first glance, such as animal prints and stripes, as long as they share a common color thread.

For example, if you’re pairing a leopard print top with a plaid skirt, try to find prints where shades of brown, black, or tan are present in both. This will help anchor the outfit and create a visual connection between the pieces.

If you’re feeling adventurous, consider using a color wheel to select complementary colors that are opposites, or analogous colors that sit next to each other. This can help you introduce bold combinations in a way that feels intentional rather than random.

Tip 3: Mix Different Print Sizes

One of the golden rules of mixing prints is to combine prints of different sizes. If you’re pairing two large prints together, they may compete with each other and create an overwhelming visual effect. On the other hand, pairing two small prints can sometimes look too matchy-matchy and lack contrast.

To strike the perfect balance, try pairing a large print with a small print. For example, an oversized floral print works beautifully with a smaller, more subtle dot pattern. The large print serves as the focal point of the outfit, while the smaller print adds texture and interest without stealing the spotlight.

It’s also important to note that mixing prints with varying scales can create a sense of depth and dimension in your look. The larger print often adds a dramatic flair, while the smaller print offers a grounding effect.

Tip 4: Play with Contrasting Patterns

Contrasting patterns are a fantastic way to make a statement when mixing prints. Instead of opting for matching patterns, try pairing prints that are different but still complementary in some way. For example, a geometric pattern, like stripes, can be paired with a floral print to create an unexpected yet stylish combination.

The key to mixing contrasting patterns effectively is finding a balance between the two. While the prints may differ, they should share at least one common element, whether it’s a shared color, shape, or style. For example, pairing a floral pattern with a gingham check can work if both prints have a similar color palette or a similar level of contrast between the colors used.

Don’t be afraid to mix patterns that initially seem like opposites. With the right approach, they can create a dynamic and visually striking outfit that stands out from the crowd.

Tip 5: Consider the Scale and Shape of the Patterns

When mixing prints, it’s important to consider the scale and shape of the patterns you’re combining. Prints with different shapes—whether geometric, organic, or abstract—can work together if they contrast each other in an interesting way.

For example, pairing a polka dot top with a striped bottom works because the round shapes of the dots contrast nicely with the straight lines of the stripes. Likewise, mixing curved shapes like florals with sharp angles, such as a plaid pattern, can create a visually interesting juxtaposition.

Similarly, prints that are too similar in shape can blend too much and lose the impact you’re hoping to create. Consider how the visual weight of each print plays a role in the overall outfit.

Tip 6: Use Accessories to Break Up the Prints

If you’re feeling nervous about going full-on with print mixing, accessories can be a great way to add a touch of print without committing to an entire outfit. Consider adding printed accessories such as scarves, bags, or shoes to complement your outfit.

For instance, if you’re wearing a striped blouse and a floral skirt, you could break up the prints with a solid-colored belt or shoes to balance the look. A printed bag can also add personality to an otherwise neutral outfit, while still maintaining a sense of harmony with the rest of your ensemble.

Accessories allow you to experiment with print mixing on a smaller scale, and they can be swapped out to create fresh combinations without the need for a complete wardrobe overhaul.

Tip 7: Keep the Rest of Your Outfit Simple

When mixing prints, it’s important to remember that the prints are the star of your look, so keep the rest of your outfit simple to avoid visual overload. If you’re already wearing multiple prints, opt for minimal or neutral accessories, and keep your makeup and hair polished but understated.

For example, if you’re pairing a floral blouse with a plaid skirt, avoid adding too many additional layers of print in the form of patterned shoes, bags, or jewelry. Instead, let the prints take center stage while keeping the other elements of your outfit calm and refined.

Tip 8: Don’t Be Afraid to Break the Rules

Fashion is all about self-expression, and one of the best things about mixing prints is the freedom to get creative. While there are plenty of guidelines to follow, there’s no need to stick rigidly to any “rules.” Sometimes, the most exciting and innovative outfits come from breaking the norms and taking risks with combinations that might seem unconventional at first.

Trust your instincts and have fun with your outfits! If you feel confident and comfortable, you’re more likely to pull off any print combination with style and ease.

Final Thoughts: Confidence is Key

At the end of the day, the secret to mixing prints like a pro is confidence. If you feel good in what you’re wearing and embrace the playful nature of prints, your outfit will always look stylish. Mix and match prints with intention, use the tips above to guide you, and above all, have fun with your fashion choices.

With practice, you’ll find your own unique print-mixing style, and soon, you’ll be confidently creating outfits that are bold, vibrant, and one-of-a-kind.
