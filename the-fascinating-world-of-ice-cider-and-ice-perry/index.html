The Fascinating World of Ice Cider and Ice Perry


Ice cider and ice perry are two unique and captivating beverages that have been gaining popularity in recent years. These delightful concoctions offer a distinct taste and a fascinating production process that sets them apart from other alcoholic beverages. In this blog post, we will delve into the mesmerizing world of ice cider and ice perry, exploring their origins, production methods, and the flavors that make them so special.

Ice Cider: A Sweet Symphony

Ice cider, also known as “cidre de glace” in French, is a luscious and sweet beverage that showcases the essence of apples in a unique way. Originating from Quebec, Canada, ice cider is made from the juice of apples that have been naturally frozen on the tree. This freezing process concentrates the sugars and flavors within the apples, resulting in a rich and intensely sweet cider.

The production of ice cider begins with the careful selection of apples, typically late-harvested varieties known for their high sugar content. These apples are left on the tree until winter arrives, allowing the freezing temperatures to work their magic. Once frozen, the apples are hand-picked and pressed, extracting the concentrated juice. This juice is then fermented slowly, often aged in oak barrels, to develop complex flavors and aromas.

The result is a golden-hued nectar with a velvety texture and a symphony of flavors. Ice cider boasts notes of caramel, honey, and ripe fruits, with a perfect balance of sweetness and acidity. It pairs wonderfully with cheese, desserts, and even savory dishes, making it a versatile and delightful choice for any occasion.

Ice Perry: Pear Perfection

While ice cider steals the spotlight, ice perry – made from pears – is a hidden gem that deserves equal recognition. Just like ice cider, ice perry undergoes a freezing process that intensifies the flavors and sugars within the fruit. This creates a unique and exquisite beverage that captures the essence of pears in a way that no other drink can.

The production of ice perry follows a similar process to ice cider. Pears are left on the tree until winter, allowing them to freeze naturally. The frozen pears are then hand-picked and pressed, extracting the concentrated juice. This juice is fermented slowly, allowing the flavors to develop and mature.

The result is a pale golden elixir with a delicate and aromatic profile. Ice perry showcases the flavors of fresh pears, with hints of floral and citrus notes. Its smooth and velvety texture adds to the overall drinking experience, making it a true delight for the palate.

Exploring the World of Ice Cider and Ice Perry

The world of ice cider and ice perry is vast and diverse, with a range of flavors and styles to explore. From traditional methods to innovative techniques, cider and perry producers around the world are constantly pushing the boundaries of what is possible with these frozen delights.

Whether you’re a cider enthusiast or a curious newcomer, discovering the world of ice cider and ice perry is an adventure worth embarking on. These beverages offer a sensory experience like no other, providing a glimpse into the craftsmanship and artistry of the producers who create them.

So, the next time you’re looking for a unique and captivating drink to savor, consider indulging in the fascinating world of ice cider and ice perry. Raise a glass and let the flavors transport you to a realm of sweetness and delight. Cheers!
