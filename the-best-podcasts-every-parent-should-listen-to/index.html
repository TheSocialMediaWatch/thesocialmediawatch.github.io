The Best Podcasts Every Parent Should Listen To


Parenting is a journey filled with joys, challenges, and a never-ending quest for wisdom. Whether you’re navigating the sleepless nights of newborn care, tackling the adventures of toddlerhood, or guiding a teenager into adulthood, one thing is certain: there’s always more to learn. Podcasts can be an incredible resource for parents, offering expert advice, relatable stories, and even the occasional comedic relief to help lighten the mood. If you’re looking for great podcasts to accompany you through the ups and downs of raising kids, you’ve come to the right place.

Here is a handpicked list of podcasts designed to educate, empower, and entertain parents at every stage of the parenting experience.

1. The Longest Shortest Time

Parenting often feels like a paradox — the days can be long and exhausting, yet the years seem to fly by when you look back. The Longest Shortest Time captures this poignant truth with remarkable grace. Hosted by Hillary Frank, who started the podcast after a challenging childbirth experience, this show dives deep into the unexpected aspects of parenting. From exploring unconventional families to interviewing parents who’ve overcome unique obstacles, each episode feels like a heartfelt conversation.

What sets this podcast apart is its ability to blend humor, vulnerability, and insight in one package. It validates the complex feelings parents often experience while providing tips and lessons we can all apply. Whether you’re a new parent or a seasoned one, you’ll find episodes that resonate deeply with your own journey.

2. Parenting Beyond Discipline

Every parent faces moments when their child’s behavior leaves them scratching their heads. Whether it’s lunchtime tantrums or bedtime resistance, having a toolbox of strategies can help immensely. Parenting Beyond Discipline is the go-to resource for understanding how to respond effectively to your children and foster healthy relationships based on trust, respect, and understanding.

Dr. Laura Markham, the author of Peaceful Parent, Happy Kids, frequently shares research-backed parenting techniques and compassionate advice in this podcast. It’s not just about discipline; it’s about creating an environment where kids can thrive emotionally and behaviorally. Perfect for parents eager to embrace a more mindful and proactive approach.

3. The Mom Hour

Parenting can often feel isolating, especially in today’s busy world. That’s why podcasts like The Mom Hour are such a comforting companion for parents. Co-hosted by Sarah Powers and Meagan Francis, this podcast feels like catching up with old friends. It’s relatable, upbeat, and packed with practical tips designed for real-life parenting.

Episodes cover everything from managing your household budget to talking to kids about difficult topics, all framed in casual and honest conversations. Whether you’re folding laundry or driving carpool, this show offers the perfect opportunity to laugh, learn, and feel understood. The beauty of this podcast is how it combines advice with a sense of community — because every parent deserves to know they’re not alone.

4. Dadville

Parenting podcasts are often tailored for moms, but dads have their own unique perspectives, challenges, and triumphs. Dadville is a refreshing addition to the parenting podcast lineup, specifically for fathers (though moms can enjoy it too!). Hosted by musicians and dads Jon McLaughlin and Dave Barnes, this podcast blends humor, heart, and wisdom into every conversation.

Some episodes feature insightful interviews with celebrity dads, while others tackle universal themes like balancing career goals with family life. It’s a space where dads can feel seen, supported, and inspired to become the best versions of themselves for their kids.

5. Spawned Parenting Podcast

For parents prone to humor and pop culture references, Spawned Parenting Podcast checks all the boxes. Hosted by Liz Gumbinner and Kristen Chase of the parenting blog Cool Mom Picks, this podcast delivers smart and snarky humor alongside honest insights on family life.

Their entertaining approach covers topics that are deeply relevant to modern parents, such as managing screen time, navigating childcare, and making intentional choices amidst a busy world. It’s perfect for anyone who enjoys laughter as much as learning. Plus, they often highlight amazing products and resources, useful for making parenting just a little easier.

6. Raising Good Humans

For parents striving to raise kind, empathetic, and emotionally intelligent kids, Raising Good Humans is an invaluable resource. Hosted by Dr. Aliza Pressman, a developmental psychologist, this podcast focuses on fostering emotional health in both adults and children.

Through expert interviews, science-backed advice, and actionable strategies, every episode equips parents to build strong bonds with their children while honoring their unique needs. Topics range from managing anxiety in kids to nurturing their curiosity and academic drive. It’s rooted in the belief that parenting is both an opportunity and a privilege — a chance to contribute positively to the next generation.

7. Unruffled

Parenting is often fraught with moments that test your patience and composure. Whether it’s dealing with toddler meltdowns or boundary-pushing big kids, managing your own emotional response is just as important as handling theirs. Enter Janet Lansbury’s Unruffled, a podcast that guides parents toward calm and confident child-rearing.

With her compassionate approach, Janet focuses on understanding a child’s perspective and responding to their behavior in nurturing ways. This podcast also addresses common parenting struggles, such as setting limits and balancing discipline with respect. Listening feels like a breath of fresh air in a chaotic parenting world — reminding you that staying ‘unruffled’ is not only possible but incredibly empowering.

8. The Child Repair Guide

As parents, we often wish we had a manual for raising kids. While every child is different and parenting is far from a one-size-fits-all endeavor, The Child Repair Guide comes close to being the ultimate handbook every parent serves to learn from. Hosted by Dr. Steve Silvestro, a pediatrician with decades of experience, this podcast offers practical tips for keeping kids physically and emotionally healthy.

The episodes are concise, easy to follow, and packed with actionable advice. You’ll find content ranging from sleep training strategies to decoding illness symptoms and other everyday parenting puzzles. Dr. Steve’s warm and approachable style makes even the most daunting topics accessible — it’s your crash course in becoming a parenting pro.

9. The Modern Mann

Parenting isn’t just about raising children — it’s also about maintaining your identity as a person. The Modern Mann is a podcast that intertwines family life with broader topics like personal growth and career balance. Hosted by Olly Mann, this podcast often features compelling interviews that help parents broaden their perspectives on both family and life beyond it.

While not strictly parenting-focused, it offers parents space to reflect on their relationships, hobbies, and aspirations, all while keeping the focus on parenting’s critical role in shaping who we are.

Final Thoughts: Choose What Resonates with You

The beauty of parenting podcasts is the sheer variety available. Whether you need a laugh, expert advice, or stories to remind you you’re not alone, the right podcast can make all the difference. These podcasts offer something for every parent, covering ages, stages, and unique challenges that arise throughout the parenting journey.

The next time you’re washing dishes, commuting to work, or catching a rare quiet moment alone, tune in to one of these incredible shows. You’ll be surprised by how much you can learn, laugh, and grow — all while navigating the remarkable adventure of parenthood.
