The Best Campsites for Kayaking and Canoeing


If you’re an outdoor enthusiast who loves both camping and water sports, combining the two can offer an unforgettable adventure. Kayaking and canoeing are two of the most rewarding ways to explore nature, and when you pair them with camping, you get the chance to fully immerse yourself in stunning landscapes. Whether you’re paddling along tranquil lakes or navigating through fast-moving rivers, the right campsite can elevate your experience to the next level. This post will highlight some of the best campsites for kayaking and canoeing, providing you with a guide to some of the most picturesque and accessible locations for your next outdoor adventure.

1. Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness, Minnesota

For those who truly want to escape into nature, the Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness (BWCAW) in northeastern Minnesota is one of the most serene and scenic places in the United States. With over a million acres of pristine wilderness and more than 1,000 lakes, the BWCAW is perfect for kayakers and canoeists who love a challenge. The area offers both easy paddling routes and more challenging, remote waters that require advanced skills.

Best For:

  • Beginners to advanced paddlers
  • Wilderness and solitude lovers
  • Multi-day canoe trips

The campsites in the BWCAW are often located on islands or along the shores of remote lakes, providing you with privacy and exceptional views. Some areas are so remote that you can camp without seeing another soul for days. Most campsites can be accessed by canoe, and once you arrive, you’ll find flat, well-maintained spots perfect for setting up a tent and enjoying a campfire under the stars.

Activities:

  • Canoe through crystal-clear waters
  • Fishing in the many lakes and streams
  • Hiking trails along the lakeshores

2. Everglades National Park, Florida

For a completely different kayaking experience, the Everglades National Park in Florida offers unique wetland ecosystems that are best explored by canoe or kayak. Paddle through mangrove tunnels, vast sawgrass marshes, and shallow waters while encountering wildlife like alligators, manatees, and a variety of bird species. The Everglades offer a true immersion into one of the world’s most diverse ecosystems.

Best For:

  • Nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts
  • Paddle through unique environments like mangroves and saltwater marshes
  • Those seeking a warm-weather adventure

While there are designated campsites in the Everglades, you’ll also find opportunities for backcountry camping. The campgrounds are often near the water, so you’ll be able to paddle right up to your site. A trip to the Everglades can range from an easy afternoon paddle to multi-day kayaking expeditions, depending on your level of experience.

Activities:

  • Kayaking through mangrove tunnels
  • Wildlife watching (alligators, dolphins, and more)
  • Fishing in the backcountry waters

3. Algonquin Provincial Park, Ontario, Canada

If you’re looking for a picturesque and challenging canoeing adventure, Algonquin Provincial Park in Ontario, Canada, is one of the best spots in North America. With over 1,500 lakes and 2,400 kilometers of canoe routes, the park offers diverse paddling experiences, ranging from calm, glassy lakes to fast-moving rivers. The area is especially beautiful in the fall, when the autumn colors paint the landscape.

Best For:

  • Experienced paddlers who enjoy longer trips
  • Those seeking a classic wilderness canoeing experience
  • Nature photography enthusiasts

The park features numerous campsites along its waterways, many of which are accessible by canoe. Most campsites are located on the shores of the lakes, giving you direct access to fishing, swimming, and paddling. Because the park is popular with paddlers, it’s recommended to book campsites in advance, especially during peak seasons.

Activities:

  • Canoeing across serene lakes and rivers
  • Fishing for bass, trout, and pike
  • Wildlife viewing, including moose and beavers
  • Hiking the park’s scenic trails

4. Lake Tahoe, California/Nevada

Known for its crystal-clear waters and stunning mountain backdrop, Lake Tahoe is a dream destination for kayakers and canoeists. While the lake itself is vast, there are numerous coves and inlets that make it perfect for a relaxed paddling experience. The campsites around the lake vary, with many located in national forests and state parks, allowing you to combine kayaking with traditional camping.

Best For:

  • Paddlers of all skill levels
  • Those looking for a mix of recreation and relaxation
  • Families and group outings

Campgrounds such as the Eagle Point Campground offer close proximity to the water, making it easy to launch your kayak or canoe right from your campsite. The crystal-clear water and breathtaking mountain views provide a perfect backdrop for paddling and camping. Whether you prefer to paddle to hidden beaches or explore the lakeshore by kayak, Lake Tahoe offers endless opportunities.

Activities:

  • Paddling around the lake’s many secluded beaches
  • Fishing for trout and bass
  • Hiking along the lake’s shoreline trails
  • Swimming in the clear, cool waters

5. Ohiopyle State Park, Pennsylvania

Ohiopyle State Park in Pennsylvania is a prime location for both kayaking and camping, offering a variety of paddling experiences, from calm river flows to exhilarating rapids. The Youghiogheny River is one of the main attractions, providing excellent opportunities for white-water rafting and kayaking. For those looking for a more relaxed experience, there are plenty of slower-moving sections ideal for beginners.

Best For:

  • White-water enthusiasts and adrenaline seekers
  • Families looking for a well-rounded outdoor experience
  • Beginner and intermediate paddlers

Ohiopyle has numerous campsites along the river that allow you to enjoy both kayaking and traditional camping. The park’s proximity to the water means you can easily launch your canoe or kayak directly from your campsite. It also features a wide range of other outdoor activities, such as hiking, biking, and fishing, making it a great destination for a well-rounded trip.

Activities:

  • White-water kayaking on the Youghiogheny River
  • Fishing for trout and smallmouth bass
  • Hiking to the beautiful Ohiopyle Falls
  • Mountain biking through the park’s trails

6. Desolation Wilderness, California

For those seeking a more remote and rugged paddling experience, Desolation Wilderness near Lake Tahoe in California offers pristine waters and breathtaking scenery. With towering granite peaks, dense forests, and crystal-clear lakes, it’s a must-see for experienced kayakers and canoeists looking for a true backcountry adventure.

Best For:

  • Experienced paddlers
  • Those seeking a secluded, challenging wilderness experience
  • Backpackers and backcountry campers

There are many campsites in Desolation Wilderness that can only be accessed by canoe or hiking, which adds to the sense of adventure. The lakes here are less crowded than Lake Tahoe, offering a more solitary and peaceful experience. If you’re looking for quiet, pristine paddling spots, Desolation Wilderness should be at the top of your list.

Activities:

  • Canoeing and kayaking on remote lakes
  • Hiking the rugged wilderness trails
  • Fishing in alpine lakes

7. Cumberland Island National Seashore, Georgia

If you’re looking for a kayaking experience with the bonus of beach camping, Cumberland Island National Seashore in Georgia is an amazing destination. Paddle through tidal salt marshes and the Atlantic Ocean, and explore the island’s beautiful beaches and maritime forests. The island is accessible only by ferry or private boat, adding a sense of exclusivity to the experience.

Best For:

  • Kayakers and canoeists who enjoy coastal environments
  • Wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers
  • Those who want a peaceful island getaway

Cumberland Island offers several campsites that are just a short paddle from the mainland or accessible by hiking. Some campsites are near the beach, so you can enjoy the sound of ocean waves and the beautiful coastal views. The island is home to wild horses and other wildlife, making it a unique and tranquil place to kayak and camp.

Activities:

  • Paddling through the tidal marshes and the ocean
  • Beach camping and swimming
  • Wildlife watching, including wild horses
  • Hiking to historic sites on the island

8. Great Smoky Mountains National Park, Tennessee/North Carolina

While primarily known for its mountainous terrain, the Great Smoky Mountains National Park also features several serene rivers and streams that are perfect for kayaking and canoeing. The park is home to the Pigeon River, a peaceful river that winds through lush forests and scenic valleys, offering a great option for paddlers of all skill levels.

Best For:

  • Those looking for a mix of mountains and water
  • Paddlers seeking a tranquil experience
  • Families or beginner paddlers

The park’s campsites are strategically located along these rivers, offering easy access for paddlers who want to camp and kayak. The combination of mountains, rivers, and lush forests provides an idyllic backdrop for your outdoor adventure.

Activities:

  • Paddling along calm rivers and streams
  • Fishing for trout
  • Hiking the park’s scenic trails

Conclusion

Whether you’re a seasoned kayaker or a beginner, these campsites provide some of the best opportunities to enjoy both paddling and camping in stunning natural environments. From remote wilderness areas to more accessible and family-friendly locations, there’s a perfect spot for everyone. So, grab your paddle, pack your camping gear, and embark on an unforgettable adventure at one of these top kayaking and canoeing campsites. Happy paddling!
