The Best Cartoon Shows for Teens: A Complete Guide


Cartoons have always been a vital part of our entertainment ecosystem, but they are not just for children anymore. In recent years, an increasing number of animated shows have emerged that cater specifically to teenagers, offering more complex themes, relatable characters, and humor that resonates with an older audience. This guide delves into the best cartoon shows for teens, highlighting why they are worth watching and what makes them stand out.

1. Adventure Time

Overview
“Adventure Time,” created by Pendleton Ward, follows the adventures of Finn the Human and Jake the Dog in the post-apocalyptic Land of Ooo. The series, known for its whimsical storytelling and vibrant animation, explores themes of friendship, love, and the complexities of growing up.

Why It’s Great for Teens
The show’s unique blend of humor and emotional depth allows it to tackle serious issues, making it relatable for teenagers. Its episodic nature means that viewers can jump in at any point, while overarching storylines provide continuity.

2. Gravity Falls

Overview
“Gravity Falls,” created by Alex Hirsch, tells the story of twin siblings Dipper and Mabel Pines, who spend their summer with their great-uncle Stan in the mysterious town of Gravity Falls. The show is filled with supernatural occurrences, puzzles, and hidden codes.

Why It’s Great for Teens
The series balances humor and mystery, making it engaging for teens who enjoy solving puzzles and uncovering secrets. The character development is also a highlight, as Dipper and Mabel navigate the challenges of adolescence while uncovering the town’s mysteries.

3. Big Mouth

Overview
“Big Mouth” is a coming-of-age animated series that tackles the awkwardness of puberty. Created by Nick Kroll, Andrew Goldberg, Mark Levin, and Jennifer Flackett, the show features characters who are going through the ups and downs of adolescence.

Why It’s Great for Teens
This series is unapologetically honest about the struggles of growing up. It addresses topics like body image, sexuality, and friendship with humor and sensitivity, making it a perfect fit for teens who are navigating similar issues.

4. The Owl House

Overview
“The Owl House,” created by Dana Terrace, follows Luz, a teenage girl who stumbles upon a portal to a magical realm. There, she befriends a rebellious witch and a tiny warrior, embarking on a journey that challenges her understanding of magic and friendship.

Why It’s Great for Teens
The show promotes themes of self-acceptance, identity, and the importance of following one’s dreams. Its diverse characters and magical storytelling resonate with teens, encouraging them to embrace their uniqueness.

5. Steven Universe

Overview
“Steven Universe,” created by Rebecca Sugar, centers around a young boy named Steven, who is part human, part Gem, a race of magical aliens. He lives with the Crystal Gems, a group of magical guardians, and learns to harness his powers while exploring themes of love, acceptance, and family.

Why It’s Great for Teens
The show is celebrated for its representation of LGBTQ+ characters and themes of emotional intelligence and empathy. Teens can find relatable storylines that deal with identity, relationships, and personal growth.

6. Rick and Morty

Overview
“Rick and Morty,” created by Justin Roiland and Dan Harmon, is an animated science fiction sitcom that follows the misadventures of an eccentric scientist and his good-hearted but fretful grandson. The series is known for its dark humor and philosophical undertones.

Why It’s Great for Teens
The show’s unique blend of science fiction and comedy appeals to teens who enjoy more mature and thought-provoking content. It challenges viewers to think critically about morality, existence, and the nature of reality.

7. Teen Titans Go!

Overview
“Teen Titans Go!” is a comedic take on the superhero genre, featuring the Teen Titans as they engage in various humorous and light-hearted adventures. The show is known for its slapstick comedy and quirky characters.

Why It’s Great for Teens
While it offers plenty of laughs, “Teen Titans Go!” also touches on themes of teamwork, friendship, and the importance of being true to oneself. Its fast-paced humor and relatable characters make it a great watch for teens looking for something fun.

8. The Dragon Prince

Overview
“The Dragon Prince,” created by Aaron Ehasz and Justin Richmond, is a fantasy series set in the magical land of Xadia, where magic comes from six primal sources. The story follows two human princes and an elven assassin who must work together to bring peace to their warring peoples.

Why It’s Great for Teens
The series excels in storytelling, character development, and world-building. Its themes of unity, friendship, and forgiveness resonate deeply with teens who are often exploring their own identities and relationships.

9. Final Space

Overview
“Final Space,” created by Olan Rogers, is a space opera that follows Gary Goodspeed, an astronaut who teams up with a powerful alien named Mooncake to save the universe from an evil entity known as Invictus.

Why It’s Great for Teens
The show combines humor, adventure, and poignant moments, exploring themes of friendship, sacrifice, and purpose. Its blend of action and emotional storytelling appeals to teens looking for a more mature animated series.

10. Hilda

Overview
Based on the graphic novels by Luke Pearson, “Hilda” follows the adventures of a blue-haired girl who leaves her home in the wilderness to explore the bustling city of Trolberg. The show is known for its stunning animation and enchanting storytelling.

Why It’s Great for Teens
“Hilda” explores themes of exploration, courage, and the importance of nature. Its whimsical charm and relatable protagonist make it a great choice for teens seeking adventure and imagination.

11. Castlevania

Overview
“Castlevania,” based on the video game series of the same name, follows the last surviving member of the disgraced Belmont clan as he fights to save Eastern Europe from Dracula and his minions. The series is known for its dark and mature themes.

Why It’s Great for Teens
With its rich storytelling, complex characters, and stunning animation, “Castlevania” appeals to teens who enjoy darker narratives and fantasy genres. It addresses themes of vengeance, redemption, and the struggle between good and evil.

12. The Loud House

Overview
“The Loud House,” created by Chris Savino, revolves around Lincoln Loud, the only boy in a house filled with ten sisters. The show humorously portrays the challenges and chaos of growing up in a large family.

Why It’s Great for Teens
The series addresses relatable family dynamics and sibling relationships, making it a fun watch for teens who can see reflections of their own lives in the Loud family’s antics.

13. Amphibia

Overview
“Amphibia,” created by Matt Braly, follows Anne Boonchuy, a teenage girl who is transported to a magical land inhabited by anthropomorphic amphibians. She befriends a frog named Sprig and embarks on adventures that challenge her courage and resourcefulness.

Why It’s Great for Teens
The show explores themes of friendship, bravery, and self-discovery, making it relatable for teens navigating their own journeys. Its humor and heartfelt moments resonate with a teenage audience.

14. She-Ra and the Princesses of Power

Overview
This reboot of the classic series follows Adora, a young soldier who discovers her magical powers and becomes She-Ra, a hero destined to fight against the evil Horde. The show features a diverse cast of characters and rich storytelling.

Why It’s Great for Teens
The series is celebrated for its themes of empowerment, identity, and friendship. Its strong female characters and progressive storytelling resonate with teens seeking representation and relatable narratives.

15. Bojack Horseman

Overview
“Bojack Horseman,” created by Raphael Bob-Waksberg, is an animated dark comedy that follows the life of Bojack, a washed-up actor living in Hollywood. The series tackles heavy themes like addiction, mental health, and the quest for happiness.

Why It’s Great for Teens
With its raw and honest portrayal of life’s struggles, “Bojack Horseman” offers teens a unique perspective on issues they may face. Its humor often masks deeper emotional truths, making it both entertaining and thought-provoking.

Conclusion

The world of animation has evolved significantly, offering a plethora of shows that cater to the teenage audience. From heartwarming adventures to dark comedies, these cartoons provide not only entertainment but also valuable lessons about life, identity, and relationships. Whether you’re looking for something light-hearted or a series that tackles profound themes, there’s a cartoon out there that will resonate with you.

As you dive into these shows, you’ll find that animation can be a powerful medium for storytelling, capable of exploring the complexities of life in ways that are both engaging and meaningful. So grab some snacks, settle in, and enjoy the vibrant world of cartoons made for teens!
