The Best Outdoor Workouts to Try This Summer


Summer is the perfect time to step outside and break free from the confines of the gym. With the sun shining, fresh air filling your lungs, and the world coming to life around you, outdoor workouts can be both invigorating and highly beneficial. Whether you’re looking to burn calories, build strength, or simply enjoy the natural beauty around you, there are countless outdoor workout options to explore. In this post, we’ll cover a variety of activities, from cardio-focused sessions to strength-building exercises, that will get you moving and help you achieve your fitness goals while soaking up the summer vibes.

1. Trail Running

If you’re looking to get your heart pumping while immersing yourself in nature, trail running is one of the best ways to work out during the summer. Unlike running on a treadmill or even a paved path, trail running provides a dynamic environment that can challenge your balance, coordination, and stamina. The uneven terrain of trails activates different muscles and can help improve your overall fitness. Whether you choose to run through forests, along mountain paths, or by a river, the changing scenery keeps the workout exciting.

Trail running also provides a great cardiovascular workout. The mix of uphill and downhill stretches boosts your endurance, while the softer surfaces compared to hard concrete put less strain on your joints. Make sure to wear proper running shoes with good grip to avoid slipping or injuring yourself.

Key Benefits of Trail Running:

  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Builds lower body strength
  • Enhances balance and coordination
  • Connects you with nature

2. Beach Workouts

For those fortunate enough to live near a beach, it’s time to take advantage of the natural environment. Beach workouts offer a unique challenge due to the soft, shifting sand that increases the intensity of your movements. Whether you’re doing squats, lunges, push-ups, or even running, the resistance from the sand activates your muscles more effectively than on solid ground.

One great exercise to try on the beach is the “sprint interval training” method. Sprint for 30 seconds at full effort, then walk for 60 seconds to recover. Repeat this cycle for 20-30 minutes. The sand makes your sprints more challenging, while the intervals help to build speed and endurance. After your cardio, move on to bodyweight exercises like planks, burpees, and mountain climbers. The resistance from the sand will enhance your strength training, helping you build muscle faster.

Key Benefits of Beach Workouts:

  • Builds endurance and speed
  • Tones muscles more effectively
  • Low-impact on joints due to soft sand
  • Great for increasing calorie burn

3. Hiking

Hiking is a fantastic outdoor workout that combines cardiovascular activity with strength-building. As you traverse up and down hills, you engage your legs, core, and glutes, providing a full-body workout. Whether you’re taking on a challenging mountain trail or a scenic nature path, hiking lets you explore new areas while simultaneously boosting your fitness levels.

Besides its physical benefits, hiking is also mentally rewarding. Spending time in nature has been shown to reduce stress, improve mood, and even boost creativity. The fresh air and beautiful surroundings make it easy to forget you’re even exercising, making hiking an enjoyable and sustainable activity throughout the summer months.

For an extra challenge, try adding a weighted backpack to increase resistance and build strength. Make sure to wear sturdy shoes, especially if you’re planning to tackle uneven or rocky terrain.

Key Benefits of Hiking:

  • Enhances cardiovascular fitness
  • Strengthens the lower body
  • Reduces stress and improves mood
  • Allows you to explore nature

4. Outdoor Yoga

Yoga is traditionally practiced indoors, but taking your mat outside during the summer can elevate your practice to new levels. Yoga in nature allows you to breathe in fresh air, listen to the sounds of birds or the ocean, and bask in the sunlight as you move through your poses. This can have a calming effect and help deepen your connection with your body and mind.

Start with gentle stretches in the morning to greet the sun, or join a local outdoor yoga class to enjoy the community atmosphere. For a more challenging practice, try vinyasa or power yoga, where you flow from one pose to the next, keeping your heart rate up and building strength. Practicing yoga outdoors can also improve balance, flexibility, and mental focus, making it a holistic workout for both your body and mind.

Key Benefits of Outdoor Yoga:

  • Increases flexibility and strength
  • Enhances mental clarity and focus
  • Promotes relaxation and reduces anxiety
  • Improves balance and coordination

5. Cycling

Cycling is an excellent full-body workout that builds endurance and strengthens the legs, core, and arms. With the warmer weather and longer days, summer is the perfect time to explore cycling as an outdoor exercise. Whether you’re riding through a park, along a scenic trail, or even commuting to work, cycling provides both a low-impact and high-intensity workout, depending on the intensity and terrain.

If you’re looking to improve cardiovascular health and build lower body strength, cycling is a great way to do so. Increase the intensity by riding uphill or incorporating interval training into your route. It’s also a great group activity—invite friends or family to join you for a cycling adventure.

Key Benefits of Cycling:

  • Builds cardiovascular endurance
  • Strengthens lower body muscles
  • Low-impact on joints
  • Great for socializing and group fitness

6. Outdoor Bootcamp

If you prefer a structured workout that combines cardio, strength, and endurance training, consider joining an outdoor bootcamp. These high-intensity workouts typically involve a combination of running, jumping, push-ups, sit-ups, squats, and other bodyweight exercises. Bootcamps are often held in parks or other outdoor spaces and can be a fun way to push yourself while getting in shape.

Outdoor bootcamps are also great for group motivation. Being part of a class with others can push you to work harder, and the trainer can provide guidance and support throughout the session. If you’re looking for variety, many bootcamps switch up the exercises regularly, so you’ll never get bored.

Key Benefits of Outdoor Bootcamps:

  • Full-body workout combining strength and cardio
  • Increases endurance and stamina
  • Provides structure and accountability
  • Great for group motivation

7. Kayaking or Paddleboarding

If you’re lucky enough to live near a body of water, kayaking or paddleboarding is an excellent way to enjoy the outdoors while getting a killer workout. These activities engage the core, arms, and shoulders as you paddle through the water, and they also improve your balance and coordination.

Kayaking offers an intense upper-body workout, especially if you’re navigating through rough waters or on long-distance journeys. Paddleboarding, on the other hand, requires a strong focus on core engagement, as you balance on the board while paddling. Both activities are also low-impact, making them perfect for people who want to protect their joints while still getting an effective workout.

Key Benefits of Kayaking and Paddleboarding:

  • Builds upper body and core strength
  • Improves balance and coordination
  • Low-impact, great for joint health
  • Provides an opportunity to explore nature

8. Swimming

Swimming is a total-body workout that’s gentle on the joints while providing excellent cardiovascular benefits. Whether you’re swimming laps in a pool or taking a dip in a nearby lake or ocean, the water offers resistance that helps build strength and endurance.

For a full-body workout, try swimming different strokes (freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke, and butterfly) to target different muscle groups. The water’s buoyancy reduces the impact on your joints, making it an ideal workout for people with injuries or those looking for a low-impact alternative to running or other high-intensity exercises.

Key Benefits of Swimming:

  • Builds full-body strength
  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Low-impact, great for joint protection
  • Provides a cooling effect on hot summer days

9. Climbing

Rock climbing, whether outdoors on natural rock formations or at a climbing gym, is an exciting and challenging workout that engages multiple muscle groups. It improves upper body and core strength while also testing your problem-solving abilities as you navigate different routes.

Outdoor climbing can take you to new heights—literally and figuratively. The varying challenges of each climbing route, from bouldering to top-rope climbing, keep things interesting and allow you to push your limits. The combination of mental and physical effort makes climbing a rewarding and unique summer workout.

Key Benefits of Climbing:

  • Builds upper body, core, and grip strength
  • Improves flexibility and endurance
  • Mental challenge and problem-solving
  • Boosts confidence and resilience

Conclusion

This summer, take your workouts outside and embrace the endless possibilities that nature has to offer. From trail running and beach workouts to swimming and kayaking, there’s something for everyone to enjoy. These outdoor workouts not only improve your physical fitness but also provide the opportunity to explore new environments, meet new people, and refresh your mind. So, lace up your shoes, grab a mat, or head to the water, and start enjoying the numerous benefits that outdoor exercise brings.

No matter your fitness level, the summer is the perfect time to try something new, have fun, and stay active. Happy summer training!
