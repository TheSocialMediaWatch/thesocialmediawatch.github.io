The Best Online Communities for Comedy Writers


Comedy writing is a unique craft that blends creativity, timing, cultural awareness, and a little bit of madness. For aspiring and professional comedy writers alike, the journey is often solitary—hours spent sculpting jokes, punchlines, sketches, and funny stories. However, tapping into the right online communities can transform this solo grind into a collaborative, enriching adventure filled with feedback, camaraderie, and inspiration.

If you’re looking to sharpen your comedic voice, connect with like-minded creatives, or break into the comedy writing scene, exploring online hubs dedicated to comedy writers is a fantastic step. This blog post dives deep into the best online communities for comedy writers, offering detailed insights into how each serves its members, what unique advantages they bring, and how you can get the most out of them. Ready to find your comedic tribe? Let’s jump right in.

Why Join an Online Community for Comedy Writers?

Before listing the top destinations, let’s consider why online communities are invaluable for comedy writers:

  • Feedback and Critique: Comedy thrives on timing and precision. Getting meaningful feedback from others who understand the craft helps sharpen your material.
  • Networking Opportunities: Many online communities connect you to industry professionals, helping open doors to writing gigs, collaborations, and mentorship.
  • Creative Inspiration: Sharing jokes, hearing others’ work, and discussing trends in comedy fuels creativity.
  • Accountability: Writing can be intimidating. A community offers motivation and deadlines that keep you moving forward.
  • Resources: Many platforms provide writing prompts, podcast recommendations, industry news, and educational materials.

1. Reddit’s r/ComedyWriting

Overview:
Reddit, often described as the front page of the internet, hosts one of the most active and accessible communities for comedy writers: r/ComedyWriting. This subreddit is a bustling forum where writers from all backgrounds share scripts, jokes, feedback, and resources.

Features:

  • Peer Reviews: Members post jokes, stand-up bits, sketches, or sitcom scripts for constructive critique.
  • AMA Sessions: Occasionally, professional comedy writers or comedians host “Ask Me Anything” sessions, providing inside tips straight from industry veterans.
  • Writing Prompts and Challenges: To spark your creativity, the community regularly posts prompts themed around current events or comedic styles.
  • Open Discussions: Topics range from dealing with writer’s block to breaking into TV writing or navigating comedy writers’ rooms.

Why It Stands Out:
r/ComedyWriting thrives on its diversity—stand-up comedians, late-night writers, web series creators, and even amateur jokers all find a place here. The fast-paced discussions and upvoting system help surface the most appreciated content, making it a highly dynamic zone for feedback and idea exchange.

2. The Second City Network Forums

Overview:
Second City is a legendary institution in comedy training and improvisation, with alumni including Tina Fey, Stephen Colbert, and Bill Murray. Their online forums, though less frenetic than Reddit, offer specialized spaces for comedy writing.

Features:

  • Structured Workshops and Threads: Writers can submit work for detailed critiques and participate in semester-based writing challenges.
  • Improv Writers Unite: Given Second City’s improv foundation, the forums are rich with advice on writing for improv, sketch, and even musical comedy formats.
  • Career Advice: Threads often include practical advice on margins for comedy writing contracts, agent scouting, and pitching ideas.

Why It Stands Out:
If you want to mix improv sensibilities with your writing, or you’re targeting television and sketch comedy, this structured, mentorship-driven forum is ideal. The connection to a long-standing comedy brand also means occasional chances to submit to live shows or get scholarships for in-person classes.

3. Stage32 Comedy Writing Group

Overview:
Stage32 bills itself as “the social network for creatives,” and their comedy writing group is a vibrant hub for television, film, and web series writers.

Features:

  • Industry Exposure: Regular live webinars with agents, managers, and established comedy writers.
  • Script Feedback: Members exchange full scripts and treatments, which is helpful for narrative comedy writers.
  • Pitch Opportunities: Members often share open calls for submissions, contests, and workshops geared towards landing paid gigs.
  • Jobs Board: A listing of comedy writing jobs and internships from around the world, including remote options.

Why It Stands Out:
Stage32 strikes a unique balance between social networking and professional development. If your goal is commerce—selling scripts, finding representation, or landing paid writing gigs—this platform is tailored to help you connect directly with the business side of comedy.

4. Comedy Writing Discord Servers

Overview:
Discord is a platform originally created for gamers but increasingly popular among creative communities for its real-time chat and voice communication. There are several thriving Discord servers focused on comedy writing.

Features:

  • Real-Time Interaction: Chat with writers anytime through text or voice channels.
  • Workshopping Rooms: Dedicated channels for sharing jokes and scripts with instant feedback.
  • Writing Challenges: Competitive and casual challenges, often with themes or reward systems.
  • Networking and Social: Voice hangouts, game nights, and collaboration spaces encourage community bonding.

Why It Stands Out:
What makes Discord servers attractive is immediacy. Unlike forums, you can bounce ideas off people in real-time, which fuels fast iterations and instant morale boosts. The social aspect helps build lasting relationships that often extend beyond writing.

5. SimplyScripts Forum: Comedy Scriptwriting Section

Overview:
SimplyScripts is a large repository for scripts of all genres. Their forums include a dedicated section for comedy scriptwriting, where writers share original scripts and receive peer reviews.

Features:

  • Script Database: Access a wealth of comedy scripts across formats including sitcoms, sketches, and feature-length films.
  • Review and Collaborate: Forums invite writers to submit scripts for review and discuss dialogue, structure, and comedic beats.
  • Educational Resources: Guides on comedic timing, character development, and formatting scripts for television and film.

Why It Stands Out:
For writers interested in script format and structure, the SimplyScripts forum is a goldmine. The focus on full-length scripts makes it perfect for film and television comedy writers aiming to polish longer-form projects.

6. Twitter and Mastodon Hashtags and Threads

Overview:
While not a traditional community platform, Twitter and Mastodon have become places where comedy writers share micro-threads, jokes, and advice. Hashtags like #ComedyWriting and #AmWritingComedy gather global writers in a lightweight, constantly refreshed stream.

Features:

  • Bite-sized Insights: Jokes, quick tips, writing processes, and storytelling threads offer endless inspiration.
  • Networking: Follow and interact with celebrated comedy writers, producers, and comedians who are active on these platforms.
  • Open Calls and Announcements: Many writing contests, workshops, and open submissions are shared globally.

Why It Stands Out:
The brevity and speed of these platforms suit humor’s fast nature. Writers build personal brands and follow trends quickly, turning social media into a rapid-fire workshop and networking tool.

7. Absolute Write Water Cooler: Humor & Comedy Threads

Overview:
Absolute Write is a large forum dedicated to writers of all genres, but it includes a well-trafficked section specifically for humor and comedy writing.

Features:

  • Professional Advice: Threads often include nitty-gritty details about publishing humor books, writing humorous essays, and crafting funny memoirs.
  • Community Support: Writers share wins, failures, and industry news with a supportive tone.
  • Cross-Genre Opportunities: Because Absolute Write covers many literary genres, comedy writers can engage with novelists, bloggers, and journalists to widen their skillset.

Why It Stands Out:
This forum caters especially to writers interested in comedic prose, humor in literary formats, or humor that blends with other genres like creative nonfiction or satire.

Tips for Maximizing Your Membership in Online Comedy Writing Communities

Having access to these communities is a great start, but what really elevates your experience is how you engage:

  • Give Feedback Generously: Comedy is subjective but giving constructive and thoughtful feedback builds trust and encourages reciprocal advice.
  • Participate Regularly: Consistent interaction through comments, critiques, or writing challenges keeps you visible and sharpens your skills.
  • Be Open to Criticism: Comedy writing demands thick skin. Use feedback as a tool for growth, not discouragement.
  • Network Authentically: Don’t just post your stuff—build relationships by commenting, celebrating others’ successes, and sharing resources.
  • Diversify Your Input: Frequent multiple communities to get a variety of perspectives. Each platform has a unique culture and expertise.
  • Stay Updated: Follow opportunities announced in these forums to enter competitions, residencies, fellowships, and gigs.

The Road Ahead: Beyond Online Communities

While online groups offer immense value, the best comedy writers complement these digital connections with real-world experiences—open mic nights, improv troupes, writing classes, and networking events. These face-to-face interactions combine with online feedback loops to create a rich, multifaceted learning environment.

Comedy writing is an evolving craft that benefits from constant practice, openness, and being plugged into vibrant communities. Leveraging one or several of the above platforms can accelerate your comedic voice, open doors, and keep the creative sparks flying.

Conclusion

Choosing the right online comedy writing community depends on your goals. Whether you seek peer feedback, professional development, networking, or inspiration, there’s a corner of the internet waiting for you. From Reddit’s r/ComedyWriting to Stage32’s industry-savvy network, from real-time Discord chats to the rich scripts vaults of SimplyScripts, these communities are proof that even solitary writers thrive best amid creative company.

Start exploring, keep writing, and keep sharing your humor with the world. Your next punchline might just come from a late-night scroll through one of these incredible communities—and that, in itself, is a laugh worth having.
