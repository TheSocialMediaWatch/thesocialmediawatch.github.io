Gin Goes Global: International Gin Trends


Gin, the spirited spirit with a storied history, has undergone a remarkable transformation in recent years, both in production and popularity. From traditional English gin to cutting-edge interpretations from around the globe, the gin landscape has diversified like an artist’s palette.

Gin’s soaring fortunes have led to a global explosion of flavors, styles, and even ingredients. As the world becomes increasingly connected, gin enthusiasts and bartenders are reaching for recipes beyond the classics of London Dry and Martinez. Let’s explore some international gin trends that are making waves.

Australian Gin: Land Down Under

Australia’s burgeoning gin scene is a testament to the country’s unique terroir and ability to experiment. Native botanicals such as lemon myrtle, wattle seed, and bush tomato have found their way into Australian gins, infusing them with local character. Australian distillers also embrace native fruits, likeabboodja berries, to create truly distinctive spirits. Look out for Australian gins in the near future – they’re making waves!

Japanese Gin: The Art of Craft

Japan has a rich history with spirits, between sake and whiskey. The entrance of Japanese gin reflects the country’s meticulous craftsmanship and attention to detail. Juniper takes center stage, but watch out for Japanese botanicals like yuzu, savine, and kabosu, offering a subtle twist that demonstrates Japanese ingenuity. Japanese gins are delicate and complex, a testament to the island nation’s ability to innovate.

Brazilian Gin: A Burst of Carioca Flair

The heart of Brazil pulses with energy, and their gins are no exception. Brazil’s thriving culture embraces bold flavors, making room for unique infusions like açaí, popcorn, and coffee. These botanicals create distinctive gins that blur the line between spirits and flavored liquors. Look for Brazilian gins to elongate your summer cocktail list.

South African Gin: A Rainbow of Flavors

South African gins are a melting pot of global influences, melding with local ingredients for remarkable results. From the Rutherglen distillery’s Rooibos gin, which infuses the vitamin-rich South African red tea, to the low-ABV Alfred gin, which takes a page from the herbal medicine books, innovation abounds. While juniper reigns supreme, South African gin pushes boundaries with native flavors like Buchu, Fynbos, and Lemon Trout.

Indian Gin: Spiced and Ready

India’s entry into the gin scene is a symphony of spices, an homage to a history of flavor explorations. From the spice-forward Jaisalmer Indian Craft Gin to the bold Maharaja Ayudhya Thai Gin, Indian distillers tout a myriad of spice combinations. Experiment with gin from this diverse nation to add a dash of exotic flair to your creations.

Iberian Gin: From Sherry to Sunshine

Gin traverses the Iberian Peninsula, with Spain and Portugal leading the charge. Sherry cask-aged gin from Bodegas Garn to the bright, citrus-heavy Alexandrian gins, Spain and Portugal’s gin scene is a testament to their winemaking prowess transferred to stills. Look out for innovative botanical combinations, reflecting the region’s passion for experimentation.

As global tastes continue to intersect, gin has found itself in the midst of a whirlwind of innovation. These international trends have opened a world of new possibilities for cocktail enthusiasts and bartenders alike. Embrace the diverse flavors and styles, and remember, the sky is the limit for gin – and the world is its canvas.
