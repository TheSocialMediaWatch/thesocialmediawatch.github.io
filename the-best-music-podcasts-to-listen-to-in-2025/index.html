The Best Music Podcasts to Listen to in 2025


Podcasts have become one of the most popular ways to consume content, and when it comes to music, the podcast world is brimming with rich discussions, artist interviews, genre explorations, and in-depth analyses of the latest trends. With 2025 fast approaching, music podcasts continue to evolve, offering a broad spectrum of content for music lovers of all kinds. Whether you’re a fan of indie rock, classical, hip-hop, or electronic, there’s a podcast for every listener who wants to stay updated on their favorite genres or explore new musical landscapes. Here’s a roundup of the best music podcasts to check out in 2025.

1. Song Exploder

If you’ve ever wondered what goes into making a song, Song Exploder is your perfect companion. This critically acclaimed podcast, created by Hrishikesh Hirway, invites musicians to break down their songs, piece by piece, revealing the creative process behind some of the most iconic tracks of the modern music era. From indie to mainstream, the show offers an inside look at how songs are composed, recorded, and produced.

Each episode takes a song from an artist, deconstructing its components — from lyrics and melodies to production techniques. Artists like Metallica, Lorde, The Killers, and Fleet Foxes have graced the podcast, sharing the personal stories and challenges behind their hits. Whether you’re a budding musician or just someone who loves to hear about the stories behind the music, Song Exploder remains one of the best music podcasts to dive into.

2. All Songs Considered

Produced by NPR, All Songs Considered is a staple in the music podcast world. Hosted by Bob Boilen and Robin Hilton, the show delivers insightful commentary on the latest music releases, from mainstream hits to underground gems. Each episode features an eclectic mix of new songs, interviews with artists, and discussions on the latest trends in music.

What makes All Songs Considered stand out is its commitment to both new music and artist discovery. The podcast frequently features up-and-coming artists alongside established names, offering listeners a diverse and engaging way to explore the evolving landscape of contemporary music. Whether you’re looking for fresh sounds or curious about what’s happening in the indie scene, this podcast is a great place to start.

3. The Talkhouse Music Podcast

If you’re looking for a podcast that offers candid, no-holds-barred conversations with musicians, The Talkhouse Music Podcast is the one to listen to. Produced by the Talkhouse, this podcast brings together musicians to discuss their craft, the music industry, and personal stories that shaped their careers.

The beauty of The Talkhouse lies in the intimate discussions between musicians, as they often interview one another, offering listeners a unique perspective on their creative process. From experimental rock to electronic to pop, this podcast covers a broad range of genres and features a variety of influential figures in the music world. If you want to hear artists get real about their experiences in the industry, this is the podcast for you.

4. Dissect

Dissect is a deep dive podcast for music fans who appreciate detailed analysis of albums. Created by Cole Cuchna, this podcast takes listeners through the entirety of an album, breaking down the music, lyrics, and themes in a meticulous, episode-by-episode format. While most music podcasts focus on interviews or discussions of new releases, Dissect stands out because of its academic approach to exploring music.

In 2025, Dissect continues to be a standout for music fans who want to gain a deeper understanding of some of the most influential albums ever made. Past seasons have covered albums like Kendrick Lamar’s To Pimp a Butterfly, Frank Ocean’s Blonde, and Kanye West’s My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy. With Cuchna’s thoughtful analysis, Dissect elevates the listener’s experience, offering new perspectives on albums they may have loved for years.

5. The FADER Uncovered

For anyone with a passion for music journalism and cultural exploration, The FADER Uncovered is an essential podcast. Hosted by the FADER’s editor-in-chief, Naomi Zeichner, this show takes a look behind the scenes of the music industry, interviewing the stars of today and yesterday to get to the heart of what makes them tick.

From hip-hop legends to emerging indie artists, The FADER Uncovered offers exclusive interviews that give listeners access to the minds of their favorite artists. Each episode focuses on an artist’s journey through the music industry, their creative process, and the cultural influences that shaped their sound. Whether you’re interested in the evolution of a genre or the personal stories of your favorite stars, this podcast provides rich, engaging content.

6. Switched on Pop

Switched on Pop is the go-to podcast for anyone who wants to understand the ins and outs of pop music, from a technical perspective. Hosted by musicologist Nate Sloan and songwriter Charlie Harding, Switched on Pop deconstructs the mechanics of pop songs, offering insights into melody, harmony, rhythm, and production techniques that make pop music so universally appealing.

This podcast does a fantastic job of blending music theory with popular culture, making it accessible even to those with no formal musical education. Whether you’re interested in the current trends in pop music or want to understand why certain songs are so irresistible, Switched on Pop is both informative and highly engaging.

7. Rolling Stone Music Now

As one of the most trusted names in music journalism, Rolling Stone has brought its vast expertise to the podcast world with Rolling Stone Music Now. This podcast covers the latest in music news, offering interviews with top artists, deep dives into trending topics, and discussions on the music industry’s most pressing issues.

With hosts like Brian Hiatt, Rolling Stone Music Now stands out for its well-researched and insightful commentary. Each episode brings listeners up-to-date on the biggest stories in music, including album reviews, upcoming tours, and controversies. For those who want to keep their finger on the pulse of the ever-changing music scene, this podcast is an excellent source of information.

8. The Great Albums Podcast

If you’re a music lover who enjoys reliving the magic of classic albums, The Great Albums Podcast is worth checking out. Hosted by Bill and Brian, this podcast takes a deep dive into the most iconic albums in music history. Each episode focuses on one album, dissecting its background, production, and impact on the music world.

From The Beatles’ Sgt. Pepper’s Lonely Hearts Club Band to Radiohead’s OK Computer, The Great Albums Podcast celebrates albums that have shaped the cultural landscape. The hosts offer insightful commentary, backed by a deep knowledge of music history. If you’re someone who enjoys revisiting legendary albums or discovering them for the first time, this podcast will certainly enhance your listening experience.

9. Pitchfork Review

If you trust Pitchfork for your music news and reviews, then their podcast, Pitchfork Review, is the natural next step. This show offers an in-depth look at the most anticipated album releases, cultural trends, and music reviews from the iconic music publication.

Pitchfork Review regularly features interviews with artists, producers, and writers, making it a fantastic source for discovering new music and learning more about the world of music criticism. Whether you’re interested in alternative, experimental, or indie music, Pitchfork Review offers intelligent discussions and analysis that is well worth your time.

10. The Library with Timereck

If you’re into hip-hop, The Library with Timereck is a must-listen. Timereck, a seasoned hip-hop journalist and cultural commentator, explores the world of hip-hop in an engaging and often thought-provoking way. Each episode features in-depth interviews with legends and rising stars in the hip-hop scene, delving into their creative process, their struggles, and their contributions to the genre.

What sets this podcast apart is its focus on storytelling. Timereck doesn’t just ask questions about music — he invites guests to tell their own stories in their own words. For anyone interested in the world of hip-hop, from its beginnings to its current state, this podcast is an essential listen.

Conclusion

2025 is shaping up to be a great year for music podcasts, with an ever-growing variety of content that caters to diverse tastes and interests. Whether you’re a casual listener or a hardcore music enthusiast, these podcasts offer something for everyone. From in-depth album analyses to interviews with your favorite artists and discussions about the music industry, these podcasts will keep you engaged and informed. So, plug in your headphones, subscribe to your favorites, and enjoy the world of music in a whole new way this year!
