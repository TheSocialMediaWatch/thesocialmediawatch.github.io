A New Model for Credit-Bearing Online Courses: Competency-Based Education


As technology continues to reshape the landscape of higher education, one educational model is gaining traction and redefining the way students approach their coursework. This innovative approach, known as Competency-Based Education (CBE), is shaking up the online course industry with its focus on skills acquisition and flexible, student-centered learning. In this blog post, we’ll explore the foundations of CBE, its key features, and its potential far-reaching impacts on online learning.

At its core, Competency-Based Education is a groundbreaking approach to online learning that emphasizes the acquisition of clearly defined competencies or skills, instead of traditional measures of academic progress, such as credit hours and letter grades. Students who participate in CBE programs are assessed based on their mastery of these competencies, with the ultimate goal of providing a more flexible and personalized educational experience.

CBE programs boast several key features that set them apart from more traditional online course models. For one, these programs tend to be more flexible, allowing students to move through the course material at their own pace. This self-directed pace is particularly beneficial for adult learners with a need for flexibility, as well as for students who may require more time to grasp certain concepts. Additionally, the focus on skill acquisition means that CBE courses tend to merge subject matter with real-world applications, better preparing students for success in their professional or personal pursuits.

Beyond flexibility and real-world orientation, CBE programs also embrace a more assessable approach to student evaluation. Rather than relying solely on traditional exams or quizzes, these programs employ various assessment methods, such as projects, portfolios, and practical applications, to evaluate a student’s mastery of competencies. Not only does this provide a more comprehensive view of a student’s grasp of the material, but it also promotes the development of essential skills such as critical thinking, communication, and problem-solving.

Now, let’s delve into the potential far-reaching impacts of Competency-Based Education on online learning as a whole. First and foremost, CBE has the potential to democratize education, making it more accessible and equitable. By offering the ability to progress at one’s own pace and emphasizing practical and transferable skills, CBE programs can provide a more inclusive and supportive environment for learners from all walks of life.

Moreover, as technology continues to play a growing role in the education space, CBE may very well serve as a unifying framework for interdisciplinary collaboration and information sharing. With its emphasis on competencies and the capacity to weave together subject matter from diverse fields, CBE is well-positioned to facilitate new connections and partnerships between institutions, educators, and students.

Finally, Competency-Based Education holds the potential to increase the efficacy of online learning itself. By prioritizing the acquisition of skills and competencies, CBE courses can be better tailored to meet the needs of individual learners, ultimately leading to a more engaging and fulfilling educational experience.

In summary, Competency-Based Education offers a unique and innovative approach to online learning, emphasizing flexibility, practical skills, and personalized assessment. With the potential to increase access, foster interdisciplinary collaboration, and enhance overall course efficacy, CBE is undoubtedly a model worth watching as it continues to reshape the landscape of higher education.
