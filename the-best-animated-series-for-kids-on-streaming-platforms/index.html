The Best Animated Series for Kids on Streaming Platforms


In a world where screens dominate leisure time, animated series have emerged as a delightful and educational way for children to engage with storytelling. With the proliferation of streaming platforms, parents now have a wealth of options at their fingertips. From whimsical adventures to educational content, animated series not only entertain but also provide valuable lessons. In this comprehensive guide, we delve into some of the best animated series for kids available on various streaming platforms.

1. Bluey

Streaming Platform: Disney+

“Bluey” is an Australian animated series that has quickly become a favorite among children and parents alike. Following the adventures of a lovable Blue Heeler puppy named Bluey and her family, this show beautifully captures the joys and challenges of childhood. Each episode is filled with imaginative play and heartwarming moments that resonate with both kids and adults.

Why It’s Great:

  • Relatable Themes: The show addresses everyday family dynamics, making it easy for kids to relate to Bluey and her experiences.
  • Imaginative Play: Encourages creative thinking and imaginative play, showcasing its importance in childhood development.
  • Emotional Intelligence: Teaches children about emotions, problem-solving, and empathy through relatable scenarios.

2. Peppa Pig

Streaming Platform: Netflix

“Peppa Pig” is a British animated series that has gained international acclaim. The show centers around Peppa, a cheeky little piggy, and her family and friends. Each episode is short, making it perfect for younger viewers, and the simple animation style and charming storytelling are captivating.

Why It’s Great:

  • Life Lessons: Each episode imparts valuable lessons about friendship, family, and social interactions.
  • Short Episodes: Ideal for toddlers’ attention spans, allowing for easy viewing without overwhelming them.
  • Cultural Diversity: Introduces children to different cultures and lifestyles through Peppa’s adventures.

3. My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic

Streaming Platform: Netflix, Hulu

“My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic” is a vibrant and engaging series that promotes the value of friendship. Set in the magical land of Equestria, the show follows Twilight Sparkle and her friends as they navigate various challenges and adventures. The rich storytelling and colorful animation captivate young audiences.

Why It’s Great:

  • Positive Messages: Each episode emphasizes traits like kindness, loyalty, and honesty, promoting positive social behavior.
  • Strong Female Characters: Features a diverse cast of strong female characters, providing excellent role models for young girls.
  • Engaging Lore: The series has a rich backstory and lore that keeps fans engaged beyond the episodes.

4. Trolls: The Beat Goes On!

Streaming Platform: Netflix

Based on the hit movie franchise, “Trolls: The Beat Goes On!” continues the adventures of Poppy, Branch, and their friends in a colorful and musical world. The series is filled with catchy songs, vibrant animation, and heartwarming stories.

Why It’s Great:

  • Music and Dance: Encourages children to appreciate music and dance, enhancing their creativity and self-expression.
  • Friendship and Teamwork: Each episode teaches valuable lessons about collaboration and the importance of friendship.
  • Fun Characters: The quirky characters and humor appeal to both kids and their parents, making it a family-friendly choice.

5. The Magic School Bus Rides Again

Streaming Platform: Netflix

A modern take on the classic “The Magic School Bus,” this series follows Ms. Frizzle’s sister, Fiona, as she takes a new class on wild educational adventures. Each episode explores different scientific concepts, from the solar system to ecosystems, making learning fun and engaging.

Why It’s Great:

  • Educational Content: Combines entertainment with science education, making complex topics accessible for kids.
  • Curiosity and Exploration: Instills a sense of curiosity and encourages children to ask questions about the world around them.
  • Diverse Representation: Features a diverse cast of characters, promoting inclusivity and representation.

6. Carmen Sandiego

Streaming Platform: Netflix

“Carmen Sandiego” reintroduces the iconic character in a new light. This animated series follows the adventures of Carmen, a globetrotting thief who steals from thieves to right the wrongs of the world. With stunning animation and clever storytelling, it keeps children engaged while teaching them about geography and culture.

Why It’s Great:

  • Educational Value: Introduces children to different cultures, landmarks, and geography through Carmen’s adventures.
  • Empowered Protagonist: Features a strong, intelligent female lead who demonstrates that being smart is just as cool as being daring.
  • Mystery and Intrigue: The plot keeps kids on the edge of their seats, encouraging them to think critically.

7. Avatar: The Last Airbender

Streaming Platform: Netflix

Though originally aired in the mid-2000s, “Avatar: The Last Airbender” has found a new audience on streaming platforms. This critically acclaimed series follows Aang, the last Airbender, as he embarks on a quest to bring peace to a war-torn world. With deep storytelling and rich character development, this series is a must-watch for older kids.

Why It’s Great:

  • Complex Themes: Tackles issues such as war, identity, and morality, making it suitable for older children and teens.
  • Cultural Richness: Draws inspiration from various cultures, providing a deeper understanding of diversity and respect.
  • Character Development: Features well-developed characters that undergo significant growth throughout the series.

8. The Loud House

Streaming Platform: Nickelodeon, Paramount+

“The Loud House” offers a humorous and relatable glimpse into the chaotic life of Lincoln Loud, the only boy in a family of 11 children. The show explores the dynamics of sibling relationships while delivering laugh-out-loud moments.

Why It’s Great:

  • Family Dynamics: Highlights the ups and downs of family life, helping children understand relationships with siblings.
  • Humor and Heart: Balances humor with heartfelt moments, making it enjoyable for kids and their parents.
  • Diverse Representation: Features a diverse cast of characters, showcasing different backgrounds and experiences.

9. The Dragon Prince

Streaming Platform: Netflix

“The Dragon Prince” is an epic fantasy series that follows two princes and an elven assassin as they embark on a quest to bring peace to their warring lands. With stunning animation and a rich narrative, this series is perfect for kids who enjoy adventure and fantasy.

Why It’s Great:

  • Engaging Storytelling: The intricate plot and character development keep viewers invested in the story.
  • Themes of Unity: Promotes messages of friendship, understanding, and collaboration across cultures.
  • Inclusive Representation: Features diverse characters and storylines, appealing to a broad audience.

10. Craig of the Creek

Streaming Platform: Cartoon Network, HBO Max

“Craig of the Creek” captures the essence of childhood adventures in a neighborhood creek. The series follows Craig and his friends as they explore their surroundings, encounter challenges, and learn valuable life lessons along the way.

Why It’s Great:

  • Realistic Friendships: Represents the ups and downs of childhood friendships, offering relatable scenarios for kids.
  • Exploration and Imagination: Encourages outdoor play and imaginative exploration, promoting physical activity.
  • Inclusivity: Features a diverse cast of characters, highlighting different backgrounds and experiences.

Conclusion

As streaming platforms continue to expand their offerings, parents have an incredible array of animated series to choose from for their children. These shows not only entertain but also provide valuable lessons, foster creativity, and promote diversity. From the heartwarming adventures of “Bluey” to the epic storytelling of “Avatar: The Last Airbender,” there’s something for every child to enjoy.

Choosing the right animated series for your child can enhance their viewing experience and provide them with opportunities to learn and grow. So, gather the family, grab some popcorn, and dive into the wonderful world of animated storytelling!
