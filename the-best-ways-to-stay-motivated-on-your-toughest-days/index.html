The Best Ways to Stay Motivated on Your Toughest Days


We all have days when motivation seems like a distant memory—when the weight of life’s challenges makes even the simplest tasks feel monumental. Whether it’s a demanding project at work, personal struggles, or just the grind of daily life, those tough days can drain your energy and willpower. But motivation is not a finite resource; it’s something you can nurture, cultivate, and even summon in the face of adversity.

Here’s a deep dive into some effective ways to stay motivated when you need it the most, helping you push through difficult days and come out stronger on the other side.

Understanding What Motivation Really Is

Before diving into strategies, it’s helpful to understand motivation itself. It’s a psychological drive that propels us to act towards a goal. Motivation can be intrinsic (coming from within, such as personal satisfaction) or extrinsic (influenced by outside rewards like approval or money).

On your hardest days, intrinsic motivation can be tougher to tap into because your emotional reserves may feel depleted. That’s why many of the best techniques to stay motivated focus on both replenishing your energy and reigniting your inner drive.

1. Break Big Tasks Into Smaller Steps

One of the biggest hurdles on tough days is feeling overwhelmed by the size of a task. When the end goal feels distant or insurmountable, breaking it down into smaller, manageable steps can reduce anxiety and provide a clear path forward.

For example, rather than thinking “I have to finish this entire report today,” shift your focus to “I’ll outline the introduction,” then “write the first section,” and so on. Crossing off these smaller tasks creates feelings of accomplishment that fuel continued motivation.

Tip:

Use techniques like the Pomodoro Technique – work 25 minutes intensely, then take a 5-minute break. These short, focused bursts can help maintain energy and concentration.

2. Revisit Your ‘Why’

When motivation wanes, it’s often because we’ve lost sight of why we’re doing something in the first place. Taking a moment to reconnect with your core reasons can reignite passion and determination.

Ask yourself:

  • What are my ultimate goals?
  • How does this task contribute to my bigger vision?
  • What will achieving this mean for me or those around me?

Writing down your reasons can make them feel more tangible and real. Keep this list somewhere visible – a sticky note on your desk or a note in your phone – so you can return to it as needed.

3. Harness the Power of Positive Affirmations

Negative self-talk is a common nemesis of motivation. On your hardest days, you might catch yourself thinking “I can’t do this” or “This is pointless.” These beliefs chip away at your confidence and energy.

Positive affirmations flip the script by intentionally focusing on empowering, uplifting statements. They might sound simple, but repeated, genuine affirmations can rewire your mindset over time.

Examples:

  • “I am capable of overcoming challenges.”
  • “Every small step takes me closer to my goal.”
  • “I have the strength to get through today.”

Say them aloud in the morning or whenever you feel your motivation dipping.

4. Create an Environment That Supports You

Your external environment has a powerful impact on your internal motivation. Clutter, noise, or distractions can drain your focus and energy, especially when you’re already struggling.

Design a workspace or area that is clean, organized, and free of unnecessary distraction. This doesn’t mean it has to be sterile or boring — adding elements that inspire you, like plants, artwork, or meaningful quotes, can improve your mood and motivation.

If possible, tailor your setting according to what suits you best—isolate yourself for quiet concentration or set up in a bustling café atmosphere if that stimulates your energy.

5. Use Visualization Techniques to See the Finish Line

Visualization is more than daydreaming. It’s a mental exercise where you vividly imagine the successful completion of your task or goal—the feelings, the sights, sounds, and rewards connected to that achievement.

This practice can increase motivation because it makes the outcome feel more attainable and real. Spend a few minutes daily imagining your success in detail. How does it feel to have finished? What is the impact on you or others?

Visualization primes your brain both emotionally and cognitively to keep pushing forward despite obstacles.

6. Incorporate Movement Into Your Day

Physical movement has a remarkable influence on mental state. Exercise stimulates the release of endorphins and neurotransmitters like dopamine and serotonin, which elevate mood and motivation.

Even on the toughest days, a brief walk, a gentle stretch, or any form of light exercise can improve your energy and focus. Taking breaks for physical activity also helps reduce mental fatigue and maintain productivity.

If you’re facing a daunting task, try pairing it with simple movement rituals: stand and stretch every hour, do a quick set of jumping jacks, or even dance to an upbeat song for a few minutes.

7. Seek Encouragement and Social Support

Humans are social beings, and tapping into supportive relationships can provide a significant motivation boost. When you’re struggling, reaching out to friends, family, or colleagues might offer fresh perspective, empathy, or simply encouragement.

Even a quick message or phone call where you share what you’re facing and receive positive feedback can lift your spirits and sharpen your resolve.

Consider joining communities or accountability groups related to your goals. Sharing progress and setbacks with others builds connection and keeps you engaged.

8. Practice Self-Compassion, Not Self-Criticism

It’s easy to fall into the trap of harsh self-judgment when motivation dips—telling yourself you’re lazy or not good enough. But these attitudes only deepen discouragement and hopelessness.

Self-compassion is about treating yourself with the same kindness and understanding you’d offer a friend. Recognize that everyone has off days and that experiencing difficulty is natural.

Instead of berating yourself, try internal messages like:

  • “I’m doing the best I can today.”
  • “It’s okay to struggle sometimes.”
  • “This is temporary, and I will keep going.”

This mindset reduces emotional resistance and creates a healthier mental space to rebuild motivation.

9. Reward Yourself Along the Way

Incentives don’t have to be grand gestures; even small rewards can create positive reinforcement loops that encourage persistence. When you complete a task or push through a tough period, celebrate that win.

Rewards can be anything that feels meaningful:

  • Taking a few minutes to enjoy your favorite snack
  • Watching a short episode of a show you like
  • Going outside for a brief stroll
  • Allowing yourself some guilt-free relaxation time

Knowing there’s a reward waiting can transform your relationship to difficult tasks, making them feel more manageable.

10. Keep Track of Progress Visually

Seeing evidence of your progress can be hugely motivating because it creates a sense of momentum. Consider maintaining a checklist, progress bar, journal, or calendar where you mark completed milestones.

This visual feedback reminds you that your efforts are building toward something larger. It also can help you identify patterns in your productivity, providing insight into what habits or techniques work best for you on tough days.

11. Manage Energy, Not Just Time

Efficient time management is valuable, but on hard days, managing your energy might be even more critical. Pay attention to when you naturally feel most alert and focused—morning, afternoon, evening—and schedule your most challenging work accordingly.

Create opportunities for rest and recovery throughout your day. Short naps, mindfulness exercises, or breaks spent in nature can restore depleted reserves, making motivation easier to sustain.

Recognizing and honoring your body’s rhythms helps you work smarter, not harder.

12. Embrace a Growth Mindset

A growth mindset—the belief that abilities and intelligence can be developed through effort—makes setbacks and challenges feel less daunting. Instead of seeing failure as a reflection of your worth or ability, view it as a step toward learning and growth.

This perspective fosters resilience and keeps motivation alive because you understand that effort leads to improvement, and tough days are part of the journey, not the destination.

Practice reframing negative experiences by asking:

  • What can I learn from this?
  • How can this challenge help me grow?
  • What’s the next small step I can take?

13. Tune Into Your Body’s Needs

Mental and physical health are intimately connected to motivation. When you’re fatigued, hungry, dehydrated, or emotionally stressed, it’s hard to summon the energy to work toward your goals.

Check in with your body regularly:

  • Are you getting enough sleep?
  • Have you eaten nourishing food?
  • Have you drunk adequate water?
  • Could you benefit from a few moments of deep breathing or meditation?

Honoring these basic needs builds a foundation where motivation can flourish.

14. Limit Distractions with Intentional Focus

On difficult days, distractions can be even more tempting—giving your brain a quick escape from discomfort. But constant interruptions often make it harder to regain focus and motivation.

Implementing intentional focus practices can help:

  • Turn off non-essential notifications.
  • Use apps or tools that block distracting websites.
  • Set clear boundaries for work versus rest periods.
  • Use noise-canceling headphones or ambient sound to improve concentration.

By protecting your attention, you create a mental space where motivation can revive itself.

15. Reflect on Past Successes

When your confidence and motivation take a hit, reminding yourself of times you succeeded against the odds can be a powerful boost. Reflect on challenges you’ve overcome, projects you completed, or personal victories.

Write them down or keep a “success log” that you revisit regularly. This practice reinforces a sense of competence and resilience, reminding your mind that you can handle tough days.

Final Thoughts

Staying motivated on your toughest days isn’t about willpower alone—it’s about creating a supportive ecosystem for yourself both mentally and physically. By combining practical strategies like breaking down tasks, managing your environment, and nurturing your mindset, you build resilience that allows motivation to bloom even through hardship.

Remember: motivation is dynamic, not static. It ebbs and flows naturally, but with intention and kindness toward yourself, you can keep it moving forward—step by step, moment by moment, until those tough days transform into progress and growth.

Take one small step today; that’s all motivation asks for to start making a difference.
