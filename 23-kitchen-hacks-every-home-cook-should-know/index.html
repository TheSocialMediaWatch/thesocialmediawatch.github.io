23 Kitchen Hacks Every Home Cook Should Know


Kitchen hacks. The progeny of innovation and necessity, they are the unsung heroes of culinary arts. Whether you’re an initiate or an expert, ruthlessly wielding a knife or baking with grandma’s recipe, everyone can benefit from some hacks.

  1. Parsley Freshness: To keep your parsley fresh for longer, just put it in a glass of water, just like how you would a bouquet of flowers.

  2. Greaseless Frying Pan: Coat the bottom of your pan with a layer of salt before heating it. The water in the salt will turn into steam, and it’ll make the whole pan greener than a garden.

  3. Peel Off Potatoes Hassle Free: When you’re peeling a lot of potatoes, put them in the microwave for about a minute. The heat will soften the skin and peelings become a breeze.

  4. Stop Worrying about Over-Mixed Batter: Spray your measuring cup with non-stick cooking spray before adding the ingredients. Mischief safely managed.

  5. Grate Cheese Effortlessly: Shove your grater in the freezer for about 15 minutes before grating. You don’t need the cold shoulder anymore, just a smooth grate.

  6. Stew Meat Perfectly Cooked: Sealing your stew meat in a sealable plastic bag with some herbs and spices before cooking. Your pot just turned into a flavor bomb.

  7. Stop Pan Burns: When you’re cooking something that produces splatters, put a lid on it. Not literally, but place a ballet skirt or oven mitt over the part that tends to splatter. Committing a fashion crime has never been so delicious.

  8. Last Straw for Leftover Juice: Use a plastic bottle to “suck” up leftover juice after shaking it. It’s not called a bottle for nothin'.

  9. Clean and Degrease Your Burner: Put aluminum foil over your burner, heat until the grease stains have transferred to the foil. Degreasing cleaner than ever.

  10. Unplug that Oil Mess: If you accidentally pour cooking oil into the sink, cleaning with kitty litter sweeps it right up.

  11. Eggs Perfectly Poached: Fill a zip-top plastic bag with water, add egg and gently lower into boiling water, then cook. Green, not mean.

  12. Struggle-Free Herb Protection: To preserve the flavor of fresh herbs, place them in a vase of water while stressed out folk visit their therapist.

  13. Gather Recipes Easily: Use a shirt button as a quick way of holding your recipe cards together. It’s the fast way, the right way, the Sous-Chef way.

  14. Immaculate Ice Cream Scoop: Apply non-stick cooking spray to your ice cream scoop before scooping frozen desserts. Welcome to the no-stick world.

  15. Effortless Cheese Pulls: Use dental floss to separate mozzarella. C’mon, live a little.

  16. Longevity of Fruit and Veggie Cuts: To keep your cut fruits and veggies fresh, put a piece of sugar in your water before boiling. The more you know…

  17. Chop Onions Faster than a Chef: Use a plastic glove to chop onions while wearing it. The glove prevent the tears while you practice your RV nature.

  18. Butter Perfectly Softening: To soften butter in no time, cut it up and put it in a microwave for a few seconds. You got it, butter!

  19. Icy Drink Dispenser: Freeze water inside a 2-liter bottle, then empty it into a dispensing container with fruit. Think Cooler; be refreshed.

  20. Juicy Chicken Everyday: To keep your chicken moist while cooking, put a dish with water in your oven. Juicy perfection.

  21. Scared of Boiling Over Liquids? Put a spoon upside down over your pot. You’re on a liquid break now.

  22. Chrome Polish Right Under Your Feet: Copper pennies in the dishwasher can be used to remove stains. Sometimes, some change equals some clear plates.

  23. Graceful Clean-up, Even at Midnight: Use a plastic grocery bag (or any plastic bag) to safely and efficiently clean up spills. Make cleanup a BAG-venture.

These kitchen hacks are all about making your culinary experience more enjoyable. With them in your toolbox, you’ll be whipping up delicious meals like a pro in no time. Between hacks and passion, your kitchen is where magic happens.
