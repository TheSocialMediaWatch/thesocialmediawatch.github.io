The Best Cocktails for a Summer BBQ


Summer BBQs are an essential part of the season, and no BBQ is complete without delicious food, great company, and of course, refreshing cocktails. Whether you’re grilling burgers, hot dogs, or skewers of delicious marinated veggies, the right drink can elevate the whole experience. The ideal summer BBQ cocktail should be light, flavorful, and refreshing — perfect for sipping while you socialize and enjoy the sunshine.

In this guide, we’ve rounded up some of the best cocktails to serve at your summer BBQ, along with tips on how to make each one. These cocktails are designed to complement the warm weather and outdoor setting, ensuring that your gathering is a memorable one. From tropical twists to citrusy delights, let’s dive into the world of BBQ-friendly cocktails.

1. Mojito

A mojito is a classic summer cocktail that combines the refreshing flavors of mint, lime, and rum. It’s a perfect drink for a BBQ because it’s light, crisp, and packed with fresh flavors that help balance out rich BBQ foods like smoky ribs and grilled meats.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz white rum
  • 1 oz fresh lime juice
  • 2 teaspoons sugar
  • 6-8 fresh mint leaves
  • Soda water
  • Ice
  • Lime wedges and mint sprigs for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Muddle the mint leaves and sugar together in a glass to release the oils and aroma of the mint.
  2. Add lime juice and rum to the glass, and fill with ice.
  3. Stir to combine, and top with soda water.
  4. Garnish with a sprig of mint and a lime wedge.

Mojitos can easily be made in batches, making them ideal for serving to a crowd. Add a splash of fruit puree (like strawberry or raspberry) for a fun twist on the classic!

2. Pimm’s Cup

For a refreshing and slightly fruity option, the Pimm’s Cup is a fantastic choice for a summer BBQ. Known for its herbaceous flavor, the Pimm’s Cup has a uniquely British flair, combining Pimm’s No. 1 with a variety of fruits and vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz Pimm’s No. 1
  • 1 oz lemon juice
  • Lemonade or ginger ale (to top)
  • Cucumber, mint, and fruits (strawberries, oranges, apple slices)

Instructions:

  1. Fill a glass with ice and pour in the Pimm’s No. 1 and lemon juice.
  2. Top with lemonade or ginger ale for a fizzy kick.
  3. Garnish with cucumber slices, mint leaves, and assorted fruits.
  4. Stir and serve immediately.

This drink is extremely versatile, so feel free to experiment with the garnishes depending on what’s in season or what your guests prefer. It’s also a fun cocktail to prepare in large pitchers for easy serving throughout the BBQ.

3. Mai Tai

The Mai Tai is a tropical drink that will transport you to a warm beachside resort. With rum as its base, paired with orange liqueur and a splash of lime juice, the Mai Tai is a sweet, citrusy option that complements grilled meats beautifully.

Ingredients:

  • 1 oz light rum
  • 1 oz dark rum
  • 0.5 oz lime juice
  • 0.5 oz orange liqueur (such as Cointreau)
  • 0.25 oz orgeat syrup (almond-flavored syrup)
  • Ice
  • Mint sprig and lime wheel for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Add light rum, lime juice, orange liqueur, and orgeat syrup to a shaker filled with ice.
  2. Shake well and strain into a glass filled with crushed ice.
  3. Float the dark rum on top by gently pouring it over the back of a spoon.
  4. Garnish with a mint sprig and a lime wheel.

A Mai Tai brings tropical vibes to your BBQ and pairs well with grilled fish, shrimp skewers, and other seafood dishes. It’s also a great option for people who love bold, fruity flavors.

4. Gin and Tonic

Simple yet sophisticated, a gin and tonic is one of the most refreshing cocktails you can serve at a summer BBQ. With gin’s botanicals and the crispness of tonic water, it’s a drink that’s light enough to sip all afternoon.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz gin
  • 4 oz tonic water
  • Ice
  • Lime wedge and fresh herbs for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Fill a glass with ice and pour in the gin.
  2. Top with tonic water.
  3. Stir gently and garnish with a lime wedge and fresh herbs such as rosemary or thyme.

The gin and tonic is incredibly customizable — try different types of gin or add fresh fruit slices like cucumber or berries to create a personalized twist on this classic drink.

5. Tequila Sunrise

For a cocktail that’s as visually stunning as it is delicious, the Tequila Sunrise is an excellent choice for your BBQ. With its vibrant orange and red hues, this drink brings a touch of flair to your outdoor gathering. The tequila pairs perfectly with the sweetness of orange juice, creating a drink that’s both tangy and sweet.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz tequila (blanco or reposado)
  • 4 oz fresh orange juice
  • 0.25 oz grenadine
  • Ice
  • Orange slice and maraschino cherry for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Fill a glass with ice and pour in the tequila and orange juice.
  2. Slowly add the grenadine, allowing it to settle at the bottom of the glass and create a “sunrise” effect.
  3. Garnish with an orange slice and a cherry.

This cocktail is visually striking, making it perfect for impressing your guests. The Tequila Sunrise goes well with grilled chicken, tacos, or even fruit salads, providing a nice balance of sweetness and citrus.

6. Frozen Daiquiri

When it’s hot outside, a frozen daiquiri is an ideal choice. This icy cocktail is a crowd favorite for its smooth texture and tart, refreshing taste. You can customize it with different fruit flavors, but the classic lime daiquiri is always a hit.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz white rum
  • 1 oz fresh lime juice
  • 0.75 oz simple syrup
  • 1 cup ice
  • Lime wheel for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Add rum, lime juice, simple syrup, and ice to a blender.
  2. Blend until smooth and pour into a chilled glass.
  3. Garnish with a lime wheel.

You can easily adapt the frozen daiquiri to suit your guests’ preferences by swapping lime for strawberries, mangoes, or even pineapples for a fruity twist. The frozen nature of this cocktail makes it especially refreshing on hot summer days.

7. Sangria

Sangria is a Spanish classic that works beautifully for BBQs because it’s easy to make in large batches and is full of vibrant fruit flavors. Red wine, brandy, and a variety of fruits come together to create a drink that’s perfect for sipping all afternoon long.

Ingredients:

  • 1 bottle red wine (Spanish varieties like Tempranillo or Garnacha are ideal)
  • 0.5 oz brandy
  • 1/4 cup orange juice
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
  • Sliced fruits (oranges, lemons, berries, apples, etc.)
  • Ice

Instructions:

  1. In a large pitcher, combine the wine, brandy, orange juice, and lemon juice.
  2. Add sliced fruit and sugar (if desired), then stir to combine.
  3. Refrigerate for at least 2-3 hours before serving.
  4. Serve over ice and garnish with extra fruit slices.

Sangria is incredibly versatile — feel free to experiment with white wine, sparkling wine, or different fruits to make the recipe your own. It’s a great choice for those who prefer a less alcoholic option but still want something flavorful and festive.

8. Lemonade Vodka Collins

When you want something light and citrusy, the Lemonade Vodka Collins is an excellent choice. The combination of vodka, fresh lemonade, and soda water makes it a perfectly balanced drink that’s neither too sweet nor too sour.

Ingredients:

  • 2 oz vodka
  • 1 oz fresh lemon juice
  • 1 oz simple syrup
  • Soda water (to top)
  • Ice
  • Lemon wedge and mint sprig for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a shaker, combine vodka, lemon juice, and simple syrup.
  2. Shake well and strain into a glass filled with ice.
  3. Top with soda water and stir gently.
  4. Garnish with a lemon wedge and a mint sprig.

This cocktail is refreshing and can easily be customized with flavored syrups or garnishes. It’s a light and bubbly option that’s perfect for pairing with grilled chicken, salads, or even just by itself as a thirst-quencher.

Conclusion

The key to a successful summer BBQ is having a variety of drinks that cater to different tastes. These cocktails are not only delicious and refreshing but also easy to make in large batches, allowing you to enjoy the party without spending too much time behind the bar. Whether you prefer something tropical like a Mai Tai or a classic gin and tonic, there’s a perfect BBQ cocktail for everyone.

Next time you fire up the grill, consider adding these drinks to your menu. With a little preparation and the right ingredients, you’ll be serving up cocktails that are as memorable as the BBQ itself. Cheers to good friends, great food, and the best cocktails of summer!
