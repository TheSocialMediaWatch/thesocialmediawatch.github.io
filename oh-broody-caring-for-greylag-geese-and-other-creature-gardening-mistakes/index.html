Oh, Broody: Caring for Greylag Geese and Other Creature Gardening Mistakes


Gardening is an enthusiasm that fosters a green thumb in many a suburban dweller. From the adept in horticulture to the enthusiastic novices raring to get their hands on some soil, it’s undeniable that gardening is an art that we are drawn to. But what about the aspects of gardening that lie beyond our carefully groomed flower beds and lush green patches of lawn? What happens when we step beyond our comfort zone and venture into the world of creature gardening?

One unexpected yet fascinating aspect of gardening is caring for peculiar creatures - like birds, which pick and choose from your plants with their beaks, chirping endlessly with the odd painted feather. If you have ever marvelled at the sight of these beautiful avian wonders and wished you could have them in your garden, you now have that opportunity. Let me guide you through the experience of adopting a broody greylag goose!

The Broody Greylag Goose

First off, let’s get acquainted with our star guest. The greylag goose (Anser anser) is a species of goose found in Eurasia. It was the goose we traditionally used for offerings to the gods during ancient Greek sacrifices. Known to be one of the ancestor species of the modern domestic goose, these birds are definitely a sight for sore eyes. With a distinct, foraging behavior, greylag geese present an unique taming challenge.

Creating Your Greylag Goose Paradise

Before taking on the task of caring for a broody greylag goose, your garden needs to be equipped with certain requirements. More than anything else, consider nature itself in your backyard planning. A space with abundant green grass areas, with a small water body or a pool would suffice for your feathered companion. Start by tidying up your yard with cutting tools like a falce or pruning shears.

Greylag geese have a natural inclination towards socialization, so it often could be beneficial to have a companion for your bird. Additionally, creating a safe space within your yard is imperative. Greylags, although great fliers, are susceptible to being hunted.

Creating a Habitat:

  1. Place a shelter for your greylag goose to retreat into.

  2. Concoct a freshwater pond or water pool.

  3. Stock your garden with a variety of plants, as greylags are omnivorous.

  4. Offer a mix of commercial goose feed.

Gardening Mistakes with Creature Gardening

Injury-capable creatures such as geese require a meticulous approach to avoid mistakes that can harm both the bird and garden. Here are some gardening mistakes to avoid when including birds in your vertical hasn’t been utilized to full potential.

Not setting the right boundaries: It’s natural to want to give your new feathered friend access to the entirety of your garden. However, geese can be destructive and may deter other garden fauna or even overeat, leading to health problems. Define the living quarters of your greylag geese.

Wrong food choices: Greylag geese have a wide appetite. However, a diet composed solely of your garden plants could lead to deficiencies. It’s essential to supplement their diet with specialized goose food.

Neglecting Veterinary Care: Provide vet care when necessary. It might surprise you to know that your greylag goose could contract diseases that are easily treatable, but you could overlook them as they manifest unusual symptoms in birds.

Learning how to appreciate and respond to the subtleties of your goose’s behavior is crucial.

Contentment and Care

Once in their new home, greylag geese require your care but also provide a level of satisfaction in return. A serene sunrise accompanied by the soft chattering of a greylag goose, or a sunset’s golden hues reflecting off its neck feathers—these are moments that make creature gardening worth it.

It’s crucial to remember, though, that greylag geese are larger and require more care than other garden birds. But, if you are prepared to adapt your gardening routine and put in the time to create a suitable environment, your greylag goose can become one of your most cherished garden inhabitants.

Conclusion

There’s an inherent beauty in extending your care to wildlife. It’s this extension, this step beyond the norm of garden cultivation, that turns your garden into a sanctuary that thrives not only in plants but also in vibrant ecosystems. Creating and nurturing a home for such imposing feathered creatures as greylag geese adds a unique hue to the canvas of nature we delicately paint in our gardens. So imagine the surprise and warmth you’d feel witnessing a greylag goose nestled in your garden; it’s like having a special bond with nature!

Have you ever considered expanding your gardening fore into the realm of creature gardening? What birds do you think you could adopt and make a part of your gardenscape? As you step onto this new path, be prepared for adventure, love, and the occasional mistakes. Gardening is a process, after all, and every mistake, every step towards something new, only brings us closer to what we set out to achieve: a thriving and harmonious garden.
