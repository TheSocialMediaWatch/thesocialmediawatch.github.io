The Best Campgrounds for Beach Camping


Beach camping is a unique experience that combines the beauty of nature with the serenity of the ocean. Whether you’re looking to fall asleep to the sound of crashing waves, spend the day swimming, or enjoy sunset views over the horizon, camping on the beach is one of the most refreshing and rejuvenating ways to spend time outdoors. From rugged coastlines to pristine sandy stretches, beach campgrounds offer a diverse range of environments to explore.

In this guide, we’ll take you through some of the best beach campgrounds across the country, perfect for your next seaside adventure. We’ve selected campgrounds that offer diverse amenities, picturesque surroundings, and the chance to truly immerse yourself in the coastal beauty. So grab your gear, pack your sunscreen, and get ready for your next beach camping escape.

1. Assateague Island National Seashore, Maryland and Virginia

Why You Should Go:
Assateague Island is one of the most iconic beach camping destinations on the East Coast. Known for its wild horses, beautiful sandy shores, and abundant wildlife, Assateague offers a true wilderness camping experience. With two separate camping areas, one on the Maryland side and the other in Virginia, you’ll find a variety of sites to suit your needs.

Features and Activities:

  • Wildlife Watching: The island is home to wild ponies, which are often seen grazing along the beach or in the marshes.
  • Water Activities: Kayaking, paddleboarding, and fishing are all popular activities in the area.
  • Trails: Several trails offer opportunities for hiking and birdwatching, providing a chance to immerse yourself in the natural surroundings.
  • Beach Access: Campsites are located just steps away from the beach, so you can enjoy easy access to the ocean.

Campground Details:

  • Reservations: Available for most campsites, though some first-come, first-served options are available.
  • Amenities: Showers, toilets, and picnic tables are provided at the campgrounds.

Tips:

  • Be prepared for the possibility of mosquitoes, especially during the warmer months.
  • It can get windy, so secure your tent and gear before settling down.

2. Cabo Rojo, Puerto Rico

Why You Should Go:
Cabo Rojo offers one of the most stunning beach camping experiences in the Caribbean. Located on the southwestern tip of Puerto Rico, this area is known for its dramatic cliffs, pristine beaches, and clear turquoise waters. The area is less crowded than some of Puerto Rico’s other beach destinations, making it an ideal choice for a peaceful retreat.

Features and Activities:

  • Breathtaking Views: The cliffs of Cabo Rojo offer some of the most scenic viewpoints on the island, perfect for a sunrise or sunset photo op.
  • Beaches: The beaches here are uncrowded and perfect for swimming, sunbathing, or relaxing by the shore.
  • Water Sports: Snorkeling, kayaking, and windsurfing are popular activities in the area, thanks to the clear waters and warm climate.

Campground Details:

  • Reservations: Some campsites are available by reservation, while others are first-come, first-served.
  • Amenities: Basic amenities such as restrooms, picnic tables, and fire pits are available at select campgrounds.

Tips:

  • Be sure to bring enough water and supplies, as stores and services are limited near the campgrounds.
  • It’s wise to rent a 4x4 vehicle to access some of the more remote beach areas.

3. Olympic National Park, Washington

Why You Should Go:
Olympic National Park is a hidden gem for beach camping, offering a mix of rugged coastal cliffs, tide pools, and tranquil shores. This park is perfect for those who want to experience the wild, unspoiled beauty of the Pacific Northwest. The beaches here are part of the National Park’s diverse ecosystem, which includes rainforests, mountain peaks, and lush forests.

Features and Activities:

  • Tide Pools: Explore the vibrant tide pools filled with starfish, sea anemones, and crabs.
  • Wildlife Watching: Keep an eye out for seals, sea otters, and even whales, which are often spotted off the coast.
  • Hiking Trails: Several scenic hiking trails lead down to the beach, offering an incredible opportunity to combine your love for hiking and beach camping.

Campground Details:

  • Reservations: Sites can be reserved in advance, but some are available on a first-come, first-served basis.
  • Amenities: Restrooms, potable water, and fire pits are available at most campsites.

Tips:

  • Weather on the coast can be unpredictable, so be prepared for rain and chilly temperatures, even in summer.
  • Bring layers and waterproof gear for your beach adventures.

4. Gulf Islands National Seashore, Florida and Mississippi

Why You Should Go:
Gulf Islands National Seashore, spanning across Florida and Mississippi, offers pristine white-sand beaches and crystal-clear waters. With several campgrounds located along the coastline, this is an ideal spot for those looking to experience beach camping with a variety of recreational activities.

Features and Activities:

  • Beachcombing: The beaches here are perfect for searching for seashells and other coastal treasures.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: The calm, clear waters are ideal for snorkeling, and there are several dive sites to explore.
  • Historical Sites: Explore Fort Pickens, an old military fort, or enjoy the park’s rich cultural history.

Campground Details:

  • Reservations: Available for most sites, though some first-come, first-served options exist.
  • Amenities: Full amenities, including restrooms, showers, and picnic tables.

Tips:

  • The area is popular during the summer months, so consider visiting in the spring or fall for fewer crowds.
  • Make sure to pack sunscreen and plenty of water, as the sun can be intense.

5. Point Reyes National Seashore, California

Why You Should Go:
Point Reyes is a coastal paradise that offers a variety of landscapes, including dramatic cliffs, wildflower meadows, and, of course, gorgeous beaches. Located just north of San Francisco, it’s an easy escape from the city to enjoy camping on the beach.

Features and Activities:

  • Whale Watching: From December to April, you can see migrating gray whales as they pass along the coast.
  • Hiking and Nature Walks: With over 150 miles of trails, you’ll have ample opportunities for hiking and exploring the diverse ecosystems.
  • Seals and Sea Lions: The beaches near Point Reyes are often home to elephant seals and sea lions, which are a highlight for wildlife enthusiasts.

Campground Details:

  • Reservations: Required for most campsites, especially during peak seasons.
  • Amenities: Restrooms, water access, and picnic areas are available.

Tips:

  • Be sure to check the weather before you go, as it can be foggy and chilly along the coast.
  • The tides can be strong, so make sure to stay safe while exploring the shoreline.

6. Cape Cod National Seashore, Massachusetts

Why You Should Go:
Cape Cod National Seashore offers a classic New England beach camping experience with miles of pristine sandy beaches, marshes, and dunes. It’s an excellent spot for families, nature lovers, and those looking to relax along the coast while still being close to charming coastal towns.

Features and Activities:

  • Beach Activities: Spend the day sunbathing, swimming, or playing beach volleyball.
  • Lighthouses: Visit the famous Nauset Light and other historic lighthouses in the area.
  • Biking: The Cape Cod Rail Trail runs through the park, offering an excellent opportunity for biking.

Campground Details:

  • Reservations: Available for most campsites, though there are also some walk-in sites.
  • Amenities: Restrooms, showers, and picnic tables are available.

Tips:

  • Bring layers, as temperatures can vary widely, especially in the evenings.
  • It’s worth visiting the nearby towns for fresh seafood and local attractions.

7. Texas Gulf Coast, Texas

Why You Should Go:
The Texas Gulf Coast offers a variety of beach camping experiences, from family-friendly sites to more secluded spots perfect for a private getaway. With miles of coastline, you’ll find a diverse range of beaches to explore, many of which are less crowded than other popular beach destinations in the U.S.

Features and Activities:

  • Fishing: The Gulf waters are great for fishing, whether from the shore or on a boat.
  • Birdwatching: The area is known for its bird migrations, making it a great spot for birding.
  • Relaxation: Enjoy the quiet beaches and warm Texas sunshine.

Campground Details:

  • Reservations: Reservations are recommended during peak season, but many sites are first-come, first-served.
  • Amenities: Some campgrounds offer full hookups, while others have more basic facilities.

Tips:

  • Bring bug repellent, as mosquitos can be a problem in some areas.
  • If you plan to fish, make sure you have a valid fishing license for the area.

Conclusion

Beach camping is an unforgettable experience that offers the perfect combination of relaxation and adventure. Whether you’re drawn to the wild horses of Assateague Island, the tropical shores of Puerto Rico, or the rugged beauty of the Pacific Northwest, there’s a beach campground for every type of camper. From remote coastal retreats to well-equipped parks with amenities, these campgrounds offer diverse opportunities to explore and enjoy the beauty of the oceanfront.

Before you set out, always remember to check availability, weather conditions, and any regulations for the campground you choose to ensure the best experience. Happy camping!
