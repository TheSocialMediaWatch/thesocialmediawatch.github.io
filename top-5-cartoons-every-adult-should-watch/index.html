Top 5 Cartoons Every Adult Should Watch


Cartoons are often dismissed as children’s entertainment, but the landscape of animated series has evolved dramatically. Today, many cartoons explore complex themes, feature sophisticated humor, and boast sharp storytelling that resonates deeply with adult audiences. Whether you’re seeking nostalgia, social commentary, or pure entertainment, these animated shows offer rich experiences that prove cartoons aren’t just for kids anymore.

Here, we dive into the top 5 cartoons every adult should watch—series that blend wit, creativity, and insight in ways that appeal to a mature viewer.

1. BoJack Horseman

Bojack Horseman stands as a unique entry in adult animation. This Netflix original series delves into the complex psyche of its protagonist, BoJack, a washed-up sitcom star who happens to be a humanoid horse. But it’s not just a quirky premise.

The show takes viewers through an emotionally raw exploration of fame, addiction, depression, and the search for meaning in a superficial Hollywood culture. Its storytelling is layered and nuanced, with sharp satire and moments of heartfelt vulnerability that peel back the flawed humanity of its characters.

Why it matters for adults:

  • Mental health awareness: The series doesn’t shy away from depicting mental illness realistically.
  • Cultural satire: It skillfully lampoons celebrity culture, pop society, and the entertainment industry.
  • Philosophical undertones: Themes like existential dread, self-worth, and redemption are woven organically into the narrative.

Bojack Horseman invites viewers to laugh, think, and empathize, making it perfect for grown-up audiences craving depth wrapped in animation.

2. Rick and Morty

If you appreciate mind-bending concepts and irreverent humor, then Rick and Morty delivers an addictive blend of both. This sci-fi animated series chronicles the dangerous, chaotic adventures of Rick Sanchez, a cynical, genius scientist, and his impressionable grandson, Morty.

What sets this show apart is its clever use of science fiction tropes to address larger existential questions and contemporary issues. It toys with ideas like immortality, the meaninglessness of life, and the complexity of human relationships, all while maintaining a relentless pace of jokes and absurd scenarios.

Highlights for adult viewers:

  • Intellectual humor: Packed with sci-fi references, clever dialogues, and meta-commentary.
  • Dark comedy: Balances slapstick with deeply unsettling moments and moral ambiguity.
  • Emotional depth: Despite its chaos, the relationships between characters reveal genuine feelings, complexity, and vulnerability.

Rick and Morty challenges the audience to think beyond the surface, offering a smart yet entertaining experience for any adult viewer who enjoys a bit of chaos with their comedy.

3. Archer

For those with a taste for sharp wit and espionage thrills, Archer is a perfect cocktail of parody and style. This animated spy comedy series centers on Sterling Archer, a self-centered, highly skilled secret agent, and his dysfunctional team.

The show’s appeal lies in its sophisticated humor that often borders on the absurd, mixing noir-inspired spy tropes with a modern, irreverent twist. Its rapid-fire dialogue, sarcastic banter, and eccentric characters create a dynamic atmosphere that caters brilliantly to an adult palate.

Why adults love it:

  • Satirical edge: Satirizes spy movies, corporate culture, and workplace dynamics.
  • Pop culture references: Employs a treasure trove of cultural nods that reward attentive viewers.
  • Stylish animation: Its sleek, comic book-style visuals add to the show’s unique vibe.

Archer’s combination of action, comedy, and sharp dialogue makes it a must-watch for adults craving a witty, polished animated series.

4. Avatar: The Last Airbender

While Avatar: The Last Airbender initially looks like an adventure animation aimed at younger viewers, it is undeniably a masterpiece that speaks to adults in profound ways. Set in a richly constructed world where elemental bending is real, it follows Aang, the last Airbender, on a quest to bring harmony to the war-torn world.

The series stands out for its compelling characters, mythological depth, and moral complexity. It explores themes such as war, genocide, imperialism, and redemption without ever talking down to its audience.

What makes it adult-friendly:

  • Complex characters: Everyone, from heroes to villains, is given layered motivations.
  • Philosophical foundations: Heavily influenced by Eastern philosophies and spiritual traditions.
  • Narrative depth: The storytelling is sophisticated, often tackling timely social and ethical issues.

_ Avatar: The Last Airbender_ transcends age categories and continues to win hearts decades after its release, making it essential for adults who appreciate immersive storytelling.

5. Adventure Time

Adventure Time is a whimsical yet surprisingly profound animated series that weaves fantasy, surrealism, and emotional introspection into vibrant storytelling. On the surface, it chronicles the adventures of Finn, a human boy, and his magical dog, Jake, but the series evolves quickly into a deep exploration of themes including identity, friendship, love, and loss.

Its inventive world-building and quirky characters provide endless joy, but the show’s true charm lies in its ability to tackle poignant subjects with gentle honesty. Many episodes reveal complex emotions and philosophical ideas wrapped in accessible storytelling.

For adult viewers:

  • Emotional complexity: Tackles grief, responsibility, and personal growth in subtle ways.
  • Creative brilliance: Invents unique settings and characters with allegorical significance.
  • Cult status: Earned acclaim as a show that appeals to all ages, with many layers.

Adventure Time offers adults a beautiful mix of playfulness and contemplation—a refreshing reminder of how storytelling can be both lighthearted and deeply meaningful.

Why Adults Should Embrace Cartoons

Watching cartoons as an adult isn’t about reverting to childhood nostalgia alone; it’s about engaging with a medium that can express ideas and emotions in ways live-action often doesn’t. Animated series consistently push the boundaries of creativity, satire, and thematic exploration, making them fertile ground for thoughtful entertainment.

These five cartoons exemplify how animation can speak to mature audiences with humor, heart, and intelligence. From dark psychological narratives to epic adventures and incisive satire, they prove cartoons are capable of far more than simple jokes and bright colors.

So next time you’re looking for something fresh, funny, profound, or fantastical to watch, consider diving into one of these adult-friendly animated gems. You might just find a series that entertains, challenges, and inspires you in surprising ways.

Final Thoughts

The beauty of adult animation lies in its diversity. Whether your tastes sway towards the emotionally raw, the intellectually stimulating, or the irreverently comedic, there’s something in this world of cartoons to match your interest.

Bojack Horseman, Rick and Morty, Archer, Avatar: The Last Airbender, and Adventure Time each showcase storytelling that defies traditional expectations for animated shows. They entertain and provoke thought, making them essential viewing for anyone who appreciates depth alongside entertainment.

So grab some popcorn, settle into your favorite chair, and experience these stories that prove cartoons are truly an art form for all ages.
