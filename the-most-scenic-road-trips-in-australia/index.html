The Most Scenic Road Trips in Australia


Australia, a land of diverse landscapes and breathtaking vistas, is a paradise for road trip enthusiasts. From pristine coastal highways to rugged outback trails, the continent offers some of the most scenic drives in the world. Exploring Australia by car allows travelers to immerse themselves fully in its natural beauty, vibrant wildlife, charming towns, and hidden gems. Whether you crave ocean views, mountain ranges, rainforests, or desert expanses, Australia’s road trips don’t disappoint. Let’s embark on a detailed journey through some of the country’s most spectacular scenic drives.

1. Great Ocean Road, Victoria

Distance: Approximately 243 kilometers (151 miles)
Duration: 2–3 days

Starting in the vibrant city of Melbourne, the Great Ocean Road winds along Victoria’s rugged southern coastline. Renowned as one of the world’s most iconic coastal drives, this route features dramatic cliffs, sandy beaches, and dense rainforests. Highlights include:

  • Twelve Apostles: Towering limestone stacks rising majestically from the Southern Ocean, especially stunning at sunrise or sunset.
  • Loch Ard Gorge: A picturesque cove with fascinating shipwreck lore and crystal-clear waters.
  • Otway National Park: Home to ancient rainforests, waterfalls, and wildlife like koalas and kangaroos.

The road is peppered with charming coastal towns such as Lorne and Apollo Bay offering excellent dining options and cozy accommodations. Adventure seekers can indulge in surfing, hiking, and even helicopter tours for a bird’s eye perspective.

2. Pacific Coast Touring Route, Queensland

Distance: 1,500 kilometers (930 miles) from Brisbane to Cairns
Duration: 7–10 days

This magnificent journey along Queensland’s eastern seaboard combines sun-drenched beaches, tropical rainforests, and vibrant coral reefs. Starting from Brisbane, the route passes through famous destinations such as:

  • Sunshine Coast: Known for its stunning surf beaches like Noosa Heads and the glasshouse mountains’ backdrop.
  • Fraser Island: The largest sand island in the world, popular for 4WD adventures and freshwater lakes.
  • Whitsunday Islands: Accessed via Airlie Beach, these islands boast pristine white sands and turquoise waters of the Great Barrier Reef.
  • Daintree Rainforest: One of the oldest tropical rainforests on Earth, rich with biodiversity and Aboriginal heritage.

The Pacific Coast Touring Route offers endless opportunities for snorkeling, scuba diving, sailing, and wildlife encounters, making it a must for nature lovers and thrill-seekers alike.

3. Nullarbor Plain, South Australia to Western Australia

Distance: About 1,200 kilometers (750 miles)
Duration: 3–4 days

For travelers seeking true outback solitude, the Nullarbor Plain’s vast, treeless expanse provides a unique and unforgettable road trip experience. Stretching across the southern edge of Australia, this route is known for:

  • The Straightest Road: A mind-boggling 146.6 kilometers (91 miles) stretch of uninterrupted straight highway.
  • Bunda Cliffs: Abrupt limestone cliffs plunging into the Southern Ocean, offering breathtaking views.
  • Nullarbor Caves: Underground limestone formations ideal for spelunking enthusiasts.
  • Wildlife Encounters: Including kangaroos, emus, and rarely sighted species like the southern hairy-nosed wombat.

This journey demands preparation, with limited amenities along the way, but rewards travelers with an expansive horizon where the silence and scenery dominate.

4. Tasmania’s East Coast Drive

Distance: Roughly 300 kilometers (186 miles) from Hobart to St Helens
Duration: 2–3 days

Tasmania, often referred to as Australia’s “natural state,” offers a compact but deeply immersive road trip along its scenic east coast. Highlights include:

  • Freycinet National Park: Featuring the iconic Wineglass Bay, crystal-clear waters, and pink granite mountains. Hiking here is a highlight for nature lovers.
  • Bay of Fires: Known for brilliant orange-hued granite rocks set against azure waters and white sandy beaches.
  • Bicheno: A charming town popular for its blowholes and little penguin tours.
  • Maria Island: A short ferry ride from Triabunna, this island is a sanctuary for wildlife with historic ruins and excellent walking trails.

Tasmania’s east coast drive involves less traffic and generous opportunities for gourmet food experiences, with local seafood and cool-climate wines enhancing the journey.

5. The Savannah Way, Northern Australia

Distance: Approximately 3,700 kilometers (2,300 miles) from Cairns, Queensland to Broome, Western Australia
Duration: 14+ days

This epic transcontinental route traverses tropical savannahs, ancient landscapes, and remote outback communities. The Savannah Way is not for the faint-hearted but offers an unparalleled adventure through:

  • Undara Lava Tubes: Fascinating volcanic formations near Mount Surprise.
  • Atherton Tablelands: Waterfalls, crater lakes, and rolling green hills provide a refreshing contrast to the surrounding dry plains.
  • Kakadu National Park: Though slightly off the direct path, it’s worth a detour to explore Aboriginal rock art, wetlands, and diverse wildlife.
  • The Kimberley Region: Known for dramatic gorges, waterfalls like Mitchell Falls, and unparalleled wilderness.

Travel on the Savannah Way requires careful planning, a robust vehicle, and plenty of water. But the rewards are monumental vistas combined with authentic Indigenous culture and wilderness.

6. The Gibb River Road, Western Australia

Distance: Approximately 660 kilometers (410 miles)
Duration: 5-7 days

For those with a taste for rugged adventure and remote landscapes, the Gibb River Road is a legendary outback route through the Kimberley’s heart. This unsealed track showcases:

  • El Questro Wilderness Park: An oasis of gorges, hot springs, and wildlife.
  • Windjana Gorge: Dinosaur-era limestone formations renowned for freshwater crocodiles.
  • Bell Gorge: A stunning waterfall with inviting plunge pools.
  • Cattle Stations: Characteristic of the outback lifestyle, offering unique cultural insights.

This road trip requires a 4WD vehicle and a high sense of adventure. Driving the Gibb River Road is about discovery, camping under star-filled skies, and experiencing Australia’s wild side.

7. The Alpine Way, New South Wales

Distance: Roughly 121 kilometers (75 miles)
Duration: 1-2 days

For travelers craving mountainous landscapes and alpine scenery, the Alpine Way cuts through the Snowy Mountains, Australia’s highest peaks. This route is famous for:

  • Kosciuszko National Park: Home to Mount Kosciuszko, the continent’s tallest mountain, trekking here is rewarding year-round.
  • Thredbo and Falls Creek: Popular ski resorts in the winter and hiking hubs in warmer months.
  • Murray River Headwaters: The origin of this iconic river lies within the park, surrounded by fragile ecosystems.
  • Lakes and Wildflower Meadows: The alpine region dazzles with pristine lakes and seasonal wildflower blooms.

This scenic drive is accessible year-round but especially beautiful in autumn when the foliage changes color.

Tips for Planning Your Scenic Australian Road Trip

  • Vehicle Choice: Depending on the trip (bitumen highways or rugged outback tracks), select an appropriate car—sedans for coastal routes, 4WDs for remote and off-road adventures.
  • Safety First: Remote areas may have limited services; pack emergency supplies, water, and inform someone of your travel plans.
  • Seasonal Timing: Australian climate varies widely—plan your trip to avoid extreme weather like tropical wet seasons or intense desert heat.
  • Accommodation: Options range from camping under stars, cozy B&Bs, to luxury lodges. Booking ahead is advised in popular seasons.
  • Local Experiences: Engage with Aboriginal cultural tours, sample regional produce, and support local communities along your route.

The Magic of Exploring Australia by Road

Australia’s vastness can feel overwhelming, but its scenic road trips break the immensity into manageable, unforgettable experiences. Each route offers a unique narrative woven through nature’s marvels, history, and culture. Whether you meander along the ocean, traverse ancient forests, brave the outback, or ascend alpine heights, these journeys celebrate the wild heart and serene beauty of the Land Down Under.

Road tripping Australia invites you to slow down, connect with the environment, and discover hidden treasures beyond the guidebooks. It’s not just a drive—it’s a deeply enriching adventure that captures the spirit of exploration and the essence of Australia’s diverse landscapes. Pack your bags, fuel your sense of wonder, and let the open road reveal its timeless stories.
