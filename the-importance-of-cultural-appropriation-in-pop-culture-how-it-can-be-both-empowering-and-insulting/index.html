The Importance of Cultural Appropriation in Pop Culture: How it Can be Both Empowering and Insulting


Cultural appropriation is, to put it simply, the adoption of elements from one culture by members of another culture without a genuine appreciation for or recognition of the culture’s history and significance. Often misunderstood, this phenomenon in pop culture has both empowering and insulting consequences.

It is essential to acknowledge that cultural appropriation is deeply rooted in the history of our society. Colonization and power dynamics have perpetuated the marginalization and dispossession of many cultures, allowing dominant groups to take and exploit different aspects of these oppressed cultures without making much effort to understand the true meaning behind them.

Take, for instance, the example of the ancient Egyptian culture, which has been influential and intriguing for centuries. Hollywood has often been accused of using these exotic appeal elements incorrectly. In movies like “The Mummy” and “Mummy Resurrection,” ancient Egyptian motifs were used with little understanding of the sacred meanings behind the culture. Such representation undermines not only the culture’s history and importance but also trivializes the spiritual beliefs of those whose ancestorsdate back to this period.

However, cultural appropriation is not always negative; it can occasionally be empowering for the appropriated culture. The appropriation can give exposure and visibility to the original culture, which may lead to increased appreciation and understanding of the values and history associated with it. It can also inspire people to learn more about the culture and give them a greater sense of connection to it.

One example of this is the sudden surge of the Indian culture in the early 2010s, especially after the release of the Bollywood Film “Slumdog Millionaire.” The movie’s success gave a significant level of prominence to the Indian culture on an international platform, eventually leading many people to appreciate and adopt some elements of Indian culture. From traditional clothing to music, the Indian influence found its way into the global pop culture scene, and even those who didn’t have a deep connection to the culture gained a newfound respect and admiration for it.

Nevertheless, it’s crucial to practice this cultural exchange with respect and the proper understanding of the history and significance behind the adopted elements. Cultural appreciation and understanding are just as, if not more, important than borrowing. This is important to recognize, as cultural appropriation can extremely insult the source culture when not done correctly.

An example of a highly sensitive culture is that of Native Americans. Their culture has been misappropriated for years, with items like headdresses and dreamcatchers being sold at festivals and markets worldwide. Not only do such actions trivialize the history and importance of the culture, but they sometimes result in profit from misinformation and misrepresentation of their history. The erasure of Native American voices in pop culture promotes a continuation of their invisibility in the dominant culture.

In conclusion, cultural appropriation has a duality in pop culture, as it has both empowering and insulting elements. It’s crucial to recognize the importance of cultural sensitivity and respect for the cultures involved, rather than simply borrowing elements and profiting from it without understanding the background. We must always ensure that we are learning from other cultures instead of exploiting them, gaining a wider perspective on their meanings and significance so as to truly appreciate the beauty and value of diverse cultures worldwide.
