The Best Soundtracks of the Decade: A Pop Culture Retrospective


Music has the incredible power to transport us to different worlds, evoke emotions, and enhance our overall cinematic experience. In the past decade, we have witnessed the rise of some truly remarkable soundtracks that have left an indelible mark on pop culture. From sweeping orchestral compositions to catchy pop tunes, these soundtracks have become an integral part of our lives. Join us on a journey as we take a look back at the best soundtracks of the decade and celebrate the music that has defined an era.

1. “Guardians of the Galaxy” (2014)

Who could forget the infectious mixtape that accompanied Peter Quill’s intergalactic adventures? “Guardians of the Galaxy” took us on a nostalgia-filled ride with its carefully curated collection of ’70s and ’80s hits. From the iconic opening scene featuring “Hooked on a Feeling” to the climactic battle set to “Ain’t No Mountain High Enough,” this soundtrack breathed new life into classic songs and introduced them to a whole new generation.

2. “La La Land” (2016)

“La La Land” brought back the magic of the golden age of Hollywood musicals. This enchanting soundtrack, composed by Justin Hurwitz, perfectly complemented the film’s bittersweet love story. From the toe-tapping opening number “Another Day of Sun” to the hauntingly beautiful “City of Stars,” the music of “La La Land” captured the essence of dreams, love, and the pursuit of happiness.

3. “Black Panther” (2018)

The groundbreaking superhero film “Black Panther” not only shattered box office records but also delivered an exceptional soundtrack. Curated by Kendrick Lamar, this album seamlessly blended hip-hop, R&B, and African influences to create a sonic masterpiece. From the empowering anthem “All the Stars” to the vibrant and energetic “Wakanda” theme, the “Black Panther” soundtrack celebrated the rich cultural heritage of Africa while pushing musical boundaries.

4. “Interstellar” (2014)

In Christopher Nolan’s epic space odyssey “Interstellar,” the music played a pivotal role in immersing the audience in the vastness and wonder of the cosmos. Composer Hans Zimmer crafted a score that was both haunting and awe-inspiring, perfectly capturing the grandeur of space and the emotional journey of the characters. The organ-driven “Cornfield Chase” and the emotionally charged “No Time for Caution” are just a few highlights from this unforgettable soundtrack.

5. “Baby Driver” (2017)

Edgar Wright’s action-packed film “Baby Driver” was a thrilling ride from start to finish, and its soundtrack played a crucial role in setting the tone. With a mix of classic rock, soul, and pop hits, this soundtrack kept us grooving along with the high-octane car chases. From the foot-tapping “Bellbottoms” to the soulful “Easy,” “Baby Driver” proved that music can be as integral to a film as its plot and characters.

6. “A Star is Born” (2018)

Bradley Cooper’s directorial debut, “A Star is Born,” gave us one of the most powerful soundtracks in recent years. The raw and emotional performances by Cooper and Lady Gaga, coupled with their undeniable chemistry, made this soundtrack truly unforgettable. From the heart-wrenching “Shallow” to the soul-stirring “Always Remember Us This Way,” the music of “A Star is Born” struck a chord with audiences worldwide.

7. “The Greatest Showman” (2017)

“The Greatest Showman” captured the imagination of audiences with its spectacular visuals and show-stopping musical numbers. This soundtrack, composed by Benj Pasek and Justin Paul, showcased the talents of its cast, particularly Hugh Jackman as P.T. Barnum. From the inspiring anthem “This Is Me” to the infectious energy of “The Greatest Show,” this soundtrack reminded us of the power of dreams and the importance of embracing our uniqueness.

8. “Stranger Things” (2016 - present)

While not a traditional film soundtrack, the music of Netflix’s “Stranger Things” has become synonymous with the show’s ’80s-inspired nostalgia. Composers Kyle Dixon and Michael Stein created a synth-heavy score that perfectly captured the eerie and mysterious atmosphere of the Upside Down. From the iconic opening theme to the hauntingly beautiful “Kids,” this soundtrack transported us back in time and immersed us in the world of Hawkins, Indiana.

9. “Crazy Rich Asians” (2018)

“Crazy Rich Asians” made waves as the first major Hollywood film to feature an all-Asian cast in 25 years, and its soundtrack was equally groundbreaking. With a mix of traditional Chinese music and contemporary hits, this soundtrack celebrated the vibrancy and diversity of Asian culture. From the enchanting “Yellow” by Coldplay to the Mandarin cover of “Material Girl,” the “Crazy Rich Asians” soundtrack was a delightful blend of East and West.

10. “The Social Network” (2010)

David Fincher’s “The Social Network” may not be a film one would associate with a memorable soundtrack, but Trent Reznor and Atticus Ross’s experimental score proved otherwise. This electronic and atmospheric soundtrack perfectly captured the intensity and paranoia of the story. From the haunting opening track “Hand Covers Bruise” to the pulsating beats of “In Motion,” “The Social Network” soundtrack added an extra layer of depth to an already gripping film.

As we bid farewell to the past decade, let us not forget the incredible contribution of these soundtracks to the world of cinema and pop culture. They have not only enhanced our movie-watching experiences but also become an integral part of our lives. So, grab your headphones, hit play, and let the music take you on a journey through the best soundtracks of the decade.
