The Best Platforms to Sell Your Handmade Products Online


If you’ve poured your heart and soul into crafting unique handmade products, the next big step is finding the right place to showcase and sell your creations. With the explosion of e-commerce, artists, crafters, and artisans have more opportunities than ever before to reach a worldwide audience. However, with so many online marketplaces and platforms available, choosing the best fit for your handmade business can be a challenge.

This in-depth guide explores some of the best platforms designed to help handmade sellers thrive, examining each option’s features, fees, audience reach, and overall user experience to help you make an informed choice.

1. Etsy: The Go-To Handmade Marketplace

When it comes to handmade goods, Etsy stands out as the most well-known and widely used platform. Established with a focus on unique, handcrafted, and vintage items, Etsy has created a massive community of buyers and sellers worldwide.

Why Choose Etsy?

  • Massive Audience: Etsy attracts millions of shoppers specifically looking for handmade and distinctive products.
  • Built-in Tools: The platform offers handy tools for promotions, analytics, and shipping, easing many operational headaches.
  • Customization Options: Vendors can personalize their shop’s appearance to match their brand, adding logos, banners, and detailed product descriptions.

What to Consider

  • Fees: Listing fees are $0.20 per item, plus a transaction fee of 6.5% on the sale price and additional payment processing fees.
  • Competition: With millions of sellers, standing out requires strong branding, great product photography, and marketing savvy.

2. Shopify: Your Brand, Your Store

For handmade entrepreneurs craving complete control over their online presence, Shopify offers a powerful e-commerce platform that lets you build your own website and online store from scratch.

Highlights of Shopify for Handmade Sellers

  • Fully Customizable: You can design your site to reflect your brand’s personality without being constrained by a marketplace’s limits.
  • Robust Backend: Advanced inventory management, payment gateways, and marketing integrations help streamline your business operations.
  • Scalability: Shopify can grow with your business—whether you sell a handful of items or want to expand into multiple product lines.

Important Considerations

  • Monthly Cost: Shopify has a monthly fee starting at $39 for its standard plan, which may be a downside for a new seller on a tight budget.
  • Marketing Needed: Since your store will be independently run, attracting visitors depends almost entirely on your own marketing efforts.

3. Big Cartel: Simplicity for Small Makers

Big Cartel caters especially to independent artists, crafters, and small business owners who want an easy and affordable way to sell online without dealing with complex systems.

What Makes Big Cartel Ideal?

  • User-Friendly: The setup process is straightforward and doesn’t require any coding skills.
  • Flexible Plans: There’s a free version for stores with up to 5 products, and affordable paid plans for bigger stores.
  • Aesthetic Templates: Handmade sellers can choose from clean, creative themes that showcase their products beautifully.

Keep in Mind

  • Basic Features: While great for simplicity, Big Cartel doesn’t have as many advanced features or apps compared to Shopify.
  • Limited Integrations: Fewer third-party integrations can mean more manual work for things like marketing and shipping.

4. Amazon Handmade: Leveraging a Giant

If you want to tap into Amazon’s enormous customer base but still emphasize the handmade nature of your products, Amazon Handmade could be the perfect choice.

Advantages of Amazon Handmade

  • Huge Reach: Amazon has millions of daily visitors, meaning vast exposure potential.
  • Handmade-Only Verification: Your products must meet strict handmade standards, which helps maintain a marketplace devoted to authentic handcrafted items.
  • Fulfillment Support: Sellers can use Amazon FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon) for streamlined shipping and customer service.

Points to Ponder

  • Application Process: Amazon Handmade requires an application to ensure products are truly handmade.
  • Fees: The platform charges a 15% referral fee on each sale, higher than some other options.
  • Marketplace Competition: There’s competition from mass-produced goods on the broader Amazon site.

5. Folksy: Handmade in the UK

For artisans based in the United Kingdom, Folksy serves as a dedicated online marketplace showcasing UK handmade crafts and art, making it attractive for sellers targeting British customers.

Why Folksy?

  • Niche Market: Focused on UK-made items, making it easier to build community and credibility locally.
  • Simple Fee Structure: No listing fees, with a straightforward commission of 6% on sales.
  • Supportive Community: Folksy provides opportunities for sellers to connect and collaborate.

Considerations

  • Smaller Audience: While ideal for UK sellers, Folksy’s reach is more limited internationally.
  • Less Advanced Tools: The platform doesn’t offer as many tools or integrations compared to larger marketplaces.

6. ArtFire: Handmade, Vintage & Craft Supplies

ArtFire stands out for combining handmade goods with vintage items and craft supplies in one marketplace focused on creative entrepreneurs.

Key Features

  • Unique Mix: Sellers can offer handcrafted items alongside supplies and vintage finds.
  • No Listing Fees: Sellers pay a monthly subscription rather than per-product listing fees.
  • Custom Shops: Vendors can create personalized storefronts with banners, logos, and product sections.

What to Watch For

  • Smaller Customer Base: ArtFire is not as large as Etsy or Amazon, so traffic may be lower.
  • Marketing Dependence: Sellers need to be proactive in driving visitors to their shops.

7. Handmade at eBay: The Online Auction Giant’s Handmade Side

eBay Handmade is a segment dedicated to artisans and creators looking to tap into eBay’s enormous global buyer network.

Selling with eBay Handmade Means:

  • Access to Global Buyers: eBay’s long-standing reputation and diverse audience can boost visibility.
  • Auction and Fixed-price Sales: Sellers can choose how to sell their items.
  • No Listing Fees: Only a commission on sales applies.

Things to Think About

  • Strict Quality Controls: Sellers need to maintain high product standards.
  • Competition: Being one of the biggest platforms, standing out requires smart listings and excellent customer service.

8. Independent Website with WordPress + WooCommerce

For handmade sellers who want ultimate control without paying high platform fees, building an independent website using WordPress paired with WooCommerce is a powerful option.

Benefits

  • Complete Customizability: Every aspect of your site, from layout to functionality, can be tailored.
  • Cost Efficiency: Hosting and plugin fees are generally affordable compared to hosted marketplaces.
  • Ownership: You own your data and storefront, free from platform policy changes or sudden fee hikes.

Challenges

  • Steeper Learning Curve: Setting up and maintaining a WordPress WooCommerce store requires more technical skill.
  • Marketing Responsibility: Driving traffic depends entirely on your promotional efforts.

Making the Final Choice

Choosing the best platform to sell your handmade products largely depends on your priorities:

  • If you want immediate access to a large community of buyers, Etsy or Amazon Handmade are top picks.
  • For sellers prioritizing customization and brand control, Shopify or an independent WordPress site can offer unmatched flexibility.
  • If you prefer a straightforward setup with fewer fees, platforms like Big Cartel or ArtFire may be ideal.
  • Local artisans focused on UK buyers might find Folksy particularly supportive.

Each platform has its own strengths and learning curves. Many successful handmade entrepreneurs combine multiple platforms to maximize exposure. For instance, some start on Etsy to build audience trust, then launch Shopify stores to deepen brand identity and customer loyalty.

Tips for Success Selling Handmade Products Online

Regardless of which platform you choose, certain strategies apply universally:

  • High-Quality Photography: Clear, attractive product images are critical in online sales.
  • Detailed Descriptions: Tell the story behind each product, highlighting craftsmanship and materials.
  • SEO Mindset: Use naturally integrated keywords in listings and shop descriptions to ensure your products are discoverable.
  • Customer Engagement: Respond promptly to inquiries, encourage reviews, and cultivate a community.
  • Consistent Branding: Keep a cohesive look and voice across all channels to build recognition.

Conclusion

The world of online selling offers countless avenues for handmade creators to transform their passion into profit. The best platform for you is one that aligns not just with your product style and customer base but also with how much control you want over your business and how much effort you’re willing to invest in marketing and store management.

Explore your options thoughtfully, combine platforms if needed, and focus on delivering quality and authenticity. Handmade sellers who embrace the unique stories behind their creations and leverage the right online tools can thrive in today’s global marketplace.

Whether you pick Etsy’s bustling marketplace, Shopify’s customizable power, or a smaller niche site, your handmade products deserve to be seen and loved by an appreciative audience — and the perfect online platform is out there waiting for you.
