Fantasy Book Clubs: How to Start and Run One


If you’re passionate about the realm of dragons, magic, epic quests, and intricate world-building, starting a fantasy book club could be your gateway to meeting like-minded enthusiasts and diving deeper into your favorite stories. A fantasy book club offers a unique blend of community, creativity, and critical discussion, making it a rewarding experience for everyone involved. In this guide, we’ll explore how to create and manage a thriving fantasy book club, ensuring your group fosters engaging discussions and builds connections through shared literary adventures.

Why a Fantasy Book Club?

Fantasy literature captivates millions because it combines imagination with emotional depth, complex characters, and sprawling narratives. Joining or starting a fantasy book club means more than just reading; it means immersing yourself in alternate realities and exploring different themes, from politics and philosophy to friendship and heroism. The fantastic settings provide ample material for debate, interpretation, and creative exploration, which can make every meeting exciting.

By creating your own fantasy book club, you tailor the experience: you decide on the reading pace, choose books that resonate with members, and nurture a space where everyone can express their views and theories without judgment.

Step 1: Define Your Fantasy Book Club’s Purpose and Style

Before inviting members, clarify what kind of club you want to run. Some questions to consider:

  • What sub-genres will you focus on? Fantasy is vast — will your club gravitate toward epic fantasy like The Wheel of Time, urban fantasy such as Rivers of London, dark fantasy, or mythological retellings?
  • What’s the ideal group size? Smaller groups (6-10 members) often result in more intimate discussions, whereas larger ones can offer diverse perspectives but might require additional coordination.
  • How often will you meet? Decide on a schedule that balances enthusiasm with practical availability—monthly meetings are common and effective.
  • Will meetings be in-person, online, or hybrid? Each format has pros and cons based on members’ locations and preferences.

Setting these parameters early helps attract members who share the same expectations and supports smooth operations.

Step 2: Gather Members Who Share Your Passion

Finding people interested in fantasy literature might be easier than you think. Here are some avenues to explore:

  • Local libraries and bookstores: These places often have bulletin boards or community announcements where you can post flyers or notices.
  • Social media groups: Platforms like Facebook, Reddit (r/Fantasy), and Discord host countless fantasy fans who might be thrilled to join a book club.
  • Fantasy conventions and fairs: If there’s a nearby event, networking there can yield enthusiastic members.
  • Friends and colleagues: Ask around your immediate circle; you might be surprised how many fantasy fans surround you.

When forming your group, consider gathering a brief introduction from interested individuals — their favorite fantasy books, expectations, and availability. This helps in curating a group dynamic that works harmoniously.

Step 3: Choose Your First Book Wisely

The inaugural book can set the tone for your entire club experience. Opt for a book that:

  • Is accessible to most members (consider length, complexity, and availability).
  • Welcomes discussion — layers of meaning, diverse characters, and a compelling plot make great fodder.
  • Aligns with the club’s theme or sub-genre preferences.

For example, start with a classic like The Hobbit or a modern favorite like The Name of the Wind. Alternatively, select a lesser-known gem to spark curiosity and discovery.

Step 4: Set Up Structures to Facilitate Engaging Meetings

Engagement is the key to a successful fantasy book club. To maintain lively, meaningful discussions, consider implementing:

A Shared Platform for Communication

Platforms like Discord, Slack, or WhatsApp serve as hubs for coordination, sharing insights, and posting reminders. Creating dedicated channels (themes, spoilers, general chat) helps keep conversations organized.

Meeting Formats

Variety keeps enthusiasm alive. You might try:

  • Roundtable discussions: Each member shares thoughts with equal time.
  • Themed meetings: Focus on specific aspects, like character development or magic systems.
  • Guest speakers or authors: Occasionally invite experts or local authors to add richness.
  • Creative sessions: Writing fan-fiction or drawing characters based on the book.

Discussion Guides

Prepare some open-ended questions to jumpstart dialogue, such as:

  • What themes did the author explore? Were they effective?
  • Which character did you relate to most, and why?
  • How well did the world-building support the story?
  • Did the magical system seem consistent and well-developed?

Rules for Respectful Dialogue

Ensure the space is safe for differing opinions. Encourage listening, openness, and discourage spoilers outside designated channels.

Step 5: Manage Book Selections and Reading Pace

Handling book choices democratically can boost members’ investment:

  • Voting: Present a shortlist for members to vote on.
  • Rotation: Let each member pick a book in turn (useful for smaller groups).
  • Themes: Plan selections around themes — dragons month, epic quests, or debut authors.

Balance ambitious reads with lighter, quicker books to maintain momentum. For pacing, track members’ preferred reading speeds and adjust accordingly. Some clubs even set milestones (e.g., chapters per week) to keep everyone aligned.

Step 6: Organize Memorable Meetings

Whether in person or virtual, the atmosphere of your meetings shapes the experience. Some ideas include:

  • Themed decorations and snacks: If meeting physically, bring thematic elements — say, Elvish-inspired cookies or dragon-shaped bookmarks.
  • Virtual backgrounds or costume days: Online meetings can still be playful—members dressing as favorite characters or virtual backdrops from fantasy worlds add fun.
  • Ice-breakers and games: Begin with quick quizzes or polls related to the current book.
  • Record highlights: Consider journaling or recording major discussion points for members to revisit, or even sharing blog posts or podcasts summarizing your insights.

Step 7: Keep Members Engaged Between Meetings

A fantasy book club thrives on ongoing excitement:

  • Online forums and chats: Keep conversations active, share related articles, fan art, or fantasy news.
  • Supplementary media: Suggest watching TV adaptations, listening to audiobooks, or exploring companion materials.
  • Challenges or contests: Encourage members to write alternate endings or design new characters.
  • Reading recommendations: Members can share personal favorites or new releases.

Maintaining momentum ensures the club remains a central part of members’ routines instead of a one-off event.

Step 8: Grow and Adapt Your Club Over Time

As your fantasy book club matures, remain flexible to members’ changing interests:

  • Feedback sessions: Periodically ask what’s working, what could improve.
  • Expanding activities: Host movie nights, participate in literary festivals, or embark on author meetups.
  • New recruitment: Occasionally invite fresh members to bring different perspectives.
  • Adjust formats: Experiment with discussion styles, pacing, or hybrid meetings based on feedback.

A successful club blends structure with adaptability, ensuring it evolves alongside its community.

Bonus: Addressing Common Challenges

Running a fantasy book club isn’t without hurdles. Here are top issues and ways to handle them:

  • Low Attendance: Send reminders early, be understanding about schedules, consider flexible meeting times.
  • Spoiler Management: Establish clear rules about spoiler warnings and keep certain communications spoiler-free.
  • Diverse Opinions: Reinforce an atmosphere of respect, encourage evidence-backed viewpoints, and moderate dominant voices.
  • Commitment Levels: Promote participation without pressure. It’s okay if some members occasionally miss meetings or finish books late.

Final Thoughts

Starting and running a fantasy book club offers enchanting benefits: you not only deepen your appreciation of the genre but build friendships grounded in shared passion. By carefully planning, nurturing engagement, and embracing creativity, your club can become a treasured space where stories live beyond the page.

Whether you’re a seasoned fantasy devotee or just embarking on this literary journey, a book club unites readers in collective exploration of magic, adventure, and the power of storytelling. So gather your fellow adventurers, pick your first tome, and set forth into the wonderful world of fantasy book clubs!
