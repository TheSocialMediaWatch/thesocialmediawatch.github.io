  • Panzotti Panzotti, or little breads, are a scrumptious holiday treat that has been passed down through generations. With a golden-brown exterior giving away to a soft, warm interior filled with sweet wisps of ricotta, the true magic of panzotti lies in their rustic presentation.

Ingredients:

  • 2 and a half cups of all-purpose flour
  • A pinch of salt
  • 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • Warm water
  • 1.5 cups of ricotta
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract
  • A dash of cinnamon
  • A teaspoon of candied orange peel chopped
  • Icing sugar

Instructions:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, and sugar.
  2. In another bowl, mix eggs, oil, and warm water.
  3. Combine both mixtures and knead until a smooth dough forms. Let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, prepare the ricotta filling by mixing ricotta, vanilla, cinnamon, and candied orange peel. Season with delicacy and taste, adding more ingredients if desired.
  5. Roll out the dough to a thickness of half an inch. Cut into 3-inch squares and spoon a small ladle of ricotta filling into the center of each square.
  6. Fold the dough over the filling and press the edges tightly to seal the Panzotti. Cook in a preheated oven on 375°F (190°C) for approximately 15-20 minutes, until lightly golden.
  7. Dust generously with icing sugar and serve warm for the ultimate festive treat.

Roland Chocolat Mini Christmas Trees:

Ingredients: Chopped chocolate (dark, milk, or white), tree cone molds, assorted Christmas fruits, and nuts.

Instructions:

  1. Temper the chocolate of your choice and pour it into the tree cone molds.
  2. Place the molds into the fridge to let the chocolate harden.
  3. Once the chocolate tree cones have hardened, insert assorted fruits and nuts into the branches.
  4. Drizzle with chocolate strands and finish by topping your tree with a candy or nut.
  5. Now, place them in festive cups, and serve them to your loved ones for an unforgettable holiday experience.
