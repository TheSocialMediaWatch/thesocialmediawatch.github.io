10 Scenic Bicycle Routes You Must Try


Cycling is not just a mode of transportation; it’s a way to connect with nature, explore new landscapes, and enjoy breathtaking views while staying active. Whether you are a casual rider or a seasoned cyclist, there are countless scenic routes waiting to be explored. Here are ten of the most picturesque bicycle routes that you absolutely must try.

1. The Pacific Coast Highway, California

The Pacific Coast Highway (PCH) is a dream for any cyclist. Stretching over 650 miles along California’s coastline, this route offers stunning views of the ocean, cliffs, and charming seaside towns. One of the most scenic sections is from Monterey to San Luis Obispo, where you can marvel at the rugged coastline and iconic landmarks like Big Sur and the Bixby Creek Bridge. Don’t forget to take breaks in charming towns like Carmel-by-the-Sea and Cambria, where you can refuel with local delicacies.

2. The Great Allegheny Passage, Pennsylvania to Maryland

The Great Allegheny Passage (GAP) is a 150-mile trail that connects Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, to Cumberland, Maryland. This former railroad corridor offers cyclists a smooth, scenic ride through lush forests, rolling hills, and charming small towns. Along the way, you can enjoy beautiful views of the Youghiogheny River, historic landmarks, and even the impressive Mason-Dixon line. The route is well-marked and features various access points, making it ideal for both long-distance cyclists and those looking for a shorter ride.

3. The Danube Cycle Path, Austria to Hungary

For an international cycling experience, the Danube Cycle Path is a must-try. Spanning over 1,200 kilometers, this route follows the majestic Danube River from Germany through Austria and into Hungary. Cyclists will be treated to picturesque vineyards, quaint villages, and stunning castles along the way. Notable stops include the historic city of Vienna and the charming town of Szentendre in Hungary. The well-maintained path and relatively flat terrain make it accessible for cyclists of all levels.

4. The Kancamagus Highway, New Hampshire

The Kancamagus Highway, or Kanc, is renowned for its breathtaking fall foliage and scenic mountain views. This 34-mile stretch in the White Mountains of New Hampshire is perfect for cyclists looking for an exhilarating ride. As you pedal along this route, you’ll encounter stunning vistas, covered bridges, and the rushing waters of the Swift River. Make sure to stop at the various scenic overlooks to capture the beauty of the changing seasons, especially in autumn when the leaves burst into vibrant colors.

5. The Blue Ridge Parkway, Virginia and North Carolina

The Blue Ridge Parkway is often referred to as “America’s Favorite Drive,” but it’s also a fantastic cycling destination. Stretching 469 miles through the Appalachian Mountains, this route boasts breathtaking views of rolling hills, lush forests, and vibrant wildflowers. Cyclists can choose from various sections to explore, each offering unique landscapes and charming stops. Don’t miss the opportunity to visit the iconic Mabry Mill or hike to one of the many waterfalls along the way.

6. The Golden Gate Bridge to Sausalito, California

If you’re looking for a shorter yet iconic ride, the route from San Francisco to Sausalito is perfect. Starting at the Golden Gate Bridge, cyclists can enjoy panoramic views of the San Francisco skyline, Alcatraz Island, and the Marin Headlands. Once across the bridge, the ride continues along the waterfront, leading to the picturesque town of Sausalito, known for its charming shops and stunning bay views. After exploring Sausalito, you can take the ferry back to San Francisco for a relaxing end to your ride.

7. The Loire Valley, France

The Loire Valley is often referred to as the “Garden of France,” and for good reason. This UNESCO World Heritage site is famous for its stunning châteaux, vineyards, and scenic riverbanks. Cycling through the Loire Valley allows you to explore historic towns like Amboise and Blois while enjoying the peaceful countryside. The gentle terrain makes it accessible for cyclists of all skill levels, and there are plenty of local wineries to stop at for a tasting or a picnic.

8. The Moab Mountain Biking Trails, Utah

For adrenaline junkies, the Moab area in Utah is a mountain biking paradise. With its stunning red rock formations and diverse terrain, Moab offers a variety of trails suitable for all skill levels. The Slickrock Trail is one of the most famous routes, providing a challenging ride with breathtaking views of the surrounding landscape. Additionally, the La Sal Mountain Loop offers a different perspective, showcasing lush forests and alpine scenery. Remember to stay hydrated and enjoy the unique geological formations along the way.

9. The C&O Canal Towpath, Maryland to Washington D.C.

The Chesapeake and Ohio Canal Towpath is a historic route that runs alongside the Potomac River from Washington D.C. to Cumberland, Maryland. This 184.5-mile trail is perfect for cyclists looking to experience a mix of history and nature. As you ride, you’ll encounter scenic river views, historic locks, and charming small towns. The towpath is mostly flat and unpaved, making it suitable for hybrid or mountain bikes. Along the way, take the opportunity to explore the nearby Great Falls Park for stunning waterfalls and hiking trails.

10. The Alpe d’Huez, France

For those seeking a challenge, the Alpe d’Huez is a legendary cycling destination known for its steep climbs and breathtaking mountain views. This iconic route is famous among professional cyclists and is often featured in the Tour de France. The climb consists of 21 hairpin turns, each offering stunning vistas of the surrounding Alps. While it’s a challenging ride, the sense of accomplishment and the views from the top make it a must-try for serious cyclists.

Conclusion

Exploring scenic bicycle routes is one of the most rewarding ways to connect with nature and experience the beauty of the world around us. Whether you prefer coastal rides, mountain trails, or charming countryside paths, there’s a route for everyone. So grab your bike, plan your adventure, and get ready to experience the joy of cycling on these breathtaking routes. Happy cycling!
