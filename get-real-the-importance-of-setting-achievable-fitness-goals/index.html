Get Real: The Importance of Setting Achievable Fitness Goals


Have you taken that important step to pursue a healthier lifestyle by signing up to a gym or embarking on a weight loss challenge? Or perhaps you’ve hunched over your computer all week and now feel the urge to hit the pavement and run for several miles. It is commendable to dream big, but the ultimate question is, have you set your fitness goals in line with your current reality?

The importance of setting realistic fitness goals cannot be overstated. But why is this crucial? Quite simply, following an attainable plan increases your chances of sticking to it and achieving results. Nothing is more demoralizing than setting excessive targets only to fall short repeatedly - a situation that can make you give up on your fitness journey altogether. In this post, we’ll explore the significance of setting achievable fitness goals and how you can accomplish them.

The pitfall of unrealistic fitness goals

Imagine setting an unrealistic goal such as losing 20 pounds in a month without exercising much or paying attention to your diet. No matter how much you stay optimistic, slimmer pants or a smaller waistline won’t happen that fast. More often than not, running on unrealistic fitness goals most of the time ends in failure, which could demotivate and discourage you.

Fitness goals that lack balance, flexibility, and realism are prone to failure since they don’t take into account an individual’s current fitness level, habits, and limitations. Moreover, it can lead to injuries and health complications if the intensity of workouts suddenly increases to accommodate a goal that’s way above one’s capacity.

Set your sights on the right goals

If you want to make your fitness journey a successful one, take time to set realistic goals. Here are some steps you can follow:

  1. Reflect On Your Current Fitness Level: Be honest with yourself. Are you a beginner, intermediate or advanced fitness enthusiast? Recognizing your fitness level helps you develop goals appropriate for where you are in your fitness journey.

  2. Be Specific: Specific goals tend to motivate more. Instead of saying, “I want to lose weight,” try saying, “I want to lose 10 pounds in the next three months.” The former is too vague, while the latter offers a precise objective.

  3. Set Achievable Deadlines: Setting a realistic timeline is essential for both motivation and tracking progress. Remember that sustainable fat loss takes several weeks, not a few days.

  4. Embrace the process: Make a plan that revolves around comprehensive health - diet, hydration, rest, and exercise. A goal should be a journey towards a sustainable lifestyle, not a race to the finish line.

  5. Stay Accountable: Share your goals with family and friends, or consider using fitness apps that track your progress. Be accountable for your actions and your progress. Hold yourself accountable for staying true to your plan.

  6. Be Flexible and Kind to Yourself: Understand that a setback doesn’t mean failure. Life happens, and your goals may shift as you go. Be prepared to revise your goals and stay true to your journey at the pace that feels right for you.

Conclusion

A crucial element of any successful fitness journey is setting achievable goals that inspire, motivate and don’t place undue stress on the body or mind. Your goals should be realistic and make you feel that you’re on steady ground as you take on the challenge of elevating your well-being. If a goal feels too hard or unrealistic, take time to reassess it. Remember, the journey to increased health and fitness can be enjoyable, inspiring and above all else, sustainable.
