Embracing the Grind: Progression Systems in Games


In the vast world of gaming, there’s one aspect that often goes unnoticed – the progression system. It is the backbone that keeps players engaged and motivated. As gamers, we invest time and effort into our virtual lives, and progression systems are the reward for our dedication. This article will delve into the various types of progression systems and how they contribute to the overall gaming experience.

There are two main categories of progression systems: XP-based and resource-based. Let’s explore both to better understand their impact on games.

XP-Based Progression

Experience points (XP) are the foundation of XP-based progression systems. Players gain XP by completing missions, tasks, or defeating enemies. Once they accumulate a certain amount of XP, they level up, granting them access to new abilities, equipment, or locations. This system motivates players to explore new areas and challenges, as they know that advancing in level will unlock more content.

One example of an XP-based progression system is the one found in classic role-playing games (RPGs) like World of Warcraft or Skyrim. Players engage in quests and battles to accumulate XP, ultimately leading to character growth and skill development. This progression system forms the backbone of RPGs and keeps players coming back for more.

Resource-Based Progression

On the other hand, resource-based progression systems revolve around acquiring resources, such as currency, materials, or items. These resources are typically earned by completing tasks or overcoming challenges. Players can then use these resources to buy better equipment, upgrade their skills or unlock new content. This system adds an extra layer of strategy, as players must decide what resources to collect and how to invest them appropriately.

A prime example of a resource-based progression system can be found in games like Diablo or Path of Exile. In these action-RPGs, players delve into the depths of darkness, looting precious resources and treasures. The accumulation of resources and the strategic allocation of those resources drive the players forward, constantly pushing them to hunt for more.

MMO Games and Persistence

Massively multiplayer online (MMO) games often implement a combination of XP-based and resource-based progression systems. This dual approach reinforces the sense of a shared, persistent world. Players invest time into building their characters, unlocking abilities, and acquiring resources, ultimately shaping their virtual identity. Since everything persists even after logging out, the value of progression deepens. Players’ progress becomes a part of the ever-evolving, shared world, forging connections among players and enhancing the overall experience.

The Appeal of Progression Systems

Progression systems tickle the hunter-gatherer instincts within us. It is gratifying to see our efforts result in growth and unlock new opportunities. Furthermore, progression systems offer us control, as we make choices about our character’s development and decide which paths to explore in a game’s vast world. These systems introduce an engaging structure to games, keeping players captivated with the allure of what lies ahead.

In conclusion, progression systems are undeniably essential in defining game experiences. Whether XP-based or resource-based, they motivate and drive players to explore, learn, and grow within the game world. By fostering a sense of achievement and progression, these systems deepen our connection with the digital realm we inhabit as gamers.
