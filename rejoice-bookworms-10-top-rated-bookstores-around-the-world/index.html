Rejoice Bookworms! 10 Top-Rated Bookstores Around the World


Books have a unique ability to transport us, whether that means journeying to different countries, eras, and even galaxies far, far away. For bibliophiles, visiting bookstores around the world only adds to the thrill. From quaint, hidden gems to expansive literary temples, here are ten top-rated bookstores that any avid reader is sure to adore.

1. The Strand - New York City, USA

Sitting pretty on 82nd St, The Strand is a bookworm’s paradise, with a collection of over 2.5 million books to sift through. Known for its affordable prices, and dusty, antique charm, The Strand is a must-visit for anyone in New York City craving a literary adventure.

2. Hey Terri - Tokyo, Japan

Located in the heart of Koenji, a hub for indie culture, Hey Terri is a cozy, unassuming bookstore that packs a punch. The store’s rare, foreign language books, and quirky selection of magazines invite explorers to get lost in their maze of aisles.

3. Livraria Lello - Porto, Portugal

Allow us to gush about one of the world’s most beautiful bookstores. Lavish ornamentation, exquisite curves, and that iconic red staircase, Livraria Lello is an ode to architectural extravagance. Be aware, however, that you may need to purchase a package tour to visit this literary gem.

4. Barnes & Noble - New York City, USA

The Barnes & Noble flagship store on 5th Ave evokes bibliophile nostalgia. Vintage charm meets modern-day bookstore with various genres to choose from, cafe, and well-organized sections. This is a one-stop-shop for those who seek to fill their shelves with books new and old.

5. Ulrike Reindell Buchhandlung - Berlin, Germany

Perched quietly along Kastanienallee, Ulrike Reindell’s bohemian-indie bookstore was the setting for the intricate love story in “The Lives of Others.” This enchanting, intimate bookstore boasts a well-curated selection of new, translated, and self-published German literature.

6. Mr. B’s Emporium of Reading Delights - Bath, England

Located in the historic Roman city of Bath, Mr. B’s Emporium offers an eclectic combination of new and used books. Customized publishing, events, and sumptuous hot chocolate in the charm-filled tea room make this an unforgettable bookish getaway.

7. Librería Parnaso - Buenos Aires, Argentina

Stay a while in the labyrinth of Librería Parnaso! This Buenos Aires landmark carries rare and antique books, as well as new works by Argentine authors. Painted tiles and hidden shelves make the bookstore a Tanglobal treasure hunt for book lovers.

8. Friends I Love You - São Paulo, Brazil

Looking for that perfect book and a friendly chat? Friend’s I Love You is São Paulo’s ultimate independent bookstore. Immersed in greenery, with expansive shelves of literature, this store offers for an atmosphere that is warm, welcoming, and brimming with inspiration.

9. Shakespeare and Company - Paris, France

A destination on many bibliophile bucket lists, Shakespeare and Company offers a bookstore gallery with an artsy feel. The iconic volunteer-hostel is located within this bookstore, so literature lovers can literally live among the pages.

10. Hanazonoanta - Tokyo, Japan

Hanazonoanta, a book-lover’s utopia, boasts books of all genres, antique books and an art gallery. Encouraging literary discourse and book publishing, this engaging bookstore is worshipped by Tokyo-based bibliophiles.

If you’re craving a literary adventure, we hope to inspire you to dive headfirst into these bookstores unrivaled in their charm, inventory, and location. Whether you’re planning a vacation or spending your days at home, rejoice in these havens of hidden gems, enticing stories, and literary wonders. Happy reading!
