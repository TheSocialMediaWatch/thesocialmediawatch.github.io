10 Lesser-Known Liquors That Are Worth Trying


In the vast, exhilarating world of spirits, there is a treasure trove of lesser-known liquors waiting to be discovered. While everyone may be familiar with the classics like whiskey, vodka, rum, and tequila, exploring more obscure liquors not only broadens your palate but also opens the door to unique cocktail possibilities and cultural experiences. Whether you’re a seasoned spirit enthusiast or an adventurous beginner, these ten lesser-known liquors deserve a spot in your bar collection and, more importantly, your glass.

1. Pisco – The South American Brandy

Originating from Peru and Chile, Pisco is a grape-based brandy that captures the essence of South American winemaking. Unlike traditional brandies made from distilled wine, Pisco is produced using a unique distillation process that preserves delicate grape aromas and flavors. It offers floral and fruity notes with hints of citrus and herbs, making it a refreshing base for cocktails like the classic Pisco Sour, which blends pisco, lime juice, simple syrup, and egg white for a frothy finish.

If you enjoy trying new twists on sour cocktails, Pisco is a fantastic choice. Plus, its versatile flavor lets you enjoy it neat or in innovative drinks.

2. Aquavit – The Nordic Spirit

Aquavit is a Scandinavian gem with a centuries-old tradition. This caraway- and dill-flavored spirit often includes other botanicals like fennel, coriander, and anise. Aquavit was originally created to aid digestion and is traditionally consumed chilled, neat, and in small sips, often alongside Nordic dishes.

With its herbaceous notes and subtle spice, aquavit offers a refreshing alternative to gin or vodka, lending bright complexity to cocktails or standing strong by itself. Bartenders in cocktail bars are increasingly embracing aquavit in modern recipes, so it’s a great liquor choice for those seeking something beyond the usual fare.

3. Chacha – The Georgian Grape Pomace Brandy

If you are a fan of grappa but want to explore something different, Georgian Chacha is a fantastic find. Made from the pomace of grapes (the leftovers from winemaking), Chacha is often referred to as “Georgian vodka” due to its high alcohol content and clear, potent character.

Chacha embodies the soul of traditional Georgian winemaking and is usually consumed in shots during lively gatherings. It carries earthy, fruity undertones that give it a distinctive flavor profile. Its robust presence makes it ideal for those who enjoy bold, intense spirits.

4. Mezcal – The Smoky Cousin of Tequila

While tequila has gained immense global popularity, mezcal remains relatively underappreciated despite its rich depth and heritage. Mezcal is crafted from agave like tequila but uses different agave species and traditional production techniques, including roasting agave hearts over wood fires, which imparts smoky, earthy flavors.

Mezcal’s smoky aroma and complex taste range from sweet and fruity to intensely smoky and herbal. It’s perfect for adventurous drinkers who want to discover agave spirits with personality. Mezcal works wonderfully both sipped slowly or mixed into cocktails like the Oaxaca Old Fashioned, combining sweetness and smoke in harmony.

5. Baijiu – China’s National Spirit

Baijiu is one of the most widely consumed liquors in the world, yet it remains a mystery to most Western palates. This Chinese spirit is distilled from fermented sorghum or other grains, known for its potent aroma and flavors that range from sweet and fruity to umami and funky.

Though an acquired taste due to its intense aroma profile, baijiu offers fascinating complexity and cultural insight. It’s often enjoyed at celebrations in China and is increasingly inspiring mixologists outside Asia to craft fusion cocktails. Trying baijiu is a journey into a unique flavor universe that’s quite unlike other spirits.

6. Cachaça – Brazil’s Rum Alternative

While rum is familiar worldwide, Cachaça sometimes flies under the radar despite its pivotal role in Brazil’s cocktail culture. Distilled from fresh sugarcane juice rather than molasses (like most rums), cachaça has a bright, grassy, and slightly funky flavor that defines Brazil’s iconic Caipirinha cocktail.

Good-quality cachaça can be sipped neat or used creatively in other tropical cocktails, providing a more herbaceous and lively flavor profile than traditional rum. For those who enjoy sweet, vibrant spirits with a tropical twist, cachaça is a must-try.

7. Soju – Korea’s Smooth Favor

Soju is a Korean distilled beverage traditionally made from rice, barley, or wheat but sometimes using sweet potatoes or tapioca. It has a lower ABV compared to many spirits, typically around 20%, and offers a smooth, mild flavor that makes it extremely versatile.

Often drunk neat or chilled in small glasses during meals and celebrations, soju is prized for its balance and subtle sweetness. Recently, flavored soju variants with fruit essences have surged in popularity, expanding the ways to enjoy it socially or in cocktails.

8. Rakia (Rakija) – The Balkan Brandy

Popular throughout the Balkans, rakia is a fruit brandy typically made from plums, apricots, pears, apples, or grapes. It holds a cherished place in Eastern European culture and hospitality, known for its strong flavor and warmth.

Rakia is traditionally served neat as an aperitif or digestif and embodies sweet, fruity, and sometimes spicy notes depending on the fruit used. It’s perfect for those who appreciate fruit-forward spirits with rich history and makes for a fantastic introduction to Eastern European liquors.

9. Sotol – The Desert Spirit from Mexico

Not as internationally famous as its agave cousin tequila or mezcal, Sotol is made from the desert spoon plant, or Dasylirion, native to northern Mexico. Sotol offers a herbal, earthy flavor with subtle smoke and minerality, reflecting its desert ecosystem.

This spirit is an exciting option for those wanting to explore Mexican liquors beyond agave. Sotol’s complex flavor profile lends itself well to sipping and sophisticated cocktails, bringing a fresh narrative to traditional Mexican spirits.

10. Mamajuana – The Herbal Dominican Elixir

Mamajuana is a unique concoction from the Dominican Republic made by soaking rum, red wine, and honey with a mixture of local herbs, bark, and spices. Though not a distilled liquor on its own, it’s often considered a distinct spirit due to its macerated flavors.

The resulting drink is aromatic and flavorful, believed to have medicinal properties and known for its warming, rich profile. It can be consumed straight or used as a bold addition to cocktails, making it an intriguing option for those interested in folklore-inspired and craft libations.

Exploring Beyond the Familiar

Diving into these ten lesser-known liquors presents an opportunity to broaden your drinking horizons far beyond mainstream spirits. Each one carries a story, a tradition, and a distinctive taste that can enrich your appreciation for global beverage culture. Whether you’re intrigued by the smoky depths of mezcal, the herbal mystery of aquavit, or the vibrant punch of cachaça, these liquors offer something fresh and captivating for your taste buds.

Even if you’re just experimenting at home, consider picking up one or two of these spirits. They’re gateways to cultural exploration, unique cocktail creations, and new social rituals. Next time you’re at the liquor store or a well-stocked bar, seek out these gems—they might just become your new favorites.

Happy tasting and cheers to discovery!
