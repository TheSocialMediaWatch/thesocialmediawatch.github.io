10 Uncommon Fishing Techniques That Will Surprise You


Fishing has long been a beloved activity for both seasoned anglers and casual outdoor enthusiasts. While many are familiar with the traditional techniques, there are a variety of lesser-known methods that can significantly enhance your fishing experience. These uncommon fishing techniques not only provide a unique challenge but also open up new possibilities for catching fish in ways you may have never imagined. From using unconventional bait to employing bizarre yet effective tactics, this guide will explore 10 fishing methods that will surprise and inspire you.

1. Bowfishing: Fishing with a Bow and Arrow

Bowfishing combines the excitement of archery with the thrill of fishing. This technique involves shooting fish with a specially designed bow and arrow while standing or sitting in a boat or wading in shallow waters. Bowfishing is commonly used to target species such as carp, gar, and even stingrays. The bow is equipped with a reel that holds the fishing line, allowing the angler to pull in the fish after the shot.

The challenge in bowfishing lies in the need to accurately aim and judge the distance between you and the fish. It’s a technique that requires patience, precision, and knowledge of fish behavior. Bowfishing is particularly popular in warmer waters, where fish tend to congregate in shallow regions. Many anglers appreciate the skillful nature of bowfishing, which adds a layer of intensity and focus to the fishing experience.

Why It’s Surprising

This method’s unusual combination of archery and fishing offers a completely different thrill compared to traditional rod-and-reel fishing. It’s not just about catching fish; it’s about the art of hitting a moving target in the water with a bow and arrow.

2. Night Fishing with Glow Sticks

Night fishing is an incredibly productive technique, especially for species like bass, catfish, and walleye. While the use of artificial lights to attract fish is not new, the use of glow sticks offers an unconventional twist. Simply attach glow sticks to your fishing lines, lures, or floats. The glow attracts baitfish, which in turn attract predator fish.

Glow sticks work best in murky waters, where visibility is limited. The glowing light creates an alluring effect, drawing fish to your bait and lures. Whether you’re fishing in a lake, river, or even off a pier, glow sticks can make your bait stand out, ensuring that fish are more likely to bite.

Why It’s Surprising

The use of glow sticks is surprisingly simple but highly effective. It’s a technique that requires minimal equipment but can result in a more fruitful night of fishing. It’s a great option for anglers who want to try something new during nighttime fishing.

3. Fly Fishing with Streamers for Big Game Fish

Fly fishing is often associated with small fish like trout, but many anglers are surprised to learn that it can be just as effective for larger species like bass, pike, and even saltwater fish. Using streamers – weighted flies designed to imitate minnows or other small fish – is a common tactic for attracting big game fish.

When fly fishing for big fish, anglers often cast a large streamer and retrieve it in a manner that mimics the movements of injured prey. This method is especially effective for predatory fish that hunt for smaller fish in deeper waters. The use of streamers requires a bit of practice, but it’s a rewarding challenge for anglers who enjoy the nuances of fly fishing.

Why It’s Surprising

The idea of fly fishing for larger species might sound unusual to those who typically associate it with trout or small rivers, but streamers open up a world of possibilities for catching big game fish.

4. Trolling with Downriggers

Trolling is a common technique for catching fish like salmon and trout, but using downriggers takes it to the next level. A downrigger is a device that allows anglers to set their bait at specific depths by attaching it to a weighted cable. This technique is particularly effective in deep, open-water fishing, where fish may be swimming at various depths below the surface.

By using a downrigger, you can precisely control the depth of your bait, ensuring that it reaches the fish where they are feeding. This method is great for targeting fish that are not close to the surface, such as lake trout or king salmon. While downriggers can be a bit of an investment, they offer an edge that can dramatically improve your trolling success.

Why It’s Surprising

The use of downriggers may be uncommon to some anglers, especially those who fish in shallow waters or rely on simpler trolling methods. However, this technique can help you access deep-water fish with greater efficiency.

5. Ice Fishing with a Jigging Rattle

Ice fishing is often viewed as a challenging yet rewarding experience, but using a jigging rattle can add a surprising twist to your ice fishing technique. A jigging rattle is a small device that makes noise and vibrations when moved through the water. The rattle attracts fish by mimicking the sounds of struggling prey, making it an excellent tool for enticing fish to bite.

When ice fishing, many anglers struggle to get fish’s attention in the quiet, cold waters beneath the ice. The addition of a jigging rattle helps solve this problem by creating vibrations and sound waves that travel through the water, alerting fish to your bait.

Why It’s Surprising

The concept of adding sound and vibration to ice fishing is quite unexpected, yet it proves highly effective in drawing fish into your area. It’s a game-changing technique for those who are tired of waiting for a bite in the silence of frozen waters.

6. Spearfishing with a Hawaiian Sling

Spearfishing has been around for centuries, but one of the lesser-known techniques is using a Hawaiian sling. This simple yet effective tool is essentially a spear with an elastic band used to launch it. While many people use guns or larger spears for spearfishing, the Hawaiian sling offers more precision and control.

This method is typically used in shallow waters where fish are abundant and not as easily scared away. The Hawaiian sling requires excellent aim and timing, making it both a challenging and rewarding technique. It’s a favorite of those who prefer a more hands-on approach to fishing rather than relying on high-tech gear.

Why It’s Surprising

The Hawaiian sling might seem like an ancient technique compared to modern spearfishing tools, but it’s incredibly effective. The simplicity of the design allows for quick and precise strikes, making it a fun and surprising way to catch fish.

7. Hand Fishing or Noodling for Catfish

If you’re looking for a truly unique and somewhat wild fishing experience, hand fishing (or noodling) might be just the thing. This technique involves wading into murky waters and reaching into the underwater holes or nests where catfish hide. The goal is to feel around with your hands until you locate a catfish, then grab it by the mouth and wrestle it out of its hiding place.

Noodling is most commonly practiced in the southern United States and is an adrenaline-packed way to catch large catfish. It’s not for the faint of heart, as it involves sticking your arm into dark, deep water where large, powerful fish reside.

Why It’s Surprising

Hand fishing is an old and somewhat unconventional method that seems like something out of an adventure movie. It’s a raw, physical challenge that requires a combination of patience, strength, and nerve.

8. Using Fish Traps or Pots

Fish traps are an uncommon method of fishing, often overshadowed by more popular techniques like casting or trolling. Fish traps, or pots, are devices that capture fish when they swim into them. These traps can be set in shallow or deep water, depending on the species you’re targeting.

The design of the trap typically includes one or more entry points that lead the fish inside but make it difficult for them to escape. Once the trap is set and baited, you can leave it for several hours or even overnight, returning to check your catch. This method is ideal for targeting species like lobster, crabs, and various kinds of fish that congregate in specific areas.

Why It’s Surprising

Fish traps may not be the first technique that comes to mind when thinking about fishing, but they’re highly effective for certain species. They offer a hands-off approach to fishing, making them a great option for those who prefer a more passive method.

9. Fishing with a Trotline

A trotline is a long line that is suspended horizontally in the water, with baited hooks placed along its length. This method is commonly used for catching catfish, but it can also be effective for other bottom-feeding species. Trotlines are typically set up in deeper waters and can be left overnight, providing a hands-off way to fish.

The advantage of using a trotline is that it covers a large area, increasing your chances of catching fish. Since the line is set with multiple hooks, you have the potential to catch several fish at once. While it requires a bit of setup, it can be an incredibly efficient way to fish when used correctly.

Why It’s Surprising

The trotline is an often overlooked technique, particularly in modern fishing methods. However, it provides a unique way to fish that’s both efficient and low-maintenance.

10. Using Baitfish on a Float

Baitfish can be one of the most effective ways to attract predatory fish, and using them on a float adds an interesting twist to traditional bait fishing. By suspending live or cut baitfish under a float, you create a natural-looking presentation that can draw in species such as bass, pike, and walleye.

This technique works particularly well in both still and moving waters, as the floating baitfish mimic the behavior of injured or weak prey. Whether you’re fishing in lakes or rivers, using a float and baitfish is a great way to increase your chances of landing a big catch.

Why It’s Surprising

While many anglers use baitfish as a natural lure, using them on a float is a less common and highly effective technique. It’s a simple yet surprising way to catch fish, and it’s particularly useful when traditional bait doesn’t seem to be working.

These 10 uncommon fishing techniques are sure to add excitement and variety to your next fishing trip. Whether you’re looking for a new challenge or simply want to try something different, these methods will help you think outside the box and experience fishing in a whole new way. So, grab your gear, step outside, and start experimenting with these unconventional tactics – who knows what you’ll catch!
