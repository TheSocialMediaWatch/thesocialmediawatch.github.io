The Most Iconic Animated Movies of the 21st Century


Animation has always been a significant part of cinema, offering audiences fantastical worlds, emotional depth, and rich storytelling. While the 20th century established animation as a mainstream art form, the 21st century has seen it evolve into something even more groundbreaking. From the visual splendor of computer-generated imagery (CGI) to hand-drawn animation’s continued allure, the 21st century has produced some of the most iconic animated films that have captivated global audiences. These films have not only pushed the boundaries of technology but have also explored complex themes, captured cultural nuances, and provided stories that resonate with viewers of all ages.

1. Shrek (2001) – A New Era of Animation

Released in 2001, Shrek marked a significant turning point in animated cinema. Produced by DreamWorks Animation, it was a revolutionary film in both its technology and storytelling. The movie, which follows an ogre named Shrek as he embarks on a quest to rescue Princess Fiona, took the world by storm for its sharp humor, pop culture references, and its ability to appeal to both children and adults.

The film’s success wasn’t just due to its humor, but also its groundbreaking CGI animation, which was state-of-the-art for the time. The movie’s success led to multiple sequels, spinoffs, and even a Broadway adaptation. The legacy of Shrek can still be felt today, with its subversive take on fairy tales influencing many animated films that came after it.

2. Spirited Away (2001) – Hayao Miyazaki’s Masterpiece

No conversation about animated movies is complete without mentioning Hayao Miyazaki’s Spirited Away. Released by Studio Ghibli in 2001, Spirited Away is widely regarded as one of the greatest animated films ever made. The film follows Chihiro, a young girl who finds herself trapped in a magical world and must navigate its strange and sometimes dangerous inhabitants to find her way home.

Spirited Away broke cultural and linguistic boundaries, becoming the first and only animated film to win the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature. The breathtaking hand-drawn animation, combined with an emotionally rich narrative and profound themes of environmentalism, greed, and the journey to maturity, left a lasting impact on audiences worldwide. The film’s intricate world-building and attention to detail set a new standard for animated storytelling.

3. Finding Nemo (2003) – Pixar’s Underwater Adventure

Released by Pixar Animation Studios in 2003, Finding Nemo captivated audiences with its stunning underwater visuals, heartwarming story, and unforgettable characters. The film tells the story of Marlin, a clownfish who embarks on a journey across the ocean to find his son, Nemo, who has been captured by a scuba diver and placed in a fish tank.

The film was groundbreaking in its depiction of the ocean’s vibrant and diverse ecosystems, and its use of CGI technology made underwater scenes come to life like never before. Finding Nemo not only won numerous awards but also solidified Pixar’s place as one of the most innovative animation studios in the world. The movie’s success was followed by a beloved sequel, Finding Dory, further cementing its cultural impact.

4. The Incredibles (2004) – A Superhero Family Adventure

The superhero genre has taken over Hollywood in recent years, but it was Pixar’s The Incredibles (2004) that helped lay the groundwork for animated superhero films. Directed by Brad Bird, The Incredibles follows the Parr family, a group of superheroes trying to live a normal life while secretly fighting crime.

What makes The Incredibles so iconic is its perfect blend of superhero action with family dynamics. It’s a film that appeals to both children and adults, addressing themes of identity, power, and the importance of family. The movie’s sleek animation style, unique character designs, and smart writing make it a standout in Pixar’s catalog. The film’s success led to a long-awaited sequel, Incredibles 2 (2018), which continued to explore these themes while bringing the superhero family back to the big screen.

5. Up (2009) – An Emotional Journey

Another triumph for Pixar, Up (2009) is one of the most emotionally resonant animated films of the 21st century. The film follows Carl Fredricksen, an elderly man who ties thousands of balloons to his house and sets off to fulfill a lifelong dream of visiting South America. Along the way, he is joined by a young boy named Russell, leading to an adventure full of heartwarming moments.

The opening sequence, which shows Carl and his late wife Ellie’s life together, is one of the most poignant scenes in animation history. Up successfully blends humor, adventure, and deep emotional depth, making it a favorite among both children and adults. Its unique visual style, coupled with a powerful story, earned it several Academy Award nominations, and it remains one of Pixar’s most beloved films.

6. Frozen (2013) – Disney’s Snowy Phenomenon

In 2013, Disney brought the magical world of Frozen to life, and the movie quickly became a cultural phenomenon. The film, loosely based on Hans Christian Andersen’s fairy tale The Snow Queen, follows two sisters, Elsa and Anna, as they navigate Elsa’s magical ice powers and the challenges of their sibling relationship.

Frozen revolutionized the Disney princess narrative, focusing not on romance but on the bond between the two sisters. The film’s musical numbers, especially the Oscar-winning “Let It Go,” became instantly iconic, and the characters, particularly Elsa, resonated deeply with audiences. The film’s success led to a sequel, Frozen II (2019), which continued the story of Elsa and Anna and delved deeper into the origins of Elsa’s powers.

7. Coco (2017) – A Celebration of Family and Culture

Pixar’s Coco (2017) is a deeply moving exploration of family, memory, and the celebration of life. The film follows Miguel, a young boy with dreams of becoming a musician, as he embarks on a journey to the Land of the Dead during the Mexican holiday Día de los Muertos. There, he uncovers the truth about his family’s history and discovers the importance of remembering the past.

The movie’s stunning visuals, vibrant depiction of Mexican culture, and the universal themes of family and remembrance made it an instant classic. Coco received widespread critical acclaim and won several awards, including the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature. The film’s portrayal of Mexican traditions and its beautiful soundtrack, including the hit song “Remember Me,” contributed to its enduring popularity and cultural significance.

8. How to Train Your Dragon (2010) – A Tale of Friendship and Adventure

The How to Train Your Dragon series, beginning with the 2010 film of the same name, introduced audiences to a Viking boy named Hiccup and his dragon companion, Toothless. Directed by Chris Sanders and Dean DeBlois, the film is set in a world where dragons are feared enemies of the Viking people. Hiccup, however, befriends a dragon and begins to change the way his people view these creatures.

The film’s breathtaking animation, particularly the scenes of flight, is a standout. The narrative is rich with themes of friendship, bravery, and understanding, and it resonates with both younger and older audiences. The success of How to Train Your Dragon led to two sequels, with the third film, How to Train Your Dragon: The Hidden World (2019), providing a satisfying conclusion to Hiccup and Toothless’s story.

9. Zootopia (2016) – A Social Commentary Wrapped in Animation

Released by Walt Disney Animation Studios, Zootopia (2016) was more than just a typical animated film—it was a sharp social commentary on race, stereotypes, and inclusivity. The film follows Judy Hopps, the first rabbit police officer in the city of Zootopia, who teams up with a con artist fox named Nick Wilde to solve a mystery.

Zootopia was praised for its clever script, vibrant animation, and the way it addressed real-world issues in an accessible manner for younger audiences. The movie’s themes of prejudice and diversity, coupled with its strong characters and humor, made it a critical and commercial success. The film won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature and cemented its place as one of the most important animated films of the 21st century.

10. The Lion King (2019) – A Groundbreaking Live-Action Remake

While not a completely traditional animated film, Disney’s 2019 live-action remake of The Lion King deserves mention. The film used cutting-edge CGI to create photorealistic versions of the original characters, setting a new benchmark for animation technology. Although it sparked some debate regarding the necessity of a remake, The Lion King (2019) still garnered significant attention for its technological achievements and stunning visual effects.

The film’s story remained largely faithful to the original 1994 animated classic, while its breathtaking animation and enhanced music sequences, particularly Beyoncé’s rendition of “Can You Feel the Love Tonight,” resonated with both new and nostalgic audiences.

Conclusion

The 21st century has been an extraordinary time for animated films, with studios pushing the boundaries of storytelling and technology to create some truly unforgettable cinematic experiences. From the iconic ogre of Shrek to the cultural richness of Coco, these films have not only entertained but also touched the hearts of audiences worldwide. Whether through visually stunning worlds, powerful messages, or emotional storytelling, these animated movies have earned their place as some of the most iconic films of the century. As technology continues to evolve, it will be exciting to see where the next wave of animated films will take us.
