10 Easy DIY Crafts to Make This Weekend


Looking for creative ways to spend your weekend? Whether you want to spruce up your living space, create thoughtful gifts, or just enjoy some hands-on fun, DIY crafts are the perfect outlet. These ten simple projects combine creativity with practicality, designed for all skill levels, requiring only basic materials and tools you likely have around. Get ready to transform ordinary items into something special with these crafts you can whip up over the weekend.

1. Painted Mason Jar Organizers

Mason jars aren’t just for preserves—they make fantastic, chic organizers! With a little paint, you can customize them to fit your décor style and organize anything from office supplies to bathroom essentials.

Materials Needed:

  • Mason jars (any size)
  • Acrylic paint or spray paint
  • Paintbrush or sponge
  • Painter’s tape (optional)
  • Clear sealant spray
  • Sandpaper (optional)

How to Make:

  1. Clean the jars thoroughly and let them dry.
  2. Lightly sand the jars if you want the paint to stick better.
  3. Tape off any areas if you want striped patterns or designs.
  4. Paint the jars with your chosen color. Two coats usually provide good coverage.
  5. Let the paint dry completely, then spray with a clear sealant to preserve the finish.
  6. Arrange your jars on a shelf or desk and fill with pens, brushes, cotton balls, or other small essentials.

This project is simple but versatile. Try pastel colors for a soft, farmhouse look or metallic tones for a modern twist.

2. Hand-Painted Tote Bags

Make your shopping trips more fun and eco-friendly by designing your own tote bag! With fabric paint and a few stencils or your own doodles, the possibilities are endless.

Materials Needed:

  • Plain cotton tote bag
  • Fabric paint or permanent markers
  • Brushes or sponges
  • Cardboard to place inside the bag
  • Stencils (optional)

How to Make:

  1. Place the cardboard inside the tote to prevent paint bleed.
  2. Sketch your design lightly with pencil or use stencils.
  3. Fill in with fabric paint or markers.
  4. Allow to dry fully, preferably overnight.
  5. Heat set if required by paint instructions (usually by ironing).

This craft is an excellent way to showcase your personality or make a memorable handmade gift.

3. DIY Scented Candles

Candles add ambiance and a fresh scent to any room. Making your own lets you control the fragrance and style.

Materials Needed:

  • Soy wax flakes or beeswax
  • Candle wicks
  • Essential oils (lavender, vanilla, eucalyptus, etc.)
  • Heat-safe containers (jars, tins)
  • Double boiler or microwave-safe bowl
  • Thermometer
  • Stirring utensil

How to Make:

  1. Melt wax in a double boiler until it reaches approximately 180°F.
  2. Remove from heat and add essential oils (about 10-15 drops per 1 cup of wax).
  3. Place the wick in the container and pour the wax carefully.
  4. Allow the candle to cool and harden completely.
  5. Trim wick to about 1/4 inch before lighting.

Making candles is relaxing and customizable. Try mixing scents to create your own signature aroma.

4. Decorative Wooden Coasters

Protect your furniture with style by crafting your own wooden coasters. This project lets you incorporate different painting or engraving techniques for a personalized touch.

Materials Needed:

  • Wooden discs or squares (available at craft stores)
  • Sandpaper
  • Acrylic paint or wood stain
  • Paintbrushes
  • Clear varnish or sealant

How to Make:

  1. Sand surfaces smooth.
  2. Paint or stain the wood. You could use geometric shapes, floral designs, or leave them natural with a glossy finish.
  3. Let dry completely.
  4. Apply sealant to prevent moisture damage.

These coasters are functional and easy to make in multiples, perfect for hosting or gifting.

5. Macrame Plant Hangers

Bring some greenery into your home with a handmade macrame plant hanger. Despite the intricate look, it’s straightforward once you learn a few knots.

Materials Needed:

  • Macrame cord or thick cotton rope
  • Scissors
  • Metal or wooden ring
  • Small potted plant

How to Make:

  1. Cut lengths of cord (e.g., four pieces at about 4-5 feet each).
  2. Attach cords to the ring using a Lark’s Head Knot.
  3. Learn basic knots like square knots and spiral stitches.
  4. Create a pattern along the length of the cords.
  5. Finish with a gathered knot at the bottom to cradle the pot.

Macrame adds a boho-chic flair and is a lovely way to display your favorite plants.

6. Personalized Photo Frames

Add a personal touch to your photos by decorating a basic photo frame. This project is fun for all ages and a great way to recycle old frames.

Materials Needed:

  • Plain photo frames
  • Hot glue gun
  • Decorations like shells, buttons, beads, fabric scraps, or artificial flowers
  • Paint (optional)

How to Make:

  1. Paint the frame if you want to refresh its color.
  2. Arrange decorations around the border before gluing.
  3. Use hot glue to attach your chosen embellishments.
  4. Let dry and insert your favorite photograph.

The tactile process of decorating these frames makes them sentimental and unique.

7. DIY Bath Bombs

Create spa-worthy bath bombs with soothing scents and beautiful colors. This craft is surprisingly simple and makes delightful gifts.

Materials Needed:

  • Baking soda (1 cup)
  • Citric acid (1/2 cup)
  • Cornstarch (1/2 cup)
  • Epsom salt (1/2 cup)
  • Water (3 tablespoons)
  • Essential oils (about 20 drops)
  • Food coloring (optional)
  • Silicone molds or ice cube trays

How to Make:

  1. Mix dry ingredients together.
  2. In a separate bowl, combine water, essential oils, and coloring.
  3. Slowly add wet mix to dry mix, stirring quickly to avoid fizzing.
  4. Press mixture firmly into molds.
  5. Let dry for 24-48 hours before removing.

Bath bombs fizz beautifully and release calming fragrances, perfect for relaxation.

8. Upcycled T-Shirt Tote Bags

Give old t-shirts new life by turning them into no-sew tote bags. This is an excellent way to recycle while making practical carriers.

Materials Needed:

  • Old cotton t-shirt
  • Scissors

How to Make:

  1. Lay the t-shirt flat.
  2. Cut off the sleeves and neckline to create handles.
  3. Cut fringes along the bottom; tie corresponding front and back fringes in knots to close the bag’s bottom.
  4. Turn inside out.

This project is fast, frugal, and eco-conscious.

9. Rustic Pallet Shelves

Add rustic charm to your walls with simple pallet wood shelves. They’re inexpensive, durable, and a great weekend wood project.

Materials Needed:

  • Pallet wood planks (cleaned and sanded)
  • Screws or nails
  • Wall brackets or mounting hardware
  • Wood stain or paint (optional)
  • Drill or hammer

How to Make:

  1. Cut pallet wood to desired shelf lengths.
  2. Sand smooth to avoid splinters.
  3. Stain or paint if desired.
  4. Attach brackets to wall.
  5. Secure wood planks onto brackets.

These shelves can hold books, plants, or decorative items, adding character to any room.

10. Fabric-Covered Notebooks

Customize blank notebooks with fabric covers for a creative and tactile feel.

Materials Needed:

  • Plain notebooks or journals
  • Fabric scraps
  • Scissors
  • Mod Podge or fabric glue
  • Paintbrush

How to Make:

  1. Cut fabric slightly larger than the notebook cover.
  2. Brush glue evenly on the front cover.
  3. Carefully place fabric and smooth out wrinkles.
  4. Fold fabric edges inside cover and glue down.
  5. Let dry completely.

This quick craft adds personality and makes great gifts or personal journaling accessories.

Final Thoughts

Crafting is much more than just a pastime — it’s a way to express creativity, unwind, and produce something tangible and beautiful. These ten easy DIY projects provide a variety of creative outlets, whether you like painting, sewing, woodworking, or just playing with colors and textures. Spend your weekend immersed in making, and you’ll feel accomplished and refreshed. Plus, these handmade creations bring a personal touch to your home or gifts that can’t be bought in any store.

Happy crafting!
