How to Grow Your Own Herb Garden Indoors


Herbs can significantly enhance the flavor and aroma of your meals, and they are incredibly easy to grow indoors. They are also incredibly therapeutic, adding a touch of greenery and natural beauty to your space. Here, we’ll be sharing our top tips for growing your own indoor herb garden.

Select the Right Herbs

Not all herbs are suitable for indoor gardening. When selecting which plants to grow, consider factors such as their light requirements, growth rate, and the anticipated growth of the plant. Some herbs, like basil and parsley, are more suitable for indoor growing, while others like cilantro and dill can be challenging.

Find the Perfect Location

Place your herbs near a sunny window or a skylight to ensure they receive the appropriate amount of light. Herbs thrive on at least five hours of sunlight each day. Rotate your pots every few weeks if your space receives uneven light.

Use the Right Soil

Choose a soil with excellent drainage and nutrients perfect for potted plants. You can make your own mix or buy one suitable for herbs. If you opt for store-bought, be aware that not all mixes are created equal - some heavily weighted towards flowers and vegetables, which may not be the best for your culinary herbs.

Get the Right Containers

When choosing pots, think about their size, drainage, and material. Some herbs grow better in shallow pots, and others need more room to stretch out. Ensure pots have drainage holes to prevent waterlogging, and select a material that allows for adequate air circulation.

Water Wisely

Water your indoor herbs evenly but sparingly. Herbs do not want to sit in damp soil, so ensure it dries out slightly between waterings. The trick is to keep the soil evenly moist but never soggy.

Fertilize Appropriately

Herbs require extra nutrition as they grow. Use a balanced fertilizer – ideally organic – when your herbs first start to grow. As they mature, they will require less fertilizer.

Prune Regularly

Regularly trim your herbs to promote bushier growth. It’s also essential to pinch back young plants to encourage branching. Make sure to remove any dead leaves or flowers to help your herbs thrive even longer.

Watch Out for Pests and Disease

Pests and disease can quickly ruin your indoor herb garden, especially if you are growing them indoors year-round. Ahead of time, be aware of the common pests and diseases that affect herbs. A regular cleaning schedule can prevent many infestations and infections.

Top Tips

  1. Succession Planting: Not all herbs grow at the same rate. Some herbs take weeks to mature, while others may take months. Succession planting is the act of starting new plants when the old ones start showing signs of wear. This method ensures that you always have a fresh, continually producing herb garden.

  2. Natural Pest Control: For a non-toxic, organic solution for pests, consider giving your plants a gentle spray of neem oil or insecticidal soap.

Enjoy your very own indoor herb garden, and get ready to elevate your culinary games with fresh, homegrown herbs!
