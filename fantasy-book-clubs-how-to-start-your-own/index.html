Fantasy Book Clubs: How to Start Your Own


Building a fantasy book club can be an incredibly rewarding experience for anyone passionate about exploring magical realms, delving into epic tales, and bonding with like-minded individuals. Whether you’re an avid reader enchanted by sword and sorcery, or someone just starting to discover the depths of fantastical worlds, creating your own book club provides a chance to form meaningful connections and share insights that amplify the joy of reading.

With the right framework, you can gather a group that not only appreciates the genre but also fosters lively discussions, encourages diverse perspectives, and sparks curiosity. Below is a step-by-step guide for starting your own fantasy book club and turning it into an unforgettable adventure.

Clarify Your Vision

Before jumping into the logistics, take a moment to shape your book club’s vision. Think about the kind of experience you want to create. Will your club focus on traditional medieval fantasy, urban fantasy, or perhaps explore subgenres like grimdark, steampunk, or magical realism? Are you catering to a tight-knit friend group, or are you aiming to reach a broader audience, such as local readers or an online community?

Defining your club’s core interest and objectives will help guide your decisions moving forward and attract the right mix of members. For example, if your goal is to introduce readers to lesser-known authors, you may want to mix popular bestsellers with hidden gems.

Set Up the Foundation

Starting a fantasy book club requires a few basic decisions to lay a solid foundation. Here’s where to begin:

Choose the Format

Decide whether you want your book club to meet in person, virtually, or adopt a hybrid approach. In-person meetings work well for fostering a sense of community, while virtual meetings allow members to join from anywhere, making it easier for readers who share niche interests to connect. Tools like Zoom, Discord, and specialized forums can make virtual discussions seamless.

Determine the Frequency

Establish how often the group will meet. Fantasy books, especially epic sagas, can be more dense than other genres, so a monthly or bi-monthly meeting schedule often works best. This gives members plenty of time to complete reading assignments while balancing other commitments.

Pick a Meeting Space

For in-person gatherings, you may choose to host meetings at a local café, library, bookstore, or even your home. Online clubs can use video conferencing platforms, discussion threads, or group chats.

Invite Members to Join

Once you’ve drafted the blueprint for your book club, it’s time to find members who share your passion for fantasy. Here are a few ideas to help form your community:

Tap into Existing Networks

Start by reaching out to friends, family, colleagues, or classmates who might share your interest in fantasy literature. Personal invitations go a long way in ensuring that your club starts with committed participants.

Leverage Social Media

Platforms like Facebook Groups, Instagram, Twitter, and Reddit are great tools for recruiting fellow fantasy enthusiasts. Just make sure to clearly promote the type of books your group will focus on. Use hashtags or join genre-related forums where readers are actively looking for communities to join.

Collaborate with Local Organizations

Libraries, independent bookstores, and community centers often support book clubs and may help you advertise or recruit members. Some spaces may even host gatherings free of charge.

Curate a Captivating Reading List

Selecting the right books is perhaps the most fun and crucial part of starting a fantasy book club. A diverse reading list will keep members engaged while exploring the genre’s vast landscape. Here are some tips for curating your list:

Start with Crowd-Pleasers

Begin with well-known and universally loved fantasy titles to generate excitement. For example, classics like The Hobbit by J.R.R. Tolkien or The Name of the Wind by Patrick Rothfuss work wonders in getting members invested in the club.

Incorporate Variety

Make sure to spotlight both established authors and lesser-known talents. Include books that cater to varied subgenres—epic fantasy, magical realism, dark fairy tales, or hybrid genres. Mix standalone novels with series installments to maintain variety.

Balance Length and Complexity

While fantasy readers love sprawling epics, consider alternating dense novels with shorter or lighter reads to avoid burnout. For example, pair intricate sagas like Mistborn by Brandon Sanderson with engaging novellas such as A Wizard of Earthsea by Ursula K. Le Guin.

Encourage Member Input

Let your members help shape the reading list. Conduct polls or votes to choose future selections, ensuring everyone feels involved in the decision-making process.

Plan Engaging Meetings

A well-run meeting is the heart of any successful book club. Here’s how to make yours dynamic and enjoyable:

Prepare Thoughtful Discussion Questions

Come to each meeting armed with questions that spark conversation. For example:

  • How do the protagonists compare to traditional fantasy archetypes?
  • Which world-building elements stood out to you the most?
  • Did the magic system feel innovative or familiar?

Create Space for Diverse Opinions

Encourage members to share their honest reactions—even if they didn’t enjoy the book. Sometimes critical takes lead to the most stimulating discussions.

Dive Deeper into Themes and Context

Fantasy literature often explores universal themes like heroism, identity, and good versus evil. Discuss how these themes interplay with the plot and characters. If possible, supplement the conversation with historical context, such as how the author’s experiences may have influenced the writing.

Add Unique Touches to Your Club

To make your fantasy book club truly stand out, consider integrating creative elements that provide memorable experiences for members:

Host Themed Events

Celebrate major milestones with themed meetings based on the book you’re reading. This might include dressing up as characters, crafting magical artifacts, or indulging in snacks inspired by the book’s setting.

Create a Shared Journal

Keep a club journal where members can jot down their thoughts, favorite quotes, or predictions as they read. Over time, this document becomes a treasure chest of collective insights.

Connect with Authors

Some contemporary authors actively engage with fans, hosting virtual Q&A sessions or accepting invitations to book clubs. Reaching out to writers of your chosen novels could lead to meaningful experiences.

Run Side Challenges

Challenge your members to write short stories inspired by the current book or participate in fantasy trivia nights. Activities like this keep energy levels high and cement membership loyalty.

Foster a Sense of Community

Building a lasting fantasy book club goes beyond reading books together; it’s about forging a community where members feel valued. Here’s how to nurture connections:

Keep Communication Open

Use a central communication channel for updates, meeting reminders, and casual conversations. Group chats on platforms like WhatsApp or Discord work well.

Celebrate Milestones

Honor achievements like completing a particularly long book or hitting the club’s anniversary. Showing appreciation for members’ dedication strengthens bonds.

Be Flexible and Inclusive

Allow members the freedom to join discussions according to their comfort levels. Some may prefer quiet observation rather than leading the conversation, and that’s okay.

Growing and Evolving

As your book club gains momentum, revisit your initial vision periodically and make adjustments. Introduce new activities, expand the reading list, or switch up the format if needed. Member feedback can be invaluable for identifying opportunities to improve.

Additionally, consider growing your club’s influence by creating online content like reviews, blog posts, or fan art inspired by the books you read. This not only attracts more members but also engages your existing audience in deeper ways.

Wrapping Up

Starting a fantasy book club offers countless opportunities for joy, growth, and exploration. By planning carefully, curating an exciting reading list, and fostering a sense of community, you can create a space where magical storytelling flourishes and cherished friendships take root. Whether your club meets to traverse Tolkien’s Middle-earth or unravel mysteries in Gaiman’s mythical realms, your group will undoubtedly find endless enchantment together. So take that first step, and let the adventure begin!
