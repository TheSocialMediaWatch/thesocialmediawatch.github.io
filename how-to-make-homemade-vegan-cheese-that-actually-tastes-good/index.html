How to Make Homemade Vegan Cheese That Actually Tastes Good


Vegan cheese has come a long way in recent years, offering a flavorful, creamy, and dairy-free alternative to traditional cheese. Whether you’re lactose intolerant, following a plant-based diet, or simply curious to try something new, making your own vegan cheese can be a rewarding and surprisingly simple experience. The best part? When done right, homemade vegan cheese can taste just as good—if not better—than its dairy counterpart.

In this guide, we will explore various methods for making vegan cheese at home, from the basics to more advanced techniques. We’ll cover the best ingredients, tools, and step-by-step instructions for creating cheeses with complex flavors, creamy textures, and the kind of depth that will satisfy even the most discerning palate. By the end, you’ll be equipped with the knowledge to make vegan cheese that you can use in a variety of dishes—whether you’re spreading it on crackers, melting it on a pizza, or incorporating it into sauces and dips.

1. Understanding the Basics of Vegan Cheese

Before jumping into the process of making vegan cheese, it’s essential to understand the primary ingredients that will form the foundation of your cheese. Unlike dairy cheese, vegan cheese doesn’t contain any milk or animal byproducts. Instead, it’s made from plant-based ingredients that can mimic the taste, texture, and meltability of traditional cheese.

Key Ingredients in Vegan Cheese

  • Nuts: Cashews and almonds are the most commonly used nuts in vegan cheese. They provide a creamy base when blended and can be seasoned to produce a wide variety of flavors. Cashews are particularly known for their rich, smooth texture when blended.

  • Nutritional Yeast: This ingredient is often described as the “secret weapon” of vegan cooking. It has a cheesy, nutty flavor that can enhance the overall taste of your vegan cheese. Nutritional yeast is a staple in many vegan cheeses for creating that familiar cheesy umami flavor.

  • Plant-Based Milks: Coconut milk, almond milk, or oat milk serve as the liquid base in many vegan cheese recipes. The type of milk you choose will affect the flavor and consistency of the final product.

  • Agar-Agar: Agar is a gelatinous substance derived from seaweed. It’s commonly used to help vegan cheese set, providing structure and firmness to cheeses like mozzarella and cheddar.

  • Lemon Juice and Vinegar: Acidic ingredients such as lemon juice or apple cider vinegar are used to mimic the tanginess that’s naturally present in dairy cheese.

  • Probiotics or Fermented Foods: For certain types of vegan cheese, you can incorporate probiotics or fermented foods (such as miso or sauerkraut) to replicate the tangy, aged flavor that develops in dairy cheeses.

Texture and Flavor

The texture of vegan cheese depends on the type of cheese you’re making. Soft cheeses like cream cheese or ricotta require less setting time and more creaminess, while harder cheeses like cheddar or gouda need time to firm up. Achieving the right consistency often involves balancing the right ratio of liquid, fat, and setting agents.

Flavor, on the other hand, is achieved through a combination of ingredients like nutritional yeast, miso, garlic, onion powder, and smoked paprika. These ingredients work together to mimic the savory, umami-rich flavors that cheese lovers adore.

2. Tools You’ll Need

Making homemade vegan cheese doesn’t require many specialized tools. However, there are a few kitchen essentials that will make the process easier:

  • Blender or Food Processor: A high-speed blender or food processor is essential for creating a smooth, creamy texture in your cheese. This is particularly important when using nuts like cashews, as they need to be blended until completely smooth.

  • Cheesecloth or Nut Milk Bag: For straining, you’ll need a piece of cheesecloth or a nut milk bag, especially if you’re making a soft cheese or cheese spread.

  • Saucepan: If your recipe involves heating the cheese mixture, a saucepan will be necessary to heat and dissolve agar-agar or thicken the sauce.

  • Molds or Containers: For cheeses that need to set or firm up, having a mold or a small container to shape your cheese is helpful. A silicone mold works best for shaping vegan cheese.

  • Spatula and Whisk: These tools will help with stirring and ensuring the consistency is just right.

3. How to Make Cashew-Based Vegan Cheese

One of the most popular types of vegan cheese is cashew cheese, known for its smooth and creamy texture. Cashews are rich in fats, which provide the creaminess that dairy cheese is known for. Here’s a simple recipe to get started:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw cashews (soaked for 4 hours or overnight)
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 cup water (or more for desired consistency)
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Cashews: Drain and rinse the soaked cashews. This softens them and helps achieve a smoother texture when blended.

  2. Blend the Ingredients: Add the cashews, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, apple cider vinegar, garlic powder, and salt to your blender or food processor. Begin blending on low speed and gradually increase to high. Add water a little at a time until the mixture reaches a smooth, creamy consistency.

  3. Taste and Adjust: Taste the cheese and adjust the seasoning. You can add more salt, nutritional yeast, or lemon juice depending on your preference. If you want a sharper, tangier flavor, a little extra vinegar will do the trick.

  4. Chill and Serve: For a softer cheese, you can serve the mixture immediately. However, for a firmer cheese, transfer the mixture to a mold and refrigerate for at least 2 hours to allow it to set.

This cashew-based cheese is perfect for spreading on crackers, mixing into pasta, or using as a dip. It’s versatile and can be modified by adding herbs, spices, or even truffle oil for an elevated taste.

4. How to Make Vegan Mozzarella

If you’re craving a melty, stretchy cheese that can go on pizzas or in baked dishes, vegan mozzarella is the way to go. This recipe uses agar-agar to help the cheese set and melt properly.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup raw cashews (soaked)
  • 1/4 cup coconut milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1 tbsp nutritional yeast
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp tapioca starch (for meltability)
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp onion powder
  • 1 tbsp agar-agar powder
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Cashews: Soak the cashews for 4 hours or overnight, then drain and rinse.

  2. Combine Ingredients: In a blender, combine the soaked cashews, coconut milk, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, garlic powder, onion powder, and salt. Blend until smooth.

  3. Make the Agar-Agar Mixture: In a saucepan, combine the agar-agar and water. Bring to a boil and simmer for about 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until the agar-agar dissolves.

  4. Mix the Agar with Cashew Blend: Slowly pour the agar-agar mixture into the blender with the cashew mixture, and add the tapioca starch. Blend again until smooth.

  5. Cook the Mixture: Pour the entire mixture back into the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly until the cheese thickens and becomes stretchy (about 5 minutes).

  6. Cool and Set: Transfer the mozzarella to a mold or container and let it cool to room temperature. Once it has set, refrigerate for at least 2 hours before using.

This vegan mozzarella is perfect for pizza, lasagna, or melted into sandwiches. It has the right texture and flavor, making it an excellent alternative to traditional mozzarella.

5. Tips for Perfecting Vegan Cheese

While making vegan cheese can be easy and fun, there are a few tips that can elevate the flavor and texture of your creations:

  • Soak Your Nuts: Soaking nuts like cashews or almonds before blending helps soften them, making it easier to achieve a smooth, creamy texture.

  • Use Agar-Agar Carefully: If you’re using agar-agar, make sure to follow the instructions carefully. Too little can result in a runny cheese, while too much can make it too firm.

  • Experiment with Seasonings: Vegan cheese can be flavored with a variety of herbs and spices. Experiment with smoked paprika, cumin, chili flakes, or fresh herbs to create unique flavors.

  • Allow Time for Aging: Some vegan cheeses, like those made with probiotics or fermented foods, benefit from aging. Store your cheese in the fridge for a few days to allow the flavors to develop.

  • Play with Texture: If you prefer a firmer, more sliceable cheese, reduce the amount of liquid in your recipe. For creamier cheeses, increase the amount of liquid and use more blended nuts.

6. Conclusion

Making homemade vegan cheese that tastes just as good as, if not better than, store-bought options is absolutely achievable. With the right ingredients and techniques, you can create a variety of delicious, creamy, and flavorful cheeses that will impress your friends and family. From cashew-based cheeses to meltable mozzarella, the possibilities are endless.

Whether you’re vegan, lactose intolerant, or simply looking for a new culinary adventure, making your own vegan cheese is a fun and rewarding experience. The best part is that you can customize your cheeses to suit your taste preferences, ensuring that every batch is as delicious as the last. So go ahead, get creative, and enjoy the wonderful world of homemade vegan cheese!
