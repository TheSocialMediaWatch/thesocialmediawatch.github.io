The Best Fantasy Novels Featuring LGBTQ+ Characters


Fantasy literature has long been a realm of escapism, adventure, and magic, where readers can immerse themselves in worlds of dragons, enchanted forests, and epic battles. However, in recent years, the genre has seen a growing trend of inclusivity, with LGBTQ+ characters becoming more prominently featured in fantastical stories. These characters are no longer confined to background roles or stereotypical portrayals but are now integral to the plot, driving their own journeys and facing challenges of their own.

This shift has brought a new depth and richness to fantasy novels, making them more relatable and diverse. If you’re looking for a collection of fantasy books that not only provide thrilling adventures but also celebrate the diversity of love, identity, and belonging, then you’re in the right place. Here’s a list of some of the best fantasy novels featuring LGBTQ+ characters, each offering a unique perspective and a compelling story.

1. The Priory of the Orange Tree by Samantha Shannon

Samantha Shannon’s The Priory of the Orange Tree is an epic fantasy that takes readers on an unforgettable journey filled with dragons, powerful women, and complex relationships. At the heart of this vast and immersive world lies a beautiful representation of LGBTQ+ characters, particularly in the form of the love story between two women, Tané and Ead. Their romance is deeply woven into the larger plot and plays a crucial role in the novel’s progression.

Set in a world divided by politics, dragons, and ancient magics, The Priory of the Orange Tree features a fully realized society where LGBTQ+ relationships are not only acknowledged but celebrated. The story’s characters navigate their identities in a world where power dynamics and loyalty are constantly tested. Shannon’s world-building is rich and imaginative, and her LGBTQ+ representation adds an authentic layer of depth to the novel, making it a standout in modern fantasy.

2. The House in the Cerulean Sea by TJ Klune

One of the most heartwarming and charming fantasy novels of recent years, The House in the Cerulean Sea by TJ Klune is a feel-good story about love, acceptance, and finding family in unexpected places. The novel follows Linus Baker, a by-the-book government worker, who is sent to investigate an orphanage housing magical children. Among the children is a young boy with the ability to summon the end of the world, and Linus is sent to determine whether the orphanage should remain open.

As Linus spends time at the orphanage, he grows close to the enigmatic caretaker, Arthur Parnassus, and their relationship blossoms into a beautiful romance. The House in the Cerulean Sea is a celebration of found families, the importance of love in all its forms, and the idea that true belonging transcends societal expectations. Klune’s portrayal of LGBTQ+ love is tender and authentic, offering a refreshing and inclusive take on fantasy.

3. Witchmark by C.L. Polk

C.L. Polk’s Witchmark is a steampunk-inspired fantasy novel that blends mystery, romance, and magic with a rich narrative about class, politics, and identity. The novel’s protagonist, Miles Singer, is a war veteran with a mysterious past and a powerful magical secret. As he uncovers a plot that threatens the stability of his world, he also begins a romantic relationship with the charming doctor, Tristan.

Witchmark features a subtle yet meaningful exploration of LGBTQ+ relationships, with its focus on the slow-burning romance between Miles and Tristan. The novel also explores themes of self-discovery, personal freedom, and the courage to be true to oneself, making it a powerful read for anyone looking for an engaging fantasy with LGBTQ+ representation.

4. A Song of Wraiths and Ruin by Roseanne A. Brown

A Song of Wraiths and Ruin is a debut fantasy novel by Roseanne A. Brown that introduces readers to a world inspired by West African mythology. The novel follows two main characters: Karina, a princess who seeks to resurrect her mother, and Malik, a young man who is on a mission to avenge his sister’s death. Their paths cross in a deadly game that involves magic, politics, and love.

Among the many facets of this novel, one of the most notable is its LGBTQ+ representation. Karina, a powerful and determined character, is shown to have romantic feelings for both men and women, adding an important layer of nuance to her journey. This inclusive portrayal of love and identity makes the novel feel more relevant and reflective of the real world, while still maintaining the enchanting qualities of traditional fantasy.

5. The Bone Shard Daughter by Andrea Stewart

The Bone Shard Daughter by Andrea Stewart is a high fantasy novel set in a world where magic is powered by the bone shards of the deceased. The protagonist, Lin, is the daughter of an emperor who has been kept in the dark about her father’s brutal and mysterious rule. As Lin begins to uncover the secrets of her father’s empire and her own powers, she discovers a complicated web of relationships, politics, and hidden truths.

One of the most important and refreshing aspects of The Bone Shard Daughter is its LGBTQ+ representation. The novel features a beautifully written romance between two women, Lin and a character named Jovis, whose relationship evolves over the course of the story. Stewart’s nuanced approach to LGBTQ+ relationships makes their romance feel organic and authentic, enhancing the emotional depth of the novel while maintaining its fast-paced and action-packed plot.

6. Cemetery Boys by Aiden Thomas

Cemetery Boys by Aiden Thomas is a supernatural fantasy that explores themes of family, identity, and love, all set against a backdrop of Latinx culture and traditions. The novel follows Yadriel, a young trans boy who is trying to prove his abilities as a brujo (a Latinx witch). In an attempt to gain his family’s acceptance, Yadriel accidentally summons the ghost of Julian Diaz, a mischievous and defiant spirit who has unfinished business.

As Yadriel and Julian’s relationship deepens, Cemetery Boys explores the intersection of queer identity, culture, and spirituality. The novel does an excellent job of representing the struggles that come with being a transgender person in a conservative family while also celebrating the joy and beauty of discovering love. Thomas’ portrayal of a trans protagonist falling in love with another boy is heartwarming and poignant, making it a standout in the fantasy genre.

7. The Long Way to a Small, Angry Planet by Becky Chambers

Becky Chambers’ The Long Way to a Small, Angry Planet is a character-driven space opera that offers a refreshing and inclusive take on the genre. While the novel is set in space, it’s the relationships and the deep exploration of identity that truly shine. The story follows a diverse crew aboard a spaceship as they navigate their personal conflicts, relationships, and the challenges of their interstellar journey.

What sets this novel apart is its portrayal of LGBTQ+ characters. In particular, the relationship between two of the crew members, Sissix and Ashby, is deeply moving and well-developed. The inclusion of different sexual orientations and gender identities in a futuristic setting helps to challenge traditional science fiction tropes and bring an added sense of realism and inclusivity to the story. Chambers’ gentle, yet impactful, exploration of queer love in this intergalactic world is both thought-provoking and heartwarming.

8. The Gilded Wolves by Roshani Chokshi

Roshani Chokshi’s The Gilded Wolves is a historical fantasy novel set in 19th-century Paris, combining elements of heist, magic, and adventure. The story follows a group of outcasts who are tasked with retrieving a powerful artifact, and their relationships with each other form the emotional core of the novel. Among these characters is Enrique, who is portrayed as a gay man, struggling with his identity and place in a world that doesn’t always accept him.

Chokshi’s writing captures the complexity of Enrique’s journey, as he grapples with his love for another character, and the challenges of existing in a society that doesn’t fully embrace his identity. This beautifully woven tale of friendship, loyalty, and self-discovery has earned praise for its LGBTQ+ representation and its vibrant world-building, making it a standout in fantasy literature.

9. Kingdom of the Wicked by Kerri Maniscalco

Kerri Maniscalco’s Kingdom of the Wicked is a dark and atmospheric fantasy novel set in a world of demons, witches, and intrigue. The story follows Emilia, a young witch who seeks to uncover the truth behind her twin sister’s murder. Along the way, she forms an unlikely alliance with a demon prince named Wrath, and a romance develops between them.

While the primary focus of the story is on Emilia’s quest for justice, the novel also features significant LGBTQ+ representation. A side character, the demon prince’s brother, has a romantic relationship with another male character, and their story adds emotional depth to the narrative. The chemistry between Emilia and Wrath, as well as the strong LGBTQ+ representation, makes this book a compelling addition to the genre.

10. An Unkindness of Ghosts by Rivers Solomon

Rivers Solomon’s An Unkindness of Ghosts is a deeply imaginative and thought-provoking science fiction novel that explores themes of race, class, and gender aboard a spaceship traveling through a post-apocalyptic world. The protagonist, Aster, is a brilliant and resourceful young woman who lives in the lower decks of the ship, facing discrimination and hardship due to her race and gender.

As the story unfolds, Aster’s journey to uncover the truth behind her mother’s death and to confront the oppressive systems aboard the ship intersects with her romantic feelings for another woman. The novel’s LGBTQ+ representation is not only integral to the character’s development but also serves as a powerful critique of societal structures, making it a deeply relevant and impactful read.

These ten novels showcase the immense diversity and richness that LGBTQ+ characters bring to the fantasy genre. Whether you’re looking for epic quests, magical worlds, or heartfelt romances, these books offer something for every reader. With their complex characters and inclusive storytelling, they’re proof that fantasy can be both thrilling and diverse, creating worlds where everyone can find their place.
