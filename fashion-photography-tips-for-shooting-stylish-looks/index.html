Fashion Photography: Tips for Shooting Stylish Looks


Fashion photography is an art that goes beyond capturing the human form. It involves creating, telling stories, and communicating. To do this, one needs more than a camera - a keen eye, creativity, and technical know-how are crucial. Here are ten tips to help you hone your fashion photography skills, capturing those stylish looks with class:

  1. Know Your Subject: Understanding fashion styles, trends, and techniques is vital. This way, you can interpret the designs for your photos effectively. Research catwalks, magazines, or study the work of renowned fashion photographers like Steven Meisel, Nick Knight, and Annie Leibowitz.

  2. Get the Lighting Right: Lighting significantly affects your photo’s mood and atmosphere. Overcast days often produce even lighting, which is suitable for most fashion photography. Elementals such as sunset lights, studio flashes, or even candlelight can dramatically enhance your images.

  3. Favor Natural Poses: Mostly, your best photos will be those least posed and most Real. In fashion photography, real expressions prove more compelling than rigid, over-staged photos. Encourage your models to relax, be themselves, and let their expressions be authentic.

  4. Understand Wardrobe: Understanding fashion is critical to producing a successful photo. Learn how garments fit, drape, or hang on the body. This will help you in orchestrating subject and wardrobe for the perfect picture. Also, don’t hesitate to ask questions - it gives the team a pointer on where they can shine.

  5. Backdrops: Don’t Overlook Them: Just as a show’s stage setting adds to the plot, your backdrop can elevate your photo. It should, in one way or another, relate to the model’s clothing or theme. A subtle urban or nature backdrop can contrast beautifully with an avant-garde couture gown.

  6. Use the Environment: In fashion photography, be sure to use the environment as part of the picture. Look for diverse locations or backgrounds that will add interest - an old stone wall, a steely industrial area, or the vibrant, lively neighborhood. Exploit the natural elements like play with shadows, natural wind for flowy gowns, or rain for unique, deep effects.

  7. The Rule of Thirds: Remember the old painting trick - to place essentials on the points where lines intersect. These are thirds uniformly divided across the canvas. Position key elements in these grid intersections to create a more visually pleasing photo.

  8. Go Wide: Break free from the standard camera lens. Experiment with wide-angled cameras for fashion landscape photos. It can bring a unique perspective, including small details of the models’ attire.

  9. Shoot from Different Angles: Change your view dramatically to capture more of the environment. Use a high-angle above for three-quarter length shots, low-angle shots to give height, or even lay down to capture shoes or societal backgrounds.

  10. Get Clear and Creative Communication: Good communication keeps the shoot on course and ensures the team is moving in the same creative direction. Clearly share your vision, ideas, thoughts, and feedback. Be precise with the model, hair, and makeup artists, and the wardrobe team. Lastly, don’t forget your click-to-edit process, and keep editing until the last image meets the vision’s exact clarity.

Fashion photography goes beyond mere capturing. As a photographer, it’s crucial to seize the moment, translate vision to lens, portray fashion, and create mesmerizing images. Keep learning, explore, and most importantly, have fun. Whether you shoot full-time or just for fun, the world of fashion photography awaits you.
