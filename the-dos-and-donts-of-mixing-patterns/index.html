The Dos and Don'ts of Mixing Patterns


When it comes to interior design, mixing patterns can add depth, personality, and visual interest to a space. However, it can be a tricky endeavor. Done right, it can elevate a room’s aesthetic appeal, but done wrong, it can result in a chaotic and overwhelming look. To help you navigate this stylish yet challenging design choice, here are the dos and don’ts of mixing patterns to create a cohesive and harmonious space.

The Dos:

1. Start with a Neutral Base

Before diving into mixing bold patterns, begin with a neutral base. Opt for a neutral-colored sofa, walls, or rugs as a foundation. This will provide a calming backdrop that allows the patterns to stand out without overwhelming the space.

2. Vary Pattern Sizes

Mixing patterns of different scales is key to achieving a balanced look. Combine large-scale patterns with smaller ones to create visual interest and prevent the space from feeling too busy. For example, pair a large floral print with a subtle geometric pattern for a dynamic contrast.

3. Stick to a Cohesive Color Palette

Choose patterns that share a similar color palette to ensure a harmonious blend. Select colors that complement each other rather than clash. This will tie the patterns together and create a unified look throughout the room.

4. Add Solid Colors for Balance

Intersperse solid colors among your patterned pieces to provide visual relief and balance out the busyness of the patterns. Solid-colored throw pillows, curtains, or furniture pieces can act as grounding elements in a room filled with various patterns.

5. Experiment with Texture

Incorporating different textures alongside patterns can add dimension and depth to your space. Mix smooth fabrics with textured ones to create a tactile experience that enhances the visual appeal of the patterns.

The Don’ts:

1. Avoid Clashing Patterns

While mixing patterns is encouraged, be cautious of combining clashing patterns that compete for attention. Patterns with conflicting styles or themes can create visual discord and disrupt the overall harmony of the space.

2. Limit the Number of Patterns

Resist the temptation to incorporate too many patterns in one space. Overloading a room with numerous competing patterns can result in a chaotic and overwhelming environment. Instead, select a few key patterns to feature and let them shine.

3. Mind the Scale

Be mindful of the scale of the patterns you choose. Mixing patterns that are all the same size can make the space feel flat and uninspired. Aim to vary the scale of patterns for a more dynamic and visually appealing design.

4. Don’t Forget Negative Space

Negative space is just as important as the patterns themselves. Allow for breathing room between patterned elements to prevent the space from feeling overcrowded. Embrace areas of simplicity to balance out the intricate patterns.

5. Skip Matching Sets

Avoid using matching patterned sets that can make a space look dated or uninspired. Opt for mixing different patterns to create a more personalized and eclectic design that showcases your unique style and creativity.

In conclusion, mastering the art of mixing patterns requires a careful balance of color, scale, texture, and restraint. By following these dos and don’ts, you can confidently experiment with patterns to create a visually stunning and harmonious interior that reflects your individual taste and style.

Remember, mixing patterns is all about creativity and personal expression. Embrace the process, trust your instincts, and have fun with it!
