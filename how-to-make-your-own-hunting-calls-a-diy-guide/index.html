How to Make Your Own Hunting Calls: A DIY Guide


Hunting calls are essential tools for any avid hunter. They allow you to mimic the sounds of animals, drawing them in closer for a better shot. While there are many commercial options available, making your own hunting calls can be both rewarding and cost-effective. In this guide, we’ll explore various methods to create your own hunting calls, ensuring you have the perfect sound for your next hunting expedition.

Understanding the Basics of Hunting Calls

Before diving into the DIY process, it’s crucial to understand the different types of hunting calls and their purposes. Hunting calls can be categorized into two main types:

  1. Mimicry Calls: These sounds imitate the natural calls of animals. They are used to attract game by replicating their vocalizations, whether that be the call of a turkey, a deer bleat, or the quack of a duck.

  2. Distress Calls: These sounds mimic the calls of animals in distress, which can trigger predatory instincts in other animals. For example, a fawn bleating can attract a doe, while the sound of a distressed rabbit can lure in predators.

Understanding these categories will help you choose the right type of call to create based on your hunting goals.

Materials You Will Need

Creating your own hunting calls requires some basic materials. Here’s a list of items you might need, depending on the type of call you want to make:

  • Wood: Hardwood is typically preferred for its durability and sound quality. Maple, oak, or cherry are great options.
  • Plastic or Metal Tubes: These can be used for reed calls or to create a resonating chamber.
  • Reeds: You can use commercially available reeds, or you can make your own from materials like cane or thin wood.
  • Drill and Drill Bits: For creating holes in your materials.
  • Saw: To cut wood to the desired size.
  • Sandpaper: For smoothing edges and surfaces.
  • Finish or Sealant: To protect your calls from the elements.

Crafting a Basic Deer Call

Step 1: Gather Materials

For a basic deer call, you will need a piece of hardwood, a drill, and a saw. A block of wood approximately 6 inches long, 2 inches wide, and 1 inch thick will work perfectly.

Step 2: Shape the Call

  1. Cut the Wood: Use the saw to cut the block of wood to the desired size. A tapered shape can help create a better sound.
  2. Drill a Hole: Using the drill, create a hole in one end of the block, about 1 inch in diameter and 2 inches deep. This will serve as the sound chamber.

Step 3: Create the Reed

  1. Cut the Reed: If you’re using a natural reed, cut it to about 2 inches long. If you’re using a manufactured reed, ensure it fits snugly in the hole you drilled.
  2. Shape the Reed: If using wood, taper one end to create a point. This will help it vibrate and produce sound.

Step 4: Assemble the Call

Insert the reed into the hole, ensuring it fits tightly. You may need to adjust the length of the reed for the pitch you desire. Once satisfied with the sound, secure the reed in place using wood glue or by creating a small notch to hold it.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

Sand down any rough edges and apply a finish to protect the wood from moisture. Let it dry completely before testing the call.

Creating a Turkey Call

Turkey calls are slightly more complex but can be very rewarding. Here’s how to make a basic box call:

Step 1: Materials Needed

  • Two pieces of hardwood (one for the box, one for the lid)
  • A piece of softwood for the striking surface
  • Wood glue
  • Drill
  • Saw
  • Sandpaper

Step 2: Constructing the Box

  1. Cut the Box: One piece of hardwood should be cut to form the body of the call. A standard size for a box call is 8 inches long, 5 inches wide, and 3 inches high.
  2. Drill Holes: Drill a small hole in the center of the box; this will be the sound hole.

Step 3: Creating the Lid

  1. Cut the Lid: The lid should be slightly larger than the box to allow for a good seal when closed.
  2. Shape the Striking Surface: Cut the piece of softwood to the same width as the box and about 1 inch thick. This will be the part you strike to create sound.

Step 4: Assembling the Call

  1. Glue the Box: Assemble the box using wood glue and clamp it until it dries completely.
  2. Attach the Lid: Ensure the lid is able to pivot on one side. You can use a small hinge or simply place it in such a way that it can be lifted to strike.

Step 5: Finishing Touches

Sand any rough edges, and apply a protective finish. To use the call, simply lift the lid and strike the surface with a wooden stick or your finger to create the desired turkey sound.

Making a Duck Call

Duck calls require a different approach, focusing on the reed mechanism. Here’s a simple guide to making your own duck call:

Step 1: Materials Needed

  • A small piece of hardwood or acrylic tube
  • A cut piece of reed
  • A drill
  • A saw
  • Sandpaper

Step 2: Creating the Call Body

  1. Cut the Body: Use a drill to create a small hole in the side of the tube for the reed. The tube should be about 5 inches long.
  2. Shape the End: One end of the tube should be tapered to allow for better resonance.

Step 3: Inserting the Reed

  1. Cut the Reed: Cut your reed to about 1.5 inches long. The thickness and length will determine the pitch of the call.
  2. Insert the Reed: Place the reed in the hole you drilled, ensuring that it fits snugly. The end should extend slightly outside the tube.

Step 4: Testing the Call

To test your duck call, blow gently into the end of the tube. Adjust the position and length of the reed as necessary to achieve the desired sound.

Tips for Successful Hunting Calls

  1. Practice Regularly: The more you practice, the better you will become at using your calls effectively. Experiment with different pitches and rhythms.
  2. Observe Nature: Spend time in the field listening to the calls of animals. This will help you understand how to mimic their sounds more accurately.
  3. Adjust for Conditions: Weather conditions can affect how sound travels. Be prepared to adjust your calls based on your environment.

Conclusion

Making your own hunting calls is a fulfilling process that can enhance your hunting experience. Whether you’re attracting deer, turkey, or ducks, having a personal touch in your calls can make a significant difference. With patience and practice, you’ll not only save money but also develop a deeper connection to the art of hunting. Enjoy the process, and happy hunting!
