Top Hotels with Unique and Stylish Interiors


Stepping into a hotel isn’t just about finding a place to rest—it’s often an invitation to explore a fusion of art, design, comfort, and innovation. Across the globe, architects and designers are pushing boundaries, transforming hospitality spaces into inspiring destinations that are as memorable as the locations themselves. For travelers who value not only comfort but also creativity, these hotels redefine the concept of accommodation with strikingly unique and stylish interiors.

The Art of Exceptional Design

Hotels with distinctive interiors are more than a sum of their furnishings. Every color, texture, and layout speaks to the identity of the space, inviting guests to immerse themselves fully into an imaginative experience. Some take inspiration from local cultures while others delve into futuristic design, historical nostalgia, or experimental art movements. The result is a carefully curated escape that transports visitors to a world apart from their everyday routine.

Let’s explore some of the top hotels worldwide that are celebrated for their exquisite and creative interiors.

1. The Muraka, Maldives

Located on the serene waters of the Indian Ocean, The Muraka at Conrad Maldives Rangali Island offers a design experience unlike any other. This undersea hotel combines cutting-edge architecture with luxury. The interiors are modern and minimalist, featuring 180-degree acrylic domes providing panoramic views of marine life. The muted tones within the underwater bedrooms complement the vibrant blues of the ocean, letting nature take center stage. Above the water, The Muraka embraces sleek interiors with soft textures and contemporary art inspired by the surrounding seascape.

2. Hotel Marques de Riscal, Spain

Nestled in the wine region of Rioja, Hotel Marques de Riscal resembles a piece of contemporary art come to life. Designed by world-renowned architect Frank Gehry, the exterior is a stunning wave of metallic ribbons, while the interiors echo his innovative style. Bold geometries, asymmetrical layouts, and artful lighting create dramatic spaces. Every guest room is curated with avant-garde furnishings, blending comfort with forward-thinking artistry. Combined with scenic vineyard views, this hotel offers a sensory feast for design enthusiasts.

3. La Mamounia, Morocco

La Mamounia in Marrakech blends traditional Moroccan craftsmanship with opulent luxury. The interiors are richly decorated with mosaics, ornate woodwork, and vibrant textiles. Every corner of this hotel pays homage to Moroccan heritage, from its intricately carved cedar furniture to zellige tiles adorning the walls and floors. The lush gardens outside further enhance the sense of timeless beauty, while the lavish spa adds a contemporary touch with sleekly modern amenities.

4. The Icehotel, Sweden

For those seeking an extraordinary experience, the Icehotel in Jukkasjärvi offers interiors that are literally frozen in time. Carved entirely from ice and snow, every room in the hotel features unique sculptures and artistic designs created by international ice artists. The lighting plays a crucial role, with hues of blue, white, and soft pink immersing guests in an ethereal glow. Although the cold setting might seem stark, you’ll find warmth in the creativity behind every frozen detail.

5. The Library Hotel, Thailand

Situated on Koh Samui’s beachfront, The Library is designed for book-loving travelers who value minimalism and modern sophistication. The bold use of color in its interiors—contrasting vibrant reds against monochromatic whites and grays—is striking. The spacious, library-themed common areas display thousands of books, creating an intellectual atmosphere complemented by sleek, simple furnishings. Open spaces and thoughtful lighting lend a harmonious flow to the interiors, making it a haven for both literary lovers and design enthusiasts.

6. The Gritti Palace, Italy

Venice is synonymous with elegance, and The Gritti Palace takes that essence to new heights. This historic 16th-century palazzo features stunning interiors steeped in luxurious Venetian traditions. Ornate Murano glass chandeliers, intricate frescoes, and antique furnishings transport guests to a bygone era. Each room is a masterpiece, with rich fabrics, gold embellishments, and artfully restored wood paneling that celebrate Venice’s artistic and cultural history.

7. The Zedwell, United Kingdom

In the heart of London, The Zedwell reimagines the hotel experience with a focus on tranquility and simplicity. The concept marries interior design with wellness, offering a minimalist aesthetic that removes excess noise—both visual and literal. Smooth wooden finishes, calming neutral tones, and ergonomic layouts promote relaxation, creating a serene environment that contrasts sharply with the bustle of urban life right outside.

8. The Keemala, Thailand

Hidden in the lush forests of Phuket, The Keemala is an imaginative retreat inspired by traditional Thai mythology. The hotel’s architecture mimics bird’s nests and treehouses, seamlessly blending with its natural surroundings. Inside, each villa is uniquely designed with earthy textures, splashes of green, and handcrafted details, all tied together through sustainable materials. This eco-luxury design provides an unparalleled connection to culture and nature.

9. Hotel Unique, Brazil

Located in São Paulo, Hotel Unique is an iconic modern structure that lives up to its name. Designed by Ruy Ohtake, its bold crescent-shaped exterior houses interiors that feel both futuristic and welcoming. A mix of avant-garde furniture, earthy tones, and metallic accents creates an immersive experience. Tall windows invite natural light to flood communal areas, while guest rooms balance cutting-edge design with functionality.

10. The Nomad Los Angeles, USA

Housed in a former neoclassical bank building, The NoMad Los Angeles blends old-world charm with contemporary flair. The interiors showcase rich marble detailing, vintage-inspired decor, and a palette of deep blues and golds. The grandeur of the building’s historic architecture is complemented by playful design touches, such as whimsical art pieces and vibrant furniture. It’s the perfect space for travelers looking to connect with history while enjoying modern comforts.

Why Interior Design Matters in Hospitality

Interior design goes beyond aesthetics—it’s about creating an experience for guests. A well-designed hotel can leave a lasting impression, offering not just a place to sleep but an opportunity to partake in a visual journey. Whether it’s through sustainable materials, cultural immersion, or artistic experimentation, these hotels showcase how inventive interiors can elevate the hospitality industry.

For travelers seeking inspiration, relaxation, or simply something out of the ordinary, these destinations prove that design can transform a simple stay into a cherished memory.

Whether you prefer the intricate beauty of Moroccan mosaics, the futuristic allure of underwater rooms, or the harmonious simplicity of Scandinavian minimalism, there’s a hotel out there with interiors that speak to your sense of style and adventure.

No matter where your next journey takes you, consider choosing accommodation that enriches your travel experience through exceptional design.
