Wholesome and Hearty Stew Recipes for Cold Nights


Winter is a season of cold nights, cozy interiors, and hearty meals. Stews, in particular, have a special place in our hearts and appetites throughout these chilly months. They’re warm, comforting, and packed with nutrition and flavor. Allow me to share three delightful stew recipes that will satisfy your taste buds and nourish your body.

Recipe 1: Slow Cooker Chicken and Vegetable Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. chicken thighs
  • 1 cup chopped carrot
  • 1 cup chopped celery
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup chopped onion
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 2 cups diced potatoes
  • 2 cups sliced green beans
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat; add minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute.
  2. Place the chicken thighs in a slow cooker.
  3. Add the cooked garlic, chopped carrots, celery, mushrooms, and onions to the slow cooker.
  4. Season with thyme, salt, and black pepper.
  5. Fill the slow cooker with chicken broth, ensuring the chicken is covered.
  6. Cook on low heat for 6-8 hours, making sure the chicken is cooked through and tender.
  7. Add green beans and frozen peas during the last hour of cooking.
  8. Serve this nutrient-dense stew over cooked rice, pasta, or bread.

Recipe 2: Vegetarian Winter Root Stew

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup chopped red potatoes
  • 1 cup sliced parsnips
  • 1 cup chopped carrots
  • 1 cup chopped onions
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup baby spinach
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat; add minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute.
  2. Add chopped onions, carrots, and parsnips to the pot, stirring occasionally.
  3. Season with dried rosemary, salt, and black pepper.
  4. Pour vegetable broth into the pot, allowing vegetables to become tender.
  5. Stir in the sliced mushrooms and cook for another 5 minutes.
  6. Fold in the baby spinach, allowing it to wilt.
  7. Serve this hearty vegetarian stew with crusty bread or a side salad.

Recipe 3: Beef, Bean, and Barley Stew

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. beef chuck, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 cup chopped onions
  • 1 cup sliced mushrooms
  • 1 cup chopped carrots
  • 1 cup dry barley
  • 1 cup cooked kidney beans
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 cloves minced garlic
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat; add minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute.
  2. Add chopped onions, carrots, and sliced mushrooms to the pot, stirring occasionally.
  3. As the vegetables become tender, add the beef, barley, and cooked kidney beans.
  4. Stir in the beef broth, ensuring the beef and barley are covered.
  5. Season with thyme, salt, and black pepper.
  6. Let the stew simmer for 1-2 hours, until the beef is tender and the barley is cooked through.
  7. Serve this rich beef stew over cooked noodles or with crusty bread.

Finish your cold nights with these wholesome and comforting stew recipes. They’ll not only warm your kitchen but also your heart and belly. Savor the flavors of winter one bowl at a time!
