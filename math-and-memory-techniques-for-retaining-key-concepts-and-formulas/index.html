Math and Memory: Techniques for Retaining Key Concepts and Formulas


Almost everyone, at some point in their lives, has had to deal with math. It may have been during their school years, or while studying engineering or finance in college. Some of us find it challenging, while others enjoy it. However, the key to mastering math lies in our ability to remember the formulas and concepts that make up the subject.

In this article, we delve into several techniques to help you remember key math concepts and formulas to make studying less stressful and more enjoyable.

  1. Visualization Techniques: While mathematics can seem abstract, visualization techniques can give you a more intuitive understanding of mathematical concepts. For instance, visualization can be beneficial for understanding geometry concepts like triangles, angles, and circles. You can use diagrams or drawings to represent abstract concepts, making it easier to remember the formulas associated with those concepts.

  2. Mnemonics: Mnemonics have long been known for their ability to help individuals learn and remember mathematical formulas. Take the times table as an example: most people can remember it because it follows a simple pattern that can be exploited using mnemonic devices. You can create your mnemonic phrases for various math concepts and formulas to make learning less strenuous.

  3. The Method of Loci: This technique, which involves associating math concepts with a physical location, is commonly employed in memory improvement techniques. By placing concepts and formulas at various points along a mental pathway, you can easily recall them when needed. For instance, imagine walking through a familiar place as you mentally place the concepts and formulas along the path. When you need the information, mentally walk through the pathway to retrieve it.

  4. Practice, Practice, Practice: The key to learning and retention is repetitious practice – a principle that applies to mathematics. The more you use your memory to recall mathematical concepts and formulas, the stronger the neural pathways become, which leads to stronger retention. You can use practice quizzes and reflections to test your knowledge and reinforce learning.

  5. Teach Someone Else: Teaching someone else can help you to understand and remember mathematical concepts and formulas better. When you explain an idea to another person, you’re forced to digest the information deeply, making it more likely that you’ll remember the information in the long term. Reach into your repertoire and share your knowledge with a friend, classmate, or family member.

  6. Arousing Curiosity: If you view math as merely a series of rules and formulas, you’re likely to find it challenging. However, seeing it as an exciting area of exploration will motivate you, leading to heightened memory retention. To do this, learn math concepts’ history, and see how they provide solutions to real-life challenges. This understanding can ignite your curiosity and make learning more enjoyable.

Remember, there’s no ‘one-size-fits-all’ strategy to learning and retaining mathematical concepts and formulas. It’s critical to find strategies that work for you. Experiment with the techniques outlined above and discover which ones resonate best with you. Ultimately, unlocking these techniques will lead to a better understanding and appreciation of math, making it a vital tool in your mental toolkit.
