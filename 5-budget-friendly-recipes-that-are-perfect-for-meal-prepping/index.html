5 Budget-Friendly Recipes That Are Perfect for Meal Prepping


Meal prepping is an effective way to save time, reduce stress, and stick to a healthy eating plan. If you’re on a budget, the good news is that you don’t have to spend a fortune on fancy ingredients to create meals that are nutritious, delicious, and easy to prepare. By making a few strategic choices, you can create cost-effective meals that will last you for days, ensuring you always have something ready to eat.

In this post, we’ll explore five budget-friendly recipes that are perfect for meal prepping. These recipes use affordable ingredients, are easy to make, and can be stored for multiple meals throughout the week.

1. Vegetable Stir-Fry with Rice

This vegetable stir-fry is not only inexpensive but also packed with nutrients. It’s a versatile recipe that can be adjusted to your taste preferences, and you can use any vegetables you have on hand. Rice is a filling, affordable base that complements the veggies perfectly.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked brown or white rice
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 2 carrots, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil (optional)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  1. Start by cooking the rice according to package instructions. Brown rice is a healthier option, but white rice works just fine for this recipe.
  2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add the onion and cook for 2-3 minutes, until softened.
  3. Add the garlic and cook for another minute, allowing it to become fragrant.
  4. Toss in the bell pepper, carrots, zucchini, and broccoli. Stir-fry the vegetables for about 5-7 minutes, or until they are tender yet still crisp.
  5. Add the soy sauce and sesame oil (if using), and stir everything together. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Divide the cooked rice into meal prep containers and top each portion with the stir-fry mixture. Let the meal cool before sealing the containers.

Why It’s Great for Meal Prep:

This stir-fry recipe is quick to make and uses ingredients that are readily available and inexpensive. It’s a fantastic option for meal prep because it can be easily divided into multiple portions and reheated throughout the week. Plus, it’s vegetarian and can be made vegan by simply leaving out any animal-based ingredients.

2. Lentil and Chickpea Curry

Lentils and chickpeas are two of the most affordable plant-based protein sources. When combined with aromatic spices and a rich tomato sauce, they make for a hearty and filling curry that’s perfect for meal prepping. This curry is not only budget-friendly but also incredibly flavorful.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried lentils (green or brown)
  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 1 can (15 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil (or any cooking oil)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat the coconut oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the garlic and ginger, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the curry powder, cumin, turmeric, paprika, and cayenne pepper (if using), and cook for 1-2 minutes to allow the spices to bloom.
  4. Add the lentils, chickpeas, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth to the pot. Bring to a simmer and cover. Cook for about 30-40 minutes, or until the lentils are tender.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve the curry with rice or naan bread, and garnish with fresh cilantro if desired. Divide into meal prep containers.

Why It’s Great for Meal Prep:

This curry is a filling, protein-packed meal that keeps well in the fridge for several days. It’s incredibly versatile, and you can adjust the level of spice to your preference. Lentils and chickpeas are both budget-friendly staples, making this dish perfect for anyone looking to eat healthy on a budget.

3. Chicken and Sweet Potato Bake

This simple sheet pan chicken and sweet potato bake is perfect for meal prepping. It’s a one-pan wonder that requires minimal ingredients and effort, but it delivers a nutritious and satisfying meal. Sweet potatoes are a great source of vitamins and fiber, while chicken provides lean protein to keep you full and satisfied.

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (or thighs for more flavor)
  • 3 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme (or rosemary)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Place the cubed sweet potatoes on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, and half of the garlic powder, paprika, and thyme. Toss to coat.
  3. Arrange the chicken breasts on the same baking sheet. Drizzle with a little more olive oil and season with the remaining garlic powder, paprika, thyme, salt, and pepper.
  4. Roast in the oven for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through (internal temperature should reach 165°F/74°C) and the sweet potatoes are tender.
  5. Remove from the oven and let it cool slightly before dividing into meal prep containers. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired.

Why It’s Great for Meal Prep:

This recipe is simple, quick, and easy to prepare, making it ideal for meal prepping. Sweet potatoes and chicken are both nutrient-dense and budget-friendly, and this dish can be enjoyed for lunch or dinner throughout the week. It’s also easy to customize by adding different vegetables or seasonings.

4. Tuna Salad with Beans and Corn

A tuna salad doesn’t have to be loaded with mayo to be delicious. This version uses healthy ingredients like beans, corn, and a light dressing to create a meal that’s both light and filling. It’s perfect for meal prepping because it keeps well in the fridge for several days.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans (5 oz) tuna in water, drained
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 can (15 oz) corn, drained
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup cucumber, diced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine the tuna, black beans, corn, red onion, and cucumber.
  2. In a separate small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt, and pepper.
  3. Pour the dressing over the tuna mixture and stir to combine.
  4. Divide the salad into meal prep containers and garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro if desired.

Why It’s Great for Meal Prep:

Tuna is an affordable source of protein, and pairing it with beans and corn adds fiber and nutrients. This salad is not only budget-friendly but also quick to assemble, making it perfect for meal prepping. Plus, it’s versatile—you can add or substitute vegetables based on what’s available.

5. Spaghetti with Tomato Sauce and Roasted Vegetables

Pasta is a pantry staple, and when paired with roasted vegetables and a simple tomato sauce, it makes for a satisfying meal that’s perfect for meal prep. This dish is easy to make in large batches, and the leftovers taste just as good, if not better, the next day.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb spaghetti
  • 1 can (15 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Parmesan cheese, for topping (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  2. Toss the chopped onion, garlic, bell pepper, zucchini, and cherry tomatoes with olive oil, oregano, basil, salt, and pepper. Spread the vegetables out on a baking sheet and roast for 20-25 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized.
  3. Meanwhile, cook the spaghetti according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  4. In a large pan, heat the tomato sauce over medium heat. Add the cooked spaghetti and toss to combine. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve the pasta topped with the roasted vegetables and a sprinkle of Parmesan cheese if desired.

Why It’s Great for Meal Prep:

This pasta dish is hearty, comforting, and filling, but it’s also incredibly affordable. The roasted vegetables add flavor and nutrients, while the pasta and tomato sauce are easy to prepare in bulk. This meal is perfect for reheating, making it a great option for meal prep.

Conclusion

Meal prepping doesn’t have to be complicated or expensive. By using budget-friendly ingredients like rice, beans, pasta, and seasonal vegetables, you can create nutritious and delicious meals that will keep you satisfied throughout the week. Whether you’re trying to save money, eat healthier, or simply make your life a little easier, these five recipes are the perfect place to start.

With just a little time and effort, you’ll have a week’s worth of meals ready to go, making it easier than ever to stick to your meal plan and budget. Happy cooking!
