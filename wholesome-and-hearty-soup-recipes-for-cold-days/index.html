Wholesome and Hearty Soup Recipes for Cold Days


Winter days are gloomy and chilly, and there’s nothing like a warm, hearty soup to chase away the blues. Soups are not only comforting but also nutritious, making them a perfect choice to power up your immune system during the cold season. In this blog post, we will share some wholesome and hearty soup recipes that will keep you warm and nourished from head to toe.

1. Chicken Noodle Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs. of chicken thighs
  • 8 cups of low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 large stalks of celery, chopped
  • ½ cup of chopped onion
  • 8 oz. of egg noodles
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, bring the chicken broth to a boil.
  2. Add the chicken thighs and simmer for about 20 minutes.
  3. Remove the chicken from the pot and shred it using two forks. Set aside.
  4. In the same pot, add the carrots, celery, onion, and bay leaves and cook for about 15 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  5. Add the shredded chicken and egg noodles and simmer for an additional 10 minutes.
  6. Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste and serve warm with crusty bread.

2. Lentil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups of green or brown lentils, rinsed and drained
  • 1 lb. of ground beef or turkey
  • 4 cups of vegetable or beef broth
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 stalks of celery, chopped
  • 1 can (14.5 oz.) of diced tomatoes
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Heat a large pot over medium heat and add the ground beef or turkey. Cook until browned, stirring occasionally.
  2. Remove the meat from the pot and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add the onion, garlic, carrots, and celery. Cook until the vegetables are softened.
  4. Stir in the lentils, diced tomatoes, and broth.
  5. Add the bay leaves, and season the soup with salt and pepper.
  6. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for at least an hour.
  7. Serve warm with crusty bread or a side salad.

3. Tomato Basil Soup

Ingredients:

  • 3 cans (28 oz.) of crushed tomatoes
  • 6 cups of vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 large carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2 stalks of celery, chopped
  • 1 can (15 oz.) of tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp. of fresh basil, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat some olive oil over medium heat and add the onion and garlic. Cook until the onion is translucent.
  2. Add the crushed tomatoes, broth, carrots, celery, and tomato paste. Stir well.
  3. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and let simmer for about 20 minutes.
  4. Stir in the chopped basil and season the soup with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve warm with crusty bread or grilled cheese sandwiches.

4. Vegetable Soup

Ingredients:

  • 4 cups of vegetable broth
  • 1 cup each of chopped carrots, green beans, and peas
  • 1 cup of diced potatoes
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 cups of chopped kale
  • 1 can (14.5 oz.) of diced tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp. of fresh thyme, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat some olive oil over medium heat and add the onion and garlic. Cook until the onion is translucent.
  2. Add the vegetable broth, carrots, green beans, peas, potatoes, and diced tomatoes. Stir well.
  3. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat and let simmer for about 25-30 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender.
  4. Stir in the chopped kale and fresh thyme, and season the soup with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve warm with crusty bread or a side salad.

Regardless of whether you’re looking for a simple chicken noodle soup or a hearty lentil soup, these recipes will keep you warm and satisfied during those cold winter days. So, gather your ingredients and start cooking!
