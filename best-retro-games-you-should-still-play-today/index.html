Best Retro Games You Should Still Play Today


The world of retro gaming holds a special place in the hearts of countless players, from those who grew up during the golden age of video games to the new generation who have discovered the charm of pixelated art and chiptune music. While the gaming industry continues to evolve, retro games remain timeless, offering a unique experience that modern titles often lack.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the best retro games you should still play today. These games are not only iconic but have influenced the gaming industry in ways that continue to resonate even in the modern gaming world.

1. Super Mario Bros. (1985) – The Birth of a Legend

No list of retro games would be complete without mentioning Super Mario Bros. This title, developed by Nintendo, is often considered the quintessential platformer and one of the most influential games of all time. Released in 1985 for the Nintendo Entertainment System (NES), it introduced players to Mario and Luigi, two brothers on a mission to save Princess Toadstool from the evil Bowser.

What makes Super Mario Bros. so enduring is its innovative gameplay mechanics, iconic music, and simple yet challenging design. The game’s controls are tight, the level design is clever, and its difficulty curve is perfectly balanced. These elements, combined with its colorful visuals and memorable soundtrack, have kept it a beloved title even decades after its release.

Whether you’re a longtime fan of Mario or just discovering him for the first time, Super Mario Bros. is a must-play. Its influence can still be seen in many of the modern platformers that dominate the gaming world today.

2. The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time (1998) – A Masterpiece of Adventure

When discussing retro games that have stood the test of time, The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time is a name that is almost always mentioned. Released for the Nintendo 64 in 1998, Ocarina of Time was a groundbreaking title that blended action, adventure, puzzle-solving, and exploration in ways that had never been seen before.

Players take on the role of Link, the hero of time, as he embarks on a quest to save the land of Hyrule from the evil sorcerer Ganondorf. The game’s world is vast, filled with hidden secrets and challenges, while its story is both engaging and emotionally impactful.

Ocarina of Time was also one of the first games to feature a dynamic 3D environment and real-time combat, which helped shape the future of adventure games. Its use of the ocarina, a magical instrument, as a central gameplay mechanic was not only innovative but also helped establish a connection between the player and the game’s world.

With its deep lore, challenging gameplay, and unforgettable soundtrack, Ocarina of Time remains one of the greatest video games of all time and is an essential part of any retro gaming collection.

3. Pac-Man (1980) – The Arcade Icon

If you’re looking for a truly classic arcade experience, look no further than Pac-Man. Released in 1980 by Namco, Pac-Man quickly became one of the most popular and recognizable games of the 1980s, and it continues to have a massive influence on gaming culture today.

In Pac-Man, players control the titular character, a hungry yellow circle, as he navigates a maze, eating dots and avoiding colorful ghosts. The game’s simplicity is one of its most appealing aspects, yet it also offers plenty of challenges. As the levels progress, the ghosts become faster and more aggressive, making it harder to avoid them.

What makes Pac-Man truly special, however, is its lasting impact on pop culture. It was one of the first games to feature a character who became a household name, and it helped solidify the arcade as the dominant gaming platform during the 1980s. Its legacy continues today in the form of numerous sequels, spin-offs, and even television shows.

If you want to experience the thrill of high-score chasing and relive the magic of the arcades, Pac-Man is a must-play.

4. Tetris (1984) – The Puzzle Game That Never Gets Old

For puzzle game enthusiasts, Tetris is an absolute must. Developed by Russian programmer Alexey Pajitnov and released in 1984, Tetris has become one of the most iconic and addictive games of all time. Its simple yet challenging gameplay has made it a timeless classic that continues to be enjoyed by players of all ages.

In Tetris, players must rotate and place falling tetrominoes (geometric shapes made up of four squares) to form complete lines, which then disappear. The challenge comes from the increasing speed at which the tetrominoes fall, forcing players to think and react quickly.

What makes Tetris so compelling is its perfect blend of simplicity and depth. While the concept is easy to grasp, the game becomes increasingly difficult as the speed increases, making it a game of skill, strategy, and reflexes. Its addictive nature has made it a staple on nearly every gaming platform, and its legacy continues to influence modern puzzle games.

If you’re looking for a game that will keep you hooked for hours, Tetris is the one to play.

5. Sonic the Hedgehog (1991) – The Fastest Blue Hedgehog in History

Super Mario Bros. may have set the standard for platformers, but Sonic the Hedgehog defined speed in video games. Released in 1991 for the Sega Genesis, Sonic the Hedgehog introduced players to Sonic, a blue hedgehog with a need for speed, as he battles the evil Dr. Robotnik (also known as Dr. Eggman).

What set Sonic the Hedgehog apart from other platformers at the time was its focus on fast-paced gameplay. Unlike other games, where players would slowly navigate through levels, Sonic encouraged players to zip through stages at breakneck speeds, avoiding obstacles and collecting rings along the way.

The game’s colorful visuals, catchy soundtrack, and innovative level design made it a standout title, and it became the flagship game for Sega during the 1990s. Sonic’s popularity would spawn numerous sequels, spin-offs, and an entire franchise, but the original game remains one of the best examples of fast-paced platforming action.

If you love speed and want to experience the thrill of racing through vibrant, challenging levels, Sonic the Hedgehog is the game for you.

6. Street Fighter II (1991) – The King of Fighting Games

Street Fighter II is often credited with revolutionizing the fighting game genre. Released by Capcom in 1991, Street Fighter II introduced players to a cast of memorable characters, each with their own unique moves and fighting styles. The game allowed players to compete against each other in one-on-one battles, a format that would become the standard for future fighting games.

What made Street Fighter II so impactful was its deep and strategic gameplay. Each character had a set of special moves, such as Hadoken (fireball) and Shoryuken (dragon punch), which could be performed using specific button combinations. The game’s mechanics were simple enough for newcomers to pick up but offered plenty of depth for more experienced players.

Street Fighter II also helped establish the competitive fighting game scene, as players would often gather in arcades to face off against each other. The game’s popularity led to numerous sequels, but the original remains a classic that any fan of fighting games should play.

7. Castlevania: Symphony of the Night (1997) – A Metroidvania Masterpiece

Released in 1997 for the Sony PlayStation, Castlevania: Symphony of the Night is considered one of the best examples of the Metroidvania genre, a subgenre of action-adventure games that combines exploration and platforming with RPG elements.

In Symphony of the Night, players control Alucard, the son of Dracula, as he explores a vast, interconnected castle filled with monsters, traps, and hidden secrets. The game features a non-linear structure, allowing players to explore the castle in any order they choose, unlocking new abilities and items along the way.

What sets Symphony of the Night apart is its emphasis on exploration, RPG elements (such as leveling up and gaining new equipment), and atmospheric storytelling. The game’s haunting soundtrack, beautifully detailed sprite-based graphics, and deep gameplay make it a standout title in the Castlevania series and a must-play for any retro gaming enthusiast.

Conclusion

Retro games are more than just a nostalgic trip down memory lane; they are the foundation of modern gaming, shaping the industry and influencing countless titles that came after them. Whether you’re revisiting these classics or experiencing them for the first time, these games offer a timeless experience that is still worth playing today.

From the platforming brilliance of Super Mario Bros. to the fast-paced action of Sonic the Hedgehog and the deep exploration of Castlevania: Symphony of the Night, there’s something for every type of gamer in the world of retro gaming. So dust off that old console, fire up an emulator, or pick up a modern re-release and experience the magic of these classic titles.

Happy gaming!
