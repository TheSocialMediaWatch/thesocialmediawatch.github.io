Peeling Back the Layers: The Different Types of Skin Peels and Their Benefits


When it comes to skin care, achieving glowing, youthful, and radiant skin is often the ultimate goal. Many people have turned to skin peels for this purpose. Skin peels, also known as chemical peels, involve applying an acid solution to the skin’s surface, removing dead skin cells, and revealing the fresher, smoother skin beneath. This process promotes the production of new collagen, reduces sun damage, and clears acne-prone skin, resulting in a more bright and even complexion.

Though chemical skin peels date back to ancient times, the process has undergone significant advancements. Today, dermatologists and estheticians offer a broad range of skin peel options that cater to various skin types and concerns. Here is a breakdown of the most popular types of chemical peels and their unique benefits.

  1. Alpha Hydroxy Acid (AHA) Peels

Alpha hydroxy acids (AHAs) are a group of natural acids derived from fruits and sugars. They work by dissolving the connections between dead skin cells, fading dark spots, and encouraging cell turnover. Glycolic acid, lactic acid, and tartaric acid are common types of AHAs, each with varying strengths and effects on the skin. AHA peels are suitable for most skin types and combat signs of aging, such as fine lines and wrinkles, sun damage, and uneven skin texture.

  1. Beta Hydroxy Acid (BHA) Peels

Beta hydroxy acid (BHA), an oil-soluble acid derived from plants and bacteria, works similarly to AHAs but has a deeper reach into the pores. Salicylic acid is the most common BHA, and its oil-soluble nature makes it an ideal choice for oilier skin types or those battling acne. BHA peels effectively unclog pores, reduce excess oil production, and alleviate breakouts, making them particularly effective for those with acne-prone skin.

  1. Fruit Acid Peels

Fruit acid peels incorporate natural fruit enzymes, such as papaya and pineapple, to gentle yet effective exfoliants for sensitive skin types. These enzymes work by dissolving the lipids that hold dead skin cells together, revealing brighter, smoother skin. Fruit acid peels are beneficial for all skin types, especially those with sensitive skin, as they are gentler than traditional AHA peels.

  1. Jessner’s Peel

Named after the famous dermatologist Dr. M. Jessner, this peel combines lactic acid and resorcinol to create a more significant impact than regular AHA peels. Jessner’s Peel is ideal for those with hyperpigmentation, sun damage, and acne-prone skin, as the peel reduces acne scarring and fine lines while promoting an even skin tone.

  1. Phen Peel

The Phen Peel, a popular option for Hollywood celebrities, combines the power of phenol and trichloroacetic acid (TCA) for a deeper exfoliation than other peels. As a result, it is not recommended for those with skin of color, as it may lead to unwanted discoloration. Phen peels are suitable for those wish to address significant signs of aging, such as deep wrinkles and texture irregularities.

Before deciding to undergo a skin peel, it is crucial to consult with a licensed esthetician or dermatologist. They will assess your skin type, discuss your goals and concerns, and recommend the most suitable option for your skin’s needs. Depending on the chosen peel, you may experience some discomfort during the process or redness afterward, making it necessary to schedule the treatment during your workday or have someone available to drive you home.

By embracing the different types of skin peels and their benefits, you now hold the key to unlocking smoother, brighter, and more youthful skin. It’s time to embrace the power of skin peels as a game-changer for your skin care routine!
