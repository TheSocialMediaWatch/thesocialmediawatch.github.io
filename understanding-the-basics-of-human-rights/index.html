Understanding the Basics of Human Rights


Human rights are the fundamental rights and freedoms that every person is entitled to, without any distinction based on race, color, sex, language, religion, political or other opinion, national or social origin, property, birth, or other status. The concept of human rights is deeply ingrained in the history of civilization, tracing back to ancient civilizations and reflective in various religious and cultural practices.

At the core of human rights are three major categories: civil, political, and social rights. Civil rights are those rights pertaining to one’s relationship with the state and involve privileges granted to individuals by the government, such as the right to a fair trial, the right to vote, and the right to privacy. Political rights, on the other hand, involve the right to participate in the governance of one’s country, such as the right to standing for elected office, the right to peaceful assembly, and the right to freedom of expression.

Social rights concern the economic, social, and cultural rights of individuals, such as the right to work, the right to housing, the right to health, and the right to education.

The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR), adopted in 1948, outlines 30 articles that embody the fundamental human rights and freedoms. It serves as an essential guide, defining the standard for all international Treaty Bodies and courts that deal with human rights matters.

The International Bill of Rights, composed of three core treaties, namely the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights (ICCPR), the International Covenant on Economic, Social, and Cultural Rights (ICESCR), and the Universal Protocol on Human Rights, serve to further define, uphold, and promote human rights for people all over the globe.

Apart from these treaties, there are numerous international and regional human rights bodies established to protect and uphold human rights. The United Nations (UN) includes commissions, committees, special rapporteurs, and independent experts who work to ensure that individuals are protected and their rights are not violated.

Human rights, however, are not exclusively protected by international and regional bodies. National governments bear the primary responsibility to respect, protect, and fulfill human rights.

Respect for human rights involves refraining from engaging in actions that would infringe on or violate an individual’s rights. Protection of human rights entails ensuring that mechanisms are in place to prevent violations and providing remedies for individuals when their rights are violated. Fulfilling human rights requires proactively working to ensure that individuals can exercise, enjoy, and realize their rights.

Understanding the basics of human rights is critical in ensuring that violations are recognized and addressed promptly. Humans have the responsibility to know and uphold their rights, and everyone has an essential role to play in promoting human rights globally.

So, the next time you interact with your government, your community, and the world, remember, your words and actions can contribute to promoting and protecting human rights, or the reverse. It’s in your hands. Let’s work together to make the world a more just and humane place.
