5 Simple and Healthy Recipes for Meal Prepping


Meal prepping is an efficient way to save time, reduce stress, and maintain a healthy eating routine. It allows you to prepare meals in advance, ensuring that you always have healthy options on hand, whether you’re rushing out the door in the morning or looking for a quick, nutritious snack. In this post, we will share five simple and healthy recipes that are perfect for meal prepping. These meals are packed with nutrients, easy to prepare, and will help you stay on track with your healthy eating goals.

1. Chicken and Veggie Stir-Fry

A chicken and veggie stir-fry is a simple, versatile, and healthy recipe that can be customized with your favorite vegetables and seasonings. This dish is not only quick to prepare but also packed with lean protein and fiber. Here’s how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • 2 chicken breasts (boneless, skinless)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce (or tamari for gluten-free)
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil (optional)
  • Cooked brown rice or quinoa, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Prep the Chicken: Slice the chicken breasts into thin strips or bite-sized pieces.
  2. Cook the Chicken: Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chicken to the pan and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until fully cooked. Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.
  3. Cook the Veggies: In the same pan, add a little more oil if needed. Add the onion, garlic, bell pepper, zucchini, and broccoli. Sauté for about 5 minutes until the vegetables are tender but still vibrant.
  4. Combine and Sauce: Add the cooked chicken back into the pan. In a small bowl, mix the soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, and sesame oil. Pour the sauce over the chicken and vegetables, stirring to coat everything evenly. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes.
  5. Serve: Serve your stir-fry with cooked brown rice or quinoa for a complete meal. Portion into individual meal prep containers for easy grab-and-go meals throughout the week.

Why It’s Great for Meal Prep:

This stir-fry is a great source of lean protein, fiber, and antioxidants from the vegetables. It’s also versatile – you can swap out vegetables based on what you have in your fridge. The sauce adds flavor without being too heavy, and it stores well for up to 4 days in the fridge.

2. Sweet Potato and Black Bean Bowls

Sweet potatoes and black beans make for a hearty, nutrient-packed combination that is perfect for meal prepping. This dish is full of fiber, antioxidants, and plant-based protein. It’s an easy, satisfying lunch or dinner option that can be enjoyed cold or reheated.

Ingredients:

  • 2 medium sweet potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 can black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cup cilantro, chopped
  • Lime wedges, for serving
  • Cooked quinoa or rice, for serving

Instructions:

  1. Roast the Sweet Potatoes: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Toss the cubed sweet potatoes with olive oil, cumin, smoked paprika, salt, and pepper. Spread them evenly on a baking sheet and roast for 25-30 minutes, or until tender and slightly crispy on the edges.
  2. Prepare the Bowls: While the sweet potatoes are roasting, cook quinoa or rice according to package instructions. Once cooked, divide into meal prep containers.
  3. Assemble the Bowls: Once the sweet potatoes are done, add them to the containers with quinoa or rice. Top with black beans, sliced avocado, chopped cilantro, and a squeeze of lime.
  4. Store: Store in airtight containers in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat when ready to eat.

Why It’s Great for Meal Prep:

This dish is full of slow-digesting carbohydrates, fiber, and healthy fats. The sweet potatoes provide a natural source of sweetness, while the black beans add protein and iron. Avocado adds creaminess, and the lime and cilantro bring a fresh kick to the bowl. It’s an easy-to-make, vegetarian meal that can be customized to suit your taste.

3. Turkey Meatballs with Roasted Vegetables

These turkey meatballs are packed with lean protein and flavorful herbs, making them a perfect addition to your meal prep. When paired with roasted vegetables, this meal becomes a balanced, nutrient-dense option that can be enjoyed throughout the week.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground turkey (lean)
  • 1/4 cup breadcrumbs (use gluten-free if needed)
  • 1/4 cup grated parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup Brussels sprouts, halved
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced

Instructions:

  1. Make the Meatballs: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). In a large bowl, combine the ground turkey, breadcrumbs, parmesan, egg, oregano, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper. Mix until just combined. Form the mixture into 16-18 meatballs and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
  2. Roast the Vegetables: On a separate baking sheet, toss the cherry tomatoes, Brussels sprouts, red onion, and bell pepper with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Roast the vegetables in the oven for 20-25 minutes, or until tender and slightly caramelized.
  3. Bake the Meatballs: Bake the turkey meatballs in the preheated oven for 15-20 minutes, or until cooked through and golden brown on the outside.
  4. Store: Divide the roasted vegetables and turkey meatballs into meal prep containers. Store in the fridge for up to 4 days.

Why It’s Great for Meal Prep:

Turkey meatballs are a great source of lean protein, while the roasted vegetables are full of fiber, vitamins, and minerals. This meal is balanced, filling, and can be easily reheated in the microwave for a quick lunch or dinner. The meatballs also freeze well, so you can make a big batch and save some for later.

4. Chickpea Salad with Tahini Dressing

If you’re looking for a lighter, plant-based meal option, this chickpea salad with tahini dressing is perfect for meal prepping. It’s refreshing, filling, and packed with protein and healthy fats. The tahini dressing ties everything together with its creamy, nutty flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup parsley, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon tahini
  • 1 teaspoon honey (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Salad: In a large bowl, combine the chickpeas, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and parsley.
  2. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, tahini, honey, salt, and pepper until smooth. Adjust seasoning to taste.
  3. Toss the Salad: Drizzle the tahini dressing over the salad and toss to combine. Taste and adjust the seasoning if needed.
  4. Store: Divide the salad into meal prep containers. Store in the fridge for up to 3 days.

Why It’s Great for Meal Prep:

This salad is a fantastic plant-based meal full of fiber, protein, and healthy fats. The tahini dressing adds richness, and the lemon juice brightens up the flavors. It’s perfect for a quick, light meal that will keep you feeling satisfied and energized.

5. Egg Muffins with Spinach and Feta

Egg muffins are an easy, low-carb breakfast option that can be made in batches and stored in the fridge or freezer for a quick meal on the go. These egg muffins are loaded with spinach and feta, making them nutrient-dense and delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 6 large eggs
  • 1 cup spinach, chopped
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/4 cup onion, diced
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the Oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a muffin tin with cooking spray or line with muffin liners.
  2. Prepare the Mixture: In a bowl, whisk together the eggs, spinach, feta, onion, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.
  3. Fill the Muffin Tin: Pour the egg mixture evenly into the muffin tin, filling each cup about 3/4 full.
  4. Bake: Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the eggs are set and lightly golden on top.
  5. Store: Let the egg muffins cool before transferring them to an airtight container. Store in the fridge for up to 5 days or freeze for longer storage.

Why It’s Great for Meal Prep:

Egg muffins are a great source of protein and can be customized with various vegetables and cheese. They are easy to reheat and can be enjoyed on their own or paired with a side salad for a complete meal. These muffins also make for a convenient breakfast or snack.

Final Thoughts

Meal prepping doesn’t have to be complicated. With these five simple and healthy recipes, you can set yourself up for a week of nutritious, delicious meals that require minimal effort and maximize your time. Whether you’re looking for a protein-packed stir-fry, a light and refreshing salad, or a hearty bowl of roasted vegetables, these recipes will keep you on track with your health goals while making your busy life a little bit easier. Happy prepping!
