Cooking with Lavender: The Sweet and Savory Uses for this Floral Ingredient


Delight your taste buds by incorporating the enchanting aroma and delicate flavor of lavender into your culinary creations. This floral ingredient is often associated with relaxation and indulgence, making it a tempting choice for both savory and sweet dishes. In this post, we’ll explore a range of delightful lavender-infused recipes and additional tips for using this unique ingredient in your kitchen. Let’s dive into the world of lavender cuisine and uncover the secrets to creating unforgettable, aromatic dishes!

  1. Lavender Lemonade

Capture the essence of summer by crafting a refreshing glass of lavender lemonade. The subtle floral notes and citrusy tang of the lemon perfectly complement each other to create an invigorating and truly unique beverage. To prepare this heavenly drink:

  • Gather a handful of fresh lavender sprigs and gently bruise them to release their aroma.
  • Combine the lavender with 2 cups of water and bring to a gentle simmer.
  • Remove from heat and let it steep for 10 minutes.
  • Strain the mixture and mix in 2 cups of freshly squeezed lemon juice and sugar to taste.
  • Serve over ice and enjoy a taste of heaven.
  1. Lavender Shortbread Cookies

For a delectable dessert that will leave your guests craving more, try your hand at lavender-infused shortbread cookies. The floral notes of the lavender perfectly balance the buttery and sweet shortbread, creating a delicate, memorable treat:

  • Preheat your oven to 325°F (165°C) and prepare a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  • Cream together 1 cup of softened unsalted butter, 1/2 cup of powdered sugar, and 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract.
  • Add 2 cups of all-purpose flour and 1 teaspoon of dried lavender (finely ground) to the mixture.
  • Knead the dough until well combined and then roll out to about 1/4-inch thickness.
  • Cut into desired shapes with a cookie cutter and place them onto the prepared baking sheet.
  • Bake for 20-25 minutes or until the edges turn a light golden color.
  • Allow to cool completely before serving, and enjoy!
  1. Lavender Chicken

Elevate your usual chicken dishes by infusing them with the delicate flavor of lavender. This aromatic herb pairs wonderfully with the subtle taste of chicken, creating a dish that’s both intriguing and utterly delicious.

  • In a small bowl, combine 1 teaspoon of dried lavender (finely ground), 1 tablespoon of olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  • Rub the lavender mixture onto 4 chicken breasts and let marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  • Preheat your grill or oven to 375°F (190°C).
  • Grill or roast the chicken for about 20 minutes or until fully cooked. Make sure the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
  • Allow the chicken to rest for a few minutes before serving, garnishing with chopped fresh parsley.
  1. Lavender Raspberry Tart

For a delightful dessert that combines floral and fruity flavors, try this delightful lavender raspberry tart. The slightly sweet pastry crust combined with a tangy raspberry lavender filling is sure to please even the most discerning of palates:

  • Prepare a pastry crust by combining 2 cups of all-purpose flour, 1/2 teaspoon of salt, 1 cup of unsalted butter (chilled), 3 tablespoons of ice water, and 1 teaspoon of dried lavender (finely ground).
  • Press the dough into a tart pan and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.
  • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C) and bake the crust for 20-25 minutes or until golden brown.
  • For the filling, combine 2 cups of fresh raspberries, 1/4 cup of sugar, 1 tablespoon of cornstarch, 1/4 cup of water, and 1 teaspoon of dried lavender in a saucepan.
  • Bring to a boil, stirring constantly, and then reduce heat to simmer for 5 minutes.
  • Let the filling cool slightly before pouring it over the baked crust.
  • Refrigerate until fully set and serve chilled.
  1. Lavender Infused Honey

Enhance your sweet and savory dishes by creating your own lavender-infused honey. This fragrant ingredient adds a touch of indulgence and adds depth to your culinary creations:

  • Combine 2 cups of honey and 1 teaspoon of dried lavender (finely ground) in a saucepan over low heat.
  • Stir gently until the lavender is fully soaked, and then let the mixture steep for about 10 minutes.
  • Strain the honey through a fine mesh sieve and store in an airtight jar.
  • Use this extraordinary honey to sweeten your coffee or tea, coat your lamb chops in it before grilling or add to your favorite salad dressing!

Incorporating lavender into your culinary creations is a delightful way to add a touch of sophistication and intrigue into your dishes. By experimenting with different recipes and flavors, you’ll soon discover new and exciting ways to use this aromatic ingredient. So gather your tools, and let your taste buds be inspired by the sweet and savory world of lavender-infused cuisine. Happy cooking!
