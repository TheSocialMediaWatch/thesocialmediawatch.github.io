Macramé Wall Hangings: Knot Your Average DIY


Macramé wall hangings have become a popular trend in home décor, adding a touch of bohemian charm to any space. With their intricate knots and unique designs, these handcrafted pieces create a stunning focal point that can transform the look and feel of a room. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of macramé and provide a step-by-step guide on how to create your own macramé wall hanging.

What is Macramé?

Macramé is the art of knotting cord or rope to create decorative patterns and designs. Originating from Arabic weaving techniques in the 13th century, macramé gained popularity during the Victorian era and has since experienced a resurgence in the modern world. Macramé is known for its versatility and can be used to create various items such as plant hangers, curtains, and, of course, wall hangings.

Choosing Your Materials

To get started with macramé, you’ll need a few basic materials:

  1. Macramé cord: Choose a cord that suits your desired look and feel. Cotton and jute cords are popular options, as they are easy to work with and provide a natural, rustic vibe.

  2. Wooden dowel or metal ring: This will serve as the base for your wall hanging. You can find dowels or rings in various sizes and shapes to suit your preference.

  3. Scissors: A sharp pair of scissors is essential for cutting the cord and trimming your knots.

  4. Measuring tape: This will help you ensure accurate measurements as you work on your wall hanging.

Getting Started: Basic Knots

Before diving into your macramé project, it’s important to familiarize yourself with a few basic knots. These knots will form the foundation of your design and allow you to create intricate patterns.

  1. Square Knot: This knot is created by crossing two cords and tying them in a square shape. It is commonly used in macramé to create a series of knots that form a pattern.

  2. Lark’s Head Knot: This knot is used to attach cords to your base. It involves folding a cord in half, placing the folded end over the dowel or ring, and pulling the loose ends through the folded loop.

  3. Half Hitch Knot: This knot is created by looping one cord over another and pulling it through the loop. It can be used to create diagonal or chevron patterns in your macramé design.

Creating Your Macramé Wall Hanging

Now that you’re familiar with the basic knots, it’s time to start creating your macramé wall hanging. Follow these steps to bring your vision to life:

  1. Prepare your base: Attach your cords to the dowel or ring using lark’s head knots. Be sure to space them evenly to create a balanced design.

  2. Start knotting: Begin by creating a row of square knots using adjacent cords. Repeat this process until you’ve reached your desired length.

  3. Add variation: To add visual interest to your wall hanging, experiment with different knot patterns and alternating cords.

  4. Incorporate fringe: If you prefer a more bohemian look, consider adding fringe to the bottom of your wall hanging. Simply cut additional cords to your desired length and attach them using lark’s head knots.

  5. Finishing touches: Once you’re satisfied with your design, trim any excess cord and tidy up your knots. You can also comb out the fringe for a softer, more textured look.

Showcasing Your Macramé Masterpiece

Now that your macramé wall hanging is complete, it’s time to find the perfect spot to showcase your creation. Macramé wall hangings can be hung in various ways, such as using a hook, a nail, or even a decorative branch. Experiment with different heights and arrangements to find what works best for your space.

Remember, the beauty of macramé lies in its handmade nature, so embrace any imperfections and let your creativity shine. Whether you’re a seasoned crafter or a beginner looking to try something new, macramé wall hangings offer a unique and rewarding DIY project that will add a personal touch to your home.

So gather your materials, unleash your creativity, and get ready to knot your way to a stunning macramé masterpiece. Happy crafting!
