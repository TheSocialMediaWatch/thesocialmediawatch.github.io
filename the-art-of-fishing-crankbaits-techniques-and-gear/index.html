The Art of Fishing Crankbaits: Techniques and Gear


Fishing with crankbaits is a popular and effective technique that anglers of all skill levels can enjoy. Crankbaits are versatile lures that can mimic the movement of various prey, making them attractive to a wide range of fish species. In this comprehensive guide, we will delve into the art of fishing with crankbaits, exploring different techniques and essential gear to enhance your fishing experience.

Understanding Crankbaits

Crankbaits are hard-bodied fishing lures designed to imitate the swimming action of baitfish or other aquatic creatures. These lures come in various shapes, sizes, and colors, each serving a specific purpose based on the fishing conditions and target species. The key feature of crankbaits is their diving lip, which determines how deep the lure will dive when retrieved.

Types of Crankbaits

  1. Shallow Diving Crankbaits: These are ideal for fishing in shallow waters, typically diving no more than 6 feet below the surface. They work well in areas with submerged vegetation or rocky structures.

  2. Medium Diving Crankbaits: These lures dive to moderate depths, around 6-12 feet, making them versatile for a variety of fishing environments.

  3. Deep Diving Crankbaits: Designed to reach depths of 12 feet or more, these crankbaits are great for targeting fish in deeper waters or along drop-offs.

Techniques for Fishing with Crankbaits

1. Casting and Retrieving

One of the most common techniques for fishing with crankbaits is to cast the lure out and retrieve it at a steady pace. The key is to vary your retrieval speed to imitate the movement of injured baitfish, triggering a predatory response from the fish.

2. Pause and Jerk

Another effective technique is to cast the crankbait, let it sink to the desired depth, then give it a sharp jerk followed by a pause. This erratic action can entice fish by simulating a wounded or disoriented prey.

3. Deflection Fishing

When fishing in areas with underwater obstacles like rocks or logs, intentionally deflecting the crankbait off these structures can trigger reactionary strikes from nearby fish. This technique requires precision casting and a keen eye on your lure’s movement.

Essential Gear for Crankbait Fishing

1. Rod and Reel

Choose a medium to medium-heavy spinning or baitcasting rod paired with a reel that provides smooth drag and good line capacity. The rod action should allow you to cast accurately and feel the subtle strikes of fish.

2. Fishing Line

Opt for monofilament or fluorocarbon line with a test weight suitable for the target species and fishing conditions. Thinner lines allow for better lure action but may be less abrasion-resistant.

3. Crankbaits

Build a collection of crankbaits in different sizes, shapes, and diving depths to cover various fishing scenarios. Experiment with colors and patterns to match the prevalent forage in the area you are fishing.

4. Terminal Tackle

Carry a selection of quality hooks, swivels, and snap connectors to ensure secure connections between your line and crankbait. Proper terminal tackle can prevent lost fish due to equipment failure.

Final Thoughts

Mastering the art of fishing with crankbaits requires practice, patience, and a good understanding of the behavior of the fish you are targeting. By honing your techniques and investing in the right gear, you can increase your chances of landing that trophy catch. Remember to adapt your approach based on the prevailing conditions and be open to experimenting with different techniques to find what works best in each fishing situation.

Happy fishing and tight lines!
