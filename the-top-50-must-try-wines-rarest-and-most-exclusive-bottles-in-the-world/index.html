The Top 50 Must-Try Wines: Rarest and Most Exclusive Bottles in the World


Wine enthusiasts are truly spoiled for choice in today’s world. From vibrant organic selections to complex aged blends, each bottle offers an opportunity for a distinct experience. But, there is so much more out there to discover! Read on for our top 50 must-try wines.

  1. Chateau Margaux 2009 Chateau Margaux, a Premier Grand Cru Classé estate in Bordeaux, stretches across the Medoc region producing remarkable red blends. Known for their'15’ and'09’ vintages, Chateau Margaux’s 2009 bottle, a blend of Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, and Petit Verdot, is renowned for its rich, concentrated flavors.

  2. DRC’s Romanée Conti Grand Cru Domaine de la Romanée-Conti, the legendary wine domaine of Vosne-Romanée, features Grand Cru wines that immediately excite the senses. Their Romanée Conti Grand Cru, renowned for a crystalline purity, and ethereal mix of fruit and spices is the epitome of grandeur.

  3. Screaming Eagle Cabernet Sauvignon This Napa Valley estate flourishes in a location that provides both ideal climate and volcanic soil types. Screaming Eagle’s Cabernet Sauvignon took the industry by storm, right after its debut, thanks to its deep, velvety texture and intense fruit flavors.

  4. De Man Goes Pinot Noir De Man Goes, a sixth-generation, family-owned vineyard of Belgium, is world-renowned for its unique Pinot Noir. It possesses a delicate balance of creaminess, red fruit, and oak that makes it a must-try bottle.

  5. **Double Magnum of Puligny-Montrachet “Clos de Caizi” The Clos de Caizi, situated in Puligny-Montrachet, boasts of its impeccable Les Faverts vineyard, housing chardonnay with exceptional character. A double magnum of this wine is the perfect treat for enthusiasts of a rich, complex mineral flavors.

  6. Penfolds Alpha1 The genius winemaking by Peter Gago & Co. produces the Penfolds Alpha series, eye-catching bottles that have a solid fanbase. Alpha1, an Australian Shiraz, Cabernet Sauvignon blend, showcases elegant oak influence and a concentrated fruit profile.

  7. Chateau Lafleur 2007 This tiny, organic Pomerol estate produces a Cabernet Franc-driven wine that confounds expectation. The 2007 Chateau Lafleur is widely acclaimed for its subtle yet complex bouquet and palate, representing a harmonious balance.

  8. Soliste’s Pinot Noir Soliste’s Pinot Noir is fruit forward, earthy, and captivating, produced from family-owned vineyard sites in Oregon’s Willamette Valley.

  9. Vinous’ VINOUS Romané-Conti’s branch of Domaines de la appeared just to launch its Vinous label, a blend of seven Grand Cru Vineyards. This exquisite red combines elegance and complexity with utmost finesse.

  10. Armando de Benedictis’ Sassovigno Brunello di Montalcino Riserva Armando de Benedictis’ Sassovigno Riserva is a masterpiece in the glass, characterized by its iconic ruby color and a whispering aroma in the bottle.

This list offers a narrow glimpse into the diverse world of wine. Considering factors like terroir, climate, and grape type, each bottle tells a unique drama of place and time. Favored above all is the story of traditional proportions, long-aged vintages, and ancient vineyards working with contemporary techniques. One sip can transport us to the heart of winemaking all across the globe, making our own corners a little more exciting. Cheers!
