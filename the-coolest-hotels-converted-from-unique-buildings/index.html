The Coolest Hotels Converted from Unique Buildings


Are you tired of the same old hotel experience? Looking for something out of the ordinary? Well, look no further! In this blog post, we will take you on a virtual tour of some of the coolest hotels that have been converted from unique buildings. From former prisons to decommissioned airplanes, these properties offer an unforgettable stay that will leave you with stories to tell for years to come.

1. The Liberty Hotel - Boston, Massachusetts

Located in the heart of Boston, The Liberty Hotel was once the Charles Street Jail, a historic prison that housed some of the city’s most notorious criminals. Today, the hotel seamlessly blends the building’s original architectural elements with modern luxury. The former exercise yard is now a stunning courtyard, while the jail’s catwalks have been transformed into stylish guest rooms. The Liberty Hotel offers a truly unique experience that combines history and elegance.

2. The Queen Mary - Long Beach, California

If you’ve ever dreamed of staying on a luxury ocean liner, The Queen Mary is the perfect destination for you. This iconic ship, which sailed the Atlantic Ocean for over three decades, has been permanently docked in Long Beach and transformed into a one-of-a-kind hotel. Guests can choose from a variety of accommodations, including staterooms and suites that have been lovingly restored to their original Art Deco splendor. The Queen Mary also offers a range of onboard amenities, including restaurants, bars, and even a spa. It’s a truly unique experience that will transport you back in time.

3. The Freehand - Miami, Florida

Nestled in Miami’s vibrant Arts and Entertainment District, The Freehand is a hip and trendy hotel housed in a former Art Deco building. The property offers a range of accommodation options, from private rooms to shared dorms, catering to both budget travelers and those looking for a more luxurious experience. The Freehand also features a lush courtyard, a rooftop pool, and a stylish cocktail bar. With its laid-back atmosphere and lively social scene, this hotel is a favorite among young travelers.

4. The Silo Hotel - Cape Town, South Africa

Situated in the heart of Cape Town’s historic V&A Waterfront, The Silo Hotel is a true architectural marvel. The property is housed in a former grain silo, which has been transformed into a luxurious hotel with breathtaking views of the city and Table Mountain. The hotel’s design pays homage to its industrial past, with the original grain elevators now serving as unique guest rooms. The Silo Hotel also features a rooftop bar and restaurant, offering a perfect spot to soak in the beauty of Cape Town.

5. The Caves - Negril, Jamaica

For a truly unforgettable Caribbean getaway, look no further than The Caves in Negril, Jamaica. This boutique hotel is built into a cliffside, offering stunning panoramic views of the Caribbean Sea. Each of the 12 private cottages is uniquely designed and features open-air living spaces and private plunge pools. The Caves also boasts a network of natural caves, which have been transformed into intimate dining spaces and a spa. It’s an idyllic retreat for those seeking romance and tranquility.

Conclusion

When it comes to hotels, why settle for the ordinary when you can stay in a truly extraordinary place? The hotels converted from unique buildings mentioned in this blog post offer a blend of history, innovation, and luxury, providing an experience like no other. Whether you choose to stay in a former jail, an ocean liner, or a grain silo, you’re guaranteed a stay that will be nothing short of memorable. So why not add a touch of adventure to your next trip and book a stay at one of these coolest hotels? You won’t be disappointed!
