The Ultimate Guide to Cross Browser Testing


Cross browser testing is an essential step in the development process to ensure that your website works correctly across multiple browsers and devices. This guide provides a comprehensive overview of cross browser testing, covering everything from understanding browser platitudes to creating a reliable testing strategy.

Browser Market Share

Before diving into cross-browser testing, it’s essential to know which browsers users are using. Understanding the market share of various browsers will help prioritize your testing efforts.

Google Chrome leads the pack, holding a 64.87% market share. Mozilla Firefox (ноч) follows with 18.81%, whereas Safari (апла) holds a 10.63% share. Microsoft Edge and Opera round out the top five with 5.64% and 1.85%, respectively.

While these percentages are crucial, local preferences and specific industries may differ. So, it’s best to cater to users’ preferences and develop a strategy that covers niche browsers and devices.

Understanding Browser Characteristics

Each browser behaves differently, which requires individual testing to ensure your website behaves uniformly across browsers. Here are some notable differences between browsers:

  • Rendering: Every browser has unique rendering engines, such as Blink (Chrome and Opera), Gecko (Firefox), WebKit (Safari), and Trident (Internet Explorer). These engines interpret HTML and CSS code differently, leading to deviations in how your website appears.

  • Support: Browsers support different technologies and standards at different rates. For instance, Safari was the last browser to adopt HTTP/2, while Chrome introduced CSS Grid late.

  • Performance: Browsers perform differently based on how they handle JavaScript execution and asset loading, impacting page speed and user experience.

  • Security: Each browser implements security measures differently, affecting cookies and local storage policies. For example, Safari blocks third-party cookies by default.

Important Note: Internet Explorer is no longer supported as Microsoft ended its support on June 15, 2021. As such, consider it during your cross-browser testing continuum.

Strategies for Cross Browser Testing

Carrying out comprehensive and quality cross-browser testing requires the following strategic approaches:

1. Device-colored Testing

Testing on various devices is crucial since users access websites via smartphones, desktops, tablets, and more. Ensure your site responds well to screen sizes and resolutions while optimizing load times for each device.

2. Automatic Testing

Utilizing automated tools such as Selenium and Cypress can ensure your site runs smoothly across browsers and platforms. These tools minimize human error factors and provide comprehensive reporting for future reference.

3. Real Device Testing

Testing on actual devices is vital to ensure your site functions as expected in real-world scenarios. Real device testing can provide scenarios that emulators and simulators may not cover.

Creating a Cross Browser Testing Strategy

Developing a cross-browser testing strategy requires considerable research and planning. Focus on the following elements when establishing your strategy:

  1. Testing Browsers: Start with the browsers holding a majority market share and gradually shift to niche browsers.

  2. Devices: Ensure your website works on top smartphones, tablets, and desktops covering various operating systems.

  3. Frequency: Opt for continuous integration and testing to discover bugs early, reducing the impact on users.

  4. Testing Environment: Use virtual machines and cloud-based platforms for flexibility and convenience.

  5. Testing Framework: Choose a suitable framework like Selenium or Cypress to minimize human error and optimize efficiency.

  6. Support Devices: Ensure your website functions as intended for older devices while taking into account the end of support for some browsers.

  7. TH’s and WCAG: Ensure your site meets the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) and follows the Contrasting Checker to ensure an inclusive experience.

Reviewing Cross Browser Testing Results

After testing, critically review your results to improve your site’s overall performance. Consider the following factors:

  1. Reporting: Generate reports outlining issues and recommendations.

  2. Performance Metrics: Analyze data on performance issues, offering insights for optimizations.

  3. Feedback Mechanism: Incorporate a feedback mechanism, allowing users to report issues directly.

Cross browser testing ensures your site performs robustly across platforms, increasing user satisfaction and credibility. Following this guide will help you develop a cross-browser testing strategy that provides the best user experience possible.
