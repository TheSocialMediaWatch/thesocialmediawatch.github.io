The Top 5 Open World Games You Should Play


When it comes to video games, few experiences can match the sense of total freedom that open world games provide. These titles invite players to lose themselves in sprawling landscapes, immersive storylines, and endless possibilities for exploration and creativity. Over the years, the genre has evolved, pushing boundaries with realistic environments, deep narratives, emergent gameplay, and multiplayer experiences. Whether you’re a newcomer or a seasoned gamer, the right open world adventure can captivate you for hours—or even months.

Let’s dive into the top five open world games that have set the gold standard and why they deserve a place in your game library.

1. The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild

The release of The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild marked a turning point not just for the beloved franchise, but for the genre as a whole. Set in a vast reimagining of Hyrule, this game offers a level of freedom and emergent gameplay that still feels revolutionary.

Why Breath of the Wild Stands Out

What makes Breath of the Wild exceptional isn’t just its size, but its density and interactivity. The world is more than something to traverse from quest A to quest B—every rock, cliff, and weather system becomes part of your interactions. The stamina system encourages creative approaches to exploration, and physics-based puzzles empower the player to use logic and experiment.

The narrative is woven subtly through environmental storytelling and memory-based cutscenes, rewarding curious players with deeper lore at every turn. The celebrated chemistry engine allows for amazing interactions, whether you’re frying apples for a quick snack or setting a whole forest ablaze to flush out enemies.

The Magic of Exploration

The absence of artificial boundaries lets you climb mountains, surf on shields down snowy slopes, or paraglide off the highest peaks. You’re encouraged to trust your curiosity—the mountain in the distance isn’t just scenery, it’s a challenge. The sense of discovery rivals that first iconic step onto Hyrule Field in Ocarina of Time, but now blown up into a persistent miracle at every corner.

2. The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt

When it launched, The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt immediately set a new benchmark for narrative depth and world-building. Based on Andrzej Sapkowski’s novels, this game thrusts you into a mature fantasy world teeming with complex stories and morally ambiguous choices.

Unparalleled Storytelling and World Depth

Every inch of the world feels handcrafted, from wind-swept city rooftops to the haunted swamps. You play as Geralt of Rivia, a monster hunter with sharp wit, even sharper swords, and a complicated past. What truly sets this world apart is how every quest feels meaningful. Side missions aren’t mere fetch quests, but deeply written stories with consequences and intricate characters.

The main storyline, involving the titular Wild Hunt and Geralt’s search for his surrogate daughter Ciri, is bursting with twists and emotional payoffs. Your choices actually matter, shaping the world and its inhabitants in profound ways.

Immersive Combat and Exploration

Don’t let the game’s literary pedigree fool you—the swordplay, magic (signs), and alchemy are satisfying and complex. Roaming across Novigrad, Velen, and Skellige, you can gallop on horseback, sail through storms, and battle everything from werewolves to political intrigue.

The two major expansions, Hearts of Stone and Blood and Wine, add dozens of hours and some of the series’ very best content, making Witcher 3 a game that could easily consume 100+ hours.

3. Red Dead Redemption 2

Open world experiences are often measured by how alive they feel, and Red Dead Redemption 2 is one of the most convincing recreations of a living, breathing world in the history of games.

Immersion in America’s Old West

Set against the crumbling myth of the American frontier, you play as Arthur Morgan, a member of the Van der Linde gang. This game’s dedication to detail borders on obsessive. The world reacts to your actions—people remember you, towns evolve, and the ecosystem pulses with surprising realism.

From weather effects and time of day to the palpable weight of choices you make, everything in Red Dead Redemption 2 is built to immerse. Whether you’re robbing a train, gambling in saloons, or hunting legendary animals, every activity ties back to shaping Arthur’s journey and the fate of his found family.

Deep Narrative and Dynamic Interactions

The emotional core of the game is its story—full of loyalty, betrayal, loss, and a sense of fading grandeur. It achieves emotional highs rarely matched, and manages to tell an introspective tale within the chaos of gunfights and lawless escapades.

Your interactions, both scripted and unscripted, feel organic. Random events and dynamic encounters lurk around every corner. Intimate conversations by the campfire can be interrupted by a sudden ambush—or the appearance of a roaming bear drawn to food scraps.

4. Grand Theft Auto V

No discussion of open world gaming is complete without Grand Theft Auto V. Rockstar’s magnum opus captures the extremes of urban life within the sprawling, fictional City of Los Santos and its surrounding countryside.

Satirical, Expansive, and Daring

Known for its biting satire and freedom, GTA V features three distinctly different protagonists whose lives intertwine through heists and misadventures. The sheer breadth of activities is staggering—from base-jumping, stock market manipulation, and racing, to simply enjoying the radio while cruising under the city’s neon haze.

The sense of scale is only matched by the cultural commentary found in every conversation, ad, and song. The city feels as unruly and unpredictable as its real-world counterparts, filled with NPCs leading their own simulated lives.

Infinite Playability—Online and Offline

Years after release, GTA Online continues to grow, adding new missions, vehicles, and properties to own. Whether you want to cause carnage in singleplayer or create havoc with friends in multiplayer, there’s a never-ending stream of content.

Part of the game’s genius lies in how it rewards curiosity—there are hidden easter eggs, outlandish subplots, absurd secrets, and elaborate stunts waiting to be discovered. It’s as much a social simulation as it is an action sandbox.

5. Elden Ring

Despite being a more recent arrival, Elden Ring rapidly carved out its place among open world classics. FromSoftware’s reputation for challenging, atmospheric games entered new territory by fusing the freedom of a vast landscape with the tight design their fanbase loves.

A New Kind of Open World Design

Elden Ring’s world, The Lands Between, is shrouded in mystery, secrets, and danger. Unlike traditional open world games that hold your hand, Elden Ring throws you into the wind. There are no map markers flooding your vision; instead, curiosity and exploration are rewarded with astonishing sights, terrifying bosses, and long-forgotten ruins.

The sense of achievement that comes from conquering a hulking foe or unearthing lost lore is intoxicating. Every region feels distinct, rife with opportunities to pick your battles, uncover shortcuts, or stumble upon hidden dungeons laden with rewards.

Fusion of Difficulty and Freedom

While FromSoftware’s notorious difficulty remains, the open world approach softens the frustration. Stuck on a menacing foe? Wander off in another direction—there’s always another adventure to pursue, another weapon to find, or an NPC’s tragic tale to unravel.

The deep combat system, wealth of classes, and the seamless multiplayer features make every journey unique. Players can leave messages, join forces, or invade each other, creating emergent stories that add to the game’s mythos.

What Makes Great Open World Games?

While each of these games has a distinct style and story, they share some fundamental traits. The worlds feel alive—brimming with stories, secrets, and personality. Exploration isn’t just a checklist to be completed, but an act of creative play and meaningful discovery. Dynamic systems interact to create memorable moments, and the freedom to tackle challenges in your own way keeps you coming back.

Whether you’re scaling Hyrule’s cliffs, charting Skellige’s stormy seas, riding across the American frontier, tearing up Los Santos’ streets, or braving the terror of Elden Ring’s castles, these worlds reward imagination and persistence.

Honorable Mentions

No “top 5” list can possibly cover every gem. Here are a few worth exploring, each bringing something unique to the table:

  • Cyberpunk 2077: After a rocky start, post-patches Night City is one of the most visually arresting and deeply interactive open worlds out there.
  • Assassin’s Creed Odyssey: Ancient Greece comes alive: a perfect choice for those who want both history and myth.
  • Horizon Zero Dawn: Combines lush landscapes with a compelling story and robotic monstrosities.
  • Minecraft: For creativity-driven explorers, the possibilities are endless.
  • No Man’s Sky: When you want an entire universe to explore.

Final Thoughts

Open world games are more than just vast maps dotted with objectives—they’re gateways to new realities. Over the past decade, these five titles have pushed the envelope, inspiring millions of players to forge their own stories, whether through cutscenes, combat, or quiet moments atop a virtual mountain.

Whatever your gaming tastes—fantasy, crime, horror, or historical epic—the breadth and quality found in these worlds guarantee there’s an open world adventure waiting for you to make it your own. All that’s left? Choose your game, step outside the hub, and see where your journey leads.
