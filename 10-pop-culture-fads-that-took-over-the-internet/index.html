10 Pop Culture Fads That Took Over the Internet


Forget what you thought you knew about fleeting obsessions. The internet has a special knack for transforming quirky trends into global phenomenons in a matter of hours. From oddball memes to dance challenges and viral games, pop culture fads have redefined the way we interact, create, and even perceive humor. Some trends vanished as quickly as they arrived, while others carved out a permanent spot in online history. Let’s take a trip down the digital rabbit hole and revisit ten unforgettable fads that took cyberspace by storm.

1. The Ice Bucket Challenge: Social Good Goes Viral

In the summer of 2014, a chilly wave swept through social media. The Ice Bucket Challenge dared users to pour ice water over themselves, post the footage, and challenge friends to do the same—all in the name of raising awareness for ALS (amyotrophic lateral sclerosis). Suddenly, feeds were flooded with drenched celebrities, politicians, and everyday folks. The powerful blend of peer pressure, public spectacle, and a good cause led to over $220 million raised for ALS research worldwide.

What made this challenge irresistible? It was just the right balance of silliness, accountability, and altruism—plus, it encouraged creative twists, leading to unforgettable videos and a whole lotta soggy summer afternoons.

2. Bottle Flipping: Skill, Luck, and Pure Satisfaction

Toss a half-filled plastic bottle, hope it lands upright, and—voila!—you’re a legend. The bottle flipping fad skyrocketed to fame in 2016, transforming lunchrooms, offices, and bedrooms into arenas for precision and luck. The satisfying “thunk” of a successful flip, often captured on video, spawned compilation montages and even high-stakes trick shots.

Schools attempted bans. Teachers pleaded for sanity. Yet the simplicity and dopamine rush of a perfect flip were impossible to suppress. For a brief, glorious window, everyone was compelled to try “just one more” flip.

3. Planking: The Original Viral Pose

Long before TikTok challenges, there was planking: the clandestine art of lying face-down, rigid like a board, in bizarre or unexpected locations. The zanier the setting—rooftops, escalators, supermarket shelves—the more internet points awarded. Photos blitzed social networks in 2011, with celebrities joining in and global competitions erupting.

Though short-lived, planking paved the way for a whole genre of visual internet humor, proving that a simple act, repeated en masse, could connect and amuse millions.

4. Harlem Shake: Thirty Seconds of Organized Chaos

Who would have guessed that a bass drop could unleash absolute anarchy? In early 2013, the Harlem Shake meme seized the web. It began innocuously: a lone person dancing in a helmet, surrounded by impassive bystanders, all set to Baauer’s thumping track. Then, at the beat drop, everyone joins in, writhing and cavorting wildly—often in costumes or with props.

Thousands of versions appeared in just weeks, spanning college campuses, offices, military bases, and more. The Harlem Shake encapsulated the spirit of DIY viral video, celebrating spontaneity and oddball humor on a global stage.

5. Gangnam Style: The First Billion-View Music Video

Before TikTok and Reels, one video reigned supreme: Psy’s “Gangnam Style.” In 2012, the Korean pop sensation and his signature horse-riding dance galloped across the world, becoming the first YouTube video to surpass a billion views. Parodies, flash mobs, and Halloween costumes soon followed, as did a cascade of new interest in K-pop culture.

The global appeal lay partly in the humorous choreography and catchy melody, but also in the universal joy of something so unabashedly weird uniting millions in synchronized silliness.

6. The Dress: Blue and Black or White and Gold?

It’s rare for a single photo to divide the entire planet. The Dress did just that in 2015. A seemingly mundane image of a dress, posted on Tumblr, sparked a furious debate: was it blue and black, or white and gold? Households were split, friendships tested, and scientists called in to explain the phenomenon of color perception and brain function.

Within days, #TheDress was trending globally, news outlets weighed in, and even celebrities posted their votes. The dress briefly turned everyone into armchair neurologists, while reminding us how easily perspectives can clash—even over something as simple as fabric.

7. Fidget Spinners: The Ultimate Distraction Device

Designed as a toy to aid focus, fidget spinners whirred their way into the halls of viral fame in 2017. With its hypnotic spin and pocket-sized portability, the spinner slid seamlessly into classrooms and offices. Videos showed off creative tricks, custom designs, and even multi-spinner manipulation.

Retailers struggled to keep shelves stocked, while teachers waged wars against the relentless humming. Like many fads before it, fidget spinners flared fast and faded, but their brief ubiquity cemented their spot in internet lore.

8. Yanny or Laurel: Auditory Illusion Madness

Move over, The Dress. In 2018, the internet met its next reality-warping debate: Yanny or Laurel. An audio clip, posted to Reddit and then Twitter, split listeners in two camps—each hearing a completely different word.

Podcasts dissected the physics of sound frequencies, linguists got involved, and users put their friends’ ears to the test. The viral frenzy underscored just how subjective perception could be. For those keeping score, science suggests both words are present—but that never stopped the arguments.

9. Cinnamon Challenge: Spice Up Your Timeline

Not all viral fads are advisable. Enter the Cinnamon Challenge, a dare that swept YouTube and social media from 2010 onward. The rules were simple but foolhardy: attempt to swallow a spoonful of ground cinnamon in under 60 seconds, without drinking anything.

Cue coughing fits, laughter, and more than a little regret. Health experts quickly cautioned against the trend, but the allure of online fame—and the spectacle of epic failure—fueled millions of attempts. As a cautionary tale, it ranks among the internet’s most infamous challenges.

10. TikTok Dances: A New Era of Digital Fame

If there’s a soundtrack to the 2020s, it’s the snappy hooks and choreographed moves of TikTok challenges. From “Renegade” to “Savage Love” and “Blinding Lights,” users across generations have joined in, learning routines and recording their own takes.

Unlike earlier trends, TikTok dances evolved in real time, with new moves and remixes popping up daily. Stars were born in bedrooms, collaborations crossed continents, and suddenly, anyone could reach millions from home. It’s democratized fame and offered a creative escape during lockdowns, establishing itself as the pop culture fad for a new generation.

The Anatomy of Viral Fads

What makes a pop culture trend go viral? A few key ingredients tend to repeat: simplicity, shareability, and a spark of unpredictability. Whether it’s the way a song embeds itself in your brain, the satisfaction of mastering a weird skill, or just the anticipation of a friend’s hilarious fail, viral fads tap into collective curiosity and the ever-present urge to belong.

Of course, not every trend has staying power. The internet’s memory can be as fickle as its attention span. Yet, time and again we see how these fads unite people, if only for a fleeting moment, around something as trivial as a plastic bottle or divisive as audio frequencies.

Looking Ahead: What’s Next for Internet Obsessions?

New platforms, fresh faces, and emerging technologies are constantly rewriting the playbook on what catches fire online. Short-form video, augmented reality, and increasing integration between virtual and physical worlds are paving the way for future fads we can’t yet imagine.

Still, the underlying appeal remains the same: surprise, laughter, and a sense of belonging. Whether you jumped on every trend or just shook your head from afar, the wild ride of internet fads is a testament to creativity, community, and the irresistible lure of the next big thing.

So, what will we be flipping, debating, or dancing to next? Only time—and maybe a little algorithmic magic—will tell.
