The Best Fantasy Series to Start Right Now


If you’ve been searching for that next gripping fantasy world to dive into, you’re in the right place. Fantasy literature has an unrivaled ability to transport readers into realms packed with adventure, epic battles, magic, and unforgettable characters. Whether you want sprawling sagas with deep political intrigue or more character-driven tales with magical twists, the following fantasy series promise immersive storytelling that will keep you turning pages nonstop.

Why Fantasy Continues to Captivate Readers

Fantasy’s enduring appeal lies in its capacity for endless creativity. Authors craft entire universes, complete with unique rules, histories, and mythologies. For readers, this means endless exploration—each series offering its distinct flavor, whether it’s high fantasy with kingdoms and dragons or modern fantasy that blends the magical with the familiar world.

Reading a well-crafted fantasy series offers more than entertainment. It challenges your imagination, encourages empathy by exploring complex characters, and provides profound reflections on power, identity, and humanity through allegory and myth.

Now, let’s get to the heart of the matter—fantasy series that are perfect for starting now.

1. The Stormlight Archive by Brandon Sanderson

Brandon Sanderson’s Stormlight Archive is a modern epic fantasy masterpiece. It instantly pulls readers into the shattered world of Roshar, a place ravaged by magical storms and ancient wars. The narrative weaves multiple perspectives—stalwart warriors, gifted scholars, and mysterious knights—building a deeply layered story of prophecy, honor, and redemption.

What makes this series a standout is Sanderson’s intricate magic system known as “Surgebinding,” combined with complex world-building and memorable character arcs. Each book is a weighty tome, but the payoff is immense, offering rich philosophical queries wrapped in grand adventure.

Ideal if you love:

  • Complex magic systems
  • Multifaceted characters who grow and change
  • Stories with mythic stakes that still feel personal

Best starting point: The Way of Kings (Book 1)

2. The Kingkiller Chronicle by Patrick Rothfuss

For readers who enjoy lyrical prose mixed with intimate storytelling, The Kingkiller Chronicle is a perfect match. The series follows Kvothe, a gifted musician and magician, as he recounts his life story filled with both remarkable achievements and devastating losses.

Rothfuss’s writing is poetic and rich, with magic that feels organic and personal. Unlike sprawling battles or epic quests, this tale’s heart is Kvothe’s journey toward understanding his own destiny mixed with storytelling about love, betrayal, and mystery.

Why start here:

  • Beautiful, immersive writing style
  • A grounded hero with depth and flaws
  • A world where magic feels textured and alive

Begin with: The Name of the Wind

3. The Broken Empire Trilogy by Mark Lawrence

If you prefer fantasy with a darker twist and morally ambiguous characters, The Broken Empire trilogy grabs your attention from the first page. The story revolves around Jorg Ancrath, a ruthless prince on a path of vengeance and conquest, navigating a brutal post-apocalyptic medieval world.

Mark Lawrence’s narrative style underscores raw, gritty realism within fantasy’s framework. The protagonist is unapologetically flawed, making it a compelling study of anti-heroism versus traditional heroism.

Reasons to jump in:

  • Dark themes blended with powerful prose
  • Character-driven storytelling with psychological depth
  • A fresh take on anti-heroes in fantasy

Start with: Prince of Thorns

4. The Wheel of Time by Robert Jordan

A titan in the fantasy genre, The Wheel of Time offers one of the most vast and detailed worlds ever created. This sprawling series follows a massive cast of characters as they contend with fate, prophecy, and an apocalyptic battle between light and shadow.

Jordan’s meticulous world-building combines elements from many mythologies and traditions, creating a diverse cultural landscape. The plot deftly balances political intrigue, personal quests, and a grand cosmic struggle.

Perfect for those who enjoy:

  • Long, immersive epics with plenty of world-building
  • A complex, interwoven cast
  • Magic systems rooted in myth and prophecy

First book: The Eye of the World

5. The Lies of Locke Lamora (Gentleman Bastard Series) by Scott Lynch

If your fantasy tastes lean toward clever schemes, witty banter, and a morally grey underworld, this series is a gem. It centers on Locke Lamora, a master thief and con artist navigating the intricate politics of a Venice-like city beset by crime and corruption.

Scott Lynch combines thrilling heist plots with vivid, well-crafted characters. The prose is engaging and sharp, often laden with humor, yet serious enough to lend emotional depth.

Great for:

  • Heist and crime story enthusiasts within fantasy
  • Characters who are charming anti-heroes
  • Intricate plotting and world details

Recommended beginning: The Lies of Locke Lamora

6. The First Law Trilogy by Joe Abercrombie

Joe Abercrombie reinvigorates grimdark fantasy with gritty realism, dark humor, and unforgettable characters. The series defies the traditional fantasy hero mold, presenting flawed, often morally compromised individuals caught in brutal conflicts.

The narrative style is sharp, direct, and injects a healthy dose of cynicism and wit. The battles are brutal, the politics ruthless, and the character interactions brutally honest, making it perfect for readers tired of black-and-white morality.

Pick this for:

  • Grimdark fantasy lovers
  • Sharp, witty dialogue
  • Complex, multi-layered characters navigating a merciless world

Start here: The Blade Itself

7. Mistborn Trilogy by Brandon Sanderson

Another hit from Sanderson, the Mistborn trilogy offers a unique magic system based on metals and alloys, coupled with a revolutionary storyline that turns traditional fantasy on its head. The story starts in a dystopian empire ruled by an immortal tyrant, with a ragtag group of rebels aiming to overthrow the status quo.

The blend of action, intricate world mechanics, and compelling characters makes this series accessible yet rich. The first trilogy’s ending is both satisfying and a setup for further exploration.

Ideal if you want:

  • Innovative magic systems
  • Fast-paced, plot-driven stories
  • Characters working against overwhelming odds

Begin with: Mistborn: The Final Empire

8. The Earthsea Cycle by Ursula K. Le Guin

For those drawn to timeless fantasy classics with philosophical undercurrents, Earthsea is essential. Le Guin tells the story of Ged, a young wizard coming to terms with his powers and the balance of life and death in a beautifully crafted archipelago world.

The series is notable for its lyrical prose and exploration of themes like identity, equilibrium, and the nature of power. Its simplicity is deceptive, with layers of wisdom embedded in every page.

Perfect for readers who appreciate:

  • Thought-provoking, literary fantasy
  • A focus on internal growth versus external conflict
  • A mythical, almost fairy-tale atmosphere

Kick off with: A Wizard of Earthsea

9. The Inheritance Cycle by Christopher Paolini

For fantasy newcomers or those who grew up loving classic quests involving dragons and heroes, Paolini’s Inheritance Cycle offers a familiar yet enjoyable high fantasy adventure. The story of Eragon, a young farm boy who finds a dragon egg, unfolds into a grand tale of rebellion against tyranny.

While it leans heavily into traditional fantasy tropes, it’s packed with the timeless excitement of discovery, friendship, and battles. The books are accessible for younger readers or anyone starting their fantasy journey.

Great for:

  • Classic high fantasy lovers
  • Readers who enjoy dragon lore
  • Those looking for a coming-of-age adventure

Start here: Eragon

10. The Lightbringer Series by Brent Weeks

Brent Weeks crafts a world where magic is based on the manipulation of light and color, creating a visually stunning and cleverly conceptualized system. The Lightbringer series blends politics, war, and personal struggles against a backdrop of vibrant magical battles and surprising twists.

Weeks’s writing is fast-paced and engaging, making it a page-turner filled with unexpected developments. Complex characters and evolving alliances keep the tension high throughout.

Recommended for:

  • Fans of innovative magic and action-packed plots
  • Political intrigue blended with fantasy
  • Multi-layered character dynamics

Start with: The Black Prism

How to Choose the Right Fantasy Series for You

The best fantasy series for you depends on what kind of experience you seek. Here are a few questions to consider:

  • Do you enjoy slow-burn, deeply immersive world-building, or do you prefer fast-paced action?
  • Are you drawn to traditional heroic quests or stories with morally complex anti-heroes?
  • Do you want extensive political intrigue, or are character-driven stories more your style?
  • How long are you willing to commit? A ten-book series can be thrilling but requires time; shorter trilogies may fit busier schedules.

Fantasy is incredibly diverse and rewarding—whether you’re seeking another Tolkien-inspired epic or something fresh and fierce. Any of the series above will provide a magical escape, new ideas, and unforgettable journeys.

Wrapping Up: Your Next Great Fantasy Adventure Awaits

From the cosmic storms of Roshar to the gritty streets of Camorr, the fantasy worlds awaiting you promise adventure, magic, and deep storytelling. Whether you begin with Sanderson’s masterful epics, Rothfuss’s poetic tales, or Abercrombie’s sharp dark fantasy, you’re stepping into universes where imagination runs wild and stories pulse with life.

Ready to lose yourself in some of the best fantasy series available? Pick one from the list above, curl up, and journey beyond the ordinary. Fantastical realms and extraordinary characters are moments away.

Happy reading!
