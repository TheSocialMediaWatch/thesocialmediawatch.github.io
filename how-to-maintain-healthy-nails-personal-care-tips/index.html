How to Maintain Healthy Nails: Personal Care Tips


Having healthy nails is a sign of overall well-being. However, many of us neglect our nail care and end up with brittle, fragile, or even damaged nails. In this article, we will discuss some personal care tips on how to maintain healthy nails.

Keep Your Nails Hydrated

One of the most important things to keep in mind is to keep your nails hydrated. Dryness can cause your nails to become brittle and prone to breaking. To keep your nails hydrated, apply a moisturizing cuticle oil or nail cream to your nails and cuticles regularly. You can also apply a thick layer of Vaseline to your nails and cuticles before bed and wear gloves to lock in the moisture.

Eat a Balanced Diet

A healthy diet is essential for maintaining healthy nails. Foods rich in vitamins, minerals, and proteins can help promote nail health. Some of the best foods for nail health include biotin-rich foods such as eggs, nuts, and leafy greens, vitamin E-rich foods such as sweet potatoes and avocados, and omega-3 fatty acid-rich foods such as salmon and walnuts.

Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Exposure to harsh chemicals can dry out your nails and cause damage. Avoid using nail polish removers that contain acetone or other harsh chemicals, and opt for gentler alternatives instead. Also, avoid exposing your hands to chemicals such as detergents, cleaning products, or pesticides, and wear gloves when washing dishes or working with chemicals.

Don’t Over-Wash Your Hands

Washing your hands too frequently or using hot water can dry out your nails and cause damage. Try to limit your hand-washing to when it’s absolutely necessary, and use warm water instead of hot water. Also, apply a moisturizing soap or hand cream after washing your hands to help lock in moisture.

Trim Your Nails Correctly

Trimming your nails correctly is important for maintaining healthy nails. Cut your nails straight across and avoid cutting them too short, as this can cause damage and lead to ingrown toenails. Also, use a nail file to smooth out any rough edges or ridges after trimming.

Avoid Biting and Picking

Biting and picking at your nails can cause damage and lead to infection. Try to break the habit by keeping your nails trimmed and using a nail strengthener to help harden your nails. Also, keep your nails moisturized to help reduce the urge to bite or pick at them.

Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help keep your nails healthy and prevent damage. Try to get a trim every 4-6 weeks, or as needed. Also, consider getting a gel or acrylic nail treatment to help strengthen your nails and prevent damage.

Take Care of Your Cuticles

Taking care of your cuticles is important for maintaining healthy nails. Apply a cuticle oil or cream to your cuticles regularly, and gently push back your cuticles with a cuticle pusher or orange stick. Avoid cutting or cutting too closely to the nail bed, as this can cause damage and lead to infection.

Avoid Extreme Temperatures

Extreme temperatures can cause damage to your nails. Avoid exposing your hands to extreme cold or heat, and wear gloves when washing dishes or working with hot or cold objects.

Consider Taking Supplements

Some supplements can help promote nail health. Biotin supplements, in particular, are known to help strengthen and thicken nails. Consult with a doctor or nutritionist before starting any supplements, and follow the recommended dosage.

By following these personal care tips, you can help maintain healthy nails and prevent damage. Remember to keep your nails hydrated, eat a balanced diet, avoid harsh chemicals, and take care of your cuticles. With a little bit of effort and attention, you can have healthy, strong, and beautiful nails.
