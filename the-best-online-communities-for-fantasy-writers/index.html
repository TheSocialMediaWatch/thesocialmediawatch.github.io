The Best Online Communities for Fantasy Writers


Fantasy writing is a realm where creativity knows no bounds, and the only limit is the writer’s own imagination. But even the most imaginative authors benefit from connection—community spaces where ideas flow freely, advice is shared generously, and encouragement fuels the journey. In this vast digital age, fantasy writers have more options than ever to find their tribe online. Whether you’re a beginner dreaming up your first dragon-filled saga, or a seasoned author polishing the final arc of an epic series, joining a thriving online community can be transformative.

Let’s explore some of the best online communities tailored for fantasy writers, exploring what makes each one unique and how they can help you hone your craft, find inspiration, and connect with like-minded creators.

1. Mythic Scribes: The Premier Hub for Fantasy Worldbuilders

If you’re passionate about constructing intricate worlds, mythologies, and lore, Mythic Scribes is a treasure trove. This community revolves around the art of crafting detailed fantasy settings, from magical systems to cultures and histories.

What You’ll Find on Mythic Scribes:

  • Forums rich with discussions on worldbuilding topics like magic rules, species design, and geographic logic.
  • Regular writing prompts to push creativity beyond comfort zones.
  • Resource libraries filled with articles and guides on fantasy tropes and how to subvert them.
  • Supportive critiques from fellow writers and worldbuilders focusing on maintaining internal consistency and depth.

Many members here are obsessed with the minutiae that add layers of realism to fantasy realms, helping you avoid pitfalls like plot holes or cliché world designs.

2. SFF World: Diverse Genres for the Speculative Fiction Writer

The Science Fiction & Fantasy World (SFF World) community brings together writers from a spectrum of speculative genres. Its fantasy section offers a melting pot of perspectives, from high fantasy and dark fantasy to urban and historical fantasy.

Highlights of SFF World:

  • Active forums with thousands of members, ensuring quick feedback and lively conversations.
  • Weekly newsletter featuring writing contests, market news, and recommended reads.
  • Dedicated critique spaces where you can upload chapters or synopses for peer review.
  • In-depth discussions about narrative structure, character development, and genre blending.

Because SFF World spans multiple speculative genres, it’s an excellent place to broaden your understanding of fantasy’s place within the wider speculative fiction landscape.

3. Reddit Communities: Dynamic Conversations and Real-Time Interaction

Among the many platforms available, Reddit hosts some of the most vibrant and easily accessible fantasy writing communities. Below are a couple of subreddits that cater specifically to fantasy writers:

r/Fantasywriters

This subreddit is a bustling hub where writers share tips, ask questions, celebrate milestones, and discuss the craft of fantasy writing.

  • Frequent AMA (Ask Me Anything) sessions with published authors and editors.
  • Threads on everything from developing magical systems to tackling writer’s block.
  • Opportunities for text-based critique and encouragement.

r/worldbuilding

Though not exclusively for writers, this community is perfect for those wrestling with the complexities of designing believable and intriguing worlds.

  • Members post maps, character sketches, and magic system ideas.
  • Constructive feedback helps to deepen and refine speculative elements.
  • Collaborative projects and challenges facilitate dynamic community involvement.

The immediacy and informality of Reddit provide a no-pressure environment for both beginners and pros.

4. Critique Circle: Structured Feedback for Refining Your Fantasy Manuscript

For writers aiming to elevate their drafts with detailed critiques, Critique Circle offers a structured approach rooted in reciprocity. Members earn credits by critiquing others’ work, which they can then spend to receive feedback on their own writing.

What Makes Critique Circle Work Well:

  • Well-defined critique forms that guide reviewers to focus on plot, characterization, pacing, and style.
  • A large user base with an active section dedicated to fantasy and speculative fiction.
  • Options to join genre-specific workshops and focus groups.

Though the platform looks utilitarian, its depth of insight and supportive atmosphere make it a go-to for serious writers who want to polish their manuscripts before publication.

5. Kifo Island: A Collaborative Story-Weaving Experience

For writers interested in co-creative storytelling, Kifo Island operates as a unique online community where collective writing takes center stage. It might not be purely a fantasy writers’ group, but fantasy is a dominant genre among its users.

Unique Features of Kifo Island:

  • Collaborative story creation where multiple authors contribute chapters to ongoing tales set in a shared universe.
  • Opportunities to explore new writing styles and narrative techniques within a community framework.
  • Social features that build friendships and creative partnerships.

This platform is perfect for expanding your skills and connecting with other writers through shared storytelling.

6. The Fantasy Writers’ Club on Facebook

While forums and specialized sites dominate the scene, Facebook is home to several large, active fantasy writing groups. The Fantasy Writers’ Club emphasizes community building through casual interactions and sharing achievements.

What to Expect:

  • Prompt-based writing challenges about dragons, magic, and mythical creatures.
  • Advice threads discussing agents, publishing, and marketing for fantasy novels.
  • Networking opportunities with other writers, beta readers, and even small publishers.

The convenience of Facebook’s interface coupled with the informal tone makes the Fantasy Writers’ Club a welcoming place for writers new to online communities.

7. Discord Servers: Instantaneous Support and Collaboration

Discord has emerged as a powerhouse platform for real-time engagement among fantasy authors. Numerous servers cater specifically to writing, where chats range from casual author banter to serious critique sessions.

Popular Features Across Fantasy Writing Discords:

  • Voice channels for discussion, brainstorming, and live writing sprints.
  • Dedicated channels for sharing worldbuilding files, character art, and plot outlines.
  • Community events like writing marathons and contests to keep motivation high.
  • Friendly and timely peer support on writer’s block, plot holes, and stylistic questions.

Because Discord is interactive and instant, it complements the deeper, more reflective conversations found on forums and social media.

8. Absolute Write Water Cooler: A Diverse and Experienced Writing Community

While not exclusively fantasy-focused, Absolute Write is a legendary forum among writers of all genres. The “Water Cooler” section often features fantasy writers discussing industry trends, query letters, and story crafting techniques.

What Distinguished Absolute Write:

  • A comprehensive set of subforums including publishing advice, freelance queries, and genre-specific writing tips.
  • Some of the most knowledgeable and experienced members willing to share practical wisdom.
  • A thriving marketplace where writers can exchange services like editing and cover design.

For fantasy authors who want to navigate the complexities of publishing alongside craft growth, this community offers invaluable insights.

Tips for Making the Most of Online Writer Communities

Merely joining an online group is the first step. To gain the most from these rich ecosystems, consider the following:

  • Engage authentically: Read through existing posts, add thoughtful comments, and share your progress or struggles.
  • Give and take feedback: Constructive critique is a two-way street, so be ready to offer help as well as accept it.
  • Participate in events: Writing sprints, challenges, and contests help boost productivity and camaraderie.
  • Respect community guidelines: Each platform has its own culture; adapting to that environment creates better relationships.
  • Cross-network: Don’t limit yourself—explore several communities to find which spark your creativity and nurture your growth best.

Final Thoughts: Choosing the Right Place for Your Fantasy Journey

The landscape of online communities for fantasy writers is both broad and rich. Whether you crave detailed discussions on magical systems, fast-paced critique exchanges, collaborative storytelling, or simply a supportive audience for your fantastical tales, there’s a community out there for you.

Look beyond the size of the group and focus on the vibe, engagement level, and resources each community offers. Often, the best support comes from a few dedicated peers you resonate with rather than thousands of distant followers.

Set out with an open heart and a curious mind. Dive into conversations, share your unique voice, and let the collective energy of these online fellowships ignite the magic you’re weaving with words.

Happy writing—and may your worlds be ever boundless and your stories enchant readers far and wide!

If you’re serious about growing as a fantasy writer, pick one or two of these communities to explore this week. The right online connection might be the muse and motivation you need to bring your next epic to life.
