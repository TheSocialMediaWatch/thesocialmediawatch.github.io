Fishing for Walleye in Rivers: Techniques and Strategies


If you’re an avid angler looking for an exciting challenge, fishing for walleye in rivers should be on your bucket list. These elusive and prized fish are known for their delicious taste and thrilling fights. In this blog post, we will explore various techniques and strategies that will help you become a successful walleye angler in river settings.

Understanding the Behavior of Walleye

Before diving into the techniques, it’s crucial to understand the behavior of walleye in rivers. Walleye are known to be ambush predators, lurking in deeper pools or behind structures such as rocks or fallen trees. They are most active during low-light conditions, such as early morning or late evening. By gaining insight into their behavior, you can plan your fishing trips accordingly and increase your chances of success.

Choosing the Right Gear

To tackle walleye in rivers, you’ll need the appropriate gear. Start with a medium to medium-light spinning rod paired with a quality reel. A 6 to 7-foot rod with a sensitive tip will allow you to detect subtle bites and make accurate casts. As for the line, opt for a 6 to 8-pound test monofilament or fluorocarbon line. This will provide the right balance between strength and sensitivity.

Locating Walleye Hotspots

When it comes to river fishing for walleye, location is key. Look for areas with a combination of depth changes, structure, and current breaks. Deep pools, eddies, and undercut banks are prime spots to find walleye. They use these features to hide and ambush their prey. Pay attention to the river’s flow, as walleye often position themselves where the current breaks, creating a natural feeding opportunity.

Effective Techniques for Walleye in Rivers

  1. Jigging: Jigging is a versatile technique that works well for walleye in rivers. Start by selecting a jig head that matches the current flow and depth you are fishing. Cast your jig upstream and allow it to drift naturally with the current. Use a slow, steady retrieve with occasional pauses to entice the walleye to strike.

  2. Crankbaits: Crankbaits are effective lures for covering a large area and triggering reaction strikes. Opt for shallow-diving or medium-diving crankbaits that mimic the natural forage of walleye, such as minnows or crayfish. Cast the crankbait upstream and retrieve it at a moderate pace, varying your retrieve speed to imitate wounded prey.

  3. Live Bait Rigging: Using live bait is a tried-and-true method for targeting walleye. Rig a slip sinker with a live minnow or nightcrawler and cast it upstream. Allow the bait to drift naturally with the current, keeping your line taut to detect any subtle bites. When you feel a bite, give the walleye a moment to fully swallow the bait before setting the hook.

  4. Vertical Jigging: Vertical jigging is an effective technique when targeting walleye in deeper pools or areas with strong current. Position your boat directly over the target area and drop your jig vertically to the desired depth. Use a lift and drop motion, mimicking the movement of an injured baitfish. Pay close attention to your line for any signs of a strike.

Seasonal Considerations

Lastly, it’s important to consider the seasonal patterns of walleye in rivers. During the spring, walleye migrate upstream to spawn, making it an excellent time to target them in river systems. As the water warms up in the summer, walleye tend to move to deeper pools and seek cooler temperatures. In the fall, they become more active again as they prepare for winter.

Final Thoughts

Fishing for walleye in rivers offers a unique and rewarding angling experience. By understanding their behavior, selecting the right gear, and using effective techniques, you can increase your chances of landing these prized fish. Remember to practice catch and release to ensure the sustainability of walleye populations in rivers. So grab your gear, head to your favorite river, and embark on an unforgettable walleye fishing adventure!
