How to Write Fantasy Poetry: A Guide


Fantasy poetry is a captivating genre that allows writers to explore fantastical realms, mythical creatures, and magical elements through the power of words. It combines the beauty of poetry with the imaginative possibilities of fantasy, creating a unique and enchanting reading experience. If you’ve ever been inspired by the works of J.R.R. Tolkien, Neil Gaiman, or Ursula K. Le Guin, and want to try your hand at writing fantasy poetry, this guide is for you. In this post, we’ll explore the key elements and techniques that can help you create captivating and evocative fantasy poetry.

1. Immerse Yourself in Fantasy Literature

To write compelling fantasy poetry, it’s essential to immerse yourself in the rich worlds created by other fantasy authors. Read widely within the genre, from classics to contemporary works, and take note of the unique elements that make each piece of writing special. Pay attention to the themes, settings, characters, and language choices employed by these authors. This will not only inspire you but also help you understand the nuances of the genre.

2. Develop Your Imaginative Skills

Fantasy poetry thrives on vivid and imaginative descriptions. To develop your imaginative skills, engage in activities that stimulate your creativity. Take long walks in nature, observe the world around you, and let your mind wander. Allow yourself to dream up fantastical scenarios and let your imagination run free. The more you exercise your imaginative muscles, the easier it will be to create vivid and captivating imagery in your poetry.

3. Create a Unique Fantasy World

Fantasy poetry often revolves around a unique world or universe. Take the time to build your own fantasy world, complete with its own set of rules, creatures, and landscapes. Consider the history, geography, and culture of your world, and infuse your poetry with the essence of this world. This will lend authenticity and depth to your writing, allowing readers to fully immerse themselves in your fantastical creations.

4. Play with Language and Sound

One of the joys of poetry is the ability to play with language and sound. In fantasy poetry, you can take this to a whole new level by incorporating mythical and magical elements into your word choices. Experiment with onomatopoeia, alliteration, and other literary devices to create a musical and enchanting quality in your poetry. Let your words dance on the page and transport your readers to otherworldly realms.

5. Embrace Symbolism and Allegory

Fantasy poetry often lends itself to symbolism and allegory. Use these literary techniques to convey deeper meanings and explore universal themes. You can use mythical creatures as metaphors, magical elements as symbols, and fantastical landscapes as allegories. This will add layers of depth and complexity to your poetry, resonating with readers on a profound level.

6. Capture Emotion and Sensory Details

To truly engage your readers, it’s important to capture emotions and sensory details in your poetry. Whether it’s the feeling of awe when encountering a majestic dragon or the scent of wildflowers in a hidden forest, bring these experiences to life through your words. Use descriptive language that appeals to the senses, allowing readers to feel, see, hear, smell, and taste the world you’ve created.

7. Revise, Revise, Revise

Writing poetry is a process of refinement. After you’ve written your initial draft, revise and edit your work multiple times. Pay attention to the flow and rhythm of your lines, the clarity of your imagery, and the overall impact of your words. Remove any unnecessary words or phrases, and ensure that each line serves a purpose. Poetry is a distilled form of expression, so every word counts.

8. Share Your Poetry

Once you’re satisfied with your fantasy poetry, share it with others. Seek feedback from fellow writers, join writing groups or workshops, or submit your work to literary magazines or online platforms. Sharing your poetry will not only help you improve as a writer but also allow you to connect with a community of like-minded individuals who appreciate the magic of fantasy poetry.

In conclusion, writing fantasy poetry is a journey into realms of imagination and wonder. By immersing yourself in the genre, developing your imaginative skills, and creating a unique world, you can craft captivating poems that transport readers to fantastical realms. Remember to play with language, embrace symbolism and allegory, and capture emotions and sensory details to create a truly magical reading experience. Happy writing!
