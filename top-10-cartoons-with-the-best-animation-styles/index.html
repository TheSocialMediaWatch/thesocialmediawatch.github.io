Top 10 Cartoons with the Best Animation Styles


Animation, as an art form, has evolved tremendously over the decades, blending intricate craftsmanship with boundless creativity to deliver stories that captivate audiences of all ages. Among the vast sea of cartoons produced worldwide, some stand out not just for their narratives but for their impeccable and groundbreaking animation styles. These shows push the boundaries of art and technology, offering unique visual experiences that leave lasting impressions. Here, we dive into the top 10 cartoons celebrated for their best animation styles, exploring what makes each a masterpiece in its own right.

1. Samurai Jack

Produced by Genndy Tartakovsky, Samurai Jack is an exceptional blend of minimalist design and dynamic action animation. The show’s style is heavily influenced by Japanese art and comic aesthetics, using sharp lines, stark contrasts, and a restrained color palette that enhances its storytelling.

The clear layout of backgrounds paired with fluid character movements invites viewers into a cinematic experience, almost like watching an animated comic strip come to life. Samurai Jack’s animation technique is notable for how it balances simplicity with complex camera angles and action sequences, making it a visually stunning masterpiece that redefined television animation.

2. The Legend of Korra

Building on the legacy of Avatar: The Last Airbender, The Legend of Korra features an exquisitely detailed animation style rooted in East Asian influences. This series combines traditional hand-drawn animation with digital enhancements, creating a rich and immersive world.

The animation shines in the fluidity of the elemental bending sequences—water, earth, fire, and air—brought to life with breathtaking choreography and visual effects. The character designs are sophisticated yet expressive, and the backgrounds are dense with detail, reflecting the show’s steampunk-inspired environments. The seamless integration of classic and modern animation techniques elevates The Legend of Korra as a top-tier example of artistic evolution in animated shows.

3. Over the Garden Wall

A whimsical mini-series, Over the Garden Wall captivates with its storybook aesthetic and hand-painted backgrounds inspired by early 20th-century Americana and folk art. The animation style amalgamates nostalgia with a mysterious undertone, using muted earth tones and sketch-like textures.

The creators rely heavily on classical animation methods, blending watercolor-like backgrounds with characters that have a vintage, slightly textured feel. This stylistic choice perfectly complements the show’s eerie yet charming narrative, making the animation an integral part of its storytelling appeal.

4. Rick and Morty

Although Rick and Morty embraces a fairly standard 2D animation format commonly found in adult animated series, its subtle artistic nuances deserve recognition. The show employs sharp, bold lines and a vibrant color scheme that accentuate the absurdity and dark humor inherent in its stories.

The creative designs of aliens, futuristic technology, and dimensions are a testament to the animators’ imaginative prowess. The show’s style also adapts flexibly to episodes that parody various genres, sometimes switching to entirely different animation methods, from retro pixel art to anime-inspired sequences, highlighting its versatility.

5. Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse

Though technically a feature-length film rather than a traditional series, Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse had such a seismic impact on animation that it merits inclusion. This groundbreaking production combines computer-generated imagery with hand-drawn comic book techniques, creating a 3D experience with a 2D aesthetic.

The film’s use of varied frame rates, halftone shading, vibrant colors, and onomatopoeic text overlays mimics comic book visuals while pushing digital animation boundaries. Its style is revolutionary, capturing the essence of the source material while exploring a fresh animated format that has influenced many contemporary productions.

6. Adventure Time

Adventure Time embraces a playful and surreal animation style defined by simple yet expressive character designs and vibrant colors. The series utilizes smooth, almost rubbery animation techniques that match its quirky, imaginative storyline.

The aesthetics feature bold outlines and a pastel-inspired palette that gives the Land of Ooo a dreamlike, otherworldly feel. The show’s approach to animation encourages creativity and storytelling flexibility, allowing characters and environments to morph in unexpected ways, making it a favorite for both children and adults.

7. Primal

Another masterpiece from Genndy Tartakovsky, Primal distinguishes itself with a primal, almost prehistoric animation style. This series uses detailed, rough line work and rich, earthy tones to evoke raw emotion and visceral action.

Uncommonly for modern cartoons, Primal frequently relies on wordless storytelling, letting the animation do the heavy lifting. The character movements are highly dynamic, employing powerful expressive motions amplified by detailed textures and striking visuals. Its unique style demonstrates that animation can transcend dialogue, building intense narratives through visuals alone.

8. Hilda

Hilda delights viewers with its whimsical and charming animation inspired by European folk tales and Scandinavian landscapes. The design is characterized by clean lines, soft colors, and smooth, almost storybook-like flow.

The animation integrates fantastical creatures and dreamy environments with a gentle, almost watercolor effect that lends warmth and wonder to the show. The style strikes a perfect balance between simplicity and detail, making the imaginative world both accessible and richly textured.

9. Gravity Falls

Gravity Falls combines traditional 2D animation with a slightly modernized edge, bringing together a mysterious and adventurous aesthetic. Its character designs lean towards caricature with exaggerated facial expressions, yet the backgrounds are loaded with intricate details, Easter eggs, and symbolic elements.

This contrast between character simplicity and environmental complexity strengthens the storytelling, immersing viewers in the show’s quirky supernatural mysteries. The animation uses sharp color contrasts and dynamic lighting effects, creating an atmosphere that is both fun and eerie.

10. The Midnight Gospel

Clint Eastwood’s brainchild mixes psychedelic visuals with existential storytelling. The animation style is eclectic, embracing a mix of surreal, kaleidoscopic imagery with fluid, experimental motion.

The show’s visuals are a riot of colors, shifting between abstract shapes, cosmic patterns, and bizarre character designs. It breaks conventional animation rules, creating a hypnotic and immersive experience tailored for viewers seeking something outside the mainstream. This distinctive approach makes The Midnight Gospel not only visually fascinating but philosophically profound.

Conclusion

Animation styles in cartoons span a diverse spectrum, reflecting varying artistic traditions, technological advancements, and storytelling ambitions. From minimalist design to intricate hand-painted scenes, each style enhances the narrative in unique ways. The cartoons listed above exemplify how animation can be more than just a medium for entertainment—it becomes a form of artistic expression that shapes how stories are experienced.

Whether it is the cinematic flair of Samurai Jack, the innovative digital-comic fusion in Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse, or the vintage warmth of Over the Garden Wall, these series illustrate the evolving canvas of animated artistry. For fans and creators alike, immersing in these best animation styles offers inspiration and appreciation for the endless possibilities animation holds.

Delve into these shows, observe their distinct animation qualities, and witness how visual storytelling continues to captivate hearts and minds around the world.
