How to Find Scholarships for Online Degree Programs


Picture this: you’re eager to advance your career, expand your skills, or perhaps make a major life pivot. The flexibility of online degree programs—whether undergraduate, graduate, or professional—is calling your name, but tuition bills are looming. Fortunately, scholarships designed for online learners can unlock the doors to higher education without tanking your finances.

But how do you actually find those scholarships? What sets online program funding apart from traditional campus-based awards? Here’s a comprehensive roadmap packed with tools, insights, and actionable steps, tailored for future students who want to make their online education more affordable.

Understanding the Scholarship Landscape for Online Learning

In the past, scholarships often focused solely on campus-based programs. With the roaring surge in accredited online colleges and universities, the scholarship scene is shifting. More organizations now recognize that virtual students are just as legitimate—and just as in need of financial support—as their on-campus peers.

Scholarships for online degree programs come in several flavors:

  • General Higher Education Scholarships: Open to all students, whether attending in-person or online.
  • Dedicated Online Degree Scholarships: Specifically for students enrolled in accredited online programs.
  • Program-Specific Scholarships: Target students in particular areas of study, like nursing, business, education, or STEM, and are open to online enrollees.
  • Nontraditional Student Awards: Designed for adult learners, career-changers, working professionals, or students with unique backgrounds.
  • Need-based and Merit-based Aid: Many scholarships prioritize financial need or academic/leadership excellence, regardless of how or where you study.

Armed with an understanding of what’s out there, let’s get practical.

Step 1: Compile a List of Accredited Online Programs

Start by confirming your target school and program are accredited. Accreditation assures eligibility for most scholarships and financial aid.

  • Visit the school’s website and look for details about online program accreditation.
  • Cross-check the school on the U.S. Department of Education’s Database of Accredited Postsecondary Institutions and Programs.
  • Ensure your chosen field is fully available online (bachelor’s, master’s, MBA, certificate, etc.).

Why start here? Many scholarships, including school- or donor-funded awards, require enrollment in a specific accredited institution or program. Having this list will help you filter relevant opportunities later.

Step 2: Locate Scholarships Offered by Your College or University

Many schools offer exclusive scholarships to their own online program students. Sometimes, these awards are a hidden gem on the school’s website—tucked away in the financial aid or admissions section.

How to Dig Them Up:

  • Visit the financial aid page for current or prospective online students.
  • Search for phrases like “Online student scholarships,” “Distance learning awards,” or “Nontraditional student scholarships.”
  • Contact the online program’s admissions or financial aid office and ask if there are special scholarships or discounts for online learners.
  • Note deadlines and requirements—some may ask for essays, past academic records, work history, or statement of purpose specifically tailored to online education.

School-based scholarships may include:

  • Online program launch awards.
  • Continuing education grants for adults returning to school.
  • Major-specific scholarships accessible to online students.

Step 3: Tap into External Scholarship Databases

There’s a dizzying number of broad-based scholarships targeting all sorts of students. The key is knowing how to filter or search for ones open to online learners.

Reliable Scholarship Search Engines:

  • Fastweb.com
  • Scholarships.com
  • College Board’s BigFuture Scholarship Search
  • Cappex
  • Scholarship Owl

Pro Tips:

  • Use search filters like “online,” “distance learning,” “adult learner,” “nontraditional student,” or your specific area of study.
  • Browse awards for your demographic group (e.g., working adults, single parents, military/veterans, minorities, first-generation students).
  • Research application requirements—some scholarships have strict in-person residency requirements, so focus on ones explicitly open to remote students.

Don’t overlook awards labeled “part-time” or “full-time.” Many online programs allow flexible pacing, and some scholarships are only available to full-time enrollees—even if you study online.

Step 4: Explore Scholarships by Organizations and Associations

Professional associations, nonprofits, and corporations often fund scholarships for people advancing their skills in particular industries—whether online or on campus.

Examples:

  • Technology: Google, Microsoft, and the Society of Women Engineers.
  • Business: National Black MBA Association, local chambers of commerce.
  • Education: National Education Association, Teach.org.
  • Healthcare: American Association of Colleges of Nursing, Future Nurses of America.

Many of these organizations welcome applications from online learners, especially those juggling work, family, and school. Most science, tech, and business associations have a scholarships page—always check the eligibility criteria.

Step 5: Consider State and Federal Grant Programs

If you’re pursuing your first undergraduate or graduate degree online, don’t ignore state or federal grant options:

  • Federal Pell Grants: Available to students in accredited online bachelor’s programs who demonstrate financial need.
  • State Grants: Many states permit their residents to use state-level education grants for online or hybrid programs within state-approved institutions. Visit your state’s higher education website for program details.

And get your FAFSA (Free Application for Federal Student Aid) done early—even if only for scholarship eligibility.

Step 6: Unearth Employer Tuition Assistance and Reimbursement

More employers want upskilled, college-educated staff—whether you’re on campus or in virtual classrooms. Many companies now cover tuition for accredited online degree programs.

  • Check Your Company’s HR Policies: Look for tuition assistance, scholarships, or “education benefit” programs.
  • Ask HR About Restrictions: Some require you to study in a specific field, work with a partner university, or maintain a minimum grade.
  • Use These Scholarships Strategically: Leverage employer funds alongside external scholarships—often, you can combine the two for greater savings.

Step 7: Apply for Scholarships Tailored for Online and Nontraditional Students

Dozens of national awards are designed purely for online students or nontraditional learners—those balancing classes with jobs, families, or other responsibilities.

Notable Examples:

  • Get Educated Online College Scholarship: $1,000 per award for students enrolled in accredited online programs.
  • Patsy Takemoto Mink Education Foundation: Offers awards for low-income women and mothers, including online students.
  • The College JumpStart Scholarship: Runs multiple application cycles annually, open to all students pursuing further education—including distance learning.
  • Imagine America Scholarship: Targets adult learners returning to school, including online programs.

Explore additional niche scholarships based on your age, parental status, career background, ethnicity, or military service.

Step 8: Personalize Applications and Plan Ahead

Scholarship competitions are intensely crowded; standing out matters. Customize every application—share why online education fits your goals, how you’ll balance remote study with other responsibilities, and your vision for post-graduation.

Common Materials You’ll Need:

  • Updated résumé highlighting relevant experience, leadership, or service.
  • Short personal essays or statements tailoring your story to the scholarship’s mission.
  • Proof of enrollment or acceptance to an accredited online program.
  • Letters of recommendation from employers, community leaders, or former professors.

Start applications months in advance of deadlines. Use a spreadsheet to track progress, due dates, and materials needed. Each hour invested upfront can result in thousands of dollars saved.

Step 9: Beware of Scholarship Scams

With remote study surging, scammers prey on hopeful applicants. Watch out for:

  • Fees to “apply” or “search” for scholarships. Legitimate scholarships are always free to enter.
  • Too-good-to-be-true offers or guarantees.
  • Requests for sensitive data, like bank details or Social Security numbers (except for reputable federal aid sites).

Stick to trusted databases and verify every opportunity with your school’s financial aid office if you’re unsure.

Step 10: Don’t Stop After Enrollment

Even once you’ve started your online program, keep applying. Lots of scholarships target advanced students or continuing online learners—often based on academic success, industry involvement, or leadership.

Each semester, check for:

  • Departmental awards tied to your major.
  • Scholarships for honors or community service.
  • Professional association grants for students with high GPAs or project-based achievements.

Persistence pays off over the full length of your online degree.

Maximizing Your Scholarship Hunt: Real-World Tips

  • Network with Other Online Students: Join social media groups or discussion forums for your school/program to share insider tips on funding.
  • Ask Program Advisors about Hidden Funds: Schools sometimes advertise new scholarships via email or in online student portals.
  • Apply Broadly: Even small awards add up. Some can be “stacked” or combined, slashing tuition dramatically.
  • Tailor Each Application: A recycled essay is easy to spot; connect your specific goals to what the committee wants to fund.
  • Stay Organized: Tools like Google Sheets, Evernote, or scholarship-specific apps can help you juggle deadlines and progress.

Final Thoughts: Online Degrees Are More Affordable Than Ever

Scholarships and grants for online degree programs are more numerous, streamlined, and accessible than ever. From national scholarships and workplace benefits to local and niche awards, committed research and persistent application efforts can yield impressive savings.

Approach the process like a job search—persistent, methodical, and strategic. The more thorough you are in seeking out funding options, the more pieces of your tuition puzzle you’ll cover—meaning you can dive into your online education with less stress and more excitement about your future.

By embracing these steps and leveraging modern scholarship resources, anyone can bridge the financial gap to a new credential, career, or lifelong dream—all from the comfort of home.
