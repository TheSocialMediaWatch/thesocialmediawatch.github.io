Astrology and Climate Change: Planetary Perspectives


Astrology is a practice that has existed for thousands of years, helping humans make sense of the world and their place in it. But what if we looked at astrology not just as a system of personal interpretation but as a broader planetary perspective? How might our understanding of climate change shift if we incorporate astrology into the conversation?

At its core, astrology examines the relationships between celestial bodies and the effects they have on human affairs. But perhaps we are overlooking a more literal interpretation by not considering the actual interactions between the planets themselves and how those interactions might impact Earth.

We know that the planets in our solar system are constantly orbiting, interacting with one another, and generating forces that influence our lives. But are these forces capable of causing climate shifts? Could planetary alignments and perturbations be contributing to the changing climate Earth is experiencing?

Before we explore this idea further, let’s quell any fears - we are not suggesting that astrology is a replacement for scientific understanding of climate change. Rather, we want to encourage a broader exploration of the relationships between the planetary phenomena we study both as astrologers and climate scientists.

Astrologers often tout the influence of Venus, Goddess of Love, and the harbinger of Earth’s weather changes. Venus’ close proximity to our planet is well-known, and some even believe this facilitates stronger vibrations and energy flows affecting Earth. Climate scientists likewise examine Venus as a point of comparison, given her extreme heat and conditions.

Jupiter, the largest planet in our solar system, isn’t just the planet of luck or fortune; it may also serve as an amplifier for climate trends. It is crucial we continue studying Jupiter’s movement and effect on Earth, both in astrological and scientific contexts.

Mars, the planet of war and conflict, may seem like an unlikely player in the climate change discussion. Yet, the conflict we are witnessing in our environment is undeniable. The Earth is literally fighting against the forces behind climate change. Mars, therefore, has significant relevancy to the climate discourse.

Let us not forget Uranus, often depicted with an umbrella, and Saturn, representing boundaries and cycles. Their roles in our current climate are both subjective interpretations and scientifically verifiable phenomena. Look no further than the eye-catching, once-in-a-lifetime conjunction of Saturn and Jupiter in 2020, which seemed to epitomize a new epoch of greater awareness of our planet’s predicament.

Ultimately, the astrological and climate change discourse need not be polar opposites. We could explore how astrologers and climate scientists can collaborate and learn from one another. By incorporating planetary perspectives into our understanding of the climate crisis, we may find new ways of creatively addressing and fighting these forces for a more sustainable future. Will you join this interdisciplinary effort in exploring the celestial bodies and the potentially powerful influence on our changing climate?
