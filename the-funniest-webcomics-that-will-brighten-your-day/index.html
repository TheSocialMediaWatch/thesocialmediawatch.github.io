The Funniest Webcomics That Will Brighten Your Day


Are you in need of a good laugh? Look no further! In this blog post, we have compiled a list of the funniest webcomics that are guaranteed to brighten your day. From witty one-liners to relatable slice-of-life humor, these webcomics cover a wide range of topics and are sure to leave you in stitches. So, sit back, relax, and get ready to experience the joy of laughter!

1. “The Awkward Yeti”

“The Awkward Yeti” is a webcomic created by Nick Seluk that hilariously personifies various organs and body parts. From the adorable and anxious Heart to the mischievous Brain, this comic explores the humorous interactions between these anthropomorphized characters. With clever dialogue and relatable situations, “The Awkward Yeti” is a must-read for anyone who enjoys light-hearted humor.

2. “Sarah’s Scribbles”

If you’re a young adult navigating the ups and downs of life, “Sarah’s Scribbles” by Sarah Andersen is the perfect webcomic for you. Through her simple yet charming illustrations, Sarah captures the everyday struggles and awkward moments that we can all relate to. From the woes of adulting to the joys of introversion, “Sarah’s Scribbles” is a humorous and refreshing take on the millennial experience.

3. “XKCD”

For those who enjoy a blend of humor and intellect, “XKCD” by Randall Munroe is a webcomic that will tickle your funny bone while also making you think. With its witty commentary on science, technology, and geek culture, “XKCD” has gained a dedicated following. Whether you’re a computer scientist or simply appreciate clever jokes, this webcomic is a treasure trove of humor and intellect.

4. “Cyanide & Happiness”

If dark humor is your cup of tea, then “Cyanide & Happiness” is the webcomic for you. Created by Kris Wilson, Rob DenBleyker, Dave McElfatrick, and Matt Melvin, this irreverent comic explores the absurd and often controversial aspects of life. With its signature stick-figure style and punchy punchlines, “Cyanide & Happiness” pushes the boundaries of comedy and is not for the faint of heart.

5. “The Oatmeal”

Matthew Inman’s “The Oatmeal” is a webcomic that combines humor with educational content. With topics ranging from grammar and spelling to the quirks of the English language, “The Oatmeal” delivers laughs while also imparting knowledge. With its unique blend of wit and information, this webcomic is a delightful and educational read.

6. “Safely Endangered”

“Safely Endangered” by Chris McCoy is a webcomic that thrives on its absurd and unexpected humor. With its simple yet expressive illustrations, this comic takes everyday situations and turns them into hilarious and often surreal scenarios. If you’re looking for a comic that will surprise you with its comedic twists, “Safely Endangered” is a must-read.

7. “Poorly Drawn Lines”

As the name suggests, “Poorly Drawn Lines” by Reza Farazmand embraces its simplistic art style to deliver humor that is both witty and offbeat. Through its quirky characters and unconventional scenarios, this webcomic explores the absurdity and randomness of life. If you appreciate humor that is delightfully strange, “Poorly Drawn Lines” will surely leave you chuckling.

And there you have it – a compilation of some of the funniest webcomics that are guaranteed to brighten your day. Whether you’re in need of a quick laugh or a dose of clever humor, these webcomics have got you covered. So, bookmark this page, grab a cup of coffee, and dive into the world of laughter that these webcomics offer. Happy reading and may your days be filled with laughter!
