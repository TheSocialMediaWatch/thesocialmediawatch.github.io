The Best Campfire Recipes for Dinner and Dessert


Gathering around a campfire is one of the most cherished outdoor experiences. It’s a time to unwind, share stories, and enjoy delicious meals cooked over an open flame. The magic of campfire cooking lies in its simplicity and the unique flavors imparted by smoky heat. Whether you’re an experienced camper or a weekend warrior, having a lineup of reliable, tasty recipes can elevate your outdoor adventure. Today, we dive into some of the best campfire recipes for dinner and dessert that will satisfy your hunger and sweet tooth alike.

Campfire Dinner Recipes: Savory, Simple, and Satisfying

1. Classic Campfire Foil Packet Meals

Foil packet meals are the quintessential campfire dinner—easy to prepare, easy to clean, and incredibly versatile. The magic of this method is sealing all your ingredients inside foil and letting them steam and roast over the fire.

Ingredients

  • Protein options: chicken breasts, ground beef, sausages, or fish fillets
  • Vegetables: diced potatoes, bell peppers, onions, corn, carrots, zucchini
  • Seasonings: salt, pepper, garlic powder, paprika, Italian herbs, or cajun seasoning
  • Olive oil or butter

Cooking Method

  1. Prepare your protein and cut vegetables into similar-sized pieces.
  2. Lay down a large sheet of heavy-duty aluminum foil.
  3. Drizzle olive oil or add a small pat of butter on the foil.
  4. Add your protein and vegetables.
  5. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, and chosen seasonings.
  6. Fold the foil tightly around the ingredients to create a sealed packet.
  7. Place the packet on hot coals or over the flames for about 20-30 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  8. Carefully open the packet—watch out for steam—and enjoy.

This method allows you to tailor the meal to your tastes and dietary needs, plus clean-up is a breeze.

2. Campfire Chili Stew

Chili is a hearty, warming option perfect for cool evenings. Made with ground beef or turkey, beans, tomatoes, and spices, chili simmers to perfection over the campfire.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef or turkey
  • 1 can kidney beans (rinsed and drained)
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup beef broth or water
  • 1 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil

Cooking Method

  1. In a cast-iron Dutch oven or heavy pot, heat olive oil over the campfire flame.
  2. Sauté onions and garlic until fragrant and translucent.
  3. Add ground meat to the pot, breaking it apart and cooking until browned.
  4. Stir in chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper to coat the meat evenly.
  5. Add kidney beans, diced tomatoes with juices, and broth.
  6. Bring to a simmer, reduce heat by moving the pot to a lower-intensity part of the fire or coals.
  7. Let it cook for at least 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. Once thickened and flavors melded, serve with rustic bread or tortilla chips.

The combination of smoky fire flavor and spices makes this chili a campfire classic.

3. Grilled Campfire Kabobs

Kabobs are a fun, interactive dinner and easy to customize for any palate. Thread your choice of marinated meats and veggies onto skewers and grill until juicy and caramelized.

Ingredients

  • Cubed chicken, beef, or shrimp
  • Bell peppers, onions, cherry tomatoes, zucchini slices, mushrooms
  • Marinade: olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, herbs, salt, and pepper

Cooking Method

  1. Marinate protein and vegetables separately for at least 30 minutes in a cooler or covered container.
  2. Thread pieces alternately onto skewers.
  3. Hold over campfire grill or place skewers above coals.
  4. Rotate every few minutes to ensure even cooking.
  5. Cook until meat is thoroughly done, and vegetables are tender and lightly charred.
  6. Serve with flatbread or rice.

Kabobs are an excellent way to enjoy a colorful, flavorful meal packed with nutrients.

4. Campfire Grilled Cheese with a Twist

Who says grilled cheese has to be boring? Upgrade this comfort food with campfire-friendly ingredients that ooze flavor and texture.

Ingredients

  • Sliced sturdy bread (sourdough or rye work well)
  • Cheese: cheddar, mozzarella, or pepper jack
  • Additions: tomato slices, cooked bacon, sautéed mushrooms, or caramelized onions
  • Butter or cooking spray

Cooking Method

  1. Butter one side of each bread slice.
  2. Place cheese and optional fillings between two unbuttered bread sides, forming a sandwich.
  3. Wrap sandwich in foil or cook directly on a campfire grill.
  4. Cook for about 5 minutes per side or until cheese is melted and bread is crispy.
  5. Carefully unwrap and enjoy the gooey goodness.

Grilled cheese is a quick, satisfying meal perfect for unexpected hunger pangs after a day of adventuring.

Campfire Desserts: Sweet Treats to End the Day

1. Classic S’mores with a Campfire Twist

No campfire experience is complete without s’mores. Elevate this simple treat by experimenting with different chocolate and marshmallow varieties.

  • Swap milk chocolate for dark chocolate, peanut butter cups, or caramel-filled chocolates.
  • Use flavored marshmallows or even fruit-filled marshmallows.
  • Toast the marshmallow slowly over the flame until golden brown and melty.
  • Sandwich with graham crackers and your choice of chocolate for an indulgent finale.

2. Campfire Banana Boats

These delightful desserts pack natural sweetness and a gooey chocolate-marshmallow center.

Ingredients

  • Bananas (one per person)
  • Mini marshmallows
  • Chocolate chips or chunks
  • Optional: chopped nuts, shredded coconut, peanut butter

Cooking Method

  1. Slice bananas lengthwise, careful not to cut all the way through.
  2. Gently pry open the split and stuff with marshmallows, chocolate, and any extras.
  3. Wrap the stuffed banana tightly in foil.
  4. Place on hot coals for 5-10 minutes, until melting ingredients bubble.
  5. Carefully unwrap and eat with a spoon for a gooey, warm dessert.

Banana boats are portable, crowd-pleasing, and incredibly easy to assemble.

3. Campfire Baked Apples

This warm, fragrant dessert combines fruit with a cinnamon-spiced filling, perfect for autumn or whenever you want a comforting treat.

Ingredients

  • Apples (choose firm varieties like Granny Smith or Honeycrisp)
  • Brown sugar
  • Cinnamon
  • Chopped nuts or raisins
  • Butter

Cooking Method

  1. Core apples lightly, creating a cavity without cutting through.
  2. Mix brown sugar, cinnamon, and chopped nuts or raisins.
  3. Stuff the mixture into the apple cavities.
  4. Place a small pat of butter on top of each apple.
  5. Wrap apples individually in foil.
  6. Place on hot coals for 20-30 minutes until tender.
  7. Carefully unwrap and serve warm.

This recipe brings a touch of home-cooked sweetness to the campfire.

4. Campfire Cinnamon Sugar Doughnuts

For a fun, fried dessert, try this easy doughnut recipe that cooks in a skillet or cast-iron pan over your campfire.

Ingredients

  • Pre-made biscuit dough (canned)
  • Oil for frying
  • Cinnamon sugar: mix 1/2 cup sugar with 1 tablespoon cinnamon

Cooking Method

  1. Heat oil in a cast-iron skillet over the fire.
  2. Cut biscuit dough into small, doughnut-sized pieces.
  3. Fry dough pieces until golden brown on all sides, about 2-3 minutes per batch.
  4. Remove with tongs and roll in cinnamon sugar while warm.
  5. Serve immediately for best taste.

These sweet, crispy treats are perfect for a crowd and easy to prepare with store-bought dough.

Tips for Successful Campfire Cooking

  • Prepare Ahead: Pre-chop vegetables, marinate meats, and pack all ingredients in labeled containers for an efficient cooking experience at the campsite.
  • Cast Iron is Your Friend: Cast iron pots and skillets distribute heat evenly and can withstand direct flame and coals.
  • Control the Fire: Spread the hot coals to create zones of heat intensity, allowing you to simmer, sear, or slow-cook as needed.
  • Safety First: Use heat-resistant gloves, long utensils, and keep a bucket of water or sand nearby for emergencies.
  • Clean Up: Bring biodegradable soap, scrubbers, and trash bags to leave the campsite pristine.

Final Thoughts: Enjoying the Outdoors One Bite at a Time

Campfire meals aren’t just about feeding your body—they’re about nourishing your spirit. They bring people together, spark conversations, and create memorable moments against the backdrop of nature’s amphitheater. With the recipes shared here, you can build a savory and sweet meal lineup that’s delicious, satisfying, and simple to prepare under the stars. So next time you head out for an outdoor adventure, bring these recipes along and savor the timeless flavors of a campfire feast.

Happy camping and happy cooking!
