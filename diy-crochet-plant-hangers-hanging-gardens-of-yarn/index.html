DIY Crochet Plant Hangers: Hanging Gardens of Yarn


If you’re a lover of plants and have a knack for crafts, then DIY crochet plant hangers are the perfect project for you! Not only will these plant hangers add a touch of charm to your home, but they also provide a creative way to display your favorite green companions. In this blog post, we’ll explore the art of creating crochet plant hangers, step by step. So grab your crochet hooks and let’s get started!

Materials You’ll Need

Before diving into the crochet process, make sure you have the following materials on hand:

  • Yarn of your choice
  • Crochet hooks (appropriate size for your yarn)
  • Scissors
  • Measuring tape
  • Wooden or metal rings (optional)

Choosing the Right Yarn

When it comes to crochet plant hangers, the type of yarn you choose plays a crucial role in the final outcome. Opt for a sturdy and durable yarn that can support the weight of your plants. Cotton yarn is an excellent choice as it’s strong, breathable, and easy to work with. Additionally, consider the color of the yarn and how it will complement your plants and home decor.

Getting Started

  1. Measure and Cut: Start by measuring the desired length of your plant hanger. Keep in mind the height at which you want your plant to hang. Once you have the measurements, cut a length of yarn three times longer than your desired length. This extra length will account for the knots and the hanging loop.

  2. Create the Loop: Fold the yarn in half and create a loop at the folded end. This loop will serve as the hanging loop for your plant hanger. Slide the loop through the wooden or metal ring if you choose to use one.

  3. Knotting: Take the loose ends of the yarn and pass them through the loop, pulling them tight to create a knot. This knot will secure the hanging loop in place.

  4. Crocheting the Hanger: Now it’s time to start crocheting! Choose a simple stitch pattern like single crochet or half double crochet. Begin by making a slip knot and placing it on your crochet hook. Insert the hook through the hanging loop and pull the yarn through, creating a chain stitch.

  5. Continue Stitching: Keep stitching using your chosen stitch pattern until you reach the desired length for your plant hanger. Remember to leave enough space at the end to accommodate the plant pot.

  6. Finishing Touches: Once you’ve reached the desired length, fasten off the yarn by cutting it, leaving a tail. Weave in any loose ends using a tapestry needle to ensure a clean finish.

Tips for a Successful Project

  • Experiment with different stitch patterns and yarn colors to create unique and personalized plant hangers.
  • Consider using variegated yarn to add visual interest to your hangers.
  • Add beads or tassels to the ends of your plant hangers for an extra touch of style.

Final Thoughts

DIY crochet plant hangers not only provide a functional way to display your plants but also allow you to unleash your creativity. With just a few simple materials and basic crochet skills, you can transform your living space into a hanging garden of yarn. So gather your supplies, get creative, and embark on a crochet adventure that will bring life and beauty to your home!

Remember, the possibilities are endless when it comes to crochet plant hangers. Experiment with different yarns, stitches, and plant arrangements to create a unique and personalized display. Happy crocheting!
