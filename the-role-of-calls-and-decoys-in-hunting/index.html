The Role of Calls and Decoys in Hunting


Hunting is an ancient practice that has transcended time and cultures. It has evolved from basic survival techniques to a competitive sport and recreational pastime. A significant aspect of successful hunting lies in imitating the sounds and movements of prey animals. This involves the use of calls and decoys to deceive and attract game.

Calls, an EssentialTool for Hunters

A call is a device that simulates the sounds of prey animals, used to lure them closer. Different species of animals vocalize differently, and hunters use species-specific calls to mimic these sounds. Using a call can work wonders in attracting animals to investigate, but using them effectively requires understanding their behavior and vocalizations.

The hound call, for example, replicates the sound of a wounded rabbit. Dogs tracker rabbits making a distressed noise, hence the use of the hound call can make them think they have sensed injured prey nearby. Similarly, a duck call can mimic the sound of a mating pair of mallards. This is especially effective during the hunting season when male mallards are eager to breed.

Calls work best when hunters remain concealed, allowing the prey to approach without suspicion. As these creatures grow closer, within effective shooting range, the hunter can take the shot. Proper knowledge of animal sounds and the right call type can significantly increase success rates.

Decoys: A Hunt’s Silent Ally

Decoys, on the other hand, serve as life-size replicas of animals, designed to fool prey into believing that one of their own is in the area. Hunters can use them alone or in conjunction with calls to represent an injured or estrus female.

Birds such as geese or ducks recognize patterns and formations while flying; hence decoys help hunters create an artificial flock for the real migrants to follow. Carefully chosen decoy placements and the right number can result in a high-inquiry zone, a space that hunters term as the flight path of the birds. Turkey decoys can also help mask the hunter’s approach while trying to lure them closer.

Another clever use of decoys is in “buck bash,” materials snagged from hunted deer’s head and mounted on a block to mimic the effect of two rival stags sparring. The motion and aura of the decoy can trigger dominant males to come investigate or challenge, thereby providing a hunting opportunity.

Considerations Beyond Equipment

The art of using calls and decoys is broader than just buying and deploying them. Understanding the specific behavior and repertoire of each game species greatly enhances their use. Experienced hunters recognize that timing is significant – whether to engage a call or decoy for grouse, woodcock, or any other prey.

In closing, calls and decoys, when properly used, can significantly improve a hunter’s success rate. Understanding the prey’s behavior and vocalizations is as essential as choosing the right call or decoy. Mastering these strategies will facilitate an immersive and successful hunting experience. Happy hunting!
