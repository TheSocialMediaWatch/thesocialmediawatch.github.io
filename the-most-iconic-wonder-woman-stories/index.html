The Most Iconic Wonder Woman Stories


Wonder Woman is one of the most iconic superheroines in the world of comic books. Since her creation in 1941 by psychologist and writer William Moulton Marston, Wonder Woman has captured the hearts of readers with her strength, compassion, and unwavering determination. Over the years, she has been the protagonist of numerous incredible stories that have become classics in the comic book industry. In this blog post, we will explore some of the most iconic Wonder Woman stories that every fan should read.

1. “The Circle” (2006)

Written by Gail Simone and illustrated by Terry Dodson, “The Circle” is a compelling story that delves into Wonder Woman’s origins and her connection to the Amazons. In this storyline, we witness Diana’s journey as she uncovers secrets about her past and the truth behind her birth. With stunning artwork and a gripping narrative, “The Circle” showcases Wonder Woman’s resilience and her desire to protect those she loves.

2. “The Hiketeia” (2002)

Written by Greg Rucka and illustrated by J.G. Jones, “The Hiketeia” is a powerful tale that explores the conflict between duty and morality. In this story, Wonder Woman finds herself bound by the ancient ritual of hiketeia, where she must protect a young woman named Danielle Wellys. However, Danielle is being pursued by Batman, who believes her to be a murderer. This story raises thought-provoking questions about justice, loyalty, and the complexities of being a superhero.

3. “Gods and Mortals” (1987)

“Gods and Mortals” is the story that relaunched Wonder Woman’s comic series in 1987, with writer George Perez and artist Greg Potter at the helm. This storyline retells Wonder Woman’s origin story, taking readers on a journey through Greek mythology as Diana embraces her role as an Amazonian princess and a champion of peace. With intricate artwork and a captivating narrative, “Gods and Mortals” reintroduced Wonder Woman to a new generation of readers and solidified her status as an iconic superhero.

4. “The Contest” (1994)

In “The Contest,” writer William Messner-Loebs and artist Mike Deodato Jr. present a gripping tale that explores Wonder Woman’s struggle to maintain her identity and prove her worth. In this storyline, a new contest is held on Themyscira to determine who will become the new Wonder Woman. Diana finds herself facing fierce competition from Artemis, a fellow Amazonian. “The Contest” showcases Wonder Woman’s determination and her unwavering commitment to justice, even in the face of adversity.

5. “The True Amazon” (2016)

“The True Amazon” is a unique and thought-provoking graphic novel written and illustrated by Jill Thompson. This standalone story offers a fresh perspective on Wonder Woman’s early years and her journey to becoming a superhero. Thompson’s stunning watercolor artwork and introspective narrative provide readers with a deeper understanding of Diana’s character, her struggles, and her growth as a hero.

These are just a few of the many iconic Wonder Woman stories that have captivated readers over the years. Each of these stories showcases the strength, compassion, and resilience that define Wonder Woman as a character. Whether you’re a longtime fan or new to the world of Wonder Woman, these stories are a must-read for anyone who wants to delve into the rich mythology and inspiring adventures of this beloved superheroine.

So, grab a copy of these iconic Wonder Woman stories and immerse yourself in the world of Diana Prince. Prepare to be inspired, entertained, and moved by the tales of this extraordinary superheroine who continues to inspire readers of all ages. Wonder Woman’s legacy is a testament to the power of storytelling and the enduring impact of characters who embody hope, courage, and justice.
