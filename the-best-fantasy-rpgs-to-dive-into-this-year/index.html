The Best Fantasy RPGs to Dive Into This Year


Stepping into a new fantasy world is one of the great joys of gaming. Whether you’re hungry for intricate lore, seeking nerve-wracking dungeons, or just want to lose yourself in magical lands for dozens—or hundreds—of hours, this year’s lineup of fantasy RPGs offers something to satisfy every taste. The genre continues to evolve, offering polished combat systems, heart-wrenching storytelling, and intricately crafted universes filled with secrets to unravel. Let’s take a deep plunge into the standout fantasy RPGs capturing imaginations this year, from grand epics to indie gems worth every minute of your time.

Baldur’s Gate 3: The Definitive D&D Adventure Expands

No list of must-play fantasy RPGs would be complete without Baldur’s Gate 3. Building on the legacy of deep Dungeons & Dragons-inspired storytelling, this game has set a new standard for branching narratives and player agency. Every decision you make—right down to the most fleeting conversation—can have far-reaching consequences, impacting alliances, endings, and even the fate of the Forgotten Realms itself.

The engine powering Baldur’s Gate 3 makes every playthrough feel unique, with dice-driven combat that brings tabletop energy to life on the screen. Customizing your party composition, experimenting with multi-class builds, and wrangling with mischief-prone companions makes every encounter feel vibrant and alive. The writing shines: Larian Studios has crafted a script dense with dramatic stakes, laugh-out-loud humor, and gut-punching tragedy.

If you’re aching for a high-fantasy adventure where each choice is meaningful and the world feels like it breathes around you, this is the adventure to undertake. Mod support and frequent updates keep the Forgotten Realms feeling fresh, so whether you’re in it for love, loot, or a labyrinthine labyrinth of secrets, there’s never been a better time to roll initiative.

Final Fantasy XVI: A Bold New Era for the Icon

The Final Fantasy franchise remains a giant in the RPG world, constantly reinventing itself with each release. Final Fantasy XVI continues that tradition, offering a darker, more mature narrative brimming with political intrigue, betrayal, and a rivalry between titanic Eikons threatening the world’s balance. This installment ditches turn-based combat in favor of a slick, action-heavy system that blends spectacle with meaningful decision-making.

What sets Final Fantasy XVI apart is its embrace of modern storytelling—echoes of epic fantasies like Game of Thrones and The Witcher are woven through the narrative tapestry, but with all the singular, heartfelt weirdness that fans expect. Sumptuous visuals, soaring music, and a world teetering between hope and despair keep you locked in, with memorable characters anchoring the drama every step of the way.

Embark on this journey not just for the thrill of combat or legendary boss battles, but for the raw, emotional stakes that underpin every quest. Whether you’re strategizing over Eikon abilities or piecing together fractured lore, this is a game where every moment has weight.

Dragon’s Dogma 2: Open World Magic and Monster-Hunting

For those who long for exploration, Dragon’s Dogma 2 offers a colossal, dynamic world. The sequel to the cult classic blends high-fantasy storytelling with heart-pounding action and emergent systems that keep every play session surprising. The unique Pawn system allows for customized AI companions, each with their own learning curve and tactical behaviors—meaning no adventure ever plays out exactly the same way twice.

Scaling enormous beasts, improvising mid-battle, and unlocking new classes—known as Vocations—ensures progression always feels fresh. The game’s day/night cycle brings real danger after dark, with foes emerging beneath the moonlight that demand careful preparation and resource management.

This is a world where curiosity is rewarded, and veering off the beaten path yields discoveries from cursed relics to hidden bosses. If you crave a sense of freedom alongside weighty combat and subtle storytelling, Dragon’s Dogma 2 should be at the top of your fantasy RPG wishlist.

Elden Ring: Shadow of the Erdtree—Unfolding New Myths

Elden Ring exploded onto the scene, transforming the “soulslike” formula into something open, mythic, and utterly mesmerizing. This year, the much anticipated Shadow of the Erdtree expansion opens new lands, advances cryptic storylines, and adds mythic horrors to overcome.

What makes Elden Ring continually captivating is its confidence in the player’s intelligence—the world rarely holds your hand, but instead invites fearless exploration. Each ruined fortress, sunken crypt, or windswept plain is a puzzle of its own, hiding lore, gear, and oddball NPCs whose motives are often as mysterious as the shattered world itself.

The expansion revitalizes the core game, bringing in new bosses, weapons, magic types, and challenging dungeons. For those aching for an RPG where discovery is constant and the line between epic and eerie is blurred, Shadow of the Erdtree ensures Elden Ring remains a gold standard in fantasy roleplaying.

Octopath Traveler II: Pixel Art and Poignant Tales

Sometimes, fantasy calls for a more nostalgic texture. Octopath Traveler II revels in its storybook visuals, blending lush pixel art with a cutting-edge “HD-2D” style that brings every town and dungeon to life. The game follows eight unique protagonists, each with their own intertwining destinies and distinct abilities.

Tactical, turn-based combat remains deep and rewarding, thanks to the successful “Break” system and creative job combinations. But where Octopath Traveler II truly shines is its storytelling: each narrative arc dabbles in mature, heart-wrenching, and sometimes surprisingly dark themes, giving real depth to its idyllic world.

Perfect for gamers who want to savor meaningful side quests, explore beautiful landscapes, and immerse themselves in individual stories without sacrificing the fantasy epic scale. If you’ve ever longed for a blend of classic RPG charm and modern presentation, don’t let this one pass you by.

Lies of P: Pinocchio Reimagined in a Dark Fantasy

A left-field entry but utterly unforgettable, Lies of P reinvents the Pinocchio legend as a twisted, dark fantasy action RPG. Set within the haunting city of Krat, mechanical horrors stalk the streets and secrets wait behind every shadow. Your choices—both moral and strategic—reverberate through the story, leading to a variety of endings shaped by your approach to humanity and deception.

The combat here is inspired by the best of “soulslike” design, combining tight encounters with weapon crafting and clever animations. Unique special abilities and customizable prosthetics add layers of strategy, ensuring that every battle feels fresh and consequential.

It’s the worldbuilding that truly sets Lies of P apart: from Victorian architecture to characters both tragic and chilling, there’s an unsettling beauty threaded through the entire experience. For players looking for something familiar yet completely unclassifiable, this game is a must-play.

Sea of Stars: Nostalgia Meets Modern RPG Sensibilities

Channeling the spirit of classic Super Nintendo-era RPGs, Sea of Stars wears its inspiration from titles like Chrono Trigger proudly. But far from just a rehash, it injects new life via inventively interactive combat, environmental puzzles, and a dazzling, hand-crafted world.

The adventure follows two Children of the Solstice, wielders of eclipse magic, as they seek to thwart an ancient evil threatening their world. Lighthearted banter, minigames, and quirky side characters bubble up throughout, offsetting the epic stakes with just the right touch of warmth.

Beneath the retro aesthetic lies real innovation. Seamless transitions between exploration and battle, a soundtrack composed in part by legendary composer Yasunori Mitsuda, and tight pacing make this one of the most satisfying new RPGs for old-school fans and newcomers alike.

Honorable Mentions: Hidden Gems and Fan Favorites

There’s no shortage of imaginative fantasy role-playing experiences this year. Mention must be made of the following titles that might not dominate headlines, but are more than worthy of your attention:

  • Pathfinder: Wrath of the Righteous Enhanced Edition — A sprawling, isometric RPG steeped in classic “choose-your-own-adventure” storytelling, with an insane amount of player freedom and intricate party customization.
  • Disco Elysium: The Final Cut — While not traditional fantasy, its surreal, dreamlike setting and branching dialogue make for an unmissable roleplaying journey.
  • Divinity: Original Sin 2 (Definitive Edition) — Old but gold, its party-based battles, co-op play, and story flexibility still set a benchmark for the genre.
  • Wartales — An open-world, mercenary sim where survival, gritty realism, and emergent storytelling give every playthrough a different flavor.

Fantasy RPGs: A Universe of Possibilities

The fantasy RPG landscape has never been more vibrant, driven by fan passion, developer innovation, and an enduring love of epic storytelling. Whether you’re questing alone or with friends, seeking mechanical mastery or rich worlds to get lost in, you’ll find something to ignite your imagination on this year’s roster.

These games offer more than just escapism—they become portals to strange, wondrous worlds where you’re free to write your own legend. There’s never been a better time to be a fan of the genre, whether you’re returning to familiar lands or setting off on uncharted adventures.

Pick your quest, sharpen your sword (or spellbook), and get ready to author your journey. Out there, new realms and unforgettable experiences await. The only question left: which fantasy RPG world will you call home this year?
