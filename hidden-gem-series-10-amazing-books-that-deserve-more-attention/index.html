Hidden Gem Series: 10 Amazing Books That Deserve More Attention


Book lovers, have you ever read a book that left you in awe, wondering why you hadn’t heard more about it? Sometimes, books fly under the radar due to a variety of reasons like marketing, publishing timing, or the complicated nature of literary themes. Each of these undervalued gems hides an irresistible story just waiting to be discovered by open minds. Today, we’re celebrating ten of these hidden gems that deserve more attention.

1. Housekeeping by Marilynne Robinson

A hauntingly beautiful novel by the Pulitzer Prize-winning author of Gilead, this book tells the story of two washed-up sisters, Ruth and Sally. As they struggle with the loss of their parents and each other, their memories turn into a mixed pot of hallucination, family secrets, and revelations. Robinson’s prose weaves and flows, painting a mesmerizing portrait of the quiet drama happening in their rambling house.

2. Small Shops by Simonetta Agnello Cipriani

Step into the world of Rome’s secret and eccentric Venice aromatic coffee market. Readers get an entrée into the sultry and labyrinthian cobblestone streets, discovering mysterious characters along the way. It takes readers on a palate-pleasing tour, entwining bittersweet love stories, secrets, and long-lost treasures that give the book a Italian Renaissance flavor that seduces the reader page after page.

3. My Name Is Red by Orhan Pamuk

If you’re a fan of murder mysteries shrouded in historic arts and Ottoman culture, then Orhan Pamuk’s My Name Is Red is a book to sink your teeth into. This novel weaves its way through a web of hypocrisy, jealousy, and intrigue, where art meets murder and anything is possible in the twilight of a dying empire.

4. The God of Small Things by Arundhati Roy

The novel, by the same author as The Ministry of Utmost Happiness, explores themes such as the caste system and political unrest in a story set against the backdrop of the southern state of India. It is a tale of forbidden love between an Indian Christian woman and a Syrian Muslim man, exploring their tragic relationship that ensues societal consequences.

5. Invisible Monsters by Chuck Palahniuk

Chuck Palahniuk dives into the chaotic world of fashion, sex, and death, with a protagonist (and narrator) who is paid for her corpse-like features to be displayed in shop windows. As she recovers from a near-fatal accident, reality takes a sinister turn as she discovers her neighbor isn’t quite who she seems. Dark humor and gritty realism combine with daring narrative experiments to create a unique and fresh experience.

6. Fates and Furies by Lauren Groff

Lauren Groff delivers a mesmerizing dual-narrative masterpiece that portrays a seemingly perfect marriage with hidden fractures beneath. The respective voices of Lotto and Mathilde – husband and wife – unravel their stories, revealing a tangled web of tragedy, betrayal, and their individual search for meaning. This gripping novel introduces a fresh spin on relationships and the unexpected behind seemingly perfect lives.

7. The Tongue-Cut Sparrow by Ryūnosuke Akutagawa

Spanning over 300 years of Japanese history, Ryūnosuke Akutagawa’s English-translated collection of short stories include the famous “Rashomon,” encouraging readers to peer through multiple perspectives to find truth. With tales of love, deceit, and loyalty, Akutagawa casts a light on the complexities of human relationships, making for a compelling reading experience.

8. Mrs. Dalloway by Virginia Woolf

A masterfully written novel that wonderfully depicts the innermost thoughts of its characters, Virginia Woolf’s Mrs. Dalloway focuses on two women living in London, Clarissa Dalloway and Septimus Warren Smith. As Clarissa prepares for a grand party, Septimus grapples with war trauma. This insightful exploration of emotional and psychological facets makes the novel a timeless classic.

9. Girl with a Pearl Earring by Tracy Chevalier

An unforgettable tale of intrigue and art told against the backdrop of 17th-century Delft, Netherlands. The protagonist, Griet, becomes assistant to the Dutch painter Johannes Vermeer, providing a glimpse of his creative process and inspirations. Through character development and descriptive writing, Chevalier brings to life a vibrant world of humans and masterpieces woven intricately together.

10. Americanah by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

The story of two young Nigerians, Ifemelu and Obinze, who leave their home country to pursue education in America and UK, respectively. Ifemelu blogs about her experiences of being a black woman in America, while Obinze attempts to navigate Britain’s immigrant system. Their return to Nigeria for different reasons brings forth identity struggles and cultural clashes, delivering a poignant exploration of race, class, and the cultural complexities of a modern “world citizen.”

These select books are by no means an exhaustive list, but offer a gateway into lesser-known literary riches. So the next time you encounter a book that doesn’t quite reach the headlines, take the chance to explore it. You might just find your favorite hidden gem. Happy reading!
