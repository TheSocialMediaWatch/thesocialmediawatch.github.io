The Best Comedy Specials for Your Weekend Binge


There’s nothing quite like kicking back after a long week with a good laugh. Whether you’re winding down from a hectic workweek, relaxing with friends, or simply enjoying some “me time,” comedy specials offer the perfect escape. The rise of streaming platforms has brought some of the funniest, most unique voices to the forefront, making it easier than ever to find a special that suits your sense of humor. So, if you’re looking for the perfect comedy special to binge-watch this weekend, look no further.

In this guide, we’ve compiled a list of some of the best comedy specials across different styles and genres. From sharp political humor to absurd storytelling, we’ve got something for everyone. Grab your popcorn, get comfy, and prepare for a weekend of uncontrollable laughter.

1. Dave Chappelle: Sticks & Stones

Dave Chappelle has solidified himself as one of the most influential comedians of our time, and Sticks & Stones only further reinforces his standing in the comedy world. Released in 2019, this special received both praise and criticism for its unfiltered, often controversial takes on modern issues. Chappelle’s trademark ability to challenge societal norms and deliver razor-sharp commentary on race, culture, and politics is on full display here.

What makes Sticks & Stones a standout comedy special is its unrelenting honesty. Chappelle never shies away from difficult or taboo subjects, making you laugh while simultaneously encouraging deeper reflection on the state of the world. His delivery is as smooth as ever, blending humor with a sense of social commentary that lingers long after the punchline lands.

If you’re looking for a comedy special that pushes boundaries while keeping you in stitches, Sticks & Stones is a must-watch.

2. Hannah Gadsby: Nanette

When it comes to transformative comedy, Nanette by Hannah Gadsby is a game-changer. This 2018 special turned the comedy world upside down by blending traditional stand-up with powerful, emotionally charged storytelling. Gadsby opens with jokes that are seemingly lighthearted but gradually delves into deeper, more personal themes surrounding identity, trauma, and the struggles of being a woman in comedy.

Nanette isn’t just a comedy special—it’s an exploration of what comedy can do. It’s a delicate balance of humor and heart, as Gadsby deconstructs the very art form of stand-up. By the end of this special, you’ll have laughed, cried, and reconsidered what you thought a comedy special could be. It’s an emotional rollercoaster, and it’s one that will leave a lasting impact.

This is the perfect pick for anyone looking for a thought-provoking, deeply personal comedy special that goes beyond the punchline.

3. John Mulaney: Kid Gorgeous

John Mulaney’s Kid Gorgeous is a comedy special that excels in both its observational humor and its flawless delivery. Released in 2018, Mulaney’s charm and timing are on full display here as he weaves through stories from his childhood, his time in Hollywood, and his experiences with quirky characters.

Mulaney’s sharp wit and perfect comedic timing make Kid Gorgeous an easy watch, but it’s his ability to elevate the simplest stories into memorable moments that truly set this special apart. From hilarious takes on growing up to absurd tales about his time in the world of showbiz, Mulaney proves that comedy doesn’t have to be loud or abrasive to be funny—it just needs to be well-crafted.

If you’re a fan of clever, clean comedy with a touch of nostalgia, Kid Gorgeous is the ideal pick for your weekend binge.

4. Ali Wong: Baby Cobra

Ali Wong burst onto the comedy scene with Baby Cobra, and it’s easy to see why this 2016 special is a fan favorite. Wong’s unapologetic, bold style brings a fresh voice to the stand-up world. In Baby Cobra, she tackles subjects like motherhood, gender roles, and the challenges of being a woman in comedy—all while delivering side-splitting laughs.

What makes Baby Cobra stand out is Wong’s raw energy and confidence. She effortlessly mixes humor with vulnerability, creating a special that feels both relatable and boundary-pushing. It’s an empowering experience to watch Wong own the stage and address issues that women often face, all while making you laugh until your sides hurt.

If you’re looking for a comedy special that’s both hilarious and empowering, Baby Cobra should be at the top of your list.

5. Chris Rock: Tamborine

Chris Rock’s Tamborine is one of his most personal and reflective specials, released in 2018. After years of making sharp, biting social commentary, Rock shifts gears in this special to delve into his own life, particularly focusing on his marriage, family, and the challenges of midlife. Tamborine is still full of Rock’s signature observational humor, but it feels more introspective than his previous work.

The beauty of Tamborine lies in its vulnerability. Rock opens up about his own faults and mistakes, giving the audience a glimpse into his personal journey. His comedic delivery, as always, is impeccable, but there’s an added layer of depth that sets this special apart from others in his repertoire.

For those who appreciate comedy that isn’t just about laughs but also about reflection and growth, Tamborine is a must-watch.

6. Tiffany Haddish: Black Mitzvah

Tiffany Haddish’s Black Mitzvah is a celebration of her incredible rise to stardom, and it’s packed with her signature energy and charisma. In this 2019 special, Haddish combines humor with moments of personal reflection, touching on everything from her childhood experiences to her newfound success in Hollywood.

Haddish’s infectious personality and unapologetic humor make Black Mitzvah an absolute joy to watch. She brings a unique perspective to the comedy scene, addressing topics such as growing up in foster care, her cultural heritage, and the challenges she faces as a woman of color in the entertainment industry.

If you’re in the mood for a feel-good comedy special with heart and humor, Black Mitzvah is a perfect choice.

7. Bill Burr: Paper Tiger

Bill Burr’s Paper Tiger is a masterclass in irreverence, where he takes aim at everything from political correctness to cultural trends. Released in 2019, Burr’s unfiltered approach to comedy is on full display as he dissects the state of modern society with his signature blend of sarcasm and biting commentary.

What makes Paper Tiger so effective is Burr’s willingness to speak uncomfortable truths. He doesn’t shy away from controversial topics, making you laugh while also challenging your perspectives. Burr’s comedic style is sharp, aggressive, and unapologetically real, making this special a must-watch for fans of his no-holds-barred approach to comedy.

If you like your comedy to come with a side of critique, Paper Tiger will keep you laughing and thinking throughout.

8. Bo Burnham: Inside

Bo Burnham’s Inside, released in 2021, is a genre-defying comedy special that combines music, satire, and social commentary in a way that no one else has quite managed to do. Created during the COVID-19 pandemic, Inside takes viewers on a journey through Burnham’s isolation, mental health struggles, and observations of the modern world.

What sets Inside apart is its innovative approach to comedy. Burnham doesn’t just stand on a stage and tell jokes—he integrates music, visuals, and absurdist humor to create a truly unique viewing experience. The special is both hilarious and deeply reflective, offering commentary on social media culture, loneliness, and the pressures of modern life.

If you’re looking for a comedy special that’s unlike anything you’ve seen before, Inside is the perfect pick.

9. Kevin Hart: Zero Fucks Given

Kevin Hart’s Zero Fucks Given is a hilarious and candid look at his life, delivered with the high-energy style that fans have come to expect from the comedian. Released in 2020, this special sees Hart in his element, reflecting on everything from fatherhood to the ups and downs of his career, all while keeping the laughs rolling.

Hart’s ability to balance humor with heart makes Zero Fucks Given a standout. He’s always been a master of storytelling, and this special is no exception, as he brings a mix of personal anecdotes, observational humor, and self-deprecating jokes that will have you laughing from start to finish.

For a feel-good comedy special that’s both hilarious and uplifting, Zero Fucks Given is the perfect choice for your weekend binge.

10. Ricky Gervais: Humanity

Ricky Gervais’ Humanity is a comedy special that’s as controversial as it is funny. Released in 2018, Humanity sees Gervais return to his roots with his dark, often provocative humor. Known for his bold takes on society and human nature, Gervais delivers a special that’s brimming with sharp insights and unfiltered opinions.

Humanity is perfect for those who enjoy comedy that doesn’t play it safe. Gervais takes on everything from celebrity culture to the absurdities of modern life, always pushing the envelope. His unapologetic style and irreverence make this special a thrilling ride for those who love to laugh while being challenged.

For a comedy special that isn’t afraid to push boundaries, Humanity is a must-watch.

Conclusion

This weekend, take a break from the ordinary and dive into one of these top-tier comedy specials. Whether you enjoy sharp political commentary, deeply personal reflections, or absurd humor, there’s something for everyone. These specials not only deliver laughs but also offer fresh perspectives and unique takes on the world around us. So, grab some snacks, settle in, and let these comedians make your weekend unforgettable.
