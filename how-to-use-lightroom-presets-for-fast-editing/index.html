How to Use Lightroom Presets for Fast Editing


In the world of digital photography, time and efficiency often play as critical a role as creativity and skill. For photographers, both amateur and professional, keeping a consistent editing style while managing large volumes of images can be a daunting task. That’s where Lightroom presets come in, revolutionizing the editing workflow by enabling quick, effective, and consistent results with just a few clicks.

Lightroom presets are a game-changer for fast photo editing. Whether you’re processing a wedding shoot, curating your travel photography portfolio, or enhancing your Instagram posts, presets can help you save hours without compromising the quality and individuality of your images. Let’s dive into everything you need to know about using Lightroom presets for fast editing, from understanding what they are to mastering their application and customization.

What Are Lightroom Presets?

Lightroom presets are pre-configured editing settings saved within Adobe Lightroom, the popular photo editing software. These settings include adjustments like exposure, contrast, color balance, tone curves, clarity, vibrance, and more. When you apply a preset, Lightroom instantly applies all these changes to your photo, creating a consistent look and style.

Think of presets as filters on steroids—they offer much more control and subtlety than typical social media filters, allowing edits that can be both artistic and professional.

Why Use Lightroom Presets?

Before we get into how to use presets efficiently, it’s worth highlighting why so many photographers rely on them:

  1. Speed
    Editing dozens or hundreds of photos individually is time-consuming. Applying a preset to all photos with similar lighting or style can reduce work dramatically.

  2. Consistency
    Presets allow you to maintain a uniform look across a collection of images, which is particularly beneficial for branding and storytelling.

  3. Learning Tool
    Exploring presets crafted by other photographers can help you understand which editing settings impact your photos, sharpening your editing skills.

  4. Creative Kickstart
    If you ever feel stuck creatively, presets provide a new baseline from which you can adapt and experiment further.

Getting Started With Lightroom Presets

Installing Presets

The first step to using presets is installing them in Lightroom. If you’re using Lightroom Classic, presets usually come as .xmp or .lrtemplate files. Newer versions also support .xmp exclusively.

Here’s how to install:

  1. Open Lightroom Classic.
  2. Navigate to the “Develop” module.
  3. On the left panel, locate the “Presets” tab.
  4. Click the + button near the top of the Presets panel and select “Import Presets.”
  5. Browse to your preset files, select them, and click “Import.”

For Lightroom CC (the cloud-based version), presets are imported slightly differently:

  1. Launch Lightroom CC.
  2. Go to the “Edit” module.
  3. Click on the Presets panel at the bottom.
  4. Click on the three dots and select “Import Presets.”
  5. Select desired preset files and import.

Once imported, your presets will appear in the presets panel, ready to use.

Applying a Preset

To apply a preset, simply select a photo and click on the desired preset. Lightroom will instantly apply all associated edits. You can toggle the effect on and off by clicking the “\” key to compare before and after.

Advanced Techniques to Speed Up Editing With Presets

Batch Editing Multiple Photos

One of Lightroom’s most powerful features combined with presets is batch editing. Instead of applying a preset one photo at a time, you can quickly edit large groups:

  1. Select multiple photos in the Library module using Shift+Click or Ctrl/Cmd+Click.
  2. Apply your preset to one photo.
  3. With all photos still selected, click “Sync” at the bottom of the Develop module.
  4. Choose the settings you want to sync (generally, presets include tone and color changes, so syncing those is key).
  5. Click “Synchronize” and Lightroom will apply the same preset and adjustments to every selected photo.

Batch editing can reduce hours of clicking individual adjustments to just a few seconds.

Creating Your Own Presets

Using presets created by others is great, but creating your own can significantly speed your workflow if you have a preferred style.

To create a preset:

  1. Edit a photo in the Develop module to your liking.
  2. Click the + icon on the Presets panel and choose “Create Preset.”
  3. Name your preset and select which settings to include.
  4. Save it to a custom preset group for easier access.

Once saved, you can instantly apply your signature style to any photo with a single click, further minimizing the time spent fine-tuning each image.

Customizing Presets Per Image

No two photos are the same, even if you want to keep a consistent style. After applying a preset, tweak exposure, highlights, shadows, or other settings to better fit the image’s lighting and detail.

This hybrid approach—preset + fine-tuning—maintains speed while ensuring each photo looks its best.

Tips for Using Presets Effectively

Start with Raw Images

Presets work best on raw files because these contain all image data, allowing maximum adjustment flexibility. JPEGs are more limited and may result in less dynamic edits.

Use Preset Groups and Organize

If you work with many presets, staying organized in groups or folders helps you locate and apply them quickly. For example, you might have categories like Portraits, Landscapes, Black & White, or Vintage.

Avoid Overuse

Although presets speed editing, avoid blindly applying them without checking the results. Lighting, skin tones, and overall image quality can change how presets look, so always evaluate before finalizing edits.

Adjust Presets to Fit Your Style

Presets are starting points, not final edits. Use them as foundations to build upon rather than a finished product.

Working With Presets and Exporting Efficiently

Once your edits are finalized, Lightroom’s export feature lets you quickly produce finished images in the format and size you need.

To speed things up for exporting:

  • Use export presets that save your preferred file type, size, and naming conventions.
  • If you batch edited your photos with presets, export them all simultaneously through the Library by selecting them and choosing Export with your preset settings.

This end-to-end process—from importing to batch editing to exporting—creates a seamless pipeline that saves countless hours.

Common Challenges When Using Presets and How to Overcome Them

Color Shifts on Different Cameras

Every camera sensor renders colors slightly differently. A preset might look amazing on photos from one model but unnatural on another. To overcome this:

  • Calibrate your camera profile in Lightroom.
  • Consider creating variations of a preset tailored for different cameras.

Presets Not Matching Your Vision

Sometimes presets may not fit your creative vision exactly. In this case:

  • Use the preset just as a foundational look.
  • Adjust individual sliders to better align with your style.
  • Tweak contrast, temperature, or saturation to suit the mood.

Over-editing with Presets

Heavy-handed presets can produce harsh or unrealistic effects. If this happens:

  • Reduce the preset’s effect by lowering its influence via the Amount slider (available in Lightroom CC).
  • Alternatively, undo and manually reapply adjustments in smaller increments.

Exploring the Creative Possibilities of Lightroom Presets

Lightroom presets don’t just speed up editing—they open doors to experimentation and artistic development. You can find presets that emulate vintage film, cinematic tones, high contrast black and white, pastel color palettes, and much more. Using presets is a great way to discover what styles appeal most to you without spending hours trial-and-error.

Many photographers develop their own presets based on their workflow and preferred aesthetics, often sharing or selling them to others looking for similar effects. This artistic sharing culture makes the photography community vibrant and continually evolving.

Final Thoughts

Mastering Lightroom presets can transform your editing process from a tedious chore into an enjoyable, creative part of photography. By leveraging presets to maintain consistency, speed up batch editing, and streamline exports, you give yourself more time to focus on shooting and storytelling.

Remember, the key to fast editing is not just using presets but integrating them thoughtfully into your workflow. Combine them with refinement and an understanding of your images to get the best results. Whether you’re a hobbyist uploading photos to social media or a professional delivering client galleries, Lightroom presets can be a cornerstone of your editing arsenal.

So go ahead, experiment with presets, craft your own, and watch your post-processing efficiency and creativity soar.

Happy editing!
