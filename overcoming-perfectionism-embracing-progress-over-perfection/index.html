Overcoming Perfectionism: Embracing Progress Over Perfection


Perfectionism can be a crippling affliction. It prevents us from taking risks, stifles creativity, and hampers our ability to grow and learn. This is because we set such impossibly high standards for ourselves, that any deviation from perfection is deemed a failure. The good news is that we can overcome this destructive mindset by embracing progress over perfection.

The first step to conquering perfectionism is to recognize its hold over us. Many of us operate under the delusion that we must be flawless in everything we do. However, this belief puts undue pressure on ourselves, easily leading to frustration, disappointment and even despair. Realize that nobody is infallible, and that’s okay. It’s a crucial aspect of being human.

Secondly, we must redefine our standards. Instead of seeking perfection, aim for progress. Progress is a far more attainable goal. Unlike perfection, progress is a continuous, fluid state that improves with time and effort. With each small step forward, we inch closer to our ultimate goal, growing stronger and more adept in the process.

In addition, remember to be kind to yourself. Treat yourself with compassion and patience, as you would a dear friend or family member. Be gentle with your self-criticism, and understand that mistakes and setbacks are an inherent part of life. They are opportunities for growth, teaching, and learning.

Moreover, surround yourself with a support system that uplifts and encourages you. Positive influences can help you to see past your flaws and concentrate on your achievements. Talk to friends and family about your struggles and share your successes with them. A shoulder to lean on and a sounding board for ideas can be invaluable assets in overcoming perfectionism.

Furthermore, practice mindfulness and self-awareness to strengthen your mental well-being. Check in with yourself daily. Isolate your thoughts, feelings, and underlying emotions. Pinpoint your triggers, recognize when perfectionism kicks in, and work to counteract it. Mindfulness techniques can provide crucial insight into your thinking and help to identify potential hurdles.

Finally, recognize that it’s okay to ask for help. This may be the most challenging task, as perfectionists often prefer to do things alone. However, asking for help shows maturity and vulnerability, qualities that are rarely associated with perfectionism. Reach out to colleagues, coworkers, or coaches to learn from their experiences and growth.

Keeping these points in mind, overcoming perfectionism becomes a more manageable feat. By accepting progress instead of striving for perfection, we open the door to personal growth, risk-taking, and creativity. The journey may be fraught with bumps and bruises, but the rewards of embracing progress far outweigh rigid perfectionism. Let go of unreasonable expectations and accept the beautiful imperfections that make us who we are.
