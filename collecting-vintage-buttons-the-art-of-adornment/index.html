Collecting Vintage Buttons: The Art of Adornment


Buttons have been an integral part of our daily lives for centuries. From the simplest fasteners to intricate pieces of art, buttons have evolved significantly over the years. One of the most fascinating aspects of buttons is their ability to tell stories of the past. Vintage buttons, in particular, are a treasure trove of history, design, and craftsmanship. In this article, we will delve into the world of collecting vintage buttons, exploring their significance, types, and the art of adornment.

The Significance of Vintage Buttons

Vintage buttons are more than just simple fasteners; they are a reflection of the era in which they were created. Each button is a tiny piece of history, bearing the marks of its time. Whether it’s the intricate designs of the Victorian era, the bold colors of the Art Deco period, or the minimalist approach of the 1960s, vintage buttons are a window into the past. They allow us to connect with our ancestors, to understand their values, and to appreciate their craftsmanship.

Types of Vintage Buttons

Vintage buttons come in a staggering array of shapes, sizes, and materials. From the delicate porcelain buttons of the 18th century to the chunky, hand-carved wooden buttons of the 19th century, each type of button has its unique characteristics. Some of the most sought-after types of vintage buttons include:

  • Cloisonné buttons: These buttons feature intricate metalwork designs, often with intricate patterns and colors.
  • Glass buttons: Made from delicate glass, these buttons come in a range of colors and shapes.
  • Metal buttons: From the elegant silver buttons of the Victorian era to the bold, chunky metal buttons of the 1960s, metal buttons are a staple of vintage collecting.
  • Coral buttons: These buttons are made from natural coral, often featuring intricate designs and patterns.
  • Woven buttons: These buttons are made from woven fibers, such as silk or wool, and often feature intricate patterns and designs.

The Art of Adornment

Vintage buttons are not just interesting pieces of history; they are also a testament to the art of adornment. Adornment has been a part of human culture for centuries, with people using various objects to express their identity, status, and creativity. Vintage buttons are a perfect example of this. Each button is a tiny work of art, carefully crafted to adorn clothing, accessories, or even furniture.

The art of adornment is not just limited to the buttons themselves; it’s also about the way they are used. Vintage buttons can be used to create a variety of looks, from the elegant and refined to the bold and playful. They can be used to add a touch of whimsy to a plain outfit or to create a statement piece of jewelry.

Tips for Collecting Vintage Buttons

Collecting vintage buttons can be a fun and rewarding hobby, but it requires patience, dedication, and a keen eye for detail. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Start with what you have: Look through your grandmother’s old trunks, your mother’s attic, or your own closet for vintage buttons. You might be surprised at what you find.
  • Join a community: Look for online forums, social media groups, or local collectible clubs focused on vintage buttons. These communities can provide valuable resources, advice, and inspiration.
  • Attend auctions and estate sales: Auctions and estate sales are a great way to find rare and unique vintage buttons. Be prepared to bid, and don’t be afraid to walk away if the price is too high.
  • Research and learn: The more you know about vintage buttons, the better you’ll be at identifying and appreciating them. Research different types of buttons, their history, and their value.
  • Store your collection properly: Vintage buttons are delicate and require proper care. Store them in acid-free tissue paper, avoid exposure to light, and keep them away from moisture.

Conclusion

Collecting vintage buttons is a fascinating hobby that requires patience, dedication, and a keen eye for detail. From the intricate designs of the Victorian era to the bold colors of the Art Deco period, vintage buttons are a window into the past. They allow us to connect with our ancestors, to understand their values, and to appreciate their craftsmanship. Whether you’re a seasoned collector or just starting out, the art of collecting vintage buttons is a journey worth taking.
