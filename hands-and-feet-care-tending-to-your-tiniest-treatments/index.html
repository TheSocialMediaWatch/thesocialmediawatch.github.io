Hands and Feet Care: Tending to Your Tiniest Treatments


When it comes to self-care, many of us tend to focus on our faces and keep our skin glowing with an array of potions and rituals. But let’s not forget the extremities that help us walk, type, and perform everyday tasks - our hands and feet. Proper hand and foot care is often overlooked, and yet, these critical body parts are as deserving of our attention as the skin on our faces. Here are some tips and tricks to make your hands and feet feel soft, supple, and well taken care of.

Why Should We All Care for Our Hands and Feet?

Before jumping into the hand and foot care routine, let’s glimpse into why proper care is essential. Hands and feet are exposed to a plethora of bacteria, viruses, and daily wear and tear. Dry skin, caused by a lack of moisture, can lead to flakiness, cracking, or even infections at severe cases. Taking care of our hands and feet is crucial to maintaining overall wellbeing and boosts our self-confidence.

Hand Care: A Step Beyond Hand Washing

Hand care doesn’t merely mean constant washing. Make it a point to follow these tips for healthier hands:

  1. Moisturize frequently: Like our faces, our hands lose moisture throughout the day and consequently become prone to dryness and cracks. Carry a travel-sized hand cream with you and apply it whenever needed, especially after washing or using hand sanitizers.

  2. Gentle exfoliation: Every week, give your hands a little spa treatment by gently exfoliating them. You can use a gentle scrub or create a mixture using sugar and olive oil. This helps remove dead skin cells and allows for better absorption of hand creams.

  3. Massage: Massaging your hands is not only relaxing but also helps stimulate blood circulation andSEA improves flexibility. Consider getting a hand massage or try to do it yourself at home.

  4. Protect your hands: When all else fails, protect your hands! Wear gloves at night while sleeping to lock in the moisture and keep your hands looking youthful and soft.

Foot Care: The Neglected Self-Care

If you think only your hands need some pampering, think again. Foot care is often ironic and overlooked. Try these measures for healthier and happier feet:

  1. Mild exfoliation: Exfoliation is not only for your hands, but it’s also for your feet. Every time you bathe, scrub your feet gently using an exfoliating cleanser or a pumice stone to remove dead skin and calluses gently.

  2. Moisturize, moisturize and moisturize: Dry, flaky feet are unpleasant, to say the least. Apply a thick foot cream post-bathing to keep them hydrated. Make sure to focus on the heel area, which tends to be the driest.

  3. Soak ’em: Treat your feet to a warm soak at least once a week. You can add Epsom salts, peppermint oil, or apple cider vinegar to enhance the foot-soaking experience.

  4. Trim and file: Regular foot maintenance includes trimming your toenails and filing down sharp edges. Don’t forget to moisturize between the toes to keep bacterial and fungal infections at bay.

Remember, consistent care for these unsung heroes (our hands and feet) is key to looking and feeling your best. Hand and foot care should not be considered as a luxury, but rather, a necessity. Pamper your hands and feet, for they truly deserve it!
