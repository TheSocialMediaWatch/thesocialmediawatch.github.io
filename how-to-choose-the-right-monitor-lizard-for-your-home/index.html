How to Choose the Right Monitor Lizard for Your Home


Monitor lizards are captivating and intelligent reptiles that have become increasingly popular as exotic pets. Their majestic appearance, inquisitive nature, and variety in size and behavior make them fascinating companions for experienced reptile enthusiasts. However, choosing the right monitor lizard for your home requires thoughtful consideration and understanding of their specific needs. Before introducing a monitor lizard into your life, it’s essential to assess your environment, experience level, and commitment to their care. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to make an informed decision.

Understanding Monitor Lizards as Pets

Monitor lizards belong to the genus Varanus, which includes over 80 species ranging from small and manageable to massive and demanding. They are native to various regions, including Africa, Asia, and Australia, and have distinct care requirements depending on their natural habitat.

Unlike more common pets such as cats or dogs, monitor lizards require specialized care and knowledge. They are not a beginner-friendly reptile species, as their size, diet, and enclosure needs can be challenging for first-time owners. Understanding their behavior and habits is essential before making your decision.

Monitor lizards are intelligent and can recognize their handlers over time. With proper taming and care, they can become interactive pets, though they still require respect and patience due to their natural instincts. Their exotic beauty and unique personalities deserve a prepared and well-informed keeper.

Assessing Your Experience Level With Reptiles

One of the first factors to evaluate when choosing a monitor lizard is your prior experience with reptiles. These lizards can be quite demanding, and owning one requires a strong understanding of reptile husbandry, including housing, heating, lighting, and diet. Beginner owners may find some of the smaller, more docile species easier to manage, while seasoned reptile enthusiasts with experience handling large reptiles might consider larger species.

If you are new to reptiles or exotic pets in general, you may want to start with easier species or even consider another reptile before venturing into monitor lizards. Handling a bearded dragon, leopard gecko, or corn snake may help you develop the skills and confidence needed to care for a monitor lizard.

Choosing the Right Species for Your Home

Monitor lizards vary drastically in size, temperament, and care needs. Here are some popular species that you may consider for your home:

1. Ackie Monitor (Varanus acanthurus)

The Ackie monitor is one of the most popular species for home reptile enthusiasts, especially for those who are new to monitor lizards. Often referred to as the “dwarf monitor,” the Ackie is small, typically growing to around 2–3 feet in length. They are relatively docile, hardy, and manageable while still displaying the energetic and curious personality common among monitor lizards.

Key Considerations:

  • Small size makes housing more manageable.
  • Requires a large, well-ventilated enclosure, preferably 4x2x2 feet or larger.
  • Needs a basking spot with temperatures close to 120–150°F and an ambient temperature around 85°F.
  • Their diet consists of insects like crickets, roaches, and the occasional pinky mouse.

2. Savannah Monitor (Varanus exanthematicus)

The Savannah monitor is a popular choice due to its relatively calm demeanor and impressive appearance. Native to Africa, these lizards can grow up to 3–4 feet in length and have stocky, muscular bodies. However, their care requirements are more demanding than those of smaller species, making them better suited for moderately experienced keepers.

Key Considerations:

  • Requires a large enclosure, at least 6x3x3 feet.
  • Diet should be high in protein, with gut-loaded insects and lean meats. Avoid overfeeding to prevent obesity.
  • Socialization and regular interaction are crucial to curb potential aggression.

3. Nile Monitor (Varanus niloticus)

The Nile monitor is not recommended for novice reptile owners. These lizards are among the largest commonly kept monitor species, reaching lengths of 5–7 feet. They are strong, highly active, and often have a feisty temperament, requiring an experienced handler with the space and commitment to meet their needs.

Key Considerations:

  • Requires an extremely spacious enclosure, often described as a “mini zoo exhibit.”
  • Can be aggressive and are difficult to tame, requiring regular handling and respect.
  • Appropriate diet includes whole prey items like rodents, fish, and eggs.

4. Blackthroat Monitor (Varanus albigularis)

The Blackthroat monitor is another large species, known for its calm demeanor when properly raised. They can grow up to 5–7 feet and are often described as “gentle giants” among monitor enthusiasts. However, their size and care requirements mean they are not suitable for all homes.

Key Considerations:

  • Requires an enclosure measuring at least 8x4x4 feet, with even bigger spaces preferred.
  • A diet consisting of lean proteins like turkey, eggs, insects, and the occasional rodent is necessary.
  • They are relatively hearty but require proper environmental control, including basking areas over 120°F.

5. Asian Water Monitor (Varanus salvator)

Asian water monitors are one of the largest monitor lizard species, with some individuals exceeding 8 feet in length. They are often bold, highly intelligent, and energetic, making them an impressive species for advanced keepers with ample space.

Key Considerations:

  • Requires an exceptionally large enclosure with access to water features for swimming.
  • A strong, well-secured habitat is necessary, as they are escape artists.
  • Needs a high-protein diet and access to UV lighting to prevent metabolic issues.

Size and Space Requirements

When choosing a monitor lizard, assess the size you can accommodate within your home. Monitor lizards grow rapidly, and an enclosure that seems appropriate for a young lizard may quickly become inadequate. Remember, the larger the lizard, the greater the demands in terms of enclosure size, furnishings, and feeding.

For large monitor lizards, you’ll need to dedicate a significant portion of your home to create a secure and enriching environment. If limited space is a concern, smaller species like Ackie monitors are often more appropriate.

Behavioral Considerations

Temperament varies widely among monitor species. Some species are relatively docile and can become accustomed to handling, while others are naturally aggressive or skittish. Monitor lizards are not typically cuddly pets; they require patience and frequent interaction to build trust.

Additionally, keep in mind that monitor lizards have strong claws and their tails can deliver painful whips. They may also bite if threatened. Regular handling in a calm and consistent manner can help reduce stress, but some species remain defensive regardless of training.

Commitment Level

Monitor lizards have a long lifespan, often living 10–20 years or more in captivity with proper care. This means that owning a monitor lizard is a long-term commitment. Before choosing a monitor lizard, ensure you can dedicate the time, resources, and effort required to provide for their needs over the decades to come.

Final Considerations: Is a Monitor Lizard Right for You?

Owning a monitor lizard is not for everyone. These reptiles require significant investment, expertise, and dedication. If you are ready to take on the responsibility, they can be highly rewarding pets that offer insight into the fascinating world of reptiles.

Before bringing a monitor lizard into your home, you may want to consult with experienced reptile keepers, join online forums or local reptile clubs, and visit reptile expos to learn more about these remarkable creatures. The more you educate yourself, the better prepared you’ll be to provide a suitable and loving home for your monitor lizard.

By carefully assessing your capabilities and researching the unique needs of different species, you can make an informed choice and enjoy years of companionship with one of these amazing animals.
