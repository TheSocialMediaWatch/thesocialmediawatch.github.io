The Best Fashion Documentaries to Watch Right Now


Fashion is not just about the clothes we wear; it’s an ever-evolving art form that reflects culture, history, and individual expression. Documentaries about fashion provide a fascinating glimpse into the lives of designers, the intricate processes behind the scenes, and the societal influences that shape trends. Here’s a curated list of the best fashion documentaries that you can watch right now, offering insights, inspiration, and a deeper appreciation for the world of fashion.

1. “The September Issue” (2009)

This documentary takes you behind the scenes of Vogue’s most important issue of the year—the September issue. Directed by R.J. Cutler, it provides an intimate portrait of Anna Wintour, the legendary editor-in-chief. The film captures the intensity of the fashion world, showcasing the creative process of producing the magazine and the challenges faced by the team. Not only does it highlight Wintour’s formidable presence, but it also gives viewers a glimpse into the lives of her team, including Grace Coddington, who plays a pivotal role in the magazine’s visual storytelling.

2. “Dior and I” (2014)

“Dior and I” is a captivating look at the inner workings of the House of Dior as designer Raf Simons prepares for his first haute couture collection. The documentary reveals the pressures and triumphs of creating an iconic collection in just eight weeks. With emotional depth and stunning visuals, it showcases the dedication of the artisans and the artistry involved in high fashion. This film is a must-watch for anyone interested in the meticulous craftsmanship that goes into creating each piece.

3. “The First Monday in May” (2016)

This documentary explores the creation of the Met Gala, one of the most significant events in the fashion calendar. Directed by Andrew Rossi, it chronicles the preparation for the 2015 gala, which celebrated the “China: Through the Looking Glass” exhibition. The film features interviews with designers, celebrities, and museum curators, providing insights into the intersection of fashion and art. It highlights the extravagance of the event and the cultural conversations surrounding it, making it a fascinating watch for fashion enthusiasts.

4. “McQueen” (2018)

“McQueen” is a poignant documentary that delves into the life and career of the late fashion designer Alexander McQueen. Directed by Ian Bonhôte and Peter Ettedgui, it combines archival footage, interviews, and stunning visuals of McQueen’s most iconic collections. The film explores his troubled upbringing, creative genius, and the impact of his work on the fashion industry. It’s a tribute to a visionary who pushed boundaries and challenged norms, making it a compelling watch for anyone interested in the darker side of fashion.

5. “Abstract: The Art of Design” (2017)

While not solely focused on fashion, this docuseries on Netflix features episodes dedicated to various design disciplines, including fashion. The series offers an in-depth look at the creative process of influential designers, showcasing how they approach their craft. The episode featuring fashion designer Raf Simons provides insights into his design philosophy and the challenges he faces in the industry. This series is perfect for those who appreciate the intersection of various design forms, including fashion.

6. “Valentino: The Last Emperor” (2008)

Directed by Matt Tyrnauer, “Valentino: The Last Emperor” chronicles the life of legendary fashion designer Valentino Garavani. The film provides an intimate look at his final couture show and the legacy he leaves behind. Through interviews and behind-the-scenes footage, viewers get a sense of Valentino’s larger-than-life personality, his relationship with his longtime partner, and the world of high fashion. This documentary is a celebration of elegance and the art of couture, offering a glimpse into a world of glamour.

7. “The True Cost” (2015)

“The True Cost” is a thought-provoking documentary that examines the fashion industry’s impact on people and the planet. Directed by Andrew Morgan, the film delves into the ethical issues surrounding fast fashion, highlighting the environmental and social consequences of consumerism. It features interviews with garment workers, industry experts, and activists, making it a crucial watch for anyone who wants to understand the darker side of fashion. This documentary serves as a call to action to rethink our shopping habits and consider the true cost of our clothing.

8. “Franca: Chaos and Creation” (2016)

This documentary pays tribute to Franca Sozzani, the former editor-in-chief of Vogue Italia. Directed by her son, Francesco Carrozzini, “Franca: Chaos and Creation” explores her life, work, and the influence she had on the fashion world. The film features interviews with various fashion icons, including Karl Lagerfeld and Giorgio Armani, reflecting on Sozzani’s unique vision and editorial choices. It’s an inspiring look at a woman who championed creativity and innovation in fashion.

9. “Bill Cunningham New York” (2010)

“Bill Cunningham New York” tells the story of the legendary fashion photographer Bill Cunningham, who captured the essence of style on the streets of New York City. Directed by Richard Press, the documentary follows Cunningham as he navigates the fashion scene, capturing candid moments of everyday people and celebrities alike. His passion for fashion and dedication to his craft is infectious, making this film a delightful watch for anyone who appreciates genuine style and creativity.

10. “House of Z” (2017)

“House of Z” chronicles the rise and fall of designer Zac Posen, who became a household name in the fashion industry. Directed by Sandy Chronopoulos, the documentary provides an inside look at Posen’s creative process, his struggles with the pressures of the fashion world, and his journey to rebuild his brand. This film is both inspiring and relatable, showcasing the resilience required to succeed in the competitive fashion landscape.

11. “The Gospel According to André” (2017)

This documentary tells the story of André Leon Talley, a towering figure in the fashion industry known for his larger-than-life personality and contributions to Vogue. Directed by Kate Novack, the film offers insights into Talley’s life, including his upbringing in the segregated South and his rise to prominence in the fashion world. Through interviews and archival footage, viewers gain a deeper understanding of Talley’s influence and the barriers he broke as a Black man in a predominantly white industry.

12. “Something Fashionable” (2021)

“Something Fashionable” is a fresh documentary that explores the evolution of fashion in the digital age. It features interviews with emerging designers and influencers, discussing how social media and the internet have transformed the way fashion is consumed and created. This film is particularly relevant for those interested in the future of fashion and how technology shapes trends and consumer behavior.

13. “Yves Saint Laurent: The Last Collections” (2007)

This documentary provides a rare glimpse into the final collections of fashion maestro Yves Saint Laurent. Directed by David Teboul, it captures the emotions and creativity behind the designer’s last shows, showcasing his enduring influence on fashion. The film reflects on his legacy and the impact he had on the industry, making it a touching tribute to one of fashion’s greatest icons.

14. “In Vogue: The Editor’s Eye” (2012)

“In Vogue: The Editor’s Eye” explores the pivotal role that Vogue’s editors play in shaping fashion photography and visual storytelling. Through interviews and behind-the-scenes footage, the documentary highlights the creative vision of influential editors and their collaborations with renowned photographers. This film is a celebration of the artistry involved in creating iconic fashion images, making it a fascinating watch for those who appreciate the intersection of fashion and photography.

15. “The Fashion Fund” (2014)

This documentary series follows emerging fashion designers as they compete for a chance to receive funding and mentorship from the Council of Fashion Designers of America (CFDA). The series provides insights into the challenges faced by new designers and the intricacies of building a fashion brand. It’s an inspiring look at the future of fashion and the innovative minds shaping it, making it a must-watch for aspiring designers and fashion enthusiasts alike.

Conclusion

Fashion documentaries provide a unique lens through which we can explore the intricacies of the fashion industry. From the creative process behind iconic collections to the ethical dilemmas surrounding fast fashion, these films offer valuable insights and inspiration. Whether you’re a fashion devotee or simply curious about the world of style, these documentaries are sure to captivate and inform. So grab some popcorn, sit back, and immerse yourself in the compelling stories of fashion’s most influential figures and movements.
