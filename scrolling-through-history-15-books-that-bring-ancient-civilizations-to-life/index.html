Scrolling Through History: 15 Books That Bring Ancient Civilizations to Life


There is something undeniably captivating about the relics, scriptures, and untold stories of ancient civilizations. These long-lost worlds lay buried beneath the sands of time, and with a single stroke of the pen, devoted historians, writers, and scholars bring these once-forgotten realms to life. In this journey, we’ll explore fifteen books that, through research, imagination, or a little bit of both, empower us to scroll through the annals of history and stand witness to the brilliance that flourished eons ago.

  1. The Iliad by Homer (circa 8th century BCE)

Let’s begin with the timeless epic poem, The Iliad, penned by the enigmatic Homer. This masterpiece tells the tale of the Trojan War, one of the most significant conflicts in ancient times. As you scroll through its pages, hop on the journey like Achilles and wander through the battlegrounds of Troy, bear witness to cunning Odysseus, and embody the valiance of heroes who fought for honor and glory.

  1. The Odyssey by Homer (circa 8th century BCE)

Complementing The Iliad is The Odyssey, arguably the finest travel literature by the ancient world. Following the exploits of Odysseus, this literary gem encapsulates the vast expanse of the known world. Skip onboard Odysseus’ ship, visit exotic lands, and confront the capricious Greek gods while unraveling the hero’s prowess and resilience.

  1. The Epic of Gilgamesh (circa 2100-1600 BCE)

An ancient Babylonian poem, The Epic of Gilgamesh, highlights the adventure of the eponymous hero, a ruler seeking immortality, battling beasts, and binding friendships. Akin to an ancient fantasy novel, it gives a glimpse into the mythology, culture, and preoccupations of the Sumerians and Babylonians.

  1. The Histories by Herodotus (circa 440 BCE)

Often hailed as the ‘Father of History,’ Herodotus pens down an incredible narrative of the Greco-Persian Wars in The Histories. Scroll through his chronicles to touch upon the empires of Lydia, Persia, and Greece, and bear witness to the mysterious customs and beasts the author encountered during his travels.

  1. The Aeneid by Virgil (circa 19 BCE)

Conjured from the pen of Virgil, The Aeneid becomes a splendid panoply of Ancient Roman history as it narrates the saga of Aeneas. Fleeing the aftermath of the Trojan War, Aeneas, an exile, undertakes a perilous journey to establish the lineage of the founding of Rome. Traverse Italy’s landscapes, witness the prophetic interludes of the gods, and scroll through the dawn of one of history’s renowned civilizations.

  1. The Republic by Plato (circa 375 BCE)

In The Republic, Plato weaves an intellectual study of the ideal city-state, exploration of justice, and political philosophy. While scrolling through the pages, take upon the mantle of the philosopher-kings, travel through the_rs of a utopian society, and glean into the depths of human nature and metaphysical essence.

  1. The Art of War by Sun Tzu (circa 500 BCE)

An invaluable treatise on military strategy and tactics, The Art of War by Sun Tzu offers exceptional advice on astute leadership and decision-making. Piercing through the pages reveals maneuvers, tactics, and principles that still resonate centuries later.

  1. The Analects by Confucius (circa 500 BCE)

Cherished in Eastern philosophy, The Analects by Confucius reflects the core principles of Chinese culture and morality. Listen to the teachings of the great thinker, trace the values of loyalty, benevolence, and filial piety, and delve into countless anecdotes that breathe life into the pages.

  1. Medieval Lives by Tony Hunt (1982)

Enter the enigmatic and mystifying world of the Middle Ages through the exploration of Tony Hunt’s Medieval Lives. Get a close peep at the lives of the knights and monks of Europe, marauding Vikings, and merchant adventurers as these characters traverse the lands, seas, and history.

  1. Siddhartha by Hermann Hesse (1922)

A spiritual odyssey from Hermann Hesse that sheds light on the life of the Buddha, Siddhartha beautifully conveys the nuances of ancient Indian philosophies. Scroll through to follow the protagonist’s journey towards self-realization, from ascetic toSamana, encounter the river’s guardian and conclude in the beautiful temple gardens.

  1. Chinua Achebe’s Things Fall Apart (1958)

Unravel the cultural complexities of Igbo society in colonial Nigeria through Things Fall Apart. Achebe’s poignant novel encapsulates the clash of cultures, bringing the rich ancestral customs to life, while chronicling the struggle and tragedy of one family caught between worlds.

  1. Gandhi Autobiography: The Story of My Experiments with Truth (1927)

Mahatma Gandhi’s autobiography is a testament to his quest for truth, teachings, and civil disobedience. As you scroll through the trials, tribulations, and triumphs of Gandhi, embark on the spiritual journey of righteousness and self-purification, understanding his pivotal role in India’s freedom struggle.

  1. The Stranger by Albert Camus (1942)

Enter into the nihilistic world of Albert Camus’ The Stranger. Paralyzed by the absurdity of life, Meursault, the protagonist, delivers a spare narrative that mirrors the post-World War context. A character study that resonates with existential themes, this book is a stepping stone to understanding the art in dissecting one’s soul.

  1. One Hundred Years of Solitude by Gabriel García Márquez (1967)

Travel across seven generations of the Buendia family across One Hundred Years of Solitude, the classic novel by Gabriel García Márquez. Scroll through time in this story that blends reality with magical realism, painting a vivid portrait of decades of struggle, love, and the inexorable passage of time.

  1. The Kite Runner by Khaled Hosseini (2003)

Karachi-born author Khaled Hosseini’s The Kite Runner narrates a poignant tale, intertwining the lives of two boys and the clash of cultures that occurs during the tumultuous events in Afghanistan. Dive into the rich backdrop of friendship, betrayal, and redemption, and feel the heartache and beauty of an ancient land.

In pouring over these books, we traverse the world’s most profound and ephemeral moments of ancient civilizations. From the anguished and rhythmic chants in The Iliad and The Odyssey to the foreboding experiences of Siddhartha’s Hindu India, scintillating myths and history in The Epic of Gilgamesh, and the brooding existential anxiety in The Stranger, this collection shines a light onto the sagas that continue to captivate human imagination.

Delve deep into the annals of history, feel the pulse of civilizations through the pulse of their best literature, and allow the boundless wisdom of the ancients to unfold anew.
