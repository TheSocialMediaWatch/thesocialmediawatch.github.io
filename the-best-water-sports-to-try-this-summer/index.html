The Best Water Sports to Try This Summer


Summer and water sports go hand in hand. Whether you’re seeking adrenaline-pumping action, serene exploration, or just a refreshing escape from the heat, the world’s waters offer countless opportunities to dive into fun and adventure. With endless lakes, rivers, seas, and oceans ready for exploration, choosing the best water sport can be as invigorating as the activity itself. This guide unveils some of the top water sports to try this summer, spanning the spectrum from thrilling rides to peaceful paddling.

1. Paddleboarding: The Ultimate Full-Body Workout on Water

Stand-up paddleboarding (SUP) has surged into popularity in recent years, and for good reason. It combines the relaxation of floating on water with the benefits of a full-body workout. Paddleboarding is accessible for beginners but still challenging for seasoned enthusiasts. You can glide across calm lakes, explore winding river paths, or ride gentle ocean waves.

The key to paddleboarding is balance and technique; with the right stance and steady paddling, you can cover significant distances or simply enjoy nature’s tranquility. Various paddleboards cater to different uses, from yoga boards designed to keep you stable during poses to inflatable boards perfect for traveling and storage.

Explore local waterways or book guided tours in well-known SUP destinations. It’s not just about physical activity — it’s about connecting with the water and scenery around you.

2. Surfing: Ride the Waves Like a Pro

Surfing remains a definitive summer water sport. The thrill of catching waves and riding them to shore offers an exhilarating feeling unmatched by many activities. While surfing has a steep learning curve, beginner-friendly spots and surfing schools make it easier to start.

Modern surfboards come in all shapes and sizes, fostering an adaptable approach based on skill level and wave conditions. For novices, foam boards provide stability and safety, while experienced surfers often prefer shortboards for maneuverability.

Besides physical strength and agility, surfing fosters a deep respect for the ocean’s mood and energy. As you progress, mastering wave reading and timing becomes a rewarding element of the experience.

Popular global surfing destinations include Hawaii, Australia’s Gold Coast, and California’s beaches, but even smaller coastal towns and artificial wave pools offer accessible surfing adventures.

3. Kayaking: Adventures for All Ages and Terrains

Kayaking is a versatile water sport perfect for solo travelers, couples, and families. From calm lakes to rushing rivers and coastal tours, kayaking lets you explore places unreachable by foot or boat.

There are two common types of kayaks: sit-on-top and sit-inside, each suited for different water conditions and preferences. Kayaking can be peaceful and introspective, allowing you to tone your upper body while observing wildlife, or it can turn daring during whitewater runs filled with twists and rapids.

Many regions have kayaking centers offering guided expeditions along scenic waterways. Sea kayaking is especially enjoyable in areas featuring cliffs, hidden caves, and marine life, while river kayaking can vary from gentle paddles to thrilling rapids aimed at adrenaline lovers.

4. Windsurfing: A Fusion of Sailing and Surfing

Windsurfing blends the dynamics of surfing with the technicality of sailing, powered by the wind rather than paddling. It requires balancing on a board while handling a sail to navigate through water with skill and finesse.

Windsurfing appeals to athletic individuals who enjoy a challenging learning curve. Once you master the basics—standing, steering, and controlling speed—you open doors to thrilling wave rides and powerful gust-driven glides across bays or seas.

Locations with steady breezes and open water make ideal windsurfing spots. Many coastal resorts feature windsurfing schools and rental equipment, making this sport accessible and a great way to enjoy a summer waterfront holiday.

5. Jet Skiing: Speed and Fun on Water

For those craving speed and muscle-powered excitement, jet skiing delivers pure adrenaline. Navigating a jet ski involves commanding a powerful motorized watercraft that zooms across lakes, rivers, or coastal waters.

Jet skis are intuitive to use and accessible even for beginners after a quick run-through of safety procedures. This sport combines agility, control, and power, with options for solo rides or helping a friend cruise side-by-side.

Many waterfront resorts and marinas rent jet skis by the hour, allowing visitors to explore vast water landscapes at exhilarating speeds. For added fun, some riders participate in watercraft races or explore challenging hydro terrain.

6. Snorkeling and Scuba Diving: Diving into the Underwater World

If your idea of fun dives below the water surface, consider snorkeling and scuba diving. Both provide immersive experiences in aquatic environments teeming with vibrant marine life and stunning coral reefs.

Snorkeling is great for beginners and families. You float on the surface, peering down through clear waters with a mask and snorkel tube. Simply glide over reefs and observe colorful fish, turtles, and underwater plants without complex gear or training.

Scuba diving involves specialized equipment that lets you breathe underwater, allowing much deeper exploration. Scuba certifications involve courses to master safety protocols and underwater navigation. Whether you explore shipwrecks, caves, or deep reefs, diving offers unparalleled access to a serene and mysterious world.

Tropical destinations such as the Great Barrier Reef, the Maldives, or the Red Sea are among the best places for spectacular underwater discovery.

7. Wakeboarding and Water Skiing: High-Speed Surface Thrills

Both wakeboarding and water skiing use motorboats towing the rider at high speed over water surfaces. These sports offer exhilarating rides boosted by jumps, spins, and tricks when mastery improves.

Water skiing typically uses two skis and focuses more on balance and smooth motion, whereas wakeboarding involves a single board similar to a snowboard, facilitating more aerial tricks.

Ideal for thrill seekers and those with moderate water confidence, these activities often require some initial lessons but provide immense adrenaline rushes.

Summer ski lakes and recreational centers usually rent equipment and offer coaching, making it easier for newcomers to try these dynamic sports.

8. Kiteboarding: Soaring Across Water and Air

Kiteboarding or kitesurfing is one of the most spectacular summer water sports combining wind power and board skills. Using a large controllable kite to harness wind energy, you glide, jump, and even perform aerial tricks across the water.

This sport demands coordination, understanding of wind conditions, and balance, especially when learning to launch and steer the kite. Professional lessons are recommended to ensure safety and proper technique from the start.

Once you build confidence, kiteboarding can be addictive, offering speed, sensation, and a unique connection to wind and water.

Some of the top kiteboarding spots worldwide include Tarifa in Spain, Maui in Hawaii, and Cape Town in South Africa.

9. Canoeing: Relaxed Exploration or Intense Paddling

Canoeing is a classic water sport perfect for any skill level. Whether you want a contemplative drift through quiet waters or an intense paddle downstream, canoeing adapts to your mood and environment.

Open canoes accommodate multiple paddlers, making it ideal for family outings or group adventures. Lightweight and easy to carry, canoes grant access to remote ponds, winding rivers, and coastal inlets.

It’s an excellent way to connect with nature on water without requiring advanced skills or equipment.

10. Bodyboarding: Simple Waves, Maximum Fun

Bodyboarding offers a straightforward way to experience wave riding without the complexity of balancing on a tall board. Lying prone or on your knees, riders glide on smaller waves, often in beach shore breaks.

Bodyboarding is beginner-friendly, affordable, and effective for learning water safety and wave reading.

For those who enjoy beach culture and quick thrills with minimal gear, this sport delivers great summer fun.

Conclusion

No matter what kind of adventure you seek or your skill level, this summer’s waters are rife with opportunity. From the serene paddleboarding on glassy lakes to the high-octane sprint of jet skiing and the spectacular aerials of kiteboarding, the best water sports encourage exploration, fitness, and joyous play under the sun.

Trying something new on the water not only cools you off from the heat but also refreshes your spirit with unique challenges and beautiful landscapes. So pack your swimwear, sunscreen, and enthusiasm, and dive into a summer filled with splash-worthy memories!

Tips for Enjoying Water Sports Safely and Responsibly

  • Always wear appropriate safety gear such as life jackets and helmets where required.
  • Take lessons from certified instructors when trying a new sport.
  • Check local weather and water conditions to avoid hazards.
  • Respect local wildlife and habitats while enjoying nature.
  • Stay hydrated and protect your skin with waterproof sunscreen.

Embracing these practices enhances enjoyment and safety, helping you make the most of your aquatic adventures all summer long.
