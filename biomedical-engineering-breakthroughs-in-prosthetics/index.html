Biomedical Engineering Breakthroughs in Prosthetics


In recent years, biomedical engineering has taken a significant leap forward in developing advanced prosthetics for amputees. The field continues to grow and amaze as researchers push the limits of what is possible in the world of limb restoration. Here, we explore a few cutting-edge breakthroughs in prosthetics, with stories of innovation and progress.

##õ MultiModal Limb Imaging

Firstly, we observe the incredible tool known as MultiModal Limb Imaging. This technology has revolutionized the way prosthetics are designed and created. By gathering data on a patient’s residual limb in multiple ways - including CT scans, MRI, ultrasound, and more - biomedical engineers can generate a digital representation that is incredibly accurate. This approach ensures that prostheses fit exceptionally well, providing amputees with a perfect fit with minimal need for adjustments. One company taking this technology to the next level is Bridgelux, who have developed an AI-assisted method for understanding the geometry of the residual limb, speeding up the design process.

##õ Myoelectric Control Systems

Myoelectric control systems have made life much easier for many amputees. These prosthetic limbs use electrical signals from the muscles in the residual limb to control their various parts. This breakthrough has advanced the experience and functionality of many prosthetics, making them more responsive to the user’s movements. A company called Ottobock leads the industry with their advanced myoelectric prosthetic technologies, providing solutions for several types of amputations, from arm to Leg, thigh to knee.

##õ Fabrication Technologies

Another key development has come in the form of new fabrication technologies. Researchers have made a significant breakthrough with the use of 3D printing technology for prosthetics. With the ability to create custom designs, resolutions, and material selections, users have benefited from both better fitting and more functional artificial limbs. This technology also allows for customization, providing prosthetics that meet specific individual needs. One visionary company, Unlimited, uses 3D printing to share designs globally and has been incredibly successful. Their offering comprises low-cost, high-functioning prosthetics designed for people in developing countries.

##õ Smart Prosthetics

Lastly, smart prosthetics have drawn attention for their ability to react to the user’s needs and environment, offering better control and an increased level of integration, as opposed to traditional prosthetics, which have limited responsiveness. Numerous companies are now doing pioneering work in this area, including Open Bionics, who leads the way with their choice of artificial intelligence-powered limbs.

Conclusion: The Future of Prosthetic Breakthroughs

Biomedical engineering breakthroughs in prosthetics have made a profound impact on the lives of many amputees worldwide. As we continue to progress, we can certainly expect even more significant advancements in the field of prosthetic design, allowing those with limb loss to enjoy a higher quality of life, more freedom, and greater independence.
