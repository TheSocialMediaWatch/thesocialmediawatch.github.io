Why Every Fantasy Writer Should Study History


Fantasy writing often takes readers into magical worlds filled with mythical creatures, heroic quests, and epic battles. It’s a genre where imagination runs wild, and anything is possible. But as a writer, how do you create a world that feels authentic, immersive, and compelling? One of the most powerful tools at your disposal is history. By studying the past, fantasy writers can build richer worlds, develop more believable characters, and craft more engaging plots.

The Foundation of Worldbuilding: Learning from the Past

When creating a fantasy world, the first challenge is always worldbuilding. Fantasy writers are tasked with designing entire realms, societies, and cultures from scratch. This can be an overwhelming task, but history offers a valuable guide.

Historical events, civilizations, and cultures can serve as templates or sources of inspiration for your fictional worlds. From the rise and fall of empires to the intricacies of medieval feudal systems, history provides a wealth of material to draw from. By studying the past, writers can design more nuanced and realistic settings for their stories. Whether it’s the social structure of an ancient civilization or the technological advancements of the industrial revolution, these historical elements can enhance the depth and complexity of your world.

For instance, the political intrigues of the Byzantine Empire, with its elaborate court conspiracies and religious conflicts, can inspire a similar system in a fantasy world. Similarly, the ancient Roman Empire, known for its engineering feats and military discipline, could serve as the basis for a powerful and advanced civilization in a fantasy setting.

Developing Realistic Political Systems and Social Structures

Fantasy worlds often feature complex political systems and social hierarchies. These systems shape the lives of the characters, influence the course of events, and define the power dynamics within the story. History is a goldmine for ideas when creating these systems.

Take the feudal system of medieval Europe, for example. The relationship between lords and vassals, the role of the church, and the constant struggle for power are themes that can be easily adapted to a fantasy world. By studying the way real-world societies have evolved and interacted, writers can create more believable political systems that feel lived-in and functional.

The rise and fall of monarchies, revolutions, and the establishment of democracies provide further inspiration for political plots. A fantasy novel set in a world where a long-standing monarchy is overthrown in favor of a new republic can mirror the events of the French Revolution or the fall of the Roman Republic. Historical examples give writers insight into how such events unfold, the forces at play, and the consequences for those involved.

Crafting Complex Characters

In fantasy stories, characters are often the heart of the narrative. They are the ones who experience the highs and lows of the plot, and their personal journeys shape the outcome of the story. To create compelling, multidimensional characters, writers need to understand how people behave in different situations. This is where history comes in.

Throughout history, there have been countless figures who have shaped the course of events, from ruthless conquerors to visionary leaders, and from scholars to revolutionaries. By studying the lives of these historical figures, writers can gain valuable insights into human behavior, motivations, and the complexities of personal and political power.

Consider the character of a king or queen in your story. You could base their character on a historical figure like Queen Elizabeth I, whose intelligence, political acumen, and diplomatic skills helped shape the course of English history. Or perhaps your character is a conqueror, inspired by figures like Alexander the Great or Genghis Khan, who built vast empires through strategic brilliance and sheer force of will.

By studying the histories of these individuals, writers can create characters who feel authentic and grounded in real human experiences. A character’s ambitions, fears, and actions should be shaped by the historical context of their world, making them more relatable and engaging to the reader.

Creating Compelling Conflicts and Plots

Every good story needs conflict, and fantasy stories are no exception. History is a treasure trove of conflicts—wars, revolutions, betrayals, and struggles for power. These events provide writers with a wealth of material for crafting dramatic, high-stakes plots.

For instance, consider the impact of the Hundred Years’ War on the medieval world. The tension between France and England during this period led to iconic battles, such as the Battle of Agincourt, and created larger-than-life figures like Joan of Arc. A writer can draw from the political and military intricacies of this war to create their own conflict between two nations or factions vying for dominance in a fantasy world.

Similarly, the rise of the Mongol Empire under Genghis Khan offers numerous plot possibilities. The Mongols were known for their ruthless conquests and innovative military strategies, which can inspire a fantasy story where an empire expands at an unstoppable pace, threatening the stability of the world.

Furthermore, history is filled with personal conflicts that make for gripping storytelling. The tensions between historical figures, the betrayals within royal courts, and the rivalries between powerful families all provide inspiration for interpersonal drama and conflict. By drawing from real historical struggles, writers can create more compelling plots with layers of intrigue, moral dilemmas, and high emotional stakes.

The Importance of Understanding Technology and Innovation

In many fantasy stories, technology plays a key role in shaping the world. Whether it’s magic, weapons, or machines, the way technology develops and impacts society is a crucial aspect of worldbuilding. Understanding how technological innovations have impacted history can help writers imagine new possibilities for their fantasy worlds.

For example, the Industrial Revolution brought about sweeping changes in society, from the rise of factories and mass production to the development of steam engines and mechanized transportation. These innovations transformed economies, societies, and cultures, creating both opportunities and challenges. In a fantasy world, similar advancements—whether in the form of magical inventions or new magical techniques—could have a profound effect on the world and its people.

By studying the history of technological progress, writers can explore the implications of new discoveries and innovations. How does the invention of a new weapon change the balance of power between factions? How does the development of new magical techniques impact the social structure of the world? These are questions that can be answered by examining how technology has shaped human history.

Drawing Parallels Between History and Fantasy

One of the most powerful aspects of fantasy literature is its ability to mirror real-world struggles and themes. Fantasy stories often explore universal concepts such as good versus evil, freedom versus oppression, and the hero’s journey. By drawing on historical events and figures, writers can create stories that resonate with readers on a deeper level.

History provides countless examples of people fighting for freedom, standing up against tyranny, or making sacrifices for the greater good. These themes are at the heart of many fantasy narratives, and by grounding them in historical events, writers can create stories that feel timeless and relevant.

For example, the story of the American Revolution, with its struggle for independence and the fight against a tyrannical monarchy, can inspire a fantasy narrative where a group of rebels rises up against an oppressive ruler. Similarly, the stories of resistance movements throughout history—whether it’s the French Resistance during World War II or the Underground Railroad in the United States—can serve as a basis for creating compelling stories of rebellion and resistance in a fantasy world.

The Psychological Benefits of Studying History

Beyond the practical applications for worldbuilding, character development, and plot crafting, studying history can have a profound impact on a fantasy writer’s creativity. History helps writers to understand the complexities of human nature, the way societies function, and the forces that shape the course of events. It provides a framework for thinking about the world and offers new ways to approach storytelling.

History also challenges writers to think critically about the world around them. By examining the successes and failures of past civilizations, writers can learn important lessons about leadership, governance, and human interaction. This knowledge can help writers create more realistic and thought-provoking narratives, filled with characters and situations that feel grounded in real human experiences.

Conclusion

In the world of fantasy writing, history is more than just a collection of past events. It is a wellspring of inspiration, a guide for worldbuilding, and a tool for creating complex characters and engaging plots. By studying history, fantasy writers can enrich their stories, making them feel more authentic, relatable, and timeless. Whether it’s drawing on the political intrigues of past civilizations, creating characters inspired by historical figures, or crafting plots that echo the struggles of the past, history offers endless possibilities for fantasy writers to explore. So, if you’re a fantasy writer looking to take your storytelling to the next level, dive into the past—it’s a treasure trove waiting to be discovered.
