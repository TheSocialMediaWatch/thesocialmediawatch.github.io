The Truth About Ouija Boards


Ouija boards, also known as “spirit” or “talking” boards, have fascinated and terrified people for over a century. These boards, which consist of a flat wooden or plastic board with the letters of the alphabet, the numbers 0-9, and the words ‘yes’, ’no’, and ‘goodbye’, are used to communicate with the spirits of the deceased. The truth behind these enigmatic boards is both intriguing and complex.

Origin of the Ouija Board

The Ouija board’s origins date back to the mid-19th century when the spiritualism movement was rampant in America. However, the first recorded use of a talking board occurred much earlier, in the 1600s. German monks used a similar device called a “witching board” to contact spirits. The modern Ouija board we know today was officially trademarked in 1966 by William Fuld.

How Does a Ouija Board Work?

A typical Ouija board session involves a group of people, often friends or family members, who gather around the board to communicate with the spirits. The players place their fingers on the two planchettes, heart-shaped or triangular wooden pieces that glide across the board. As they ask questions, the planchette supposedly moves to spell out answers or provide messages.

However, skeptics argue that the movement of the planchette is due to subtle clues, imposters, or even the power of suggestion. Scientists have explained this phenomenon as a form of ideomotor response, where unconscious muscle movements cause the planchette to move.

Why do People Use Ouija Boards?

The primary rationale behind using a Ouija board is to connect with the spirits of deceased loved ones. Some may believe that they can receive guidance, closure, or reassurance through communication with the dead. Others are drawn to the board out of curiosity or fascination with the supernatural.

However, despite numerous stories of ghostly encounters and communication with loved ones, there is no scientific evidence that the Ouija board can genuinely connect with the afterlife.

Should You Use a Ouija Board?

Using a Ouija board is a deeply personal choice. Proponents argue that it can be an insightful, healing, and spiritually enlightening experience. On the other hand, critics warn of the risks associated with opening a doorway to the unknown.

It is essential to approach any spiritual practice, including Ouija boards, with respect, skepticism, and an open mind. Those who are genuinely searching for closure or communication with the deceased should tread carefully and consider seeking support from mental health professionals rather than relying on supernatural methods.

In conclusion, although the origin and operation of Ouija boards are intriguing, there remains no concrete evidence of their ability to communicate with the afterlife. As with any spiritual practice, the personal experience and outcome will vary greatly. It is crucial to weigh the potential risks and benefits before deciding to use a Ouija board. Remember, while questing for ultimate truths, be mindful of what lies within our own minds.
