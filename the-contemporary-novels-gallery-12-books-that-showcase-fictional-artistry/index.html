The Contemporary Novel's Gallery: 12 Books that showcase Fictional Artistry


Art has always intrigued us, and its presence in literature is no exception. The intricate worlds of fictional artistry have the ability to captivate our hearts and minds, taking us on a journey filled with exquisiteness and profound emotions. To skim through the contemporary novel’s gallery and appreciate these gemstones carefully placed in the fictional canvas, here are 12 novels that showcase remarkable artistry, their sumptuous storytelling combining the realms of aesthetics and literature to deliver a rich reading experience.

  1. “Bonjour Tristesse” by Françoise Sagan

Immerse yourself in the world of youthful rebellion and summer escapades, where the protagonist’s attraction to a person’s aura of emptiness and indifference is inspired by an inclination towards art. The book eloquently explores themes of rebellion, philosophy, and aesthetics through its enigmatic characters and wistful prose.

  1. “A Visit from the Goon Squad” by Jennifer Egan

Egan constructs a tapestry of portraiture with her book, taking readers on a journey across time. Throughout the narrative, she unravels the evolution of burgeoning artists amidst a tangled web of relationships and career struggles. With an eclectic mix of text and image, her novel not only showcases an unconventional approach to storytelling but also effectively conveys the vital role of creativity and imagination in individual human experiences.

  1. “The Night Circus” by Erin Morgenstern

The enchanting beauty of Le Cirque des Rêves is certainly worth savoring. An enchanting world of magic and mystery, where the darkness of night reveals its hidden treasures. The delicate balance of emotional undercurrents, combined with a visual spectacle of tidal waves of prose, creates an unforgettableReading experience that will leave you mesmerized.

  1. “The Paris Architect” by Charles Belfoure

Set against the backdrop of World War II, this novel showcases the artist’s crucial and hidden role in the French resistance. Following the life of a Parisian architect, the narrative delves into the tribulations of defying the Nazis, the moral dilemmas of deceit, and the question of morality - a combination of suspense, intrigue, and genuine aesthetics that unveils a fascinating world of architectural intrigue.

  1. “Zadie Smith’s NW”

This novel presents a raw take on the hypermodern ethic of existence, where the characters’ stories collide in diverse ways. It provides a powerful narrative that not only communicates the essence of the characters, their fears, and ambitions but also immerses readers in a vibrant and contemporary display of life in present-day London.

  1. “The Brief Wondrous Life of Oscar Wao” by Junot Díaz

Embark on an enchanting journey that unfolds the life of Oscar, a naive and endearing lover of science fiction, through the vivid imagery and lush language overshadowed by treads of history and magical realism. Delve into the kaleidoscope of literary devices, where the protagonist’s love for comics, expansive knowledge of Poe’s tales, and intense fascination with Super Mario Bros illuminate his emotional and spiritual growth in an unforgettable saga.

  1. “Extremely Loud & Incredibly Close” by Jonathan Safran Foer

In this poignant story of grief and healing, the protagonist’s fascination with images, broken codes, and an array of symbols scrupulously crafts a symbolic mosaic. The author’s intricate exploration of the edifice of memory and how a seemingly unconnected world can intersect in moments of loss is poignantly presented through artful storytelling, leaving you awestruck.

  1. “The Secret River” by Kate Grenville

This winner of the Commonwealth Writers Prize unearths the complex story of colonialism and identity formation in Australia, entwined beautifully with love, contestation, and the intricate structures of ownership. The novel brilliantly coveys the emotion of the terrain and its people, as the reader journeys within the heart of a nation.

  1. “The Remains of the Day” by Kazuo Ishiguro

Elevated to new heights, this narrative elegantly weaves the story of Stevens, a butler whose impeccable profession promises a semblance of stability. However, as the plot unfolds, readers encounter a wealth of emotions and masterful reflections that permeate this compelling bildungsroman, solidifying its timeless status within literature.

  1. “The Goldfinch” by Donna Tartt

An undeniably intricate and riveting narrative of one boy’s incredible journey, this novel embraces themes of art, loss, and the human condition. In addition to its powerful storytelling, Tartt’s intricate artistic and cultural references enrich the readers’ experience, further elevating the expectations for her forthcoming novel.

  1. “The Unchangeable Spark of Divinity: A Novel in Stories” by Katherine A. Powers

Shimmering with life and warmth, this collection of interrelated stories beautifully blends ordinary characters, extraordinary love, and a keen understanding of human relations. The thematic threads of creativity, courage, and individuality remark the resonating significance of art with pure sincerity and grace.

  1. “Fates and Furies” by Lauren Groff

Drawing readers into the intricately woven tale of married couple, Lotto and Mathilde, this novel exquisitely navigates the thin line between love and deception. The novel masterfully captures the vivacity of artistic genius alongside the passionate intricacies of life, love, and humanity in its many-raised lines and heartfelt prose.

These gripping and imaginative novels encapsulate the fascinating artistry of contemporary storytelling. The myriad techniques employed by authors in their written works present the literary artistry and creative spirit in unexpected and ingenious ways. Whether it’s mapping out intricate details, skillful character development, or weaving an enchanting tale, exploring these novels through the lens of artistry provides an illuminating view towards our shared passion for storytelling.
