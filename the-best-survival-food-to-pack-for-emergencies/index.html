The Best Survival Food to Pack for Emergencies


When disaster strikes, whether it’s a natural calamity, an unexpected power outage, or any other emergency situation, one of the most crucial things to consider is your survival food supply. In these challenging times, having access to high-quality, nutritious, and long-lasting food can make a world of difference. The right survival food can keep you nourished and energized until you’re able to return to normalcy.

The key to selecting the best survival food lies in balancing shelf life, ease of preparation, nutritional value, and the ability to withstand a range of environmental conditions. This guide will walk you through the best survival food options to pack for any emergency.

Understanding the Criteria for Survival Food

Before diving into the specific types of survival food, it’s essential to understand the factors that make some foods better suited for emergencies than others. Here’s what you should keep in mind when selecting your survival food:

  • Shelf Life: In an emergency, you need food that can stay edible for an extended period without refrigeration or freezing. Foods with a long shelf life are essential, as they will still be good when you need them.

  • Nutritional Value: Emergency foods need to provide the necessary vitamins, minerals, and macronutrients (proteins, fats, carbohydrates) to keep your body functioning well during a stressful situation.

  • Ease of Preparation: During emergencies, you may not have access to kitchen appliances or even clean water. Opt for foods that require minimal preparation, such as those that only need boiling water or that are ready to eat.

  • Compact and Lightweight: Survival food should be easy to pack and carry, especially if you need to evacuate or travel for long periods.

  • Taste: While taste might not be the first thing you think about in an emergency, having food that’s pleasant to eat can help maintain morale. Nobody wants to eat bland, unappetizing food when they’re already stressed.

Now, let’s explore the best survival food options to include in your emergency pack.

1. Freeze-Dried Meals

Why Freeze-Dried Meals are Essential

Freeze-dried meals are a popular choice for emergency preparedness because of their long shelf life and ease of preparation. They are lightweight, compact, and often come in ready-to-eat pouches or cans. Simply add hot water, and you’ll have a hot meal within minutes.

These meals typically offer a variety of options, from hearty soups and stews to pasta dishes and curries. They can pack a lot of calories and nutrients into a small, portable package, which makes them ideal for emergencies where food resources are limited.

Top Freeze-Dried Meals to Consider

  • Mountain House Classic Bucket: A well-known brand in the survival food industry, Mountain House offers a range of freeze-dried meals with up to 30 years of shelf life. Their meals, such as Beef Stroganoff and Chicken Teriyaki, provide the perfect balance of taste and nutrition.

  • Backpacker’s Pantry: This brand provides a selection of organic, non-GMO freeze-dried meals that are both nutritious and delicious. Their dishes, like Pad Thai and Chicken Curry, are perfect for adding variety to your emergency food supply.

  • Wise Company: Wise Company’s meals come in resealable, compact pouches, making them easy to store and transport. With meals like Chili Mac and Pasta Primavera, they offer great options for different tastes and dietary preferences.

2. Canned Foods

Why Canned Foods Are Reliable

Canned foods are a great option for emergency food storage due to their long shelf life and ability to withstand various temperature conditions. These foods are pre-cooked, meaning they can be eaten cold in a pinch or heated for a more satisfying meal. Many canned foods are high in protein, making them an excellent choice to maintain your energy levels in stressful situations.

Best Canned Foods for Emergencies

  • Canned Meats: Tuna, chicken, and salmon are some of the best canned meats to pack. They’re high in protein and easy to open with a can opener. Brands like Starkist and Bumble Bee offer convenient single-serve packages that are ideal for emergencies.

  • Canned Vegetables and Fruits: Canned vegetables and fruits provide necessary vitamins and minerals to keep your body healthy. These foods can be eaten on their own or mixed into other meals. Opt for low-sodium options to ensure you’re maintaining a balanced diet.

  • Canned Soups and Stews: Pre-made canned soups and stews are a quick and easy option for emergency food. They typically contain a variety of vegetables, proteins, and carbohydrates, offering a complete meal in one can.

3. MREs (Meals Ready to Eat)

Why MREs Are a Top Choice for Survival

MREs are designed to provide a complete, balanced meal that requires minimal preparation. Originally developed for the military, these meals are now widely used by preppers and survivalists. MREs come in vacuum-sealed pouches that include an entrée, side dish, dessert, and even a beverage. They are calorie-dense, easy to store, and have a shelf life of up to 5 years or more, depending on the brand and packaging.

Popular MRE Brands to Consider

  • The Ready Store MREs: The Ready Store offers a wide variety of MRE options that are perfect for emergency preparedness. They provide meals like Chili Mac, Beef Stew, and Pasta with Marinara Sauce. These meals are packaged for long-term storage and are designed to withstand extreme conditions.

  • Sopakco MREs: Sopakco’s MREs are widely recognized for their durability and reliability. They offer well-balanced meals that include proteins, carbs, and fats, ensuring that you stay energized during a crisis.

  • MRE Star: This brand provides high-quality MREs with a wide range of meal options, including vegetarian and gluten-free choices. MRE Star meals are known for their high caloric content, making them perfect for keeping your energy up.

4. Energy Bars

Why Energy Bars Are Essential

Energy bars are another staple of survival food. These compact, nutrient-dense bars are ideal for providing quick energy and are easy to store and transport. Whether you’re facing a natural disaster, going on a long hike, or simply need a quick snack, energy bars can provide the sustenance you need to keep going.

Best Energy Bars for Emergency Kits

  • Clif Bars: Clif Bars are a favorite among hikers and survivalists. They offer a variety of flavors and provide a good balance of carbohydrates, protein, and fat. Their long shelf life makes them a great addition to any emergency food supply.

  • RXBARs: Known for their minimal ingredients, RXBARs provide a protein-packed snack made from real food ingredients like egg whites, nuts, and fruit. These bars are a great option for those looking for a more natural energy boost.

  • PowerBars: PowerBars are another popular choice for survival food. They’re designed to provide a quick burst of energy and are available in a variety of flavors and nutritional profiles. They also have a long shelf life, making them perfect for emergency preparedness.

5. Dried Fruits and Nuts

Why Dried Fruits and Nuts Are a Smart Choice

Dried fruits and nuts are excellent sources of essential vitamins, minerals, and healthy fats. These foods are lightweight, compact, and have a long shelf life. They’re also calorie-dense, meaning you don’t need to pack as much to get the necessary nutrients. Dried fruits, such as raisins, apricots, and apples, provide a natural source of energy, while nuts like almonds, walnuts, and cashews offer protein and healthy fats.

Top Dried Fruits and Nuts to Pack

  • Almonds and Cashews: These nuts are packed with healthy fats and protein, making them a great choice for keeping your energy up during an emergency. They’re also a good source of magnesium, which can help reduce stress and improve sleep.

  • Dried Apricots and Apples: Dried fruits are a great way to get essential vitamins like vitamin A and C. They’re easy to snack on and can be eaten with nuts for a balanced meal.

  • Trail Mix: Trail mix, which combines dried fruits, nuts, and sometimes chocolate or seeds, is a convenient, calorie-dense option. It’s a great way to enjoy a variety of nutrients in one snack.

6. Powdered Drink Mixes

Why Powdered Drink Mixes Are Useful

During an emergency, staying hydrated is just as important as eating. However, water may not always be available in large quantities, and plain water can become monotonous. Powdered drink mixes like powdered electrolyte drinks, instant coffee, or powdered tea can help keep you hydrated and provide a much-needed morale boost.

Recommended Drink Mixes

  • Electrolyte Powders: Electrolyte powders like Nuun or Emergen-C can help replenish vital minerals like potassium, sodium, and magnesium, which are essential for hydration and proper muscle function.

  • Instant Coffee: For coffee lovers, having a stash of instant coffee or coffee sachets can be a great way to maintain a sense of normalcy during a stressful situation. Plus, the caffeine can help keep you alert.

  • Powdered Tea: Powdered tea, including green tea and black tea, can be a comforting and hydrating option. It’s lightweight and easy to prepare, making it a great addition to your emergency kit.

Conclusion

When it comes to emergency preparedness, selecting the right survival food is crucial to ensure you stay nourished and energized during a crisis. Freeze-dried meals, canned foods, MREs, energy bars, dried fruits, and nuts all offer the convenience, long shelf life, and nutritional content needed to survive in difficult conditions. By packing a variety of these foods, you can ensure that you’re well-equipped to handle any emergency situation that comes your way.

Remember to consider your dietary preferences, specific needs, and storage space when choosing your survival food. The more prepared you are, the better you’ll be able to handle whatever comes your way.
