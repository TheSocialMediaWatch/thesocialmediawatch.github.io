How to Make the Perfect Mashed Potatoes from Scratch


There’s something universally comforting about mashed potatoes. Whether as the star of a holiday feast or a simple weeknight dinner, mashed potatoes have a way of bringing people together around the table. While it may seem like a straightforward dish, achieving the perfect mashed potatoes from scratch requires a little care and technique. This guide will walk you through everything you need to know to create smooth, creamy, and flavorful mashed potatoes every time.

Choosing the Right Potatoes

The foundation of great mashed potatoes starts with the right type of potato. Not all potatoes are created equal, and choosing the right variety can make a world of difference in texture and flavor.

Russet Potatoes

Russets are the most commonly used potato for mashed potatoes. Known for their starchy flesh, they break down easily when boiled, resulting in a fluffy and smooth consistency. They are the go-to choice for traditional mashed potatoes.

Yukon Gold Potatoes

Yukon Golds are another excellent choice. These potatoes have a creamy, waxy texture that helps create mashed potatoes with a denser, richer consistency. They are ideal if you prefer a slightly creamier mash with a bit more flavor.

Red Potatoes

Red potatoes are less starchy and more waxy than Russets, which gives mashed potatoes made from them a slightly chunkier, more textured result. They are great for mashed potatoes that have more body and bite, often used in rustic-style preparations.

For the smoothest mashed potatoes, we recommend Russets, but if you’re looking for a more buttery flavor, try using Yukon Gold potatoes.

Preparing the Potatoes

Once you’ve chosen your potatoes, it’s time to prepare them for cooking. Proper preparation can make the difference between smooth mashed potatoes and a lumpy disaster.

Peeling the Potatoes

Start by peeling your potatoes. While some people prefer to leave the skins on for a more rustic mash, peeling them will give you a smoother, more refined texture. Use a vegetable peeler to remove the skins, then rinse the potatoes under cold water to remove any remaining dirt or debris.

Cutting the Potatoes

After peeling, cut your potatoes into even chunks, about 1 to 1.5 inches in size. This helps them cook more evenly, ensuring that they all become tender at the same time. If you’re using larger potatoes, it’s especially important to cut them into uniform pieces.

Rinsing the Potatoes

Before boiling, give your potato chunks a quick rinse under cold water. This helps remove excess starch, which can cause the potatoes to become gluey when mashed. A quick rinse will help ensure the best possible texture.

Boiling the Potatoes

The next step is boiling the potatoes, and this process requires a little attention to detail. Getting the cooking time just right is essential for achieving the perfect consistency.

Starting with Cold Water

Place the cut potatoes in a large pot and cover them with cold water. Starting with cold water allows the potatoes to cook evenly, preventing them from becoming overcooked on the outside while undercooked on the inside.

Adding Salt

Add a generous pinch of salt to the water. This step not only seasons the potatoes but also helps them cook more evenly. The salt will infuse the potatoes with flavor from the inside out.

Boiling the Potatoes

Bring the pot of water to a boil over medium-high heat. Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium and simmer the potatoes for about 10-15 minutes, or until they are fork-tender. You can check this by inserting a fork or knife into a potato piece—if it slides in easily, the potatoes are done.

It’s important not to overcook the potatoes. If they cook for too long, they can become waterlogged, which will affect the texture of your mashed potatoes.

Draining the Potatoes

Once the potatoes are tender, drain them in a colander. Allow the potatoes to sit for a minute to ensure any excess water has drained off. Excess water can make your mashed potatoes too thin or watery, so it’s crucial to remove it.

Mashing the Potatoes

Now comes the fun part—mashing! There are several tools you can use for this, and the choice of tool can have a significant impact on the texture of your mashed potatoes.

Tools for Mashing

  • Potato Masher: A simple and effective tool, the potato masher gives you control over the texture of your mashed potatoes. It’s perfect for creating mashed potatoes with a bit of texture, rather than an ultra-smooth finish.
  • Potato Ricer: For ultra-smooth mashed potatoes, a potato ricer is an excellent choice. This tool forces the potatoes through small holes, creating a fine, velvety texture.
  • Hand Mixer or Stand Mixer: If you’re looking for a fluffy, light mash, a hand mixer or stand mixer works wonders. The beaters will incorporate air into the potatoes, resulting in a light, airy texture.

Mashing Process

Start by mashing the potatoes while they’re still hot. Begin with a few gentle presses using your tool of choice. For a smoother mash, use the potato ricer or stand mixer. For a chunkier, rustic mash, the potato masher will give you the perfect texture.

As you mash, keep the potatoes moving, breaking them up evenly and incorporating the air for a smooth result.

Adding the Good Stuff

Once your potatoes are mashed, it’s time to add the ingredients that bring the flavor and creaminess to life. Here’s where you can get creative with butter, cream, milk, and other add-ins.

Butter

Butter is essential for the rich, creamy flavor of mashed potatoes. Add about 1/2 cup of unsalted butter for every pound of potatoes. Melt the butter before adding it to the potatoes for the best consistency. You can adjust the amount depending on your taste preference.

Cream or Milk

Next, add cream or milk to the mashed potatoes. For ultra-creamy potatoes, use heavy cream. For a slightly lighter version, use whole milk. Start by adding a little at a time and stirring until you reach your desired consistency. A combination of both milk and cream can be used for a perfect balance of richness and creaminess.

Seasoning

Don’t forget to season your mashed potatoes. Taste them and adjust the seasoning with salt and pepper. A pinch of garlic powder or fresh minced garlic can also be added for extra flavor. Some people like to stir in fresh herbs, like chives or parsley, for a burst of color and freshness.

Cheese (Optional)

For a more indulgent version, consider adding grated cheese. Cheddar is a popular choice, but you can also use Parmesan, Gruyère, or even cream cheese for a different flavor. Add the cheese while the potatoes are still hot so it melts into the mash.

Tips for Perfect Mashed Potatoes Every Time

  • Don’t Overwork the Potatoes: Over-mashing or beating potatoes too much can lead to a gummy texture. Mash just until you reach the desired consistency.
  • Heat Your Dairy: Always heat your butter, cream, or milk before adding it to the potatoes. Cold dairy can cause the potatoes to seize up, making them difficult to mash smoothly.
  • Use a Sturdy Pot: When boiling your potatoes, use a large pot with thick sides to ensure even cooking.
  • Season Throughout: Taste and adjust seasoning throughout the process, not just at the end. This ensures the flavors are balanced.

Storing Leftover Mashed Potatoes

If you happen to have leftovers (although that rarely happens), storing mashed potatoes properly is important to keep them tasting fresh. Allow the mashed potatoes to cool to room temperature before transferring them to an airtight container. They can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. To reheat, you can use the microwave or heat them gently on the stovetop, adding a bit more butter or cream to bring them back to life.

Conclusion

Mastering the art of mashed potatoes from scratch doesn’t have to be complicated. With the right potatoes, proper boiling technique, and the right balance of creamy ingredients, you can make mashed potatoes that are smooth, rich, and absolutely irresistible. Whether you prefer them fluffy or creamy, classic or with a twist, these mashed potatoes will never disappoint. So, roll up your sleeves, gather your ingredients, and get ready to create the ultimate comfort food.
