The Best Fashion Documentaries to Watch for Inspiration


Fashion is not just about clothing—it’s an art form, an expression of culture, and an ever-evolving industry. The world of fashion is filled with creativity, innovation, and inspiring individuals who shape trends and influence the way we dress. One of the best ways to dive into the world of fashion is through documentaries. These films provide an inside look at the lives of designers, the history of fashion, and the challenges faced by those at the forefront of the industry. Whether you’re a budding designer, a fashion enthusiast, or someone looking for inspiration, fashion documentaries offer a wealth of insights and creative energy.

In this post, we’ll take you through some of the best fashion documentaries to watch. These films not only showcase the glitz and glamour of the fashion world but also highlight the struggles, triumphs, and personal stories behind the brands and icons that have shaped fashion today.

1. The September Issue (2009)

“The September Issue” is a must-watch for anyone interested in the inner workings of the fashion industry. Directed by R.J. Cutler, this documentary takes viewers behind the scenes at Vogue magazine during the production of its 2007 September issue, which was the largest in the magazine’s history. The film follows Vogue editor-in-chief Anna Wintour, known for her cold demeanor and impeccable taste, as she and her team work to put together the highly anticipated issue.

This documentary provides a rare, intimate look into the fashion publishing world and the creative process behind one of the most important fashion publications. It explores the relationship between Wintour and her staff, particularly creative director Grace Coddington, who provides a contrasting perspective on the industry. “The September Issue” is an inspiring watch for anyone interested in the editorial side of fashion, showcasing the importance of vision, collaboration, and leadership in the world of fashion publishing.

2. The First Monday in May (2016)

If you’ve ever wondered what goes into the planning of the Met Gala, “The First Monday in May” is a documentary you won’t want to miss. Directed by Andrew Rossi, this film offers a behind-the-scenes look at the creation of the 2015 Met Gala, themed “China: Through the Looking Glass.” The Met Gala is one of the most glamorous and exclusive events in the fashion calendar, and this documentary showcases the incredible effort that goes into curating the theme, organizing the event, and ensuring everything runs smoothly.

The film not only highlights the glamorous aspects of the gala but also explores the intersection of fashion and art. It features interviews with notable figures like Anna Wintour, designer John Galliano, and film director Wong Kar-wai. “The First Monday in May” gives a fascinating glimpse into the world of high-fashion events, where creativity, culture, and commerce come together to create a spectacle unlike any other.

3. McQueen (2018)

Alexander McQueen was a visionary designer who changed the landscape of fashion with his dark, avant-garde, and often controversial collections. “McQueen” is a powerful documentary that chronicles the life and career of the British fashion icon. Directed by Ian Bonhôte and Peter Ettedgui, the film offers a detailed look at McQueen’s rise to fame, his personal struggles, and his untimely death at the age of 40.

Through archival footage, interviews with those who worked closely with McQueen, and a deep dive into his collections, “McQueen” explores his genius, his impact on fashion, and the dark forces that influenced his work. The documentary delves into the emotional and psychological toll that the fashion industry can have on designers, making it a poignant and thought-provoking watch. McQueen’s story is one of brilliance, but also one of tragedy, and this film captures both aspects with grace and sensitivity.

4. Dior and I (2014)

The world of haute couture is often seen as distant and exclusive, but “Dior and I” offers a rare glimpse behind the scenes of this luxury fashion house. Directed by Frédéric Tcheng, this documentary follows Raf Simons as he takes on the role of creative director at Christian Dior in 2012. Simons, known for his minimalist and modern approach, was tasked with reviving the legacy of Dior while creating a new vision for the brand.

The documentary follows Simons as he designs his first haute couture collection for Dior, providing insight into the immense pressure and creativity involved in the process. It also explores the craftsmanship and dedication of the artisans who bring the designer’s vision to life. “Dior and I” is a beautiful and inspiring look at the intersection of tradition and innovation in the world of high fashion.

5. Bill Cunningham New York (2010)

Bill Cunningham was a legendary photographer whose work for The New York Times captured the everyday elegance and quirky fashion of New York’s streets. “Bill Cunningham New York” is a heartwarming documentary that explores the life of this beloved photographer, who spent decades documenting the fashion trends of everyday people. Directed by Richard Press, the film chronicles Cunningham’s humble beginnings, his passion for fashion, and his unique approach to street photography.

The documentary showcases Cunningham’s deep love for fashion as a form of self-expression, and it highlights his ability to spot trends long before they hit the runway. Through interviews and personal anecdotes, the film paints a portrait of a man who was dedicated to his craft and who saw beauty in the everyday moments of life. “Bill Cunningham New York” is a celebration of the democratization of fashion, where style isn’t limited to the elite but can be found in the streets of the city.

6. The True Cost (2015)

While the fashion industry is often associated with luxury and glamour, there is a darker side to it—the environmental and ethical cost of fast fashion. “The True Cost,” directed by Andrew Morgan, is a documentary that takes a hard look at the global impact of fast fashion. The film examines the exploitation of workers in developing countries, the environmental destruction caused by the fashion industry, and the enormous waste generated by consumerism.

This documentary is a call to action for both consumers and designers to consider the long-term effects of their choices. “The True Cost” explores the rise of fast fashion brands and the unsustainable nature of the industry. It also highlights the efforts of ethical designers and activists who are working to create a more sustainable and responsible fashion world. This is an essential watch for anyone who wants to understand the true cost of their fashion choices and be inspired to make more conscious decisions.

7. Valentino: The Last Emperor (2008)

Valentino Garavani is one of the most iconic designers in fashion history, known for his elegant and luxurious designs. “Valentino: The Last Emperor” is a documentary that offers a glimpse into the life and career of the Italian designer. Directed by Matt Tyrnauer, the film captures the final years of Valentino’s career as he prepares to retire from his fashion house after more than 45 years in the industry.

The documentary is a fascinating look at the life of a fashion legend, showcasing Valentino’s dedication to his craft and the people who helped him build his empire. It also explores the changing dynamics of the fashion world, as younger designers began to take over, and the challenges of maintaining a legacy in a rapidly evolving industry. “Valentino: The Last Emperor” is a beautiful tribute to the artistry and perseverance that defined Valentino’s career.

8. Franca: Chaos and Creation (2016)

Franca Sozzani was the long-time editor-in-chief of Vogue Italia and a powerful figure in the fashion world. “Franca: Chaos and Creation” is a documentary about her life and legacy, directed by her son, Francesco Carrozzini. The film provides an intimate look at Sozzani’s career, her influence on fashion, and her personal struggles. It also examines her role in shaping the fashion industry and her commitment to pushing boundaries and challenging conventional norms.

The documentary explores Sozzani’s vision for Vogue Italia, which became known for its bold, sometimes controversial editorial choices. It also delves into her role as a mentor and her personal relationships, offering a nuanced portrait of a woman who was both a visionary and a trailblazer in the fashion industry. “Franca: Chaos and Creation” is a moving and insightful look at one of the most influential figures in fashion journalism.

9. House of Z (2017)

Zac Posen is an American designer known for his glamorous and feminine designs. “House of Z” is a documentary that follows Posen’s career, focusing on his rise to fame, the challenges he faced in the fashion industry, and his eventual struggle to rebuild his brand after experiencing financial difficulties. Directed by Sandy Chronopoulos, the film offers a candid look at the pressures of running a fashion house and the highs and lows of being a designer.

The documentary highlights Posen’s passion for fashion and his dedication to creating beautiful clothes, even in the face of adversity. It also explores the changing dynamics of the fashion industry, particularly the rise of social media and its impact on designers. “House of Z” is an inspiring story of resilience, creativity, and the constant drive to reinvent oneself in the competitive world of fashion.

10. Lo and Behold: Reveries of the Connected World (2016)

While not strictly a fashion documentary, “Lo and Behold: Reveries of the Connected World,” directed by Werner Herzog, provides a fascinating exploration of the internet and technology’s impact on various industries, including fashion. The film delves into how the digital revolution has transformed the way we communicate, shop, and experience fashion. From online shopping platforms to the rise of social media influencers, the documentary provides valuable insights into the intersection of technology and fashion in the modern world.

“Lo and Behold” is a thought-provoking exploration of the future of fashion in the digital age, offering a unique perspective on how technology has reshaped the industry.

Conclusion

The world of fashion is multifaceted, and fashion documentaries offer a captivating way to explore its many layers. Whether you’re interested in the creative process behind the designs, the history of iconic fashion houses, or the ethical considerations surrounding the industry, there’s a documentary for you. These films not only entertain but also inspire, offering valuable lessons and insights for anyone looking to understand the power of fashion and its influence on culture.

These documentaries provide a wealth of inspiration for designers, fashion lovers, and anyone with an interest in the art of clothing. So, grab some popcorn, sit back, and get ready to be inspired by some of the best fashion documentaries that have shaped the way we view the world of fashion today.
