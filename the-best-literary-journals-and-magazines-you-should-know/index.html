The Best Literary Journals and Magazines You Should Know


For every writer, reader, or literary enthusiast, discovering the right literary journals and magazines can be a game changer. These publications often serve as the heartbeats of literary culture, showcasing emerging talent, celebrated voices, and groundbreaking work across fiction, poetry, essays, and criticism. Whether you’re seeking platforms to submit your own writing or sources to stay updated with contemporary literature, certain journals consistently stand out for their quality, prestige, and influence.

In this comprehensive guide, we explore some of the most respected and exciting literary journals and magazines that you should know about. We’ll cover their histories, editorial focuses, submission climates, and why they resonate with both contributors and readers. By the end, you’ll have a curated list to keep handy for submissions or simply for enriching your reading list.

1. The New Yorker

Arguably one of the most influential magazines in the world, The New Yorker isn’t just a staple of journalism, but a bastion of literary fiction and poetry. Since its establishment in 1925, it has consistently published work by some of the finest contemporary authors including Jhumpa Lahiri, George Saunders, and Zadie Smith.

Why It Stands Out

  • Exceptional editorial standards: Every piece is meticulously curated.
  • Cross-genre breadth: It combines short stories, poetry, critiques, and essays seamlessly.
  • Wide audience reach: Exposure here can profoundly impact an author’s career.

Submission Tips

Preparing submissions for The New Yorker is no small task due to its rigorous editorial expectations. Writers should be prepared with flawless, polished work that often requires multiple revisions. While unsolicited submissions can be challenging to get through, persistence and unique voice are key.

2. Ploughshares

Founded at Emerson College in 1971, Ploughshares has made its mark as a venue dedicated to new and emerging voices. It’s an excellent choice for writers aiming to publish serious literary fiction, poetry, or essays that engage with both personal and broader societal themes.

Features

  • Guest-edited issues: Each issue is curated by a different guest editor, adding diverse perspectives.
  • Workshops and online content: Beyond print, it offers community resources valuable for writers.

Submission Considerations

Ploughshares accepts unsolicited submissions during specific periods and has a reputation for thoughtful editorial feedback. Writers can benefit from closely reading past issues to align their submissions with its tone and quality.

3. Tin House

Tin House was founded on a principle of nurturing creative voices, publishing bold and original work in fiction, poetry, and nonfiction. It has evolved into a prominent platform blending avant-garde aesthetics with accessibility.

Highlights

  • Innovative storytelling: It often showcases experimental prose and poetry.
  • Award-winning contributors: Published authors frequently receive significant literary prizes.
  • Workshops and summer camps: Hosting writerly events aimed at growth and networking.

Submission Strategy

They accept submissions online twice yearly, welcoming fresh and daring writing that challenges conventional forms. Writers should aim to produce work that pushes boundaries, demonstrating a strong authorial voice.

4. The Paris Review

Known globally for its literary interviews and first-rate fiction and poetry, The Paris Review has shaped literary taste since the 1950s. Its signature feature, “The Art of Fiction” interview series, has immortalized conversations with some of the most influential writers.

What Makes It Special

  • In-depth author interviews: Providing readers and writers insight into the craft.
  • Discovery platform: Often the first place breakthrough authors are published.
  • High editorial standards: The poetry and fiction sections set benchmarks for quality.

How to Approach Submissions

Due to high demand, submissions can be a lottery, but the journal frequently seeks innovative work that combines craft with compelling narratives. Pay close attention to their reading periods and guidelines.

5. Granta

Granta, based in the UK, is renowned for its contemporaneous cultural commentary alongside literary fiction and memoir. The publication often challenges political and social issues through powerful storytelling.

Core Attributes

  • Thematic issues: Each edition has an overarching theme, which encourages cohesion among contributors.
  • Global outlook: It features a broad spectrum of voices from around the world.
  • Literary prestige: Winning the George Orwell Prize and other accolades, its impact is substantial.

Submission Notes

Writers interested should familiarize themselves with past themes, as submissions need to resonate well with the issue’s focus. Memoirs and narratives that engage with identity, place, and politics tend to be favored.

6. The Missouri Review

The Missouri Review offers a balanced blend of fiction, poetry, nonfiction, and criticism. Since its inception in 1978, it has cultivated an attentive readership and contributed significantly to literary culture.

Strengths

  • Prize-winning submissions: Regularly features authors recognized by major literary awards.
  • Committed to diversity: Actively seeks work from underrepresented voices.
  • Strong online presence: Their digital archive allows easier access to back issues.

Submission Tips

Strong narrative voices and richly layered poetry find a home here. Writers are advised to submit during open reading periods and to follow all formatting instructions carefully for greater consideration.

7. AGNI

AGNI is noted for spotlighting new work that combines literary excellence with an ethical dimension, often exploring the intersections of culture, identity, and memory.

What To Expect

  • Focus on emerging writers: Known for discovering talent early.
  • Use of language: Prioritizes lyrical and thought-provoking prose and poetry.
  • Essay and nonfiction quality: Exceptional narrative nonfiction pieces appear regularly.

Submission Pointers

They encourage submissions that show intellectual depth and emotional resonance. Paying attention to their editor’s notes and past awards can guide prospective contributors.

8. The Southern Review

Hailing from Louisiana State University, The Southern Review has been a champion of Southern and national voices since 1935. The magazine combines literary tradition with contemporary innovation.

Key Points

  • Blend of classic and modern: Showcases an eclectic mix of styles.
  • Noted contributors: Authors like Eudora Welty and Robert Penn Warren have appeared.
  • Conducive to emerging and established writers: Offers visibility for both.

Submission Suggestions

Writers with nuanced portrayals of place, character, and language will find resonance here. The journal prefers thoughtful stories and poetry that reflect deep engagement with human experience.

9. The Kenyon Review

With a rich history beginning in 1939, The Kenyon Review belongs among the top literary journals fostering new voices and innovative literature. It crosses genres to combine poetry, fiction, essays, and reviews.

Highlights

  • Author-driven community: Provides opportunities for writer development.
  • Royalty payment: Pays contributors—a sign of respect for the craft.
  • Theme issues occasionally: Bring focused attention to cultural or literary trends.

Submission Advice

The Kenyon Review champions writing that challenges norms and explores new narrative or poetic terrains. Writers should submit polished work and engage with the publication’s history and values.

10. Poetry Magazine

A beacon for the poetry world since 1912, Poetry Magazine publishes some of the most influential and transformative poetry globally. It has been a starting point for many renowned poets.

What Sets It Apart

  • Poetry-focused: Dedicated solely to poetry, emphasizing diverse styles.
  • Educational resources: Provides workshops, contests, and historic archives.
  • Prize-winning history: Many poems published here go on to win major awards.

For Poets

Consistent submissions of strong, original poems with attention to craft and innovation can find a home in Poetry Magazine. They are known for encouraging emerging poets and provide detailed submission guidelines online.

How to Maximize Your Literary Journal Experience

Reading Widely

The first step in finding a journal that fits your own style or interests is reading extensively. Delving into back issues of these publications can fine-tune your understanding of what kind of work each one prefers.

Submitting Strategically

  • Research reading windows: Many journals have open and closed submission periods.
  • Tailor your material: Match your work’s tone and themes to that journal’s editorial focus.
  • Follow guidelines meticulously: Formatting and submission rules matter for initial consideration.

Building Relationships

Engage with editorials, attend literary events, and participate in workshops or forums related to these journals. Connections often open doors in literary publishing.

Conclusion

Literary journals and magazines are more than just platforms for publishing; they are crucibles where literature is tested, refined, and celebrated. From the polished halls of The New Yorker to the experimental spaces of Tin House, each publication carries its own distinctive voice and mission.

Whether you’re a writer searching for your perfect publication partner or an eager reader wanting to stay connected to the latest literary currents, familiarizing yourself with these journals broadens your world. Your journey through the literary landscape becomes richer when you explore these well-curated spaces—where words don’t just convey meaning but transform the way we see ourselves and the world.

Keep this guide close as you navigate the vast and vibrant world of contemporary literary journals. They are welcoming places where great stories, poems, and essays await discovery, for every reader and writer who dares to listen.

Further Exploration

For those interested, many of the journals mentioned also have accompanying podcasts, reading series, and digital content worth exploring. These platforms extend the written word into new formats, enriching the literary experience beyond the page.

Thank you for joining this literary tour—may it inspire many engrossing reads and successful submissions!
