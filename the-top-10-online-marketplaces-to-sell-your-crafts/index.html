The Top 10 Online Marketplaces to Sell Your Crafts


If you’re a craft enthusiast looking to turn your passion into a profitable business, the internet provides you with countless opportunities to sell your handmade goods. Whether you’re creating jewelry, knitting scarves, or painting custom artwork, there are platforms tailored for every type of craft and every level of seller. Choosing the right marketplace can be the difference between making a few sales and turning your craft into a full-fledged business. Below are the top 10 online marketplaces to sell your crafts, each with its own unique features and advantages.

1. Etsy: The Leading Craft Marketplace

When it comes to selling handmade items, Etsy remains the most popular and widely recognized platform. Founded in 2005, Etsy has become the go-to marketplace for artisans, crafters, and creators of all kinds. The platform’s user-friendly interface and community-driven approach make it easy for new sellers to set up shop and get started.

Why Choose Etsy?

  • Large Audience: Etsy attracts millions of buyers looking for unique, handmade, and vintage items, which provides a huge potential for sales.
  • Customization: Sellers can customize their shops with branding, product descriptions, and professional photos, helping your craft stand out.
  • Support for Small Businesses: Etsy’s tools are designed to support small business owners, including inventory management, shipping options, and customer service support.

Key Features:

  • Marketplace specifically for handmade, vintage, and craft supplies.
  • Flexible pricing and listing options.
  • Extensive promotional tools, such as ads and discounts.
  • Seller community offering tips and insights.

Etsy’s combination of targeted audience and ease of use makes it a top contender for anyone wanting to sell their crafts online.

2. Amazon Handmade: A Giant with a Crafting Niche

Amazon Handmade offers sellers the ability to list their handmade goods on one of the largest e-commerce platforms in the world. If you want to tap into the global reach of Amazon while still focusing on handcrafted products, this is the ideal platform.

Why Choose Amazon Handmade?

  • Global Reach: Amazon has a massive customer base, which increases the likelihood of your products being discovered by buyers from around the world.
  • Trust and Reputation: Amazon’s reputation and robust customer service infrastructure help build trust with shoppers, which is particularly useful when selling handmade items.
  • Easy Shipping: With Amazon FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon), sellers can store inventory in Amazon’s warehouses, making the shipping process smooth and efficient.

Key Features:

  • Exclusively for handmade goods.
  • Integration with Amazon’s powerful search engine and advertising tools.
  • Streamlined fulfillment through Amazon FBA.
  • Professional storefronts and product listings.

While Amazon Handmade is more competitive than platforms like Etsy, its broad reach and association with Amazon’s established reputation make it a great platform for craftspeople looking to expand their market.

3. eBay: The Marketplace for Everything

Though traditionally associated with auctions, eBay also offers a great platform for selling handmade and unique items. With over 180 million active buyers worldwide, eBay gives sellers the chance to reach a global audience, whether you’re selling one-of-a-kind crafts or smaller batches of products.

Why Choose eBay?

  • Auction and Fixed Price Options: eBay’s auction system allows buyers to bid on your crafts, which can result in higher-than-expected prices, or you can set fixed prices for your items.
  • International Sales: eBay makes it easy to sell internationally, expanding your customer base beyond local borders.
  • Low Fees: Compared to other platforms, eBay’s fees are generally lower, and they offer various payment options for sellers.

Key Features:

  • Wide variety of selling options, including auction-style listings.
  • International shipping made simple.
  • Buyer protection policies that add trust to your listings.
  • Ability to create a store to organize your listings.

eBay is an ideal choice for sellers who want flexibility in their pricing strategy and who are looking to reach an international market.

4. Handmade at Amazon: A More Curated Approach

For those who love the Amazon platform but want a more curated environment for their handcrafted products, Handmade at Amazon is a dedicated section within Amazon that focuses exclusively on artisanal goods. This platform is perfect for sellers who want to be part of Amazon’s expansive network but also want to be positioned among other high-quality handmade sellers.

Why Choose Handmade at Amazon?

  • Premium Market Positioning: Unlike the general marketplace, Handmade at Amazon focuses only on handcrafted goods, which ensures your products are seen by the right audience.
  • Amazon’s Fulfillment Options: Like the broader Amazon platform, Handmade offers access to the FBA program, allowing you to streamline your shipping and customer service.
  • Exclusive Seller Tools: Amazon provides specialized tools for Handmade sellers, such as customizable storefronts and order management systems.

Key Features:

  • A specialized area for handmade goods within Amazon.
  • Customizable storefronts with branding options.
  • Direct access to Amazon’s vast customer base and prime shipping options.
  • Tools to help you manage your inventory and orders efficiently.

Handmade at Amazon is perfect for craftspeople who want the exposure of Amazon but with a more refined marketplace focused exclusively on handcrafted products.

5. Zibbet: A Niche Marketplace for Independent Artists

Zibbet is an up-and-coming marketplace that specifically targets independent artists and makers. It’s a great alternative to larger, more crowded platforms, offering a more intimate and artist-driven environment.

Why Choose Zibbet?

  • Focused Audience: Zibbet caters to a smaller, niche audience who values unique and artistic products, allowing sellers to stand out.
  • Multiple Platforms in One: Zibbet allows sellers to manage their listings across multiple platforms, including Etsy and Shopify, from a single dashboard.
  • Affordable Fees: Zibbet offers affordable subscription plans and lower fees compared to some larger platforms.

Key Features:

  • Niche marketplace for independent creators.
  • Ability to sync products across multiple platforms.
  • Simple, user-friendly interface for managing listings.
  • No listing fees, just subscription-based plans.

For sellers who want a more hands-on approach to managing their craft sales and prefer a less crowded marketplace, Zibbet offers an appealing alternative to Etsy and Amazon.

6. Big Cartel: A Platform for Artists and Makers

Big Cartel provides a simple and easy-to-use platform for artists, crafters, and designers looking to sell their products online. While it may not have the same traffic as Etsy or Amazon, Big Cartel focuses on supporting independent creators with its flexible tools and affordable pricing.

Why Choose Big Cartel?

  • Customizable Storefronts: Big Cartel allows you to create a completely customizable online store, giving you control over how your crafts are displayed.
  • Affordability: With no monthly fees for up to five products, Big Cartel is a great platform for beginners.
  • Simple Setup: The platform is easy to navigate, and setting up your store can be done in a matter of hours.

Key Features:

  • Free plan for small businesses with up to five products.
  • Simple setup with customizable store designs.
  • Low fees for higher-tier plans with more features.
  • Focused on independent artists and makers.

Big Cartel is an excellent choice for new crafters who want to build their brand with a more personal touch while keeping costs low.

7. ArtFire: A Growing Community for Creators

ArtFire has been around since 2008 and is steadily growing as a popular marketplace for handmade goods. It’s similar to Etsy in that it caters specifically to artisans, but it offers its own set of unique features that might appeal to those looking for a more personalized experience.

Why Choose ArtFire?

  • Specialized Audience: ArtFire focuses specifically on handmade, vintage, and craft supplies, meaning your products are going to be seen by the right people.
  • Affordable Plans: ArtFire offers a variety of pricing plans, including a free plan for smaller shops, making it easy for crafters to get started without upfront costs.
  • Integrated Marketing Tools: Sellers have access to email marketing, social media integrations, and other promotional tools to increase visibility.

Key Features:

  • Focused on handmade, vintage, and craft supplies.
  • Affordable pricing plans for small shops.
  • Built-in marketing tools and SEO features.
  • Ability to list products on other online marketplaces.

For sellers looking for a growing community with niche-focused marketing, ArtFire provides an excellent platform to grow your online craft business.

8. Folksy: The UK’s Premier Handmade Marketplace

Folksy is a UK-based marketplace that specializes in handmade goods. If you’re in the UK or targeting UK buyers, Folksy is an ideal option. It’s specifically focused on showcasing British craftsmanship, making it a great platform for local makers.

Why Choose Folksy?

  • UK-Based Focus: If you’re a UK seller or want to reach UK customers, Folksy is a great option as it specifically caters to British artisans.
  • Simple Setup: The platform is user-friendly, making it easy to list products and manage your shop.
  • Low Fees: Folksy offers low transaction fees, making it cost-effective for independent crafters.

Key Features:

  • Focused on UK-based handmade goods.
  • Simple setup and low transaction fees.
  • Integrated marketing tools for visibility.
  • Focus on promoting local craftsmanship.

Folksy provides an excellent platform for UK-based artists looking to reach a domestic audience and sell their crafts in a focused, specialized marketplace.

9. Redbubble: Perfect for Artists and Designers

Redbubble is a print-on-demand marketplace where artists can sell their designs on a wide variety of products, such as clothing, wall art, home decor, and accessories. If you specialize in graphic design, illustration, or photography, Redbubble offers a unique opportunity to monetize your creations.

Why Choose Redbubble?

  • Print-on-Demand: You don’t need to worry about inventory or shipping because Redbubble handles it all. This allows you to focus purely on creating and selling designs.
  • Wide Range of Products: Redbubble allows you to sell your designs on a range of products, from t-shirts to phone cases, mugs, and stickers.
  • Global Reach: Redbubble has an international customer base, giving your designs the potential for global exposure.

Key Features:

  • Print-on-demand platform for artists.
  • Wide range of products to put your designs on.
  • No inventory or shipping concerns.
  • International exposure.

Redbubble is ideal for digital artists and graphic designers who want to turn their creations into products without the need for physical inventory.

10. Society6: Where Art Meets Home Decor

Similar to Redbubble, Society6 is a print-on-demand platform, but it focuses more heavily on home decor and artwork. If you create original artwork or designs and want to sell them as high-quality prints, furniture, or textiles, Society6 offers a perfect venue.

Why Choose Society6?

  • High-Quality Products: Society6 focuses on premium quality home decor, including wall art, rugs, and furniture, providing artists with the opportunity to sell their work in various high-end formats.
  • Global Marketplace: Society6 offers global exposure, which can help your work reach a large and diverse audience.
  • Creative Freedom: As an artist, you have the freedom to choose which designs to sell, and Society6 handles the production and shipping.

Key Features:

  • Focus on high-quality home decor and artwork.
  • Print-on-demand, no inventory needed.
  • Access to a global customer base.
  • Professional-grade printing and product creation.

For artists focused on home decor and fine art, Society6 offers an excellent marketplace to sell your work without worrying about production and logistics.

Conclusion

There are countless online marketplaces available for selling crafts, and choosing the right one can significantly impact your business’s success. Whether you prefer the large, established platforms like Etsy and Amazon or are drawn to niche markets like Zibbet and ArtFire, there’s a marketplace suited for every type of craft and artisan. By understanding the features and audience of each platform, you can make an informed decision that aligns with your goals and helps your handmade business thrive. Start selling your crafts today and take your business to new heights!
