The Journey Never Ends: How Successful Musicians Keep Evolving Their Careers


Life on the fast track in the entertainment industry has enough twists and turns to make even the most seasoned musicians reconsider their career choices. Amidst the global competition, redefining success and staying relevant are just a handful of the challenges musicians face in their quest for creative expression and professional fulfillment. But as we’ve seen time and time again, the most successful musicians not only have the talent to back up their ambition; they’re also masters at evolving their careers amidst the ever-changing landscape.

In an industry that thrives on innovation, it’s the musicians who adapt and grow with the times who tend to stay relevant. The ability to reinvent oneself is a cornerstone of their career evolution, and it’s essential to their long-term success.

Whether it’s by embracing new technology, incorporating fresh genres into their sound, or exploring unexpected partnerships, these musicians embrace the unknown and learn from their experiences. Today, we’ll discuss the unique strategies that successful musicians employ to keep their careers fresh and exciting, and how you can apply these lessons to your own musical endeavors.

Embrace the New and Unexpected

One of the most defining characteristics of successful musicians who are constantly innovating is their openness to learning and trying new things. Kicking off a collaboration with an artist from an unexpected genre, learning a new instrument, or exploring fresh production techniques can open up a world of new creative avenues.

For instance, rapper Logic’s collaboration with the jazz trio partitioned beneath Skywalker Records. By working with this genre’s most important living composer and jazz pianist, John Beasley, Logic defied expectations and shifted perspectives. The fusion of these different genres created something truly unique and provided yet another platform for Logic’s artistic capabilities to shine through.

By extending the boundaries of their comfort zones, musicians like Logic help to redefine not only their personal music styles but also the genre’s scope and influence in music. In doing so, they keep their listener’s engaged and hungry for more.

Experiment, Learn and Evolve

For musicians, experimentation goes beyond genre exploration; it’s also about integrating new technology into their work and the way they create. In recent years, we’ve seen the emergence of AI-driven music generation tools, virtual reality performances, and interactive gaming soundtracks that blend both modern and traditional styles.

GRiZ, an innovative electronic musician known for his distinctive sound fusing jazz, blues, funk, and hip-hop, is one such artist who has fully embraced these new technologies. His choice to collaborate with Grateful Dead on several occasions demonstrates a willingness to experiment with sight and sound – an indicator of his adaptability.

To be a successful musician at the top of their game, one should not shy away from experimentations but embrace the opportunity for continuous evolution and development – whether it is via a fresh sound, a genre-bending collaboration, or a unique blend of technology and artistry. Musicians who do this end up creating unforgettable memories and milestones in their career while enriching their audience’s music experience.

Pursue Multiple Pillars of Income

They say nothing is certain except for death and taxes. But within the music industry, nothing is as certain as the fluctuating demand for your music. While your passion and talent are the best main drivers, building multiple income streams can add a sense of security to your career.

For the younger artist, factors such as streaming royalties, social media income, and merchandise sell-through front their financial landscape. As they grow, these income sources become more sustainable and steady.

Looking at, for example, Tiesto and Armin van Buuren who have delved into fashion, having their respective clothing lines which supplement and underpin their djing and production careers, musicians understand the importance of diversifying their revenue streams in order to build a self-sustainable and independent career.

Successful musicians also understand the importance of empire-building – securing lucrative commercials, ambassadorial roles, and other side gigs, which bolster their musical career without overshadowing their primary focus.

Keep Your Audience Involved and Excited

Successful musicians know the importance of staying relevant, and one of the most effective ways to do this is by engaging with fans in new and exciting ways. This could mean streaming live concerts, releasing hits in unison with specific cultural moments or pushing the boundaries of social media engagement.

For instance, in late 2020, BTS, a globally celebrated Korean pop group, unveiled their engrossing monumental “BTS Army Bomb”, a social media project to enhance fan engagement. Each member distributed a unique code to fans, to be shared between fellow fans as digital collectibles. When a certain number of these codes were combined, it activated a special video that thanked the fans for their unwavering support, keeping their fans crucially involved and excited for their music.

It’s important to implement different marketing strategies and prioritize audience engagement to build that connection between an artist and their fans which stays robust and sustained regardless of the ever-evolving contemporary industry demands.

Conclusion

In an ever-changing world of music, musicians must adapt and evolve their careers if they want to carve long-lasting success. They should not be limited or held back by a single facet of their past but ought to embrace change, explore new territories, learn from different experiences and contribute to the industry’s constant progress. Reinvesting in their music careers, adapting to altering trends, and developing multiple income type pillars – these are all vital elements required to keep evolving and becoming successful musicians.
