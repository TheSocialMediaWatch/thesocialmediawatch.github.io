The Best Wines to Enjoy with Seafood


Pairing wine with seafood can be an art form, elevating the flavors of both the dish and the wine to create a harmonious dining experience. Whether you are enjoying delicate shellfish, rich fish fillets, or a hearty seafood pasta, choosing the right wine can make all the difference. In this guide, we will explore the best wines to complement various types of seafood, ensuring you have the perfect bottle for any dish.

Understanding Wine and Seafood Pairing

Wine pairing is not just about matching flavors; it’s about balancing textures, acidity, and weight. Seafood tends to have a lighter, fresher profile compared to many meats, which is why white wines, with their crispness and subtlety, are often the best companions. However, reds and rosés can also work wonders, particularly with more robust seafood dishes. The key is to complement, not overpower, the delicate flavors of the seafood.

Basic Principles of Pairing

  1. Acidity: Seafood, with its fresh, briny flavors, tends to pair well with wines that have high acidity. The acidity in the wine helps to cut through the richness of the seafood and enhances the natural flavors of the dish.

  2. Lightness and Body: Lighter wines, such as many whites and rosés, are often ideal for delicate fish and shellfish. Full-bodied wines may overwhelm the subtlety of some seafood dishes but can work with heartier seafood options, like lobster or grilled tuna.

  3. Regional Pairing: A fun rule to remember is that wines from regions known for producing seafood often pair wonderfully with local seafood. For example, a crisp white wine from the Loire Valley in France pairs perfectly with oysters, just as a Sardinian Vermentino complements the region’s seafood.

Best White Wines for Seafood

Sauvignon Blanc

Sauvignon Blanc is one of the best choices for pairing with seafood due to its vibrant acidity and citrusy notes. It has a refreshing crispness that works well with a variety of seafood, from oysters and shrimp to grilled fish and ceviche. The zesty acidity of Sauvignon Blanc enhances the natural flavors of the seafood while providing a nice contrast to richer dishes.

  • Pairing Suggestions: Grilled shrimp, oysters, crab, and grilled fish such as sea bass or snapper.

Chardonnay (Unoaked)

While Chardonnay can be a heavy wine when oaked, the unoaked versions offer a lighter, crisper profile. With clean citrus flavors and a smooth finish, unoaked Chardonnay complements seafood without overwhelming it. The wine’s natural acidity pairs well with the saltiness and sweetness of seafood, making it a versatile choice for various dishes.

  • Pairing Suggestions: Lobster, crab cakes, sushi, scallops, and grilled white fish like halibut or cod.

Albariño

Originating from Spain, Albariño is a white wine known for its bright acidity and tropical fruit notes, such as peach and apricot. It has a mineral undertone that complements the brininess of shellfish and seafood. Albariño’s crispness makes it an excellent match for seafood, especially for those looking for a wine that isn’t too overpowering but still packs flavor.

  • Pairing Suggestions: Shellfish, oysters, mussels, and grilled fish like sardines or mackerel.

Vermentino

Vermentino is a medium-bodied white wine from Italy with a zesty citrus profile and a hint of herbal notes. It’s perfect for pairing with seafood dishes that feature Mediterranean flavors, such as grilled fish with olive oil and lemon. Vermentino’s lively acidity and fresh finish make it an ideal wine for pairing with seafood pasta, seafood salads, and light seafood stews.

  • Pairing Suggestions: Grilled fish, seafood pasta, seafood salads, and mussels in white wine sauce.

Best Rosé Wines for Seafood

Provençal Rosé

Provençal rosé, from the south of France, is one of the most versatile rosés when it comes to seafood. Light, crisp, and refreshing, Provençal rosé has delicate floral and red berry aromas that pair beautifully with fresh, light seafood dishes. The wine’s clean, refreshing finish enhances the freshness of the seafood without overshadowing it.

  • Pairing Suggestions: Grilled sardines, tuna tartare, shrimp cocktails, and smoked salmon.

Dry Rosé

Dry rosé wines from regions like Spain (such as Rosado) or California are another great option to consider when enjoying seafood. These wines tend to have subtle berry flavors, coupled with the acidity and freshness that make rosé an excellent companion to lighter seafood dishes. The added benefit of dry rosé is its ability to pair with a wide variety of seafood, making it a fantastic option for mixed seafood platters.

  • Pairing Suggestions: Crab cakes, shrimp scampi, grilled fish, and ceviche.

Best Red Wines for Seafood

Pinot Noir

While red wines are not typically associated with seafood, certain lighter reds, like Pinot Noir, can make a wonderful pairing for more robust seafood dishes. Pinot Noir has a soft tannin structure and high acidity, which allows it to complement grilled or roasted seafood without overpowering it. The wine’s red fruit flavors and earthy undertones work particularly well with dishes that include mushrooms or smoky grilled fish.

  • Pairing Suggestions: Grilled salmon, tuna steaks, blackened swordfish, and seafood risotto.

Beaujolais

Beaujolais is a light, fruity red wine that offers bright acidity and gentle tannins, making it a surprisingly good match for seafood. The fresh berry flavors in Beaujolais are perfect for pairing with seafood that features a bit of sweetness, such as lobster or crab. Beaujolais is also great with grilled seafood, offering a nice contrast to the charred flavors while enhancing the taste of the dish.

  • Pairing Suggestions: Lobster, crab, grilled salmon, and seafood paella.

Best Sparkling Wines for Seafood

Champagne

Champagne is a classic wine to enjoy with seafood, and for good reason. The high acidity, effervescence, and minerality of Champagne complement a variety of seafood dishes. Whether you are enjoying raw oysters or lobster, Champagne’s crisp and refreshing qualities enhance the natural flavors of the seafood. It’s also an excellent choice for celebrating a seafood feast.

  • Pairing Suggestions: Oysters, caviar, lobster, smoked salmon, and shrimp.

Prosecco

Prosecco is a more affordable sparkling wine that shares many of the same characteristics as Champagne, such as bright acidity and refreshing bubbles. It has a fruity, slightly sweeter profile that pairs well with light, sweet seafood dishes. Prosecco is perfect for seafood appetizers, such as shrimp cocktails or mussels in white wine sauce.

  • Pairing Suggestions: Shrimp cocktail, mussels, oysters, and seafood salads.

Best Wine Pairing for Specific Seafood Dishes

Grilled Fish

Grilled fish has a smoky, savory flavor that pairs wonderfully with wines that have bright acidity and subtle fruit notes. A white Burgundy or Sauvignon Blanc is perfect for this type of dish. If you prefer a red, a light Pinot Noir can provide an interesting contrast without overwhelming the flavors of the fish.

  • Best Wines: Sauvignon Blanc, Pinot Noir, Chardonnay (unoaked), and Beaujolais.

Oysters

Oysters are delicate and briny, which makes them an ideal match for crisp, mineral-driven white wines. A classic pairing is a Sauvignon Blanc from the Loire Valley, but a glass of Champagne also does wonders with oysters.

  • Best Wines: Champagne, Sauvignon Blanc, Muscadet, and Albariño.

Lobster

Lobster has a sweet, rich flavor that pairs best with wines that have a touch of weight and complexity. A buttery Chardonnay or even a light red like Pinot Noir can complement the sweetness of the lobster, especially when served with drawn butter or a rich sauce.

  • Best Wines: Chardonnay (unoaked or lightly oaked), Pinot Noir, and Champagne.

Shrimp

Shrimp is one of the most versatile seafood options, pairing well with both white and rosé wines. Sauvignon Blanc is an excellent choice due to its bright acidity, while a dry rosé offers a refreshing contrast to shrimp dishes with their mild sweetness.

  • Best Wines: Sauvignon Blanc, Dry Rosé, Vermentino, and Prosecco.

Salmon

Salmon’s rich, oily texture pairs well with wines that have a little more body. A medium-bodied white wine, such as Chardonnay, or a light red wine like Pinot Noir, will complement the flavors of the fish. Grilled or smoked salmon pairs particularly well with a red wine like Beaujolais.

  • Best Wines: Pinot Noir, Chardonnay (unoaked), Beaujolais, and Champagne.

Final Thoughts

Pairing wine with seafood is all about finding balance and harmony. The right wine can elevate your dining experience, enhancing the natural flavors of the seafood while providing a refreshing contrast. Whether you prefer white, red, rosé, or sparkling wines, there is a perfect wine for every seafood dish. Don’t be afraid to experiment and explore new pairings – the world of wine and seafood is vast and filled with delicious possibilities.
