The Ghostly Residents of the Myrtles Plantation


Nestled in St. Francisville, Louisiana, the Myrtles Plantation stands as a prominent historical landmark shrouded in tales of mystery and hauntings. With a history dating back to the late 18th century, this majestic plantation has earned a reputation for being one of the most haunted places in America. Let’s delve into the eerie stories that surround the ghostly residents of the Myrtles Plantation.

The Legend of Chloe

One of the most famous ghost stories associated with the Myrtles Plantation revolves around the spirit of Chloe, a former slave who allegedly haunts the premises. According to the legend, Chloe was a slave owned by Clark Woodruff, the plantation owner. Seeking to eavesdrop on conversations in the house, Chloe was caught and as a punishment, her ear was reportedly severed.

Distraught by her disfigurement, Chloe wore a green turban to conceal the injury. Some accounts suggest that Chloe, seeking revenge or forgiveness, baked a birthday cake laced with poison that inadvertently led to the death of Woodruff’s wife and children. Chloe’s spirit is said to wander the grounds, appearing in hallways and rooms, still wearing her green turban.

The Ghostly Handprints

Visitors and staff at the Myrtles Plantation have reported numerous encounters with the supernatural. One common phenomenon is the appearance of ghostly handprints on mirrors throughout the house. These mysterious handprints, which seemingly appear out of nowhere and cannot be wiped away, have baffled skeptics and believers alike. Some attribute these handprints to the restless spirits that inhabit the plantation.

The Specter of William Drew Winter

Another chilling tale linked to the Myrtles Plantation involves the ghost of William Drew Winter. Winter, an attorney who resided in the house during the 19th century, was shot on the front porch by a stranger. Despite being mortally wounded, Winter managed to climb the stairs to the 17th step before collapsing and succumbing to his injuries.

Since then, visitors have reported seeing the ghostly figure of Winter staggering up the stairs, leaving behind bloody footprints. The sound of his dying footsteps reverberating through the corridors adds to the eerie ambiance of the plantation.

The Haunted Mirror

One of the most intriguing artifacts at the Myrtles Plantation is the haunted mirror located in the grand foyer. Legend has it that this mirror holds the spirits of Sara Woodruff and her children, who tragically died in the house. Visitors claim to have seen ghostly apparitions reflected in the mirror or handprints mysteriously appearing on its surface, further fueling the plantation’s reputation as a paranormal hotspot.

Conclusion

The Myrtles Plantation stands as a testament to a bygone era, where history and hauntings intertwine to create an aura of mystery and intrigue. Whether you are a skeptic or a believer, the ghostly residents of this historic estate continue to captivate the imagination of all who dare to step foot on its hallowed grounds. So, if you ever find yourself in Louisiana, consider a visit to the Myrtles Plantation – who knows, you might just have a ghostly encounter of your own.

Remember, the past is never truly dead; it lingers on in the whispers of the wind and the shadows of the night.
