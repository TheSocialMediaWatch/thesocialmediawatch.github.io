The Best Fantasy Podcasts for Book Lovers


Fantasy literature has long captivated readers with its boundless imagination, expansive worlds, and unforgettable characters. But sometimes, the craving for more magic and adventure extends beyond the constraints of book pages. For book lovers who can’t get enough of the genre, fantasy podcasts are a perfect companion, offering discussions, storytelling, and rich explorations of the worlds you love—or introducing you to new realms entirely. Podcasts bring the magic of fantasy to life in fresh ways, whether through expert analysis of beloved sagas or original tales spun by talented narrators.

In this post, we’ll delve into some of the top fantasy podcasts, each with its unique take on the genre. These podcasts are not only entertaining but also stand out for their immersive narratives and passionate creators who infuse their work with love for fantasy literature. Whether you’re searching for book recommendations, deep dives into popular series, or original stories to sweep you off your feet, you’re guaranteed to find something that ignites your imagination.

Imaginary Worlds

For anyone who loves to examine the deeper layers of fantasy, Imaginary Worlds is a must-listen. Hosted by Eric Molinsky, this podcast explores the origins, history, and cultural impact of fictional worlds created for books, movies, and other media. Each episode is crafted with astounding attention to detail, offering thoughtful insights into topics ranging from magical realism to worldbuilding techniques.

Book lovers will particularly enjoy episodes that dissect iconic literary worlds, from Middle-earth to Earthsea. If you’ve ever wondered why certain fictional realms captivate audiences for decades or even centuries, Imaginary Worlds provides answers steeped in informed analysis and engaging storytelling. It’s perfect for listeners who take pleasure in unpacking the mechanics of fantasy storytelling while gaining glimpses into the minds behind such creations.

LeVar Burton Reads

LeVar Burton is a household name among book enthusiasts, and his podcast LeVar Burton Reads is nothing short of incredible for fans of fantasy fiction. In every episode, Burton selects a short story to read aloud, weaving the narrative with his exceptional storytelling abilities that have enchanted audiences since his Reading Rainbow days.

While Burton reads across many genres, fantasy highlights are frequent, featuring both established authors and rising voices. This podcast serves as a wonderful gateway to discovering lesser-known stories packed with magic, wonder, and richly imagined worlds. Whether you’re on a commute or settled in for a cozy evening, Burton’s voice makes each tale immersive and unforgettable.

The Legendarium Podcast

Fantasy aficionados often love to debate, especially when it comes to long-standing book series. That’s why The Legendarium Podcast is ideal for those who thrive on discussions that dive deep into literary fantasy and beyond. Covering beloved classics like The Lord of the Rings and The Stormlight Archive, this podcast offers panel-style reviews and analytical commentary that bring multiple perspectives into the mix.

The hosts balance thoughtful criticism with lighthearted banter, making The Legendarium an excellent choice for book clubs or fans on Reddit boards who enjoy conversations about future theories, favorite characters, and overarching themes. Don’t be surprised if the show inspires you to reread your favorites with new questions swirling in your mind.

Writing Excuses

While not exclusively fantasy-focused, Writing Excuses is a superb resource for readers who are interested in the craft behind the stories they love. Co-hosted by bestselling fantasy authors like Brandon Sanderson, Mary Robinette Kowal, and Dan Wells, this podcast offers expert advice on writing techniques, including building magical systems and creating dynamic characters.

For book lovers fascinated by the mechanics behind their favorite tales, Writing Excuses walks listeners through everything from brainstorming ideas to polishing a finished product. Episodes are structured to be concise, clocking in at 15 minutes each, which makes it convenient for engaging in bite-sized episodes on literary craft while learning from some of the genre’s top creators.

Welcome to Night Vale

One of the defining examples of original storytelling via podcast, Welcome to Night Vale is a beloved staple among fantasy fans who enjoy surreal and quirky narratives. Featuring a fictional radio broadcast from a strange desert town where the supernatural is mundane, this podcast blends humor, horror, and fantasy with impeccable creativity.

Though not a traditional fantasy novel discussion podcast, Night Vale’s literary roots run deep. Its clever wordplay, atmospheric tone, and strange characters echo the qualities of fantasy literature that make fans fall in love with unique and immersive storytelling. Best served to listeners who enjoy experimental approaches to worldbuilding and character arcs, Welcome to Night Vale is an ongoing masterpiece that evokes the eerie charm of Neil Gaiman’s works.

Overdue

There’s no denying it—book lovers often have growing piles of unread novels staring at them from shelves or nightstands. Enter Overdue, a comedic podcast dedicated to discussing books that the hosts (and their listeners) have meant to read but never quite got around to finishing.

While spanning multiple genres, Overdue often celebrates major works of fantasy by exploring their history, themes, and significance. Episodes are infused with humor and relatability, reminding listeners why beloved classics stand the test of time. Whether the hosts are reviewing Tolkien, Lewis, or lesser-known gems, their unpolished yet deeply passionate conversations resonate with anyone who has ever struggled to tackle their TBR list.

Dark Tome

If you’re looking for a podcast that delivers spellbinding fantasy stories, Dark Tome is the ideal choice. This audio fiction anthology weaves magical narratives told by different voices, offering listeners fresh journeys into realms of mysticism and fantastical lore.

Each episode highlights original short stories with themes of magic, transformation, and otherworldly mysteries. Fans of epic prose and fantastical storytelling will appreciate the opportunity to dive into tales that feel like condensed novels, brimming with vivid imagery and unforgettable characters. Featuring well-produced soundscapes, Dark Tome is an auditory experience that immerses listeners with rich fantasy from start to finish.

Books & Boba

Fantasy book lovers with an affinity for diverse stories will love Books & Boba. This podcast focuses on Asian and Asian American authors across multiple genres but frequently dives deeply into fantasy literature. By showcasing stories that often feature cultural mythologies, striking symbolism, and unique perspectives, Books & Boba expands the horizons of a genre that occasionally risks leaning into familiar tropes.

Books highlighted on the show include magical adventures with historical twists, epic coming-of-age tales, and myth-inspired sagas with breathtaking imagery. Whether you’re exploring your heritage or simply seeking fresh voices in fantasy, this podcast is packed with literary treasures that deserve attention.

Epic Realms

Epic Realms is a podcast designed for fans of rich storytelling, vivid worldbuilding, and narrative-driven artistry. The show frequently features interviews with authors, actors, and other creatives whose works align with the grandiose, larger-than-life qualities of epic fantasy.

Episodes provide behind-the-scenes glimpses into how storytellers shape their craft, offering listeners a deeper appreciation for the magic behind the works they adore. Whether it’s exploring a new fantasy novel or diving into the lore of detailed video game narratives, Epic Realms celebrates creativity at its core, making it an ideal choice for voracious readers.

Conclusion

Fantasy podcasts represent a natural extension of the magic and wonder found within the pages of books. Whether you’re diving into thoughtful discussions of classic novels, enjoying enthralling performances of original stories, or discovering behind-the-scenes secrets of your favorite series, these podcasts deliver pure enchantment in portable, bite-sized doses.

For book lovers, these podcasts not only complement your reading habits but also create new avenues to explore your favorite genre. Each offers a treasure trove of perspectives, stories, and ideas, igniting the imagination in ways that feel just like turning the pages of a fantastical tome. Choose a few of these gems to add to your rotation—and let the magic unfold.
