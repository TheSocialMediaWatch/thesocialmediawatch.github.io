Collecting Vintage Pottery: From Roseville to McCoy


If you’re a lover of all things vintage and have an eye for unique home decor, collecting vintage pottery can be a fascinating hobby. From the intricate designs of Roseville to the charming simplicity of McCoy, the world of vintage pottery offers a wealth of beauty and history. In this blog post, we’ll explore the allure of collecting vintage pottery, delve into the stories behind Roseville and McCoy, and provide tips for starting your own collection.

The Allure of Vintage Pottery

Vintage pottery holds a special place in the hearts of collectors for several reasons. Firstly, each piece tells a story. From the skilled hands of the artisans to the historical context in which it was created, vintage pottery is a tangible link to the past. Whether it’s a delicate vase or a practical kitchenware item, these pieces have a unique charm that mass-produced items cannot replicate.

Secondly, vintage pottery offers a wide variety of styles and designs. From the elegant and ornate patterns of Roseville to the simple and rustic appeal of McCoy, there is something to suit every taste and aesthetic. Collectors can choose to focus on a specific brand, era, or design motif, allowing them to curate a collection that reflects their personal style.

The Stories Behind Roseville and McCoy

Roseville: The Epitome of Elegance

Roseville pottery, produced from the late 19th century to the mid-20th century, is renowned for its exquisite craftsmanship and intricate designs. The company’s success can be attributed to its talented team of artists, who created innovative and visually stunning pieces. From the floral-inspired patterns of the Zephyr Lily line to the Art Deco-inspired Futura line, Roseville pottery is a testament to the artistry and creativity of its makers.

McCoy: Simplicity with a Touch of Whimsy

In contrast to the elaborate designs of Roseville, McCoy pottery is known for its simplicity and charm. Produced from the early 20th century to the late 20th century, McCoy pieces were often characterized by their earthy colors and textured finishes. The company’s signature “cookie jar” designs, featuring whimsical animal and fruit motifs, became iconic and highly sought-after by collectors. McCoy pottery embodies a sense of nostalgia and warmth, evoking a simpler time.

Tips for Starting Your Collection

  1. Do Your Research: Before diving into the world of vintage pottery, take the time to educate yourself. Learn about the different brands, styles, and eras of pottery, and familiarize yourself with the distinguishing features of each. This will help you make informed decisions when purchasing pieces for your collection.

  2. Start Small: Begin your collection with a few key pieces that catch your eye. This will allow you to develop a sense of your preferences and refine your collecting focus. Remember, it’s quality over quantity.

  3. Attend Auctions and Estate Sales: These events can be treasure troves for vintage pottery collectors. Keep an eye out for upcoming auctions and estate sales in your area, as they often feature unique and rare pieces at reasonable prices.

  4. Connect with Fellow Collectors: Join online forums, social media groups, or local collector clubs to connect with other enthusiasts. Sharing knowledge and experiences with fellow collectors can enhance your understanding of vintage pottery and provide valuable insights.

  5. Care for Your Collection: Proper care is essential to preserve the beauty and value of your vintage pottery. Avoid exposing your pieces to direct sunlight or extreme temperature fluctuations, and handle them with care to prevent accidental damage.

Conclusion

Collecting vintage pottery is a rewarding and enriching hobby. From the elegant craftsmanship of Roseville to the whimsical charm of McCoy, each piece carries a unique story and adds character to any space. By exploring the world of vintage pottery, educating yourself, and connecting with fellow collectors, you can embark on a journey filled with history, beauty, and personal expression. Start your collection today and let the magic of vintage pottery captivate you.
