Hunting Gear for Game Calls: Choosing the Right Call


When it comes to hunting, the right gear can make all the difference between a successful outing and a frustrating one. One essential piece of hunting gear that often gets overlooked is the game call. Game calls are used to mimic the sounds of various animals, attracting them closer for a better shot. Choosing the right game call for your hunting needs can be a daunting task, but with the right knowledge, you can make an informed decision that will improve your hunting experience.

Understanding the Types of Game Calls

Before delving into the specifics of choosing the right game call, it’s essential to understand the different types available. There are several categories of game calls, each designed to imitate different animal sounds. These include:

1. Mouth Calls

Mouth calls, also known as diaphragm calls, are operated by the hunter’s breath and tongue. They are versatile and allow for hands-free use, making them ideal for hunters who need to remain mobile.

2. Electronic Calls

Electronic calls use pre-recorded sounds of animals and are operated by batteries. They offer a wide range of sounds and are particularly effective for luring in wary game from a distance.

3. Box Calls

Box calls produce sounds by sliding a small wooden lid across the top of the box. They are relatively easy to use and produce loud, realistic sounds that can carry over long distances.

4. Reed Calls

Reed calls, also known as friction calls, produce sounds by rubbing a striker against a metal or glass surface. They are popular for imitating the sounds of turkeys and other birds.

Factors to Consider When Choosing a Game Call

Now that you have an understanding of the different types of game calls, it’s time to consider the factors that will influence your decision when choosing the right call for your hunting needs.

1. Target Game

The type of game you are hunting will dictate the type of call you need. For example, if you are targeting waterfowl, a duck call would be the appropriate choice, while a deer call would be more suitable for deer hunting.

2. Versatility

Consider how versatile the call is. Some calls are designed to mimic a wide range of sounds, while others are more specialized. Depending on your hunting style, you may prefer a versatile call that can imitate multiple animals.

3. Durability

Look for a call that is durable and can withstand the rigors of outdoor use. The last thing you want is for your call to fail when you need it most.

4. Ease of Use

Some calls require more skill and practice to master than others. Consider your level of experience and choose a call that aligns with your abilities.

5. Sound Quality

The quality of the sound produced by the call is crucial. Look for a call that produces realistic and clear sounds to effectively attract game.

6. Range

Depending on the terrain you hunt in, you may need a call with a longer range to reach game at a distance. Consider the acoustics of your hunting environment when choosing a call.

Making the Right Choice

Now that you are armed with the knowledge of the types of game calls and the factors to consider, it’s time to make the right choice for your hunting gear. Take the time to research different brands and read reviews from fellow hunters to get an idea of the performance and reliability of various calls. Additionally, visiting a hunting supply store and testing out different calls can provide valuable hands-on experience to help you make an informed decision.

Remember, the right game call can be the difference between a successful hunting trip and a missed opportunity. By understanding the types of game calls available, considering the factors that influence your decision, and taking the time to make an informed choice, you can enhance your hunting experience and increase your chances of a successful hunt.

Happy hunting!
