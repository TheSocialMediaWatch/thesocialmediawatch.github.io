The Best Hiking Apps to Help You Find New Trails


Hiking is more than just a leisure activity; it’s an adventure into the natural world that offers exercise, tranquility, and a chance to escape the urban grind. Whether you are a beginner stepping out on local paths or a seasoned trekker looking for remote wilderness trails, the right hiking app can enrich your experience, enhance safety, and introduce you to trails you might never discover otherwise.

With today’s technology, finding new trails and navigating them has never been easier. A myriad of hiking apps on the market cater to various needs—from detailed topographic maps to trail reviews, real-time GPS navigation, and community features. This guide dives deep into the best hiking apps that will help you find new routes, plan your hikes, and stay safe on the trail.

Why Use a Hiking App?

Before we explore specific apps, it’s worth considering what benefits a hiking app offers:

  • Trail Discovery: Access thousands of trails ranging from easy strolls to challenging summits.
  • Navigation: Real-time GPS tracking helps prevent getting lost.
  • Offline Access: Many apps work offline, a boon in areas with poor cell coverage.
  • Trail Data: Elevation profiles, distance, difficulty ratings, and user reviews give meaningful insights.
  • Safety: Some apps include features like emergency alerts and location sharing.
  • Community and Sharing: Connect with other hikers, share your experiences, photos, and tips.

Whatever your hiking goals are, having an app dedicated to tackling new terrain adds a layer of preparation and confidence.

Top Hiking Apps to Find New Trails

1. AllTrails

Overview: AllTrails is arguably the most popular hiking app and for good reasons. It offers a massive database of over 200,000 trails worldwide—including walking paths, mountain hikes, and biking routes. Its user-friendly interface and rich community content make it an outstanding choice.

Key Features:

  • Extensive trail database with detailed maps.
  • User reviews, photos, and difficulty ratings.
  • GPS tracking with real-time stats.
  • Offline map access for Pro subscribers.
  • Filters for dog-friendly, kid-friendly, or wheelchair accessible trails.

Why It Stands Out: The active community regularly updates trail information, which helps you find hidden gems and avoid crowded or degraded paths. The Pro subscription unlocks advanced mapping tools and offline capabilities, which are invaluable on longer or remote hikes.

2. Gaia GPS

Overview: Gaia GPS excels as a navigation and mapping tool favored by backcountry adventurers and serious hikers. It provides topographic maps, satellite imagery, and overlays that deliver detailed terrain insight.

Key Features:

  • Offline map downloads with multiple layers (topo, satellite, road maps).
  • Route planning and tracking.
  • Integration with GPS devices.
  • Weather data and waypoints management.
  • Export and import GPX/KML files for custom routes.

Why It Stands Out: If you love venturing off the beaten path, Gaia GPS is your go-to app. The sophisticated maps and tools allow meticulous planning for complex trips, making it popular for mountaineers and multi-day backpackers.

3. Komoot

Overview: Komoot combines trail discovery with route planning for a variety of outdoor activities beyond hiking, including cycling and trail running. It shines in its customization capabilities and intuitive interface.

Key Features:

  • Personalized route planning based on fitness level and activity type.
  • Turn-by-turn voice navigation.
  • Detailed offline maps.
  • Highlights for points of interest like viewpoints, water sources, or scenic spots.
  • Seamless integration with smartwatches and bike computers.

Why It Stands Out: Its emphasis on personalized route creation allows you to tailor the hike precisely. Beginners appreciate the clear instructions, while experienced hikers benefit from rich recommendation algorithms.

4. ViewRanger (now part of Outdooractive)

Overview: ViewRanger has long been revered among outdoor enthusiasts for blending detailed topographic maps with social trail sharing. Recently merged with Outdooractive, the combined platform offers even broader coverage and features.

Key Features:

  • Extensive trail library and worldwide maps.
  • Augmented reality (Skyline) that identifies peaks and landmarks via camera view.
  • Offline use and route recording.
  • Safety feature to share real-time location with contacts.
  • Community sharing of GPS tracks and photos.

Why It Stands Out: Skyline AR technology enhances educational and exploratory aspects by helping hikers identify distant peaks or waypoints, making it interactive and fun during hikes.

5. Trailforks

Overview: Originally designed for mountain bikers, Trailforks has become a reliable resource for hikers too, focusing on detailed trail data with user contributions.

Key Features:

  • Extensive trail maps with detailed layers showing terrain, trail conditions, and closures.
  • User reports for real-time trail status updates.
  • Elevation profiles and trail difficulty ratings.
  • Ability to download offline maps.
  • Filters to pick trails by type, difficulty, and condition.

Why It Stands Out: The app’s focus on crowd-sourced updates keeps trail condition info current, crucial for planning safe outings, especially when weather impacts trails.

6. Hiking Project

Overview: Hiking Project is a crowd-sourced hiking guide brought to you by REI, aimed at helping hikers locate trails across the United States and beyond.

Key Features:

  • Community-maintained trail information.
  • Detailed trail maps and segments.
  • Elevation profiles, length, and difficulty ratings.
  • Photos and reviews.
  • Offline map availability.

Why It Stands Out: Its extensive regional trail coverage and REI’s trusted reputation give the app credibility. Perfect for hikers who want comprehensive trail facts and insights from fellow outdoor lovers.

7. PeakFinder

Overview: For hikers who pursue mountain summits and panoramic views, PeakFinder enhances the experience by identifying visible peaks from your vantage point.

Key Features:

  • Works offline.
  • Database of over 900,000 peaks worldwide.
  • Interactive panoramic camera that labels mountain names.
  • Adds a deeper layer of appreciation to scenic hikes.

Why It Stands Out: While not a trail finder per se, PeakFinder complements any hiking session with stunning educational value, making it an excellent tool for mountainous trail explorers.

Choosing the Right Hiking App for You

While all these apps offer tremendous value, your ideal choice depends on how and where you hike:

  • Casual or social hikers: AllTrails and Hiking Project boast rich community engagement and a broad database for varied trail options.
  • Backcountry explorers: Gaia GPS and ViewRanger provide advanced mapping vital for complex or wilderness expeditions.
  • Custom route planners: Komoot offers personalized navigation and seamless multi-sport usage.
  • Safety-conscious adventurers: Apps with real-time location sharing and offline maps such as Outdooractive (ViewRanger) provide peace of mind.
  • Mountain peak enthusiasts: Combine your hiking app with PeakFinder for visualizing and identifying summit views.

Most of these apps offer free versions with optional premium features, so you can test multiple before settling on your favorite.

Maximize Your Hiking Experience with These Tips

  1. Download maps offline: Connectivity can be unreliable on trails, so ensure your app supports offline access.
  2. Read recent trail reviews: Weather or seasonal changes might alter trail conditions.
  3. Keep your phone charged: Carry a portable charger to ensure your GPS and trail app remain functional.
  4. Plan for emergencies: Share your route with someone and familiarize yourself with your app’s emergency features.
  5. Combine apps: For top-notch trekking, use a trail finder alongside a peak identifier or weather app.
  6. Contribute back: Leave reviews, upload photos, and report trail conditions to help the community.

Final Thoughts

Technology is transforming the way we explore the outdoors. By leveraging the power of hiking apps, you can discover fresh trails, navigate safer routes, and enrich your connection with nature. Whether it’s a peaceful riverside walk, intense mountain hike, or multi-day backpacking trip, these tools empower you to get outside more confidently and adventurously.

So grab your boots, download an app, and hit the trail with fresh enthusiasm. The next unforgettable journey into the wilderness is just a tap away.
