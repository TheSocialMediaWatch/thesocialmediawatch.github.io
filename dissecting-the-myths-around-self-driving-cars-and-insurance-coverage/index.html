Dissecting the Myths around Self-Driving Cars and Insurance Coverage


The advent of self-driving cars brings in a new era of technological advancement in the automotive industry. While their ability to roam highways and byways autonomously is impressive, a more complex layer developing under the surface is the issue of insurance coverage. The insurance industry is caught in the throes of grappling with how to cover self-driving vehicles. We often hear debates ranging from insurance coverage for autonomous vehicles, to liabilities and more. In this article, we will dissect those myths revolving around self-driving cars and insurance coverage.

1. Myth 1: “Self-Driving Cars Will Make Insurance Obsolete”

Not so fast! The idea that self-driving cars will render insurance obsolete is an overstatement. While the technology is designed to prevent collisions, human error, and other factors still play a part in automobile accidents. Added to this, as these vehicles become more mainstream, we can expect a new set of problems and mechanical failures that require insurance. So, while the frequency of accidents may decrease, the need for insurance definitely lingers.

2. Myth 2: “Who Will Be at Fault in an Accident, Humans or Machines?”

This is a tricky question, as accidents are quite unpredictable. In a human-driven car, the driver is usually deemed at fault in most cases. But with self-driving cars, there are third-party liability issues. Claims could potentially be brought against the vehicle manufacturer, software engineers, or car owners, depending on the circumstances of the accident. Technology manufacturers will need to invest more in robust insurance coverage as a means of protection.

3. Myth 3: “Rates Will Plummet with Autonomous Vehicles”

Quite the contrary, insurance rates have the potential to increase. As insuring robots becomes a necessity, underwriters must develop sophisticated risk models. Each company will have to address various variables such as the complexity of software, driving history, maintenance history, and many other technical factors. So, while it may be tempting for people to believe that widespread autonomous ownership will decrease the cost of their insurance policy, in reality, insuring a self-driving car may develop into quite a challenging and expensive endeavor.

4. Myth 4: “All Autonomous Cars need is Digital Licenses, No Physical Plates”

This scenario might sound interesting in theory, but it contradicts fundamental principles of insurance. Physical plates are a unique identifier for each vehicle, thus pivotal for insurance coverage to know which policy to apply when an accident occurs. You can’t simply not have a physical license plate and still be insured. Hence, it’s improbable this will change anytime soon.

5. Myth 5: “The Driver is Always Covered, or Not Covered, Regardless of Driving Conditions”

Wrong! There are premiums tied to different driving scenarios. While an increased risk might see a bump in premiums, there is no suggestion that the insurance market will be willing to disregard some form of distinction based on the conditions or context of a specific driver’s actions.

In summary, the advent of self-driving cars brings forth an entirely different landscape of policy provisions, more so than we are currently experiencing. Although these innovations bring their benefits, careful considerations need to be given to the insurance coverage of self-driving cars as we embrace this new wave of technology. As the technology evolves, the insurance industry will also indeed have to march to the beat of its drum, develop alongside, and adapt. It’s upon us to be open to changes in the way we think of what it means to be covered under the vast umbrella of insurance – it’s not only about us anymore, but also our machines.
