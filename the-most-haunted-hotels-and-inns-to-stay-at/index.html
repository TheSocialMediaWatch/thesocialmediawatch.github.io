The Most Haunted Hotels and Inns to Stay At


When it comes to traveling, some people seek adventure, relaxation, and new experiences, while others crave something more eerie—ghosts, spirits, and the supernatural. If you’re one of the brave souls who wants to experience the thrill of a haunted stay, these hotels and inns will send shivers down your spine. Whether you’re a seasoned paranormal investigator or simply enjoy the idea of spending the night in a place with a dark history, these haunted spots promise more than just a good night’s rest.

1. The Stanley Hotel – Estes Park, Colorado

Widely known for inspiring Stephen King’s The Shining, The Stanley Hotel is one of the most famous haunted hotels in the United States. Built in 1909 by F.O. Stanley, the grand hotel has a rich history, with tales of ghostly sightings and eerie occurrences. Guests report hearing piano music playing when no one is around, doors that open and close by themselves, and even sightings of the ghostly figures of former guests.

The most famous spirit is that of Lucy, a former housekeeper who is said to still wander the halls, particularly in Room 217, where she once worked. Many guests have reported strange occurrences in this room, including sudden temperature drops, unexplained noises, and a feeling of being watched.

Aside from Lucy, several other spirits haunt the hotel. The sound of children’s laughter is often heard in the hallways, particularly in the winter months when guests can no longer access the outdoor pool. The Stanley Hotel also offers ghost tours where you can learn about its haunted history and explore some of its most eerie locations.

2. The Queen Mary – Long Beach, California

Docked permanently in Long Beach, California, the Queen Mary was once a luxurious ocean liner that sailed the seas from 1936 to 1967. Today, it’s a hotel and museum, but it’s also one of the most haunted places in the world. During its years of service, the ship saw its fair share of tragedies, including deaths, accidents, and even a mysterious fire. These events, some of which occurred during wartime, have left a dark imprint on the ship’s history.

Guests who stay on the Queen Mary often report strange occurrences like sudden temperature changes, ghostly apparitions, and the sound of unexplained footsteps in the halls. One of the most frequently reported hauntings is that of a young girl named Jackie, who is believed to have drowned in the ship’s pool. Her ghost has been seen near the pool area, and her playful spirit is often blamed for mischievous happenings around the ship.

There are also reports of a malevolent entity known as the “Engine Room Ghost,” believed to be the spirit of a crew member who perished in an accident. Visitors often claim to feel a heavy, unsettling presence in the engine room, and some have even experienced physical contact from unseen hands.

3. The Myrtles Plantation – St. Francisville, Louisiana

Located in the heart of Louisiana, The Myrtles Plantation is one of the most haunted places in the South. The plantation was built in 1796, and its history is riddled with tragedy, including murders, betrayal, and slavery. One of the most infamous stories associated with the plantation is that of a woman named Chloe, a slave who allegedly poisoned the owner’s family.

Chloe’s spirit is said to still haunt the plantation, along with the ghosts of other former residents. Guests have reported seeing her apparition, along with sightings of other spirits who wander the grounds. One of the most well-known paranormal occurrences at the Myrtles is the so-called “ghost children” who have been seen running and playing on the property. Some guests have even reported hearing children’s laughter or footsteps when no children are present.

Room 214 is particularly famous for its hauntings, with many visitors claiming to have felt the presence of spirits during their stay. The plantation offers ghost tours where you can learn about its dark past and visit some of the most haunted spots on the property.

4. The Olde Pink House – Savannah, Georgia

Savannah, Georgia, is known for its charming, historic buildings, but it’s also home to some of the most haunted locations in the country. One such location is The Olde Pink House, a former residence that dates back to 1771. The house has served as a tavern, restaurant, and even a bank, but it’s the hauntings that have made it famous.

The most well-known spirit at The Olde Pink House is that of James Habersham Jr., a wealthy plantation owner who died in the house in the early 1800s. Guests often report seeing his apparition in the upper floors of the building, where he is believed to have passed away. There are also reports of a woman in a white dress who is believed to be the spirit of a former servant who died tragically on the property.

In addition to these apparitions, many guests have experienced strange sounds, including footsteps and voices, and cold spots that seem to appear out of nowhere. The house’s eerie atmosphere, combined with its fascinating history, makes it a must-visit for anyone interested in the supernatural.

5. The Chateau de Brissac – Brissac-Quincé, France

If you’re looking for a truly spooky experience in Europe, look no further than the Chateau de Brissac in France. This majestic castle, which dates back to the 11th century, is one of the most haunted places in the country. The most famous ghost here is that of Charlotte de Brissac, the wife of the castle’s owner, who was tragically murdered by her husband after discovering that he had been unfaithful.

Charlotte’s spirit is said to haunt the castle, particularly the tower where she was killed. Guests have reported seeing her apparition, often dressed in a flowing green gown, and hearing her weeping in the hallways. Her ghost is said to be both tragic and vengeful, as she wanders the castle seeking justice for her untimely death.

In addition to Charlotte, there are other spirits that are said to haunt the castle, including former residents and even guests who stayed at the castle throughout its long history. The eerie atmosphere, combined with the rich history of the chateau, makes it an ideal spot for those looking for a haunted adventure.

6. The Ridges – Athens, Ohio

The Ridges, located on the campus of Ohio University, was originally built as a mental asylum in 1868. Over the years, the asylum saw many patients, some of whom were subjected to harsh treatments and lived out their final days within its walls. It’s no wonder, then, that this location has earned a reputation for being haunted.

Guests who stay in the nearby area often report hearing strange noises, such as whispers and footsteps, and some have claimed to see the apparitions of former patients who haunt the halls. One of the most famous ghosts is that of a patient named Margaret Schilling, who vanished in 1978 and was later found dead in a room in the building. Her spirit is said to haunt the halls, and visitors have reported seeing her ghostly figure near the site of her death.

In addition to Margaret, there are other reports of paranormal activity, including doors that open and close on their own and sudden temperature drops. The Ridges is an eerie place to visit, especially for those interested in the supernatural and the history of mental asylums.

7. The Hawthorne Hotel – Salem, Massachusetts

Salem, Massachusetts, is infamous for its witch trials, and the Hawthorne Hotel, built in 1925, is one of the city’s most haunted locations. The hotel is named after Nathaniel Hawthorne, the author of The Scarlet Letter, whose ancestors were involved in the witch trials.

Guests at the Hawthorne Hotel have reported hearing strange sounds, including footsteps and whispers, and some have even seen the apparitions of former guests. One of the most famous hauntings is that of a woman in a long, dark gown who is believed to be the spirit of a former hotel guest who died mysteriously in the building.

The hotel also offers ghost tours, where visitors can learn about the history of the hotel and the paranormal activity that has been reported over the years. If you’re looking for a haunted stay in a city known for its dark past, the Hawthorne Hotel is a must-visit destination.

8. The Biltmore Hotel – Coral Gables, Florida

The Biltmore Hotel in Coral Gables, Florida, is a luxurious hotel with a rich history, but it also has a reputation for being haunted. Built in 1926, the hotel has seen a number of famous guests, including presidents, actors, and royalty. However, it also has a dark side, with reports of strange occurrences and ghostly sightings.

One of the most famous hauntings at the Biltmore is that of a former guest who died in the hotel under mysterious circumstances. Guests have reported seeing his ghostly figure walking through the hotel’s halls, often near the area where he passed away. There have also been reports of cold spots, unexplainable sounds, and objects moving on their own.

Despite its ghostly reputation, the Biltmore remains a popular destination for travelers looking for luxury and a little bit of the supernatural.

Conclusion

For those with a love of the supernatural and a thirst for the unknown, these haunted hotels and inns offer a unique opportunity to experience a piece of history while encountering the eerie and unexplained. Whether you’re drawn to ghostly apparitions, strange sounds, or chilling stories, these haunted locations promise a spine-tingling stay that you won’t soon forget. Whether you’re in search of a ghostly encounter or simply fascinated by the history behind these mysterious places, a night in one of these haunted hotels is sure to be an unforgettable experience.
