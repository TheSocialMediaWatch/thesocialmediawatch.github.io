Top 10 Underrated Cartoons You Should Watch


As a cartoon enthusiast, I’ve spent countless hours scouring through various platforms, from classic VHS tapes to modern streaming services, in search of hidden gems that often get overlooked. In this post, I’ll share my top 10 picks for underrated cartoons that deserve your attention. From nostalgic throwbacks to innovative new series, these shows will transport you to a world of imagination, excitement, and emotional depth.

10. The Venture Bros. (2004-2018)

This adult-oriented animated series follows the adventures of the Venture family, a dysfunctional family of superheroes and villains. With its clever writing, complex characters, and pop culture references, The Venture Bros. is a must-watch for fans of dark humor and satire.

9. Gankutsuou: The Count of Monte Cristo (2004-2005)

This Japanese anime series reimagines Alexandre Dumas’ classic novel The Count of Monte Cristo in a futuristic, steampunk setting. With its unique blend of science fiction and historical drama, Gankutsuou offers a fresh take on a timeless tale.

8. The Grim Adventures of Billy and Mandy (2001-2007)

This Cartoon Network series follows the misadventures of two curious children, Billy and Mandy, and their Grim Reaper friend, who becomes their reluctant companion. With its blend of humor, adventure, and dark fantasy, The Grim Adventures is a cult classic that has gained a loyal following.

7. Ed, Edd n Eddy (1999-2009)

This Cartoon Network series follows the misadventures of three friends, Ed, Edd, and Eddy, who try to scam their way to riches in their suburban neighborhood. With its quirky humor, relatable characters, and nostalgic value, Ed, Edd n Eddy is a beloved classic that still holds up today.

6. Mushi-shi (2005-2008)

This Japanese anime series explores the world of supernatural creatures, known as Mushi, and their interactions with humans. With its slow-paced, atmospheric storytelling and beautiful animation, Mushi-shi is a thought-provoking and visually stunning series that appeals to fans of psychological horror and fantasy.

5. The Amazing World of Gumball (2011-2019)

This British-American animated series follows the misadventures of a cat named Gumball and his family in the fictional town of Elmore. With its blend of humor, satire, and emotional depth, The Amazing World of Gumball is a critically acclaimed series that explores complex themes and relationships.

4. Samurai Jack (2001-2004, 2017)

This Adult Swim series follows the adventures of a samurai warrior, Jack, who is transported to a dystopian future and must navigate a strange and unfamiliar world. With its stunning animation, thought-provoking themes, and epic storytelling, Samurai Jack is a masterpiece of adult animation.

3. Adventure Time (2010-2018)

This Cartoon Network series follows the adventures of Finn, a human boy, and his best friend Jake, a dog with magical powers, in the post-apocalyptic Land of Ooo. With its unique blend of humor, drama, and fantasy, Adventure Time has become a cult classic and a staple of modern animation.

2. Over the Garden Wall (2014)

This miniseries follows two brothers, Wirt and Greg, who become lost in a mysterious forest and must find their way home. With its haunting atmosphere, beautiful animation, and poignant storytelling, Over the Garden Wall is a critically acclaimed series that explores themes of childhood, family, and identity.

1. BoJack Horseman (2014-2020)

This Netflix series follows the life of BoJack, a washed-up actor who starred in a popular 90s sitcom, and his struggles with depression, addiction, and existential crises. With its dark humor, complex characters, and thought-provoking themes, BoJack Horseman is a critically acclaimed series that has become a benchmark for adult animation.

These underrated cartoons offer a mix of humor, adventure, and emotional depth that will appeal to fans of animation and storytelling. Whether you’re a nostalgic throwback or a newcomer to the world of cartoons, these hidden gems are sure to captivate and inspire. So, next time you’re browsing through streaming services or scouring through VHS tapes, be sure to give these underrated cartoons a chance. You might just discover your new favorite show.
