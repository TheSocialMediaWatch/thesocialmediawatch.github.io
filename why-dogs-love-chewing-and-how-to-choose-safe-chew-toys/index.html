Why Dogs Love Chewing and How to Choose Safe Chew Toys


Chewing is one of the most instinctual and satisfying behaviors for dogs, deeply rooted in their nature. To many pet owners, the act of chewing might seem like an amusing quirk or even a nuisance when it leads to damaged shoes and furniture. However, understanding why dogs are so drawn to chewing and knowing how to select safe toys for them can create harmony between you and your furry companion, while ensuring their health and happiness.

The Instinct Behind Chewing

Chewing isn’t simply an idle pastime for dogs; it’s part of their biological and psychological makeup. Wolves, the closest ancestors of modern dogs, engage in chewing as part of their survival activities, whether it’s consuming prey or handling tough bones. This innate behavior has been passed down through generations, making chewing a natural outlet for dogs in addressing various needs.

Here’s a closer look at why dogs love chewing:

  1. Exploration and Curiosity
    Puppies, in particular, use their mouth as a way to discover the world. Much like human infants, they explore their surroundings by tasting, sniffing, and touching with their mouths. Chewing different objects allows them to satisfy their curiosity and learn about textures, resistance, and their environment.

  2. Stress Relief
    Chewing can be soothing for dogs who feel stressed, anxious, or bored. The repetitive motion of biting and gnawing on objects releases pent-up energy and produces relaxation through the release of endorphins. It can also provide mental stimulation, helping dogs manage both boredom and nervousness.

  3. Oral Health
    Chewing can play a role in your dog’s dental care, as it naturally helps scrape away plaque and tartar buildup on teeth. Dogs who enjoy chewing regularly on appropriate toys often have better oral hygiene and less risk of periodontal diseases.

  4. Teething in Puppies
    During the teething phase, puppies experience discomfort as their baby teeth fall out and adult teeth come in. Chewing provides relief from the soreness of teething by massaging their gums and reducing irritation.

  5. Entertainment and Satisfaction
    Some dogs chew simply because it’s comforting and fun. Different textures and flavors can engage them for hours, keeping them entertained and fulfilled. Dogs who do not receive enough physical exercise or mental enrichment may exhibit destructive chewing habits as a way to express their need for stimulation.

The Importance of Choosing Safe Chew Toys

While chewing brings immense benefits to your dog’s well-being, it’s equally important to ensure that their toys are safe. Not all chew toys are created equal, and selecting the wrong item can lead to health hazards, choking risks, or ingested fragments that cause digestive obstruction. Prioritizing safety alongside enjoyment will keep your dog happy and healthy.

Here’s what to consider when picking chew toys:

  1. Durability and Material
    Chew toys need to be resilient enough to withstand powerful jaws yet pliable enough that they won’t break into shards under pressure. Hard plastic toys or brittle materials can splinter, leading to an injury in the mouth or digestive system. Look for high-quality rubber or nylon toys designed for heavy chewers, as these materials are typically safer and longer-lasting.

  2. Size Matters
    Always select a toy that is appropriate for your dog’s size and breed. Toys that are too small may pose a choking hazard, while oversized toys can be difficult for smaller dogs to chew. A general rule of thumb is to choose a toy that is large enough that your dog can’t entirely fit it into their mouth but small enough they can comfortably grip it.

  3. Avoid Toxic Additives
    Some chew toys, especially poorly manufactured ones, may contain harmful chemicals like phthalates or lead. When shopping, ensure the toy is labeled as non-toxic and safe for pets. Checking the manufacturer’s reputation and reviews can also reassure you about product safety.

  4. Interactive Features
    Many chew toys are designed with interactive components, such as ridges for dental care, squeakers for added entertainment, or compartments for treats. These features can make toys more engaging and mentally stimulating, ensuring your dog remains occupied longer while also benefiting from bonus functionalities like improved oral hygiene.

  5. Age-Specific Toys
    Puppies, adult dogs, and seniors have distinct chewing needs. For example, teething toys made from softer materials are ideal for young puppies, while sturdier toys can withstand the full chewing power of an adult dog. Senior dogs may prefer gentler options to accommodate any dental sensitivities they develop with age.

  6. Supervision and Rotation
    No matter how safe a toy seems, it’s important to monitor your dog while they chew to ensure they don’t break off large pieces or misuse the toy. Rotating their toys regularly can also keep them engaged and prevent boredom while extending the lifespan of each toy.

Chewing Alternatives to Avoid Destructive Behavior

Providing safe chew toys is essential to preventing destructive chewing of household items. Dogs who don’t have proper outlets for their chewing instinct may turn their attention to furniture, shoes, or other objects. To redirect them to appropriate behavior, consider these strategies in addition to introducing designated chew toys:

  • Training: Teach your dog the “leave it” command to dissuade them from chewing inappropriate items. Offer praise and rewards when they opt to play with their toys instead.
  • Exercise: Physical activity is one of the best ways to ward off boredom and destructive habits. Daily walks, runs, or games of fetch can expend your dog’s energy, making them less likely to resort to unwanted behaviors.
  • Mental Enrichment: Puzzle toys or treat-dispensing toys provide modified chewing activities paired with cognitive stimulation that keeps dogs occupied while satisfying their instincts.

Chew Toys vs. Chew Bones: Which is Better?

Many dog owners wonder whether natural bones or chew toys are safer for their pets. While raw bones can provide excellent dental benefits and satisfy chewing instincts, they come with risks. Splintered bones can puncture the digestive tract, and cooked bones are especially dangerous. Should you choose to offer natural bones, ensure they are large, raw, and appropriately sized for your dog.

Chew toys, on the other hand, are specifically designed to accommodate safety and consistent chewing habits. They can be a better long-term option for most dogs, given their durability and variety of textures, shapes, and functions.

Signs of Unsafe Chewing

It’s crucial to recognize signs that something may be wrong during chewing. Watch for the following red flags:

  • Excessive wear or breakage of the toy
  • Behavioral changes such as guarding or aggression around the toy
  • Signs of oral injury, including bleeding gums or chipped teeth
  • Vomiting or difficulty passing swallowed fragments

Promptly remove any damaged toy and consult your veterinarian if ingestion or injury occurs.

Conclusion

Chewing is an integral part of a dog’s life—a behavior that satisfies their instincts, promotes their health, and keeps them entertained. By understanding why dogs love to chew and embracing their natural desires, you can better support their needs with safe, appropriate chew toys tailored for their age, size, and personality.

Providing your canine companion with well-chosen toys will save your belongings from destruction, foster their mental and physical health, and reinforce the bond you share. With patience and care, you’ll find the ideal balance between nurturing their love for chewing and ensuring their safety.
