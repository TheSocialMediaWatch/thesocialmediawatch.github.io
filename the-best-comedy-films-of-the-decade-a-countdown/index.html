The Best Comedy Films of the Decade: A Countdown


Laughter is often considered the best medicine, and over the past decade, filmmakers have crafted comedic gems that have kept audiences rolling in the aisles. Comedy, as a genre, is both universal and deeply personal, with humor reflecting cultural nuances, social dynamics, and even existential musings. The past ten years have seen a diverse range of comedies—from sharp satire to laugh-out-loud absurdity—that have left an indelible mark on cinema. Here, we count down some of the best comedy films of the decade, exploring their impact, narratives, and that ineffable magic that makes them so unforgettable.

10. “Game Night” (2018)

What starts as a light-hearted weekly group activity quickly spirals into sheer chaos in Game Night. Directed by John Francis Daley and Jonathan Goldstein, the film combines clever storytelling with razor-sharp humor to deliver one of the most entertaining movies of recent years. The story centers on a couple, played by Jason Bateman and Rachel McAdams, hosting their usual game night with friends—only to find themselves entangled in a real-life mystery involving kidnapping and desperate criminals.

The film thrives on fast-paced wit, physical comedy, and strong ensemble performances. Bateman’s dry humor pairs effortlessly with McAdams’ charm, while standout supporting roles—including Jesse Plemons as the hilariously unsettling neighbor—make Game Night a true comic delight. Its blend of suspense and comedy offers just the right amount of unpredictability, ensuring viewers laugh while staying glued to the unfolding drama.

9. “Booksmart” (2019)

Olivia Wilde’s directorial debut, Booksmart, reimagines the coming-of-age story with a fresh lens and gives audiences a heartwarming yet hysterical ride. The film follows two academically gifted high school seniors, Molly (Beanie Feldstein) and Amy (Kaitlyn Dever), who realize they’ve spent their teenage years too focused on grades, neglecting the carefree fun of adolescence. Determined to cram years of partying into one wild night, they embark on a misadventure that’s as relatable as it is hilarious.

What sets Booksmart apart is its ability to combine raunchy humor with sincere emotional depth. The earnest friendship between Molly and Amy feels authentic, and the razor-sharp dialogue crackles with energy. Add in memorable supporting characters and brilliant comedic timing, and Wilde’s film delivers a perfect synthesis of heart and hilarity, resonating strongly with viewers of all ages.

8. “Jojo Rabbit” (2019)

Taika Waititi’s Jojo Rabbit defied convention and emerged as one of the boldest dark comedies of the decade. The story revolves around Jojo, a 10-year-old boy in Nazi Germany whose imaginary friend happens to be a caricatured version of Adolf Hitler, played by Waititi himself. As Jojo grapples with indoctrination and his understanding of the world, he discovers his mother (Scarlett Johansson) is hiding a Jewish girl in their home, which flips his worldview upside down.

The audacity of blending delicate themes—war, fanaticism, and prejudice—with humor is what makes Jojo Rabbit so remarkable. Waititi expertly balances absurd visual gags and biting satire with heartfelt moments, creating a comedy that challenges perceptions while evoking both laughter and tears. It’s the perfect example of how comedy can serve as a vessel for deeper social commentary.

7. “Crazy Rich Asians” (2018)

Romantic comedies rarely gain the kind of universal acclaim that Crazy Rich Asians did, and for good reason. Directed by Jon M. Chu and based on Kevin Kwan’s novel, the film centers on New Yorker Rachel Chu (Constance Wu) accompanying her boyfriend Nick Young (Henry Golding) to his best friend’s wedding in Singapore—only to discover Nick’s family is obscenely wealthy, with a penchant for grandeur and meddling drama.

From jaw-dropping visuals to hilarious cultural clashes, Crazy Rich Asians fuses romance with humor in a way that feels fresh and authentic. Awkwafina’s scene-stealing comedic performance as Rachel’s best friend provides non-stop laughs, while Michelle Yeoh’s icy matriarch character adds layers of tension to the humor. This film was more than just comedy; it was a breakthrough moment for Asian representation in mainstream Hollywood, making its success all the more significant.

6. “Deadpool” (2016)

Superhero films aren’t often synonymous with comedy, but Deadpool turned that assumption on its head. Directed by Tim Miller and starring Ryan Reynolds as the titular anti-hero, the film unapologetically embraces its raunchy, fourth-wall-breaking, meta-humor style while lampooning traditional superhero tropes.

The endlessly quotable script, peppered with self-aware and absurd humor, ensures Deadpool isn’t just a superhero movie—it’s a stand-up comedy act in cinematic form. Reynolds’ impeccable comedic timing and delivery breathed life into a character that felt tailor-made for him. Whether you’re laughing at Deadpool’s crude jokes or marveling at his ridiculous antics, this film redefined what was possible within the superhero genre.

5. “The Grand Budapest Hotel” (2014)

Wes Anderson’s The Grand Budapest Hotel is a masterclass in dry humor and quirky storytelling. Set in the fictional European Alps, the story follows Gustave H., a charming yet eccentric concierge played impeccably by Ralph Fiennes, and his loyal lobby boy, Zero. Together, they navigate wrongful accusations, stolen paintings, and absurd murder mysteries in Anderson’s signature whimsical style.

Teeming with visual eccentricities, deadpan humor, and memorable characters, The Grand Budapest Hotel effortlessly blends slapstick comedy with artistic brilliance. Anderson’s direction infuses every frame with charm and wit, and Fiennes’ comedic prowess steals the show. It’s a comedy that is equal parts aesthetically stunning and delightfully odd.

4. “Thor: Ragnarok” (2017)

Taika Waititi makes another appearance on this list, this time for turning the often-serious Thor franchise into a laugh-out-loud spectacle. Thor: Ragnarok reinvents its titular character as a charismatic, self-deprecating hero trapped in the midst of chaos. With Waititi’s trademark humor and imaginative vision, the film finds Thor teaming up with Hulk (Mark Ruffalo), Valkyrie (Tessa Thompson), and Loki (Tom Hiddleston) to save Asgard from Hela (Cate Blanchett).

The comedic chemistry between the cast, paired with Waititi’s absurd yet brilliant direction, transforms Thor: Ragnarok into one of Marvel’s funniest movies. From hilarious quips to zany action sequences, this film strips away pretentiousness to embrace the joy of storytelling.

3. “The Nice Guys” (2016)

Shane Black’s The Nice Guys delivers a clever, action-packed buddy comedy reminiscent of classic noir films—and it’s every bit as entertaining as it sounds. Set in 1970s Los Angeles, the movie pairs Russell Crowe’s brutish enforcer with Ryan Gosling’s bumbling private investigator as they unravel a conspiracy involving adult films and corporate greed.

The chemistry between Gosling and Crowe is electric, with Gosling showcasing a comedic range many audiences had never seen before. Black’s signature snappy dialogue and unconventional narrative structure ensure that The Nice Guys strikes the perfect balance between thrilling and hilarious.

2. “Knives Out” (2019)

Rian Johnson’s Knives Out may technically be a murder mystery, but its wickedly humorous tone and sharp writing make it one of the finest comedic films of the decade. After renowned crime novelist Harlan Thrombey is found dead under mysterious circumstances, eccentric private detective Benoit Blanc (played brilliantly by Daniel Craig) is called in to unravel the web of deceit within the dysfunctional Thrombey family.

Johnson’s witty script creates comedy from the sheer absurdity of the characters’ entitlement, greed, and cluelessness. With an impressive ensemble cast that includes Ana de Armas, Chris Evans, and Jamie Lee Curtis, Knives Out is a gleeful satire masquerading as a whodunit, and it delivers nonstop entertainment from start to finish.

1. “Ted” (2012)

Seth MacFarlane’s Ted remains one of the most iconic comedy films of the past decade, primarily for its ability to blend raunchy humor with heartfelt storytelling. Centered on a man (played by Mark Wahlberg) and his vulgar, foul-mouthed talking teddy bear, this hilariously bizarre concept turned into comedic gold.

The chemistry between Wahlberg and the CGI-animated bear, voiced by MacFarlane, is truly standout, propelling the film into an unforgettable ride filled with outrageous moments. Whether it’s Ted’s mischievous antics or the ridiculous conversations that feel both inappropriate and endearing, this movie carved its niche in pop culture and continues to be a comedy fans return to for unbridled laughter.

Comedy has the unique ability to transcend barriers and bring people together through shared moments of joy. Whether through clever dialogue, absurd situations, or heartwarming connections, the films above have left a lasting legacy for audiences of all kinds. Each offered a glimpse into humanity’s love for laughter, reminding us to find humor in even the simplest of things. What are your favorites of the decade? Let the countdown inspire your next movie night!
