10 Healthy and Easy Vegan Meal Prep Recipes


Meal prepping is a game-changer for anyone looking to simplify their week, eat healthier, and stick to a plant-based diet. Whether you’re just starting out or looking to refresh your recipe rotation, these ten healthy and easy vegan meal prep recipes are here to fuel your days and free up your evenings. Each dish is nutrient-dense, easy to store, and perfect for busy schedules. Let’s jump right in and set you up with a week’s worth of delicious, hassle-free vegan meals—no endless shopping lists or complex techniques required.

1. Chickpea Salad Mason Jars

Ditch boring salads and embrace a rainbow of flavors with these mason jar salads.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, rinsed and drained
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cucumber, chopped
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cups baby spinach
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp mustard
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, mustard, salt, and pepper for the dressing.
  2. Layer dressing at the bottom of each mason jar.
  3. Add chickpeas, onion, tomatoes, bell pepper, cucumber, and finally spinach.
  4. Seal and refrigerate. Shake before serving.

2. Creamy Tahini Stir-Fry Noodles

Hearty, satisfying, and bursting with savory goodness. Perfect hot or cold.

Ingredients:

  • 8 ounces whole wheat noodles (soba or rice noodles)
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1 large carrot, julienned

Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup tahini
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1 tsp grated fresh ginger
  • 2 tbsp warm water

Instructions:

  1. Cook noodles according to package directions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté broccoli, bell pepper, snap peas, and carrot in a large skillet for 5-6 minutes.
  3. Whisk together sauce ingredients.
  4. Toss noodles and veggies in the tahini sauce.
  5. Divide into containers and store for up to 4 days.

3. Southwest Quinoa Burrito Bowls

Packed with protein and fiber—these colorful bowls make lunchtime exciting.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels
  • 1 red onion, diced
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 avocado, sliced (add fresh daily for best results)
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • Juice of 1 lime

Instructions:

  1. Rinse quinoa and simmer with water until fluffy, about 15 minutes.
  2. In a large bowl, mix quinoa, black beans, corn, onion, and tomatoes.
  3. Season with chili powder, cumin, lime juice, salt, and pepper.
  4. Pack into containers. Add avocado right before eating.

4. Sweet Potato and Red Lentil Curry

Warm, comforting, and perfect for make-ahead lunch or dinner.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tbsp curry powder
  • 2 sweet potatoes, diced
  • 1 cup red lentils, rinsed
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 can coconut milk
  • 2 cups spinach

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a pot. Sauté onion and garlic until soft.
  2. Stir in curry powder, cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add sweet potato, lentils, and broth. Simmer 20 minutes.
  4. Stir in coconut milk and spinach. Simmer 10 more minutes.
  5. Cool and pack into containers for up to 5 days.

5. Mediterranean Chickpea Wraps

Minimal prep and maximum flavor. Great for grab-and-go lunches.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, mashed
  • 1/4 cup vegan mayo or hummus
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup diced cucumber
  • 1/4 cup diced red onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp chopped parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Whole wheat wraps or large collard leaves

Instructions:

  1. Combine chickpeas, mayo/hummus, lemon juice, cucumber, onion, tomatoes, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  2. Spread mixture onto wraps or leaves.
  3. Roll, slice, and refrigerate for up to 3 days.

6. Baked Falafel Power Bowls

Enjoy this oven-baked version of the Middle Eastern classic, great for meal prepping.

Ingredients:

  • 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 1 cup fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup oats
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 tsp coriander
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

For the bowls:

  • Cooked brown rice or quinoa
  • Fresh greens
  • Cherry tomatoes, cucumbers, and tahini sauce

Instructions:

  1. Pulse falafel ingredients in a food processor until combined but still textured.
  2. Form into balls, place on baking sheet. Brush with olive oil.
  3. Bake at 400°F for 25 minutes, flipping halfway.
  4. Layer bowls with grains, greens, veggies, and falafel.

7. Roasted Veggie Sheet Pan Bowls

A simple way to make a week’s worth of satisfying plant-based meals.

Ingredients:

  • 1 zucchini, chopped
  • 2 bell peppers, chopped
  • 1 red onion, quartered
  • 1 head broccoli, chopped
  • 1 large sweet potato, diced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons Italian seasoning
  • Salt and pepper
  • Cooked farro, brown rice, or couscous

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
  2. Toss all veggies with olive oil, seasoning, salt, and pepper.
  3. Spread on a lined sheet pan and roast for 35-40 minutes, stirring halfway.
  4. Divide grains and roasted veggies into containers.

8. Easy Lentil Bolognese

Hearty, rich, and ideal for freezing. The best friend of busy vegans everywhere.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dry brown or green lentils, rinsed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 carrot, diced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 ½ cups vegetable broth
  • Salt and pepper
  • Cooked pasta of choice

Instructions:

  1. Sauté onion, garlic, carrot, and celery in oil until softened.
  2. Add lentils, tomato paste, crushed tomatoes, broth, and seasoning.
  3. Simmer, covered, for 35 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Serve over pasta or zucchini noodles.

9. Spicy Tofu Stir Fry

Protein-packed and vibrant with crispy tofu and colorful vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 cups broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snow peas
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced

For the sauce:

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sriracha
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, minced
  • 1 clove garlic, minced

Instructions:

  1. Toss tofu with cornstarch. Pan-fry in oil until golden.
  2. Add veggies, stir-fry 4-5 minutes.
  3. Mix sauce ingredients and add to pan, tossing to coat.
  4. Portion over cooked rice or quinoa.

10. Overnight Chia Pudding Jars

A quick breakfast or snack, customizable with your favorite toppings.

Ingredients:

  • 1/4 cup chia seeds
  • 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
  • 1 tablespoon maple syrup
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Fruit, granola, or nuts for topping

Instructions:

  1. Stir chia seeds, almond milk, maple syrup, and vanilla in a bowl or jar.
  2. Let sit 10 minutes, stir again to break up clumps.
  3. Refrigerate overnight.
  4. In the morning, top with fruit, granola, or nuts.

Meal Prep Tips for Success

-Stick to versatile grains and legumes that can be batch-cooked and used in multiple recipes. -Invest in quality storage containers to keep meals fresh. -Cool all cooked ingredients before sealing containers to prevent sogginess. -Keep fresh elements (like avocado or tender greens) separate until ready to eat. -Label your meals with the date to easily track freshness.

Final Thoughts

Switching up your vegan meal prep routine is the best way to stay inspired and excited about eating healthy. With a bit of planning and these easy-to-make recipes, you can enjoy variety, flavor, and balance all week long. The beauty of plant-based meal prepping lies in its flexibility—swap veggies depending on what’s in season, make your bowls heartier with extra grains, or amp up protein with extra legumes. Before you know it, you’ll have built a habit that saves money, reduces stress, and makes healthy eating genuinely enjoyable.

No more scrambling for healthy lunches or weeknight dinners—just wholesome, flavorful meals ready whenever hunger hits. Give these vegan meal prep recipes a try and see how simple prepping for the week can be!
