The Healing Properties of Traditional Maori Medicine


Traditional Maori medicine, also known as Rongoā Māori, is a holistic healing system that has been practiced by the indigenous people of New Zealand for centuries. Rooted in the belief that everything in the natural world has its own unique life force, Maori medicine focuses on restoring balance and harmony within the body, mind, and spirit. In this blog post, we will explore the healing properties of traditional Maori medicine and its significance in the modern world.

The Concept of Whakapapa

Central to Maori medicine is the concept of whakapapa, which refers to the interconnectedness of all living things. According to Maori tradition, every plant, animal, and element of nature is descended from a common ancestor. This belief forms the foundation of Maori healing practices, as it acknowledges the role of nature in maintaining overall well-being.

Herbal Remedies

Herbal remedies play a vital role in traditional Maori medicine. The native flora of New Zealand offers a rich array of plants with medicinal properties. One such plant is kawakawa (Macropiper excelsum), which has been used for centuries to treat various ailments. Its leaves are often infused in hot water to make a soothing tea that can aid digestion, relieve pain, and reduce inflammation.

Another notable plant used in Maori medicine is harakeke (Phormium tenax), commonly known as flax. The leaves of this plant are renowned for their antiseptic properties and can be used to treat wounds, burns, and skin infections. Harakeke is also valued for its ability to soothe skin irritations and promote healing.

Massage and Energy Healing

Massage, or mirimiri, is another essential component of traditional Maori medicine. Skilled practitioners use a combination of touch, pressure, and energy work to release tension, promote circulation, and restore balance to the body. Mirimiri can be particularly effective in alleviating musculoskeletal pain, improving flexibility, and reducing stress.

Energy healing, known as wairua healing, is closely related to massage in Maori medicine. It involves channeling and manipulating the life force energy within the body to promote healing on a deeper level. Wairua healing can assist in clearing energetic blockages, restoring vitality, and supporting emotional well-being.

Rituals and Ceremony

In addition to herbal remedies, massage, and energy healing, Maori medicine also incorporates rituals and ceremonies as part of the healing process. These rituals serve to honor the spiritual connection between humans and nature, and to create a sacred space for healing to take place.

One such ritual is the practice of karakia, which involves reciting prayers or chants to invoke spiritual guidance and protection. Karakia can be performed before administering herbal remedies or during massage and energy healing sessions. It helps to focus the mind, amplify the healing intentions, and create a sense of peace and harmony.

The Relevance of Traditional Maori Medicine Today

While modern medicine has made significant advancements, traditional Maori medicine continues to hold relevance and value in the contemporary world. Its holistic approach to healing addresses not only the physical symptoms but also the mental, emotional, and spiritual well-being of individuals.

Traditional Maori medicine encourages a deep connection with nature and promotes sustainable practices that benefit both human health and the environment. Its emphasis on balance and harmony offers a valuable perspective in an increasingly fast-paced and disconnected society.

In conclusion, traditional Maori medicine is a profound healing system that encompasses herbal remedies, massage, energy healing, and rituals. Rooted in the belief of whakapapa, it acknowledges the interdependence between humans and the natural world. By embracing the healing properties of traditional Maori medicine, we can cultivate a deeper connection with ourselves, each other, and the earth.
