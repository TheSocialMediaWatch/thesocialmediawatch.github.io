How to Hunt and Fish for Survival


In a survival situation, knowing how to hunt and fish can be the difference between life and death. Both skills are essential for securing food and maintaining the energy you need to survive. Whether you’re lost in the wilderness, camping in the backcountry, or simply preparing for an emergency, learning how to hunt and fish for survival is a fundamental skill that could save your life.

This guide will walk you through the basic techniques and strategies for hunting and fishing, offering advice on how to make the most of the resources around you. By mastering these skills, you can ensure that you have a reliable food source no matter where you are.

1. Hunting for Survival: Understanding the Basics

Before heading out into the wild, it’s important to understand the basics of hunting and the types of animals you may encounter in different environments. When hunting for survival, efficiency is key, and being prepared is crucial.

1.1 The Importance of Scouting

Scouting is the first step in any successful hunt. Before attempting to hunt, you need to learn about the area and its wildlife. Research the types of animals that inhabit the region, the local terrain, and the seasonal behaviors of those animals. Animals typically follow patterns in their feeding, watering, and resting, so understanding these patterns will greatly increase your chances of success.

  • Know the Animal Tracks: Tracks are one of the best indicators of animal activity. Study the animal tracks in the area to determine the species and the direction of travel. Look for signs like footprints, broken branches, or droppings that indicate an animal’s presence.

  • Look for Animal Signs: In addition to tracks, keep an eye out for other signs of animals, such as nests, dens, or feeding areas. These clues can guide you to areas where you are likely to find prey.

1.2 Choosing the Right Weapon

When hunting for survival, you may not always have access to modern firearms or ammunition. In these situations, it’s essential to know how to use primitive weapons such as bows, arrows, traps, and even improvised tools. The type of weapon you choose depends on the animal you’re hunting and the resources available to you.

  • Bows and Arrows: A well-made bow and arrow can be an effective tool for hunting small to medium-sized animals. They are relatively quiet, allowing for stealthy approaches, and are reusable. However, they require practice to use effectively.

  • Spears and Traps: If you don’t have a bow, you can craft a spear from a sturdy branch. Spears can be used for both fishing and hunting larger animals like deer. Additionally, creating simple traps—such as snares or pitfall traps—can be highly effective for capturing smaller prey.

  • Improvised Weapons: In some survival situations, you may need to improvise weapons from the materials available. A sharp rock or knife can be used as a cutting tool, while a stick can be fashioned into a spear.

1.3 Hunting Strategies

Hunting isn’t just about the weapon; it’s about using the environment to your advantage. Understanding how to move quietly, stay concealed, and track your prey can make a huge difference.

  • Stalking: When hunting, stealth is your greatest ally. Approach your target carefully, moving slowly and avoiding noise. Use the terrain to hide your movements, such as tall grass, rocks, or trees.

  • Ambushing: Setting up an ambush involves positioning yourself near a water source or a well-known trail where animals are likely to pass. You can then wait patiently for your target to approach within shooting or striking range.

  • Using Calls: Some animals can be attracted using calls that mimic the sounds they make. This technique is particularly effective for hunting predators, such as coyotes or wolves, who may be drawn to the sounds of prey animals.

1.4 Field Dressing and Butchering

Once you’ve successfully hunted your prey, knowing how to properly field dress and butcher it is crucial to preserving the meat and making it easier to cook. Begin by carefully removing the internal organs, ensuring you don’t puncture the stomach or intestines to avoid contaminating the meat. After removing the organs, you can separate the meat into manageable portions.

  • Store the Meat Properly: If you’re in a survival situation without refrigeration, it’s essential to preserve the meat. Smoking, drying, or salting can help preserve the meat for later consumption.

2. Fishing for Survival: A Vital Skill

In many survival situations, fishing can provide a vital source of food. Freshwater and saltwater environments both offer fish species that can sustain you. Here’s how to fish effectively when you’re relying on it for survival.

2.1 Selecting the Right Fishing Gear

Fishing gear can vary depending on the environment and what you have on hand. However, there are several basic tools and techniques that you can use, even without a fishing rod.

  • Fishing Lines and Hooks: If you have fishing lines and hooks, you can catch fish using a simple hook and line setup. If you don’t have pre-made hooks, you can fashion them from metal or bone.

  • Snares and Nets: In addition to hook and line fishing, you can use primitive snares and nets. Fish traps are effective in shallow waters, especially in areas where fish congregate, such as near rocks or vegetation.

  • Spearfishing: If you’re near water, spearfishing can be a highly effective method. A sharpened spear can help you catch fish while swimming or standing in the shallows.

2.2 Fishing Techniques

Fishing for survival is all about patience and resourcefulness. Here are a few key techniques for increasing your chances of success.

  • Still Fishing: Still fishing involves casting a line into the water and waiting for the fish to bite. It’s a slow but steady method that requires patience and careful observation of the water.

  • Fly Fishing: If you’re in an area with fast-moving water, fly fishing may be an effective technique. By mimicking the movement of insects or small fish, you can attract predatory fish like trout or bass.

  • Night Fishing: Many fish species are more active at night. Using a light source to attract fish to your bait can significantly improve your chances of catching something after dark.

  • Fishing in Streams: In freshwater survival situations, streams are often the best place to fish. Look for calm pools or deeper sections where fish are likely to congregate.

2.3 Cleaning and Preparing Fish

After catching your fish, you need to clean and prepare it for cooking. The first step is to remove the scales, followed by gutting the fish. Cut off the head, tail, and fins, and carefully remove the internal organs. You can then either cook the fish whole or cut it into fillets.

  • Preserving Fish: If you’re in a survival situation and can’t cook the fish immediately, you can preserve it by smoking or drying it to extend its shelf life.

3. Cooking Your Game: Turning Raw Food into Nutrition

Whether you’ve hunted or fished, turning your catch into a meal is the next important step. Knowing how to cook your meat or fish can ensure that it’s safe to eat and more enjoyable.

  • Roasting: One of the simplest methods of cooking meat or fish is by roasting it over an open flame. Skewer the meat on a branch or use a makeshift grill, and rotate it periodically to ensure it cooks evenly.

  • Boiling: If you have access to a pot or container, boiling is another effective method. You can boil fish or meat in water to make a stew. Add any edible plants or herbs you find along the way to enhance the flavor.

  • Smoking: Smoking is a great way to preserve meat, especially if you don’t have access to refrigeration. Create a smoke pit or use a makeshift smoker to dry and preserve your food.

4. Conclusion: Be Prepared and Stay Calm

Hunting and fishing are invaluable survival skills that can help you find food in even the most challenging environments. By understanding the local wildlife, choosing the right tools, and using effective techniques, you can greatly increase your chances of survival. Whether you are in the wilderness, facing a natural disaster, or exploring a remote area, these skills will help you stay nourished and focused on your survival.

Above all, remain calm and think clearly. The ability to hunt and fish for survival is a skill that requires patience, observation, and practice. By honing these skills ahead of time and preparing yourself mentally and physically, you can increase your chances of surviving in any situation.
