The Best Tablets for Artists and Designers in 2025


As the creative industry continues to evolve, the demand for powerful tools that can keep up with the imagination of artists and designers has never been higher. Whether you’re a professional illustrator, 3D artist, graphic designer, or hobbyist diving into digital art for the first time, finding the right tablet can make all the difference in bringing your vision to life. 2025 has ushered in a new generation of tablets tailored to meet the needs of creatives, combining cutting-edge technology with intuitive designs. Here, we dive into the best tablets for artists and designers this year, covering options for every skill level and budget.

Why Tablets Are Essential for Modern Creatives

The world of art and design has largely shifted to digital platforms, offering artists and designers capabilities that traditional mediums simply cannot match. Tablets provide unmatched portability, seamless interaction, and direct control over creative processes—making them indispensable for modern creators. The combination of stylus precision, high-resolution displays, and advanced software compatibility has revolutionized how art is conceived and produced.

When choosing a tablet that caters specifically to artistic and design needs, considerations include display quality, stylus features, processing power, software options, durability, and price. Keeping these factors in mind is essential to maximize artistic potential without sacrificing convenience.

1. Apple iPad Pro (2025 Edition)

Apple continues to dominate the landscape of creative tools, and the 2025 edition of the iPad Pro is no exception. Equipped with Apple’s latest M3 Pro chip, this version pushes the boundaries for speed, performance, and multitasking. The Liquid Retina XDR display presents vibrant colors with an unparalleled level of accuracy, perfect for illustrators and graphic designers who rely on precision.

Paired with the second-generation Apple Pencil, the iPad Pro offers incredible responsiveness and tilt sensitivity. Advanced features such as hover gestures and pressure sensitivity add versatility to sketching, drawing, and painting workflows. The pencil magnetically charges and pairs instantly, offering frictionless simplicity.

Moreover, apps like Procreate, Adobe Fresco, and Affinity Designer run seamlessly on the iPad Pro, making it a powerhouse for digital art. Whether you work on animations, editorial illustrations, or UX/UI mockups, this tablet is a tool that excels on all fronts.

Pros:

  • Stunning display with HDR capabilities
  • Exceptional stylus functionality
  • Lightweight and portable

Cons:

  • Higher price point than other options
  • Limited freedom in the OS customization

2. Wacom Cintiq Pro 27

When it comes to serving professional creatives, Wacom products remain the gold standard. The Cintiq Pro 27 raises the bar with its unrivaled 4K display, offering the most realistic feel for digital art. Featuring industry-leading color accuracy at 99% Adobe RGB and Pantone Validated certification, this tablet is ideal for those working on detailed visual projects such as photorealistic art or animation.

Wacom’s Pro Pen 3 is a game-changer. With customizable grip options, adjustable weight, and support for 8,192 levels of pressure sensitivity, it aligns perfectly with an artist’s unique working style. The pen’s tilt recognition allows for lifelike strokes, providing the closest experience to traditional media.

The Cintiq Pro 27 is particularly popular among professionals in animation studios, film production, and large-scale graphic design. While it isn’t portable, its large screen size and incredible ergonomics make it a must-have device for in-depth creative work.

Pros:

  • Best-in-class display and color fidelity
  • Highly customizable stylus options
  • Designed for heavy-duty professional use

Cons:

  • Substantial cost
  • Bulky and requires a dedicated workspace

3. Microsoft Surface Pro 10

Microsoft’s Surface series continues to close the gap between tablets and laptops. The Surface Pro 10 is a versatile option for creatives juggling design work and broader productivity tasks. The tablet’s PixelSense Flow Display supports a dynamic refresh rate of up to 120Hz, ensuring fluid visuals and highly responsive touch gestures.

Paired with the Surface Slim Pen 3, this tablet delivers precision and control for illustration, note-taking, or design. The stylus offers haptic feedback, creating a tactile feel that mimics drawing on paper. The Surface Pro 10 also integrates seamlessly with the Adobe Creative Cloud software suite, making it ideal for multi-platform designers.

With its detachable keyboard and full Windows functionality, this tablet doubles as a reliable productivity tool for those who need versatility in both creative and professional environments.

Pros:

  • Combines tablet functionality with laptop-grade performance
  • Works seamlessly with software like Photoshop and Illustrator
  • Affordable option compared to other premium tablets

Cons:

  • Stylus performance doesn’t match Wacom or Apple Pencil
  • Screen size may feel limiting for intricate artwork

4. Samsung Galaxy Tab S9 Ultra

The 2025 iteration of Samsung’s Galaxy Tab series positions itself as a direct competitor to the iPad Pro, and it’s earning its spot at the top. The Galaxy Tab S9 Ultra boasts a gorgeous AMOLED display with a vivid 14.6-inch screen, perfect for artists who prioritize wide working areas and high-definition visuals. The inclusion of the Snapdragon 8 Gen 3 processor ensures smooth performance across demanding apps.

The tablet is paired with the redesigned S Pen, which delivers supreme precision thanks to ultralow latency rates. Features like the ability to program gestures for custom shortcuts further enhance its appeal. The S Pen is also water-resistant, making it durable for extended use.

Samsung’s One UI software creates thoughtful tools tailored for creators, including multitasking panes and improved compatibility with third-party apps. For artists who want portability with advanced hardware capabilities, the Galaxy Tab S9 Ultra presents an excellent choice.

Pros:

  • Outstanding AMOLED display quality
  • Comes with stylus included
  • Supports multitasking for complex workflows

Cons:

  • Heavier than other portable options
  • Limited dedicated art apps

5. Xencelabs Pen Display 16

Xencelabs’ Pen Display is an emerging favorite among professionals who want premium features at a more affordable cost than Wacom products. The Xencelabs Pen Display 16 focuses on delivering all the essentials without compromising on quality.

The 16-inch display offers 4K UHD resolution, ensuring crystal-clear visuals for complex design work. Built-in features like an etched glass surface simulate the feel of drawing on paper, which many traditional artists transitioning to digital love. The accompanying Xencelabs Pen ships with multiple pen nibs and offers customizable shortcut buttons to streamline the creative process.

This tablet also stands out for its user-friendly interface and integrated Quick Keys remote for faster navigation of tools. While it lacks flashy add-ons, the Pen Display 16 delivers reliable performance and is particularly suited for freelance designers.

Pros:

  • Affordable for its performance level
  • Etched glass provides a tactile drawing experience
  • Quick Keys remote adds efficiency

Cons:

  • Smaller screen compared to competitors
  • Fewer advanced stylus features

6. Huion Kamvas Pro 24 (2025 Edition)

Huion has built a reputation as a budget-friendly alternative for artists who still want outstanding performance, and the Kamvas Pro 24 exemplifies this perfectly. The 2025 version packs a beautifully calibrated 2.5K QHD display with anti-glare technology for long hours of use.

The accompanying Huion Pen supports 8,192 levels of pressure sensitivity and a quick response rate, delivering professional-grade pen performance at a fraction of the cost. This tablet supports multiple interfaces like HDMI and USB-C, making connectivity seamless for different workflows.

Its design is durable and ergonomic, catering to artists and designers who work intensively on detailed projects while keeping costs manageable.

Pros:

  • Budget-friendly option for professionals
  • Wide compatibility with major design software
  • Sturdy, ergonomic build

Cons:

  • Display resolution lags behind premium models
  • Heavyweight makes it less portable

Choosing the Right Tablet for Your Needs

While the market is brimming with options, making the right choice depends entirely on your specific requirements. Consider factors such as portability, display quality, stylus precision, software compatibility, and budget. For illustrators focused on drawing, a tablet with excellent stylus sensitivity and tilt recognition may take precedence. For 3D artists and animators, screen responsiveness and processing power might be more critical.

Ultimately, the best tablet for artists or designers in 2025 will not only enhance productivity but also serve as an extension of your artistic vision. With any of the options listed above, you’ll be equipped to take your creativity to new heights.
