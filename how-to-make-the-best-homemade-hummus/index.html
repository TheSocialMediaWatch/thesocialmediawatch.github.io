How to Make the Best Homemade Hummus


Hummus is a delightful, creamy spread that has become a beloved staple in kitchens around the world. Whether you’re preparing it as an appetizer for a dinner party or as a snack for yourself, homemade hummus stands out with its rich texture and vibrant flavors. The beauty of hummus lies in its versatility – it pairs wonderfully with fresh vegetables, pita bread, or even as a topping for grilled meats and bowls.

Making hummus at home not only allows you to control the ingredients but also ensures you get the freshest taste possible. While store-bought versions can be convenient, they often contain unnecessary preservatives and additives that can compromise the flavor. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of creating the best homemade hummus that will impress both you and your guests.

Ingredients for Homemade Hummus

To get started on the path to hummus perfection, you’ll need a few simple ingredients. Here’s what you’ll need:

1. Chickpeas (Garbanzo Beans)

Chickpeas are the heart and soul of hummus. While you can use canned chickpeas for convenience, using dried chickpeas offers a richer flavor and smoother texture. If you opt for canned chickpeas, make sure to rinse them thoroughly to remove excess salt and preservatives.

2. Tahini

Tahini, a sesame seed paste, adds the signature nutty flavor that distinguishes hummus from other spreads. It’s essential for that creamy, slightly bitter note that balances the chickpeas’ earthiness. Be sure to choose high-quality tahini that’s smooth and runny.

3. Lemon Juice

Freshly squeezed lemon juice will brighten up the hummus, providing a necessary tanginess. Avoid bottled lemon juice as it lacks the fresh, zesty flavor that freshly squeezed juice offers.

4. Garlic

Garlic is another key ingredient that gives hummus its savory depth. Fresh garlic adds a pungent, aromatic flavor that complements the smoothness of tahini and chickpeas.

5. Olive Oil

Olive oil not only makes hummus creamy but also enhances its richness. Choose extra virgin olive oil for the best flavor. This oil is cold-pressed and retains its full range of flavors, adding complexity to the hummus.

6. Cumin

Cumin is a common spice used in hummus for its warm, earthy flavor. It pairs well with the other ingredients, providing a subtle smokiness that rounds out the taste.

7. Salt

Salt helps to enhance all the other flavors in the hummus. Make sure to taste as you go to ensure the salt is well-balanced, not overpowering.

8. Baking Soda (Optional)

If you’re using dried chickpeas, baking soda can help soften them during the cooking process, leading to a smoother hummus texture.

Preparing the Ingredients

1. Cooking Chickpeas from Scratch

While using canned chickpeas can save time, cooking your own from dried beans results in a superior texture. To cook dried chickpeas, follow these steps:

  • Soak the Chickpeas: Place the dried chickpeas in a large bowl and cover them with water. Allow them to soak overnight. This softens them and reduces the cooking time.
  • Cook the Chickpeas: Drain and rinse the soaked chickpeas. Add them to a large pot and cover them with fresh water. Optionally, add ½ teaspoon of baking soda to help soften the chickpeas. Bring the water to a boil, then reduce the heat and let the chickpeas simmer for 1-1.5 hours, or until they are tender.
  • Drain and Cool: Once cooked, drain the chickpeas, but reserve a cup of the cooking water. This starchy water can be used to adjust the hummus consistency later.

2. Preparing the Tahini

Tahini is a simple ingredient, but the quality varies greatly between brands. To prepare tahini, give it a good stir as it often separates into a solid mass and oil. The smoother and creamier the tahini, the smoother your hummus will be. If your tahini seems too thick, you can thin it out with a little bit of warm water.

3. Fresh Lemon Juice

For optimal flavor, use fresh lemon juice. Squeeze the juice from two medium-sized lemons. The zest can also be added for an extra burst of citrus if desired.

4. Garlic Preparation

Peel and mince fresh garlic cloves. Depending on how strong you want the garlic flavor to be, adjust the quantity. Start with one clove and taste-test before adding more.

5. Olive Oil

Choose a high-quality extra virgin olive oil. You’ll need some for blending the hummus, but you can also reserve a tablespoon or two to drizzle on top of the final dish for a richer, more decadent flavor.

6. Spices

Toast the cumin lightly in a dry skillet over medium heat for about 1-2 minutes until fragrant. This step intensifies its flavor, giving your hummus an even more aromatic quality. You can also add a pinch of paprika or cayenne pepper if you prefer a spicier version.

Blending the Hummus

Once all your ingredients are prepared, it’s time to make the hummus. The blending process is crucial for achieving that perfectly smooth and creamy texture. Here’s how to do it:

1. Combine the Ingredients

In a high-speed blender or food processor, add the cooked chickpeas, tahini, garlic, lemon juice, cumin, and salt. Begin by blending the mixture on a low speed to break it down into smaller pieces.

2. Add Olive Oil and Water

While the mixture is blending, slowly drizzle in the olive oil. Once incorporated, begin adding the reserved chickpea cooking water, one tablespoon at a time, until the hummus reaches your desired consistency. If you’re using canned chickpeas, you may need a bit more water. Be sure to blend thoroughly between additions.

3. Taste and Adjust

Stop the blender and taste the hummus. Adjust the seasoning to suit your preferences, adding more salt, lemon juice, or garlic if necessary. If you like a creamier texture, continue blending, adding a bit more olive oil or water as needed.

4. Blend Until Smooth

Continue to blend the hummus until it becomes silky and smooth. This may take a few minutes, depending on your blender or food processor. If you prefer an extra-smooth hummus, you can pass the mixture through a fine mesh sieve or cheesecloth.

Serving the Hummus

Once your hummus is perfectly blended, it’s ready to serve. Transfer it to a serving bowl, and you can garnish it with a drizzle of olive oil, a sprinkle of paprika or cumin, and a few whole chickpeas for a bit of texture. Fresh parsley or cilantro can also be used as a garnish for an herbal note.

Serving Suggestions

  • With Pita Bread: Serve the hummus alongside warm pita bread for a simple and delicious snack or appetizer.
  • With Fresh Veggies: Cut up some crunchy vegetables like carrots, cucumbers, and bell peppers for dipping.
  • In a Grain Bowl: Spread hummus over a grain bowl topped with quinoa or rice and your choice of protein for a satisfying meal.
  • On Sandwiches or Wraps: Use hummus as a spread on sandwiches or wraps for a healthy alternative to mayonnaise or butter.

Storing Homemade Hummus

If you happen to have leftover hummus, don’t worry—it stores well in the fridge. Transfer it to an airtight container and refrigerate it for up to 4-5 days. You can also freeze hummus for longer storage. Just make sure to transfer it to a freezer-safe container, leaving a little space for expansion.

Tips for the Best Homemade Hummus

1. Use Fresh Ingredients

Freshly squeezed lemon juice, garlic, and high-quality tahini will elevate your hummus to the next level. It’s worth taking the extra step to ensure you’re using the freshest ingredients possible.

2. Don’t Skimp on Olive Oil

A good-quality extra virgin olive oil will not only enhance the flavor but also improve the texture of your hummus, giving it that creamy, luxurious consistency.

3. Cook Your Chickpeas for the Best Texture

If you want the creamiest hummus, cooking your chickpeas from scratch is the best option. You’ll be able to control the texture and make sure they’re cooked to perfection.

4. Blend Thoroughly

The key to a smooth and creamy hummus is to blend it thoroughly. Don’t rush this process—allow the blender or food processor to do its job, and you’ll end up with a silky-smooth spread.

5. Customize Your Flavor

Hummus is versatile and can be customized with different flavors. You can add roasted red peppers, sun-dried tomatoes, olives, or even spices like turmeric or coriander to give your hummus a unique twist.

Conclusion

Making the best homemade hummus is simple and rewarding. With just a handful of ingredients and a few basic techniques, you can create a delicious and creamy spread that is sure to impress. Whether you serve it with pita, fresh vegetables, or as a spread on sandwiches, homemade hummus is a healthy and satisfying choice that will quickly become a staple in your kitchen. Enjoy experimenting with flavors and ingredients to make it your own!
