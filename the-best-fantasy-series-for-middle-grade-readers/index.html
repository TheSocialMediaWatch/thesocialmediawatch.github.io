The Best Fantasy Series for Middle-Grade Readers


Fantasy stories hold the power to transport readers to magical realms, introduce them to awe-inspiring characters, and spark their imaginations in ways no other genre can. For middle-grade readers, fantasy is much more than entertainment—it’s a gateway to creativity, exploration, and personal growth. Whether it’s epic quests, whimsical worlds, or heroic journeys, the best fantasy series are crafted to enchant and inspire young minds while teaching profound lessons along the way.

Finding the perfect fantasy series for middle-grade readers can be a daunting task, with so many choices vying for attention. That’s why we’ve curated a list of outstanding fantasy series tailored for children and tweens. From classics to modern favorites, these books promise thrilling adventures, unforgettable characters, and timeless themes that will resonate with young readers.

1. Harry Potter by J.K. Rowling

Undoubtedly one of the most beloved fantasy series for middle-grade readers, Harry Potter has captivated millions around the globe. Following the story of an ordinary boy who discovers he’s a wizard, this series immerses readers in the magical world of Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry.

What sets Harry Potter apart are its richly developed characters, intricate plots, and universal themes of friendship, bravery, and the triumph of good over evil. Each book adds new layers to the story, introducing fascinating creatures, spells, and engaging mysteries. It’s a perfect blend of humor, heart, and adventure that resonates with readers of all ages.

2. Percy Jackson & The Olympians by Rick Riordan

An extraordinary blend of fantasy, mythology, and modern-day humor, Rick Riordan’s Percy Jackson series is an ideal pick for middle-grade readers. Centered on Percy Jackson, a demigod and son of Poseidon, the series catapults readers into adventures steeped in Greek mythology.

The fast-paced narrative, relatable characters, and clever twists make this series both educational and entertaining. With themes like loyalty, identity, and overcoming challenges, Percy’s adventures spark curiosity while teaching invaluable lessons. The accessible writing style ensures younger readers stay engaged without feeling overwhelmed by complex mythology.

3. The Chronicles of Narnia by C.S. Lewis

For generations, The Chronicles of Narnia has been a cornerstone of middle-grade fantasy literature. Starting with The Lion, the Witch and the Wardrobe, the series follows children who travel to the magical world of Narnia, where they encounter mystical creatures, fight evil forces, and discover their courage.

C.S. Lewis’s writing combines imaginative storytelling with deep moral and ethical themes, including redemption, sacrifice, and the importance of faith. The world-building in Narnia is nothing short of magnificent, and the adventures are timeless. This series stands out for its ability to engage readers while imparting meaningful lessons.

4. Artemis Fowl by Eoin Colfer

Eoin Colfer’s Artemis Fowl series is a wonderfully inventive take on the fantasy genre. Featuring a genius boy and criminal mastermind, Artemis Fowl, the books mix high-tech gadgetry with magical intrigue to create an action-packed saga young readers will adore.

The series offers a different perspective on fantasy by delving into “underground societies” filled with fairies, dwarves, and other mythical beings. Artemis is an unconventional hero, whose growth over the series keeps readers hooked. With snappy dialogue, suspenseful stories, and morally complex characters, this series offers a unique flavor of fantasy.

5. Wings of Fire by Tui T. Sutherland

Few middle-grade fantasy series are as engrossing and imaginative as Wings of Fire. This dragon-centric saga transports readers to the mystical world of Pyrrhia, where dragon clans vie for power, peace, and survival. Centered on dragon characters, each with distinct personalities and abilities, the series offers unlimited thrills and twists.

Tui T. Sutherland brings to life an epic tale packed with action, suspense, and heartwarming friendships. Young readers who love dragons or dream of soaring through the skies will find themselves completely immersed in this sprawling, adventure-filled universe. The series is also notable for its nuanced treatment of themes like destiny, teamwork, and bravery.

6. Fablehaven by Brandon Mull

Enter the magical world of Fablehaven, a sanctuary for mystical creatures that serves as the setting for Brandon Mull’s captivating middle-grade fantasy series. When siblings Seth and Kendra discover their grandparents’ hidden role as caretakers of Fablehaven, their lives become intertwined with magical creatures and dangerous mysteries.

Rich descriptions, creative world-building, and compelling characters make this series a hit among tweens. The emphasis on bravery, responsibility, and acceptance of differences is woven seamlessly into the action-packed narrative. Each book builds in intensity, keeping readers riveted from beginning to end.

7. His Dark Materials by Philip Pullman

Though slightly darker in tone than some of its middle-grade counterparts, Philip Pullman’s His Dark Materials is a masterpiece that challenges and mesmerizes young readers. With its thought-provoking exploration of science, religion, and freedom, it presents a complex yet accessible narrative.

Anchored by relatable protagonists, Lyra and Will, the series takes readers on an extraordinary journey through parallel worlds filled with daemons, witches, and armored bears. Pullman’s storytelling is luminous and layered, encouraging middle-grade readers to question and think deeply while enjoying an epic adventure.

8. The Last Kids on Earth by Max Brallier

A quirky mix of fantasy, humor, and post-apocalyptic adventure, The Last Kids on Earth series by Max Brallier is perfect for middle-grade readers who crave fast-paced action and laugh-out-loud moments. The books follow a group of kids who band together to survive in a world overrun by zombies and monsters—while having as much fun as possible.

Featuring bold illustrations and an engaging narrative voice, this series appeals to reluctant readers as well as avid bookworms. Themes of teamwork, resilience, and creativity are explored in a way that blends seamlessly with the witty, larger-than-life escapades.

9. The Secret of the Unicorn Queen by Josepha Sherman

Josepha Sherman’s The Secret of the Unicorn Queen combines magic, fantasy, and powerful unicorns to create a delightful series for younger readers. When a modern-day girl is transported into a magical realm, she befriends a herd of unicorns and joins them in defending their land.

This lesser-known series is a hidden gem that is both empowering and imaginative. With strong female protagonists and themes of bravery, trust, and environmental stewardship, it’s a charming read for middle-grade fantasy fans seeking a whimsical adventure.

10. Keeper of the Lost Cities by Shannon Messenger

Shannon Messenger’s Keeper of the Lost Cities series is a gripping tale about Sophie Foster, a young girl who discovers she’s from a secret race with remarkable abilities. Thrust into a dazzling and perilous world, Sophie must learn who she truly is while unraveling mysteries and navigating friendships.

With rich details, well-developed characters, and intense emotions, this series hooks middle-grade readers from the very beginning. The themes of identity, loyalty, and standing up for others add depth and resonance to the fantasy adventure, making it an unforgettable experience for readers.

What Makes a Fantasy Series Ideal for Middle-Grade Readers?

Middle-grade fantasy books share certain characteristics that set them apart. They often feature protagonists who are close to the target audience’s age, making it easier for young readers to relate. These stories introduce magical elements and imaginative settings while maintaining a sense of groundedness with relatable themes like friendship, courage, and self-discovery.

Another critical aspect is accessibility. Language and pacing are tailored to ensure middle-grade readers remain engaged without feeling overwhelmed. While the stories may delve into serious topics, they balance excitement and introspection to create an enjoyable experience.

Choosing the Right Fantasy Series

Selecting the right fantasy series depends on the interests and maturity level of the young reader. If they enjoy magical schools and mystical creatures, classics like Harry Potter and The Chronicles of Narnia may be perfect. For fans of high-action adventures and mythical lore, series such as Percy Jackson or Wings of Fire are excellent choices. If they lean toward humor with a unique twist, The Last Kids on Earth might be just right.

Fantasy is a genre that sparks endless curiosity and wonder, encouraging kids to dream big and think creatively. With so many incredible series available, the magic of reading is only a page away.
