How to Find Hotels That Welcome LGBTQ+ Travelers


Traveling as an LGBTQ+ individual can be a rewarding experience, but it’s essential to feel safe and welcomed wherever you go. Finding hotels that are inclusive and welcoming to LGBTQ+ travelers is crucial for a comfortable and enjoyable trip. Here are some tips on how to find hotels that embrace diversity and provide a welcoming environment for all guests.

1. Research LGBTQ+ Friendly Hotels

Start by researching hotels that have a reputation for being LGBTQ+ friendly. Look for hotels that have received certifications or awards for their inclusivity. Websites like LGBTQ+ travel guides and directories can be valuable resources for finding LGBTQ+ friendly accommodations. Reading reviews from LGBTQ+ travelers can also provide insight into the experiences of other guests.

2. Check for Inclusivity Policies

Before booking a hotel, check if they have inclusivity policies in place. Look for hotels that have non-discrimination policies that specifically mention sexual orientation and gender identity. Hotels that display symbols like the rainbow flag or advertise themselves as LGBTQ+ friendly are more likely to provide a welcoming environment for LGBTQ+ guests.

3. Reach Out to the Hotel Directly

If you’re unsure about a hotel’s stance on LGBTQ+ inclusivity, don’t hesitate to reach out to them directly. Send an email or give them a call to inquire about their policies regarding LGBTQ+ guests. This direct communication can help you gauge the hotel’s commitment to inclusivity and how they prioritize the comfort and safety of all guests.

4. Look for LGBTQ+ Events or Offers

Some hotels host LGBTQ+ events or offer special packages for LGBTQ+ travelers. Check if the hotel you’re interested in has any upcoming events or promotions that cater to the LGBTQ+ community. Staying at a hotel that actively engages with LGBTQ+ guests can enhance your travel experience and connect you with like-minded individuals.

5. Consider Location and Surroundings

When choosing a hotel, consider the location and surroundings to ensure a safe and inclusive environment. Opt for hotels located in LGBTQ+ friendly neighborhoods or areas known for their diversity and acceptance. Researching the local laws and attitudes towards the LGBTQ+ community can also help you make an informed decision about where to stay.

6. Support LGBTQ+ Owned or Affiliated Hotels

Supporting LGBTQ+ owned or affiliated hotels is another way to ensure a welcoming stay. Look for hotels that are part of LGBTQ+ hotel chains or owned and operated by members of the LGBTQ+ community. By supporting LGBTQ+ businesses, you contribute to the promotion of diversity and inclusivity in the hospitality industry.

7. Share Your Experiences

After your stay at a hotel, consider sharing your experiences with other LGBTQ+ travelers. Write reviews on LGBTQ+ travel websites, social media platforms, or LGBTQ+ forums to help fellow travelers find LGBTQ+ friendly accommodations. Your feedback can guide others in making informed decisions and promote a more inclusive travel culture.

Finding hotels that welcome LGBTQ+ travelers is essential for a positive travel experience. By conducting thorough research, checking for inclusivity policies, reaching out to hotels directly, considering the location, and supporting LGBTQ+ owned establishments, you can ensure a safe and welcoming stay wherever your travels take you. Remember, everyone deserves to feel valued and respected while exploring the world.

Enjoy your travels and embrace the diversity around you!
