The Chemistry Behind Everyday Products


Have you ever stopped to consider the role of chemistry in your daily life? From the moment you wake up to the moment you go to sleep, countless chemical reactions occur, all working together to make your day possible. In this article, we’ll delve into the surprising complexity of everyday products and explore the chemical processes behind them.

Let’s start with the omnipresent plastic. At its core, plastic is a polymer - a long, complex chain of repeating molecules. Polymers can be made from a wide variety of monomers, the building blocks of these molecular chains.

One type of plastic, polyethylene, is made by combining ethylene molecules together into long chains. This simple, flexible polymer is found in many low-cost applications such as plastic bags, food packaging, and bubble wrap. Next time you’re juggling textbooks wrapped in polyethylene, remember the humble role of ethylene in your daily life!

Another common polymer is polypropylene, which is widely used in food containers, automotive parts, and textiles. It is made by combining together the monomer propylene, and its most notable property is its durability. When you crack open a bottle of medicine in a plastic bottle, you’re relying on the strength and resilience of polypropylene.

Many of us couldn’t imagine starting our day without a cup of coffee. This beloved beverage is made from the roasted and ground seeds of the Coffea plant, but the real magic happens chemically during the roasting process. When coffee beans are roasted, the chemical structure of the oils inside them changes, imparting the characteristic rich aroma and taste we associate with coffee. The complexity of this transformation highlights the many-sidedness of chemistry in food preparation.

Soap is another staple in our daily lives, used for everything from cleaning dishes to washing our bodies. At its core, soap is made up of molecules with a polar (positive) end, which is attracted to water, and a nonpolar (negative) end, which is attracted to grease and oil. When soap is added to water, it forms small “micelles,” where the polar ends are attracted to the water and the nonpolar ends are pushed outward. This interaction allows soap molecules to surround and lift dirt and grease from surfaces, leaving them sparkling clean.

It’s often easy to overlook the role of chemistry in everyday items, but looking closer reveals the fascinating complexity hidden beneath the surface. From the molecular bonds that make plastics so versatile to the intriguing chemical processes behind our morning cup of coffee, these everyday miracles are a testament to the power of chemistry.

In the next cups of coffee we savor, the next articles of clothing we put on, and the next products we use to keep our homes clean and our bodies fresh, remember the quiet, steady work of chemistry that enables these conveniences. It is truly miraculous that such a small number of chemicals can combine to create the vast array of products we use daily.
