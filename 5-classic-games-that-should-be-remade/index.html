5 Classic Games That Should Be Remade


Nostalgia draws us toward the pixelated worlds and pixel-perfect gameplay of classic video games. They hold a unique charm, capturing moments in gaming history when ingenuity triumphed over hardware limitations. Yet, as screens get sharper and gaming expectations shift, some legends start to feel dated—not for lack of brilliance, but because technology has sprinted forward. Still, every so often, a remake comes around that revives these classics, reinventing them for fresh audiences without losing what made them unforgettable. Below, let’s highlight five classic games whose DNA cries out for an expertly crafted remake.

1. Chrono Trigger

Few RPGs have inspired as much reverence as Chrono Trigger. Developed by a “dream team” at Square (now Square Enix), it forged timelessness with its intricate story, innovative time-travel mechanics, and layered soundtrack by Yasunori Mitsuda and Nobuo Uematsu. While Chrono Trigger remains remarkably playable (thanks to strategic ports and an engaging battle system), recent remakes like Final Fantasy VII Remake demonstrate just how transformative a modern touch can be.

Imagine a version with full 3D environments, vibrant cinematic cutscenes, and a more dynamic, visually captivating combat system—while always maintaining that crucial turn-based DNA. There’s a massive appetite for single-player, narrative-driven adventures with meaningful choices and multiple endings. Remaking Chrono Trigger could mean reacquainting longtime fans and introducing new ones to radical characters like Frog, Magus, and Lucca, with updated art and voice acting that honors the original spirit.

Why a Remake Is Needed

  • Timeless Storytelling: Time-travel in games is still a tough trick to pull off. A remake could perfect the formula and reestablish Chrono Trigger as the gold standard.
  • Expanded Endgame and Side Quests: There’s room to deepen side quests, subplots, and even create brand-new scenarios using modern storytelling tools.
  • Crossover Potential: Square Enix could even tie threads from Chrono Cross and other titles, strengthening the Chrono universe for future installments.

2. Silent Hill 2

Psychological horror in games rarely gets more haunting than Silent Hill 2. It turned fog and silence into weapons, weaving a story that lingers long after the controller’s put away. The recent buzz around horror game remakes sets the perfect stage for this title to reclaim its crown.

A respectful rebuild would keep the oppressive atmosphere, but unleash new graphics engines to deepen that suffocating dread—dynamic lighting, volumetric fog, disturbing soundscapes, and motion-captured acting would all ratchet up the unease. Modern AI could make enemies unpredictable and terrifying, while environmental storytelling could flourish with more subtlety. A retouched Silent Hill 2 wouldn’t just serve nostalgia. It could redefine psychological horror for a new era.

Why a Remake Is Needed

  • Graphical Overhaul: The impact of pure dread and environmental design could escalate with today’s technology.
  • Enhanced Audio: Sound design was always a core pillar—modern 3D audio and atmospheric effects could turn headphones into instruments of terror.
  • Expanded Narrative: New lore and additional endings could expand the mythology without watering it down.

3. Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic

The galaxy far, far away hit an RPG high note with Star Wars: Knights of the Old Republic (KOTOR). Set thousands of years before the Skywalker saga, KOTOR carved a unique identity with choice-driven storytelling and memorable characters. Given the current appetite for Star Wars content and the franchise’s massive reach, this RPG deserves a proper modern remake that balances tactical battles, sweeping cinematic presentation, and impactful choices.

Imagine reimagined worlds like Taris and Dantooine with next-gen visuals, bringing to life the dusty cantinas, ancient Jedi temples, and Sith lairs in 4K detail. Modern gameplay sensibilities could sand down the original’s rough edges, giving combat—whether lightsaber duels or Force standoffs—the fluidity and drama of contemporary RPGs. A deeper, more adaptive morality system could provide consequences even more nuanced and personal.

Why a Remake Is Needed

  • Modernization of Systems: Many RPG fans now expect seamless integration between action and strategy; KOTOR’s turn-based hybrid could adapt with real-time fluidity and tactical depth.
  • Expanded Worldbuilding: Enhanced visuals would immerse players in the universe, from bustling cities to ancient ruins, while new voiceover work could revitalize old favorites.
  • Cross-Platform Play: Accessibility on multiple platforms would ensure new generations could experience KOTOR’s galaxy-spanning story.

4. Jet Set Radio

A riot of color and attitude, Jet Set Radio captured the thumping, rebellious energy of street culture and rollerblading. Its cel-shaded graphics remain iconic, but the hardware it first launched on is now a relic. Still, no skater, trick, or grinding rail has matched the stylistic punch of this Dreamcast gem. With the explosion of online multiplayer and customization, there’s fertile ground for a next-generation remake.

Think about the possibility of sprawling city hubs, seamlessly interwoven neighborhoods, live online events, and a bigger, even more infectious soundtrack. Bring back the expressive tagging, slick tricks, and outrunning the law, but with the ability to customize avatars, graffiti, and playgrounds on a much grander scale. Multiplayer modes (competitive, cooperative, or just plain chaotic) could turn Jet Set Radio into both a nostalgia trip and a trendsetting hit.

Why a Remake Is Needed

  • Stylized Graphics Live On: Modern hardware could push the cel-shaded style to stunning heights, with bigger environments and more animation varieties.
  • Community and Customization: User-generated content and social hubs could make Jet Set Radio a true cultural platform—maybe even a metaverse space for artists and music lovers.
  • Vibrant Soundtrack: With more licensing possibilities, a new soundtrack could blend underground legends and emerging artists, giving the game a contemporary edge.

5. EarthBound (Mother 2)

Few games merge playful absurdity with genuine emotion quite like EarthBound. Its off-kilter world, sly humor, and subversion of genre norms have turned it into a cult legend. Yet, outside of emulation or a few select re-releases, it remains frustratingly hard to play for most fans.

A full remake could open up new ways to experience Ness’s odyssey—from Onett to Magicant—while modernizing controls, deepening combat, and lushly repainting its quirky sprites in a beautiful 2D/3D blend. Imagine exploring twisted dungeons and meeting outlandish characters with voice acting, reimagined music, and a careful expansion of the world. EarthBound’s singular mix of parody and poignancy could reach a broader, more diverse audience than ever before.

Why a Remake Is Needed

  • Accessibility: A proper remake means accessibility—wider distribution, options for younger gamers, and even quality-of-life updates like autosaves.
  • Expanded Lore: Side quests and new characters might add dimension, deepening an already rich world.
  • Modern Art Direction: A visual overhaul would let the game’s offbeat energy shine, making “weird” the new wonderful in gaming.

Honorable Mentions

Games like Legend of Dragoon, Metal Gear Solid, and The Legend of Zelda: Oracle of Ages/Seasons just missed the cut, but they, too, offer opportunities for developers to build bridges between generations of gamers.

The Magic Ingredient: Respecting the Core

Reviving classic games isn’t about slapping a new coat of paint on old ideas. It’s about understanding their core—their emotional impact, gameplay loops, and cultural resonance. Developers need to ask: what must stay, and what can evolve to thrill players today?

The best remakes (think Resident Evil 2 or Final Fantasy VII Remake) take creative risks while honoring the old magic, and their success has reignited the industry’s hunger for more. As technology pushes boundaries further, these five classics offer a unique chance not just to revisit their worlds, but to reimagine what gaming can be when fond memories meet bold innovation.

So, whether you booted up these titles on a dusty console years ago or discover them through a modern lens, their stories and experiences stay relevant—waiting, perhaps, for a new generation to pick up the controller and begin again.
