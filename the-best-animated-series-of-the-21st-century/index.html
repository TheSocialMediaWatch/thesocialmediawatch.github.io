The Best Animated Series of the 21st Century


The 21st century has ushered in a golden age of animation, showcasing a wide array of styles, narratives, and innovative storytelling techniques. As technology has advanced, so too has the potential for animated series to explore complex themes and address contemporary issues, appealing to audiences of all ages. This blog post delves into some of the best animated series of the 21st century, celebrating their unique contributions to the medium and their impact on popular culture.

1. Avatar: The Last Airbender (2005-2008)

One of the most acclaimed animated series of the 21st century is undoubtedly Avatar: The Last Airbender. Created by Michael Dante DiMartino and Bryan Konietzko, this Nickelodeon series is set in a world where people can manipulate the four elements: water, earth, fire, and air. The story follows Aang, the last Airbender and the Avatar, who must master all four elements to bring balance to the world.

What sets Avatar apart is its rich storytelling, character development, and mature themes. The series tackles issues such as war, genocide, and identity, making it resonate with both children and adults. With its stunning animation and compelling narrative arcs, Avatar has garnered a dedicated fanbase and remains a cornerstone of modern animation.

2. The Simpsons (1989-Present)

While The Simpsons began in the late 20th century, its influence and relevance have only grown in the 21st century. This long-running series created by Matt Groening holds the title of the longest-running American sitcom and animated program. It satirizes American culture, society, and television, using humor to address complex societal issues.

In the 21st century, The Simpsons has continued to evolve, incorporating current events and pop culture into its episodes. The show’s ability to adapt while maintaining its core humor has kept it fresh and engaging for new generations of viewers. With over 30 seasons and countless memorable moments, The Simpsons remains a cultural phenomenon.

3. Adventure Time (2010-2018)

Adventure Time, created by Pendleton Ward, revolutionized children’s television with its unique blend of whimsy, adventure, and deep emotional storytelling. The series follows the adventures of Finn the Human and Jake the Dog in the Land of Ooo, a post-apocalyptic world filled with bizarre creatures and magical landscapes.

The show’s vibrant animation style, combined with its surreal humor and complex character arcs, allows it to explore themes of friendship, love, and loss. Adventure Time has been praised for its ability to resonate with both children and adults, making it a landmark series in modern animation. Its impact can be seen in the rise of other animated series that prioritize emotional depth and narrative complexity.

4. Bojack Horseman (2014-2020)

Bojack Horseman, created by Raphael Bob-Waksberg, is one of the most groundbreaking animated series of the 21st century. This Netflix original series follows the life of Bojack, a washed-up actor who happens to be a horse, as he navigates the challenges of fame, mental health, and personal relationships.

What sets Bojack Horseman apart is its unflinching exploration of depression, addiction, and self-destructive behavior. The series uses dark humor and a unique animation style to tackle heavy themes, making it both relatable and thought-provoking. Through its complex characters and poignant storytelling, Bojack Horseman has redefined what animated narratives can achieve.

5. Rick and Morty (2013-Present)

Created by Justin Roiland and Dan Harmon, Rick and Morty has become a cultural juggernaut since its debut in 2013. This science fiction animated series follows the adventures of an eccentric and often reckless scientist, Rick Sanchez, and his good-hearted but easily influenced grandson, Morty.

Combining dark humor, philosophical themes, and intricate storytelling, Rick and Morty has captivated audiences around the world. The series explores complex concepts such as existentialism, the multiverse, and the consequences of one’s actions, all while delivering laugh-out-loud moments. Its unique blend of humor and depth has made it a standout in the realm of adult animation.

6. Gravity Falls (2012-2016)

Gravity Falls, created by Alex Hirsch, is a beloved animated series that combines mystery, adventure, and humor. The story follows twins Dipper and Mabel Pines as they spend the summer with their great-uncle Stan in the mysterious town of Gravity Falls, Oregon.

The show is praised for its clever writing, memorable characters, and engaging plot twists. It masterfully balances humor with darker themes, appealing to younger audiences while also drawing in adult viewers. The intricate mysteries and character development keep fans invested, making Gravity Falls a standout series of the 21st century.

7. The Amazing World of Gumball (2011-2019)

The Amazing World of Gumball, created by Ben Bocquelet, is known for its unique animation style and innovative storytelling. Set in the fictional city of Elmore, the series follows Gumball Watterson, a blue cat, and his family as they navigate absurd situations and hilarious misadventures.

The show’s distinct blend of 2D and 3D animation, along with its clever humor and relatable themes, has made it a favorite among viewers of all ages. The Amazing World of Gumball is celebrated for its creativity and ability to tackle everyday issues in a fun and entertaining way.

8. Big Mouth (2017-Present)

Big Mouth, created by Nick Kroll, Andrew Goldberg, Mark Levin, and Jennifer Flackett, is an animated series that delves into the often awkward and tumultuous journey of adolescence. The show follows a group of middle school friends as they navigate the challenges of puberty, relationships, and self-discovery.

Using humor and frank discussions about topics often considered taboo, Big Mouth has sparked important conversations about sexuality, mental health, and the complexities of growing up. Its bold approach and relatable characters have resonated with audiences, making it a significant entry in the realm of adult animation.

9. Steven Universe (2013-2020)

Steven Universe, created by Rebecca Sugar, is a groundbreaking animated series that explores themes of identity, love, and acceptance. The story follows Steven, a young boy who is half-human and half-Gem, as he learns about his powers and the responsibilities that come with them.

The series has garnered acclaim for its representation of LGBTQ+ characters and its exploration of complex emotional themes. With its beautiful animation, catchy musical numbers, and heartfelt storytelling, Steven Universe has left a lasting impact on both fans and the animation industry.

10. Hilda (2018-Present)

Based on the graphic novels by Luke Pearson, Hilda is an enchanting animated series that follows the adventures of a fearless blue-haired girl as she navigates the magical wilderness filled with fantastical creatures. The series is known for its stunning animation, whimsical storytelling, and strong character development.

Hilda has been praised for its focus on themes of friendship, bravery, and the importance of nature. Its charm and creativity make it a delightful addition to the animated landscape of the 21st century, appealing to both children and adults alike.

Conclusion

The 21st century has brought forth a diverse array of animated series that have pushed the boundaries of storytelling and artistry. From the heartfelt narratives of Avatar: The Last Airbender and Steven Universe to the dark humor of Bojack Horseman and Rick and Morty, these shows have captivated audiences and left a lasting impact on the animation landscape.

As technology continues to evolve, the potential for animated series to explore new themes and narratives will only expand. The best animated series of the 21st century have not only entertained us but have also challenged us to think critically about ourselves and our world. Their legacy will undoubtedly inspire future generations of animators and storytellers to continue pushing the envelope and creating compelling content that resonates with audiences around the globe.
