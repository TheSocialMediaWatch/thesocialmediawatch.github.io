The Best Astronomy Podcasts to Follow


Astronomy is a field that captivates the imagination of many, from stargazing enthusiasts to professional scientists. The wonders of the universe, the mysteries of black holes, and the exploration of distant galaxies continue to spark curiosity across the world. While books and documentaries are traditional ways to learn about space, podcasts have become a popular medium for both casual listeners and hardcore space aficionados. They offer the convenience of learning on the go, whether you’re commuting, working out, or relaxing at home.

In this post, we’ll explore some of the best astronomy podcasts that offer in-depth discussions, fascinating facts, and expert insights into the cosmos. Whether you’re a beginner just starting your exploration of the universe or an experienced astronomer seeking advanced topics, there’s a podcast for every level of interest.

1. Planetary Radio: The Planetary Society Podcast

One of the most well-known astronomy podcasts, Planetary Radio is a fantastic resource for anyone passionate about space exploration. Hosted by Mat Kaplan, the podcast brings together the best minds in the field of astronomy, planetary science, and space missions. It covers a wide range of topics from robotic space exploration to human missions to Mars and beyond.

Why Listen?

Planetary Radio takes listeners behind the scenes of space missions, providing first-hand accounts from the people who are making space exploration a reality. The interviews with scientists, astronauts, and engineers shed light on the exciting developments taking place within the field. Additionally, the podcast includes updates on the latest astronomical discoveries, making it an invaluable resource for keeping up-to-date with the latest happenings in space.

2. StarTalk Radio

StarTalk Radio is a must-follow for anyone who loves a mix of humor and science. Hosted by renowned astrophysicist Neil deGrasse Tyson, this podcast blends insightful discussions on astrophysics and cosmology with pop culture and humor.

Why Listen?

With episodes featuring famous guests like Bill Nye, Richard Dawkins, and Seth MacFarlane, StarTalk Radio offers a fun yet informative take on the universe. Tyson’s ability to make complex scientific concepts accessible while injecting humor into the conversation makes the podcast engaging for both new learners and seasoned astronomy enthusiasts.

The show touches on a variety of topics, from space travel and black holes to the search for extraterrestrial life. Its unique blend of entertainment and education sets it apart from other astronomy podcasts.

3. The Infinite Monkey Cage

Hosted by physicist Brian Cox and comedian Robin Ince, The Infinite Monkey Cage brings a delightful mix of comedy and science, exploring the mysteries of the universe in an engaging, often lighthearted manner. This BBC-produced podcast is perfect for listeners who enjoy a humorous yet thought-provoking discussion about everything from quantum mechanics to the nature of time.

Why Listen?

Each episode is filled with wit and humor, making even the most complex topics more enjoyable and easier to understand. The podcast features a range of guest experts, including Nobel Prize winners, who share their knowledge and experiences in space science and physics. With its unique format and broad range of topics, The Infinite Monkey Cage stands out as one of the best astronomy podcasts available.

4. Cosmos with Neil deGrasse Tyson

Another offering from Neil deGrasse Tyson, Cosmos is an extension of the popular TV show Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey. The podcast covers similar themes, diving deep into topics like the Big Bang, the nature of the universe, and the evolution of life on Earth.

Why Listen?

Cosmos offers a more structured and thematic approach to discussing the universe, with each episode delving into a specific topic in great detail. Tyson’s narration is captivating and provides an excellent mix of history, science, and storytelling. This podcast is perfect for those who want to dive deeper into the cosmos and explore the origins and future of the universe in a way that is both informative and easy to understand.

5. Orbital Path

For those who want to explore the relationship between astronomy and society, Orbital Path is an excellent choice. Hosted by Michelle Thaller, an astronomer and assistant director for science communication at NASA, this podcast explores the intersection of space science and human life.

Why Listen?

Orbital Path offers unique insights into the human experience of space exploration. It goes beyond the technical aspects of astronomy, exploring the cultural, philosophical, and personal impacts of our understanding of the universe. Episodes cover topics such as the search for life beyond Earth, the ethics of space exploration, and the search for Earth-like planets. If you’re interested in how space affects life on Earth, this podcast provides a thought-provoking perspective.

6. Skywatcher’s Paradise

Skywatcher’s Paradise is a fantastic podcast for those who enjoy stargazing and want to learn how to make the most of their time observing the night sky. Hosted by amateur astronomers, this podcast provides tips, tricks, and techniques for observing stars, planets, and other celestial objects.

Why Listen?

For anyone who enjoys the beauty of the night sky, Skywatcher’s Paradise is a must-listen. The podcast covers topics such as how to identify constellations, the best times to observe particular celestial events, and the latest developments in amateur astronomy. Whether you’re using a telescope or just gazing at the stars with the naked eye, this podcast helps make the experience more rewarding.

7. The Cosmic Shed

For those who are passionate about astronomy and physics but enjoy a more informal, conversational style, The Cosmic Shed is a great option. Hosted by a group of scientists and science communicators, the podcast features discussions on a wide range of topics, from the latest astronomy news to the nature of black holes and dark matter.

Why Listen?

The Cosmic Shed stands out for its relaxed, friendly style. The hosts often engage in lively debates and discussions, making the podcast feel more like a chat between friends than a formal lecture. Whether they’re talking about space exploration or the mysteries of the universe, the hosts’ enthusiasm is contagious, and the episodes are always packed with fascinating information.

8. The Verge Science Podcast

For those who want a more broad, yet insightful look at science, technology, and astronomy, The Verge Science Podcast offers a well-rounded exploration of various topics. While it is not strictly dedicated to astronomy, it frequently covers important space-related developments, such as NASA missions and new discoveries in the field.

Why Listen?

The Verge Science Podcast is a great way to stay informed about the latest advancements in space exploration, planetary science, and other scientific fields. The episodes feature expert guests and deep dives into major scientific stories, providing listeners with a comprehensive understanding of current events in science and space.

9. NASA’s Unexplained Files

If you’re a fan of the mysterious and the unexplained, NASA’s Unexplained Files might be the perfect podcast for you. It explores the weird and unexplained phenomena encountered by NASA during its missions and observations. From strange sounds recorded in space to mysterious signals from distant stars, this podcast delves into the things that don’t always have an easy answer.

Why Listen?

For those with a deep interest in the unknown, NASA’s Unexplained Files offers an intriguing look at the mysteries that remain unsolved in the world of space exploration. The podcast uncovers cases where NASA scientists have encountered strange phenomena that don’t quite fit into our current understanding of the universe. It’s a captivating listen for anyone fascinated by the unknown.

10. AstroCast

For listeners who want quick, bite-sized episodes on the latest in astronomy, AstroCast offers short but informative discussions on the most recent discoveries and events in the world of space.

Why Listen?

AstroCast is perfect for those who are short on time but still want to stay informed about the latest in astronomy. Each episode typically runs between 10-15 minutes, making it easy to listen to during a short break or while commuting. Despite the brief format, the podcast still provides plenty of fascinating insights into the cosmos, from new star discoveries to the latest space missions.

Conclusion

Whether you’re an avid stargazer, a professional astronomer, or simply someone with a curiosity about the universe, there is a podcast out there for you. These top astronomy podcasts offer a diverse range of content, from interviews with experts to discussions about the latest discoveries, to fun and engaging stories about the cosmos. By subscribing to these podcasts, you’ll gain new insights, stay informed about the latest space missions, and get closer to understanding the vastness and mysteries of the universe.

So grab your headphones, tune in, and embark on an auditory journey through the stars.
