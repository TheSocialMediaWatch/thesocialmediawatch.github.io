The Dos and Don'ts of Blogging Etiquette


When it comes to blogging, many of us focus on the technical aspects of creating engaging content, optimizing for search engines, and building a loyal audience. However, there are certain unwritten rules that can make or break your online presence. In this post, we’ll explore the dos and don’ts of blogging etiquette, and provide you with valuable insights to improve your online reputation.

The Dos

1. Be respectful and considerate

When commenting on other blogs, always be respectful and considerate of the author’s opinions and views. Avoid being confrontational or aggressive, as this can lead to a negative experience for both parties.

Example: Instead of saying “I disagree with your point of view,” say “I understand where you’re coming from, but I have a different perspective. Here’s my take on it…”

2. Be authentic and transparent

Be honest and authentic in your writing, and always disclose any biases or conflicts of interest. This helps build trust with your audience and establishes credibility.

Example: If you’re reviewing a product or service, disclose whether you received it for free or if you have a personal connection to the company.

3. Be concise and clear

Keep your writing concise and clear, avoiding jargon and complicated terms that may confuse your readers. Break up long paragraphs into shorter ones, and use headings and subheadings to make your content scannable.

Example: Instead of writing a lengthy paragraph, break it up into three shorter ones with headings like “The Problem,” “The Solution,” and “Conclusion.”

4. Be responsive and engaged

Respond to comments and messages in a timely and professional manner. This shows that you value your readers’ feedback and are committed to building a community around your blog.

Example: If someone leaves a comment on your blog, respond within 24 hours with a thoughtful and engaging response.

5. Be patient and persistent

Don’t get discouraged if your blog doesn’t take off immediately. Building a successful blog takes time, effort, and perseverance. Stay focused, and keep creating high-quality content.

Example: Don’t get discouraged if your blog gets only a few visitors per day at first. Keep creating content, engaging with your audience, and promoting your blog on social media.

The Don’ts

1. Don’t be spammy or promotional

Avoid using your blog as a platform to promote your products or services excessively. This can lead to a negative experience for your readers and damage your reputation.

Example: Instead of writing a blog post that’s nothing but a sales pitch, write a valuable and informative piece that provides real value to your readers.

2. Don’t be confrontational or aggressive

Avoid being confrontational or aggressive in your comments or social media interactions. This can lead to a toxic online environment and damage your online reputation.

Example: Instead of saying “You’re wrong and I’m right,” say “I understand where you’re coming from, but I have a different perspective. Here’s my take on it…”

3. Don’t be dishonest or misleading

Avoid being dishonest or misleading in your writing, and always fact-check your information before publishing. This helps maintain your credibility and trust with your audience.

Example: If you’re writing about a study or statistic, make sure to fact-check it before publishing. Avoid using fake or misleading information to support your argument.

4. Don’t be overly critical or negative

Avoid being overly critical or negative in your writing, as this can be off-putting to your readers. Instead, focus on providing constructive feedback and suggestions.

Example: Instead of saying “This product is terrible,” say “I was disappointed with this product, but here are some suggestions for improvement.”

5. Don’t be unprofessional or unresponsive

Avoid being unprofessional or unresponsive in your online interactions, as this can damage your reputation and credibility. Always maintain a professional tone and respond to messages and comments in a timely manner.

Example: If someone leaves a comment on your blog, respond within 24 hours with a thoughtful and engaging response. Avoid using slang or jargon in your responses.

In conclusion, blogging etiquette is essential for building a successful and reputable online presence. By following the dos and don’ts outlined above, you can establish trust with your audience, build a loyal following, and maintain a positive online reputation. Remember to always be respectful, authentic, and transparent in your writing, and to prioritize building a community around your blog. With time, effort, and perseverance, you can create a successful and engaging blog that resonates with your audience.
