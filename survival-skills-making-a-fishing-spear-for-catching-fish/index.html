Survival Skills: Making a Fishing Spear for Catching Fish


Fishing is an essential skill for survival in the wild. When you find yourself in a situation where food is scarce, knowing how to catch fish can mean the difference between starvation and survival. In this blog post, we will discuss a valuable survival skill: making a fishing spear.

Materials Needed

To make a fishing spear, you will need a few basic materials. These include:

  • A sturdy branch or pole: Look for a straight and strong branch or pole that is about 5 to 6 feet long. This will serve as the main body of your fishing spear.
  • A sharp knife: You will need a knife to carve and shape the spearhead.
  • Paracord or strong rope: This will be used to secure the spearhead to the branch.

Step 1: Choosing the Right Branch

Start by finding a suitable branch or pole. Look for one that is long and straight, with a diameter of about 1 to 1.5 inches. This will provide the necessary strength and stability for your fishing spear.

Step 2: Carving the Spearhead

Using your knife, carve the spearhead at one end of the branch. The spearhead should be sharp and pointed, allowing it to penetrate the fish easily. Shape the spearhead by removing bark and smoothing the surface. Be careful while carving to avoid any accidents.

Step 3: Securing the Spearhead

Once you have carved the spearhead, it’s time to secure it to the branch. Cut a length of paracord or rope, approximately 2 feet long. Tie one end of the cord securely around the base of the spearhead, ensuring it is tightly fastened. Wrap the remaining length of the cord around the spearhead and the branch, creating a tight and secure binding. This will prevent the spearhead from coming loose while fishing.

Step 4: Testing the Spear

Before using your fishing spear, it is important to test its strength and balance. Swing the spear gently and check if it remains stable. If it feels unbalanced or wobbly, adjust the binding or carving as needed to improve its stability.

Step 5: Fishing with the Spear

To catch fish with your newly crafted fishing spear, choose a suitable location near a body of water. Stand near the edge and look for fish swimming close to the surface. When you spot a fish, aim the spearhead towards it and throw the spear with a quick and forceful motion. Aim to pierce the fish directly, as this will increase your chances of a successful catch.

Conclusion

Knowing how to make a fishing spear can be a valuable survival skill when you find yourself in a situation where food is scarce. By following these steps and practicing your technique, you can increase your chances of catching fish and ensuring your survival in the wild.

Remember, fishing with a spear requires practice and patience. It may take time to master the skill, but with perseverance, you can become proficient in catching fish using this primitive yet effective method. Stay safe and always prioritize your well-being when venturing into the wilderness.
