The Best Campgrounds for Scenic Views


When it comes to camping, one of the most sought-after experiences is waking up to breathtaking natural beauty. Whether it’s a serene mountain vista, a tranquil lakeside sunrise, or a sprawling forest filled with wildlife, the best campgrounds offer spectacular views that enhance the overall experience. For nature lovers and outdoor enthusiasts, selecting the perfect campground can make all the difference in creating unforgettable memories. Here, we will explore some of the best campgrounds around the country that offer scenic views that will take your breath away.

1. Yosemite National Park - California

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Yosemite National Park is an iconic destination for anyone who loves to immerse themselves in the beauty of the natural world. With its towering granite cliffs, cascading waterfalls, and giant sequoia trees, the park offers some of the most magnificent views you can find in any campground. The park’s diverse landscape offers something for everyone, from peaceful meadows to rugged mountain peaks.

Best Campground for Views

  • Glacier Point Campground – One of the best spots for panoramic views of Yosemite Valley, Half Dome, and the High Sierra peaks. Imagine enjoying a warm campfire as the sun sets behind the granite formations. Glacier Point provides one of the most stunning viewpoints in the park, especially at sunrise or sunset.
  • North Pines Campground – Situated along the Merced River, North Pines Campground offers incredible views of the surrounding cliffs and the iconic Cathedral Spires. The combination of riverfront camping and dramatic mountain vistas is what makes this campground a favorite for those seeking both serenity and beauty.

2. Great Smoky Mountains National Park - Tennessee/North Carolina

Why It’s a Must-Visit

The Great Smoky Mountains are known for their ethereal mist that rolls over the landscape, providing a hauntingly beautiful backdrop for any camping experience. This national park is home to diverse wildlife, rich biodiversity, and an array of hiking trails that offer breathtaking views from every angle.

Best Campground for Views

  • Elkmont Campground – One of the most popular campgrounds in the Smokies, Elkmont offers easy access to trails like the Elkmont Nature Trail and the nearby Jakes Creek Trail. Campers can enjoy the peaceful forest setting with views of the distant mountain ridges.
  • Cataloochee Campground – This remote campground offers views of rolling hills and the chance to spot elk in the surrounding meadows. If you enjoy a mix of scenic beauty and wildlife watching, Cataloochee provides an authentic, off-the-beaten-path experience.

3. Banff National Park - Alberta, Canada

Why It’s a Must-Visit

For those looking to venture beyond the U.S., Banff National Park in Alberta, Canada, offers a jaw-dropping landscape of turquoise lakes, rugged mountains, and sprawling valleys. Known for its stunning natural beauty, Banff is a true outdoor paradise where you can camp under the shadow of the Canadian Rockies.

Best Campground for Views

  • Two Jack Lakeside Campground – Set on the shore of Two Jack Lake, this campground offers pristine views of the surrounding mountains, especially Mount Rundle. The tranquil waters reflect the peaks, creating a picturesque setting that’s perfect for photographers and nature lovers alike.
  • Tunnel Mountain Campground – If you’re looking for a more accessible campground with breathtaking views, Tunnel Mountain offers a panoramic view of Banff’s iconic peaks, including the Banff Springs Hotel and Mount Cascade. The campground’s proximity to the town of Banff makes it a great base for exploring the area.

4. Acadia National Park - Maine

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Acadia National Park in Maine is known for its rugged coastline, lush forests, and charming mountain views. What sets Acadia apart is the opportunity to enjoy both coastal and forested camping experiences. The park’s proximity to the Atlantic Ocean offers spectacular vistas that change with the tide and seasons.

Best Campground for Views

  • Blackwoods Campground – Located on the eastern side of Mount Desert Island, Blackwoods offers stunning views of the surrounding ocean and the mountainous terrain. As one of the most popular campgrounds in the park, it’s perfectly situated to enjoy sunrise over the ocean and the changing colors of the landscape.
  • Seawall Campground – This quieter campground on the western side of the island offers a serene atmosphere with a view of the ocean, especially at sunset. Seawall is less crowded than other campgrounds, making it perfect for those seeking solitude along the shore.

5. Glacier National Park - Montana

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Glacier National Park, located in the northern Rocky Mountains, is known for its dramatic peaks, crystal-clear lakes, and alpine meadows. This park offers some of the most stunning natural scenery in the United States, and the campgrounds provide a front-row seat to the magnificence of the area.

Best Campground for Views

  • Apgar Campground – Nestled near the shores of Lake McDonald, Apgar Campground offers a stunning view of the surrounding mountains. The campground is surrounded by dense forests and is just a short distance from the lake, where you can enjoy activities like kayaking or hiking along the scenic shoreline.
  • Many Glacier Campground – Located in the heart of Glacier National Park, Many Glacier offers breathtaking views of the surrounding mountain ranges, including Mount Gould and the Continental Divide. It’s a popular campground for hikers who want to explore the numerous trails in the area, with many offering views of nearby lakes and glaciers.

6. Zion National Park - Utah

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Zion National Park is a favorite among hikers and outdoor enthusiasts due to its dramatic red rock formations and canyons. The park’s towering sandstone cliffs and narrow slot canyons create a unique, awe-inspiring landscape. Camping in Zion allows visitors to be fully immersed in the striking beauty of the desert landscape.

Best Campground for Views

  • Watchman Campground – Located near the park’s entrance, Watchman Campground offers incredible views of the Watchman, a prominent sandstone peak. The proximity to Zion Canyon allows campers to easily explore iconic hiking trails like Angels Landing and the Narrows while enjoying unbeatable views from their campsites.
  • South Campground – Situated along the Virgin River, South Campground offers serene views of the canyon and nearby cliffs. It’s a great spot for those who want to wake up to the sounds of the river and enjoy a peaceful atmosphere before heading out on the trails.

7. Lake Tahoe - California/Nevada

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Lake Tahoe, located in the Sierra Nevada Mountains, is known for its stunning alpine scenery and crystal-clear waters. With a variety of campgrounds around the lake, you’ll find a range of camping experiences, from forested sites to lakefront views.

Best Campground for Views

  • DL Bliss State Park – Situated on the western shore of Lake Tahoe, DL Bliss offers panoramic views of the lake and surrounding mountains. It’s a perfect spot for those who enjoy hiking, swimming, and boating. The park’s scenic beauty makes it one of the top campgrounds for breathtaking lake views.
  • Fallen Leaf Campground – Located near South Lake Tahoe, Fallen Leaf Campground is nestled in a forested area with stunning views of Fallen Leaf Lake and Mount Tallac. It’s a peaceful spot where campers can enjoy a tranquil atmosphere while taking in the surrounding beauty.

8. Rocky Mountain National Park - Colorado

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Rocky Mountain National Park is a haven for nature lovers, offering stunning vistas of snow-capped peaks, alpine lakes, and lush meadows. The park’s diverse ecosystems and high-altitude trails make it a perfect spot for those seeking both adventure and scenic beauty.

Best Campground for Views

  • Moraine Park Campground – Located at an elevation of 8,160 feet, Moraine Park offers fantastic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The campground is also home to an abundant variety of wildlife, including elk, making it an ideal location for nature watchers.
  • Timber Creek Campground – Situated in the western part of the park, Timber Creek offers quiet, secluded camping with views of the Never Summer Mountains. It’s perfect for those who want to escape the crowds and enjoy a peaceful camping experience in the heart of the Rockies.

9. Shenandoah National Park - Virginia

Why It’s a Must-Visit

Shenandoah National Park, located along the Blue Ridge Mountains, offers sweeping vistas, diverse wildlife, and a variety of hiking trails. The park is known for its beauty in every season, with spring wildflowers, summer greenery, autumn foliage, and winter snow-covered peaks.

Best Campground for Views

  • Big Meadows Campground – Situated in the central part of the park, Big Meadows offers spectacular views of the Blue Ridge Mountains and expansive meadows filled with wildflowers in the summer. The campground is a popular spot for those hiking the Appalachian Trail, which runs through the park.
  • Loft Mountain Campground – Loft Mountain provides incredible views of the Shenandoah Valley and the surrounding mountains. Located at an elevation of 3,000 feet, it offers some of the highest views in the park, especially during sunset.

Conclusion

Whether you’re a seasoned camper or a first-timer, choosing a campground with incredible views can transform your outdoor experience. From the majestic peaks of the Rockies to the serene shores of tranquil lakes, these campgrounds provide a perfect mix of adventure and relaxation. No matter where you choose to camp, one thing is certain: the beauty of nature is best experienced when you’re surrounded by it. So, pack your gear, head out to one of these stunning locations, and let the scenic views leave you speechless.
