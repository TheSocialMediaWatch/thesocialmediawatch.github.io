The Best Fishing Knives for Every Angler


Fishing is more than just a pastime; it’s a passion, a lifestyle for many. Whether you’re out on the lake, deep in the ocean, or casting your line in a stream, the right tools can make all the difference. One of the most crucial tools for an angler is a good fishing knife. A high-quality knife can be the difference between a frustrating fishing trip and a successful one.

A fishing knife serves a variety of purposes: cutting lines, filleting fish, and preparing bait. Some knives are designed specifically for these tasks, while others are multipurpose tools that can handle a range of tasks related to fishing. With so many options available, choosing the right knife can be a bit overwhelming. To help you make an informed decision, we’ve rounded up the best fishing knives for every type of angler.

Factors to Consider When Choosing a Fishing Knife

Before diving into the best knives available on the market, it’s important to understand what to look for when purchasing a fishing knife. Here are the key factors to keep in mind:

1. Blade Material

The blade material is one of the most important factors when choosing a fishing knife. The best knives are made from high-carbon steel, stainless steel, or ceramic. High-carbon steel blades are known for their sharpness and ability to hold an edge, but they can rust easily if not properly cared for. Stainless steel is more corrosion-resistant, making it a good option for saltwater fishing. Ceramic blades, while incredibly sharp, are more fragile and require extra care.

2. Blade Length

Fishing knives come in various blade lengths, typically ranging from 4 to 9 inches. A longer blade can help with tasks like filleting large fish, while a shorter blade offers more control for tasks like gutting or cutting lines. The right blade length will depend on the types of fish you’re targeting and the specific tasks you need to perform.

3. Handle Material

The handle is just as important as the blade. A good handle should provide a secure and comfortable grip, even when wet. Materials like rubber, textured plastic, or synthetic composites are popular because they offer a non-slip surface. Ergonomics also play a role in reducing hand fatigue during long fishing trips.

4. Sheath

A quality sheath is essential for safe storage and easy access to your knife. Look for a sheath that is made from durable material like leather, nylon, or plastic. It should securely hold the knife in place, preventing it from slipping out accidentally.

5. Corrosion Resistance

Fishing knives are often exposed to water, especially saltwater. Therefore, a knife that is resistant to rust and corrosion is crucial. Stainless steel knives are a great choice for anglers who fish in saltwater, but make sure they are made with marine-grade stainless steel to prevent rust.

Top Fishing Knives for Every Angler

Now that you know what to look for, let’s take a look at some of the best fishing knives for every angler, regardless of experience or location.

1. Morakniv Companion Heavy Duty

The Morakniv Companion Heavy Duty is a favorite among anglers for its durability, sharpness, and versatility. This knife features a 4.1-inch stainless steel blade that is razor-sharp out of the box. The blade is made from high-carbon steel, ensuring it holds an edge for long periods. The ergonomic, rubberized handle provides a secure grip, even in wet conditions.

This knife is ideal for anglers who need a reliable, all-purpose tool for cutting line, preparing bait, or even filleting smaller fish. Its affordable price point makes it an excellent choice for both novice and experienced anglers.

2. Benchmade 15500 Fixed-Blade Knife

For those looking for a high-end, premium fishing knife, the Benchmade 15500 Fixed-Blade Knife is a top choice. This knife boasts a full tang, stainless steel blade that is corrosion-resistant, making it perfect for saltwater fishing. The 3.5-inch blade is designed for precision, allowing anglers to perform detailed tasks such as filleting fish or cleaning them with ease.

The Benchmade 15500 is known for its exceptional build quality. The textured G10 handle provides a firm, non-slip grip, ensuring the knife stays secure in your hand during wet conditions. Benchmade knives are made to last, and this model is no exception.

3. Gerber River Shorty Fixed Blade Knife

The Gerber River Shorty is a compact, versatile fishing knife designed for maximum portability and convenience. Its 3.5-inch blade is made from corrosion-resistant stainless steel, making it ideal for both freshwater and saltwater fishing. The blade is curved to provide excellent control for tasks such as cutting lines or removing hooks.

What sets the Gerber River Shorty apart is its unique, brightly-colored handle, which makes it easy to locate in your tackle box or on the boat deck. The ergonomic handle is designed for comfort and security, even when your hands are wet.

4. Victorinox Fillet Knife

When it comes to filleting fish, few knives perform as well as the Victorinox Fillet Knife. Known for its precision and sharpness, the Victorinox fillet knife features a flexible, 6-inch blade that makes it easy to remove skin and bones from fish. The blade is made from high-carbon stainless steel, providing excellent edge retention and resistance to corrosion.

The knife’s ergonomic, non-slip handle ensures comfort and safety, even during extended use. Whether you’re working with trout, salmon, or larger fish, the Victorinox Fillet Knife is a reliable tool that will make your fish-cleaning process smoother and more efficient.

5. Rapala Superflex Fillet Knife

Rapala is a well-known name in the fishing world, and their Superflex Fillet Knife is one of the best options for anglers looking to fillet fish with precision. This knife features a flexible, 7-inch blade made from stainless steel that is highly resistant to rust and corrosion. The Superflex design allows for maximum maneuverability, making it easier to work around bones and skin.

The handle of the Rapala Superflex is made from a textured, non-slip material that ensures a secure grip in wet conditions. The knife also comes with a protective sheath for safe storage, making it an excellent choice for those who need a reliable filleting tool.

6. Spyderco Pacific Salt 2

If you’re an angler who frequently fishes in saltwater, the Spyderco Pacific Salt 2 is an excellent knife to consider. This knife is designed with corrosion resistance in mind, featuring a blade made from Spyderco’s exclusive H-1 steel. H-1 steel is completely rustproof, ensuring your knife will remain sharp and functional even in the harshest saltwater environments.

The Pacific Salt 2 features a 3.5-inch blade that is perfect for cutting through fish bones, cleaning fish, or preparing bait. The handle is made from textured fiberglass-reinforced nylon, which provides a firm, secure grip even in the wettest conditions. The knife also features Spyderco’s signature thumb hole for easy, one-handed opening.

7. Buck Knives 119 Special

The Buck Knives 119 Special is an iconic American-made knife that has earned a reputation for its durability and performance. Featuring a 6-inch, high-carbon stainless steel blade, the Buck 119 is ideal for larger fish and heavy-duty tasks. The blade retains its sharpness well, making it an excellent choice for cutting through tough materials like fish bones.

The full tang construction ensures strength and stability, while the ergonomic handle made of leather or plastic provides a comfortable grip. Although the Buck 119 is slightly larger than most fishing knives, its versatility makes it an excellent tool for anglers who require a knife that can handle a range of tasks.

8. Cold Steel Recon 1 Folding Knife

While most fishing knives are fixed-blade, the Cold Steel Recon 1 is a folding knife that can still handle all the tasks an angler needs. This knife features a 4-inch blade made from premium AUS-8A stainless steel, known for its durability and resistance to corrosion. The blade is designed with a clip point, which makes it ideal for precise cuts and tasks like cutting lines or removing hooks.

The folding mechanism is smooth and secure, ensuring the knife remains safely closed when not in use. The handle is made from G-10 material, providing a non-slip grip, even when wet. The Cold Steel Recon 1 is an excellent choice for anglers who prefer the portability and convenience of a folding knife without sacrificing performance.

Conclusion

A quality fishing knife is an essential tool for any angler. Whether you’re cutting through tough fishing lines, filleting fish, or preparing bait, the right knife will make your experience more efficient and enjoyable. With so many options available, it’s important to consider your specific needs when choosing a fishing knife. Whether you need a compact, versatile knife for everyday tasks or a specialized fillet knife for delicate fish preparation, the knives listed above are some of the best on the market.

Remember to choose a knife that suits your style of fishing, the conditions in which you’ll be fishing, and the tasks you need to perform. A well-chosen fishing knife can become your trusted companion on every fishing trip.
