How to Make Your Own Homemade Vegan Mayonnaise


If you’ve ever been frustrated by the lack of flavor or poor ingredients in store-bought vegan mayonnaise, you’re not alone. Thankfully, creating your own homemade vegan mayonnaise is not only incredibly easy but also ensures that you can control every ingredient, making it healthier and more flavorful. Whether you’re a seasoned vegan or just looking to reduce your animal product intake, this guide will walk you through the process of making your own creamy, rich, and completely plant-based mayonnaise at home.

Why Make Vegan Mayonnaise at Home?

Making your own mayonnaise gives you the freedom to customize the flavor to your liking. Most store-bought vegan mayo options contain preservatives, additives, or oils that might not align with your dietary preferences. Homemade vegan mayo, on the other hand, can be made using simple and wholesome ingredients, ensuring that you get a cleaner, fresher, and more personalized result.

Additionally, making your own mayonnaise can be more cost-effective in the long run. Once you have the basic ingredients, you’ll find that the amount you can make for the price of one jar of commercial vegan mayo is a great value.

What Ingredients Do You Need?

The beauty of vegan mayonnaise lies in the simplicity of the ingredients. Here are the essential components you need to get started:

1. Aquafaba (Chickpea Brine)

Aquafaba, the liquid from canned chickpeas or the cooking liquid of dried chickpeas, is a key ingredient in vegan mayo. It serves as an excellent egg substitute, mimicking the consistency and emulsifying properties of egg whites. You don’t need to worry about the flavor of chickpeas seeping into your mayo; the aquafaba has a neutral taste.

2. Oil (Neutral-Flavored)

The type of oil you choose affects the flavor and texture of the mayo. A neutral-flavored oil such as sunflower oil, canola oil, or grapeseed oil works best for a classic mayo flavor. You can experiment with olive oil for a more robust taste, but be careful, as olive oil can be overpowering in mayonnaise.

3. Vinegar or Lemon Juice

To provide the tang that mayonnaise is known for, you’ll need either vinegar or lemon juice. White wine vinegar, apple cider vinegar, or fresh lemon juice can all work wonderfully. This is where you can play around with flavor nuances to match your preference.

4. Dijon Mustard

Dijon mustard adds a subtle tangy flavor and helps with the emulsification process. It also gives your mayo a more complex, savory taste.

5. Salt

A pinch of salt enhances the overall flavor of the mayo. You can always adjust this based on your personal taste preferences.

6. Optional: Garlic Powder, Onion Powder, or Nutritional Yeast

To take your homemade vegan mayonnaise to the next level, you can add seasonings like garlic powder, onion powder, or nutritional yeast. These ingredients provide extra flavor and depth to the mayo.

The Basic Process of Making Vegan Mayonnaise

Step 1: Gather Your Ingredients

Before starting, ensure that all your ingredients are ready. It’s important to use room-temperature ingredients for a smooth emulsion. The aquafaba, oil, and mustard should all be at a similar temperature to ensure that the mayo thickens properly.

Step 2: Blend the Aquafaba and Mustard

Start by placing the aquafaba and Dijon mustard in a high-speed blender or food processor. Blend them together until they are well-combined and slightly frothy. This creates the foundation for your mayo’s texture.

Step 3: Slowly Add the Oil

With the blender or food processor running on low, begin slowly drizzling in the oil. It’s crucial to add the oil gradually—this allows it to emulsify with the aquafaba and mustard mixture, creating a thick, creamy consistency. If you add the oil too quickly, the mayo may break and fail to thicken properly.

Step 4: Add Vinegar or Lemon Juice, Salt, and Seasonings

Once the oil has been fully incorporated, add the vinegar or lemon juice, salt, and any optional seasonings like garlic powder or nutritional yeast. Blend until fully combined. Taste the mayo and adjust the seasoning to suit your preference. If you prefer a tangier mayo, add more vinegar or lemon juice.

Step 5: Check Consistency and Adjust

If the mayo is too thin for your liking, you can add a little more oil and blend again until the desired thickness is achieved. The mayo should be smooth, creamy, and spreadable.

Step 6: Store Your Vegan Mayonnaise

Once you’re satisfied with the texture and flavor, transfer your homemade vegan mayo into an airtight container or jar. Store it in the refrigerator, where it will keep for up to one week.

Troubleshooting: What to Do If Your Mayo Doesn’t Thicken

There’s nothing more frustrating than when your mayo doesn’t thicken properly, but don’t worry—this can be fixed! Here are a few common issues and their solutions:

  • Mayo is too thin: Add more oil slowly, continuing to blend. Sometimes, using a small amount of non-dairy milk can also help achieve a creamier consistency.
  • Mayo is too thick: Add a small amount of aquafaba or water to loosen the consistency. Blend again until smooth.
  • Mayo is too oily: If your mayo is too oily, you may have added the oil too quickly. Try adding a bit more mustard or vinegar to bring it back together. A few more seconds of blending might help too.

Flavor Variations for Your Vegan Mayonnaise

Now that you’ve got the basic recipe down, it’s time to get creative! Homemade vegan mayonnaise is incredibly versatile, and there are many variations you can experiment with to make it your own. Here are a few ideas:

1. Spicy Vegan Mayo

Add a teaspoon of hot sauce, sriracha, or chili paste to your mayo for a kick of heat. You can also mix in a pinch of cayenne pepper for an extra fiery bite.

2. Garlic Mayo

If you love garlic, try adding a clove of minced garlic to your mayo mixture. This creates a creamy garlic aioli-style mayo that’s perfect for sandwiches, fries, and dipping sauces.

3. Herb-Infused Mayo

Blend in fresh herbs like parsley, dill, or basil for a fresh and fragrant mayo. You can also use dried herbs if fresh ones are unavailable.

4. Smoky Vegan Mayo

For a smoky flavor, add a teaspoon of smoked paprika or liquid smoke. This variation is great for barbecue-style sandwiches or as a dip for roasted vegetables.

5. Curry Mayo

Incorporate a teaspoon of curry powder or garam masala to add an exotic flair to your vegan mayo. It pairs wonderfully with falafel or roasted sweet potato.

How to Use Vegan Mayonnaise

Homemade vegan mayonnaise is incredibly versatile and can be used in many different dishes. Here are some ideas:

  • Spread for Sandwiches and Burgers: Vegan mayo is the perfect creamy spread for your favorite sandwiches or veggie burgers. It adds moisture and flavor to otherwise dry fillings.
  • Dipping Sauce: Use it as a dip for fries, onion rings, or roasted vegetables. You can even add herbs and spices to create your own signature dipping sauce.
  • Salad Dressing: Mix it with a bit of vinegar or lemon juice and herbs to make a creamy salad dressing.
  • Pasta Salad: Combine it with pasta, veggies, and fresh herbs to create a delicious vegan pasta salad. It’s an excellent option for picnics or potlucks.
  • Coleslaw: Vegan mayo can be used as the base for a creamy coleslaw dressing. Just mix it with shredded cabbage, carrots, and any other desired ingredients.

Conclusion

Making your own homemade vegan mayonnaise is a simple, fun, and rewarding process. Not only does it allow you to control the quality and ingredients of your mayo, but it also opens up a world of flavor possibilities. With just a few basic ingredients and a little creativity, you can create the perfect mayo to suit your taste and dietary preferences. So why not give it a try? Your taste buds (and your wallet) will thank you!
