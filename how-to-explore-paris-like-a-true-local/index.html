How to Explore Paris Like a True Local


Paris is a city that transcends mere tourism. Its cobblestone streets, quaint cafés, and centuries-old architecture make it a place where every corner tells a story. But to truly experience Paris in its authentic form, one must go beyond the usual tourist attractions. In this guide, we’ll explore how to navigate Paris like a true local, uncovering the hidden gems, local customs, and unique ways to make the most of your Parisian adventure.

1. Embrace Parisian Cafés and Bistros

One of the most iconic aspects of Parisian life is its café culture. To explore Paris like a local, start your day in one of the city’s many cafés. However, avoid the tourist-packed spots near famous landmarks like the Eiffel Tower or Notre-Dame. Instead, opt for a smaller, more intimate bistro in neighborhoods like Le Marais, Montmartre, or the Latin Quarter.

In these local spots, enjoy a simple breakfast of a croissant and a café crème. But don’t rush! Parisians take their time at cafés, engaging in quiet conversation, people-watching, or reading the morning paper. So, sit back, relax, and soak in the authentic Parisian vibe.

Pro tip: Don’t ask for a “to-go” coffee in a Parisian café. Parisians rarely take their drinks to-go. Order your coffee and enjoy it at the table to truly embrace the local experience.

2. Stroll Through the Lesser-Known Arrondissements

Paris is divided into 20 arrondissements, each with its own unique charm and character. While the central arrondissements (like the 1st, 4th, and 7th) are home to well-known attractions such as the Louvre, the Eiffel Tower, and the Champs-Élysées, the real magic of Paris can often be found in the quieter, more residential areas.

Explore the hidden gems of the 11th, 12th, or 18th arrondissements, where you’ll find a blend of local markets, charming shops, and vibrant street art. In neighborhoods like Belleville, you can witness a cultural blend of old Paris and contemporary influences, offering a more authentic and less commercialized experience of the city.

3. Shop at Local Markets

No trip to Paris is complete without experiencing the city’s local markets. These markets offer a true taste of Parisian life, from fresh produce to artisanal products. Skip the overpriced souvenirs and head to one of the many open-air markets where locals shop for their daily needs.

Visit the Marché d’Aligre in the 12th arrondissement, a bustling market offering everything from fresh fruits and vegetables to local cheeses and meats. For a more intimate experience, head to Marché des Enfants Rouges in the Marais, one of the oldest covered markets in Paris. Here, you can sample everything from French pastries to Moroccan couscous.

Engage with the market vendors, who are often more than happy to share cooking tips or offer recommendations. It’s an excellent way to immerse yourself in the local lifestyle and gain insights into the Parisian food scene.

4. Indulge in Parisian Pastry Culture

Paris is the birthplace of some of the world’s most delicious pastries, and indulging in these sweet treats is an essential part of the local experience. While the famous patisseries such as Pierre Hermé and Ladurée are well-known for their macarons, local bakeries or boulangeries are where the true Parisian magic happens.

Start your day with a pain au chocolat or a pain de campagne (French country bread) from a local bakery. For something more indulgent, try a tarte Tatin or éclair from one of the many neighborhood pastry shops. If you’re feeling adventurous, explore the world of chouquettes—small, airy puffs of dough sprinkled with sugar, a Parisian favorite.

Don’t forget to enjoy your pastry with a café or hot chocolate in hand, as Parisians typically savor these treats slowly while enjoying the ambiance of their surroundings.

5. Take a Walk Along the Seine at Sunset

While the Eiffel Tower and the Arc de Triomphe are famous Parisian landmarks, nothing beats the serenity of a quiet walk along the banks of the Seine River at sunset. This is a favorite pastime for many locals who appreciate the calm beauty of the river as it winds through the city.

Pick up a bottle of wine and a few local cheeses, and head to one of the many quais (banks) that line the Seine. As the sun sets behind the bridges and buildings of Paris, you’ll have a perfect view of the city’s landmarks bathed in golden light.

If you’re looking for a more intimate experience, cross over to Île Saint-Louis or Île de la Cité, where the pace is slower, and you’ll find quaint cafés and quiet corners perfect for reflection.

6. Explore Hidden Courtyards and Passageways

Paris is full of hidden courtyards, passageways, and gardens that are often overlooked by tourists. These tucked-away spaces provide a unique opportunity to discover Paris away from the crowds.

Take a detour through the Passage des Panoramas in the 2nd arrondissement, a historic covered arcade filled with quirky boutiques, antique stores, and traditional restaurants. Or explore the Cour du Commerce Saint-André in the 6th arrondissement, where you’ll find cozy cafés and independent art galleries tucked within a beautiful stone courtyard.

These hidden gems offer an authentic slice of Parisian life, far removed from the bustle of the tourist hotspots.

7. Take Public Transportation Like a Parisian

One of the best ways to experience Paris like a local is to use the city’s excellent public transportation system. Parisians rely heavily on the Métro, buses, and even the RER (regional express train) to get around the city efficiently.

The Métro is fast, reliable, and relatively easy to navigate, with stations located throughout the city. To blend in, buy a Navigo card, which allows you to travel like a local without worrying about individual tickets. Avoid the rush hours, as Parisians are often on edge during their daily commutes, but when traveling off-peak, it’s an incredibly convenient and authentic way to explore the city.

Pro tip: Parisians are known for their politeness and orderliness when using public transport. Keep your voice low, give up your seat for those in need, and be mindful of personal space.

8. Experience Parisian Nightlife Beyond the Tourist Areas

Paris has a vibrant nightlife scene that extends well beyond the crowded bars in the city center. If you want to explore Paris after dark like a true local, head to some of the city’s more under-the-radar spots.

For a unique Parisian night out, visit the Canal Saint-Martin, where locals gather at trendy bars and cafés by the water. The area is filled with a bohemian atmosphere, offering a laid-back vibe that’s perfect for a relaxing evening.

For a more eclectic experience, try Le Marais, which has a mix of stylish cocktail bars, quirky wine cellars, and small jazz clubs. Parisians love to socialize over drinks, often accompanied by a game of pétanque (a French bowling game) or a lively discussion about the latest cultural trends.

9. Learn the Art of Parisians’ Slow Living

Paris is famous for its laid-back lifestyle. In contrast to the fast-paced nature of many other big cities, Parisians take their time, whether they’re dining, walking, or simply chatting with friends. To truly experience Paris like a local, you need to embrace this slower, more intentional approach to life.

Instead of rushing from one attraction to the next, give yourself time to linger in cafés, explore neighborhoods on foot, or simply sit in one of Paris’s many public parks and people-watch. It’s about savoring the little moments—whether it’s enjoying a pastry at sunrise or watching the evening lights flicker across the Seine.

10. Engage with the Locals

The final tip for exploring Paris like a local is to connect with the people who call this beautiful city home. Parisians are proud of their city, and many are happy to share their recommendations or give you an insider’s perspective on where to go and what to do.

Practice a few basic French phrases, even if it’s just a simple “Bonjour” (hello) or “Merci” (thank you). Making an effort to speak the local language is often appreciated and can open doors to more authentic interactions.

If you’re in the mood for something extra special, consider attending a local event, whether it’s a French cooking class, an art exhibition, or a local concert. This is the best way to experience Parisian culture firsthand and connect with the true essence of the city.

Exploring Paris like a local is about embracing the city’s pace, savoring its flavors, and uncovering the hidden corners that make it unique. By steering clear of the typical tourist traps and immersing yourself in the everyday life of Parisians, you’ll gain a deeper appreciation for this enchanting city and create memories that go far beyond the usual sightseeing experience. So, take your time, explore the local spots, and enjoy Paris as the locals do!
