The Best Music Documentaries to Watch This Year


Music documentaries have become an essential part of music culture, offering a unique glimpse into the lives of our favorite artists. With the rise of streaming services and social media, music documentaries have become more accessible than ever, allowing fans to delve deeper into the stories behind their favorite songs and albums. In this article, we’ll explore the best music documentaries to watch this year, covering a range of genres and styles.

1. “The Last Waltz” (1978)

Directed by Martin Scorsese, “The Last Waltz” is a classic music documentary that captures the final concert of the iconic rock band The Band. Featuring performances by Bob Dylan, Neil Young, and Joni Mitchell, among others, this documentary is a must-watch for any music fan. The film’s blend of music, interviews, and behind-the-scenes footage makes it a timeless classic.

2. “Stop Making Sense” (1984)

Directed by Jonathan Demme, “Stop Making Sense” is a concert film featuring the iconic new wave band Talking Heads. The documentary follows the band as they perform a series of concerts in 1983, featuring hits like “Psycho Killer” and “Burning Down the House.” The film’s unique blend of music and dance makes it a standout in the music documentary genre.

3. “The Decline of Western Civilization” (1981)

Directed by Penelope Spheeris, “The Decline of Western Civilization” is a documentary that explores the LA punk rock scene in the late 1970s and early 1980s. Featuring interviews with artists like Black Flag, X, and The Germs, this documentary provides a unique glimpse into the early days of punk rock.

4. “The Last Days of Left Eye” (2006)

Directed by Lauren Schwartzman, “The Last Days of Left Eye” is a documentary that explores the life and career of Lisa “Left Eye” Lopes, the rapper and songwriter from the R&B group TLC. The film features interviews with Lopes’ friends and family, as well as archival footage of the group’s performances and music videos.

5. “The September Sessions” (2004)

Directed by Danny Clinch, “The September Sessions” is a documentary that captures the reunion of the rock band Eric Clapton and B.B. King in 2004. The film features performances by the two guitar legends, as well as interviews and behind-the-scenes footage.

6. “The T.A.M.I. Show” (1964)

Directed by Steve Binder, “The T.A.M.I. Show” is a documentary that captures a 1964 concert featuring some of the biggest names in music at the time, including James Brown, The Beach Boys, and The Rolling Stones. The film’s blend of music and comedy makes it a standout in the music documentary genre.

7. “Shut Up and Play the Hits” (2012)

Directed by Dylan Southern and Will Lovelace, “Shut Up and Play the Hits” is a documentary that captures the final concert of the indie rock band LCD Soundsystem. The film features a live performance by the band, as well as interviews and behind-the-scenes footage.

8. “The Wrecking Crew” (2015)

Directed by Denny Tedesco, “The Wrecking Crew” is a documentary that explores the lives and careers of the musicians who played behind some of the biggest names in music in the 1960s and 1970s. The film features interviews with artists like Brian Wilson, Cher, and Dick Clark, among others.

9. “Montage of Heck: The Home Recordings” (2015)

Directed by Brett Morgen, “Montage of Heck: The Home Recordings” is a documentary that explores the life and career of the late musician Kurt Cobain. The film features home recordings and demos by Cobain, as well as interviews with his friends and family.

10. “The Apollo” (2017)

Directed by Roger Ross Williams, “The Apollo” is a documentary that explores the history of the iconic Apollo Theater in Harlem, New York. The film features interviews with artists like Patti LaBelle, Stevie Wonder, and Jamie Foxx, among others.

Conclusion

These music documentaries offer a unique glimpse into the lives and careers of some of the biggest names in music. From classic rock to hip-hop, these films provide a fascinating look at the music industry and the artists who make it happen. Whether you’re a music fan or just looking for something new to watch, these documentaries are sure to entertain and educate.
