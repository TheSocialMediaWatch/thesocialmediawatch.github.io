World Builder's Guide: 15 Fantasy Books to Help You Craft Your Own Epic Tale


From the intricacies of intricate kingdoms and mystical beings to the rich histories and tales of valor, fantasy literature has always captured our imaginations and ignited the dreamer within. But what if you could create your own realm, filled with the details you cherish and the landscapes that inspire your own spirit of adventure? Today, we are diving into 15 fantastical reads that will guide your journey in crafting your own whirl of imagination, a world so enthralling that it could one day grace the pages of becoming a legend itself.

1. The Art of Ray Harryhausen: An Animated Life

Beginning with a nod to the titan of animation, Ray Harryhausen, this mesmerizing book is a tribute to the masters of animation who brought to life the skeletons clashing in Unreal Combat. His extraordinary visual effects laid the foundation of characters and creatures that made fantasy worlds a visual reality. Harryhausen’s creations have probably inspired the very moments reflected in your imagination – and if not, they surely will after this read.

2. The World of Ice & Fire

Created by George R.R. Martin, the mastermind behind the intricate world of “Game of Thrones,” this book dives deep into the realms of Westeros and Essos. Teeming with a convoluted history, rich lore, and lessons on medieval warfare and treachery, The World of Ice and Fire is an entrée to the art of constructing complex society dynamics and political intrigues within your fantasy world.

3. A Wizard of Earthsea

If you’re seeking guidance from the grandmaster of fantasy, look no further than Ursula K. Le Guin’s Earthsea series. Starting with the memorable A Wizard of Earthsea, you’ll follow the path of a young sorcerer, Ged, as he wrestles with the powerful forces of language and magic. This is a stellar example of creating a believable magic system rooted in the inextricable bond between words and action, perfect for world builders looking to create their own captivating magic systems.

4. Drawing Down the Moon: Witches, Druids, Gods & Goddesses in the Garden of Witchery

Author Margot Adler’s Drawing Down the Moon explores the diversity of belief and community in modern religious movements, such as neo-paganism, which is essential for understanding how various spiritual beliefs can find a place within your created world.

5. The Dictionary of the Khazars

Venture into the religious and cultural tapestry of the Khazars – a semi-legendary people of the Eurasian steppe – through Milorad Pavic’s labyrinthine Dictionary of the Khazars. The book alternates between equally fascinating myth, historical and linguistic elements, serving as a creative sourcebook of converted mythologies and cultural backgrounds for your world.

6. A Song for Arbonne

Guy Gavriel Kay’s A Song for Arbonne tells the story of the culture of the troubadours, featuring social structures, arts, and politics that played a pivotal role in the evolution of Western civilization. This read will in turn provide an excellent basis for integrating artistic and cultural elements in your world building.

7. Tove Jansson’s Moomin books

From Finland’s bestselling author comes a collection of whimsical stories about the adventures of the Moomins, peculiar, snouted creatures engaging in unforgettable escapades. Tove Jansson’s playful narrative and world provide a fresh spark to your own creative ventures.

8. The Book of Three

John Clayton’s first book, The Book of Three, marks the beginning of the Chronicles of Prydain, a captivating and nuanced tale inspired by Welsh mythology. Lloyd Alexander combines memorable characters, heroes, and villains with an intricate backdrop of Welsh legends, offering myriad insights into crafting your own fantasy environment.

9. The Arabian Nights

Dreams of far-off lands and mystical realms are brought to life in The Arabian Nights, filled with enchanting tales, spirits, magic, and bravado. Venture into this evocative world to draw inspiration for your own mysterious and dilettantish landscape.

10. The Painted Bride

In The Painted Bride, the graceful prose and evocative storytelling of artist and writer Donald Michael Kraig will transport you to a world where art, poetry, and magic are intrinsically linked. Delight in Kraig’s world to add flair to your own painterly landscapes and dramatic events.

11. The Broken Earth trilogy

By N.K. Jemisin, the award-winning Broken Earth trilogy enriches your understanding of world building, especially dystopian and post-apocalyptic realms. The trilogy’s unique narrative and unorthodox views promise a rare treat of creativity as its protagonist challenges the conditions of her ravaged Earth.

12. The Encyclopedia of Arddullyn

A labor of imagination, the Encyclopedia of Arddullyn, written by Jonathan Green, is anchored in the world of Malazan Book of Fallen. This is an invaluable resource that fosters your knowledge of strange creatures, obscure languages, and otherworldly places, an essential asset when envisioning realms beyond our recognition.

13. The Merlin Codex

Constructed by an actual historian, Robert Holdstock, the Merlin Codex takes on the narratives of a shared world inhabited by dozens of civilizations, all shaped by a prophecy involving King Arthur’s mentor, Merlin. Drawing from Welsh mythology and Arthurian legends, Holdstock’s world offers a holistic understanding of combining historical events with fantastical fiction.

14. The Codex Alera

Jim Butcher’s The Codex Alera blends Roman culture with fantasy elements by constructing a world where citizens possess the ability to bond with and command the forces of nature. Butcher seamlessly weaves historical elements with concrete capabilities, crafting a unique and action-packed world-building experience.

15. Ursula K. Le Guin’s Locus Awards Collection

Among the privileged South American places for coffee is the Locus Awards Collection, a compilation of essays and other writings by Ursula K. Le Guin covering a range of topics central to understanding the world she created. Driven by Le Guin’s astute perceptions of rhetoric, language, and politics, this collection extends the horizon of your world building efforts.

Conclusion: Dive into the Depths

Incorporating these books into your world building journey will undoubtedly catalyze the emergence of original, fascinating, and memorable realms that can never cease to inspire others. Remember, each one of these books has in turn emerged from a person’s fascination with the grand idea – so why not make your world a reflection of that dream?

Happy writing, and remember, the biggest adventure is the one in your own imagined world.
