The Best Online Tutoring Platforms to Make Money


The internet has radically changed how education works, not only making learning more accessible but also creating unprecedented opportunities for skilled tutors worldwide. Whether you’re a seasoned educator, a university student with specialized knowledge, or someone passionate about teaching, online tutoring offers a flexible, rewarding way to earn extra income or even build a full-time business from anywhere.

With the sheer number of platforms available, knowing where to start or which site will provide the most lucrative and sustainable prospects can be overwhelming. Dive into this in-depth look at the best online tutoring platforms, highlighting their features, earning potential, requirements, and what sets them apart. By the end, you’ll have a clear roadmap to help you turn your teaching skills into a thriving income stream.

1. VIPKid

VIPKid has become a household name for teaching English to children primarily based in China and other Asian countries. What sets it apart is its focus on connecting North American tutors (Canada and US residents) with eager young learners looking for engaging English lessons.

Highlights:

  • Curriculum Provided: No lesson planning necessary; follow a pre-designed curriculum.
  • One-on-One Lessons: Focus on personalized instruction.
  • Booking Flexibility: Set your own availability and accept bookings that fit your schedule.
  • High Paying: Tutors can earn between $15 to $22 per hour based on experience and performance.

Requirements:

  • Bachelor’s degree in any field.
  • Eligibility to work in the US or Canada.
  • Teaching experience (including informal or volunteering).

If you’re a native English speaker and enjoy working with kids, VIPKid’s interface and steady demand make it a consistently popular choice.

2. Chegg Tutors

Chegg’s origins as a textbook seller quickly expanded into a massive tutoring marketplace. The platform boasts a wide variety of subjects, from algebra and calculus to computer science, engineering, humanities, and standardized test prep.

Highlights:

  • On-Demand Sessions: Students ask questions around the clock, so you can jump in whenever you have spare time.
  • Weekly Payouts: Prompt, reliable payments.
  • ** Global Reach**: Work with students worldwide on a huge range of topics.

Earnings:

  • Chegg pays around $20 per hour, with top tutors making more due to increased demand and high ratings.

Requirements:

  • Proof of college enrollment or degree.
  • Application process with subject tests.

Chegg’s flexibility and student volume make it a solid choice for university students and recent graduates looking for a stable part-time job or those testing the waters in online education.

3. Wyzant

Wyzant connects tutors with learners seeking help in both academic subjects and specialized skills like coding, languages, music, and business writing. It acts more like a marketplace than a managed service, offering more autonomy for tutors.

Highlights:

  • Set Your Own Rate: Tutors control their hourly rates, which can range from $20 to $80+ depending on expertise.
  • Diverse Subjects: Opportunities span far beyond K-12, ideal for those with unique skills.
  • Video and In-Person Sessions: Online and in some locations, face-to-face tutoring is possible.

How It Works:

  • Tutors create detailed profiles showcasing qualifications, experience, and specialties.
  • Students browse profiles, select tutors, and message them directly.
  • Wyzant takes a sliding commission, decreasing as you book more hours.

Wyzant suits those who prefer building relationships with repeat students and appreciate setting their own pricing strategy.

4. Tutor.com

With decades of experience, Tutor.com remains a trusted platform for K-12, college, and professional learners. Backed by The Princeton Review, its quality standards are high, as is the reliability for regular tutoring work.

Features:

  • Broad Subject Range: Over 40 subjects, including advanced STEM, business, essay review.
  • Flexible Scheduling: Work as much or as little as you like.
  • Integrated Tools: Shared whiteboards, chat, and file uploads enhance the digital classroom.

Compensation:

  • Pay rates are hourly and determined by subject and demand, generally between $12 and $20 per hour, with potential for bonuses.

Application Process:

  • Requires relevant degrees and subject tests, plus a background check.

Tutor.com is well-regarded for its professional support, training resources, and clear path to consistent bookings for qualified tutors.

5. Preply

Preply has rapidly gained attention thanks to its modern, user-friendly approach to language tutoring. However, it’s not limited to languages; tutors in math, science, arts, and hobbies also find steady clients here.

What Stands Out:

  • Global Student Base: Reach learners in over 180 countries.
  • Set Your Own Rates: Many tutors start lower to attract clients, but top experts often command $30+ per hour.
  • Recurring Sessions: Helps build stable, long-term income via ongoing language or skill development.

How to Get Started:

  • Submit a profile with a short intro video.
  • List your specialties, methodology, and availability.
  • Preply takes a commission (100% on the first lesson, then less on subsequent bookings).

Perfect for multilingual individuals or those seeking to teach conversational skills and cultural fluency, Preply rewards those who excel at self-marketing and relationship building.

6. Skooli

Skooli is ideal for those with a background in education who prefer academic tutoring in K-12 and college-level subjects, especially math, sciences, and languages.

Benefits:

  • On-Demand and Scheduled Lessons: Allowing both instant sessions and planned meetings.
  • Transparent Pay: Set at $25 per hour for qualified tutors.
  • Professional Environment: Emphasizes quality, requiring teaching credentials or advanced degrees.

Getting Hired:

  • Submit degrees and certifications for verification.
  • Pass qualification exams.

Skooli’s streamlined booking and premium rates make it a go-to for credentialed teachers and specialists.

7. Italki

For tutors fluent in more than one language, Italki offers a unique twist. Rather than focusing on set lesson plans, the platform encourages conversational education, cultural exchange, and flexible pricing.

Key Points:

  • Open Marketplace: Tutors set their lesson structures and pricing.
  • Community Features: Students often seek low-pressure conversation partners as well as formal instruction.
  • Diverse Earnings: Rates typically range from $10 to $40 per hour, depending on language rarity and tutor experience.

Requirements:

  • Solid grasp of the chosen teaching language(s).
  • Clear video introductory profile.

If you love sharing your language and culture, and enjoy flexible, informal lessons, Italki represents a community-oriented alternative.

8. Brainfuse

Brainfuse supports students and professionals at all levels with individualized advice in subjects from elementary math to professional writing and resume help.

Advantages:

  • 24/7 Availability: Engage with students at any time.
  • US-Based Population: Great fit for American educators wanting to support local learners.

How the Platform Works:

  • Tutors apply, complete subject tests, and verify credentials.
  • Pay is competitive, usually ranging from $10 to $20 per hour, depending on the subject.

Brainfuse’s blend of live chat, collaborative whiteboards, and focus on academic integrity attracts both K-12 and higher-ed tutors who want to make a positive impact.

9. Revolution Prep

Renowned for its rigorous approach and professional environment, Revolution Prep only hires full-time tutors and provides them with benefits such as paid training, healthcare, and ongoing development.

What’s Unique:

  • Full-Time, Salaried Positions: Consistency and security rare in this space.
  • Salary & Benefits: Earnings may top $40,000 annually.
  • Advanced Content: From SAT/ACT prep to AP classes and beyond.

Who is This For?

  • Teachers or subject matter experts seeking a stable, career-level commitment to online education.

For those seeking to make online tutoring a main career, Revolution Prep is a cut above.

10. TutorMe

TutorMe leverages AI-powered matching to pair tutors with students across hundreds of subjects, promising a streamlined experience for both parties.

Features:

  • Tutors often report earning $16–$26 per hour.
  • On-demand option means you can log in and pick up work as your schedule allows.
  • Extensive subjects, including computer science, test prep, and humanities.

Qualifications:

  • Must be at least 18, with experience and a strong academic background in your subjects.

TutorMe is suitable for quick side gigs or those building a broader client base.

11. Udemy and Skillshare (Bonus: Creating Educational Content)

While not traditional tutoring sites, platforms like Udemy and Skillshare empower educators to create courses on any topic—academic, professional, artistic, or lifestyle. Instead of direct one-on-one teaching, you record course material, upload it, and earn passive income each time students enroll.

Why Consider This Approach?

  • Passive Income: Earn while you sleep, as students watch your lessons worldwide.
  • Set Your Price and Content: Complete autonomy in subject, length, format.
  • Scalable: Reach thousands without managing live sessions.

If you can package your knowledge into a series of video lessons, these platforms are powerful for long-term earning potential and building a personal brand.

Choosing the Right Platform

Not every tutoring site is created equal, and the “best” one depends on your background, subject expertise, location, schedule, and personal preferences.

Consider:

  • Certification Level: Some prioritize or require teaching credentials; others are fine with subject matter expertise or lived experience.
  • Payout Method: Are you looking for stable weekly pay, or do you want the ability to set your own rates?
  • Student Population: Prefer younger kids, college students, or adults seeking professional upskilling?
  • Flexibility: Do you want maximum freedom, or security through a salaried position?
  • Subject Range: Some sites specialize in English/languages; others offer every academic and personal development area imaginable.
  • Marketing Support: Certain platforms actively match students to tutors, while others expect you to market yourself.

Maximizing Earnings as an Online Tutor

Building a steady income stream from online tutoring depends not just on picking the right platform, but also on smart strategies:

  1. Leverage Multiple Platforms: When starting out, list yourself on several websites to widen your reach and compare which delivers the best flow of students and rates.
  2. Stand Out With Your Profile: Invest time in a professional, friendly headshot and a compelling intro video. Highlight teaching style and what makes your lessons unique.
  3. Collect Reviews: After every session, politely request feedback. Glowing testimonials will boost your ranking and attract more students.
  4. Niche Down: Specialize in high-demand or advanced topics where fewer tutors compete. For example, SAT math, AP Chemistry, GRE preparation, or coding bootcamps.
  5. Upskill Constantly: Platforms and students gravitate toward tutors who demonstrate ongoing learning and certification.
  6. Be Responsive and Reliable: Quick replies and consistent session quality lead to repeat bookings and referrals.
  7. Offer Package Deals: If allowed, offer multi-session bundles at a discounted rate to encourage recurring packages.

Common Questions About Online Tutoring

1. How much can you really make?
Earnings vary massively. Entry-level tutors or those covering general subjects may start around $10–$15 per hour, while specialized experts can exceed $50 or more. Building a loyal clientele, offering premium services, or teaching rare subjects can push earnings much higher.

2. Do you need teaching credentials or a degree?
Some platforms require a university degree or teaching license, but plenty welcome those with only subject matter expertise or college experience, especially in language, creative, or coding categories.

3. What equipment do you need?
A reliable internet connection, a quality webcam, headset with microphone, and a distraction-free environment are usually the minimum. Some platforms may have specific software preferences.

4. Are online tutoring jobs legit?
The platforms listed above are all reputable and pay reliably. Be wary of sites that charge high upfront “registration” or “training” fees, or that promise unrealistically high earnings with little effort.

5. Is there room for growth?
Absolutely. Many tutors initially take online jobs for supplemental income, then transition to full-time careers, start their own agencies, or create digital courses for passive income.

Final Thoughts

Online tutoring is thriving, with more students than ever turning to digital learning for academic support, language acquisition, and personal development. Whether you want to supplement your income with flexible hours, build a reliable freelance career, or scale a thriving educational venture, the platforms above put the power in your hands, no matter your subject or specialty.

Choosing the right site—or combination of sites—for your unique skills and goals is the first step. Invest in your profile, keep learning, and soon you’ll find yourself rewarded not only with steady earnings but also the satisfaction of helping learners grow worldwide.

Ready to jump in? Pick your platform, polish your profile, and start connecting with students from across the globe. Your next great tutoring opportunity is just a click away.
