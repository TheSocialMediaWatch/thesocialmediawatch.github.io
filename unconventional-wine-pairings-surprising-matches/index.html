Unconventional Wine Pairings: Surprising Matches


Wine pairing is often seen as a sophisticated art, with strict rules dictating which wine should be paired with which dish. However, exploring unconventional wine pairings can lead to delightful surprises and a whole new world of flavors. In this blog post, we will dive into some unexpected wine pairings that will challenge your taste buds and expand your wine knowledge.

1. Popcorn and Champagne

Yes, you read that right. Popcorn, the quintessential movie snack, can be paired with Champagne for a unique and enjoyable experience. The light and crisp texture of popcorn complements the effervescence and acidity of Champagne, creating a harmonious combination. The buttery notes of the popcorn enhance the subtle flavors of the Champagne, resulting in a delightful contrast that is both refreshing and indulgent.

2. Pizza and Riesling

Pizza is a beloved comfort food that can be enjoyed in various forms, from cheesy Margherita to spicy pepperoni. While beer is often the go-to beverage for pizza, don’t overlook the surprising match of Riesling. The slight sweetness and vibrant acidity of Riesling cut through the richness of the pizza, balancing the flavors and cleansing the palate. Opt for a slightly off-dry Riesling to complement the savory elements of the pizza and elevate your dining experience.

3. Sushi and Beaujolais

Traditionally, sushi is paired with sake or white wine. However, the light and fruity red wine, Beaujolais, can be an unexpected match that brings out the flavors of the delicate fish and rice. The bright acidity and low tannins of Beaujolais complement the umami flavors of the sushi, creating a harmonious fusion of taste. Next time you indulge in a plate of sushi, consider reaching for a bottle of chilled Beaujolais for a surprising and delightful combination.

4. BBQ Ribs and Zinfandel

Barbecue ribs are a prime example of bold and flavorful dishes that can be challenging to pair with wine. However, the robust flavors of BBQ ribs find a perfect partner in Zinfandel. The full-bodied and fruit-forward nature of Zinfandel stands up to the smoky, tangy, and spicy flavors of the ribs, resulting in a mouthwatering experience. The rich fruitiness of Zinfandel complements the caramelized crust of the ribs, creating a heavenly match that will leave you craving for more.

5. Dark Chocolate and Cabernet Sauvignon

Chocolate and wine are often enjoyed separately, but when paired together, they create a luxurious and indulgent experience. Dark chocolate with a high percentage of cocoa can be paired with a bold and tannic Cabernet Sauvignon. The bitter-sweetness of the chocolate enhances the rich flavors of the wine, while the tannins in Cabernet Sauvignon soften the intensity of the chocolate. This unconventional pairing is a decadent treat for those who appreciate the finer things in life.

Conclusion

Exploring unconventional wine pairings opens up a world of possibilities and allows you to experience flavors in new and exciting ways. Whether it’s popcorn with Champagne, sushi with Beaujolais, or BBQ ribs with Zinfandel, breaking the traditional rules of wine pairing can lead to surprising matches that will delight your taste buds. So, step out of your comfort zone and embark on a journey of discovery as you explore the unconventional side of wine pairing. Cheers!
