Immortal Tales: 12 Supernatural Books for the Paranormal Aficionado


Are you a fan of the supernatural, the otherworldly, and things that go bump in the night? Then buckle up, because we’ve curated a list of 12 must-read supernatural books perfect for the paranormal aficionado! These haunting tales, spanning centuries and genres, will take you on a mesmerizing journey through the realms of the supernatural. So, dim the lights, grab your favorite beverage, and get ready for an unforgettable experience.

1. Dracula by Bram Stoker

Year: 1897

Genre: Gothic Horror

Ah, the classic that started it all – Bram Stoker’s Dracula is an undeniable must-read for any supernatural buff. Equally thrilling and terrifying, this unequaled vampire tale introduces us to Count Dracula, a seductive, sinister, and oddly charming figure. Moreover, the story offers a deep exploration of various themes, including the powers of love and faith, and is guaranteed to have you questioning the reality of the world around you.

2. Frankenstein by Mary Shelley

Year: 1818

Genre: Gothic Horror, Science Fiction, Romantic Fiction

This classic novel by Mary Shelley, often regarded as the quintessential science fiction novel, introduces us to Victor Frankenstein’s chilling and tragic tale of obsession and ambition. The gargantuan task of creating life leads to disastrous consequences – a reanimated creature, thanks to reckless experimentation and moral indifference, wreaks havoc on the world around it. The quintessential story of ambition and its consequences, Frankenstein has remained relevant for over 200 years, inspiring countless sequels, retellings, and adaptations.

3. The Picture of Dorian Gray by Oscar Wilde

Year: 1890

Genre: Gothic Horror, Philosophical Fiction

Oscar Wilde’s only novel is a tantalizing tale about a portrait, an instrument of temptation, and the nature of corruption. Dorian Gray, a strikingly handsome young man, finds his whole world view changed upon encountering the enigmatic painter Basil Hallward’s masterpiece – a portrait that seems to almost possess a primal, life-imbued force. Gray, as mesmerized by his reflection as we human beings are, utterly surrenders to hedonism and decadence and, in the process, sets the wheels of revenge in motion against his former self and mentor.

4. The Shining by Stephen King

Year: 1977

Genre: Horror, Psychological Thriller

For those who have ever fantasized about the idea of isolation in an abandoned, snow-covered hotel, The Shining brings to light a nightmarish scenario. This psychological horror extravaganza by Stephen King brings us to the Overlook Hotel, where Jack Torrance, affectionately referred to as “The Shining” within the confines of this maddening story, takes up residence with his family for the winter. With endless amounts of time to think and plenty of alcohol-soaked soliloquies, reality starts to blur for Jack, leading him down a destructive path that brands itself in the minds of horror lovers everywhere.

5. Salem’s Lot by Stephen King

Year: 1975

Genre: Horror, Supernatural Fiction

In this classic tale of vampiric terror, Stephen King treats readers to the small town of Jerusalem’s Lot, Maine, where darkness lurks in the shadows. Suspense masterfully builds as novelist Ben Mears returns to his childhood home, only to discover the eerie truths about his past and the unsettling presence that haunts the town. As the body count begins to rise, the battle between man and beast ensues in a bloody and unforgettable struggle.

6. The Turn of the Screw by Henry James

Year: 1898

Genre: Horror, Ghost Story, Psychological Fiction

Henry James’ chilling story, The Turn of the Screw, is a masterful work of psychological horror and a classic in the world of supernatural fiction. The tale follows a young governess who is hired to care for two young orphans at a sprawling estate only to find herself confronted by dark and unexplainable occurrences. A ghostly ex-servant, determined to claim her living, Nunes the coachman, and a young boy grappling with an evil presence all pose grave dangers to our unsuspecting governess.

7. The Vampire Lestat by Anne Rice

Year: 1985

Genre: Gothic Horror, Paranormal Fiction

Anne Rice’s gothic masterpiece, The Vampire Lestat, is an indulgence into the world of vampiric existence. This engrossing tale follows the extraordinary story of Lestat de Lioncourt, a fierce, powerful, and exquisitely charismatic vampire. The Vampire Lestat offers readers complete immersion into the rich, menacing, and beguiling world of the undead, putting forth compelling questions about the nature of evil and the morality of immortality.

8. Jonathan Strange & Mr Norrell by Susanna Clarke

Year: 2004

Genre: Fantasy, Historical Fiction, Magical Realism

Susanna Clarke’s intricate story is a masterful blend of historical fiction and magical fantasy, reminiscent of the magic hinted at in the works of Jane Austen. In this labyrinthine tale of magical duels, two gentlemen, Jonathan Strange and Mr Norrell, emerge as the most skillful practitioners of the arcane arts – a feat long thought to be missed. Their competition and rivalry intensify as they summon ancient spells and call to life beings thought to be myth; and through their magic, a war of words and dreams awakens the world to a new reality.

9. The Witchfinder’s Sister by Beth Underdown

Year: 2017

Genre: Historical Fiction, Supernatural Thriller

Beth Underdown’s gripping novel presents a unique perspective on the famous English witch trials. Set in the 17th-century English countryside, the story follows Tituba, the intriguing Barbadian slave forced to endure the fears and suspicions of the Salem Witch Trials. As the town’s tensions rise and her fate becomes increasingly uncertain, secrets, lies, and misunderstandings weave a spooky and mysterious tapestry that form the heart and soul of this unforgettable novel.

10. Bird Box by Josh Malerman

Year: 2014

Genre: Horror, Psychological Fiction

In a gripping tale of survival, Bird Box by author Josh Malerman introduces us to a harrowing world where the sight of the supernatural entities drives people to madness and imminent doom. A nameless-worldwhere resources are scarce and trust is a distant memory, our characters must navigate through this menacing landscape while trying to stay blindfolded and alive. A mix of psychological drama and horrifying suspense, Bird Box is a testament to human hope and determination in an unfathomable world.

11. The Jonestown by Gylen Whited

Year: 2015

Genre: Psychological Thriller, Supernatural Fiction

Gylen Whited’s original take on the classic horror story delves deep into fear’s darkest corners, exploring the unknown realms that exist between reality and nightmare. In this terrifying tale, a woman searches for her missing brother in the haunted woods that surround their childhood home. As she uncovers dark secrets about her family’s past, she must confront the frightening truth: every person has their own personal jonestown, a place in their inner existence where they feel vulnerable, afraid, and alone.

12. Dark Souls:andel by Pauline A. Christopher

Year: 2017

Genre: Gothic Horror, Psychological Fiction

Breathing life into the myth of the tenebrous and immortal soul, Pauline A. Christopher’s novel delves deep into the heart of darkness that lies within every human being. The story follows Rev. Father Allan Miklosi, who studies the corrupt bloodline of one of history’s most sinister entities – the demonic Lucifer. Miklosi learns about the origins of evil and the methods used by the malevolent beings to deceive mankind. As darkness befalls him, he must confront his own demons and choose between redemption or descending into the devil’s embrace.

These twelve supernatural novels offer a spine-tingling and unforgettable take on the darker side of the paranormal world. Each is a must-read for those who believe that fear and mystery are the most compelling forces in our lives, and who yearn for the chills that only a ghostly, monstrous tale can provide. Happy reading!
