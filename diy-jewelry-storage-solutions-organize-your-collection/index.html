DIY Jewelry Storage Solutions: Organize Your Collection


Jewelry is not only a statement of style but often holds sentimental value, making it essential to store it properly. A cluttered jewelry collection can become a frustrating mess, leading to tangled necklaces, misplaced rings, and lost earrings. Organizing your jewelry collection doesn’t need to be difficult or costly. With a little creativity and some DIY effort, you can create functional and aesthetically pleasing storage solutions that will keep your jewelry organized and easy to access.

In this post, we’ll explore a variety of DIY jewelry storage solutions to help you organize your collection, no matter how big or small. From simple storage boxes to repurposed furniture and wall-mounted racks, there’s a solution to fit every style and space.

1. Upcycled Drawer Organizer

One of the simplest ways to store jewelry is by using an old drawer or small box you already have. Rather than buying a commercial jewelry box, consider upcycling an old drawer into a custom jewelry organizer. This DIY solution works especially well if you want to keep your jewelry in separate compartments for easy access.

Materials Needed:

  • Old drawer (could be from an old dresser or desk)
  • Dividers or small containers
  • Felt or fabric (optional for lining)
  • Glue gun (optional)

How to Make It:

  1. Start by cleaning the drawer thoroughly. If the drawer is in good condition, you can leave it as-is, or you may want to paint it to give it a fresh look.
  2. Next, divide the drawer into sections to organize your jewelry. You can use dividers made from cardboard, wood, or even small containers to separate your earrings, rings, bracelets, and necklaces.
  3. For added protection, line the bottom of the compartments with felt or fabric. This soft lining will prevent your jewelry from scratching or tangling.
  4. Organize your jewelry by type and color. Rings can go in one section, earrings in another, and necklaces in yet another. If you’re short on space, you can also roll your chains and store them in a small container to prevent them from tangling.
  5. If you’d like, use a glue gun to secure the dividers in place.

With this DIY jewelry organizer, you’ll have an easy-to-use, customized solution that will help keep your collection neatly stored and easy to find.

2. Wall-Mounted Jewelry Display

If you’re short on drawer space or prefer to have your jewelry displayed where you can see it, a wall-mounted jewelry display might be the perfect solution. By mounting hooks, shelves, or trays on your wall, you can keep your jewelry on display while also organizing it in a way that’s both functional and decorative.

Materials Needed:

  • Wooden board or shelf
  • Hooks or nails
  • Small containers or trays
  • Decorative items (optional)

How to Make It:

  1. Choose a space on your wall where you’d like to mount the jewelry display. A space above a vanity or near a mirror works well, as it provides easy access while getting ready.
  2. Use a wooden board or shelf as the base for your display. You can paint or stain the wood to match your room’s decor, or leave it natural for a more rustic look.
  3. Attach hooks to the board for hanging necklaces, bracelets, or watches. Make sure to space them out evenly so that each piece has enough room.
  4. Install small containers or trays on the shelf to store rings, earrings, or other small items. You can use glass jars, small bowls, or decorative trays to add a personal touch to the display.
  5. Optionally, you can add decorative elements like plants, candles, or small figurines to enhance the look of your jewelry display.

By mounting your jewelry on the wall, you can not only organize your collection but also create a beautiful display that adds a touch of elegance to your room. This method also prevents necklaces from tangling and keeps everything easily accessible.

3. Repurposed Frame Jewelry Organizer

Another creative DIY solution is to repurpose an old picture frame into a jewelry organizer. This option allows you to display your jewelry while keeping it neatly organized. It works especially well for earrings, necklaces, and bracelets.

Materials Needed:

  • Old picture frame (with or without glass)
  • Chicken wire or mesh
  • Staple gun or nails
  • Paint (optional)

How to Make It:

  1. Start by selecting a picture frame you no longer use. It can be an old frame you have lying around the house, or you can find one at a thrift store.
  2. If the frame has glass, remove it, as you’ll be replacing it with chicken wire or mesh.
  3. Cut a piece of chicken wire or mesh to fit the size of the frame. Use a staple gun or nails to secure the mesh inside the frame. The mesh will serve as a place to hang your jewelry.
  4. Optionally, paint the frame to match your room’s color scheme.
  5. Hang the frame on the wall and start organizing your jewelry! You can hang necklaces, bracelets, and even earrings on the mesh, keeping everything visible and easy to reach.

This upcycled frame organizer is not only functional but also adds a unique, rustic charm to your space. It’s a great way to recycle old materials and create something both practical and decorative.

4. DIY Jewelry Roll

For those who prefer a portable solution, a DIY jewelry roll is a fantastic option. This compact organizer can be easily stored in a drawer, suitcase, or on a shelf. It works especially well for travel, as it keeps your jewelry secure and tangle-free.

Materials Needed:

  • Soft fabric (such as felt or cotton)
  • Ribbon or elastic bands
  • Needle and thread (or sewing machine)
  • Scissors

How to Make It:

  1. Cut a piece of fabric large enough to hold your jewelry when rolled up. The fabric should be soft to protect your jewelry from scratches.
  2. Fold the fabric in half, with the outside facing inward, and sew along the edges, leaving one side open.
  3. Turn the fabric inside out so that the soft side is on the outside.
  4. Create small pockets by sewing dividers into the fabric. These pockets will hold rings, earrings, and other small pieces of jewelry.
  5. For necklaces and bracelets, sew a loop at the top of the roll where you can thread a ribbon or elastic band to keep everything in place.
  6. When you’re ready to use it, roll up the fabric and tie it with the ribbon or band. This keeps your jewelry secure and organized while on the go.

This simple yet effective solution is perfect for those who travel frequently or want a portable way to store their jewelry collection.

5. Repurposed Jewelry Tree

If you’re looking for a way to display and organize your jewelry in a way that doubles as décor, a DIY jewelry tree is a perfect choice. By using a branch or wire frame, you can create a stylish and functional jewelry holder.

Materials Needed:

  • Branch (or wire coat hanger)
  • Vase or pot
  • Rocks or gravel
  • Glue gun or wire
  • Paint (optional)

How to Make It:

  1. Start by selecting a branch that suits your style. It can be an actual tree branch, which gives a natural look, or you can use a wire coat hanger bent into the shape of a tree.
  2. If using a branch, place it in a small vase or pot. Fill the pot with rocks or gravel to secure the branch in place.
  3. If using a wire frame, shape it into a tree-like structure, then secure it in a pot or base.
  4. Optionally, paint the branch or wire to match your room’s décor.
  5. Hang your necklaces, bracelets, and earrings from the branches. This makes for a beautiful and functional display, allowing you to easily see and access your jewelry.

This DIY jewelry tree is not only a great organizational tool but also adds a rustic, boho charm to any room.

Conclusion

With a little creativity, you can easily organize your jewelry collection without breaking the bank. Whether you’re upcycling old furniture, creating custom jewelry displays, or crafting portable storage solutions, DIY jewelry storage is both a practical and enjoyable project. These ideas not only help you keep your jewelry neat and organized, but they also allow you to showcase your pieces in a way that adds style and personality to your space.

Choose a solution that fits your needs and personal style, and soon you’ll have an organized jewelry collection that’s easy to access and display. Happy crafting!
