The Top 10 Music Podcasts Every Music Lover Should Listen To


In a world where music is an essential part of daily life, there’s no better way to explore and expand your musical knowledge than through podcasts. Music podcasts offer an incredible mix of genres, artist interviews, deep dives into music history, and discussions about the latest trends. Whether you’re into discovering new artists, exploring the stories behind iconic albums, or learning more about the evolution of music, there’s a podcast for every kind of music lover.

Here’s a list of the top 10 music podcasts every music enthusiast should tune into.

1. Song Exploder

Song Exploder takes music analysis to a whole new level. Hosted by Hrishikesh Hirway, this podcast features musicians breaking down their songs, explaining the creative process, and revealing the stories behind each track. Every episode dives deep into one song, offering insight into how the artist structured the music, wrote the lyrics, and worked in the studio. From indie artists to mainstream acts, Song Exploder gives listeners a behind-the-scenes look at the art of making music.

If you’ve ever wondered about the inspiration behind your favorite song or how musicians build their sound, this podcast is a must-listen.

2. All Songs Considered

Hosted by Bob Boilen and Robin Hilton, All Songs Considered from NPR is a long-standing favorite among music fans. This podcast is your go-to for discovering new tracks, learning about emerging artists, and exploring fresh releases across all genres. The show is a blend of music reviews, artist interviews, and listener feedback, making it a dynamic resource for music lovers. Whether you’re into pop, rock, jazz, or experimental sounds, All Songs Considered will keep you up to date with the latest and greatest in the music scene.

Each episode features in-depth commentary on new albums, along with special episodes that dive into broader topics, from genre explorations to festival highlights.

3. The Talkhouse Music Podcast

The Talkhouse Music Podcast is a unique show where musicians and music industry professionals come together to talk about the craft of making music. Hosted by the team behind Talkhouse, an online platform where artists write about their work, this podcast offers a series of informal conversations between some of the most respected names in music.

Unlike most interviews, the conversations on The Talkhouse Music Podcast often delve into personal stories, creative struggles, and the evolution of an artist’s work. If you’re passionate about hearing the music world through the eyes of its most influential voices, this podcast is perfect for you.

4. The New York Times: Popcast

Popcast is a pop culture podcast hosted by Jon Caramanica, a music critic for The New York Times. This podcast is perfect for those who love to stay on top of pop music trends, album releases, and the impact of music on wider culture. Each episode dives into the hottest topics in pop music, with discussions on everything from chart-topping hits to genre-blending phenomena.

Popcast features interviews with artists, producers, and critics, providing listeners with diverse perspectives on the music industry and its latest shifts. If you love to hear about music in the context of cultural movements and shifts, Popcast is a great choice.

5. Tiny Desk Concerts (From NPR)

NPR’s Tiny Desk Concerts is one of the most well-loved series in the podcast world. The show takes the form of intimate, live performances by artists from a variety of genres, filmed in the NPR office. It’s an intimate and unpolished look at live music that highlights both well-known and emerging artists.

The podcast captures the magic of these performances in audio format, giving listeners access to incredible, up-close musical experiences. From legendary acts to new talents, Tiny Desk Concerts is an excellent way to experience live performances from the comfort of your home or on the go.

6. Switched on Pop

If you’re a fan of pop music and want to get a deeper understanding of what makes a song catchy, Switched on Pop is the podcast for you. Hosted by musicologist Nate Sloan and songwriter Charlie Harding, this podcast dissects the science behind pop music, exploring the intricate details that make popular songs so appealing.

Each episode focuses on a different song, breaking down elements like chord progressions, rhythms, and lyrical themes. Switched on Pop doesn’t just talk about what’s popular—it analyzes how and why certain tracks resonate with audiences. If you’re interested in the art of songwriting and pop music’s evolution, this podcast offers an informative and fun way to dig deeper.

7. Dissect

Dissect is a must-listen for anyone who enjoys deep analysis of modern music. This podcast breaks down entire albums, looking at them from every angle—from lyrics to production. Host Cole Cuchna takes listeners on an intellectual journey through one album at a time, deconstructing the themes and meanings behind each track.

With a focus on hip-hop and R&B, Dissect explores the artistry behind some of the most influential albums of recent years. This podcast is ideal for those who want a thorough, thoughtful analysis of their favorite records, from concept albums to landmark releases.

8. KEXP Song of the Day

KEXP’s Song of the Day podcast offers a daily dose of fresh music from the Seattle-based station’s curated playlist. Each episode highlights a song from a lesser-known artist or a rising star, giving you a unique glimpse into the music scene both locally and internationally. With a commitment to showcasing diverse genres and sounds, KEXP helps listeners discover music that might not show up on mainstream radio.

If you’re looking for something new to listen to every day and want to broaden your musical horizons, the KEXP Song of the Day podcast is a fantastic resource.

9. Pop Music Critical Podcast

For music lovers who want a thoughtful critique of mainstream pop, the Pop Music Critical Podcast is an insightful listen. The podcast delves into the trends and phenomena in the pop world, with a focus on how popular music reflects cultural moments and societal shifts. Hosted by music journalists and critics, the show provides in-depth analysis and critiques of pop songs and albums, often comparing them to the past and exploring their impact on the future of the genre.

Whether you’re a pop music enthusiast or someone who enjoys hearing music in a critical context, this podcast offers a fresh and critical perspective on the biggest hits.

10. Revolutions in Music History

Revolutions in Music History takes a different approach, focusing on the historical side of music. Hosted by a team of music historians, this podcast explores the revolutions in musical style, technique, and culture that have shaped the music we know today. From the rise of classical music to the birth of jazz, each episode examines key moments in history and how they altered the course of music.

If you’re someone who’s interested in how music has evolved over centuries and the cultural movements that shaped it, this podcast offers a thorough and engaging exploration.

Conclusion

These 10 music podcasts offer a wide array of content for any music lover. Whether you’re looking to discover new artists, gain insight into the music industry, or explore the history of music, there’s something here for everyone. By tuning into these podcasts, you can deepen your appreciation for music, stay up to date with the latest trends, and gain a broader perspective on the artistry behind your favorite songs. So plug in your headphones, hit play, and enjoy the musical journey ahead!
