The Best Internet Providers for Rural Areas


Living in rural areas can often feel like being cut off from the world. While the quiet and scenic nature of rural life offers its own charm, one common challenge faced by rural dwellers is the limited access to high-speed internet. Whether you’re working remotely, streaming your favorite shows, or just browsing the web, a reliable and fast internet connection is crucial for day-to-day life. Unfortunately, traditional broadband services are often unavailable in these less populated areas, leaving residents with few options. However, the internet landscape is changing, and there are now several internet providers who cater specifically to rural communities.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the best internet providers for rural areas, comparing their features, plans, pricing, and availability. Whether you’re looking for satellite, fixed wireless, DSL, or fiber internet, we’ve got you covered.

1. Viasat: Best for Satellite Internet

Viasat has long been a reliable option for those living in rural areas. With its satellite internet service, Viasat offers coverage across most of the United States, including areas where traditional cable or fiber services are unavailable. Satellite internet works by sending and receiving data to and from satellites in orbit, providing coverage to virtually any location, even the most remote ones.

Key Features:

  • High-Speed Plans: Viasat offers internet speeds ranging from 12 Mbps to 100 Mbps, depending on the plan and location. While speeds can vary based on your proximity to the satellite, it generally provides enough bandwidth for most online activities such as browsing, email, and video streaming.
  • No Data Caps: Viasat doesn’t impose strict data caps on its plans. While certain plans may have reduced speeds after a set data threshold, there are no hard limits on how much data you can use.
  • Nationwide Coverage: Viasat’s satellite internet service is available in most rural areas across the United States. Whether you live in a small town or a remote farm, you can likely access Viasat’s services.

Pros:

  • Reliable coverage in remote areas
  • High-speed options available
  • Flexible data plans

Cons:

  • Higher latency due to the nature of satellite internet
  • More expensive than some other options
  • Speeds can fluctuate depending on weather conditions

Best For: Rural homes in remote locations where other broadband services aren’t available.

2. HughesNet: A Satellite Internet Alternative

Another satellite internet provider, HughesNet is often compared to Viasat due to its similar service offerings. With coverage across much of rural America, HughesNet is one of the best options for areas that lack traditional broadband infrastructure.

Key Features:

  • Speeds up to 25 Mbps: HughesNet offers speeds that are perfect for everyday internet tasks like browsing, social media, and video calls. While it’s not the fastest option, it’s sufficient for basic online needs.
  • Data Flexibility: HughesNet’s plans come with varying data limits. You’ll typically get between 15 GB to 100 GB of data per month, depending on your plan. If you exceed your data limit, your speeds may be slowed until the next billing cycle.
  • Free Wi-Fi Router: HughesNet provides a free Wi-Fi router with most plans, making it easier to set up your home network.

Pros:

  • Reliable coverage in remote areas
  • Affordable plans for basic use
  • No contracts on some plans

Cons:

  • Slower speeds compared to fiber or cable internet
  • Data limits that can restrict heavy usage

Best For: Budget-conscious individuals or families who need basic internet access in rural areas.

3. Frontier Communications: Best for DSL Internet

Frontier Communications is a widely available option in many rural areas, offering digital subscriber line (DSL) internet service. DSL works over existing phone lines, making it an attractive option for rural areas where newer technologies like fiber-optic networks haven’t been built yet.

Key Features:

  • Speeds up to 100 Mbps: Frontier offers internet speeds of up to 100 Mbps in select areas, though speeds can vary based on your location and proximity to Frontier’s infrastructure.
  • Affordable Plans: Frontier’s DSL plans are generally priced lower than other internet options, making it an affordable choice for families and individuals on a budget.
  • No Contracts: Frontier allows customers to choose plans without being tied to long-term contracts, providing flexibility for those who might move or need to cancel service.

Pros:

  • Affordable pricing
  • Available in many rural communities
  • No contracts required

Cons:

  • Speeds may not be as fast as fiber or cable internet
  • Availability can be limited to specific rural areas

Best For: Rural areas with existing phone lines and individuals seeking an affordable internet option.

4. Starlink: The Future of Rural Internet

Starlink, a satellite internet service provided by SpaceX, is one of the most promising internet providers for rural areas. While the service is still expanding, Starlink has quickly gained attention for its ability to provide high-speed internet to remote areas that were previously underserved by other providers.

Key Features:

  • Speeds up to 150 Mbps: Starlink offers speeds that are competitive with other broadband options, providing fast enough speeds for gaming, streaming, and working remotely.
  • Low Latency: Starlink boasts significantly lower latency compared to traditional satellite internet providers like Viasat and HughesNet, making it a better option for activities like video conferencing and online gaming.
  • Global Coverage: As part of SpaceX’s ambitious plan to provide global internet coverage, Starlink aims to provide high-speed internet to even the most remote regions across the world.

Pros:

  • High speeds and low latency
  • Availability in very remote areas
  • Future-proof with continued expansion and upgrades

Cons:

  • The service is still being rolled out and may not be available in all areas
  • Expensive equipment and installation costs
  • Service may fluctuate depending on weather conditions

Best For: Early adopters and those in remote areas looking for high-speed, low-latency internet.

5. T-Mobile Home Internet: Best for Fixed Wireless

T-Mobile Home Internet provides another viable internet option for rural areas, particularly those that are within T-Mobile’s service coverage. This fixed wireless internet service uses T-Mobile’s 4G and 5G networks to provide high-speed internet to homes without the need for a physical cable.

Key Features:

  • Speeds up to 100 Mbps: T-Mobile’s home internet service delivers speeds that are great for video streaming, gaming, and work-from-home activities.
  • No Data Caps: Unlike many other providers, T-Mobile Home Internet doesn’t impose data limits, which is ideal for heavy users or families who need to stream and download large files.
  • Affordable Plans: The pricing for T-Mobile Home Internet is highly competitive, with one flat fee that covers all costs (including equipment).

Pros:

  • Affordable with no hidden fees
  • Fast speeds with 5G coverage
  • Easy setup with no long-term contracts

Cons:

  • Service availability is limited to areas with T-Mobile’s network coverage
  • Speeds can fluctuate based on the network’s congestion

Best For: Rural areas with access to T-Mobile’s 4G or 5G coverage.

6. Windstream: Best for Rural Fiber Internet

Windstream is one of the few internet providers offering fiber-optic internet in rural areas. While fiber is not available everywhere, Windstream has been expanding its coverage in rural areas, bringing gigabit-speed internet to communities that previously had limited options.

Key Features:

  • Gigabit Speeds: Windstream offers speeds up to 1,000 Mbps, making it one of the fastest options for rural areas.
  • Low Latency: Fiber-optic internet provides lower latency compared to other types of internet, which is important for activities like video conferencing, gaming, and real-time streaming.
  • Reliable Performance: Fiber-optic connections are typically more stable than satellite or DSL, providing consistent speeds and fewer disruptions.

Pros:

  • Fast, reliable gigabit speeds
  • Low latency
  • Expanding coverage in rural areas

Cons:

  • Fiber service is not available in all rural areas
  • Prices can be higher than DSL or satellite internet

Best For: Rural homes that are lucky enough to have access to fiber-optic internet.

7. CenturyLink: A Good Option for DSL and Fiber

CenturyLink is another option for rural internet, providing both DSL and fiber-optic internet service depending on your location. While DSL is more common in rural areas, CenturyLink’s fiber-optic service is available in select locations, offering higher speeds and better performance.

Key Features:

  • Speeds up to 100 Mbps with DSL: In rural areas, CenturyLink offers reliable DSL internet that works well for basic activities like browsing, social media, and email.
  • Fiber Plans with Speeds up to 1,000 Mbps: In areas with fiber availability, CenturyLink offers some of the fastest speeds in the industry.
  • Price Lock Guarantee: CenturyLink offers a price lock for some of its plans, which means the price won’t increase during your contract period.

Pros:

  • Affordable pricing for DSL plans
  • Reliable fiber options in select areas
  • Price lock on some plans

Cons:

  • Availability of fiber is limited in rural areas
  • DSL speeds are slower than fiber

Best For: Rural areas with access to either DSL or fiber-optic services.

Conclusion

Choosing the best internet provider for your rural home depends on your location, internet needs, and budget. Satellite providers like Viasat and HughesNet are perfect for remote areas, while Starlink offers a promising future for high-speed internet in rural locations. T-Mobile’s fixed wireless service provides fast, affordable internet, and for those lucky enough to have fiber access, Windstream and CenturyLink offer some of the fastest and most reliable speeds.

While rural internet options may not yet be as abundant as in urban areas, the situation is steadily improving. With more providers offering innovative solutions, rural residents now have better access to the internet than ever before. So, take the time to explore your options and choose the plan that works best for you, ensuring you stay connected to the world no matter where you live.
