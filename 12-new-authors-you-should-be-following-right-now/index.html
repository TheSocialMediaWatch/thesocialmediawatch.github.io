12 New Authors You Should Be Following Right Now


The literary world is constantly evolving, with new voices emerging to take the mantle and usher in fresh perspectives. As avid readers and passionate advocates for laborious storytelling, we embark on an exciting odyssey to unearth twelve thoroughly original authors poised to captivate your imagination. Embrace the unparalleled depths of their thought-provoking words and assemble your reading list of future classics.

  1. Charlie Jane Anders - Redefining Speculative Fiction

Charlie Jane Anders is a potent force in the world of science fiction and fantasy. Her immersive novella, All the Birds in the Sky, weaves a mesmerizing tale that bridges magic and science alike. Anders hails from a background of award-winning publications such as io9, and her subsequent novels, Vintage, and The City in the Middle of the Night, are bound to leave you breathless.

  1. Sarah Dauncey - Empowering Women’s Voices

Sarah Dauncey, a talented British author, delves deep into the complexities of female identity and experience. Her debut novel, Man-Eater, follows the story of Sophie, an ambitious businesswoman juggling the intricacies of her personal and professional life. Dauncey’s raw and relatable storytelling shines through her next works, offering a fresh lens to the modern woman’s world.

  1. Javier Fernandez Madero - Enchanting, Poetic Prose

Prepare to be swept away to another era of enchantment and romance with the elegant prose of Javier Fernandez Madero. A true maestro of minimalist storytelling, Madero skillfully crafts novels such as The Last Lifeboat and Shadows in the Bed, exploring the nuances of human relationships spanning generations. Lose yourself in the timeless allure of his masterful storytelling.

  1. R. F. Kuang - The Art of Historical Grappling

R. F. Kuang, a young and prodigious author, has drawn accolades shining a light on her unique talents. Renowned for her philosophical reflections on historical interpretations in novels such as The Poppy War and The Dragon’s War, Kuang challenges readers to question the veracity of history and their own preconceived notions.

  1. Natalie Zina Walschots - Comic Book Legends

Natalie Zina Walschots is a burgeoning star in the realm of comic book aficionados with her enthralling and masterfully crafted series, Apostasy. The riveting story is fueled by societal and moral debates, guaranteeing that her works will captivate readers from the very first page to the last.

  1. Irenosen Okojie - Vibrant, Daydream-Inducing Narratives

Irenosen Okojie’s novel, Nuditi, paints a vivid and surreal portrait of her protagonist’s journey to self-discovery. Her writing is a dazzling tapestry of vibrant characters and thought-provoking themes that leave readers feeling as though they bear witness to their dreams brought to life.

  1. Roxane Gay - The Bard of Empathy

Roxane Gay is one of the most influential voices of our time. Her provocative explorations of empathy, intersectionality, and difficult truths in works such as An Untamed State, Disappearing Earth, and the bestselling Hunger beckon with an irresistible pull. She invites readers to partake in an unflinching journey through intimate human experiences.

  1. Esmé Weijun Wang - Illuminating the Spectrum of Neurodivergent Minds

Esmé Weijun Wang’s poetry collection, The Collected Schizophrenias, presents a deeply personal and impassioned examination of her struggles with mental health. Her reflections weave through the nebulous nature of reality in Firefly, providing an essential narrative for the diverse tapestry of human existence.

  1. Chloe Gong - Somber and Enthralling Historical Fiction

Chloe Gong breathes new life into the world of 1920s Shanghai in her phenomenal debut novel, These Violent Delights. Drawing equal influence from the timeless emblem of Romeo and Juliet, Gong’s tale takes readers on a whirlwind of betrayal, lust, and power amongst warring gang lords. Fear not, for her prequel, Our Violent Ends, arrives soon – the perfect follow-up to an enthralling saga.

  1. Imtiaz Dharker - Taleweaver Exquisite

Imtiaz Dharker, a critically-acclaimed poet/author, interweaves her captivating narratives with a uniquely candid voice. Her body of work, such as Overload, paints an evocative tableau of penurious lives, shining a light on the battles faced by besieged communities in India.

  1. Laura van den Berg - Miraculous Realities and Escape

Laura van den Berg’s novels, The Third Hotel and Find Me, plunge readers into alternate dimensions and surreal landscapes, entrenching them in the fantastic circumstances that challenge their perceptions. These vivid journeys ensure that each and every page teems with life and intrigue.

  1. Marlon James - Marauding Athletes in Their Prime

In the tradition of Homer’s Iliad, Marlon James’ phenomenon of a novel, Black Leopard, Red Wolf, dives deep into a protagonist’s journey in a fantastical realm. The epic adventure is networked by the interconnected destinies of powerful mythical creatures, exploring the eternal battle of good and evil.

Conclusion

Delight in the marvelous tapestry woven by these twelve luminaries as they stoke the embers of innovation and craft new chapters of literary brilliance. Their collective voices reflect the rich complexities of the human experience and invite us to venture onto uncharted terrains of imagination. So, clear your schedule, treat yourself to a slice of heaven, and unearth a treasure trove of unforgettable reading experiences with these twelve authors.
