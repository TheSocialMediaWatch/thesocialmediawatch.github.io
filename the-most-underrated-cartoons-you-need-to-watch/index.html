The Most Underrated Cartoons You Need to Watch


When it comes to animated series, everyone has their go-to shows that defined their childhood or current binge sessions. Classics like The Simpsons, Rick and Morty, or Adventure Time often come to mind instantly. But beyond these well-celebrated giants lie a treasure trove of hidden gems—cartoons that delivered extraordinary storytelling, unforgettable characters, and artistic magic but didn’t quite gain the widespread recognition they deserved. These underrated cartoons offer a fresh perspective on animation and are essential viewing for anyone looking to expand their horizons beyond mainstream hits.

Why Seek Out Underrated Cartoons?

The charm of diving into underrated animated series is that you get to enjoy fresh narratives free from the overwhelming hype and massive fan expectations that often shape popular shows. Underrated cartoons frequently offer unique artistic styles, diverse storylines, and innovative humor. They can challenge conventions and tell stories that resonate deeply, all while flying beneath the radar of most casual viewers. These cartoons also tend to foster cult followings, creating passionate, tight-knit communities of fans who treasure every episode.

Now, let’s dive into some phenomenal, lesser-known animated series that deserve your attention.

1. Foster’s Home for Imaginary Friends

At first glance, this Cartoon Network show might seem like just a whimsical kids’ animation, but Foster’s Home for Imaginary Friends is brimming with creativity and heart. Created by Craig McCracken (also behind The Powerpuff Girls), the series centers around a mansion that acts as a foster home for imaginary friends left behind by children.

Why It’s Underrated

While the show garnered some awards and critical acclaim, it never fully broke into the mainstream consciousness. The series excels in character development, featuring a wide cast of quirky, imaginative friends, each with their own unique personalities. The humor resonates with both kids and adults, integrating clever pop culture references and witty dialogues. The vibrant animation style feels timeless, combining a classic 2D look with vibrant colors that bring this fantasy world to life.

What Makes It Worth Watching

  • Inventive character designs that inspire creativity
  • A touching exploration of childhood friendships and imagination
  • Sharp humor layered for all ages

2. Sym-Bionic Titan

From Genndy Tartakovsky, the visionary behind Samurai Jack and Dexter’s Laboratory, Sym-Bionic Titan flew under the radar despite its high quality. This sci-fi action series blends mecha battles with teenage drama, following an alien princess, a soldier, and a robot as they adapt to life on Earth while defending it from monstrous invaders.

Why It’s Underrated

The show was canceled after just one season, leaving many fans hungry for more. Its unique mix of genres—combining high school struggles with epic alien warfare—and sophisticated storytelling make it stand out. The animation is fluid and stylish, showcasing Tartakovsky’s signature kinetic action scenes, while the emotional arcs are surprisingly nuanced, especially for a show targeted at younger viewers.

What Makes It Worth Watching

  • Exceptional blend of action, drama, and sci-fi
  • Deep character development packed into a brief run
  • Stunning animation and fight choreography

3. The Marvelous Misadventures of Flapjack

This show is a quirky, dark comedy that is as eerie as it is funny. Created by Thurop Van Orman, it tells the adventures of Flapjack, a curious young boy, and his talking whale Bubbie, as they pursue adventure in the strange maritime town of Stormalong Harbor.

Why It’s Underrated

Flapjack embraces surrealism and weird humor that only a small segment of viewers initially embraced, but it has since become a cult favorite. Its clever writing and surreal tone were a stark contrast to more straightforward cartoons of its time. It’s also notable for being the launching ground for creators like J.G. Quintel (Regular Show) and Pendleton Ward (Adventure Time).

What Makes It Worth Watching

  • Unique artistic style mixing whimsy with the macabre
  • Bold humor that pushes the boundaries of conventional cartoon storytelling
  • A bizarre, wonderfully distinctive atmosphere

4. Gravity Falls

While Gravity Falls achieved moderate popularity, it still doesn’t get as much credit as it deserves for its intelligent writing and rich mythology. Created by Alex Hirsch, it follows twins Dipper and Mabel Pines as they uncover supernatural mysteries in the strange town of Gravity Falls.

Why It’s Slightly Underrated

Though it amassed a dedicated following and praised critical acclaim, it often gets overshadowed by longer-running series. Its carefully woven mysteries, layered characters, and clever humor make it a standout, yet some casual viewers might have missed it due to its relatively short two-season run.

What Makes It Worth Watching

  • Deep, engaging mystery spanning multiple episodes
  • Strong humor balancing spooky and light-hearted tones
  • Heartwarming sibling dynamic and character growth

5. Over the Garden Wall

This eminently underrated mini-series is a jewel of American animation. Composed of ten short episodes, Over the Garden Wall tells the eerie, poetic tale of two brothers lost in a mysterious forest called The Unknown.

Why It’s Underrated

Because it is a limited series rather than an ongoing show, it tends to get overlooked in lists of great cartoons. However, the richness of its themes—touching on loss, hope, and the power of storytelling—makes it stand out. Its vintage art style harkens back to early 20th-century Americana, and the haunting original soundtrack adds to its memorable atmosphere.

What Makes It Worth Watching

  • Unique blend of folklore, gothic horror, and whimsy
  • Atmospheric storytelling with a melancholic undertone
  • Short and binge-worthy with no filler episodes

6. Infinity Train

Infinity Train is a hidden ageless masterpiece that explores complex ideas about identity, trauma, and personal growth through a surreal train journey where each car holds a different bizarre universe.

Why It’s Underrated

Despite glowing reviews and a strong fan base, it never quite hit mainstream stardom. The show’s narrative depth and mature themes are unusually sophisticated for an animated show primarily targeted at younger audiences. Its anthology-like format challenges viewers to piece together mysteries, while its evolving protagonists make it emotionally compelling.

What Makes It Worth Watching

  • Exceptional writing tackling psychological and philosophical themes
  • Bold and innovative serial storytelling
  • Stunning variety in animation design with each train car

7. The Owl House

A fresh and bold fantasy series, The Owl House combines magic, monsters, and coming-of-age themes brilliantly. It revolves around Luz, a girl who stumbles into a magical realm and finds herself apprenticed to a rebellious witch.

Why It’s Underrated

Though its audience grew quickly, the show’s limited exposure compared to Disney’s mainstream hits has kept it somewhat under the radar. The series is praised for diversity, LGBTQ+ representation, and its adult-friendly approach to magical world-building and character relationships.

What Makes It Worth Watching

  • Rich, imaginative fantasy world with well-developed lore
  • Inclusive storytelling reflecting a variety of identities
  • Sharp humor paired with meaningful emotional arcs

8. Star vs. The Forces of Evil

A colorful and fast-paced adventure comedy, Star vs. The Forces of Evil follows Star Butterfly, a magical princess who battles villains while adjusting to life on Earth.

Why It’s Underrated

Despite a solid fan base, it hasn’t grown quite as big as other Disney shows in recent years. The series is distinctive for mixing magical girl tropes with wild humor and energetic storytelling. Its creativity shines through in character designs and action sequences.

What Makes It Worth Watching

  • Blends comedy and action with emotional depth
  • Diverse cast and strong female protagonist
  • Unique magical settings that evolve with the story

Final Thoughts: The Joy of Rediscovery

Exploring underrated cartoons is like opening a vault of creative treasures. Beyond the familiar, there’s a world teeming with imaginative stories and thinking outside the box in animation. Whether you want spine-tingling mysteries, heartfelt character journeys, or just offbeat humor, these shows invite you to experience fresh perspectives that challenge the status quo.

Diving into these lesser-known animated series not only broadens your entertainment palate but often reveals storytelling and artistry that rival or exceed mainstream favorites. So next time you scroll for something to watch, why not venture into these underrated gems and rediscover the limitless creativity animation has to offer? Your next favorite show might just be waiting off the beaten path.
