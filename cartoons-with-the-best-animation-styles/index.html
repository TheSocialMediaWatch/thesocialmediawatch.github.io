Cartoons with the Best Animation Styles


Animation is much more than just moving images—it’s a language artists use to speak directly to our imagination. Over the years, certain cartoons have stood above the rest, creating unforgettable worlds through daring, innovative, or simply exquisitely crafted animation. These series and films push boundaries, experiment with form, and invite viewers to see what’s possible when artistry meets storytelling. Let’s explore a spectrum of cartoons renowned for their exceptional animation styles, and break down what gives each one its signature visual magic.

The Artistry of Animation: What Makes a Style Stand Out?

Narrative in animation is important, but often what first grabs attention is how a cartoon looks—its lines, color palette, movement, and sense of life. Great animation isn’t just technically perfect; it has soul, personality, and intent behind every stroke. From dynamic action sequences to subtle character moments, style sets the tone and elevates the viewer’s experience.

Below is a curated celebration of several cartoons that have defined, revolutionized, or reinvented the medium through their animation. Each comes packed with distinctive visual flair, imaginative worldbuilding, and an ability to spark wonder in fans of all ages.

1. Samurai Jack – Minimalism Meets Cinematic Mastery

Genndy Tartakovsky’s Samurai Jack is instantly recognizable: clean, spare backgrounds paired with striking, fluid figures. The series draws inspiration from classic Japanese art and knits it with experimental layouts reminiscent of European graphic novels. Key elements set this style apart:

  • Minimalist backgrounds: Scenes often feature large swathes of empty space, making every detail that does appear more impactful.
  • Cinematic composition: Tartakovsky often “shoots” the action as if it’s a movie, with wide shots, dramatic angles, and intricate long takes.
  • Vivid color: The show isn’t afraid of bold contrasts—think deep reds for battles, icy blues for sorrow.

The result is a cartoon that feels epic even in silence, with every frame serving as a potential piece of art.

2. Avatar: The Last Airbender – Fluidity and Fusion

Avatar: The Last Airbender melds Western storytelling with anime sensibilities, producing a lush, character-rich world. Its animation is notable for:

  • Naturalistic movement: Bending styles are based on real martial arts, leading to graceful, believable action sequences.
  • Expressive faces: Characters display a range of complex emotions thanks to nuanced animation.
  • Layered backgrounds: Lush digital paintings set the stage, echoing traditional East Asian ink artwork.

“Avatar” stands out with its balanced fusion of influences, resulting in a visual approach that supports its themes of harmony and adventure.

3. Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse – Comic Book Odyssey

If there’s a modern cartoon that redefined visual style, it’s Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse. Sony’s animation team created an explosive, comic book-inspired world. Highlights include:

  • Frame rate experimentation: By using variable frame rates, the film pays homage to the hand-drawn feel of comics while intensifying action moments.
  • Ben-Day dots and halftone: Classic comic textures are woven directly into the animation, making scenes feel like animated panels.
  • Unconventional color usage: Vivid, neon colors pop against darker cityscapes, mirroring a comic’s pulpy vibrancy.

“Spider-Verse” proved cartoons could mimic the graphic splendor of comics without compromising movement or emotional resonance.

4. Steven Universe – Pastel Dreamworlds

Rebecca Sugar’s Steven Universe is a masterclass in mood-setting via color and design. The show’s distinct visuals are shaped by:

  • Whimsical character shapes: Round, soft forms give the cast an approachable, endearing look.
  • Gentle gradients: Soft pastel palettes evoke a dreamlike, whimsical tone that matches the show’s emotional complexity.
  • Stylized backgrounds: From Gem homeworlds to Beach City sunsets, backgrounds burst with painterly flourishes and careful color choices.

This cartoon didn’t just tell stories of growth and acceptance—it showed them, visually, at every turn.

5. Arcane – Painterly Perfection

Riot Games’ Arcane set a new bar for digital animation with an art style both gritty and gorgeous. Standout features include:

  • Oil painting textures: Backgrounds feel textured and tangible, as if they were lifted directly from a canvas.
  • Expressive facial animation: Using advanced 3D rigs, the animators capture delicate emotion with subtlety rarely seen in the medium.
  • Dynamic lighting effects: Lighting sculpts the world, bringing a sense of realism to the fantastical cityscape.

“Arcane” demonstrates how technology and artistry can work hand-in-hand to craft a living, breathing universe.

6. Adventure Time – Surreal Simplicity

Pendleton Ward’s Adventure Time proves that simplicity can be ingenious. Its animation charms with:

  • Rubber hose movement: Limbs stretch and bend in ways reminiscent of early cartoon classics, lending a delightfully unpredictable sense of motion.
  • Playful color design: Bright, often clashing colors mirror the show’s embrace of the bizarre.
  • Modular backgrounds: Landscapes shift in ways that feel organic, enhancing the sense of a living world.

The show’s off-kilter visuals twist expectations and keep things fresh with every journey through the Land of Ooo.

7. Over the Garden Wall – Autumnal Americana

Over the Garden Wall is a love letter to vintage illustration, folklore, and classic animators like Fleischer. Its style relies upon:

  • Muted autumnal palette: Every episode immerses viewers in the haunting atmosphere of a perpetual fall.
  • Detailed linework: Characters and creatures have a slightly worn, storybook quality.
  • Layered compositions: Foregrounds and backgrounds softly interact, enhancing the sense of depth and mystery.

The animation underscores the storyline’s blend of nostalgia and unease, creating a fairy tale atmosphere unlike any other.

8. The Legend of Korra – Art Deco Influence

As a spiritual successor to “Avatar”, The Legend of Korra offers even more intricate, energetic visuals, drawing from:

  • Art Deco architecture: Republic City’s skyline echoes the grandeur of early 20th-century design.
  • Dynamic fight choreography: Fluid animation turns element-bending duels into balletic spectacles.
  • Richly textured backgrounds: Scenes are filled with nuanced details—cracked pavement, city posters, patterned interiors.

The result is a world simultaneously modern and fantastical, setting a new standard for genre storytelling in animation.

9. Primal – Wordless Brilliance

Tartakovsky returns to this list with Primal, a series that strips animation down to its raw essence:

  • Hyper-detailed characters: Every scar, muscle, and grimace tells a story.
  • Visceral movement: The show’s action is kinetic and weighty—each blow and sprint feels immediate.
  • Mood-driven color: Tonal palettes align directly with narrative, making visual mood a core storytelling device.

Despite its lack of dialogue, “Primal” is a showcase in how animation alone can convey emotion, plot, and atmosphere.

10. Kipo and the Age of Wonderbeasts – Vibrant Post-Apocalypse

DreamWorks’ Kipo and the Age of Wonderbeasts adds fresh flair to the end-of-the-world genre. Standout elements comprise:

  • Candy-colored landscapes: Even ruined cities are painted in vivid, almost psychedelic hues.
  • Expressive creature design: Mutant animals sport inventive designs, mixing cuteness with surrealism.
  • Musical synergy: Animation often visually syncs with soundtrack beats, enhancing the narrative’s rhythm.

It’s a visual feast that makes the apocalypse look oddly inviting.

11. BoJack Horseman – Melancholy Surrealism

While “BoJack Horseman” may not dazzle with technical spectacle, its distinctive style is packed with symbolism:

  • Human-animal hybrids: Anthropomorphic characters mirror the show’s themes of identity and isolation.
  • Background gags: Layers of visual jokes and references reward attentive viewers.
  • Color as emotion: Scenes employ color shifts to mirror character moods—saturated yellows for mania, dull grays for depression.

Here, animation’s quiet details do the heavy lifting, illustrating a world where comedy and pathos walk hand in hoof.

12. Wakfu – French Flair and Fantasy

Ankama’s Wakfu leverages French animation traditions to create a series with jaw-dropping action:

  • Elaborate action choreography: Battle scenes are animated with a kinetic energy and precision few shows rival.
  • Cartoonishly elastic design: Characters and environments stretch, smush, and transform in dynamic ways.
  • Crisp digital backgrounds: The backgrounds provide a sense of scale, giving each locale its own unique visual signature.

“Wakfu” blends humor and high fantasy, making every fight a visual treat.

13. The Amazing World of Gumball – Mixed-Media Mayhem

Perhaps no show is as visually experimental as The Amazing World of Gumball. Its signature style thrives on chaos:

  • Mixed media: 2D characters, 3D models, puppets, and live action elements all co-exist.
  • Ever-shifting art: Characters may switch art styles mid-episode as part of gags or story beats.
  • Layered environments: The blend of photography and illustration makes Elmore a bizarre, unpredictable setting.

The animation style becomes a playground, constantly surprising both kids and adults.

14. Clone Wars (2008) – Stylized Realism

Lucasfilm’s Clone Wars (especially from its later seasons) shows how computer animation can be stylized, not just photorealistic:

  • Sharp angles: Characters are defined by polygonal features, recalling classic action figure aesthetics.
  • Star Wars design: Ships, planets, and droids blend the familiar with the new, all rendered with sleek lighting.
  • Staging and movement: Action scenes are composed with the gravitas of live-action cinema, bolstered by clean, kinetic animation.

This series finds a unique voice between 3D modeling and graphic art.

15. Gravity Falls – Mystical Modern Classic

Alex Hirsch’s “Gravity Falls” invites viewers to get lost in a world of hidden mysteries and cryptic clues. Its art direction excels with:

  • Clean, expressive lines: Characters are easy to read and instantly memorable.
  • Scenic Pacific Northwest: Landscapes echo the show’s supernatural undertones, pairing misty forests with warm, inviting lighting.
  • Careful color grading: Nighttime in Gravity Falls sparkles with blues and purples, while daylight feels golden and nostalgic.

The animation’s storytelling power lies in its ability to create a world that feels both magical and grounded.

Why Animation Styles Matter

Cultural touchstones, meme factories, or awards winners—great cartoons endure because of more than just their stories. They stay with us because of how they look and feel. Animation style isn’t window dressing; it’s shorthand for emotion, culture, and intent. It offers a visual voice.

Sit with any of the cartoons listed above and take a moment to just absorb the artistry before you. Notice how lines, colors, and movement work together to create a specific mood or intensify a dramatic moment. Whether it’s Samurai Jack’s minimalist beauty or Spider-Verse’s chaotic dynamism, each series or movie captures what makes animation such an endlessly inventive artform.

Honorable Mentions: More To Explore

  • The Iron Giant – 2D glory and heart.
  • Sailor Moon (1990s) – Iconic shoujo shimmer.
  • Rick and Morty – Science fiction with slick, cynical visuals.
  • Foster’s Home for Imaginary Friends – Wild, dreamlike design.

There are endless more, limited only by the creativity of their makers and the imaginations of audiences.

The Future of Animation Styles

What’s on the horizon? As tech advances and international collaborations continue, expect animation to keep blurring the borders between film and fine art, realism and abstraction. Hybrid styles, AI-assisted creation, and interactive elements are all in play. No matter the tools, though, it’s vision and ingenuity that steal the show.

So next time you sit down to watch a cartoon, pay close attention to the animation style. You might just discover an innovative technique, a clever use of color, or even a new favorite world to get lost in.

What cartoons do you think have the most brilliant animation styles? What are your visual comfort foods? Dive in, share your favorites, and keep watching for the next wave of groundbreaking animated stories just waiting to be discovered.
