The Best Art Supplies for Beginners and Experts


When it comes to exploring the world of art, the right supplies can make all the difference in your creative journey. Whether you’re just starting out or you’re a seasoned artist, having the best tools for your craft is crucial for producing stunning works of art. From basic essentials to advanced materials, there is a vast array of options available, making it essential to understand what suits your needs. In this guide, we’ll cover the best art supplies for beginners and experts, ensuring that you have everything you need to create amazing masterpieces.

Choosing the Right Art Supplies

Selecting the appropriate art supplies often depends on the type of art you want to create. Different mediums require specific materials that can enhance the experience and final result. As a beginner, it’s easy to feel overwhelmed by the sheer variety of options available. Don’t worry — this guide will help you navigate through the basics and highlight some expert-level tools that can take your art to the next level.

Art Supplies for Beginners

If you’re new to the world of art, the key is to start with the basics. You don’t need to invest in expensive supplies right away. Start with what’s essential and experiment with different materials to see what suits your style. Here are some beginner-friendly art supplies that can help you get started:

1. Pencils and Graphite

Every artist’s journey begins with a pencil. A basic set of graphite pencils is essential for sketching, drawing, and learning the fundamentals of shading and detail. For beginners, it’s recommended to start with a range of pencil grades, such as:

  • 2H: Great for light sketching and fine lines.
  • HB: The standard pencil for general-purpose use.
  • 2B to 6B: These pencils are ideal for shading and adding depth with darker lines.

A good pencil set can offer flexibility in your drawing style and help you understand how different hardness levels affect your work. If you’re just starting, try out several brands to find one that feels comfortable in your hand.

2. Sketchbooks and Paper

The type of paper you choose plays a significant role in your artwork. For beginners, a medium-weight sketchbook with a smooth texture is ideal for pencil work, ink, and light washes. Some sketchbooks are designed specifically for different media, like watercolor or markers, but for general purposes, look for a sketchbook that offers:

  • Acid-free paper: Ensures your work will last longer without yellowing.
  • Weight of 70-90 gsm: Lightweight for smooth sketching but still durable enough to withstand erasing.
  • Size: A medium-sized sketchbook (around 8.5” x 11”) is ideal for beginners. It’s portable and offers enough space for detailed work.

Once you get comfortable, you can experiment with specialty papers for different mediums like watercolor, pastel, or charcoal.

3. Colored Pencils

Colored pencils are versatile and beginner-friendly, making them an excellent choice for creating vibrant artwork. They can be used for precise lines, blending, and layering of colors. When selecting colored pencils, go for a set with a range of colors to give you more creative freedom. Some recommended brands for beginners include Prismacolor Scholar, Crayola, and Arteza.

For more control and smoother results, you can opt for higher-quality colored pencils, which allow for better blending and shading.

4. Watercolors

Watercolor painting is an excellent starting point for beginners because of its gentle nature and ease of use. For novice artists, a basic set of watercolor paints and brushes can provide endless opportunities to experiment. Look for:

  • Watercolor sets: A small palette with a range of colors, including primary hues and some secondary shades, is a good starting point.
  • Brushes: Watercolor brushes come in various shapes and sizes. For beginners, start with a round, synthetic brush and a flat brush.
  • Watercolor paper: It’s essential to choose high-quality, textured watercolor paper (around 200 gsm or higher) to prevent buckling and ensure smooth paint application.

5. Acrylic Paints

Acrylic paints are a fantastic medium for beginners because they are easy to use and versatile. They dry quickly, which allows you to work in layers and make adjustments without much delay. For a beginner set, you’ll want a few key colors:

  • Primary colors (red, blue, yellow)
  • Black and white for mixing
  • A few earth tones such as burnt sienna and raw umber

Acrylic paints can be applied to a variety of surfaces, including canvas, paper, wood, and fabric, which opens up plenty of creative possibilities for beginners.

Art Supplies for Experts

Once you’ve gained experience and refined your skills, it’s time to explore more advanced art supplies that can help you elevate your work. These materials may require more expertise but can result in stunning, professional-grade art. Here are some must-have supplies for expert artists:

1. High-Quality Graphite Pencils

While beginners can get by with a basic pencil set, expert artists may require a broader range of pencils to achieve fine details, shading, and texture in their work. Consider investing in a high-quality set of graphite pencils, such as:

  • Staedtler Mars Lumograph: Known for its smooth texture and fine lines.
  • Faber-Castell 9000: Offers a wide range of pencils, from light to dark grades, for precise control.
  • Caran d’Ache Grafwood: These pencils provide rich pigment and durability, making them a favorite among professional artists.

With these pencils, you’ll have more control over your drawing technique, allowing for detailed portraits, landscapes, and more.

2. Professional-Grade Paper

For expert-level artwork, the quality of paper becomes even more important. Professional artists often prefer paper that’s specifically designed for certain media. If you’re working with graphite, charcoal, or colored pencils, look for heavyweight, acid-free paper that can handle detailed work without warping or deteriorating over time. Some excellent options include:

  • Arches Watercolor Paper: A top choice for watercolor and mixed media.
  • Strathmore Bristol Paper: Smooth surface ideal for graphite, pen, and ink.
  • Canson XL Mix Media Paper: Great for both dry and wet media, perfect for experimentation.

A quality sketchbook or pad will allow you to achieve better results and preserve your work for longer.

3. Oil Paints

Oil paints have been a staple of professional artists for centuries. These paints have a rich, vibrant quality and a slow drying time that allows for blending and layering. However, they can be challenging for beginners due to their complexity, making them more suitable for advanced artists. If you decide to work with oil paints, consider investing in:

  • Winsor & Newton Artists’ Oil Color: Known for its rich pigments and smooth consistency.
  • Gamblin Artist Oil Colors: Offers a wide range of colors with excellent lightfastness.
  • Liquin Mediums: These can help speed up the drying time and improve the flow of your paint.

To get the most out of your oil paints, you’ll also need high-quality brushes, solvents, and mediums that enhance the texture and consistency of your paint.

4. Charcoal

For dramatic, expressive drawings, charcoal is an excellent choice. It allows for rich darks, soft shading, and strong contrast. There are several types of charcoal, including compressed and vine, each offering a different texture and effect. Expert artists often use charcoal for life drawing, portraits, and still life.

  • Generals Charcoal: Well-known for its high quality and deep, rich tones.
  • Winsor & Newton Willow Charcoal: Known for its smoothness and ability to create soft, blended effects.

Charcoal requires special care to prevent smudging, so investing in a good fixative is also important.

5. Premium Paint Brushes

The type of brush you use has a significant impact on the texture and finish of your artwork. Professional-grade brushes come in a variety of shapes, sizes, and materials, such as sable, hog bristle, and synthetic fibers. Each type of brush is suited for different painting styles and media. Some of the top brands for professional brushes include:

  • Winsor & Newton Series 7: Known for their fine sable hair, these brushes are perfect for watercolor and detailed work.
  • Princeton Velvetouch Brushes: Synthetic brushes that mimic the qualities of natural hair, offering a great balance between durability and performance.
  • Da Vinci Maestro: Excellent for oil and acrylic paints, these brushes are made from the highest quality sable and are designed to retain their shape.

Choosing the right brush for your painting technique will allow you to achieve precision and control, whether you’re working with oil paints, acrylics, or watercolors.

Final Thoughts

Whether you’re a beginner just discovering your passion for art or an expert looking to refine your craft, having the right supplies is essential. As you grow and experiment with different mediums, your art supplies will evolve, helping you push your creativity and technical skills to new heights. By starting with the basics and gradually investing in higher-quality materials, you’ll be able to elevate your artwork and bring your creative vision to life.

Remember, the best art supplies are those that resonate with your style and workflow. Don’t be afraid to experiment, explore, and find the tools that help you create your most beautiful and expressive pieces.
