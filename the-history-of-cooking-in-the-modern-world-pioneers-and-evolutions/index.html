The History of Cooking in the Modern World: Pioneers and Evolutions


The way societies cook and eat food is deeply rooted in their culture, values, and history. As the world evolved, so too did cooking methods, garnering us to the modern culinary scene that we know today. Let’s take a stroll down memory lane and explore the history of cooking in the modern world.

Industrial Revolution: The Start of Modern Cooking

The Industrial Revolution, spanning from the 18th to early 19th century, marked the beginning of modern cooking. Mass production, consumerism, and urbanization gained momentum, revolutionizing the way people cooked and ate.

One of the most prominent creations during the industrial era was kitchen appliances. The first mechanical refrigerator was invented in 1834 by John Gorrie, and later, in 1913, the first electric home freezer was introduced, making it possible for households to store and preserve food for longer periods.

The Rise of Convenience Foods

The rapid progress of technology had a significant impact on the way we cook and eat. Mass production of food items and the advent of frozen and canned foods made cooking meals more accessible and time-efficient for households during the early 20th century.

Rapidly growing cities and the lack of available time due to increase in employment opportunities led to a greater demand for convenient, easily-prepared foods. Companies like Nestle rapidly expanded to accommodate this growing demand, creating and popularizing items like Carnation milk, which did not require refrigeration.

Pioneers and Innovators

The culinary landscape owes a great deal to some pioneering figures, who have continually pushed boundaries and brought creative change to the way we cook and serve food. Some of these notable figures include:

Julia Child (1912-2004)

Named as the “godmother of tv cooking shows,” Julia Child brought French cooking to the masses. In 1963, she revolutionized cooking education with her landmark show, “The French Chef” on public television. Child’s cookbook, “Mastering the Art of French Cooking,” has become a staple in many kitchens around the world.

Jamie Oliver (1975-present)

As the “naked chef,” Jamie Oliver brought cooking into the everyday conversation, teaching viewers to make simple, healthy meals from scratch. By championing the use of fresh, local ingredients, Oliver raised awareness about the school food system, creating a shift toward healthier eating habits and inspiring millions to cook at home.

Rachael Ray (1968-present)

Rachael Ray’s 30-minute meals not only changed how people cook, but also transformed their approach towards meal planning and preparation. With her rapid-fire cooking shows and approachable recipes, she made cooking attractive and easy for aspiring and busy home cooks.

Grant Achatz (1976-present)

Grant Achatz redefined molecular gastronomy with his revolutionary restaurant, Alinea in Chicago. By integrating art and science, his avant-garde dishes pushed the limits of what is possible in the culinary world.

Modern Cooking Trends

In recent years, the culinary world experienced an explosion of fusion cuisines, culinary tourism, and a renewed focus on sustainable and seasonal ingredients. Popularity for healthier eating, including paleo, keto, and vegan diets, have paved the way for innovative cooking methods and ingredient sourcing.

The rise of food blogs, social media, and online communities have also played a significant role in shaping how we cook and share our culinary experiences. Through seamless exchange of ideas, cooking techniques, and experimentation, cooking enthusiasts have formed a powerful global community.

Conclusion

As we can see, the history of cooking in the modern world is far from ordinary. It’s a narrative that speaks to humanity’s collective desire for exploration, innovation, and shared experiences. From the marvellous artistry of French chefs to singular acts of defiance in school cafeterias, the path we have walked has led us to a world full of flavour, curiosity, and culinary adventure. And as we continue to write this story, there’s no doubt that new pioneers will continue to shape our future and redefine our kitchen tables.
