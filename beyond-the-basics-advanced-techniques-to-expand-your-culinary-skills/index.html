Beyond the Basics: Advanced Techniques to Expand Your Culinary Skills


Cooking is both an art and a science. While some may argue that it lacks the grandeur of painting or the precision of chemistry, food is a universal way to express oneself and connect with others. As cultures around the world intertwine and trade culinary techniques, it’s no wonder that enthusiasts are constantly seeking to take their cooking abilities - or their kitchen alchemy - to new heights.

In this blog post, we’re not simply expanding our culinary repertoires; we’re diving headfirst into the pool of advanced techniques that could potentially become your next cooking showstoppers.

1. Temperature and Appliance Control

To elevate your culinary expertise, you need to understand the role of precise temperature control. Rather than relying on the traditional recommendation or guessing, utilize a remote meat thermometer to closely monitor the internal temperature of your protein as it cooks. This will allow you to achieve that perfect cooking temperature every single time and will understand how different techniques such as sous-vide, searing, or slow roasting work and what levels of cooking precision are necessary for exceptional results.

2. Modernist Cuisine Techniques

Modernist cuisine brings surprising new techniques to the kitchen through a mix of classic cooking methods and modern food science. Techniques like cryogenics, prototyping, and computerized control systems have the potential to change the culinary experience. While molecular gastronomy might have sparked some controversy over the years, there is little doubting its creativity and innovation. From altering the taste and texture of a dish with flavor molecules and thickeners to incorporating liquid nitrogen into artful cocktails, the possibilities are as vast as one’s imagination.

3. Fermentation and Pickling

Fermentation and pickling are techniques that offer your dishes a rich, complex taste and texture. By harnessing the magic of naturally occurring microorganisms, fermentation lends its umami richness to many incredible dishes including kimchi, sauerkraut, and Kombucha. Pickling, on the other hand, allows you to capture the freshness of fruits and vegetables even as they develop a pickled taste. Begin with simple quick pickles and work your way up to chutneys, jams, and fermented condiments.

4. Artisan Bread Baking

Bread baking is an art, combining all the science skills and love for crafting delectable food items. The secret lies not in the over-complicated process - but in allowing the time and temperature to work their magic. Perfect your skill by learning about autolyse, the windowpane test, and the many types of levains, sourdough starters, or yeast cultures. It may take time, but once mastered, the art of bread making will reward you with delicious homemade bread, croissants, bagels, and beyond.

5. Herbalism and Ethnobotany

Herbs are essential ingredients in many dishes, enhancing flavors and adding medicinal benefits. One step further, the field of ethnobotany offers interesting insights into the relationship between people and plants, and how these time-honored partnerships have resulted in dishes, beverages, and remedies that have deep cultural roots. Explore how various spices and herbs can complement flavors or even serve as standalone stars at the center of a dish.

6. Cooking with Pressure

The pressure cooker offers a world of opportunities and is a powerful time and energy saver. It harnesses high-pressure steam to cook food up to 70% faster than traditional cooking methods. Using a pressure cooker, you can turn cheap and tough cuts of meat into tender and delicious dishes, cook beans, rice, and grains in half the time, and even bake custards, cakes, and other treats.

Concluding Thoughts

While it’s clear that culinary skills can benefit from an infusion of advanced techniques, keep in mind that the subjective nature of food means that mastering and honing basic skills is equally essential. As you experiment with the techniques outlined above, try to understand the underlying principles and adapt them to incorporate flavors and ingredients you love. After all, cooking is a creative outlet. As you venture beyond the basics, the possibilities are essentially limitless. Happy cooking!
