The Best Fishing Spots Around the World


Fishing is more than just a pastime; it’s a skill, an art, and for many, a lifetime passion. The thrill of feeling a tug on your line, the anticipation of what lies beneath the water’s surface, and the serenity of spending time surrounded by nature make this activity truly special. Whether you’re an experienced angler seeking new adventures or just someone looking for tranquil waters to try your hand at catching fish, the world offers countless incredible fishing destinations. Let’s embark on a journey to explore the best fishing spots globally—places that promise unforgettable experiences, stunning views, and bountiful catches.

1. Cairns, Australia

Known as the “Gateway to the Great Barrier Reef,” Cairns offers access to one of the most spectacular marine environments on the planet. Anglers flock here for the chance to target giant black marlin, which are abundant in these tropical waters, especially between September and December. With the rich biodiversity of the reef, you can also expect to catch species like yellowfin tuna, barracuda, and coral trout. Charter services cater to both beginners and experts, ensuring that everyone can enjoy the experience.

But Cairns isn’t just for deep-sea fishing enthusiasts. The saltwater estuaries and mangroves around the area provide excellent opportunities for catching barramundi, mangrove jack, and various species of flathead. This variety makes Cairns one of the best fishing destinations for anglers of all skill levels.

2. Alaska, USA

If you’re looking for rugged wilderness filled with pristine rivers, lakes, and coastal waters, Alaska is a dream come true. The Last Frontier boasts an abundance of fish species year-round, making it one of the most iconic fishing destinations in the world. Fly fishing for rainbow trout in the Kenai River is legendary, and the river’s salmon runs are equally remarkable.

Kodiak Island, sometimes referred to as the “Island of the Giant Halibut,” offers anglers the chance to reel in halibut that often weigh well over 100 pounds. Other available species include rockfish and lingcod. Wherever you go in Alaska, the scenery alone—snow-capped mountains, glaciers, and untouched forests—makes the experience magical.

Don’t forget ice fishing in the winters! The state’s frozen lakes provide opportunities to catch Arctic char, lake trout, and northern pike.

3. Lake Victoria, Africa

Spanning Tanzania, Uganda, and Kenya, Lake Victoria is Africa’s largest lake and a haven for freshwater fishing enthusiasts. Nile perch is the main draw here, as these giant fish can grow to enormous sizes, offering a thrilling challenge to anglers. The lake also features delightful tilapia populations for those seeking a simpler fishing experience. Local fishing guides are often readily available and are invaluable for navigating this vast aquatic expanse.

One of the significant appeals of Lake Victoria is its rich cultural backdrop. Fishing here isn’t just an activity; it’s a chance to immerse yourself in a vibrant community that relies heavily on the lake for livelihood and sustenance.

4. Iceland

With its otherworldly landscapes of volcanic rock, glacial rivers, and deep fjords, Iceland is a unique and stunning destination for anglers. The country is famed for its clean waters, which teem with Arctic char, brown trout, and salmon. Fly fishing enthusiasts gravitate toward the Laxa River, known for having some of the finest salmon runs in the world.

Another gem is Thingvallavatn, Iceland’s largest natural lake. Here, anglers can catch wild trout so big that they’ve earned the title “Ice Age Trout.” Fishing in Iceland isn’t just about the catch—it’s also about feeling connected to the raw beauty of nature at every step.

5. Amazon River Basin, Brazil

The Amazon River Basin is a bucket-list destination for exotic fishing experiences. These waters are home to incredibly unique species, such as the predatory peacock bass—renowned for its vibrant colors and aggressive nature. If you’re in the mood for a challenge, you can target monstrous catfish and intimidating payara, also known as the “vampire fish” due to their formidable sharp teeth.

Aside from fishing, the Amazon offers unparalleled opportunities to explore the world’s largest rainforest. It’s a place where the environment feels alive, buzzing with biodiversity, and the backdrop always enhances the thrill of the angling adventure.

6. British Columbia, Canada

British Columbia is a dream destination for anglers due to its diverse fishing opportunities and breathtaking scenery. Whether you’re fly fishing along fast-moving streams or trolling the deep ocean waters off Vancouver Island, this region delivers unmatched experiences.

The Fraser River is synonymous with salmon fishing, particularly during the annual salmon runs. The river also provides chances to catch massive sturgeon—fish so big they’ve become legendary among anglers. For saltwater enthusiasts, BC’s coastal waters host halibut, lingcod, and rockfish, while the interior lakes boast trout aplenty.

7. New Zealand

Both the North and South Islands of New Zealand are bursting with incredible fishing opportunities. Known for its crystal-clear rivers and lakes, the country is a paradise for fly fishing enthusiasts. Brown trout and rainbow trout are particularly abundant, with the South Island’s Waikato River being a top spot for trophy catches.

New Zealand’s coastal waters attract deep-sea anglers in pursuit of marlin, tuna, snapper, and kingfish. The Bay of Islands, located on the North Island, remains one of the most famous fishing destinations globally, offering stunning views and an array of fish species that challenge and excite anglers.

8. Scotland, United Kingdom

Scotland is steeped in fishing tradition, making it one of the top spots in Europe for anglers seeking a mix of historical charm and natural beauty. The country’s lochs and river systems, set among dramatic landscapes, offer serene environments for catching wild salmon, trout, and even pike.

The River Tweed is a favored location for salmon fishing, while Loch Lomond tempts anglers with its diverse array of freshwater species. Scotland is also known for its sea fishing opportunities along the rugged coastline. Cod, haddock, and mackerel are popular targets, and you’ll often catch these species with stunning views of cliffside castles in the background.

9. Maldives

The Maldives may immediately evoke images of tropical white-sand destinations, but below the shimmering turquoise waters lies a haven for fishing enthusiasts. Whether you’re trolling for big-game fish or practicing sustainable jigging techniques, this paradise offers exciting angling opportunities.

The local reefs are thriving with species like grouper, barracuda, and snapper, while the deep waters surrounding the islands are known for marlin, tuna, and sailfish. Fishing charters in the Maldives provide luxurious experiences, often combining angling with snorkeling and gourmet dining aboard the boat.

10. Norway

Norway’s breathtaking fjords and coastline are synonymous with world-class fishing opportunities. Cod fishing in the snow-covered Arctic landscape of Northern Norway is particularly popular, offering anglers the chance to catch the highly prized skrei, or migratory cod. Haddock, halibut, and wolffish are also abundant, ensuring thrilling fishing opportunities.

If freshwater fishing is more your style, Norway’s rivers and lakes teem with Atlantic salmon, trout, and Arctic char. These locations combine excellent fishing with endless panoramic views that make every outing truly unforgettable.

11. Puerto Rico

Fishing in Puerto Rico is a unique experience that blends the vibrancy of Latin culture with the thrill of tropical angling. The island’s waters are known for their year-round availability of tarpon, often referred to as the “Silver King” due to its impressive size and aerial acrobatics when hooked. Fly fishing for tarpon in the San Juan Lagoon is particularly popular.

For offshore fishing enthusiasts, the deep blue waters off Puerto Rico’s coasts promise encounters with marlin, tuna, mahi-mahi, and bonito. Experienced guides and charters are plentiful, ensuring visitors have a chance to make the most of this island gem.

Fishing is a universal pursuit, offering both relaxation and adventure in stunning locations across the globe. Whether you’re drawn to the towering mountains of Alaska, the tropical seas of the Maldives, or the mysterious allure of the Amazon, the world is full of incredible destinations waiting for you to cast your line. So grab your rods and reels, plan your next adventure, and get ready to discover the fishing spots that truly make an angler’s heart sing. Happy fishing!
