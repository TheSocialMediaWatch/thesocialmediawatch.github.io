10 Things Every New Gamer Should Know


Whether you’re diving into the world of gaming for the first time or have decided to reignite an old passion, there’s a lot to learn. Gaming can be a bit overwhelming with its vast genres, ever-evolving technology, and dedicated communities. However, once you understand the basics, gaming can become an incredibly rewarding hobby, offering everything from casual entertainment to competitive esports opportunities.

If you’re just starting your journey, here are 10 things every new gamer should know to make the most of their experience.

1. Choose the Right Platform for You

The first thing every new gamer should do is decide on which platform they want to play. The major platforms include:

  • PC: Offers the most customization and performance but can be expensive. Perfect for gamers who want the highest-quality visuals and the flexibility to upgrade their hardware.
  • Consoles: Includes systems like PlayStation, Xbox, and Nintendo Switch. Consoles are great for plug-and-play simplicity, and they offer exclusive titles that aren’t available on other platforms.
  • Mobile: If you’re on a budget or want something convenient, mobile gaming offers a wide range of free and paid games. Although the experience might not be as immersive as a PC or console, mobile gaming has become a significant part of the industry.

Each platform has its strengths, so think about what fits your budget, your gaming habits, and your interest in specific titles.

2. Understand the Different Game Genres

The world of gaming is full of different genres, each offering unique experiences. Here are some of the most popular ones:

  • Action/Adventure: Games like The Legend of Zelda and Uncharted combine elements of exploration, puzzle-solving, and combat, providing an immersive story experience.
  • RPGs (Role-Playing Games): Games like The Witcher 3 or Final Fantasy immerse you in rich narratives where you control a character and make impactful decisions.
  • FPS (First-Person Shooters): These games, such as Call of Duty or Halo, focus on shooting mechanics and are often multiplayer-based.
  • MOBA (Multiplayer Online Battle Arena): Titles like League of Legends and Dota 2 revolve around team-based gameplay with a focus on strategy and coordination.
  • Simulation: From building houses in The Sims to running entire cities in SimCity, these games focus on managing resources and creating virtual experiences.

The more familiar you are with different genres, the easier it will be to find the games that suit your playstyle.

3. Invest in Comfortable Gear

While it may be tempting to go for the cheapest gear available, comfortable and functional gaming accessories can make a huge difference in your experience. Here are some essentials to consider:

  • Headset: A good gaming headset is important not only for immersive sound but also for clear communication if you play online multiplayer games. Look for a headset that is comfortable for long hours and has a quality microphone.
  • Keyboard and Mouse (for PC Gamers): If you’re on a PC, investing in a mechanical keyboard and an accurate gaming mouse will improve your gameplay. These peripherals often come with customizable buttons and lighting, adding to both function and style.
  • Controller (for Console and PC Gamers): For those playing on consoles, a reliable controller is a must. On PC, controllers are also an option, especially for games that are better suited to a gamepad, like racing games or platformers.

Comfortable gear can help you avoid physical strain and enhance your performance, especially if you plan on gaming for extended periods.

4. Start with Easy Games

When you’re new to gaming, it’s a good idea to start with easier, less complex games to build up your skills. Look for titles that don’t overwhelm you with intricate mechanics or steep learning curves. Games like Minecraft, Super Mario Odyssey, or Stardew Valley are perfect for newcomers. These games allow you to experiment, learn, and have fun without feeling stressed.

Once you get the hang of it, you can gradually move on to more complex and challenging games.

5. Practice Regularly and Be Patient

Gaming, like any other skill, takes time to master. Whether you’re learning a new game or improving in an online competitive title, practice is key. Don’t get discouraged if you don’t win your first few matches or if a game’s mechanics seem tricky at first.

Be patient with yourself and remember that improvement comes with time. Set realistic goals for yourself, like mastering one game mechanic or completing a particular mission, and celebrate your progress along the way.

6. Learn About Online Communities

Online gaming communities are a great way to meet other gamers, share tips, and improve your skills. Most online multiplayer games have active communities where players share strategies, news, and updates. Platforms like Reddit, Discord, and Twitch also host communities where you can interact with fellow gamers and even watch professional streamers.

However, remember that online gaming communities can sometimes be toxic. If you encounter negativity or harassment, don’t hesitate to mute or block users, and always report inappropriate behavior. Maintaining a positive gaming environment will make your experience more enjoyable.

7. Understand the Importance of Game Settings

Every gamer should take the time to adjust their game settings before jumping in. Depending on your system and personal preferences, the default settings might not be optimal for performance or comfort. Here are some settings you may want to tweak:

  • Graphics Settings: Adjust the resolution, texture quality, and shadows to ensure your game runs smoothly on your system.
  • Controls: Customize your controls to match your playstyle. Many games offer options to remap buttons or adjust sensitivity.
  • Audio: Fine-tune the volume of in-game sounds and music so you can enjoy the experience without the audio becoming distracting or too loud.

Taking the time to adjust your settings will make the game feel more comfortable and responsive, enhancing your overall experience.

8. Stay Mindful of In-Game Purchases

Many modern games come with microtransactions, offering players the option to purchase cosmetics, skins, or other content. While it can be tempting to spend money on in-game items, especially when there are frequent sales or discounts, it’s important to stay mindful of your spending habits.

Set a budget for in-game purchases and avoid impulse buying. Be aware that some games may use tactics to encourage spending, like offering rare items that can only be obtained by paying real money. Always evaluate whether a purchase enhances your gaming experience or if it’s just a temporary addition that doesn’t provide lasting value.

9. Know How to Stay Safe Online

While gaming is a fun and social activity, it’s also important to be aware of the risks that come with online interactions. Here are a few tips to keep you safe:

  • Protect your personal information: Never share sensitive details like your home address, phone number, or credit card information with strangers.
  • Use a secure password: Choose a strong and unique password for your gaming accounts, and enable two-factor authentication where possible.
  • Be cautious of scams: Be wary of offers that sound too good to be true, such as free game giveaways or “hack” websites. Stick to official sources when purchasing games or content.

By being aware of potential risks, you can enjoy online gaming without compromising your safety.

10. Take Breaks and Manage Your Time

Gaming can be an immersive experience, but it’s important to take breaks and manage your time. Prolonged gaming sessions can lead to physical strain, eye fatigue, and even mental burnout. Follow these tips to maintain a healthy balance:

  • Take regular breaks: Follow the 20-20-20 rule – every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet away for 20 seconds to rest your eyes.
  • Stretch your body: Sitting in the same position for long periods can cause discomfort. Stand up, stretch, and move around every once in a while to avoid stiffness.
  • Set time limits: Avoid playing for hours on end by setting a timer or creating a schedule for your gaming sessions. This will help you maintain balance with other activities in your life.

By managing your time and health, you’ll ensure that gaming remains a fun and sustainable hobby.

Final Thoughts

Becoming a gamer is an exciting journey, filled with new experiences, challenges, and fun moments. By following these tips, you’ll be well on your way to enjoying gaming to its fullest. Remember to choose the right platform, start with games that suit your skill level, invest in comfortable gear, and practice patience as you improve. Above all, enjoy the process and the vibrant world of gaming that awaits you!
