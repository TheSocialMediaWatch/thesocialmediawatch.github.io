10 Hidden Gem Games You Need to Play This Year


As the gaming industry continues to evolve, more and more games are being released every year. While blockbuster titles like The Legend of Zelda: Tears of the Kingdom and Elden Ring dominate the charts, there are countless hidden gems that often slip under the radar of mainstream players. These underrated titles may not have the massive budgets or the widespread recognition of their bigger counterparts, but they offer unique and compelling experiences that are worth your time. If you’re looking for something fresh and exciting to play, look no further. Here are 10 hidden gem games you absolutely need to play this year.

1. Outer Wilds

If you haven’t heard of Outer Wilds, you’re missing out on one of the most thought-provoking space exploration games in recent memory. Created by Mobius Digital, this indie game combines mystery, exploration, and a unique time-loop mechanic that sets it apart from other space adventures. Players are placed in a solar system trapped in a 22-minute time loop, where they must uncover secrets, solve puzzles, and navigate the cosmos before the loop resets.

The beauty of Outer Wilds lies in its sense of discovery. With each loop, players learn something new about the world and its history, while also experiencing the sheer joy of unraveling the narrative at their own pace. The game’s atmospheric music and stunning visuals provide an immersive experience that will keep you coming back for more.

2. Axiom Verge 2

The Axiom Verge series is a love letter to classic Metroidvania games, and the sequel, Axiom Verge 2, is no exception. Developed by Thomas Happ Games, this 2D action-platformer continues the series’ signature blend of tight controls, exploration, and mind-bending puzzles. Unlike the first game, Axiom Verge 2 introduces a new protagonist and a more varied set of gameplay mechanics.

Players will guide Indra, a new character with a set of abilities that differ from those of the original protagonist, Trace. The game’s atmosphere is dense, the story deep, and the world is beautifully rendered, offering hours of exploration and challenge for fans of the genre. Axiom Verge 2 is an excellent example of how to honor the past while still innovating and improving upon established formulas.

3. The Pathless

If you’re someone who appreciates a stunning visual experience combined with fast-paced, fluid movement, then The Pathless should be on your radar. Developed by Giant Squid, the studio behind Abzû, this action-adventure game takes place in a lush, open-world environment filled with mythical creatures and ancient ruins. You play as the Hunter, a skilled archer who must traverse the world to lift a curse that has befallen it.

The game’s most striking feature is its traversal system. With the help of an eagle companion, you’ll zip across vast landscapes, gliding through the air and using momentum to reach high points or evade dangers. The world feels alive and full of mystery, and the puzzles you encounter on your journey are a perfect mix of challenge and creativity. With an emphasis on exploration and fluid movement, The Pathless is a hidden gem that should not be overlooked.

4. Katana Zero

If you’re looking for a fast-paced, stylish action game with a strong narrative, Katana Zero offers a thrilling experience. Developed by Askiisoft, this neo-noir action platformer puts you in the shoes of a hitman who has the ability to slow down time. Players must use their wits, reflexes, and stealth to navigate each level, taking down enemies in choreographed sequences of sword combat.

Katana Zero doesn’t just rely on its combat mechanics; the game also features an engaging story that dives into themes of memory, trauma, and personal identity. The pixel art style, combined with a jazzy soundtrack, creates a gritty, atmospheric vibe that perfectly complements the fast action. This is a title that will leave you thinking long after you’ve finished it.

5. Disco Elysium

For players who enjoy narrative-driven experiences with deep character development and rich storytelling, Disco Elysium is a must-play. Developed by ZA/UM, this role-playing game offers an incredibly unique approach to traditional RPG mechanics. Rather than focusing on combat, Disco Elysium emphasizes dialogue, choices, and skill checks to shape the outcome of the story.

In Disco Elysium, you play as a detective with a troubled past, tasked with solving a murder in a city filled with political unrest and complex characters. The game offers a deep, branching narrative with multiple paths, depending on the skills and decisions you make. The art style is reminiscent of classic noir, and the writing is sharp and full of wit. Disco Elysium offers a compelling, introspective gaming experience that rewards those who love to immerse themselves in rich dialogue and character-driven storytelling.

6. Hades

Though it’s gained some attention in recent years, Hades still remains one of the finest examples of a hidden gem that’s found its way into the limelight through sheer quality. Developed by Supergiant Games, this roguelike dungeon crawler places you in the shoes of Zagreus, the son of Hades, as he attempts to escape the underworld.

What sets Hades apart from other roguelikes is its incredible character development and narrative. As you progress through the game’s challenging dungeons, you’ll encounter a wide range of gods, heroes, and mythological creatures, each with their own personalities and stories. The game’s combat is fast, satisfying, and constantly evolving, with new weapons and abilities to experiment with each run. The artwork, music, and voice acting are top-notch, making Hades a must-play for any fan of action-packed roguelikes.

7. Return of the Obra Dinn

If you enjoy solving puzzles and piecing together mysteries, Return of the Obra Dinn is an absolute treasure. Developed by Lucas Pope, this game presents players with the mysterious death of the crew aboard the Obra Dinn, a merchant ship that has returned after years of being lost at sea. You play as an insurance investigator, tasked with uncovering the fates of the ship’s passengers and crew.

The game’s unique visual style, which mimics the look of an old Macintosh computer, perfectly complements its intricate puzzle-solving mechanics. By examining the ship’s scenes and using a special pocket watch, players can rewind time to witness key moments and deduce the identities and causes of death of those involved. Return of the Obra Dinn is a masterclass in narrative design and puzzle-solving, offering a truly unique and satisfying gameplay experience.

8. Dead Cells

Fans of challenging action-platformers will find Dead Cells to be an absolute treat. Developed by Motion Twin, this roguelike Metroidvania blends fast-paced combat with a procedurally generated world that changes every time you play. As you explore the game’s interconnected areas, you’ll fight your way through hordes of enemies, unlock new abilities, and discover new weapons to aid in your journey.

What makes Dead Cells so addictive is its fluid combat mechanics and rewarding progression system. The game offers a variety of playstyles, whether you prefer to use a sword, bow, or magic, and each weapon feels distinct and satisfying to use. The difficulty is high, but each run is short enough that it encourages you to keep going back for more. If you love fast-paced action and challenging platforming, Dead Cells is one of the best hidden gems to play this year.

9. The Forgotten City

Inspired by a mod for Skyrim, The Forgotten City is a time-loop mystery game that revolves around an ancient Roman city caught in a deadly cycle. Developed by Modern Storyteller, the game places players in a city where the inhabitants must adhere to a strict set of rules. If any of these rules are broken, the entire city is destroyed and the cycle begins again.

As the player, you must investigate the city, uncover the secrets behind its mysterious time loop, and find a way to break the cycle. The game’s branching narrative, multiple endings, and thought-provoking themes make it a standout experience. The Forgotten City is an immersive, narrative-driven adventure that will keep you on the edge of your seat as you unravel its many mysteries.

10. Sunless Sea

Set in a vast underground ocean, Sunless Sea is a survival and exploration game that offers a unique and atmospheric experience. Developed by Failbetter Games, this title combines resource management, storytelling, and exploration in a way that few other games manage to achieve. Players captain a steamship, navigating through the dark and mysterious waters while dealing with threats from both within and outside the ship.

The game’s haunting atmosphere, combined with its deep lore and challenging mechanics, creates a truly immersive experience. As you venture deeper into the unknown, you’ll encounter strange creatures, dangerous factions, and increasingly difficult survival challenges. Sunless Sea is a game that rewards patience and curiosity, and it’s one of the most unique hidden gems out there.

Conclusion

In a year filled with incredible games, it’s easy for some gems to get lost in the shuffle. However, these 10 hidden gems offer exceptional experiences that are well worth your time. Whether you’re in the mood for an atmospheric exploration game, a fast-paced action experience, or a deep narrative-driven adventure, there’s something here for everyone. Don’t let these underappreciated titles pass you by. Give them a chance, and you might just discover your new favorite game of the year.
