The Best Comedy Specials on Netflix Right Now


Netflix has become a treasure trove of entertainment, offering everything from gripping dramas to laugh-out-loud comedy specials. In recent years, the streaming giant has committed heavily to expanding its library of stand-up comedy, inviting a diverse range of comedians to showcase their talents. With so much available, it can be hard to decide which comedy special is worth your time, especially when you’re looking for something to brighten your day. Whether you’re in the mood for sharp observations, irreverent humor, or heartfelt storytelling infused with laughs, Netflix has a comedy special to suit every taste.

Here’s a look at some of the best comedy specials currently streaming on Netflix that are guaranteed to leave you in stitches.

Hannah Gadsby: Nanette

Hannah Gadsby’s Nanette redefined what a comedy special could be. Though filled with sharp wit and humor, it’s also deeply introspective and challenging, offering a mix of catharsis and laughter that stays with the audience long after the credits roll. In this groundbreaking special, Gadsby dives into topics like gender identity, trauma, and societal expectations, combining brutal honesty with comedic timing.

What makes Nanette different is its refusal to stick to the standard formula of observational humor. It’s a performance that pushes boundaries, blurring the lines between comedy and storytelling while peppering each revelation with humor as only Gadsby can deliver. If you’re looking for a comedy special that’s as thought-provoking as it is funny, this is where to start.

John Mulaney: Kid Gorgeous at Radio City

John Mulaney’s Kid Gorgeous at Radio City is widely considered one of the finest stand-up specials in recent years. Known for his clever wordplay, impeccable delivery, and universal appeal, Mulaney offers a masterclass in stand-up comedy. This special features his trademark style—clean yet cutting—exploring topics like growing up, generational divides, and the absurdity of daily life.

One of the most memorable segments of Kid Gorgeous is Mulaney’s story about a peculiar assembly speech on stranger danger, which perfectly captures his talent for turning childhood anecdotes into laugh-out-loud moments. Packed with punchlines and polished to perfection, this special is a testament to why Mulaney is one of stand-up’s most revered stars.

Ali Wong: Baby Cobra

Ali Wong burst onto the comedy scene with her wildly funny special Baby Cobra, which brilliantly showcases her unique perspective on relationships, motherhood, and the cultural nuances of being Asian-American. Filmed while Wong was seven months pregnant, the special adds an extra layer of hilarity as she tackles taboo topics with unabashed humor and raw energy.

The real draw here is Wong’s ability to discuss life’s absurdities in a way that feels both relatable and endlessly entertaining. Her boldness in addressing subjects often considered off-limits makes Baby Cobra stand out, and her sharp comedic voice ensures you’ll be laughing from start to finish.

Dave Chappelle: Sticks & Stones

Few comedians have the cultural cachet of Dave Chappelle, and Sticks & Stones is a prime example of his ability to provoke thought while delivering laughs. In this special, Chappelle takes aim at political correctness, cancel culture, and social issues with his signature razor-sharp wit. It’s a bold, fearless performance that invites both laughter and reflection.

While Sticks & Stones has sparked debates due to its controversial content, there’s no denying Chappelle’s talent for crafting comedy that resonates on a deeper level. If you appreciate comedy that pushes boundaries and isn’t afraid to delve into uncomfortable truths, this special will hit the mark.

Taylor Tomlinson: Look at You

Taylor Tomlinson is a rising star in the comedy world, and Look at You cements her status as one of the most relatable comedians of her generation. This special is both deeply personal and universally funny, as Tomlinson navigates mental health, heartbreak, and growing into adulthood with sharp, self-deprecating humor.

What makes Tomlinson’s approach so compelling is her ability to weave personal experiences into broader observations that everyone can laugh at. If you’re looking for a comedy special that feels fresh, heartfelt, and hilarious, Look at You is an excellent choice.

Bo Burnham: Inside

Though skirting the traditional format of a stand-up special, Bo Burnham’s Inside is a comedic masterpiece that deserves a spot on your watchlist. Filmed entirely during the pandemic, this musical comedy extravaganza captures the isolation, frustration, and absurdity many experienced during lockdown. Burnham blends satire, music, and visual artistry to create a piece of work that is as funny as it is poignant.

With clever songs like “White Woman’s Instagram” and “Welcome to the Internet,” Burnham delivers comedy that is equal parts entertaining and intelligent. Inside pushes creative boundaries while keeping you laughing at its surreal yet relatable themes.

Sebastian Maniscalco: Is It Me?

Sebastian Maniscalco’s energetic style and knack for observations make him one of the most dynamic comedians around. In Is It Me?, Maniscalco doesn’t disappoint, delivering a torrent of hilarious sketches about everyday life, family interactions, and the quirks of modern culture. His exaggerated physical comedy and unique delivery distinguish him from other performers.

Maniscalco’s humor shines brightest when he’s talking about relatable struggles—whether it’s preparing for social situations or interacting with family members who rarely see eye to eye. If you’re looking for a comedy special that offers big laughs with no complexities, Is It Me? deserves a spot on your list.

Nicole Byer: Big Beautiful Weirdo

Nicole Byer’s first Netflix special, Big Beautiful Weirdo, showcases her infectious personality and sharp humor. Byer brings her unique blend of charm and candidness to topics like dating, body positivity, and navigating life as a Black woman in America. Her comedic timing, combined with her ability to tell a story, makes this special a breath of fresh air.

What truly makes Byer stand out is her unapologetic approach. Whether she’s riffing on online dating or embracing her quirks, her humor feels authentic and empowering. Big Beautiful Weirdo is the kind of special that’ll have you laughing and cheering Byer on in equal measure.

Kevin Hart: Irresponsible

Kevin Hart’s Irresponsible serves up the comedic anecdotes that have made him a household name. With his magnetic presence and rapid-fire delivery, Hart dives into personal stories about his life, marriage, and parenting, finding humor in moments that others might see as mundane. His ability to transform relatable situations into laugh-out-loud material is unparalleled.

Whether you’re already a Kevin Hart fan or new to his comedy, Irresponsible is an excellent showcase of his talent. His upbeat energy and knack for storytelling make this one of Netflix’s standout specials for fans of light, easy-to-enjoy comedy.

Netflix offers an immense selection of comedy specials, each with its own unique flavor. From heartfelt narratives to unapologetic humor that challenges norms, the platform has become the go-to destination for fans of stand-up. While these specials are just the tip of the iceberg, they represent the broad spectrum of talent and perspectives currently defining comedy. No matter your mood or sense of humor, there’s something on Netflix waiting to make you laugh. So grab your popcorn, settle in, and enjoy the comedic brilliance streaming right at your fingertips!
