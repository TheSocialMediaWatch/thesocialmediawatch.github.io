Cooking with Cast Iron: Tips and Tricks for Perfectly Cooked Meals


Cast iron cookware has been in use for centuries, dating back to the colonial era. Whether your cast iron is a well-seasoned skillet, Dutch oven or even a griddle, it can be an indispensable kitchen tool for cooking everything from eggs to decadent desserts. In this guide, we’ll share tips and tricks to help you make the most of your cast iron cookware for perfectly cooked meals every time.

  1. Care and Maintenance

Cast iron requires proper care and maintenance for optimal performance. Here are some crucial steps to make sure your cast iron stays in top condition:

  • Rinse with hot water: After each use, quickly rinse your cast iron with hot water. Avoid using soap or abrasive cleaners as they can remove the seasoning and damage the surface.
  • Dry thoroughly: Dry your cast iron thoroughly with a towel or let it air dry on the stovetop. Water trapped in the iron can lead to rust.
  • Season before first use: If you just bought your cast iron or want to re-season an old one, follow this simple process: clean the cast iron with hot, soapy water, rinse, dry and then apply a light layer of oil (vegetable, canola, or shortening) to the entire surface. Place it in a 375°F oven for an hour, then remove and cool it before storing.
  1. Preheating and Heat Management

Cast iron retains and distributes heat well which makes it perfect for slow-cooking and searing. You’ll need to know how to manage the heat properly.

  • Preheat before cooking: Always preheat your cast iron cookware on a low to medium heat for a few minutes before adding your ingredients. This ensures that your food cooks evenly and prevents sticking.
  • Use the right size: The size of your cast iron determines the quantity of food you cook and how you position it for even cooking. For example, larger cast iron skillets are best for cooking large roasts, while a smaller skillet works well for pancakes or eggs.
  • Know the temperatures: Different foods require different temperatures. For example, sauteing usually requires medium-high heat, while slow-cooking needs low heat.
  1. Cooking Techniques

Here are some cooking techniques that work particularly well with cast iron:

  • Searing: Use cast iron to create a beautiful, flavorful sear on your steak or burger by preheating your skillet on high for a few minutes, adding the protein and searing for just a few minutes on each side.
  • Slow cooking: Cast iron excels in slow cooking. Its ability to retain and distribute heat evenly makes it perfect for soups, stews, and even braising.
  • Frying: Whether you are making classic Southern fried chicken or homemade hash browns, cast iron can handle it. The even cooking and natural non-stick surface of a well-seasoned cast iron makes for perfect fried food.

Cooking with cast iron is a versatile and economical way to create many delicious meals. A well-cared-for cast iron can last a lifetime and give you perfectly cooked meals every time. Now that you know the tips and tricks for using your cast iron, go experiment with your favorite recipes and take your culinary creations to the next level.
