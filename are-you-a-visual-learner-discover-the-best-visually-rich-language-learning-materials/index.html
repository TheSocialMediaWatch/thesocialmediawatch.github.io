Are You A Visual Learner? Discover The Best Visually-rich Language Learning Materials


Visual learners have distinctive preferences in the way they absorb and process information. This group of individuals recall and understand concepts better when information is visualâ€”through images, colors, maps, diagrams, and flowcharts. While some people primarily learn through listening (auditory learners) or by doing (kinesthetic learners), visual learners benefit the most from materials that incorporate rich visual stimuli, such as diagrams, animations, videos, and infographics. As language learning evolves with an abundance of materials available for students of all learning styles, here we focus on the best resources for visual learners to acquire languages efficiently and effectively.

1. Duolingo

Duolingo is an engaging language learning platform where visual learners can develop their vocabulary, grammar, pronunciation, and immersive communication skills using a variety of interactive exercises.

Pros:

  • Over 30 languages available.
  • Gamified learning to keep users motivated.
  • Free to use.
  • Bite-sized lessons, ideal for busy learners.

Cons:

  • Limited visual aids and videos.

2. FluentU

FluentU offers a unique approach to language learning with native video content, such as commercials, music videos, news, interviews, and inspiring talks. Visual learners will appreciate the videos’ subtitles for easier comprehension of the language while enjoying the engaging content.

Pros:

  • Wide variety of video content.
  • Subtitles in multiple languages for understanding.
  • Tangential contextual learning.

Cons:

  • Video duration may be too long for some learners.

3. Memrise

Memrise is a well-rounded language learning platform that focuses on vocabulary development. Visual learners can appreciate the various memory techniques and spaced repetition that Memrise employs, using engaging images, games, and movies to reinforce foreign language terms.

Pros:

  • Wide range of languages available.
  • Memorable visuals for vocabulary building.
  • Friendly user community.

Cons:

  • Visual content mainly revolves around vocabulary acquisition.

4. MosaLingua

MosaLingua is an effective language learning app with its core focus on vocabulary retention through spaced repetition. Its “Remember Forever” methodology allows visual learners to easily track their progress through visual progress bars and charts, making it an excellent resource for reinforcing language acquisition.

Pros:

  • Measurable progress through visual aids.
  • Daily lesson updates.
  • Polish and Spanish languages offered.

Cons:

  • Limited language offerings.
  • Not as visually rich as other resources.

5. Anki

Anki is a powerful memory aid tool created around spaced repetition. Visual learners will benefit from Anki’s customizable flashcards designed with images, text, and audio to improve language recall and long-term retention. Users can even create their decks based on foreign language textbooks and video content.

Pros:

  • Highly customizable.
  • Freeware.
  • Wide range of languages supported.

Cons:

  • Learning curve to understand the basic mechanics.

6. Rosetta Stone

Although Rosetta Stone has moved away from the traditional deck method, it remains a popular choice among language learners. Visual learners will appreciate its immersive method, visual cues, and cues that provide context to help with remembering and understanding new vocabulary and grammar concepts.

Pros:

  • Modern and innovative approach to language learning.
  • Eye-catching visuals.
  • All-in-one solution with speaking practice and an online community.

Cons:

  • Significant upfront investment.
  • Less focus on specific, visual learning strategies.

7. iTalki

For visual learners who prefer one-on-one interaction, iTalki connects them with language teachers from around the world. While iTalki itself does not offer visual content, you can supplement your lessons with video materials that cater to your visual preferences.

Pros:

  • Personalized learning experience.
  • Flexible schedule and session options.
  • Variety in native speakers and teachers.

Cons:

  • Costly for some learners.
  • Quality of teachers varies.

In summary, visual learners can benefit from the rich variety of language learning materials each platform offers. It’s worth trying different tools, and you might discover that different resources cater to your preferences at various stages of language acquisition. Ultimately, choosing the best visual-rich language learning resources depends on your level, your desired language, and how you like to learn.

Happy language learning!
