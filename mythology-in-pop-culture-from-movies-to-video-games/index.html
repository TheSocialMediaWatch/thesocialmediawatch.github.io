Mythology in Pop Culture: From Movies to Video Games


Mythology has existed as long as humans have gathered around campfires to share stories. These narratives have provided us with a window into our ancestors’ beliefs, values, and the way they understood the world around them. Over time, mythology has become a rich vein of inspiration for pop culture, appearing in various forms like movies and video games. This post will explore how these ancient tales have infiltrated modern media and become a part of our shared cultural lexicon.

One of the most notable influences of mythology on pop culture is the legendary stories of ancient Greece. The fascinating tales of gods and goddesses, their amorous dalliances, and the terrible punishments they dealt out to humanity have long held a captivate audience. In movies like Disney’s “Hercules” and the critically acclaimed “Wonder Woman,” the gods of Mount Olympus are given a chance to shine.

The Greek pantheon’s complexities and intricacies have also made their way into video games. “God of War” places players in the sandals of Kratos, a brutal Spartan warrior who seeks to settle his score with the gods. Through meticulous storytelling and richly developed characters, the game breathes new life into the myths of ancient Greece, keeping players enthralled and engaged.

But mythology is not limited to Greek stories alone. The mythologies of Norse and Celtic traditions have also captivated audiences worldwide. In the acclaimed movie “The Dark Crystal,” the mystical world of Thra and its intricate tale of unity and reconciliation draws heavily from both Celtic and Norse lore.

Similarly, video games like “Assassin’s Creed: Valhalla,” delve into the world of Norse mythology. The game’s protagonist, Eivor, is a Viking raider who uncovers a deadly conspiracy spanning centuries. Through gripping gameplay and an immersive narrative, “Assassin’s Creed: Valhalla” breathes life into the twelfth-century Viking world, shedding light on their incredible adventures and beliefs.

Mythology’s ancient tales also touch upon universal themes, making them relevant in our modern context. Horus, the Egyptian god of the sky, is represented in the “Saints Row” video game series, which features a criminal gang led by a character named Lieutenant Kin岛nley, or “The Boss.” The game’s creative team drew inspiration from the mythological persona of Horus and transformed it into a captivating and unique character. This connection to mythology further enriches the game’s narrative, making it appeal to fans across the globe.

In recent years, another form of mythology that has made its mark on pop culture is Asian mythology. Notable examples include “The Mask,” a movie inspired by the Haida myth of the raven trickster, and the wildly popular video game “Final Fantasy.” The game’s rich narrative integrates elements of Asian mythology and folklore, immersing players in worlds that intertwine ancient customs with high-stakes adventure.

As we continue to explore the stories our ancestors left behind, mythology’s influence on pop culture will only continue to grow. With the fascinating world of ancient mythology as their playground, modern storytellers create amazing works that captivate, inspire, and transcend time. From movies to video games, this rich source of inspiration serves to remind us that while we may have evolved in countless ways, the stories that unite us are still very much alive in this modern age.
