The Best Investment Apps for Beginners in 2025


Investing in the stock market, bonds, or other assets can be a powerful way to build wealth over time. However, for beginners, the idea of diving into the world of investments might feel overwhelming. Fortunately, with the rise of investment apps, starting your investing journey has never been easier. These platforms are designed to simplify the process of investing, providing intuitive interfaces and educational resources to help you make informed decisions.

In 2025, the landscape of investment apps has evolved significantly, offering a variety of tools, features, and strategies that cater to both new and experienced investors. Whether you’re looking to invest in stocks, ETFs, cryptocurrencies, or retirement accounts, there’s an app that fits your needs.

This post will explore the best investment apps for beginners in 2025, focusing on their features, ease of use, and overall value to new investors. By the end, you’ll have a clearer understanding of which apps are worth considering as you start your investment journey.

1. Robinhood: Best for Beginners and Zero Fees

When most people think of commission-free trading, Robinhood is one of the first names that come to mind. Since its launch in 2013, the app has become synonymous with accessibility and ease of use. Its straightforward, clean interface makes it an excellent choice for beginners who are just getting started with investing.

Key Features:

  • Zero commission fees: Robinhood revolutionized the industry by offering commission-free trades for stocks, options, and ETFs. In 2025, Robinhood continues to provide this benefit, making it an attractive option for beginners who want to minimize costs.
  • Fractional shares: This feature allows you to invest with smaller amounts of money by buying fractions of shares instead of whole ones. This is especially useful for new investors who might not have large amounts of capital to start with.
  • Cash management: Robinhood also offers a cash management account, which acts like a savings account, allowing you to earn interest on your uninvested cash.
  • Educational resources: The app offers a variety of resources and articles to help you learn about investing basics, stock analysis, and market trends.

Robinhood is an ideal platform for individuals who want a no-frills, easy-to-use investing experience without worrying about commission fees. However, it may not be the best choice for more advanced investors due to its limited research tools.

2. Acorns: Best for Micro-Investing and Automated Portfolios

Acorns is a great app for beginners who want to ease into investing without having to actively manage their portfolio. It is specifically designed for people who want to start investing small amounts of money, often referred to as “micro-investing.”

Key Features:

  • Round-ups: Acorns automatically rounds up your purchases to the nearest dollar and invests the spare change. For example, if you buy a coffee for $2.50, Acorns will round it up to $3.00 and invest the additional 50 cents.
  • Automated portfolios: Based on your risk tolerance and financial goals, Acorns creates an automated portfolio of ETFs. You don’t need to pick stocks yourself—the app does the heavy lifting.
  • Educational content: Acorns offers resources to help users understand investing and personal finance basics, which is particularly helpful for new investors.
  • Retirement accounts: Acorns offers IRAs, which are beneficial for long-term retirement planning. This can be an excellent option for beginners looking to plan for the future.

Acorns is particularly suitable for individuals who want to start investing with minimal effort and little capital. The app’s simplicity and automation make it ideal for those who have a limited understanding of investing but want to start building wealth.

3. Wealthfront: Best for Hands-Off, Long-Term Investing

Wealthfront is an automated investing platform, or “robo-advisor,” that provides a more hands-off investment experience. It is designed for beginners who want to invest for the long term without having to worry about choosing individual stocks or managing their portfolio.

Key Features:

  • Automated investing: Wealthfront uses algorithms to manage your portfolio, selecting low-cost index funds and rebalancing your portfolio automatically.
  • Tax-loss harvesting: This feature helps you reduce your tax bill by selling investments that have declined in value and replacing them with similar assets.
  • Financial planning tools: Wealthfront offers a range of planning tools, including retirement and college savings calculators, that can help you set and track your financial goals.
  • Low fees: Wealthfront charges a management fee of 0.25% per year, which is relatively low compared to traditional financial advisors.

Wealthfront is an excellent choice for beginners who prefer a completely hands-off approach to investing. If you’re looking for a platform that will take care of all the decisions for you while offering a diversified, long-term strategy, Wealthfront is worth considering.

4. Stash: Best for Customizable Portfolios and Learning Resources

Stash offers a unique blend of simplicity and customization, making it one of the top choices for beginner investors. The app allows you to create personalized portfolios based on your financial goals, risk tolerance, and interests.

Key Features:

  • Customizable portfolios: Stash gives you the flexibility to select investments based on themes such as sustainability, technology, or socially responsible investing (SRI).
  • Fractional shares: Like Robinhood, Stash allows you to buy fractional shares, making it accessible for beginners who want to start with smaller amounts of money.
  • Stash Learn: The app provides a library of educational content that explains investing concepts in simple terms, helping beginners understand how markets work.
  • Personalized recommendations: Based on your preferences, Stash suggests specific stocks and ETFs that align with your values and risk level.

If you’re a beginner who wants more control over your investments while still benefiting from educational resources, Stash is an excellent choice. It combines the ease of use of robo-advisors with the ability to choose individual investments that align with your personal values.

5. Fidelity Investments: Best for Comprehensive Investment Options

Fidelity is one of the most established names in the investment world, and its app provides a comprehensive range of investment options. It’s a great choice for beginners who want access to a wide variety of asset classes, including stocks, bonds, mutual funds, and more.

Key Features:

  • Wide range of investment options: Fidelity offers a wide selection of assets, including individual stocks, bonds, mutual funds, ETFs, and even cryptocurrencies. It’s an excellent choice if you want to diversify your investments.
  • Zero fees on some funds: Fidelity offers a number of index funds and ETFs with no expense ratio, which helps you keep your costs low.
  • Research and analysis tools: Although Fidelity’s app is more advanced than some of the other platforms on this list, it still provides plenty of educational resources and easy-to-read data for beginners.
  • Customer support: With a reputation for excellent customer service, Fidelity provides a wealth of resources and live support for new investors who need assistance.

Fidelity is a great option if you’re a beginner who wants to start with a trusted platform that offers a broad range of investment options. If you want the flexibility to grow your portfolio over time and possibly move into more advanced strategies, Fidelity is a good place to start.

6. SoFi Invest: Best for Social Investing and Financial Integration

SoFi is another popular investment app that has gained traction in recent years. It’s known for its easy-to-use interface and wide range of financial products. SoFi’s investment platform makes it simple for beginners to access stocks, ETFs, and even cryptocurrency.

Key Features:

  • Fractional shares: SoFi allows you to buy fractional shares, which is great for beginners who don’t want to invest large sums of money upfront.
  • SoFi community: One unique feature of SoFi is its focus on community and education. Users can connect with other investors and access resources that help them make informed decisions.
  • Automated investing: Like other robo-advisors, SoFi offers automated investing, which selects and manages your portfolio based on your risk profile and goals.
  • No management fees: SoFi doesn’t charge management fees for automated investing, making it a cost-effective option for beginners.

SoFi is an excellent option if you’re looking for a social investing platform with low fees and easy-to-use features. The community aspect sets it apart from other platforms, making it ideal for beginners who want to learn from others while investing.

7. Merrill Edge: Best for Bank Integration and Advanced Tools

Merrill Edge, a part of Bank of America, is a strong contender for beginners who are also interested in integrating their banking and investment services. It provides easy access to both investment options and everyday banking tools.

Key Features:

  • Bank integration: If you’re already a Bank of America customer, Merrill Edge allows you to seamlessly connect your bank and investment accounts, making it easier to manage your finances in one place.
  • Educational resources: Merrill Edge offers a robust collection of articles, tutorials, and investment guides to help beginners get up to speed with the basics of investing.
  • Research tools: While Merrill Edge is beginner-friendly, it also offers advanced research and analysis tools for when you’re ready to dive deeper into your investment strategy.

Merrill Edge is an ideal choice if you’re looking for a simple investment platform that integrates well with your existing bank account. While its research tools may appeal more to advanced users, the platform still offers great resources for beginners.

Conclusion

Investing as a beginner can be intimidating, but with the right tools and apps, the process becomes much more manageable. Each of the apps listed above has unique features tailored to different types of investors. Whether you’re interested in low fees, automated investing, or customizable portfolios, there’s an app for you in 2025.

Choosing the right investment app depends on your personal preferences, goals, and level of involvement you want in your investment strategy. Regardless of which app you choose, remember that investing is a long-term endeavor, and consistency is key. Start small, educate yourself along the way, and watch your financial goals become a reality.
