Top 5 Online Platforms to Make Money by Teaching Music


For musicians who have a passion for teaching and want to monetize their skills, the internet offers a vast array of opportunities. Online music teaching platforms enable instructors to reach students worldwide, set their own schedules, and build a steady income stream. Whether you’re a piano virtuoso, a guitar guru, or a vocal coach, these platforms provide flexible avenues to share knowledge and create a rewarding teaching career.

Let’s dive into the top five online platforms that musicians can leverage to make money by teaching music. This comprehensive guide covers the distinctive features, pros, cons, and tips to maximize earnings on each platform.

1. LessonFace

LessonFace is a premier destination for live online music lessons connecting students with instructors across a variety of instruments and music disciplines.

Why Choose LessonFace?

  • Wide Instrument Range: Covers everything from common instruments like guitar, piano, and drums to niche options such as accordion and ukulele.
  • Live Video Lessons: The platform uses integrated video chat, so you don’t need third-party software.
  • Flexible Scheduling: Teachers can set their available hours and reschedule lessons easily.
  • Secure Payment System: Simple and secure methods for receiving payments.

How It Works

Music teachers create their profiles, upload introductory videos, and set lesson rates. Potential students browse profiles and book lessons directly through the system. LessonFace takes a 15% commission on each lesson fee.

Pros

  • User-friendly interface for both teachers and students.
  • No subscription fees — you earn based on lessons given.
  • Great for teachers at all experience levels.

Cons

  • Commission fee reduces overall earnings.
  • Some competition from a large number of tutors.
  • Income can be inconsistent at the start.

Tips for Success on LessonFace

  • Upload a professional, engaging intro video showcasing your teaching style.
  • Specialize in popular instruments or skills (e.g., beginner guitar, music theory) to attract more students.
  • Encourage student reviews and ratings to build trust.

2. TakeLessons

TakeLessons stands out with its massive user base and broad spectrum of learning categories, including music, singing, and songwriting.

Key Features

  • Diverse Subjects: Not limited to music; teachers can also offer language, arts, and lifestyle lessons.
  • Marketing Support: TakeLessons promotes profiles through their website and partners, helping you attract students.
  • Flexible Formats: Offers online, in-person, or blended lessons.
  • Mobile App: Streamlines connecting and teaching on-the-go.

How TakeLessons Benefits Music Teachers

TakeLessons offers a tried-and-true marketplace for music teachers to find students without heavy marketing effort. The platform handles lesson billing and scheduling, which saves time and reduces administrative headaches.

Pros

  • Access to a wide student pool actively seeking lessons.
  • Responsive customer support.
  • Built-in tools for managing bookings and payments.

Cons

  • Commission fees vary between 20-30%.
  • Limited pricing flexibility — lesson prices must fall within set ranges.
  • Some teachers report delays in payouts.

Maximizing Your Income on TakeLessons

  • Craft detailed and informative profiles that highlight your experience, credentials, and achievements.
  • Offer package deals or trial lessons to entice beginners and casual learners.
  • Stay active on the platform and respond quickly to inquiries to boost your visibility.

3. Preply

Preply is a booming global platform that connects tutors to learners with a focus on one-on-one sessions in numerous subjects, including music instruction.

What Makes Preply Special?

  • Global Reach: Students search tutors by language, price, and availability.
  • Customizable Lessons: Tutors have freedom to adapt curricula according to individual student goals.
  • User Ratings System: Building a positive profile is key to attracting more students.
  • Secure Payments: Preply manages transactions and protects tutors and students alike.

How Music Tutors Can Thrive on Preply

Music educators can build a dedicated student base by emphasizing personalized lesson plans and progress tracking. The platform appeals both to casual hobbyists and serious learners aspiring to improve technique or prepare for exams.

Pros

  • Efficient platform with a strong reputation.
  • Tutors set their own hourly rates starting at $10.
  • Wide range of student demographics.

Cons

  • Takes a commission starting at 33% for new tutors, decreasing with higher lesson volume.
  • Platform rules on communication can be limiting.
  • Requires consistent activity to rank higher in search results.

Growth Strategies for Tutors on Preply

  • Offer trial lessons discounted or even free to showcase your teaching abilities.
  • Gather and display glowing student testimonials.
  • Continuously update your profile with video introductions and sample lesson plans.

4. Wyzant

Wyzant is a well-established tutoring marketplace that includes music lessons among its many subjects.

Why Wyzant Works for Music Teachers

  • Local and Online Options: Teachers can offer face-to-face or online lessons.
  • Teacher-Set Rates: Tutors determine their own pricing with no upper or lower limits.
  • Robust Scheduling Tools: Calendar integration and messaging features streamline communications.
  • No Upfront Costs: Teachers only pay a commission when a lesson is successfully booked.

Features Helping Music Educators Succeed

Wyzant’s forte is its flexibility and autonomy. Instructors control their availability, lesson prices, curriculum, and teaching style. This level of control allows for tailoring lessons to specific niches like classical piano, music theory, or jazz improvisation.

Pros

  • Strong focus on tutor quality and discipline-based filtering.
  • Ability to build long-term relationships with students for recurring income.
  • Wide range of subjects translates to potential cross-promotion opportunities.

Cons

  • Commission structure can be steep (25% initially, lessens over time).
  • Competition from many tutors, especially in popular instruments.
  • Marketing efforts rely heavily on tutor self-promotion for visibility.

Insider Tips for Wyzant

  • Develop a niche expertise and highlight your credentials prominently.
  • Request detailed feedback and reviews after lessons.
  • Use the platform’s promotional tools and keep profile information current.

5. Fiverr

Fiverr is slightly different from the other platforms as it revolves around gig-based services, but it offers plenty of scope for music teachers to monetize their skills creatively.

How Fiverr Fits into Music Teaching

Teachers create “gigs” for specific lesson types or musical services—such as 30-minute beginner guitar lessons, personalized vocal coaching, or music composition tutorials. Buyers browse gigs and place orders directly.

Advantages of Using Fiverr

  • Gig Creation Flexibility: Offer anything from quick tips to comprehensive lesson packages.
  • Global Exposure: Access millions of buyers across diverse categories.
  • Upselling Options: Add extras to your gigs, including extended lesson times or additional materials.

Downsides

  • Significant competition can drive prices down.
  • Initial visibility can be challenging without strong reviews.
  • Managing a high volume of small gigs can impact work-life balance.

Making the Most Out of Fiverr

  • Design clear, attractive gig descriptions and compelling video introductions.
  • Set competitive prices initially and raise rates gradually as reviews accumulate.
  • Use buyer requests feature to find and pitch directly to potential students.

Conclusion: Choosing the Right Platform for Your Music Teaching Career

Each platform offers distinct strengths and challenges, so choosing the right one depends on your teaching style, schedule flexibility, and income goals. LessonFace and TakeLessons shine for live video lessons with integrated tools and student traffic. Preply and Wyzant allow for high autonomy and direct student engagement, with Wyzant especially suited for those valuing tutoring control. Fiverr offers an innovative, gig-based approach attractive for instructors looking to package distinct services creatively.

Final Pointers

  • Build a professional online presence with well-crafted profiles and video intros.
  • Engage with students actively and encourage feedback to build reputation.
  • Consider diversifying across two or more platforms to maximize income.

Teaching music online can be deeply rewarding and financially viable. By leveraging these top platforms thoughtfully, passionate musicians can transform their expertise into thriving educational businesses, broadening their reach well beyond their local communities.

Your journey in music education awaits — all you need is to pick the platform that resonates with your goals and start sharing your musical talents with the world!
