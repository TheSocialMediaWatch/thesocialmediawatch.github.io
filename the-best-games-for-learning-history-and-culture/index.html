The Best Games for Learning History and Culture


In an age where traditional educational methods often struggle to engage students, the integration of gaming into learning has emerged as a powerful tool. Games that focus on history and culture provide an immersive experience, bridging the gap between entertainment and education. Here, we explore some of the best games that effectively teach history and culture, offering players a chance to learn while having fun.

1. Assassin’s Creed: Origins

Set in Ancient Egypt during the Ptolemaic period, Assassin’s Creed: Origins is not just a game about stealth and combat; it is a rich tapestry of historical events and cultural intricacies. Players explore a beautifully recreated version of ancient Alexandria, where they can interact with historical figures like Cleopatra and Julius Caesar. The game includes an educational mode called “Discovery Tour,” which allows players to roam freely and learn about the history, architecture, and daily life of Ancient Egypt without the pressure of combat.

2. Civilization VI

The Civilization series has long been heralded for its educational value, and Civilization VI is no exception. This turn-based strategy game challenges players to build and expand their civilizations from the Stone Age to the Information Age. Players engage with historical leaders, develop technologies, and make decisions that affect the course of their civilization. The game prompts players to think critically about cultural development, diplomacy, and the impact of war on society. Each civilization comes with unique traits and units, providing insight into their historical context.

3. Oregon Trail

A classic educational game, Oregon Trail has been teaching players about the westward expansion of the United States since the 1970s. Players assume the role of a wagon leader, making decisions about supplies, route choices, and managing the health of their party as they journey across the American frontier. The game presents real historical challenges faced by pioneers, such as disease, weather, and resource management, making it an engaging way to learn about this pivotal time in American history.

4. Total War Series

The Total War series offers a unique blend of turn-based strategy and real-time tactics, immersing players in various historical periods. From the Roman Empire in Total War: Rome II to feudal Japan in Total War: Shogun 2, players can experience significant historical events and military campaigns. The games encourage players to understand the complexities of politics, warfare, and culture in different eras. Additionally, the series often includes detailed historical context and lore, enriching the player’s understanding of the time period.

5. Age of Empires II: Definitive Edition

A beloved classic, Age of Empires II has been updated for modern gamers while retaining its educational charm. Players can choose from various civilizations and guide them through history, from the Dark Ages to the Renaissance. The game emphasizes resource management, strategic planning, and military tactics. Each civilization is rooted in historical accuracy, allowing players to learn about their unique contributions to the world.

6. Rome: Total War

Rome: Total War is an iconic strategy game that allows players to control one of the great powers of the ancient world. The game combines turn-based strategy with real-time battles, giving players the chance to experience the political and military challenges of the Roman Empire. Players can learn about key historical figures, including Julius Caesar and Hannibal, while managing their empire’s economy, diplomacy, and military conquests. The game’s depth and complexity make it an insightful tool for understanding Roman history and warfare.

7. Hearts of Iron IV

For those interested in World War II, Hearts of Iron IV offers a deep and complex simulation of the global conflict. Players can take control of any nation during this tumultuous period and guide them through political, military, and economic challenges. The game emphasizes historical accuracy, allowing players to explore the intricacies of war strategies and the impact of different leaders. The detailed mechanics of diplomacy, espionage, and warfare provide a unique perspective on the cultural and historical context of the era.

8. Never Alone (Kisima Ingitchuna)

Never Alone is a platformer that tells the story of a young Iñupiat girl and her arctic fox companion. Based on the folklore of Alaska Native culture, the game offers a rich narrative and stunning visuals. Players learn about the Iñupiat way of life, their traditions, and the challenges they face in a changing environment. Throughout the game, players can unlock cultural insights and videos that provide deeper context about the Iñupiat people, making it an engaging way to learn about indigenous cultures.

9. Spelunky

While Spelunky may not seem like an obvious choice for learning about history and culture, its incorporation of ancient temples and artifacts provides a unique educational experience. Players navigate through procedurally generated caves filled with traps, enemies, and treasures inspired by various cultures. As players encounter relics and artwork, they can learn about the cultures and histories from which these elements are drawn. The game’s emphasis on exploration and discovery encourages curiosity about different historical contexts.

10. The Oregon Trail: American Settler

A modern take on the classic Oregon Trail, this game allows players to build and manage a frontier town. While it retains the core elements of resource management and decision-making, it also incorporates cultural aspects of the time, such as trade with Native Americans and the challenges of pioneer life. The game serves as a fun way to engage with historical narratives while promoting critical thinking about the consequences of settling and expansion in America.

11. Civilization: Beyond Earth

Civilization: Beyond Earth takes players into the future, exploring how humanity might colonize alien planets. While not strictly historical, it encourages players to think about the cultural and ethical implications of exploration and colonization. The game challenges players to create new societies, merging historical themes with futuristic concepts. It promotes an understanding of how culture evolves and adapts in response to new environments and challenges.

12. Kerbal Space Program

While primarily a space flight simulation game, Kerbal Space Program introduces players to the principles of rocket science and space exploration. Players design and launch spacecraft, learning about physics, engineering, and the history of space travel. The game emphasizes the importance of scientific discovery and innovation, encouraging players to appreciate the cultural impact of space exploration on society.

13. Vikings: War of Clans

This strategy game allows players to immerse themselves in the Viking Age, exploring the culture and history of Norse civilizations. Players build their own settlements, engage in battles, and forge alliances while learning about Viking mythology and traditions. The game encourages players to understand the societal structure of Viking culture and the significance of trade, exploration, and warfare in their history.

14. The Banner Saga

The Banner Saga is a beautifully crafted RPG that draws heavily from Norse mythology and Viking culture. Players navigate a world inspired by the struggles of a civilization facing extinction, making choices that affect the course of the story. The game’s art style, narrative depth, and cultural references provide a rich learning experience about the values and beliefs of ancient Norse societies.

15. This War of Mine

This War of Mine offers a unique perspective on war, focusing on the civilian experience during conflict. Set in a besieged city, players must manage resources and make moral choices to survive. The game portrays the impact of war on culture, community, and individual lives, encouraging players to reflect on the human cost of conflict. This approach to storytelling provides a powerful lens through which to understand the complexities of war and its effects on society.

Conclusion

The intersection of gaming, history, and culture creates a dynamic educational landscape that encourages engagement and critical thinking. From ancient civilizations to modern conflicts, the games listed above offer players a unique opportunity to explore historical events and cultural contexts in an interactive and immersive way. These games not only entertain but also inform, making them valuable tools for learners of all ages. By embracing the potential of gaming as an educational medium, we can foster a deeper understanding of our shared history and culture. So, whether you’re a student, a history buff, or simply curious about the world around you, these games can provide a fun and informative journey through time.
