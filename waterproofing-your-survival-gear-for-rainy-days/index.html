Waterproofing Your Survival Gear for Rainy Days


When you’re out in the wilderness or preparing for emergency situations, having reliable gear can make all the difference. One factor that can turn your survival tools into a life-saving asset—or a burdensome liability—is how well they perform under wet conditions. Whether you’re hiking through a downpour, caught in an unexpected storm, or navigating through a flood-prone area, knowing how to waterproof your survival gear is crucial. In this guide, we’ll explore various methods to protect your gear from rain, ensuring that you stay dry, safe, and prepared for anything nature throws your way.

Why Waterproofing Matters

Survival gear serves a variety of purposes, from keeping you warm and dry to ensuring that you can navigate, build shelter, and sustain yourself. But no matter how well-made your tools are, if they get soaked, their effectiveness will be compromised. Electronics can short-circuit, clothing can lose its insulation properties, and food can spoil faster. In extreme cases, the failure of critical gear like fire starters, medical kits, or communication devices can be disastrous.

Waterproofing your gear is not just a precaution; it’s a necessary step to ensure that your equipment remains functional when you need it the most. Proper waterproofing extends the lifespan of your gear, prevents rust and mold, and ensures you have what you need to survive the elements.

Assessing Your Survival Gear for Waterproofing Needs

The first step in waterproofing your gear is identifying which items are most vulnerable to water damage. Here’s a quick breakdown of what you should focus on:

1. Clothing and Footwear

Clothing is essential for maintaining body temperature, but wet clothing can quickly lower your core temperature and lead to hypothermia. Similarly, wet feet are susceptible to blisters and fungal infections. Ensuring that your clothing and footwear are waterproofed can keep you comfortable and safe.

2. Shelter and Sleeping Bags

A waterproof shelter is crucial in rainy or snowy conditions. If your tent or tarp isn’t properly sealed, you could be in for a long, uncomfortable night. Additionally, a damp sleeping bag is ineffective at retaining warmth.

3. Backpacks and Bags

You likely carry all of your gear in a backpack or similar bag. If the bag is not waterproof, everything inside it can get wet. This includes important items like first aid kits, fire starters, and food.

4. Electronics

Modern survivalists often carry electronics like GPS units, radios, or smartphones. These items are not designed to be exposed to water and can quickly be damaged if they aren’t properly waterproofed.

5. Tools and Equipment

Tools like knives, multitools, and other metal implements can rust if exposed to moisture for extended periods. Water-resistant coatings or waterproofing sprays can prevent corrosion.

Waterproofing Methods for Different Gear

Now that you know which items are most at risk, it’s time to get into the specifics of waterproofing your gear. Below, we’ll break down the best methods to keep your survival equipment dry.

1. Waterproofing Clothing and Footwear

Clothing
When it comes to clothing, layering is essential. Begin with moisture-wicking base layers that will keep sweat off your skin. Over that, you’ll need a waterproof outer layer, such as a rain jacket, pants, or poncho. However, even the best waterproof clothing can lose its effectiveness over time, especially if it’s not treated properly.

To enhance the waterproofing of your clothing, consider applying a durable water repellent (DWR) treatment. This treatment can be sprayed or washed into the fabric and will cause water to bead off, rather than soaking in. Ensure that you also treat areas that tend to be most exposed to rain, such as the shoulders and lower legs.

Footwear
Keeping your feet dry is just as important as keeping your body dry. Waterproof boots or shoes are a must for any survivalist. However, even waterproof boots can leak if they’re not properly maintained.

To waterproof your footwear, apply a waterproofing spray or wax. Pay attention to seams and stitching, as these are common entry points for water. Additionally, use gaiters to cover the tops of your boots and prevent rain or snow from entering.

2. Waterproofing Shelters and Sleeping Bags

Tents and Tarps
Your shelter needs to be completely waterproof to keep you dry during rainy conditions. Most tents come with a waterproof coating, but after repeated use, this coating can wear off. Reapply a waterproofing treatment to the fabric to restore its effectiveness.

If your tent or tarp has seams, consider seam-sealing them. Seams are common places for water to seep in, so applying a waterproof seam sealer can keep water from entering the shelter.

Sleeping Bags
A wet sleeping bag is a quick way to ruin your night in the wilderness. To prevent this, consider using a waterproof sleeping bag liner. These liners not only keep water from seeping into the bag but also provide an additional layer of insulation.

For extra protection, store your sleeping bag in a waterproof compression sack. This will protect it from the rain and ensure that it stays dry until you need it.

3. Waterproofing Backpacks and Bags

Backpacks are one of the most critical pieces of gear when it comes to waterproofing. If your bag is not waterproof, everything inside it is at risk.

Waterproof Bags
The simplest solution is to invest in a waterproof backpack. These bags are made from materials like nylon or PVC and are fully sealed to prevent water from entering. However, waterproof bags tend to be more expensive.

Waterproof Covers
If you don’t want to invest in an entirely new bag, you can use a waterproof cover. These covers are designed to fit over your backpack and provide a barrier against rain. Look for one that fits snugly around the bag and is made of durable, waterproof material.

Dry Bags
For items that are particularly sensitive to moisture, use dry bags. These are completely waterproof and are ideal for storing electronics, first aid kits, or other essential gear. Dry bags come in a range of sizes, so you can choose one that fits your needs.

4. Waterproofing Electronics

Electronics are among the most vulnerable items in your survival gear. A waterproof case or pouch is a must if you’re carrying anything like a GPS, radio, or smartphone.

Waterproof Cases and Pouches
Invest in waterproof cases or pouches specifically designed for electronics. These cases will not only protect your device from rain but will also keep it safe from dust, dirt, and other elements.

Plastic Bags
In a pinch, a simple plastic bag can work to protect your electronics. Ensure that the bag is sealed tightly and that there’s no room for water to get in. While this may not be as durable as a dedicated waterproof case, it can be a temporary solution.

5. Waterproofing Tools and Equipment

Metal tools can rust quickly when exposed to moisture, so it’s important to keep them dry. The best way to do this is by applying a thin layer of oil to the metal parts of your tools. This will create a barrier that prevents moisture from reaching the metal and causing rust.

For added protection, store your tools in waterproof cases or bags. This will not only keep them dry but also ensure that they don’t get damaged by other items in your pack.

Additional Tips for Waterproofing Your Survival Gear

  • Store gear properly: When not in use, store your gear in a dry place. This will prevent unnecessary exposure to moisture and prolong the life of your equipment.
  • Regular maintenance: Even after waterproofing your gear, it’s important to check it regularly. Reapply waterproofing treatments as needed, and ensure that all seams and zippers are in good condition.
  • Test your gear: Before heading into the wilderness, test your waterproofed gear in controlled conditions. This will give you confidence that everything will stay dry when you need it most.

Conclusion

Waterproofing your survival gear is an essential step in ensuring that you’re always prepared for any situation. Whether you’re caught in a storm, traversing a rainy trail, or dealing with floodwaters, knowing that your gear can withstand the elements will give you peace of mind. By applying the right treatments and using waterproof bags, cases, and covers, you can protect your gear and ensure that it remains functional when you need it most. Remember, in survival situations, every detail counts, and staying dry is one of the most important aspects of staying alive.
