Best Outdoor Hiking Destinations in the World


Hiking is an exhilarating experience that connects us with the natural world in a way few other activities can. From towering mountain ranges to expansive forests, lush valleys to barren deserts, the variety of landscapes makes outdoor hiking an incredibly diverse and rewarding activity. Whether you’re a seasoned adventurer or someone who enjoys a casual walk in nature, the world offers some of the most spectacular hiking trails that will leave you awe-struck. Below is a list of the best outdoor hiking destinations that should be on every nature lover’s bucket list.

1. The Inca Trail, Peru

The Inca Trail is arguably one of the most famous and sought-after hikes in the world. Located in the Andes Mountains of Peru, it offers a unique blend of history, culture, and breathtaking natural beauty. The trail takes you through lush rainforests, high-altitude deserts, and ancient Inca ruins, eventually leading to the iconic Machu Picchu.

This 4-day trek is an unforgettable experience, offering a combination of challenging terrain and scenic views. Along the way, you’ll pass through cloud forests, experience the rich history of the Inca civilization, and be treated to panoramic views of the Andes. The final destination, Machu Picchu, is one of the Seven Wonders of the World and a fitting reward for completing the journey.

Key Highlights:

  • Historical ruins and landmarks
  • Diverse landscapes, including cloud forests and alpine tundra
  • View of Machu Picchu at sunrise

2. Torres del Paine National Park, Chile

For those who enjoy rugged wilderness, the Torres del Paine National Park in southern Chile offers one of the most awe-inspiring hikes on the planet. Known for its dramatic granite spires, glaciers, lakes, and diverse wildlife, this destination is a paradise for nature enthusiasts.

The W Trek, one of the most popular routes within the park, takes hikers through the heart of Torres del Paine, offering spectacular views of the park’s signature granite towers, massive glaciers, and turquoise lakes. With its pristine environment and stunning vistas, the park promises an unforgettable hiking experience.

Key Highlights:

  • Towering granite peaks
  • Beautiful turquoise lakes
  • Diverse wildlife including guanacos, foxes, and condors

3. Mount Everest Base Camp, Nepal

For many, hiking to the base camp of the world’s tallest mountain is the ultimate challenge. The Everest Base Camp trek is not only physically demanding but also rich in cultural experiences. As you ascend the rugged terrain of the Himalayas, you’ll encounter ancient monasteries, Sherpa villages, and an intimate view of life in the highest mountain range in the world.

The trek itself is a multi-day journey that takes you through breathtaking mountain scenery, including views of Everest, Lhotse, and Nuptse, all while immersing you in the unique Sherpa culture. Reaching Everest Base Camp is an achievement that many adventurers dream of for years.

Key Highlights:

  • Views of Mount Everest and surrounding peaks
  • Unique Sherpa culture and hospitality
  • A mix of challenging terrain and scenic mountain landscapes

4. The Dolomites, Italy

The Dolomites, located in northeastern Italy, are a UNESCO World Heritage site and offer some of the most stunning alpine hiking in Europe. Known for their dramatic, jagged peaks and beautiful valleys, these mountains are ideal for both casual hikers and experienced trekkers. The region’s well-marked trails offer something for everyone, from short day hikes to multi-day treks.

The Alta Via 1 is one of the most popular long-distance hikes, running across the Dolomites from north to south. The route takes you through some of the most breathtaking landscapes, including high-altitude plateaus, alpine meadows, and awe-inspiring cliffs. Along the way, you’ll also find quaint mountain huts where you can relax and enjoy some traditional Italian mountain cuisine.

Key Highlights:

  • Stunning alpine landscapes
  • UNESCO World Heritage site
  • Historic mountain huts offering local cuisine

5. The Appalachian Trail, United States

Stretching from Georgia to Maine, the Appalachian Trail is one of the longest continuous footpaths in the world, spanning over 2,190 miles. Whether you are hiking a short section or completing the entire trail, the Appalachian Trail offers a rich and diverse experience, showcasing the beauty of the eastern United States.

The trail passes through 14 states and offers a mix of terrain, from rugged mountains to serene woodlands. Along the way, hikers can enjoy peaceful streams, dense forests, and expansive views of the Appalachian Mountains. It’s a haven for wildlife enthusiasts as well, with opportunities to see deer, black bears, and a variety of bird species.

Key Highlights:

  • One of the longest hiking trails in the world
  • Beautiful forests, mountains, and streams
  • A range of difficulty levels for all types of hikers

6. Banff National Park, Canada

Banff National Park is a jewel of the Canadian Rockies and one of the most scenic places to hike in North America. Located in the province of Alberta, this park is home to turquoise lakes, towering peaks, glaciers, and abundant wildlife. Whether you’re hiking along the shores of Lake Louise, exploring the Icefields Parkway, or summiting one of the park’s many peaks, Banff offers endless hiking opportunities.

The Plain of Six Glaciers trail is a popular route that rewards hikers with views of glaciers and towering mountain peaks. The scenery in Banff is nothing short of spectacular, and the park’s well-maintained trails make it accessible for hikers of all levels.

Key Highlights:

  • Stunning mountain scenery and pristine lakes
  • Glaciers and abundant wildlife
  • Range of trails suitable for all experience levels

7. Zion National Park, United States

Zion National Park, located in southern Utah, is known for its striking red rock landscapes and dramatic canyon views. The park offers a wide range of hiking experiences, from easy walks to challenging scrambles. Zion is famous for its narrow slot canyons, towering sandstone cliffs, and unique rock formations.

One of the most popular hikes is Angels Landing, which is not for the faint of heart. This trail takes you to a high point with panoramic views of the canyon below. For those looking for a less challenging hike, the Riverside Walk along the Virgin River offers an easy path through Zion Canyon.

Key Highlights:

  • Striking red rock landscapes and canyons
  • Angels Landing, a thrilling hike with stunning views
  • Unique rock formations and slot canyons

8. The Swiss Alps, Switzerland

Switzerland’s Swiss Alps offer some of the best hiking in Europe, with a mix of challenging trails, alpine meadows, and charming mountain villages. Known for its precision trail networks and breathtaking scenery, the Swiss Alps attract hikers from around the world. Whether you’re strolling through the idyllic Zermatt area or tackling the Jungfrau region, you’ll be treated to unforgettable views of towering peaks and crystal-clear lakes.

One of the most famous hikes in the Swiss Alps is the Haute Route, a challenging trek that spans from Chamonix in France to Zermatt in Switzerland. This multi-day hike takes you through remote valleys, over glaciers, and provides some of the best alpine vistas in the world.

Key Highlights:

  • Breathtaking alpine views
  • Well-maintained trail networks
  • Charming mountain villages and high-altitude lakes

9. Fiordland National Park, New Zealand

Fiordland National Park in the southwest corner of New Zealand is home to some of the country’s most stunning hiking trails. Known for its dramatic fjords, towering cliffs, and lush rainforests, Fiordland offers an unforgettable experience for nature lovers.

The Milford Track is one of the most famous hikes in New Zealand and takes hikers through valleys, rainforests, and past crystal-clear rivers. The views of Mount Kepler, cascading waterfalls, and lush vegetation make this a must-do hike for anyone visiting New Zealand.

Key Highlights:

  • Majestic fjords and towering cliffs
  • Rich rainforest landscapes
  • Iconic trails like the Milford Track

10. The Overland Track, Australia

The Overland Track is one of Australia’s premier hiking trails, located in the UNESCO World Heritage-listed Cradle Mountain-Lake St Clair National Park. The trail stretches for 40 miles and takes hikers through diverse landscapes, including temperate rainforests, alpine meadows, and rugged mountains. Along the way, you’ll be treated to stunning views of Cradle Mountain, ancient forests, and crystal-clear lakes.

The track can be completed in six days, with well-marked trails and camping spots along the way. The Overland Track is a fantastic way to experience the wild beauty of Tasmania and is a favorite among serious hikers.

Key Highlights:

  • Diverse landscapes, from alpine meadows to rainforests
  • Views of Cradle Mountain and Lake St Clair
  • Well-maintained trail with camping spots

Conclusion

The world is full of remarkable hiking destinations, each offering unique landscapes, challenges, and rewards. Whether you’re hiking in the snow-covered peaks of the Swiss Alps, exploring the rugged terrain of Torres del Paine, or walking the ancient paths of the Inca Trail, each hike promises a memorable adventure. Hiking allows us to connect with nature, push our limits, and witness the beauty of the planet in its most raw and untouched form.

So, if you’re looking for your next great adventure, consider one of these top outdoor hiking destinations. Whether you’re an experienced mountaineer or a casual day hiker, these trails offer something for everyone and will undoubtedly leave you with lifelong memories. Happy hiking!
