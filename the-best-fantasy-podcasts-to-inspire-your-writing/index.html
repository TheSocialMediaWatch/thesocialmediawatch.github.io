The Best Fantasy Podcasts to Inspire Your Writing


Writing fantasy is an art, a craft that demands not only imagination but also a nuanced understanding of how to build immersive worlds, create compelling characters, and weave stories that captivate readers. For writers seeking fresh inspiration or a new way to enhance their craft, diving into fantasy podcasts can be a goldmine of creativity and knowledge. These auditory journeys not only entertain but offer lessons in storytelling, world-building, and character development that can be invaluable for any fantasy author.

Why Fantasy Podcasts Are Essential for Writers

Podcasts uniquely blend storytelling with the power of sound and voice acting, creating an immersive experience that can spark ideas and motivate writers. Listening to different styles of narration, experimentations with pacing, and diverse thematic explorations helps writers understand what works and how to adapt or innovate their own writing. Moreover, many fantasy podcasts include interviews with authors, discussions of genre tropes, and writing tips that provide practical advice alongside inspiration.

Below, you’ll find a curated selection of some of the best fantasy podcasts designed to inspire writers, whether you are crafting your first novel or looking to breathe new life into an ongoing series.

1. Imaginary Worlds

Hosted by Eric Molinsky, Imaginary Worlds is a deep-dive podcast that explores everything from fantasy to science fiction by analyzing how speculative worlds are constructed in popular culture. The show takes a thoughtful look at storytelling techniques, myth-making, and genre conventions, making it prime listening for fantasy writers seeking to understand the mechanics behind world-building.

Each episode dissects elements like magic systems, folklore inspirations, and narrative structures, blending interviews with creators and insightful commentary. The analytic nature of Imaginary Worlds can help writers uncover subtle layers within fantasy storytelling, encouraging them to think critically about the worlds they create and how their stories resonate on multiple levels.

Why writers love it: It offers a balance of interview, analysis, and storytelling, perfect for writers wanting to enrich their understanding of speculative fiction.

2. Writing Excuses

If you’re looking for a podcast that mixes writing advice with short bursts of witty banter, Writing Excuses fits the bill perfectly. Hosted by authors Brandon Sanderson, Mary Robinette Kowal, Howard Tayler, and Dan Wells, this show breaks down the craft of writing in fast-paced, 15–20 minute episodes. There’s a special focus on speculative fiction, including fantasy.

The hosts cover topics ranging from character motivations and plot development to pacing and series continuity. They also invite guests from the writing community, providing a broad spectrum of perspectives. For fantasy writers, Sanderson’s insights into magic systems and world-building are especially valuable.

Why writers love it: The concise, high-energy episodes make learning about writing manageable and fun, perfect for busy writers seeking practical advice.

3. LeVar Burton Reads

While not exclusive to fantasy, LeVar Burton Reads is an exceptional podcast for fantasy writers seeking narrative inspiration. Each episode features LeVar Burton reading a carefully selected short story, many of which delve into speculative and fantastical themes.

Listening to masterful storytelling in this format allows writers to experience varied voices and styles, learning pacing, tone, and descriptive techniques simply through appreciation. The diversity of stories provides a rich harvest of ideas and narrative approaches, which can help writers break free from creative ruts.

Why writers love it: Exposure to high-quality short fiction helps develop an ear for rhythm and style that can elevate one’s own writing.

4. The Dungeon Master’s Block

For fantasy authors who focus on intricate world-building and magic systems, The Dungeon Master’s Block provides a treasure trove of content. Although aimed primarily at tabletop role-playing game enthusiasts, the show’s focus on storytelling, character archetypes, and narrative diversity has huge crossover value for writers.

The hosts discuss how to craft compelling villains, create believable magic constraints, design imaginative settings, and tackle common storytelling challenges—all essentials for fantasy narratives. The conversational format lends a sense of community and shared questing, mirroring the collaborative nature of storytelling.

Why writers love it: It’s a practical, approachable resource for learning expansive storytelling techniques applicable in fantasy novels.

5. Sword & Laser

For those interested in the broader fantasy community and literary trends, Sword & Laser offers book reviews, author interviews, and lively discussions about fantasy and science fiction literature. Hosted by Veronica Belmont and Tom Merritt, the podcast delves into genre news, spotlighting emerging authors and trends.

This podcast is a great way to keep a pulse on what the fantasy reading community is excited about, which can in turn influence a writer’s understanding of the market and reader expectations. Interviews with bestselling authors give behind-the-scenes glimpses into writing processes and publishing experiences.

Why writers love it: Staying connected to the fantasy readership and publishing landscape helps writers write stories that resonate and find an audience.

6. Hello from the Magic Tavern

If you’re looking for a fantasy podcast that delivers purely on imaginative storytelling and humor, Hello from the Magic Tavern is a must-listen. It’s a serialized improv comedy podcast set in a bizarre, fictional fantasy world, blending absurd humor with sharp dialogue.

Writers can benefit from hearing how spontaneity fuels storytelling, how character voice shapes narrative, and how humor can be intertwined with fantasy elements. The series inspires creative risk-taking and serves as a reminder that fantasy doesn’t always have to be serious—it can be playful and experimental.

Why writers love it: Encourages improvisation and creativity while highlighting unique character dynamics and world-building through humor.

7. Word Burglar

Word Burglar offers a mix of fantasy storytelling and meta commentary on storytelling itself. The host, a Canadian author, benefits from a blend of humor, nerd culture, and insightful discussions focused on both writing and enjoying speculative fiction.

Episodes discuss narrative methods, obstacles for writers, and the cultural impact of fantasy stories. It’s an inspiring listen for those who love witty introspection and commentary alongside fantasy narration.

Why writers love it: Unites humor and intellectual consideration about genre storytelling, fostering new ways to approach fantasy writing.

8. The Fantasy Fiction Podcast

Dedicated specifically to new fantasy short stories, The Fantasy Fiction Podcast is a brilliant resource for writers. Focused on delivering fresh, original stories with professional narration, it offers an intimate look at how fantasy narratives can vary in tone, theme, and structure.

Exposure to a wide range of voices, story structures, and imaginative ideas helps writers see possibilities beyond their usual approach. Paying attention to how stories hook listeners and build suspense within a short time frame can directly influence novel pacing and plot structure.

Why writers love it: It’s a continuous source of fresh ideas and narrative experimentation, perfect for creative rejuvenation.

How to Use These Podcasts to Enhance Your Fantasy Writing

To maximize the benefits of these podcasts, consider these approaches:

  • Note-taking: Jot down interesting storytelling techniques, world-building ideas, or phrases that spark your creativity.
  • Practice inspired writing: After listening to a story or discussion, try writing a short scene inspired by the themes or styles you heard.
  • Analyze storytelling: Pay attention to how pacing, tone, and character development are handled and think about how to apply those lessons in your own work.
  • Mix learning with entertainment: Use podcasts during daily commutes or downtime to keep your creativity engaged even when not actively writing.

Final Thoughts

Fantasy podcasts offer a rich, varied resource for writers at any stage of their journey. Whether you’re seeking practical advice on craft, fresh narrative ideas, or just want to immerse yourself in fantastic worlds, these podcasts deliver inspiration directly to your ears. By integrating listening into your creative routine, you can cultivate new perspectives, sharpen your storytelling skills, and fuel the imagination necessary to bring your fantasy worlds to life.

Exploring these podcasts is more than just entertainment; it’s an investment in your craft and your creative growth. So plug in, listen deeply, and let the best fantasy podcasts light the way to your next great story.
