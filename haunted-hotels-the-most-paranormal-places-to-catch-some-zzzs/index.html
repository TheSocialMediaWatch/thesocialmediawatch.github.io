Haunted Hotels: The Most Paranormal Places to Catch Some Zzz's!


Are you an adrenaline junkie with a penchant for hair-raising experiences? Or perhaps you’re a ghost-loving thrill-seeker who isn’t afraid to spend a night in some of the most rumored-to-be-paranormal hotels in the world? Well, pull up a chair and prepare to become enthralled with our list of the most eerie, hair-raising, and otherworldly stays that will have your spine tingling all night long.

Lewiswood Rd Bed & Breakfast, Ontario, Canada

This hauntingly beautiful bed & breakfast sits nestled in the lush greenery of Ontario, Canada. Perched on the edge of a cliff overlooking Georgian Bay, Lewiswood’s dark history and countless chilling stories make it a popular spot for paranormal enthusiasts.

The Myrtles Plantation, St. Francisville, Louisiana, USA

If Southern charm is your cup of tea, The Myrtles might just conjure up a taste of that sweet tea along with a ghostly chiller. This 200-year-old Antebellum mansion in Louisiana has no shortage of supernatural lore. As the most haunted house in the South, it bristles with some of the supposedly leftover spirits of multiple murders that have taken place on its grounds over the years.

One chilling tale of what has come to be known as the ‘Dark Room’ story: A family patriarch had his mistresses secretly living in a ‘dark’ room on the property, passing off the women as servants. When a carriage driver inadvertently discovered their secret, the driver was poisoned, and his body was hurled into the Mississippi River. It’s said that there are a few restless, vengeful souls lurking in this storied abode, making for quite the spine-tingling sleepover.

The Stanley Hotel, Colorado, USA

Riding upon the back of palpable history, this sensational hotel in Colorado is steeped in enigmatic occurrences. The beloved lodging is rumored to be the secret home to myriad ghostly inhabitants. The most notable of these spirits is called “Alice," an elderly lady believed to be Elizabeth Wilson, an employee of the original Stanley Steamers company, who passed away in the hotel in 1910.

But its spookiest moment came when Stephen King stayed there and was inspired to write his magnum opus, “The Shining.” In the book, a psychic, “Alice,” helps to warn the protagonist’s wife about the malevolent forces that reside there. So, if you’re brave enough, spend a night at the hotel and perhaps you’ll have a ghostly encounter of your own.

Monte Carlo Inn - Airport, Mississauga, Ontario, Canada

This unexpected haunted gem might just be Canada’s “little secret”. Paranormal experts and reporters sometimes take refuge at the Monte Carlo Inn near Toronto’s Pearson International Airport. The hotel is rumored to have a spirit that likes to play pranks on unsuspecting guests. From an invisible visitor rearranging furniture at 3 a.m. to unexplained knocking in the night, you’ll find yourselves wishing for a pillow and blanket to hide beneath.

What Makes Haunted Hotels So Alluring?

For many, the allure of these chilling stories lies in the fact that these hotels may house such profound, albeit sorrowful, tales of love, betrayal, and drama from the past. Perhaps there’s something alluring and even therapeutic about crossing paths with those otherworldy spirits who have grappled with their own tribulations and trials. After all, we’re all just humans trying to find our place on this strange, mortal coil.

The next time you’re planning a vacation or road trip, perhaps you’ll find it beneficial to seduce your wanderlust with a dash of adrenaline, vivid entertainment, and a few ghost stories along the way. With these paranormal hotels in your itinerary, the memories you’ll make—including those spine-chilling shivers that keep you awake at night—will undoubtedly last a lifetime.
