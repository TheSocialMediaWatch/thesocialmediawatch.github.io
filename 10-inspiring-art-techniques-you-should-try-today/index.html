10 Inspiring Art Techniques You Should Try Today


Whether you are a seasoned artist or a beginner looking to explore new creative avenues, trying different art techniques can help you discover your unique style and enhance your artistic skills. Each technique provides opportunities for self-expression and experimentation, pushing the boundaries of what you can create. In this post, we’ll explore 10 inspiring art techniques you should try today to take your artwork to the next level.

1. Watercolor Painting with Salt Texture

Watercolor painting is one of the most versatile and mesmerizing techniques. By adding salt to a wet watercolor painting, you can create unique textures that are rich in depth. This technique is ideal for landscapes, seascapes, or abstract art, as the salt creates spontaneous, beautiful patterns.

How to do it:

  1. Paint a wet surface with watercolor.
  2. While the paint is still wet, sprinkle salt (coarse or fine) onto the surface.
  3. Watch as the salt absorbs the pigment, creating fascinating textures and patterns.
  4. Allow the painting to dry fully before removing the salt.

Experiment with different amounts of water and salt to see how the texture changes, and feel free to combine this technique with other watercolor techniques for even more dynamic results.

2. Acrylic Pouring

Acrylic pouring is a fun and fluid art technique that has gained a lot of popularity in recent years. It involves mixing acrylic paints with a pouring medium and then allowing the paint to flow over the canvas. The result is abstract art with bold colors and stunning patterns.

How to do it:

  1. Mix your acrylic paints with a pouring medium (available at most art stores).
  2. Pour the mixed paint onto the canvas using various methods such as dirty pouring, flip cup, or swipe techniques.
  3. Tilt the canvas to help the paint spread naturally and create interesting effects.
  4. Let the paint dry, and once it’s completely dry, you’ll be left with an eye-catching abstract piece.

The beauty of acrylic pouring is that no two pieces are ever the same, and it offers endless possibilities for creative exploration.

3. Collage Art

Collage is a technique where different materials—such as paper, fabric, photographs, and other found objects—are layered and glued onto a surface to create a new composition. This technique allows artists to combine elements from the past with the present, making it a fantastic way to experiment with storytelling and visual expression.

How to do it:

  1. Collect a variety of materials, such as magazines, newspapers, fabric, old books, or photographs.
  2. Cut or tear these materials into shapes or pieces you find interesting.
  3. Arrange them on a surface (canvas, wood panel, or paper) in an interesting composition.
  4. Use glue to adhere the materials and create texture and layers.
  5. Add paint, ink, or other mediums to complete the piece.

Collage art encourages creativity by allowing you to explore different textures, patterns, and contrasts in your work.

4. Ink Blot Art

Ink blot art, often associated with the Rorschach test, involves creating symmetrical designs by folding paper with ink splatters. This technique is perfect for those who love spontaneous, abstract art and enjoy exploring their subconscious mind through shapes and patterns.

How to do it:

  1. Drop ink or watercolor paint onto one side of a piece of paper.
  2. Fold the paper in half, pressing gently to spread the ink across the surface.
  3. Open the paper to reveal a mirrored, symmetrical design.
  4. Use fine-tipped markers, pens, or additional ink to enhance or add details to the design.

Ink blot art is all about embracing the randomness and unpredictability of the technique, and it can be a fun way to explore your creative intuition.

5. Sgraffito Technique

Sgraffito is a technique used primarily in painting, ceramics, and printmaking, where a top layer of paint or material is scratched or scraped to reveal a contrasting layer beneath. This technique is fantastic for adding texture and intricate details to your artwork.

How to do it:

  1. Apply a layer of paint or ink to your canvas or surface.
  2. Once the paint has dried slightly, use a sharp tool (such as a palette knife or a toothpick) to scratch off the top layer.
  3. Expose the contrasting layer underneath and create detailed textures or images.

Sgraffito is often used in combination with other techniques, such as oil painting, to create visual interest and depth.

6. Digital Painting

Digital painting involves using software like Photoshop, Procreate, or other digital tools to create artwork that mimics traditional painting techniques. This art form gives you the flexibility to explore styles, textures, and techniques that are nearly impossible with physical mediums.

How to do it:

  1. Choose your preferred digital painting software or app.
  2. Select your brushes and colors and start creating as you would on a traditional canvas.
  3. Experiment with layers, textures, and blending modes to add depth to your work.
  4. Take advantage of digital tools like undo/redo and the ability to manipulate elements easily to refine your composition.

Digital painting allows for endless creativity and opens up an entirely new world for artists. Whether you want to create hyper-realistic pieces or whimsical, fantastical art, the possibilities are endless.

7. Printmaking (Linocut or Woodcut)

Printmaking techniques like linocut and woodcut involve carving images into a surface (usually a linoleum block or a wooden block) and then printing the design onto paper. These techniques are popular for their ability to create multiple copies of the same artwork, making them great for creating art prints.

How to do it:

  1. Choose a linoleum or wooden block and sketch your design on it.
  2. Use carving tools to carefully carve away the areas of the block that will remain unprinted.
  3. Apply ink to the surface using a roller (brayer).
  4. Press the inked block onto paper to transfer your image.
  5. Repeat the process if you wish to make multiple prints.

Printmaking is an excellent way to explore bold lines, contrasts, and patterns in your artwork. It also offers a fantastic opportunity to experiment with repetition and series work.

8. Charcoal Drawing

Charcoal drawing is an age-old technique that allows you to create stunning works with rich contrasts and dramatic shading. Whether you’re sketching portraits, landscapes, or abstract pieces, charcoal’s deep blacks and velvety smooth textures provide an intense effect that can’t be achieved with other mediums.

How to do it:

  1. Use soft or compressed charcoal sticks or pencils to create your drawing.
  2. Start with light sketching and gradually build up darker values and shading.
  3. Use a blending stump or your fingers to smudge the charcoal for softer transitions.
  4. Erase parts of the drawing to create highlights or add texture.

Charcoal drawing is all about building up layers and playing with contrasts. The versatility of charcoal allows for a wide range of artistic expression, from detailed portraits to bold, expressive sketches.

9. Pointillism

Pointillism is a technique that involves creating an image using small dots or strokes of color rather than traditional brushstrokes. This method allows colors to blend optically when viewed from a distance, creating vibrant, dynamic effects.

How to do it:

  1. Choose your subject and begin by applying small, distinct dots of color onto the canvas.
  2. Use complementary or analogous colors to create visual interest.
  3. Focus on the arrangement of colors and their placement to build up your image.
  4. Step back often to see how the colors blend and take shape from a distance.

Pointillism can be a time-consuming technique, but the result is often mesmerizing, with color patterns and textures emerging in unexpected ways.

10. Mixed Media

Mixed media is the art of combining various materials and techniques into a single artwork. This technique allows you to explore a wide variety of materials, from traditional paints and pencils to fabrics, metals, and found objects.

How to do it:

  1. Start by selecting a surface, such as a canvas, wood, or paper.
  2. Experiment with layering different media such as paint, markers, fabric, and paper.
  3. Consider adding texture with gesso or modeling paste.
  4. Use collage elements, like cut-out pictures or newspaper clippings, to enhance your design.
  5. Finally, integrate different artistic techniques (such as painting, drawing, or printmaking) to create a truly unique piece.

Mixed media can be a liberating way to express yourself, allowing you to explore new materials and approaches in a single work of art.

Final Thoughts

Experimenting with different art techniques is not just about improving your skills—it’s about discovering new ways of thinking, creating, and expressing yourself. Whether you’re drawn to the fluidity of acrylic pouring, the texture of sgraffito, or the intricate details of charcoal, each technique offers a unique journey into the world of art. So why not try something new today and unleash your inner creativity?

By incorporating these techniques into your artistic practice, you’ll not only expand your repertoire but also gain a deeper appreciation for the endless possibilities that art provides. Start experimenting, have fun with your creativity, and watch your artistic skills grow in ways you never expected!
