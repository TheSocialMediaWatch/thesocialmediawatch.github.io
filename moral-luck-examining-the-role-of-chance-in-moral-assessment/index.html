Moral Luck: Examining the Role of Chance in Moral Assessment


Luck plays a significant role in our lives. From the moment we are born, we are subjected to a series of circumstances that are entirely beyond our control. These circumstances, often dictated by chance, shape our experiences, choices, and ultimately, our moral character. This phenomenon, known as moral luck, raises intriguing questions about the fairness of moral assessment and the extent to which individuals should be held accountable for their actions.

The Concept of Moral Luck

Moral luck, first introduced by philosopher Bernard Williams, challenges the traditional notion that moral assessment should be solely based on an individual’s intentions and actions. It argues that luck, in the form of external factors, can significantly influence the outcome of one’s actions and subsequently impact moral judgments.

Consider the example of two drivers. Driver A, who is intoxicated, chooses to drive home and manages to reach his destination safely. Driver B, on the other hand, is completely sober but encounters an unavoidable accident due to a mechanical failure in his car. By sheer luck, Driver A escapes unscathed, while Driver B causes severe harm to another individual. In this scenario, Driver A’s moral assessment may be deemed less severe due to the luck of avoiding an accident, despite making an irresponsible choice.

Types of Moral Luck

Moral luck can be categorized into four distinct types: resultant luck, circumstantial luck, constitutive luck, and causal luck.

  1. Resultant Luck: Resultant luck refers to the luck that impacts the actual outcome or consequences of our actions. In the example above, Driver A’s fortunate outcome is a result of resultant luck, while Driver B’s unfortunate outcome is a prime example of the same.

  2. Circumstantial Luck: Circumstantial luck involves the luck of being in the right place at the right time or having favorable circumstances that influence the moral assessment. Consider a situation where two individuals attempt to rescue a drowning person. If one of them happens to be a strong swimmer, their success in saving the person might be attributed to circumstantial luck.

  3. Constitutive Luck: Constitutive luck refers to the luck of having a particular set of traits or characteristics that affect moral assessment. For instance, being naturally inclined towards empathy and compassion due to genetic factors can be seen as constitutive luck.

  4. Causal Luck: Causal luck pertains to the luck that influences our motivations and intentions. It suggests that our desires and motivations can be influenced by factors beyond our control, such as genetic predispositions or upbringing. These factors can significantly impact our moral judgments.

The Implications of Moral Luck

The concept of moral luck challenges the traditional understanding of moral assessment, which tends to focus solely on an individual’s intentions and actions. It suggests that luck, in all its forms, plays a crucial role in shaping the outcomes of our choices and actions.

Understanding the role of luck in moral assessment has profound implications for our society. It calls into question the fairness of holding individuals accountable for factors beyond their control. Should someone be praised or condemned solely based on the outcome of their actions, even if that outcome was influenced by luck?

Conclusion

The concept of moral luck presents a compelling argument for reevaluating our approach to moral assessment. It forces us to consider the role of chance in shaping our lives and challenges the notion that individuals are solely responsible for the outcomes of their actions.

By acknowledging the influence of luck, we can cultivate a more compassionate and understanding society. It is crucial to recognize that moral judgments should not solely rely on outcome-based assessments but also take into account the complex interplay between intention, action, and luck.

As we continue to navigate the intricacies of moral assessment, it is essential to reflect on the profound impact that chance has on our lives. By embracing the concept of moral luck, we can foster a more nuanced understanding of human behavior and promote empathy and understanding in our moral judgments.
