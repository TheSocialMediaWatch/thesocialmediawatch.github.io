The Most Unique Hotels in the World You Have to Stay At


When it comes to travel, accommodation plays a monumental role in shaping the experience. While traditional hotels offer comfort and convenience, some places go beyond the ordinary, becoming destinations in their own right. The world is dotted with extraordinary hotels that blur the line between hospitality and art, architecture, culture, and nature. For those who crave adventure and the thrill of staying somewhere truly original, here is a curated list of the most unique hotels around the globe that you absolutely need to add to your travel bucket list.

1. Icehotel – Jukkasjärvi, Sweden

Imagine sleeping in a hotel built entirely from ice and snow, surrounded by beautifully sculpted ice art, with temperatures hovering below freezing. The Icehotel in Sweden is reconstructed every winter, showcasing stunning ice suites and a chapel made entirely of frozen water. The experience includes specialized thermal sleeping bags and warm lounges for comfort. This hotel perfectly marries art, environment, and architecture, making it an unmissable Nordic adventure.

What makes it unique?

  • Sculpted ice rooms that transform annually.
  • Custom ice furniture and chandeliers.
  • Thrilling cold-weather adventure experiences nearby.

2. Kakslauttanen Arctic Resort – Saariselkä, Finland

For an ethereal experience under the northern lights, the Kakslauttanen Arctic Resort offers glass igloos that let you stargaze from your bed. This Finnish retreat combines nature’s grandeur with cozy, warm interiors, delivering a surreal way to witness the Aurora Borealis. It’s also a hub for snowmobile safaris, husky sledding, and traditional Finnish sauna rituals.

What makes it unique?

  • Transparent igloo accommodations for unparalleled sky views.
  • Proximity to Lapland wilderness and winter activities.
  • Cozy timber cabins that complement the glass igloos.

3. Treehotel – Harads, Sweden

Perched among the pines of the Swedish forest are ultra-modern treehouse suites. Each “room” adopts an imaginative design, such as a mirrored cube reflecting the surroundings or a UFO-shaped pod. Elevated above the ground, guests immerse themselves in nature without sacrificing luxury or style. The innovative architecture invites guests to see forest living from a bold new perspective.

What makes it unique?

  • Artistic and thematic treehouse designs.
  • Integration with untouched Scandinavian forest.
  • Eco-friendly practices and spectacular views.

4. The Manta Resort – Pemba Island, Zanzibar

Ever dreamed of sleeping underwater surrounded by vibrant marine life? The Manta Resort fulfills that dream with its underwater room, floating decks, and a hammock buoyed by the Indian Ocean’s waters. The suite offers a 360-degree underwater panorama of coral reefs and exotic fish, creating a living aquarium for overnight guests. On the surface, private tropical bungalows provide stylish comfort with easy beach access.

What makes it unique?

  • Submerged bedroom beneath the ocean surface.
  • Exotic island setting with rich marine biodiversity.
  • Combination of floating and land accommodations.

5. Giraffe Manor – Nairobi, Kenya

In Nairobi’s suburb of Lang’ata, Giraffe Manor provides a rare experience where you can share your morning coffee with endangered Rothschild giraffes that poke their heads through the windows. This boutique hotel’s 1930s architecture combined with the intimate wildlife encounters creates an unforgettable safari–meets–luxury stay.

What makes it unique?

  • Direct interaction with resident giraffes.
  • Colonial-era mansion with lush gardens.
  • Location near Nairobi National Park.

6. Hotel Marques de Riscal – Elciego, Spain

A masterpiece of contemporary architecture designed by Frank Gehry, this hotel resembles a flowing sculpture clad in titanium, glass, and stone. Nestled among Rioja’s world-famous vineyards, the hotel offers a fusion of avant-garde design, haute cuisine, and wine culture. Staying here involves indulging in local gastronomic delights while soaking in architectural brilliance.

What makes it unique?

  • Iconic Gehry-designed structure.
  • Immersion in premium wine country.
  • State-of-the-art spa and culinary experiences.

7. The Null Stern Hotel – Switzerland

Translating to “zero star,” the Null Stern Hotel turns the concept of accommodation inside out by offering “rooms” in nature with breathtaking panoramic views instead of walls. Guests sleep outdoors in transparent capsules nestled into pristine alpine landscapes. It’s an authentic way to connect with the environment, free from the distractions of typical hotel life.

What makes it unique?

  • Open-air sleeping concept in protected nature.
  • Minimalist design emphasizing natural beauty.
  • Personalized outdoor hospitality.

8. Hotel Costa Verde – Costa Rica

Here, guests can stay in a refurbished 1965 Boeing 727 airplane perched high in the jungle canopy. Converted into an elevated hotel suite, the plane overlooks lush tropical forest and the Pacific Coast. The surrounding hotel grounds feature eco-friendly bungalows embracing the wildness of Costa Rica’s Guanacaste region.

What makes it unique?

  • Ultimate aviation-themed jungle accommodation.
  • High perched panoramic views.
  • Blend of sustainability and aviation history.

9. The Dog Bark Park Inn – Cottonwood, Idaho, USA

For a playful stay that’s truly out of the ordinary, the Dog Bark Park Inn is a giant beagle-shaped bed and breakfast where you sleep inside the dog’s belly. Created by local chainsaw artists, this whimsical structure is both a functioning hotel and an art installation. It makes for a quirky road trip stop and a memorable family adventure.

What makes it unique?

  • Oversized dog-shaped building guesthouse.
  • Artisan-crafted interiors celebrating chainsaw art.
  • Unique roadside attraction with a fun twist.

10. Poseidon Undersea Resort – Fiji (Concept)

Though not fully operational yet, the Poseidon Undersea Resort promises to deliver the next frontier of luxury underwater living. Guests would enjoy suites beneath coral reefs, glass walls revealing vibrant marine ecosystems from every angle. This futuristic resort aims to blend immersive ocean exploration with high-end hospitality.

What makes it unique?

  • Futuristic subaquatic accommodation.
  • Direct interaction with ocean habitats.
  • Exclusive privacy and luxury underwater.

Why Choose Extraordinary Hotels?

While many travelers prioritize location, comfort, and price, uniqueness adds a lasting emotional and sensory dimension to a stay. Unique hotels offer:

  • Memorable experiences: They create stories you tell for years.
  • Cultural immersion: Many integrate local artistry, history, or nature.
  • Visual stimulation: Architecture and design that spark inspiration.
  • Connection to surroundings: From forests to oceans, wildlife to deserts.

If you want your next trip to be more than just a getaway but a genuine experience, choosing one of these remarkable hotels will elevate your journey.

Tips for Booking Unique Hotels

  1. Plan early: Many unique hotels have limited rooms or seasonal availability.
  2. Consider location: Some are remote and require extra travel planning.
  3. Check amenities: Unique accommodations vary widely in luxury standards.
  4. Be open-minded: Some experiences involve unusual conditions like extreme cold or open-air sleeping.
  5. Read guest reviews: Previous visitor insights can help set expectations.

Final Thoughts

Travel is as much about the journey as the destination, and where you stay can transform a simple trip into an extraordinary adventure. These hotels invite you to break from the mundane and embrace creativity, nature, and culture in unexpected forms. Whether you’re floating underwater, nestled in a tree canopy, or sharing breakfast with giraffes, these hotels promise more than a bed—they promise memories that linger forever.

So the next time you plan a trip, consider stepping off the beaten path with a stay at one of the world’s most unique hotels—you won’t regret the adventure that follows.
