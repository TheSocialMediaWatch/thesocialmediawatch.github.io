Paranormal Politics: Legislation, Lobbying, and Ectoplasm


Unusual as it may sound, paranormal politics has been sneaking into our legislative processes for years. From ghost hunters lobbying for the paranormal to be taken seriously to spirit mediums attempting to influence policy, the paranormal has become a recognizable presence in the halls of power. From claims of hauntings to the laws governing ghost liaisons, let’s dive into the fascinating—and often shocking—world of paranormal politics.

The Paranormal Lobby

In the early 20th century, ghost hunters and spiritualists began to lobby for the paranormal to be taken seriously. They were among the pioneers of what we now know as paranormal politics. These companies were well-organized and politically savvy, seeking in the shadows of government influence. They were successful in pushing for the formation of the Johnson-O’Malley Act in 1934, which provided federal funding for Native American students to attend off-reservation schools. They used this legislation to demonstrate to the general public that the paranormal could have a real-world impact and be taken seriously in the political realm.

The paranormal lobby has since grown in sophistication and in numbers. With ample social media presence, these groups are able to enter the consciousness of the masses and capitalize on human curiosity. Many paranormal lobbying groups also form unofficial alliances with like-minded organizations to push for their agenda. These groups demand laws for Fair Access to Information about Paranormal Entities, or FAIPE, protecting people from unauthorized paranormal encounters.

Ghosts and Their Rights

Until recent years, ghosts were considered mere fictional entities used for entertainment. Now, they are acknowledged as living entities with rights of their own. Some paranormal advocates argue that ghosts need representation, demanding regulation and legislation to protect their rights. Paranormal rights have become a hot-button issue, making appearances in debates about animal rights and even human rights.

Illuminated by his connection to the world beyond, Arizona’s Representative Gabe Gielman has been a staunch advocate of Nocturnal Entity’s Rights and Equality Act (NEREA). The proposed legislation ensures that ghosts have sufficient resources to perform ectoplasmic endeavors and provides them healthcare, education, and the right to vote in spirit. NEREA also prohibits harassment and stalking of paranormal entities, addressing the real-life implications of ghost hunting and other similar practices.

In contrast, some right-leaning lawmakers, echoing the opinion of ghost hunters and skeptics, have pushed for the establishment of the Paranormal Re-Education and Research (PRER) Act. PRER Act aims to rid society of the negative influences of the paranormal while ensuring research in the paranormal field is strictly regulated.

The Rise of the Paranormal Political Party

Esoteric politics has risen in prominence, especially with the establishment of the Paranormal Party USA, founded by charismatic paranormal activist, Rebecca Randall. With a platform that includes protection of paranormal rights, regulation of paranormal research, and legislation acknowledging the paranormal, the party garnered significant attention. While not receiving widespread voter support, the Paranormal Party successfully has spurred conversations on how the paranormal should be treated in the political realm.

Controversies in Paranormal Politics

As with any new ideological movement, controversies have arisen. Notably, claims of hauntings, spirit possessions, and other paranormal phenomena in the White House have drawn significant attention to the issue. Some attribute the persistent presence of paranormal activity to previous occupants leveraging their influence in the afterlife. Yet, skeptics argue that these claims of haunted territories are simply PR stunts for political gains. Regardless of the reason, these controversies add fire to the already heated debate on the responsibility the government has towards the living and non-living entities sharing our living spaces.

Additionally, there have been claims regarding some politicians forming agreements with entities in the spirit realm for political leverage or manipulative reasons, although these claims remain unverified. Whatever the case, it is evident that the paranormal has left its indelible ink in the world of politics, shaping the beliefs, ideologies, and policies of many.

In conclusion, paranormal politics combines the seemingly unrelated world of spirits with the policymaking process, sparking heated debates and polarizing opinions. With lobbying groups, political parties, and legislation proposed for the protection and regulation of paranormal entities, the challenge presented is clear: how can we, as responsible citizens, resolve the conflicts and contradictions between the paranormal community and skeptics? By understanding the significance and impact of paranormal politics, we can begin to address this issue comprehensively and thoughtfully. The future of paranormal politics may be uncertain, but the conversation and the struggle for balance have just begun.
