Simple, Tasty, and Quick Dinner Recipes for One


Cooking dinner for one can sometimes feel like a chore, but it doesn’t have to be. Whether you’re living solo, enjoying some “me time,” or just want to whip up a satisfying meal without spending hours in the kitchen, these recipes fit the bill. They’re designed to be easy, flavorful, and fast—perfect for busy weeknights or lazy weekends where convenience doesn’t mean sacrificing taste.

This guide features a variety of simple dinner ideas tailored to individual servings. With ingredients commonly found in most pantries and fridges, these dishes empower solo diners to create meals that are not just filling but genuinely enjoyable. Let’s dive into a collection of quick, delicious recipes that prove single-serving dinners can be anything but boring.

1. One-Pan Garlic Butter Shrimp and Asparagus

This dish comes together in under 20 minutes and requires minimal cleanup—a win-win for solo cooks.

Ingredients

  • 6 oz shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 cup asparagus, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 tablespoon butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 lemon wedge
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a skillet over medium heat, melt the butter.
  2. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant (about 1 minute).
  3. Toss in the shrimp and cook for 2-3 minutes per side or until pink and opaque.
  4. Add asparagus pieces, season with salt and pepper, then cook until asparagus is tender-crisp, about 4-5 minutes.
  5. Squeeze fresh lemon juice over the shrimp and asparagus.
  6. Garnish with parsley if desired and serve immediately.

This straightforward recipe balances the flavors of garlic, butter, and citrus for a light yet satisfying meal. It pairs well with a small serving of rice or crusty bread to soak up the buttery sauce.

2. One-Pot Creamy Tomato Basil Pasta

Craving pasta but not keen on washing a mountain of pots? This creamy tomato basil pasta is perfect for cooking all components in one pot.

Ingredients

  • 2 oz dry pasta (e.g., penne or fusilli)
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 cup vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream or milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh basil, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Grated Parmesan, for topping (optional)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a medium saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add minced garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  3. Pour in broth and add pasta; bring to a simmer.
  4. Stir occasionally, cooking until pasta is almost tender (check package instructions but typically around 8-10 minutes).
  5. Add cherry tomatoes, cream, basil, salt, and pepper. Stir to combine and cook for an additional 2 minutes.
  6. Remove from heat, sprinkle with Parmesan cheese, and serve hot.

This recipe is a creamy, comforting choice that feels indulgent despite its simplicity. It’s customizable—swap cream for milk or add spinach or mushrooms for extra veggies.

3. Quick Chickpea Stir-Fry with Spinach and Feta

Vegetarian dinners can be just as speedy and flavorful as meat-based ones. This stir-fry highlights protein-rich chickpeas paired with fresh spinach and salty feta.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup canned chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 1 small red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • Juice of half a lemon

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a skillet on medium-high heat.
  2. Add sliced onion and cumin, stirring until onions soften (about 3 minutes).
  3. Add chickpeas and cook until warmed through and slightly crispy, roughly 4-5 minutes.
  4. Toss in fresh spinach and cook until wilted.
  5. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Remove from heat, drizzle with lemon juice, and top with crumbled feta.

This stir-fry is vibrant and textured, combining bright greens with tangy cheese for a nutrient-packed, satisfying solo meal.

4. Speedy Salmon and Avocado Salad

For those days when you want something fresh and no-cook, this salmon and avocado salad is nutrient-dense and super quick to assemble.

Ingredients

  • 4 oz cooked salmon (can be canned or leftover)
  • 1/2 avocado, cubed
  • 1 cup mixed greens or baby spinach
  • 1/4 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, Dijon mustard, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
  2. In a salad bowl, combine greens, cherry tomatoes, cubed avocado, and salmon.
  3. Drizzle dressing over the salad and toss gently to combine.
  4. Serve immediately.

This no-fuss salad is an ideal option for warm evenings or when you want something light yet nourishing. The creamy avocado and heart-healthy salmon provide slick richness balanced by fresh greens.

5. Easy Chicken and Broccoli Stir-Fry

When craving a home-cooked stir-fry but only cooking for one, this recipe scales down classic flavors without disappointment.

Ingredients

  • 1 small chicken breast, sliced thinly
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon honey or brown sugar
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 green onion, sliced (optional)
  • Cooked rice for serving (about 1/2 cup)

Instructions

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a wok or skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add garlic and ginger, stir for 30 seconds.
  3. Add chicken slices and stir-fry until nearly cooked, about 4-5 minutes.
  4. Toss in broccoli and cook until tender-crisp, about 3-4 minutes.
  5. Mix soy sauce and honey, pour over the stir-fry and toss well.
  6. Remove from heat and garnish with sliced green onions.
  7. Serve immediately with warm rice.

This savory stir-fry hits all the notes—sweet, salty, and umami—featuring vibrant veggies and lean protein that’s ready fast.

6. Cheesy Veggie Omelette

Omelettes aren’t just breakfast—they’re an excellent quick dinner option for one that can be customized endlessly.

Ingredients

  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup diced bell peppers
  • 1/4 cup chopped mushrooms
  • 2 tablespoons shredded cheddar or your favorite cheese
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Beat eggs with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat.
  3. Sauté bell peppers and mushrooms until tender, about 3-4 minutes.
  4. Pour eggs over veggies and cook without stirring until edges set.
  5. Sprinkle cheese on one half, fold omelette over, and cook another minute for cheese to melt.
  6. Slide onto a plate and enjoy hot.

This omelette comes together in under 10 minutes and offers endless variations depending on what veggies or cheese you have on hand.

Tips for Cooking Solo Without Wasting Food

Cooking for one comes with the challenge of avoiding waste while still having a variety of meals. Here are some strategies that make single-serving cooking easier and more efficient:

  • Use versatile ingredients: Choose items like canned beans, frozen veggies, and quick-cooking grains that can be mixed and matched.
  • Batch prep: Cook larger quantities of staples like rice, quinoa, or roasted chicken, then portion them out to use throughout the week.
  • Freeze leftovers: If you cook a bit extra, freeze individual portions to pull out on busy days.
  • Invest in small cookware: A 1-quart pot or a small frying pan is perfect for one, faster to heat up, and easier to clean.
  • Embrace simple recipes: Focus on dishes with fewer ingredients and steps that deliver maximum flavor with minimal effort.

Wrapping Up

Creating simple, tasty, and quick dinner recipes for one is about embracing minimalism without compromising on flavor or nutrition. These recipes reflect a range of cuisines and cooking techniques, from stir-fries to salads and one-pot wonders. Each can be made with everyday ingredients and adjusted to your preferences. Cooking solo is a chance to enjoy mealtime your way—flexible, flavorful, and effortlessly satisfying.

Put a few of these ideas into rotation, and dinner for one will never feel dull again. Whether you prefer lean proteins, hearty veggies, or cheesy comfort food, there’s a solo cooking recipe here that will become your new favorite.

Feel free to experiment and add your personal touch—after all, the best recipe is the one that makes you happy and excited to eat.
