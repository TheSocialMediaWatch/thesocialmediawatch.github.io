How to Choose the Right Swimwear for Your Body Type


Swimwear shopping is one of those seasonal tasks that can either excite or unsettle you. With countless styles, cuts, and patterns to choose from, finding the perfect swimsuit that complements your body type can feel overwhelming. However, making the right choice doesn’t have to be stressful. By understanding your body shape and knowing how different styles enhance your natural features, you’ll feel more confident and comfortable during your next beach or pool outing.

Whether you’re gearing up for a tropical getaway or simply want to update your swimwear collection, this guide will help you identify the best swimwear options for your body type, ensuring you look and feel your best.

Understanding Your Body Type

Before diving into swimwear styles, it’s essential to identify your body type. Your shape determines the silhouettes, colors, and designs most likely to flatter your figure. Here are the five key body types to consider:

  1. Hourglass: Curvy with a well-defined waist, hips, and shoulders that are proportionate.
  2. Pear: Narrow shoulders with wider hips and thighs.
  3. Apple: Fuller waistline with less definition between the bust, waist, and hips.
  4. Athletic (Rectangular): Straight lines with little curve definition and balanced proportions.
  5. Inverted Triangle: Broad shoulders tapering to a narrower waist and hips.

Knowing your body type serves as your compass for navigating swimwear options. Once you’ve identified your shape, it’s time to explore designs that work harmoniously with your features.

Swimwear for Hourglass Figures

Hourglass figures are characterized by balanced proportions and a defined waistline. For this body shape, the goal is to accentuate curves while providing support.

Best Swimwear Styles:

  • High-Waisted Bottoms: These emphasize the waist and provide a vintage-inspired look that highlights your curves.
  • Bikinis with Adjustable Ties: Triangle tops with adjustable straps allow optimal customization and highlight the bust.
  • One-Pieces with Side Cutouts: These draw attention to your waistline while maintaining elegance.
  • Underwire Tops: Supportive tops lift the bust while enhancing your natural shape.

Design Elements to Try:

  • Bold prints and vibrant colors
  • Plunging necklines to elongate the torso
  • Solid color panels for a sleek aesthetic

Hourglass figures often suit most styles, so don’t hesitate to experiment with trends, textures, and fabrics!

Swimwear for Pear Shapes

Pear-shaped bodies feature narrower shoulders and a broader lower half. The key is to create balance by drawing attention to the upper body and minimizing focus on the hips.

Best Swimwear Styles:

  • Ruffled or Embellished Tops: These add volume to the upper body, creating visual balance.
  • High-Neck Swim Tops: A chic and effective choice for elongating the shoulder area.
  • Skirted Bottoms: Skirted swimsuits gently camouflage the hips while adding a feminine touch.
  • Bold Bikini Tops with Solid Bottoms: Eye-catching tops paired with darker, subtle bottoms shift focus upward.

Design Elements to Try:

  • Bright and patterned tops
  • Horizontal stripes or detailing on the upper half
  • A-line swim dresses that flow away from the hips

By pairing a statement-making top with understated bottoms, you’ll nail the proportions and feel confident at the beach.

Swimwear for Apple Shapes

Apple-shaped bodies make their widest point the midsection, with slender legs and often an ample bust. Your swimwear goal is to draw attention away from the waistline while highlighting assets like your legs or neckline.

Best Swimwear Styles:

  • Ruched One-Pieces: The gathered fabric creates a slimming effect across the torso, helping define curves.
  • Tankinis: These ensure upper body coverage while providing flexibility and style.
  • Bikinis with High-Waisted Bottoms: They visually trim the waist area while accentuating the hips.
  • Deep V-Necklines: These elongate the upper body and frame the chest beautifully.

Design Elements to Try:

  • Dark colors or color-block designs
  • Asymmetrical patterns that divert focus
  • Flowy swim cover-ups for an effortlessly chic look

Comfortable, flattering options are crucial to help you feel good about your body’s natural shape.

Swimwear for Athletic Shapes

Athletic or rectangular shapes look lean and streamlined, with proportions that are balanced but lack defined curves. Swimwear for athletic builds aims to create the illusion of curves while maintaining simplicity.

Best Swimwear Styles:

  • Color-Blocked Pieces: Strategic patterns add contour and dimension.
  • Padded Bikini Tops: Extra padding enhances the bust line.
  • One-Pieces with High Leg Cuts: These elongate the legs and subtly create curves.
  • Swimsuits with Frills and Ruffles: Adding texture softens angular features and plays up femininity.

Design Elements to Try:

  • Bold geometric designs
  • Playful embellishments like bows and tassels
  • Bright, vibrant hues that stand out

Athletic body types can experiment with details that add softness to their physique while celebrating their toned appearance.

Swimwear for Inverted Triangle Shapes

The inverted triangle shape is marked by wider shoulders and a narrower lower body. Finding swimwear that balances the top half with the hips is key to an effortless look.

Best Swimwear Styles:

  • Plunge Necklines: These draw the eye downward, easing the focus on broad shoulders.
  • Solid Tops with Patterned Bottoms: Create balance by opting for a darker upper half and bolder designs below.
  • Skirted or Flared Bottoms: Adding volume to the lower body equalizes proportions.
  • Halter Necklines: Provides structured support while flattering wide shoulders.

Design Elements to Try:

  • Brightly detailed bottoms
  • Wrap-style one-pieces for a tapered silhouette
  • Horizontal stripes or pleats on lower pieces

These choices bring symmetry to your look while ensuring your frame is beautifully highlighted.

General Tips for Choosing Swimwear

No matter your body type, keep these universal tips in mind as you shop for swimwear:

Comfort Is Key

Always prioritize swimwear that fits well and makes you feel at ease. No amount of style will compensate for discomfort, whether it’s caused by tight straps or loose bottoms.

Fabric Quality Matters

Opt for high-quality fabrics that offer stretch, durability, and support. Strong elastics and seamless construction help your swimsuit maintain its shape over time.

Pay Attention to Color

Colors play a significant role in swimwear aesthetics. Darker shades slim the frame, while brighter hues draw attention. Prints can enhance or camouflage features depending on their placement.

Test for Functionality

If you’re wearing swimwear for activities like swimming, surfing, or volleyball, ensure the design is functional. For example, full coverage and extra support are necessary for active water sports.

The Perfect Swimwear Awaits You

Choosing the best swimwear for your body type doesn’t have to be an intimidating task. Armed with the knowledge of your figure and the array of flattering options available, you can confidently navigate the racks in search of styles that match your personality and accentuate your natural beauty.

Remember, swimwear is more than just clothing—it’s an opportunity to express yourself and celebrate your body. Whether you’re lounging on the beach, taking a dip in the pool, or soaking up the summer sun, the right swimwear ensures you look and feel incredible.
