The Top 10 Music Podcasts for Fans and Musicians


Music has been a fundamental part of human culture for centuries, and today, it’s more accessible than ever. With podcasts becoming an essential medium for learning, discovering, and sharing music, there’s no shortage of incredible audio content for music lovers and aspiring musicians alike. Whether you’re looking to expand your musical knowledge, learn more about the industry, or find inspiration for your own work, music podcasts are an invaluable resource.

Here, we’ve compiled a list of the top 10 music podcasts that offer something for everyone—from casual fans to seasoned musicians. These shows cover a wide range of topics, from interviews with artists to deep dives into music theory and industry trends.

1. Song Exploder

“Song Exploder” is a must-listen for anyone interested in the creative process behind their favorite songs. Hosted by Hrishikesh Hirway, the podcast features musicians breaking down their tracks, explaining how they wrote them, recorded them, and produced them. It’s an inside look at the magic that happens behind the scenes, from initial idea to final mix.

What makes “Song Exploder” stand out is its diversity in artists. From pop stars like Lorde to indie favorites like The War on Drugs, the show covers a broad spectrum of genres. Each episode is a short, bite-sized exploration of a specific song, making it perfect for those who want to dive deep into the art of songwriting.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Insight into the songwriting and production process.
  • Wide variety of artists and genres.
  • Perfect for both fans and musicians.

2. All Songs Considered

As a staple of NPR’s music programming, “All Songs Considered” is a beloved podcast that brings the latest in music discovery. Hosted by Bob Boilen, the show features in-depth interviews with musicians, discussions about new albums, and analysis of emerging trends in the music world.

The show is known for its thoughtful commentary and a relaxed, conversational style that makes it easy for listeners to feel like they’re hanging out with their music-loving friends. Whether you’re into indie rock, electronic, or world music, “All Songs Considered” offers a little something for everyone.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Discover new music from a wide range of genres.
  • Get recommendations and insights into the latest albums.
  • Interviews with both established and up-and-coming artists.

3. The Talkhouse Music Podcast

For fans of indie and alternative music, “The Talkhouse Music Podcast” is an excellent choice. Hosted by the editors of The Talkhouse, a site that features articles and interviews written by musicians themselves, this podcast takes a unique approach by having artists interview one another.

The show’s format is relaxed, giving artists a chance to share stories, discuss their creative processes, and reflect on their careers. With an emphasis on collaboration and community, “The Talkhouse Music Podcast” provides a fresh perspective on the music industry and offers insights that you won’t find in more traditional interviews.

Why You Should Listen:

  • A unique format where musicians interview musicians.
  • Insight into the indie music scene and creative processes.
  • Casual, candid conversations that are engaging and fun.

4. The FADER Uncovered

“The FADER Uncovered” is a podcast that delves into the lives and careers of some of the biggest names in the music world. Hosted by FADER magazine editor-in-chief, Alex Robert Ross, the show takes an in-depth look at the stories behind influential albums and the impact they’ve had on the music industry.

This podcast offers listeners a deep dive into the history and cultural relevance of iconic tracks and albums, while also touching on topics like industry trends, the business of music, and the challenges of navigating fame. If you’re a fan of popular culture and the music industry’s inner workings, this is a great choice.

Why You Should Listen:

  • In-depth interviews with influential artists.
  • Explore the stories behind iconic albums and songs.
  • Get a behind-the-scenes look at the music industry.

5. The Trapital Podcast

“The Trapital Podcast” focuses on the business side of the music industry, making it perfect for aspiring musicians, managers, or anyone interested in the economic aspects of the business. Hosted by music industry expert Dan Runcie, the show features discussions on topics like branding, marketing, and how artists build their empire.

Each episode provides actionable insights into the business decisions that help artists thrive. From partnerships and endorsements to album releases and financial strategies, “The Trapital Podcast” offers valuable lessons for anyone hoping to break into the music business or better understand its dynamics.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Focus on the business side of the music industry.
  • Learn how artists make money and grow their brand.
  • Insights from industry experts and successful entrepreneurs.

6. Dissect

If you’re a fan of hip-hop or any genre with complex, layered lyrics, “Dissect” is the podcast for you. Hosted by Cole Cuchna, this show takes a deep, academic approach to analyzing entire albums, breaking down the lyrics, production, and thematic elements track by track.

“Dissect” has gained a dedicated following for its meticulous and thoughtful analysis of music. The podcast has explored albums from artists like Kendrick Lamar, Kanye West, and Frank Ocean, offering listeners a deeper understanding of the artistry behind these projects. It’s perfect for fans who love to dive into the details and uncover hidden meanings in their favorite albums.

Why You Should Listen:

  • In-depth analysis of entire albums.
  • Focus on lyrics, production, and themes.
  • Great for fans who appreciate detailed music criticism.

7. The Music Business Podcast

For those looking to learn more about the business of music, “The Music Business Podcast” is a must-listen. Hosted by music industry veterans, the show covers everything from music distribution and licensing to the intricacies of managing an artist’s career. With its wealth of industry knowledge and practical advice, it’s an invaluable resource for anyone looking to break into the business.

The podcast also features interviews with music managers, producers, and executives, giving listeners an insider’s view of the industry. Whether you’re an aspiring musician or someone interested in the technical aspects of music production, this podcast is filled with insights that can help guide your career.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Focus on the business and practical aspects of the music industry.
  • Learn about music management, distribution, and licensing.
  • Interviews with industry experts and professionals.

8. Switched on Pop

For fans of pop music, “Switched on Pop” is an excellent podcast that takes a deep dive into the mechanics of pop songs. Hosted by musicologist Nate Sloan and songwriter Charlie Harding, the show analyzes the structure, lyrics, and production of popular songs, helping listeners understand what makes pop music so effective.

With its academic yet accessible approach, “Switched on Pop” is perfect for anyone who’s ever wondered why certain songs become hits. The show explores everything from the cultural impact of pop music to the scientific aspects of music theory that help create catchy, unforgettable tracks.

Why You Should Listen:

  • A fun, analytical look at pop music.
  • Learn the secrets behind hit songs and why they work.
  • Deep dives into song structure, lyrics, and production.

9. The Moth

While not strictly a music podcast, “The Moth” is a storytelling podcast that often features musicians and music-related stories. This podcast is all about live storytelling, with people from all walks of life sharing their personal experiences on stage. For music lovers, this can include stories about the highs and lows of a music career, the struggles of being an artist, or even funny anecdotes from the road.

The Moth offers a unique and emotional perspective on music and the human experience. Listening to these personal stories can give listeners a deeper appreciation for the power of music to shape lives and communities.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Real-life stories from musicians and music fans.
  • Emotional, personal narratives about the music experience.
  • Great for anyone interested in storytelling and the human side of music.

10. Rolling Stone Music Now

“Rolling Stone Music Now” is a podcast from one of the most iconic music magazines in the world. Hosted by Rolling Stone’s editors, the show features interviews with artists, discussions on the latest trends, and debates on hot topics in the music world. Whether it’s an interview with a legendary rock band or a conversation about the state of the music industry, “Rolling Stone Music Now” keeps listeners up to date on the latest happenings in the music world.

The podcast also provides a behind-the-scenes look at the making of Rolling Stone’s features and covers, making it a must-listen for fans of the magazine.

Why You Should Listen:

  • Get the latest news from one of the most trusted names in music journalism.
  • In-depth discussions on music trends and culture.
  • Interviews with industry heavyweights and rising stars.

Conclusion

These 10 music podcasts offer a wealth of knowledge, entertainment, and inspiration for both fans and musicians alike. Whether you’re looking to explore the creative process behind your favorite songs, learn about the business side of the industry, or simply enjoy some engaging conversations with artists, there’s something on this list for you.

The beauty of podcasts is that they provide a platform for diverse voices and viewpoints, allowing listeners to connect with the music they love in new and exciting ways. As the music world continues to evolve, podcasts remain a dynamic and engaging medium for discovering, discussing, and celebrating the art of music. So, plug in your headphones, press play, and get ready to dive deep into the world of music!
