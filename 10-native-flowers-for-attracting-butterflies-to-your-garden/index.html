10 Native Flowers for Attracting Butterflies to Your Garden


If you’re a nature enthusiast and love to see butterflies fluttering around your garden, then planting native flowers that attract these delicate creatures is a must. Not only do native flowers add beauty to your outdoor space, but they also provide essential nectar and pollen for butterflies, supporting their life cycle. Here are 10 native flowers that will entice and nourish butterflies in your garden.

1. Milkweed (Asclepias)

Milkweed is a must-have for any butterfly garden, especially for monarch butterflies. Its vibrant flowers and thick foliage provide a perfect habitat for monarch caterpillars, making it an essential plant for the survival of this iconic butterfly species.

2. Butterfly Weed (Asclepias tuberosa)

As the name suggests, this native perennial is a magnet for butterflies. Its bright orange flowers not only add a pop of color to your garden but also attract a variety of butterflies, including swallowtails and monarchs.

3. Coneflower (Echinacea)

Coneflowers are not only stunning to look at, but their nectar-rich blossoms are irresistible to butterflies. These hardy perennials come in a range of colors, making them a versatile addition to any butterfly garden.

4. Black-Eyed Susan (Rudbeckia hirta)

With its cheery yellow petals and dark centers, black-eyed Susans are a favorite among butterflies. These resilient wildflowers bloom throughout the summer, providing a consistent food source for butterflies.

5. Joe Pye Weed (Eutrochium)

Tall and majestic, Joe Pye weed is a native wildflower that attracts a myriad of butterfly species. Its pinkish-purple blooms are a rich source of nectar and provide a stunning backdrop for butterfly watching.

6. Blazing Star (Liatris)

The striking purple spikes of the blazing star are a beacon for butterflies. This native perennial blooms from top to bottom, ensuring a long-lasting food source for your fluttering visitors.

7. Bee Balm (Monarda)

Bee balm, also known as wild bergamot, is a fragrant and colorful addition to any butterfly garden. Its tubular flowers are a favorite among long-tongued butterflies such as swallowtails and skippers.

8. Ironweed (Vernonia)

Ironweed’s deep purple blossoms are a sight to behold and a delight for butterflies. This hardy perennial is a valuable late-season nectar source, ensuring that butterflies have sustenance well into the fall.

9. Goldenrod (Solidago)

Despite its undeserved reputation as a cause of hay fever, goldenrod is a crucial late-season food source for butterflies. Its bright yellow flowers are a beacon for migrating butterflies fueling up for their journey.

10. Asters (Symphyotrichum)

Asters are a diverse group of wildflowers that provide a vital food source for butterflies in the late summer and fall. Their daisy-like blooms come in a variety of colors, attracting a wide range of butterfly species to your garden.

By incorporating these 10 native flowers into your garden, you can create a haven for butterflies while adding natural beauty to your outdoor space. Not only will these flowers attract butterflies, but they will also support other pollinators, making your garden an essential wildlife habitat. So, roll up your sleeves, grab your gardening tools, and get ready to welcome a kaleidoscope of butterflies to your garden!
