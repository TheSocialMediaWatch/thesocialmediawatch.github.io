Mother May I? The Fruits of Strict Raising on Instruments in Youth


Mother May I refers to the game that many parents played with their children, where they sought permission from the mother before taking a given step. However, in the context of this article, we will explore how strictly raised children can thrive when exposed to musical instruments during their adolescence.

Growing up in an environment of strict parenting has its advantages and disadvantages. On one hand, it can produce organized, disciplined, and thorough individuals. On the other hand, it can also lead to stifled creativity, lack of self-expression, and over-reliance on external validation. In this piece, we will delve into the impact of strict parenting on the development of musical talents in adolescence and how it can produce award-winning musicians.

The power of strict discipline and regimented practice lies in its ability to instill a work ethic that is essential for success in any field, including music. Children who grow up with highly structured routines and strict expectations often develop an innate ability to focus and work tirelessly to perfect their craft. In the realm of music, this discipline can manifest in several ways.

First, adherence to a strict practice regimen enables musical proficiency. Children who undergo rigorous coaching and enforcement of practice routines are far more likely to develop the dexterity and technique necessary to excel at their chosen instrument. This discipline contributes to long-term dedication and perseverance, fostering true musical aptitude.

Second, strict parenting can lead to an increased attention to detail. Musicians who come from a disciplined background are more likely to analyze and improve upon their playing. This meticulousness translates into an acute awareness of their tone, rhythm, and musicality, leading to extraordinary performances.

Thirdly, strict parenting often fosters coordination, separately from musical abilities. Improved physical coordination can enhance a musician’s playing precision and speed, leading to more accurate timing and faster execution of complex musical pieces.

Lastly, discipline cultivates passion. Through strict training in music, children may discover a newfound love for their chosen instrument. Exposure to a diverse range of genres and styles can ignite the performing artist within them, spurring creative expression and personal fulfillment.

However, the pure discipline fostered by strict parenting should be balanced with the allowance for creative exploration and independence. Adolescents need guidance, of course, but they should also be encouraged to experiment, innovate, and contribute to the ever-evolving world of music.

In conclusion, strict raising can present newfound opportunities for musical proficiency and self-expression for young musicians. When balanced correctly with an allowance for artistic freedom and independence, strict upbringing can contribute to the development of disciplined, creative, and outstanding musicians.

Let us endeavor to nurture and foster talent, while allowing individuals the space to develop their unique style and identity. Only then can we produce musicians who can truly make a difference in the world of music.
