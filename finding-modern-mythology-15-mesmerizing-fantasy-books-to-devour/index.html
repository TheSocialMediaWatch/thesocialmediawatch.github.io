Finding Modern Mythology: 15 Mesmerizing Fantasy Books to Devour


Are you captivated by the allure of fantastical worlds, legendary heroes, and boundless adventures? If so, you’ve likely stumbled upon the enchanting realm of modern mythology. These mesmerizing fantasy novels transport you to realms rich with magic, striking characters, and unputdownable narratives. Below we have compiled a list of 15 standout titles that are well worth exploring.

  1. A Song of Ice and Fire series by George R. R. Martin Bestselling author George R. R. Martin’s acclaimed series inspires unforgettable tales of political intrigue, power struggles, and the overarching theme of the necessity of making difficult decisions.

  2. The Wheel of Time series by Robert Jordan and Brandon Sanderson A captivating tapestry of fate, destiny, and legendary heroes, the Wheel of Time series transports you to a world of intricate magic and political intrigue, spanned across fourteen epic novels.

  3. The Stormlight Archive series by Brandon Sanderson A marvel of world-building and diverse, memorable characters, Brandon Sanderson’s The Stormlight Archive books incorporate epic battles, creatures of myth and legend, and the eternal struggle between good and evil.

  4. A Discovery of Witches by Deborah Harkness A Discovery of Witches revolves around the captivating love story between a historian and a witch. The book’s intricate plot spans multiple eras and includes mythology, magic, and a whirlwind of global intrigue.

  5. American Gods by Neil Gaiman Neil Gaiman’s imaginative vision in American Gods breathes new life into a pantheon of supernatural beings that reside in modern America. This inventive tale is a beguiling blend of fantasy, mythology, and dark comedy.

  6. The Chronicles of Narnia by C. S. Lewis A timeless series for both adults and children alike, C.S. Lewis transports readers to the enchanting, timeless world of Narnia. Each book is a grand adventure filled with noble beasts, demigods, and memorable battles.

  7. The Kingkiller Chronicle series by Patrick Rothfuss In the Kingkiller Chronicle, an enigmatic arch-magician recounts his life story to a scribe. The narrative weaves together themes of culture, religion, and self-discovery as Kvothe navigates the evolution of a young adventurer into a legendary figure.

  8. The Way of Kings by Brandon Sanderson Within this epic novel, Brandon Sanderson tackles a variety of intriguing topics, including emotion, relationships, and the nature of belief, all against the backdrop of a fascinating fictional world and its inhabitants.

  9. The Name of the Wind by Patrick Rothfuss The Name of the Wind is both a coming-of-age story and a thrilling allegory, drawing influence from Kurosawa films and operas while engrossing readers with evocative storytelling and noble characters.

  10. Theautumn Republic series by Brian McClellan

Filled with magic, political intrigue, and epic conflicts, the Promise of Blood kicks off an ambitious new series by Brian McClellan, offering independence and originality in a saturated genre.

  1. The coldlight War by Brian Staveley

A sprawling new epic fantasy series launching with The Empire’s Bones, Brian Staveley weaves together multiple disparate elements into the mesmerizing web of a world in conflict amidst an intricate political landscape.

  1. The Broken Earth series by N. K. Jemisin

A riveting look at the concept of resilience and the collapse of society, N. K. Jemisin’s The Broken Earth series is a breathtaking saga that mixes mythology, race, and gender as it uncovers the fragility of existence.

  1. The Blacktvue Deadline by Victor Dixen

Drawing inspiration from real-life historical events, such as the English Civil War, Victor Dixen explores the evolution of war and the psychological impact it has on individuals, as well as the question of how much surrender is justifiable in the face of gruesome conflict.

  1. The Wool series by Hugh Howey

In a post-apocalyptic world, Hugh Howey’s The Wool series portrays a fascinating society, revealing how a broken earth can breed an entirely new subspecies of humanity. Each installment delivers an inventive twist on the genre.

  1. The Raven Cycle series by Maggie Stiefvater

A captivating story revolving around the supernatural friendship between four boys and a hysterical girl, The Raven Cycle offers resonance in its exploration of loyalty, self-acceptance, and the quest for belonging in a world that can be harsh and unforgiving.

Dive headfirst into these enchanting realms and partake in battles waged in the name of fate, discover dark forces lurking in the wings, and sail towards your own legend. The world of modern mythology awaits with open arms, and these 15 fantasy novels are but the beginning of your journey.
