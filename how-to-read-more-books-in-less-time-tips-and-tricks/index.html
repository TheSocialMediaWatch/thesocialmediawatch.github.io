How to Read More Books in Less Time: Tips and Tricks


In today’s fast-paced world, finding time to read books can feel like an impossible task. Between work, family responsibilities, social obligations, and personal downtime, sitting down with a good book often slips down the list of priorities. However, reading more books doesn’t necessarily require an endless amount of free time. By adopting the right techniques and mindset, you can significantly increase the number of books you read in a shorter span of time.

This guide will provide actionable tips and tricks that can help you maximize your reading efficiency, minimize distractions, and make the most out of every minute you dedicate to reading.

1. Set Clear Reading Goals

One of the best ways to get started on a reading journey is by setting clear, achievable goals. Without a target in mind, it’s easy to get distracted or overwhelmed by the sheer number of books available. By defining specific, measurable, and time-bound reading goals, you can stay motivated and track your progress.

Examples of Reading Goals:

  • Read one book per week/month.
  • Finish a certain number of pages per day (e.g., 20 pages).
  • Complete a specific genre or series within a set time.

Tracking your progress can be a huge motivator. Many readers use apps or journals to record how much they’ve read, and this can help keep you accountable.

2. Prioritize Reading

Finding time to read starts with making it a priority in your life. One of the easiest ways to fit more reading into your day is to carve out dedicated time for it. Look for small pockets of time throughout your day where you can read a few pages.

Effective Strategies for Prioritizing Reading:

  • Morning Routine: Start your day with 15-30 minutes of reading before getting caught up in work or daily activities.
  • Commute Time: If you take public transport, use this time to read. Even if you drive, consider audiobooks.
  • Lunch Breaks: Rather than scrolling through social media, spend your lunch break reading a chapter or two.
  • Before Bed: Reading before sleep can be a great way to wind down. It also helps to ensure you get enough rest, as you’re not staring at a screen right before bedtime.

Making reading a part of your regular routine ensures that you don’t need to find extra time—you create it.

3. Utilize Audiobooks

Audiobooks have become a game-changer for many readers. You can listen to books while doing other tasks like commuting, exercising, cleaning, or cooking. Audiobooks allow you to absorb information without needing to physically sit down and read.

Not only does this help you “read” more books in less time, but it also exposes you to the richness of storytelling through narration and voice acting. Audiobooks are particularly useful for nonfiction, as they can provide an immersive experience.

Many people find that alternating between reading physical books and listening to audiobooks increases their overall book intake. You might also find that certain genres or types of books are more enjoyable as audiobooks than others.

4. Speed Reading Techniques

Speed reading is the practice of increasing your reading speed without sacrificing comprehension. Although it takes some practice, speed reading can help you get through more material in less time.

Here are a few techniques to help you read faster:

  • Eliminate Subvocalization: Subvocalization refers to the habit of silently “saying” the words in your head as you read. While this may seem helpful, it actually slows down your reading speed. Try to focus on recognizing words without silently vocalizing them.
  • Use a Pointer or Guide: Running your finger, a pen, or a pointer along the text can help guide your eyes and improve focus, leading to faster reading.
  • Avoid Backtracking: Many readers have the tendency to re-read sentences or phrases. This slows down reading. Try to read straight through without going back unless necessary.
  • Expand Your Peripheral Vision: Train your eyes to take in more words at once. This reduces the need to stop and focus on each individual word, thereby increasing reading speed.

Although speed reading can be difficult at first, with consistent practice, you’ll be able to process information faster and cover more pages in a shorter time.

5. Read Multiple Books at Once

While some people prefer to read one book at a time, reading multiple books simultaneously can be an effective way to tackle more material. This method works particularly well if you’re reading different types of books—such as fiction and nonfiction—or books of varying difficulty levels.

Tips for Reading Multiple Books:

  • Categorize Your Books: Keep track of the type of books you’re reading (e.g., one for leisure, one for learning, one for professional development) and ensure that they don’t overlap too much in content.
  • Switch Between Books Based on Your Mood: If you’re feeling mentally drained, switch to something lighter and more entertaining. If you’re feeling focused and energized, dive into something more challenging.
  • Set a Limit: Try to limit yourself to reading two or three books at a time. Reading too many books simultaneously can lead to confusion or a lack of focus.

By having multiple books to choose from, you won’t feel forced to finish one before starting another, and this can help keep your reading fresh and engaging.

6. Make Use of Book Summaries

Sometimes, you might want to consume books quickly but without spending too much time on lengthy chapters or subplots. Book summaries and condensed versions of books can give you an overview of the main points, ideas, and arguments without the need to read the entire text.

There are various platforms and services that provide summaries of popular books. You can use them to get the essence of a book before deciding if it’s worth investing time in reading the full version. This is particularly helpful for nonfiction books, where you may only need the key takeaways.

However, don’t rely solely on summaries. While they are an excellent way to quickly gather information, they cannot replace the full reading experience and the depth that comes from engaging with the entire text.

7. Minimize Distractions

Distractions are one of the biggest barriers to reading efficiently. In our digital age, the lure of social media, notifications, and multitasking can easily pull your attention away from the book in your hands. To read more books in less time, you need to reduce distractions as much as possible.

Techniques to Minimize Distractions:

  • Turn Off Notifications: When reading, put your phone on silent or airplane mode to avoid interruptions.
  • Create a Reading Environment: Find a quiet place to read, free from distractions like TV, loud music, or other people.
  • Set a Timer: Use a timer to allocate a specific amount of time for reading. This will keep you focused and ensure that you don’t waste time on other activities during this period.

Creating a distraction-free reading environment is essential to maintaining focus and getting through more pages in less time.

8. Choose the Right Books

Not all books are created equal, and some books will require more time and effort than others. If your goal is to read more books in less time, you need to choose books that align with your interests and reading habits.

Considerations When Choosing Books:

  • Read What You Enjoy: The more engaged you are with a book, the more likely you are to finish it quickly. Choose books that pique your interest, whether they are fiction or nonfiction.
  • Skip Long, Dull Sections: Not every part of every book is essential. If a section is not engaging or relevant to you, feel free to skip ahead to the next chapter.
  • Avoid Overly Complex Books: Some books are dense and require slow, careful reading. While these books are valuable, they may not be the best choice if you’re looking to read more books in a short time.

By picking books that you are genuinely excited about, you’ll naturally read faster and more frequently.

9. Join a Reading Group or Challenge

Sometimes, the best way to motivate yourself to read more is to join a community of readers. Reading groups and challenges can provide a social and supportive environment to help you stay committed to your reading goals.

Benefits of Joining a Reading Group or Challenge:

  • Accountability: A reading group provides a sense of accountability. When you know others are expecting you to finish a book by a certain date, it encourages you to prioritize reading.
  • New Recommendations: Group members often share book recommendations, exposing you to new genres and authors you may not have considered.
  • Discussions and Insights: Reading groups offer the opportunity to discuss books with others, enhancing your understanding and enjoyment of the material.

Whether it’s an online group, a local book club, or participating in reading challenges like “Read 100 books in a year,” joining a community can provide both motivation and support for reading more books in less time.

Conclusion

In a world where time is precious and distractions are constant, reading more books in less time is achievable with the right strategies. By setting clear goals, prioritizing your reading, using audiobooks, practicing speed reading, and minimizing distractions, you can increase your reading efficiency and reach your literary goals.

Remember, reading should be an enjoyable experience, not a race. Embrace these tips, adapt them to fit your lifestyle, and soon you’ll be amazed at how many books you can read in a shorter amount of time. Happy reading!
