The Best Astronomy Podcasts to Tune Into in 2023


If you’re fascinated by the mysteries of the cosmos, astronomy podcasts offer an exciting and accessible way to explore the universe. Whether you’re drawn to black holes, the hunt for exoplanets, or the latest developments in space exploration, these audio gems bring the wonders of the universe straight to your ears. With engaging discussions, expert interviews, and fascinating stories, astronomy podcasts not only entertain but also educate, making complex concepts accessible to all. If you’re looking for fresh content to fuel your curiosity, here are the best astronomy podcasts to tune into in 2023.

StarTalk Radio

Hosted by astrophysicist and popular science communicator Neil deGrasse Tyson, StarTalk Radio is a staple for anyone interested in astronomy and broader science topics. The podcast seamlessly blends humor, pop culture, and cutting-edge scientific discussion, often featuring celebrity guests alongside experts in astrophysics and cosmology. Tyson’s ability to explain complex phenomena like gravitational waves or the search for habitable exoplanets is unmatched, making this podcast accessible to curious minds of all levels. Episodes often delve into topics like the future of space travel, NASA’s latest missions, and the philosophical implications of our place in the universe.

Astronomy Cast

Veteran science journalists Fraser Cain of Universe Today and Dr. Pamela Gay deliver weekly episodes that provide deep dives into astronomical phenomena. Astronomy Cast has been running for over 15 years, offering a treasure trove of episodes that explore everything from the origins of the universe to the mechanisms behind star formation. What makes this podcast stand out is its methodical approach to explaining intricate topics without overwhelming listeners. Whether you’re intrigued by dark matter or want to learn the nitty-gritty of how telescopes work, this podcast delivers clear and accessible explanations that deepen your understanding of astronomy.

The Orbital Path

A captivating podcast that merges science with storytelling, The Orbital Path explores the human side of space exploration and astronomy. Hosted by astrophysicist Michelle Thaller, this podcast dives into the emotional, philosophical, and cultural aspects of observing the cosmos. Episodes often tackle existential questions like what it means to be “star stuff” or how humanity might adapt to living on distant worlds. While the show occasionally touches on technical subjects, its focus on storytelling ensures listeners leave each episode inspired and with a broader appreciation for the universe.

Planetary Radio

If you’re eager to stay updated on planetary science and space exploration, Planetary Radio, produced by The Planetary Society, is a must-listen. Hosted by Mat Kaplan, this podcast brings you interviews with scientists, engineers, and thought leaders at the forefront of space exploration. Topics range from missions to Mars and Europa to discussions about the future of interstellar travel. The podcast also includes coverage of emerging technologies that could revolutionize our understanding of the cosmos. With years of archives to explore, Planetary Radio is a goldmine for space enthusiasts wanting to stay informed on the latest developments.

Event Horizon

For those captivated by black holes, quantum mechanics, and the mind-bending mysteries of theoretical physics, Event Horizon is an excellent choice. Hosted by John Michael Godier, this podcast often delves into cutting-edge ideas about the universe from experts in astrophysics, cosmology, and advanced research areas. The discussions, though intricate, are presented in a way that sparks curiosity and makes you want to learn more. Beyond astronomical phenomena, the show explores topics like artificial intelligence and its role in understanding the universe, adding a futuristic touch to its episodes.

Our Universe Podcast

Hosted by Professor Brian Cox, Our Universe Podcast takes you on a spectacular journey through time and space. Cox’s poetic narration and profound insights into the nature of existence make this podcast uniquely engaging. From the Big Bang to the ultimate fate of the universe, Cox’s discussions are both awe-inspiring and informative. In addition to astronomy, he often connects scientific themes to philosophical questions, making the discussions both accessible and thought-provoking for listeners.

Practical Astronomy Podcast

For amateur astronomers looking to deepen their knowledge or improve observational skills, the Practical Astronomy Podcast delivers actionable insights. This podcast blends technical advice, stargazing tips, and news updates about celestial events like eclipses, meteor showers, and planetary transits. Episodes often focus on tools such as telescopes and binoculars, as well as strategies for observing specific phenomena like galaxies and nebulae. It’s an excellent resource for hobbyists and aspiring astronomers who want to take their knowledge to the next level.

Universe Today Podcast

Universe Today needs no introduction for seasoned space enthusiasts. The Universe Today Podcast expands on the website’s in-depth articles, bringing experts and journalists together to discuss the latest developments in astronomy and space science. Host Fraser Cain asks insightful questions that help dissect complex topics like space weather, interstellar travel, and the discovery of potentially habitable planets. The podcast’s conversational tone and timely topics ensure each episode is informative and relevant to ongoing research and exploration efforts.

SpaceTime with Stuart Gary

Australian journalist Stuart Gary delivers one of the most comprehensive astronomy and space science podcasts available. SpaceTime covers breaking news, discoveries, and in-depth explanations of celestial phenomena. With episodes released several times a week, the podcast has up-to-date coverage of subjects ranging from the James Webb Space Telescope’s latest findings to developments in rocket technology. Gary’s style is both engaging and authoritative, making complex science digestible without oversimplifying it.

Deep Astronomy

With a mission of making astronomy accessible and engaging, Deep Astronomy covers a wide range of topics, from telescopes and observational techniques to the search for alien life. Host Tony Darnell shares his expertise in astrophotography and space sciences through laid-back yet insightful discussions. The podcast occasionally includes guest experts who provide their perspectives on cutting-edge research, making it a great way to learn more about the tools and methods used to study the cosmos.

Why Space Matters

Host Hakeem Oluseyi’s passion for astronomy shines through every episode of Why Space Matters. This podcast bridges the gap between astronomy and societal impact, exploring how space technology influences various aspects of human life—from communications to climate monitoring. Episodes often highlight the human ingenuity behind groundbreaking missions and emphasize the interconnectedness of science and humanity. This unique perspective makes the podcast a refreshing alternative for listeners interested in the real-world applications of astronomical advances.

Cosmic Vertigo

If you’re drawn to the mind-bending concepts of astrophysics, Cosmic Vertigo will keep you coming back for more. Hosted by physicists Dr. Amanda Bauer and Dr. Alan Duffy, the podcast explores dizzying topics like dark energy, light-speed travel, and the multiverse. The duo’s palpable enthusiasm for science is contagious, offering listeners a blend of technical insights and fun discussions. Episodes often include thought experiments and “what-if” scenarios, sparking curiosity and encouraging a deeper engagement with astronomy.

Looking Up with Allan Chapman

For a historical take on astronomy, Looking Up with Allan Chapman is an excellent choice. Hosted by historian, author, and astronomer Allan Chapman, this podcast dives into the stories behind pivotal figures and discoveries in astronomy. Topics range from Galileo’s groundbreaking observations to the development of modern space telescopes. Chapman’s captivating storytelling and deep knowledge of astronomy’s past make this podcast a fascinating exploration of how science evolves over time.

Finding the right astronomy podcast can transform your commute, workouts, or downtime into immersive journeys through the cosmos. Each podcast on this list brings its unique flavor, whether through its storytelling, technical insights, or philosophical musings. So, grab your headphones, pick one from the list, and prepare to marvel at the wonders of the universe—from black holes to distant galaxies—right from the comfort of your daily routine. Who knows? You might just find a new favorite way to stargaze, one episode at a time.
