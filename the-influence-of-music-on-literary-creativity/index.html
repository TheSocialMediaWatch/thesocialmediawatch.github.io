The Influence of Music on Literary Creativity


Music and literature, the two art forms have a long and intertwined history. They are intertwined in the depths of human experiences. As we’re captivated by the rhythm of a symphony or the melodious beats of a song, we also find ourselves immersed in the constructive power of words.

For authors, music plays an essential role in inspiring and fueling creative thoughts. Like painters who find a muse in their songs, authors also connect with music to create a narrative.

Literature and music are often seen as two different languages of art. But, there’s more than meets the eye. Music interweaves itself in literature, impacting creative writing techniques, providing inspiration, and contributing to developing characters. Moreover, music shapes and defines the atmosphere and mood of fictional worlds.

The Inspiration

Music shapes our thoughts, frames our perspectives and stirs emotions. It plays a significant part in the journey of “where to start?” when we embark on creating a new piece of work. Authors use music as a launchpad to ignite the creative spark.

Ray Bradbury, renowned author of Fahrenheit 451, said, “Werians” write to music. For him, writing was all about rhythm, pace, and lyrics. The inspiring melodies of Aaron Copland’s “Appalachian Spring” spurred the creation of Bradbury’s magnum opus.

The piano music of Frédéric Chopin has inspired writers and poets for centuries. Authors like Edgar Allan Poe and Robert Schumann found solace and inspiration in Chopin’s pieces. Schumann even named his novel, “Dichtergarten” (‘Poets’ Garden’) after his love and appreciation for Chopin’s music.

Even modern authors find solace in music. Stephen King incorporates hard rock music, finding the thumping beats producing adrenaline that fuels his narratives. Stephenie Meyer of the “Twilight” series fame admits that music played a defining role in creating the brooding atmosphere of Forks, Washington.

Creating Characters

Characters are the heart and soul of literature. They embody our stories, carrying our worlds and weaving our plots. Music punches life into these characers, shaping and molding them.

Music transcends the tangible, making characters multidimensional. It adds depth, making readers believe in their humanity. Look at the character of James Brown in Richard Pryor’s semi-autobiographical film ‘Harlem Nights’. Brown’s love for, and mastery of, music created an iconic character.

The Atmosphere and Mood

Gatsby’s world was framed by the jazzy tones of the roaring era. The dark and mysterious world of Sherlock Holmes was interlaced with the Victorian era’s somber tunes. Music sets the mood and atmosphere for these literary worlds, and enhances immersion.

Books have been set to music, in what we call Music Literature. A prime example is Pink Floyd’s musical adaptation of “The Wall” by Alan Parker. This masterpiece not only fed visual storytelling but also touched the audience emotionally with its auditory melody.

In literature, music can create the perfect atmosphere, just as a peacock’s shimmering feathers portend its beauty. Similarly, in music literature, the right melody can haunt listeners with the chilling tones of a disturbing narrative.

Music’s impact on literary creativity lies in its power to influence and be influenced by it. Its role is multi-faceted, ranging from inspiring to character-building and world-shaping. We see music as a magical wand in an author’s hand, enhancing the immersive power of literature.

Conclusion

Don’t think of music as something that simply ‘is’. Think of it as a spectrum, a game changer, an inspiration. With the right atmosphere, it can alter our perception, take us to the world of imagination, and frame the characters of our creation.

Never underestimate its influence. For music is the background score to the movie that is your imagination. This soundtrack changes from Author, to Reader and then the creation. So, when looking to understand the influence of music on literary creativity, we understand our very essence.
