The Thrill of Deep Dropping: Exploring New Depths


Imagine yourself sunken into the world’s most vibrant and mysterious depths, your fishing line stretching out like a lifeline. This intoxicating experience is what deep dropping is all about. Let’s dive into the thrilling world of deep-sea fishing.

What is Deep-Dropping?

Deep dropping is an advanced form of fishing that takes place far out into the ocean. It calls for tying hooks together on long steel or bronze clips. These clusters are dropped into the abyss, typically in areas rich in nutrients around underwater cliffs known as ledges or at sea mounts. These baited bombs impact the sea floor, causing any living creature in the area to take notice, a technique often called ‘jump shooting.’

The Gear

Anglers use high-powered tackles to cast their lines in the deep blue, sometimes measured from hundreds of feet to well over a thousand feet down. Reels need to have the requisite strength and weight to handle the prey and the depth they’re dropped to. Over time, more advanced technology has been incorporated, with underwater cameras allowing anglers to monitor their bait thereby increasing accuracy and efficiency.

Depth & Pressure - Surviving the Deep

In the deep sea, pressure increases by one atmosphere for every 10 meters of depth, which means the angler needs to bear these overwhelming pressures. Oxygen plays a crucial role, and divers can’t descend below 200 feet without being tethered to a specialized submersible supply of air. Persistence pays off because each new depth reveals marine life never before seen.

Common Deep-sea Species Caught

Deep dropping helps catch some of the rarest and most sought-after deep-sea fish. The most popular are the eminent Bluefin tuna, Grouper, and Snapper. The wily stripedmarlin and Yellowfin tuna, though notoriously elusive, are also fighters worth the pursuit. Swordfish are known for their agility and speed and are ferocious when hooked. Each of these creatures displays unique baritone behaviors in the depths that contribute to the allure.

Depths and Periods of Day and Night

Deep dropping can be done in daylight or nighttime. The game changes as the sun sets. Creatures change as some predators become active. Sharks, for example, are nocturnal and frequent deep waters to hunt, making nighttime deep dropping particularly thrilling.

Techniques of Deep Dropping

Anglers employ various techniques, but most use a drop shot rig, allowing them to keep their bait at a specific depth. One technique involves setting multiple hooks on a clam or sea urchin clamp and dropping it on an underwater ledge, creating a smorgasbord for lurking predators.

Deep dropping is a tricky, old-world technique. As more people strive to get closer to the water, to earthquakes under the sea, this niche section of angling offers limitless excitement, the size and substance of which most will ever dare to comprehend.

The appeal of dropping deep isn’t merely about the fish but the mystery and awe associated with the unknown. This extreme version of fishing isn’t for the timid; it takes a patient soul willing to brave the dark and unknown waters. It’s an invitation for the bold to explore depths where only a few venture, a challenge where one can be cast into a battlefield of giants unseen, each pull from the deep echelons being an epic struggle of survival.
