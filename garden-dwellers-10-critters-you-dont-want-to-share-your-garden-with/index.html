Garden Dwellers: 10 Critters you Don't Want to Share Your Garden With


Welcome to our lovely little garden sanctuary! A place filled with fragrant flower beds, lush green grass, and the sweet sound of birds chirping in the trees. However, nature can sometimes be a little too giving, and those adorable critters can turn into unwelcome garden dwellers. It’s an unfortunate truth that these small animals, while perhaps cute in appearance, can wreak havoc on your prized green space. To help you become more aware of the not-so-fun aspects of sharing your garden, here is a list of 10 critters you might not want as houseguests.

  1. Slugs and Snails

Who could resist those chubby little bodies with their spiral shells? Unfortunately, they are quite the delicacies for both birds and our own culinary adventures. In your garden, however, they are notorious for eating anything soft and leafy. This secret snacking habit can leave your beautiful vegetation wilted and damaged overnight, making these gastropods some of the most infuriating garden dwellers.

  1. Rabbits

With their soft fur and fluffy tails, rabbits often graze gently on our gardens’ edges, seemingly posing no threat to our precious plants. But beware! These frisky critters will happily munch on tulips, roses, and even root vegetables if left unchecked. Their burrows can also cause damage to the structural integrity of your garden beds, and that cute cuddly creature turns into an unwelcome and destructive guest.

  1. Deer

Graceful and majestic creatures, deer might just be the quintessential garden dwellers that we all wish to avoid. Deer are persistent eaters and won’t hesitate to nibble on any plant they fancy. Your delicate hydrangeas might look delicious by day, and by dawn, you’ll be mourning their absence. Deer can be particularly difficult to deter and might require costly measures to keep their distance.

  1. Squirrels

Those bushy-tailed acrobats we adore in children’s stories do not fare so well in our gardens. Squirrels have a penchant for digging up seedlings, burying their newfound treasures right in your flower beds, and nibbling on freshly planted bulbs. They can be incredibly agile, making them quite sneaky in disruptions.

  1. Caterpillars

As hosts to the beautiful butterflies, caterpillars are a natural part of the ecosystem. However, their voracious appetites can sometimes be troubling for gardeners. Egg-laying female moths can ravage your garden, defoliating trees, and leaving plants vulnerable to disease. While supporting the life cycle of butterflies is admirable, keeping them out of certain sections of your garden might be necessary.

  1. Chinch Bugs

These true bugs are notorious for wreaking havoc on lawns and landscaping. During hot summer months, chinch bugs feed on turf grass, causing it to yellow and die. Unlike other garden dwellers, these mighty insects leave the mature grass alive but completely bare, creating an unsightly brown patch that can snowball into a full-blown infestation.

  1. Ladybugs

Ah, such elegant creatures, right? But appearances can be deceiving. While it’s pleasant to have ladybugs around, they tend to feast on other insects that might do more damage to your plants. They can be destructive to certain crops such as spider plants, leaving a path of carnage behind them. Ladybugs are more than capable of annihilating even the most diligent pupae in their pursuit for their next tasty morsel.

  1. Groundhogs

Renowned for their voracious appetites and burrowing capabilities, groundhogs can damage flower beds, gardens, and even furniture. They will munch on anything from ornamental plants to cherished vegetables with a complete disregard for property value.

  1. Voles

Surface-casting pests, voles can wreak havoc on your garden by munching on plant roots, stems, and leaves. These nocturnal critters are often mistaken for mice but are distinct in their cone-shaped noses and small needle-like teeth that give them their characteristic nicknames “field mice.”

  1. Raccoons

They may be adorable, amicable characters in cartoons, but trust us when we say raccoons are not your garden’s best friends. They have a knack for rummaging through pots, scattering seeds, and entirely upturning your hard work in search of grubs and tasty morsels hidden beneath the soil.

Having the pleasure of a well-manicured garden can sometimes be less “pleasure” and more of a “challenge” when it comes to sharing it with these undiscovered garden wreckers. Here’s to hoping this list helps protect your green oasis from the unwelcome guests that might try to make it their home! Happy gardening!
