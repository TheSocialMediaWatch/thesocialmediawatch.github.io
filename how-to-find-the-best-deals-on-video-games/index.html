How to Find the Best Deals on Video Games


Video games have become a significant part of modern entertainment, and with their growing popularity, finding the best deals on video games can be a rewarding pursuit for savvy gamers. Whether you are looking for the latest AAA titles or seeking hidden gems from indie developers, there are various methods you can use to save money while still enjoying your gaming experience. In this guide, we’ll explore proven strategies, tips, and resources to help you discover the best deals on video games.

1. Take Advantage of Digital Game Stores

Online Marketplaces and Discounts

One of the most effective ways to find video game deals is by keeping an eye on digital game stores. These online platforms often offer significant discounts during sales events or promotions, making it possible to grab games for a fraction of the regular price. Popular digital game marketplaces include:

  • Steam: Known for its seasonal sales, Steam regularly offers discounts on popular titles and indie games. During events like the Summer Sale, Winter Sale, and Black Friday Sale, discounts can reach as high as 90%, offering a great opportunity to stock up on games at unbeatable prices.

  • PlayStation Store: For those who own a PlayStation console, the PlayStation Store often offers discounts on both full-price games and digital bundles. Additionally, subscribing to PlayStation Plus can unlock exclusive discounts and free games every month.

  • Xbox Store: Xbox users can enjoy weekly sales, seasonal discounts, and price cuts on bundles. Subscribing to Xbox Game Pass also gives access to a library of games, helping you try out new titles without committing to full purchases.

  • Nintendo eShop: Nintendo’s digital store is known for offering sales on both first-party and third-party games. With a Nintendo Switch, you can take advantage of seasonal discounts and limited-time offers on titles ranging from big franchises to indie games.

Subscriptions for Digital Libraries

Digital libraries have become a great alternative to traditional game purchases. Services such as Xbox Game Pass, PlayStation Now, and EA Play give gamers access to a vast library of games for a monthly or annual subscription fee. These services often include newer titles as well as older classics, making it a cost-effective option for gamers who want to play multiple games without breaking the bank.

  • Xbox Game Pass: A subscription service that allows access to hundreds of games on both console and PC, with new titles being added regularly. You can even access upcoming releases on day one, which makes it an excellent way to explore games that would otherwise cost a lot.

  • PlayStation Now: Similar to Xbox Game Pass, PlayStation Now offers a subscription model that grants access to hundreds of PlayStation games. Additionally, new releases are available on the service, making it a good option for Sony fans looking for a broader selection of games.

  • EA Play: EA Play offers early access to EA’s new releases as well as a selection of classic titles. While not as expansive as Game Pass or PlayStation Now, it’s a great choice for fans of EA sports games or franchises like Battlefield, The Sims, and Mass Effect.

2. Keep an Eye on Seasonal and Flash Sales

Sales events have become a staple of the gaming industry, with digital and physical retailers alike offering discounts during key sales periods. Some sales are seasonal, while others are short-term flash sales that can last for a few days or even hours. Here’s how you can make the most of these sales:

Seasonal Sales

  • Black Friday and Cyber Monday: These sales events are synonymous with discounts across nearly every category, including video games. Both online stores and brick-and-mortar retailers drop prices on games, consoles, and accessories, making it the perfect time to score significant savings.

  • Summer and Winter Sales: Many platforms, such as Steam, Epic Games Store, and GOG, hold annual summer and winter sales that offer massive discounts on a wide range of games. It’s worth waiting for these sales to get titles that may have been out for a while, as the discounts can often go up to 75% or more.

  • Holiday Sales: Major holidays like Christmas, New Year’s, and Easter are often marked by gaming sales. Be sure to check in on both digital and physical stores as these periods approach.

Flash Sales

Flash sales are typically short-lived events that last for only a few hours or days. These sales offer deep discounts on a limited selection of games. To ensure you don’t miss out on a deal, sign up for newsletters or follow official accounts on social media for notifications.

Many game retailers, like Humble Bundle and Green Man Gaming, regularly hold flash sales, providing discounts on games that might otherwise be full-price. Checking daily during these events can help you grab the best deals before they expire.

3. Explore Bundle Deals

Humble Bundle

Humble Bundle is an excellent resource for finding great deals on video games. Their model offers bundles where you can pay what you want for a group of games, with a portion of the payment going to charity. Often, Humble Bundles include AAA titles and indie games, so it’s worth checking back frequently to see what’s available.

The key benefit of Humble Bundle is that you can pay more than the average price to unlock additional games or choose the games that interest you the most. Plus, you get Steam keys for most of the titles, which means you can play them on your Steam library.

Fanatical and Bundle Stars

Other websites, like Fanatical and Bundle Stars, offer similar game bundles. These sites frequently offer curated bundles of games at heavily discounted prices, including many well-regarded games. While the selection may not be as broad as Humble Bundle, the deals are still fantastic, especially for collectors or players looking to explore new genres.

4. Use Price Tracking Websites and Apps

Price tracking tools can help you monitor the price of specific games and receive alerts when they go on sale. These services save you time by tracking the price history of games, allowing you to make informed purchasing decisions.

  • CheapShark: CheapShark tracks the prices of games across several digital game stores, including Steam, Green Man Gaming, and others. You can set alerts to notify you when a game drops below a certain price point.

  • IsThereAnyDeal: IsThereAnyDeal is another popular site that offers price tracking and alerts for video game deals. It pulls data from various digital storefronts and helps you find the best prices on a wide variety of titles.

Browser Extensions

Many browser extensions can automatically find the best deals on video games while you browse. These tools often integrate with major game stores, helping you to see discounts and price drops in real-time. Some of the most popular extensions include:

  • Honey: This browser extension helps you find promo codes and discounts while shopping online, including for video games. Honey automatically applies any available discounts at checkout, ensuring that you never miss out on a deal.

  • Keepa: Keepa is a price tracking extension primarily for Amazon, but it can also track game prices on other retailers. It allows you to track historical prices and set alerts for games you want to purchase.

5. Buy Used Games or Physical Copies

For gamers who prefer physical copies or want to explore older titles, buying used games can be a great way to save money. You can often find great deals on pre-owned games at your local game stores, pawn shops, or online marketplaces like eBay or Craigslist.

  • GameStop: GameStop is known for its pre-owned game section, where you can find older games at discounted prices. The quality of used games is generally very good, and you can sometimes trade in your old games for store credit toward new purchases.

  • eBay: eBay is a great platform for finding used games, especially older or rare titles. Many sellers offer auctions or “Buy It Now” options, so you can often snag a deal by bidding strategically.

  • Pawn Shops and Thrift Stores: Don’t overlook local pawn shops or thrift stores. They often have hidden gems that you can buy at a fraction of the price, especially when it comes to older game titles.

6. Follow Gaming Communities and Social Media

Being part of the gaming community can also help you discover deals and discounts. Many gaming communities, subreddits, and forums regularly post information about sales, promotions, and bundles.

  • Reddit: Subreddits like r/GameDeals and r/DealAlert are great resources for discovering the latest discounts and offers. Members frequently share deals from various retailers, allowing you to find discounts that you might have missed otherwise.

  • Social Media: Follow gaming companies and digital storefronts on platforms like Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram. Many game developers and stores announce flash sales and limited-time discounts on these platforms, so staying connected will help you act fast.

Conclusion

Finding the best deals on video games requires a combination of strategies, including staying informed about sales events, using digital stores, subscribing to game libraries, and leveraging tools like price tracking websites and apps. Whether you’re looking for the latest releases, exploring bundles, or hunting for second-hand games, the opportunities for savings are abundant. By being proactive and using the right resources, you can build an impressive game collection without spending a fortune. Happy gaming!
