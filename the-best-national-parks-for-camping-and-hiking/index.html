The Best National Parks for Camping and Hiking


National parks are some of the most cherished outdoor destinations in the world, offering diverse landscapes, rich ecosystems, and unparalleled opportunities for adventure. For those who love camping and hiking, national parks are an oasis where nature’s beauty can be explored and experienced up close. Whether you are an avid hiker or someone who enjoys a peaceful camping retreat under the stars, national parks provide the ideal environment. Here are some of the best national parks for camping and hiking that will undoubtedly leave you with unforgettable memories.

1. Yosemite National Park, California

Yosemite National Park is a classic American destination, famous for its towering granite cliffs, ancient giant sequoias, and dramatic waterfalls. Located in California’s Sierra Nevada, Yosemite is a hiker’s paradise with over 750 miles of trails. Whether you’re an experienced mountaineer or a beginner looking for a casual stroll, Yosemite offers trails for all skill levels.

Camping in Yosemite

Yosemite boasts 13 campgrounds, each providing a unique experience, from rustic tent camping to more developed options with modern amenities. The most popular campgrounds are located near Yosemite Valley, where you can wake up with stunning views of iconic landmarks like El Capitan and Half Dome. For a more remote experience, head to the Tuolumne Meadows Campground in the high Sierra.

Best Hikes in Yosemite

  • Half Dome: One of the most iconic hikes in the world, Half Dome requires permits and offers a challenging yet rewarding ascent.
  • Mist Trail: A shorter but strenuous hike that leads to Vernal Fall and Nevada Fall, providing stunning views and a refreshing mist from the waterfalls.
  • Glacier Point to Little Yosemite Valley: This hike provides access to the summit of Half Dome, with breathtaking views of Yosemite Valley below.

Why Yosemite?

Yosemite is renowned not only for its natural beauty but also for its excellent infrastructure for camping and hiking. The park is well-maintained, and there are numerous services available to help you make the most of your visit. Whether you’re staying for a weekend or a week, the combination of scenic trails and world-class campsites makes it a top destination for outdoor enthusiasts.

2. Grand Canyon National Park, Arizona

The Grand Canyon is a geological wonder that offers some of the most dramatic landscapes on Earth. The canyon’s immense size and vibrant colors create a sense of awe that draws visitors from around the globe. Hiking in the Grand Canyon is an experience unlike any other, with trails that descend into the canyon’s depths or lead to overlooks with sweeping vistas of the canyon.

Camping in the Grand Canyon

There are several campgrounds within the Grand Canyon, with the most popular being the Mather Campground located in the South Rim. This campground offers both tent and RV sites, and its location makes it ideal for accessing many of the park’s hiking trails. For those looking for a more remote camping experience, the North Rim Campground provides a quieter, more secluded environment.

Best Hikes in the Grand Canyon

  • Bright Angel Trail: A challenging but rewarding hike that descends from the South Rim to the Colorado River. It’s one of the most popular trails, offering incredible views as you descend.
  • South Kaibab Trail: Known for its panoramic views, this trail provides a quicker descent into the canyon and is perfect for day hikes.
  • Rim-to-Rim: This multi-day hike is for the experienced adventurer, crossing from the South Rim to the North Rim, with overnight camping along the way.

Why Grand Canyon?

The Grand Canyon offers an unparalleled combination of breathtaking scenery, challenging hikes, and memorable camping experiences. Hiking here provides a sense of accomplishment that’s hard to match, with the rugged beauty of the canyon serving as your constant companion. Whether you’re looking for a short day hike or a multi-day adventure, the Grand Canyon delivers an unforgettable experience.

3. Zion National Park, Utah

Zion National Park, with its towering sandstone cliffs, narrow canyons, and diverse ecosystems, is one of the most iconic parks in the American Southwest. The park’s unique geography creates a paradise for hikers, offering everything from easy walks to adrenaline-pumping climbs.

Camping in Zion

Zion’s South Campground, located near the park’s main entrance, is the most accessible camping option. The Watchman Campground, located just outside the main entrance, offers beautiful views of the surrounding cliffs and is a great option for those looking to be close to the action. For a more rustic experience, consider camping at Kolob Canyons, which offers a quieter, less crowded atmosphere.

Best Hikes in Zion

  • Angels Landing: One of Zion’s most famous and thrilling hikes, Angels Landing takes you to a breathtaking overlook with sheer drops on both sides. It’s a challenging hike that requires permits but offers a rewarding experience for those brave enough to complete it.
  • The Narrows: This hike takes you through the Virgin River, where you wade and occasionally swim through the water while surrounded by towering canyon walls. It’s an unforgettable adventure and a must-do when visiting Zion.
  • Observation Point: Offering one of the best views of Zion Canyon, this trail is a great alternative to Angels Landing, providing a less crowded but equally stunning perspective.

Why Zion?

Zion offers a range of hiking experiences, from moderate walks to challenging climbs, making it a great destination for hikers of all abilities. The park’s unique geology and the ability to hike through rivers and up sheer rock faces provide an experience that’s unlike any other national park. Zion is also known for its excellent shuttle system, making it easy to access trailheads without worrying about parking.

4. Rocky Mountain National Park, Colorado

Nestled in the heart of the Colorado Rockies, Rocky Mountain National Park is a haven for those who enjoy hiking at high altitudes. With over 350 miles of trails, visitors can explore the park’s stunning alpine lakes, towering peaks, and expansive meadows.

Camping in Rocky Mountain

Rocky Mountain National Park offers several campgrounds, including the Moraine Park Campground and the Glacier Basin Campground. Both are located in the park’s main area and provide access to some of the best hiking trails. For those seeking a more remote camping experience, the Wild Basin Campground offers a quieter, less developed atmosphere.

Best Hikes in Rocky Mountain

  • Longs Peak: A challenging hike to one of Colorado’s most iconic summits, Longs Peak is a strenuous trek that requires planning and preparation, but the summit views are worth the effort.
  • Bear Lake to Emerald Lake: This popular trail offers breathtaking views of alpine lakes and the surrounding peaks, making it a must-do for visitors with limited time.
  • Alpine Ridge Trail: A short but steep hike that takes you to the highest point in the park accessible by trail, offering stunning panoramic views of the surrounding mountains.

Why Rocky Mountain?

Rocky Mountain National Park’s combination of rugged peaks, alpine lakes, and diverse wildlife makes it one of the most scenic parks in the United States. The park’s high-altitude trails offer a unique hiking experience, with varying degrees of difficulty to accommodate all levels of hikers. The cool mountain air and stunning vistas provide an ideal setting for both camping and hiking.

5. Olympic National Park, Washington

Olympic National Park is a diverse and breathtaking park located on the Olympic Peninsula in Washington. From lush rainforests and wild coastline to snow-capped peaks and alpine lakes, Olympic offers an incredible range of ecosystems to explore. It’s a paradise for those who love both hiking and camping in varied landscapes.

Camping in Olympic

Olympic National Park offers numerous campgrounds, with options ranging from primitive sites to more developed areas. The Kalaloch Campground, located along the Pacific Coast, offers stunning views of the ocean, while the Sol Duc Hot Springs Campground provides access to natural hot springs and great hiking trails.

Best Hikes in Olympic

  • Hoh River Trail: This iconic trail takes you through the lush Hoh Rain Forest, where the towering trees and verdant undergrowth create an otherworldly atmosphere.
  • Hurricane Ridge: A short but rewarding hike that provides panoramic views of the Olympic Mountains and the Strait of Juan de Fuca. It’s a great option for those with limited time.
  • Sol Duc Hot Springs: Hike to the natural hot springs where you can relax after a long day of hiking.

Why Olympic?

Olympic National Park stands out due to its diverse ecosystems and incredible range of outdoor experiences. Whether you prefer the serenity of hiking through a temperate rainforest or the challenge of summiting a mountain peak, Olympic has something to offer. The park’s remote location and rich biodiversity make it one of the most unique places to camp and hike in the United States.

6. Acadia National Park, Maine

Acadia National Park, located on Mount Desert Island in Maine, is one of the most beautiful and accessible parks in the northeastern United States. With its rugged coastline, scenic vistas, and charming New England atmosphere, Acadia is a perfect destination for those who love both hiking and camping.

Camping in Acadia

Acadia offers several campgrounds, with Blackwoods Campground being the most popular. It’s located near the park’s main entrance and provides access to many of the park’s trails and attractions. Seawall Campground, located along the coast, is another great option for those looking for a more secluded experience.

Best Hikes in Acadia

  • Precipice Trail: A challenging and exhilarating climb that takes you to the top of Champlain Mountain. The trail involves scrambling up steep rock faces and provides incredible views of the surrounding landscape.
  • Jordan Pond Path: A relatively easy hike around the picturesque Jordan Pond, with stunning views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Cadillac Mountain: The highest point on the East Coast, Cadillac Mountain offers both a challenging hike and an incredible view, especially at sunrise.

Why Acadia?

Acadia National Park’s combination of coastal beauty and mountainous terrain makes it a unique destination for outdoor lovers. The park offers a range of hiking experiences, from easy walks to more challenging climbs. Its proximity to the charming town of Bar Harbor also means that visitors can enjoy both the serenity of nature and the quaint atmosphere of a coastal town.

Each of these national parks offers a unique outdoor experience, blending natural beauty with excellent opportunities for hiking and camping. Whether you prefer towering cliffs, alpine lakes, dense forests, or rugged coastlines, these parks provide diverse landscapes that cater to a variety of tastes. The rich ecosystems, breathtaking views, and the freedom of the outdoors make these national parks not just places to visit, but destinations that will stay with you long after your adventure ends. If you’re looking for the best places to camp and hike, these national parks should be at the top of your list.
