Ethics of Care: Interpersonal Relationships and Moral Philosophy


Many of us have been taught, and subsequently, learn instinctively, how to navigate and establish healthy interpersonal relationships. From our family dynamics and friendships to our romantic partnerships and professional networks, we develop various skill sets in communication, trustbuilding, and conflict resolution. While we may understand in our hearts the value of these activities, it is crucial to connect these everyday experiences to our broader understanding of moral philosophy, specifically, the “Ethics of Care”.

Firstly, it is pivotal to dissipate the misguided notion that the ‘Ethics of Care’ is strictly related to or interchangeable with empathy. It’s undoubtedly an integral component but encompassing a broader perspective, revolving around responsibility, accountability, and, most importantly, action.

Historically, the ‘Ethics of Care’ emerged as a response to the inadequacy of traditional and dominantly patriarchal moral theories, which wilfully overlooked, or worse, took for granted the emotional labor and caretaking acts performed predominantly by women. As such, the ‘Ethics of Care’ emerged to offer a more holistic moral framework.

Appreciating the interconnections of people, utilizing an expanded understanding of relationships beyond the dyad (two people), and placing emphasis on context are hallmarks of the Ethics of Care. The concept promotes a focus on individuals’ interconnectedness, community and societal implications, rather than narrowly focusing on individual choice or action.

How Does It Relate To Interpersonal Relationships?

Let’s delve into applying the ‘Ethics of Care’ to typical interpersonal relationships:

  1. Collaboration and Partnership: The philosophy advocates and precisely embraces the concept of equality and mutual support, especially in the realm of collaboration. This might consist of teamwork at the office or any other place, where irrespective of our positions, we are engaged in shared processes.

  2. Empathy and Stockholm Syndrome: The resonance of care ethics can create an atmosphere of empathy, notwithstanding the initial animosity that might exist among parties in conflict. This might deviate from the prevalent understanding of empathy and could signify identifying with the feelings, circumstances, and perspectives of the other party, even if they may be considered hostile.

  3. Resilience and Vulnerability: The ‘Ethics of Care’ imbues in us a sense of resilience and fortitude to cope with conflicts or distress, which, according to it, are inevitable incidents in the path of our relationships. It instills a deeper understanding that sometimes, it’s okay not to be okay.

  4. Diversity of Relationship Dynamics: The scope of ‘Ethics of Care’ extends beyond coincidence sections or bonds that require a shared experience or circumstance. It includes acquaintances, coworkers, neighbors, and even politicians. The recognition that every relationship has its significance and impact on society is another element at its crux.

Incorporating ‘Ethics of Care’ into our understanding of relationships and moral philosophy isn’t just an intellectual exercise. It alters our human behavior, challenging traditional roles, steeping us in empathy, resilience, and often, dismantling the oppressive hierarchies, on which we have built our society.

In a nutshell, ‘Ethics of Care’ pushes us to redefine fundamentals in a changing world, reshaping our understanding of interpersonal relationships and Moral Philosophy, which are otherwise assumed to be trained and preserved within rigid boundaries.

As such, going forward, let’s consider embedding these principles in our daily lives, examining the dynamics which nourish healthy relationships, helping us regain balance and cohesion wherever we find ourselves in this labyrinth of existence – be it a family situation, a tumultuous team situation, or just appreciating our barista’s brewed artistry. It’s not a simple task, but envisioning it through the ‘Ethics of Care’ frame could illuminate the path.
