The Best Cars for Dog Owners


Choosing the perfect car as a dog owner involves more than just style, performance, or fuel economy. It’s about creating an environment suited for your canine companion—a space where they can travel comfortably, safely, and happily. Whether you’re planning short trips to the park or long cross-country journeys, the right vehicle can make all the difference for both you and your dog.

In this comprehensive guide, we’ll explore what features matter most for dog owners and highlight some of the best cars on the market that excel in accommodating your four-legged friends. We’ll cover how interior space, ease of entry, cargo design, safety features, and climate control contribute to a pet-friendly automotive experience.

What Makes a Car Ideal for Dog Owners?

Before diving into specific models, let’s discuss the qualities that make a vehicle a top choice for dog owners:

Ample Interior and Cargo Space

Dogs need room to stretch out, change position, or even lie down comfortably on car rides. Spacious interiors and large cargo areas are invaluable, especially for large breeds or multiple dogs. Hatchbacks, SUVs, and wagons usually provide more usable space than sedans.

Easy Access and Low Step-In Height

Getting a dog in and out of the car should be simple to prevent injuries or discomfort. Vehicles with low cargo floors or wide-opening rear doors make access easier, particularly for older dogs or breeds prone to joint issues.

Durable and Waterproof Materials

Dogs can be messy. From shedding fur to muddy paws or accidental spills, a car with scratch-resistant, easily cleanable seats and floors can save you time and preserve the vehicle’s interior.

Climate Control and Ventilation

Temperature regulation prevents overheating or chills, which can be harmful to pets. Ventilated seats, rear air vents, and rear windows that can be lowered independently help maintain a comfortable environment.

Safety Features Specific to Pets

While public safety features protect passengers, dog owners often emulate that level of care for their pets. Features like built-in pet barriers in SUVs, tether points to secure dog harnesses, or flat cargo floors to transport crates add significant value.

Top Dog-Friendly Cars in 2024

Keeping all these factors in mind, here are some of the best vehicles that pet owners swear by:

1. Subaru Outback

The Subaru Outback consistently ranks as one of the best vehicles for dog owners. Its spacious interior and large cargo area with a low lift-over height make it easy for dogs to jump in and out. The rear seats fold flat to extend cargo space, perfect for crates or dog beds.

Subaru’s symmetrical all-wheel-drive also ensures stable rides on various terrains, ideal for adventurous pet owners who often explore nature trails with their dogs. With durable upholstery options and standard rear seat vents, your dog’s ride can remain comfortable and clean.

Key Perks for Dogs:

  • Low cargo floor
  • Flat-folding rear seats
  • Rear air vents
  • Optional pet barrier

2. Honda CR-V

This compact SUV impresses with a roomy cabin and versatile cargo area. The Honda CR-V offers wide-opening rear doors and a flat load floor, easing the loading of dogs and gear. The rear seats can recline for added comfort, making longer trips enjoyable.

Interior materials resist stains, and the cabin features lower noise levels, ensuring a less stressful environment for nervous dogs. The CR-V’s efficient climate control system includes rear vents that help maintain temperature consistency.

Highlights:

  • Spacious rear cargo area
  • Easy pet access
  • Rear climate control vents
  • Quiet cabin

3. Volvo V90 Cross Country

For a premium option, the Volvo V90 Cross Country excels in safety and space. Volvos are renowned for their top-notch safety ratings, and the V90 Cross Country is no exception. This wagon provides a large and flat cargo space, useful for harnessed crates or dog beds.

The rear seats fold nearly flat, expanding room for your dog to relax. The vehicle also offers built-in seat Anchors compatible with many dog harnesses and carriers. Thanks to triple-zone climate control and rear windows offering a dog-friendly breeze, this vehicle is a luxury haven for pets.

What Dog Owners Appreciate:

  • Flat, spacious cargo area
  • Advanced safety features
  • Seat tether points for pet harnesses
  • Multi-zone climate control

4. Jeep Wrangler Unlimited

If your dog thrives on outdoor adventures, the Jeep Wrangler Unlimited offers rugged capability with high dog-friendliness. The four-door model provides ample rear seat space, and rear windows open wide, delivering fresh air.

The removable roof and doors let your dog experience outdoor sensations safely when stationary, and the cargo space, though smaller than a wagon’s, suits medium breeds well. The durable materials can handle mud, dirt, and water, making post-adventure clean-up easier.

Ideal for:

  • Outdoor-loving dogs
  • Ventilated airflow options
  • Easy to clean interiors
  • Rugged durability

5. Tesla Model Y

Electric vehicles might not be the first things that come to mind for dog owners, but the Tesla Model Y delivers a surprising blend of pet-friendly features. It has a spacious interior with a flat floor, making space for dogs to stretch out.

The panoramic glass roof bathes the cabin in natural light, which some dogs find soothing. Tesla’s robust automatic climate control ensures optimal temperatures on the go, including the ability to remotely pre-cool or heat the car before you and your pet enter.

Notable for:

  • Flat floor and roomy interior
  • Remote climate pre-conditioning
  • Spacious rear doors
  • Easy to clean synthetic seating materials

Additional Tips for Traveling with Your Dog

Selecting the right car is only one piece of the puzzle. To ensure safe and comfortable journeys with your dog, consider these additional practices:

  • Use harnesses or crates: Secure your dog with a vehicle-approved harness attached to seat belts or place them inside well-ventilated travel crates to prevent distractions or injuries.
  • Protect seats and floors: Invest in waterproof seat covers or cargo liners to guard against fur, scratches, or spills.
  • Pack essentials: Bring water, a travel bowl, toys, and waste bags. Frequent stops for hydration and exercise are beneficial.
  • Temperature checks: Never leave your dog alone in the car, especially during hot or cold weather.
  • Window safety: Use pet sunshades or keep windows partially open (where safe) to provide ventilation without risking escape.

Conclusion: Prioritize Your Dog’s Comfort and Safety on the Road

Finding the perfect car as a dog owner is about merging practical features with your lifestyle needs. Whether you’re hauling a Great Dane across states or taking a quick trip with a small pup to the vet, vehicles like the Subaru Outback, Honda CR-V, Volvo V90 Cross Country, Jeep Wrangler Unlimited, and Tesla Model Y offer unique combinations of space, safety, and convenience tailored for pets.

Investing in the right car means more enjoyable, stress-free travels for both you and your beloved dog. After all, every adventure is better when shared with your best friend by your side.
