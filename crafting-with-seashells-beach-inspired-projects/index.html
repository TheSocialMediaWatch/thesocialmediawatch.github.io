Crafting with Seashells: Beach-Inspired Projects


Everyone loves the beach, the sun, sand, and yes, the seashells. The fascinating shapes and colors of these exoskeletons are not just beautiful to behold but also perfect for making unique artworks and decorations. In this blog post, we’ll explore the many ways to craft with seashells and create projects inspired by the beach.

  1. Seashell mobiles

Seashell mobiles can add a delightful touch of beach aesthetics to any room in your house. Here’s how to make one:

  • For this project, you’ll need a variety of seashells in different shapes and sizes, a few large wine corks or wooden dowels, string or fishing line, and optionally glue or tape.

Thread each seashell onto the string or fishing line. Space the shells appropriately to create a natural pattern as you hang them – larger shells towards the bottom, and gradually smaller upwards. Then, attach the line to the corks or dowels, distributing weight evenly. Leave enough slack for gentle movement, and hang against a window or any sunlight streaming area for your seashell mobile.

  1. Wall art

Who says that mirrors only have to be boring framed glass? Give your mirror an exquisite makeover with seashells:

  • You’ll need a large piece of driftwood, various seashells, a glue gun, and a picture frame or mirror.

Arrange your collection of seashells to match the curve and character of the driftwood. Once satisfied with the placement, glue the shells securely to the wood. When dry, use the frame or mirror to complete the look as you attach it to the wall.

  1. Candle holders

Pamper your home with beachy ambiance by creating candle holders from gathered seashells:

  • Collect various seashells, coasters, or sand dollar blanks, and tea candles, tealights, or LED lights.

If using larger shells for the candle holders, slice off the bottom with a knife to create a flat surface. Place the tea candles or tealights inside, and if desired, paint the shell rim to match your home decor. If opting for sand dollars or coaster blanks, apply a thin layer of mod podge, followed by a layer of chosen fabric or photos. Let it dry, insert your tealights, and enjoy!

  1. Seashell jewelry

Make memories last longer by creating homemade necklaces and bracelets from the beach treasures you collected:

  • Need a sturdy set of wire cutters, 18-24" of copper wire, waterproof jewelry glue or jewelry findings like clasps and bead caps, and your favorite seashells.

Shape the wire into the desired shape for the jewelry piece. Then, thread the seashells onto the wire and arrange in a pattern. Cup the other end of the wire into a small loop, and squeeze tight. Apply waterproof jewelry glue to further secure this loop. Finalize on the jewelry finding of your choice, and voila – your beach-inspired creation is ready for a dip in the ocean or a stroll on the shoreline.

Conclusion:

As you can see, crafting with seashells is not just limited to painting and arranging on walls or tabletops – they can also be used creatively for functional art pieces like mobiles, candle holders, and much more. So next time you visit the beach, take home more than just memories of sun-kissed sand and tide blue waters; gather some seashells for fulfilling artsy endeavors. It’s not just a way to use materials sustainably, it’s also a way to bring the beach indoors, and with it, the calming energy of waves and the sound of seashells. Happy crafting!
