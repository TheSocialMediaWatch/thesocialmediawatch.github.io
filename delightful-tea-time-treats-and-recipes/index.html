Delightful Tea Time Treats and Recipes


Tea time is a beloved tradition in many cultures around the world. It’s a time to relax, unwind, and enjoy some delightful treats that perfectly complement a steaming cup of tea. Whether you’re hosting a tea party or simply treating yourself to a cozy afternoon break, having a selection of scrumptious tea time treats at hand can elevate the experience. In this blog post, we’ll explore a variety of delicious recipes that are perfect for your next tea time gathering.

Classic Scones with Jam and Clotted Cream

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1 tablespoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1 large egg
  • Jam and clotted cream, to serve

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) and line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, mix together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
  3. Add the cubed butter to the dry ingredients and use your fingertips to rub the butter into the flour mixture until it resembles breadcrumbs.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together the milk and egg. Slowly pour this mixture into the dry ingredients, stirring until just combined.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and gently knead it a few times until it comes together.
  6. Roll out the dough to about 1-inch thickness and use a cookie cutter to cut out scones. Place the scones on the prepared baking sheet.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes or until the scones are golden brown.
  8. Serve warm with jam and clotted cream.

Mini Cucumber Sandwiches

Ingredients:

  • 1 English cucumber, thinly sliced
  • 8 slices of white bread
  • 1/4 cup cream cheese, softened
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • Fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, mix together the cream cheese, mayonnaise, fresh dill, salt, and pepper.
  2. Spread the cream cheese mixture onto each slice of bread.
  3. Arrange the thinly sliced cucumber on half of the bread slices and top with the remaining slices to make sandwiches.
  4. Trim off the crusts and cut each sandwich into quarters or halves, depending on your preference.
  5. Serve the mini cucumber sandwiches with a garnish of fresh dill.

Lemon Drizzle Cake

Ingredients:

  • 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • Zest of 1 lemon
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 cup milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C) and grease a loaf pan.
  2. In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
  3. In a separate bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
  4. Stir in the lemon zest and juice.
  5. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, alternating with the milk, and mix until just combined.
  6. Pour the batter into the prepared loaf pan and bake for 45-50 minutes or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
  7. While the cake is still warm, drizzle it with a mixture of fresh lemon juice and sugar.
  8. Allow the cake to cool before slicing and serving.

Conclusion

Tea time is a wonderful opportunity to indulge in delicious treats and spend quality time with loved ones. Whether you prefer sweet or savory options, there are countless recipes available to suit every taste. The next time you plan a tea party or simply want to elevate your afternoon break, consider trying out these delightful tea time treats and recipes. Happy baking and tea sipping!

Remember, the key to a successful tea time spread is to balance flavors, textures, and presentation. Experiment with different recipes, get creative with your ingredients, and most importantly, enjoy the process of preparing these delightful treats. Happy tea time!
