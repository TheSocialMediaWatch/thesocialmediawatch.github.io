How to Clean Your Catch After a Fishing Trip


After a successful fishing trip, the excitement of hauling in a great catch is often followed by the necessary but sometimes tedious task of cleaning the fish. Whether you’re a seasoned angler or a beginner, knowing how to clean your fish properly is an essential skill that ensures your catch is ready to be prepared and enjoyed. This comprehensive guide will walk you through the entire cleaning process, from the initial steps right after the catch to the final touches before cooking.

Preparing for the Cleaning Process

Before you start cleaning your fish, there are a few essential tools and preparations you need to have in place to make the process smoother.

1. Essential Tools for Cleaning Fish

To clean your fish effectively, you’ll need a few basic tools:

  • Sharp filleting knife: A good filleting knife with a flexible blade is crucial for making precise cuts and getting the most meat from your fish.
  • Cutting board: A sturdy surface that can handle the knife’s cuts. Choose a non-slip one for safety.
  • Bucket or container: You’ll need something to discard waste such as guts, scales, and bones.
  • Fish scaler: If you’re not filleting your fish, this tool is helpful for removing scales quickly.
  • Towel or rag: For cleaning your hands or wiping off excess slime.
  • Gloves (optional): Protect your hands from sharp fins and to keep your hands clean during the process.

2. Clean and Prepare Your Workspace

Before beginning, it’s important to ensure you’re working in a clean, safe environment. Whether you’re at the campsite, dock, or at home, make sure you have a clean, accessible area to handle the fish. A clean bucket for waste and a place to wash your hands or tools are essential. Keeping the area organized also minimizes any mess and reduces the risk of cross-contamination if you’re working with multiple fish.

The First Steps: Handling the Fish Right After the Catch

Once you’ve caught your fish, it’s crucial to take a few immediate steps to preserve the quality of the meat and make cleaning easier.

1. Kill the Fish Humanely

The first thing you should do after catching a fish is to ensure it is killed as humanely as possible. The most effective and common method is to “brain spike” the fish. To do this, you’ll need to insert a sharp tool, like a spike or a knife, into the fish’s brain to kill it instantly. This minimizes stress on the fish, preserving the quality of the meat.

For larger fish, such as tuna or pike, you may need to use a gaff to secure it safely on the boat before proceeding.

2. Bleed the Fish

Bleeding your fish is important for removing the blood, which can make the meat taste gamey. Bleeding is especially crucial for fish that are eaten fresh or for high-end species like tuna or salmon.

To bleed the fish:

  • For smaller fish: Cut the gills and allow the blood to drain out into the water or a container.
  • For larger fish: Make a cut behind the pectoral fin on both sides, ensuring you cut into the blood vessels. Let the fish bleed out completely before proceeding to the next step.

3. Keep the Fish Cool

The key to preserving your fish’s quality is to keep it cool until you’re ready to clean it. Fish spoil quickly at higher temperatures, so store it in a cooler with ice if you’re not cleaning it immediately. If you’re fishing in hot weather, consider placing your fish on ice right after catching it to maintain freshness.

Step-by-Step Process for Cleaning Your Fish

Now that the initial steps are out of the way, it’s time to start the actual cleaning process. Depending on the species of fish you’re cleaning, you may choose to gut it or fillet it. Here’s a breakdown of both processes.

Option 1: Gutting Your Fish

Gutting is the process of removing the internal organs, which is the first step if you’re not filleting the fish. For smaller fish, this can be done quite easily, but larger fish will require more effort.

1. Place the Fish on a Cutting Board

Lay your fish flat on the cutting board. Use one hand to hold it steady while the other hand holds the knife.

2. Make the First Cut

Using your sharp knife, make a shallow cut along the belly of the fish, starting just below the gills and extending down to the anus. Be careful not to cut too deeply into the belly to avoid puncturing any internal organs.

3. Remove the Internal Organs

After making the cut, gently pull the internal organs out of the cavity. Depending on the species, you may need to cut through a few tougher connective tissues. Ensure you remove all the internal organs, including the swim bladder, as well as the dark lining that runs along the inside of the fish’s body.

4. Clean the Cavity

After removing the organs, rinse the cavity with cold water to remove any remaining blood, tissue, or other matter. A thorough cleaning is necessary to ensure there are no residual impurities.

Option 2: Filleting Your Fish

Filleting is ideal for fish with a lot of meat and those you plan to cook in steaks or fillet cuts.

1. Make the Initial Cuts

Begin by placing the fish on its side and cutting just behind the gills. Slice down the length of the fish, following the natural line along the backbone. You’ll want to make smooth, deliberate cuts to avoid wasting any meat.

2. Remove the Fillet

Once you’ve cut along the length of the body, gently lift the fillet away from the rib cage. Continue cutting along the spine until the fillet comes free from the fish’s bones.

3. Repeat for the Other Side

Flip the fish over and repeat the same process on the other side to remove the second fillet.

4. Skinning (Optional)

If you prefer to remove the skin, place the fillet skin-side down on the cutting board. Make a small incision between the skin and the meat at the tail end, and then slowly work your knife along the fillet, separating the skin from the flesh.

Removing the Bones

Even after filleting, some fish may have residual bones, particularly small pin bones. You can remove these by carefully running your fingers along the fillet and using tweezers or pliers to pull out any visible bones.

Storing Your Fish After Cleaning

Once you’ve cleaned and filleted your fish, it’s essential to store it properly until you’re ready to cook or freeze it.

1. Keep It Cold

Place the cleaned fish fillets in a container or bag and store them in a cooler with ice if you plan to cook them soon. If you intend to keep the fish for a longer period, freezing is your best option.

2. Freezing Your Fish

For long-term storage, freezing is the best option. Wrap each fillet in plastic wrap, aluminum foil, or a vacuum-sealed bag to prevent freezer burn. Ensure the fish is tightly sealed, and label it with the date so you can keep track of how long it has been stored.

Final Thoughts

Cleaning your catch may seem like a daunting task at first, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a simple and rewarding part of the fishing experience. Whether you’re gutting or filleting your fish, each method provides you with fresh, delicious meat that’s ready to be cooked. The key is to handle the fish with care, keep it cool, and clean it thoroughly. By following these steps, you’ll ensure that your catch is in the best condition possible, ready to be transformed into a tasty meal.
