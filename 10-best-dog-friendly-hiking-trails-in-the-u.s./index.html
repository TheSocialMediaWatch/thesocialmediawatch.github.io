10 Best Dog-Friendly Hiking Trails in the U.S.


Hiking with your four-legged companion is one of the simplest joys a dog owner can experience. The fresh air, exercise, scenic views, and the bond you share on these ventures make each trail memorable. But not all hiking trails are created equal, especially for dogs. Rugged terrain, unwelcoming policies, or hazards like steep drops can turn a pleasant outing into a challenge. To help you plan the ultimate adventure with your furry best friend, we’ve rounded up ten of the best dog-friendly hiking trails across the United States. Each of these trails offers unique landscapes and experiences, ensuring both you and your pup have an unforgettable time.

1. Runyon Canyon Park – Los Angeles, California

Situated in the heart of Los Angeles, this urban oasis is perfect for dog owners looking for moderate hikes with panoramic city views. Runyon Canyon Park offers several dog-friendly trails that vary in difficulty, ensuring options for all fitness and experience levels. The trails wind through dry chaparral hills and feature open spaces where dogs can roam freely off-leash in designated areas. This popular hiking spot is not only ideal for enjoying Southern California’s sunshine, but it also gives you a chance to meet other dog-loving hikers along the way.

2. Smugglers’ Notch State Park – Vermont

For those seeking cooler mountain air and lush forested trails, Smugglers’ Notch State Park is a must-visit. Nestled in the Green Mountains, the park offers dog-friendly routes like the Sterling Pond Trail, a moderately challenging hike with rewarding views of one of Vermont’s highest elevation ponds. Dogs are allowed on-leash, and the thick canopy overhead provides shade even during the warmer months. This trail is great for active dogs used to inclines and rocky paths. Don’t forget to pack extra water for both you and your pup!

3. Acadia National Park – Maine

Acadia National Park is renowned for its stunning coastal views and inviting trails, and best of all, it’s incredibly pet-friendly. With over 100 miles of trails to explore, dogs are welcome on most routes, provided they remain on a leash no longer than six feet. One favorite among visitors is the Wonderland Trail, a gentle coastal walk with plenty of opportunities for your dog to sniff the ocean air. If your pup enjoys climbing, the Jordan Pond Path is another excellent option that features serene water views and forested paths.

4. White River Falls State Park – Oregon

Oregon is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts, and the White River Falls State Park combines scenic beauty with opportunities for canine exploration. This park’s trails offer picturesque views of waterfalls and a pleasant mix of flat paths and steeper descents. Dogs are allowed on-leash, and the Main Trail leading to the falls provides plenty of space for sniffing around and resting by the river. It’s an ideal trail for those looking to enjoy a day in nature with their dog while staying away from overly crowded areas.

5. Blue Ridge Parkway Trails – North Carolina and Virginia

Stretching for nearly 469 miles through North Carolina and Virginia, the Blue Ridge Parkway is not just a road trip destination; its diverse trails are also a canine paradise. The dog-friendly Humpback Rocks Trail near Waynesboro, Virginia, is a favorite for its striking mountain views and moderately challenging ascent. For a more relaxed hike, the Flat Top Mountain Trail offers shaded paths and vibrant wildflowers in the spring. Dogs are welcome on-leash, making this scenic slice of Appalachia a perfect destination for bonding with your pet.

6. Mount Rainier National Park – Washington

Washington State boasts incredible hiking destinations, but Mount Rainier National Park stands out, particularly for dog-friendly options like the Naches Peak Loop. While many national parks restrict dog access, this specific trail allows leashed dogs and offers breathtaking views of alpine meadows, wildflowers, and cascading streams. The trail’s gentle grades make it suitable for dogs of all ages and fitness levels. It’s a fantastic option for those looking for a peaceful hike while enjoying views of Washington’s iconic peak.

7. Custer State Park – South Dakota

Nestled in the Black Hills of South Dakota, Custer State Park is an underrated gem for dog owners. The park offers several dog-friendly trails, including the Sylvan Lake Shore Trail, which loops around a tranquil body of water with plenty of spots to stop and enjoy the scenery. This relatively short and easy hike is ideal for older dogs or those who prefer less rugged terrain. The park does require dogs to stay on-leash at all times, but its wide-open spaces and abundant wildlife make it a delightful experience for both humans and pets.

8. Aspen and Maroon Bells Wilderness Area – Colorado

For high-altitude adventure and jaw-dropping scenery, the Aspen and Maroon Bells Wilderness trails in Colorado are simply unparalleled. The Maroon Lake Scenic Trail is a dog-friendly option that showcases the stunning Maroon Bells peaks while staying relatively flat and easy to traverse. Dogs are required to be on-leash, as the popularity of the area means you’ll encounter other hikers often. For more seasoned hikers with active dogs, the Crater Lake Trail offers a longer route and an elevation gain that rewards you with breathtaking views.

9. Forest Park – Portland, Oregon

Portland’s Forest Park is a natural retreat tucked within city limits, offering over 80 miles of trails perfect for exploring with your pup. The easy-going Lower Macleay Trail to Pittock Mansion is a local favorite, combining shaded woodland paths with rewarding views of Portland’s skyline once you reach the mansion. If you’re seeking a longer adventure, the Wildwood Trail winds through dense forests and connects you with other parts of the park. Dogs are welcome on-leash, making Forest Park an ideal location for nature explorers who want to enjoy the serenity of the outdoors without venturing far from urban amenities.

10. Great Smoky Mountains National Park – Tennessee and North Carolina

One of the most visited national parks in the U.S., the Great Smoky Mountains offer a handful of dog-friendly trails that allow you to immerse yourself in its stunning landscapes. Among them, the Gatlinburg Trail is a top choice for those traveling with pets. This relatively flat, tree-lined path runs along the river, offering plenty of shade and opportunities for water breaks along the way. Dogs are permitted on-leash, ensuring safety amidst the park’s abundant wildlife. Whether it’s a short weekend trip or an extended vacation, this trail is a peaceful retreat for both you and your canine companion.

Exploring the beauty of the U.S. with your dog is one of the best ways to reconnect with nature while strengthening your bond. These trails span diverse landscapes, from coastal paths to mountainous climbs, ensuring there’s something for both casual and experienced hikers. Before hitting the trails, ensure your pup is up-to-date on vaccinations, remains hydrated, and is protected from external elements like ticks. With a little preparation, these dog-friendly adventures will leave both you and your canine companion coming back for more!
