How to Make Money Online by Testing Websites and Apps


In the ever-evolving landscape of the internet, making money online has become more accessible than ever before. One fascinating way to earn income remotely is by testing websites and apps. This method merges curiosity with practicality — you get to explore the latest digital platforms while providing valuable feedback that shapes the user experience. If you’ve ever wondered how you can turn your knack for spotting glitches or your interest in technology into cash, this comprehensive guide breaks down everything you need to know about making money through usability testing.

What Is Website and App Testing?

Website and app testing involves evaluating a digital product’s user interface, functionality, and overall experience. Companies, startups, and developers rely on real users to identify bugs, usability issues, and performance problems before launching or updating their platforms. When you participate in testing, you interact with the site or app, perform specific tasks, and report your findings.

Beyond simple bug hunting, testing often includes giving subjective feedback: Is the navigation intuitive? Are the features engaging? Does the product hold your interest or frustrate you? Your input helps businesses craft better experiences, boost customer satisfaction, and increase sales.

Why Is Testing Websites and Apps a Lucrative Online Opportunity?

Several factors make this opportunity compelling:

  • Low Barrier to Entry: You don’t need advanced technical skills or certifications. Basic computer literacy, attention to detail, and clear communication usually suffice.
  • Flexible Schedule: Testing gigs often allow you to work when you want, fitting around other responsibilities or commitments.
  • Variety of Tasks: The work involves different platforms and industries, helping prevent boredom and expanding your knowledge.
  • Real Impact: The feedback you provide directly influences product quality and usability, making the role satisfying.
  • Pay Per Test: Compensation varies by complexity, but many testers earn anywhere from $10 to $60 per test.

How to Get Started Testing Websites and Apps

1. Identify Reputable Testing Platforms

Finding reliable platforms that connect testers with companies is the first step. Popular usability testing websites include:

  • UserTesting: Known for paying around $10 per 20-minute test.
  • TryMyUI: Offers tests where you perform tasks and narrate your experience.
  • Userlytics: Focuses on in-depth feedback and polls.
  • Testbirds: Engages testers for both websites and mobile app testings.
  • UserFeel: Easy entry, emphasizes detailed verbal feedback.
  • Enroll: Great for quick tests and surveys.

Each platform has its own application process, which usually includes a sample test to demonstrate your capability.

2. Prepare Your Devices

Testing could require different devices depending on the project. It’s common to need:

  • A desktop or laptop for website testing.
  • A smartphone or tablet for mobile app testing.
  • Stable internet connection.

Having these devices ready allows you to engage in diverse testing opportunities and maximize income.

3. Sharpen Your Testing Skills

The better your feedback, the more valuable you become, increasing your chances of getting invited for more tests and higher-paying gigs. Key skills include:

  • Clear verbal communication; often tests require you to speak your thoughts aloud.
  • Attention to detail to catch subtle bugs or design flaws.
  • Basic knowledge of web navigation and app functionality.

You can practice these by completing free sample tests online or volunteering to test new apps for free.

What Does a Typical Testing Session Look Like?

After qualifying to be a tester on a platform, you’ll receive notifications about available tests. Once you accept, the process typically follows this flow:

  1. Review Instructions: Understand the goals of the task. For example, you may be asked to purchase an item, sign up for a newsletter, or find specific information.
  2. Voice Narration: As you interact, narrate your experience clearly. Mention any confusion, delays, or unexpected behaviors.
  3. Complete Tasks: Navigate the website or app according to guidelines.
  4. Answer Post-Test Questions: These might focus on usability ratings or specific issues encountered.
  5. Submit Your Review: Upload your recording and answers through the platform.

Depending on your clarity and adherence to instructions, some test sessions may be rejected if they don’t meet the quality standards, so it’s important to pay attention and be thorough.

Real Examples of Test Tasks

To give a clearer idea, here are some sample tasks testers might encounter:

  • E-commerce Flow: Test buying a product on an online store. Report how easy it is to find items, add to the cart, and checkout.
  • App Navigation: Download a new app and explore how intuitive the menus are.
  • Content Usability: View articles on a blog; check readability and layout on mobile and desktop.
  • Form Testing: Fill out registration forms to see if validation messages are clear and helpful.
  • Feature Testing: Use a new chat function or booking system and evaluate responsiveness.

These tests often last about 15-30 minutes and pay proportionally.

How Much Can You Expect to Earn?

The pay scale depends on:

  • Length of the test.
  • Complexity of tasks.
  • Platform and client budgets.
  • Your reliability and reputation as a tester.

Typical hourly equivalent rates range from $10 to $30. Some advanced or niche tests may provide upwards of $60 per session. Consistent testers working across multiple platforms can augment their income substantially, sometimes earning several hundred dollars monthly as a side hustle.

Tips to Maximize Earnings from Testing

Diversify Across Platforms

Registering on multiple testing sites increases your chance of receiving more frequent test invitations. Remember to manage your schedule to avoid test overlaps.

Maintain High-Quality Feedback

Clear, concise, and thoughtful feedback gets recognized. Good reputation may unlock higher-paying projects or priority invitations.

Stay Updated With Testing Trends

Mobile devices and apps evolve quickly. Keep abreast of new devices and UX trends to stay competitive.

Use Good Audio Equipment

Most tests require voice narration; clear audio means better reviews and acceptance rates.

Manage Your Time Efficiently

Tests vary from short surveys to longer sessions. Allocate your time smartly to maximize income without burnout.

Common Challenges and How to Overcome Them

  • Rejected Tests: Not all submissions qualify for payment. Rejection often relates to poor audio, unclear speech, or incomplete tasks. Practice speaking naturally and test your mic before sessions.
  • Irregular Workflow: Testing gigs aren’t always daily. Treat it as a supplementary income source rather than a full-time job.
  • Technical Issues: Slow internet or incompatible devices can interfere. Ensure your setup meets platform requirements.
  • Confusing Instructions: If directions are unclear, try replaying or rereading the task before proceeding.

Long-Term Prospects in Website and App Testing

While usability testing is mainly suitable as a side income or part-time job, it can act as a springboard into digital career paths. Testers often gain:

  • Insight into software development cycles.
  • Passion for user experience design.
  • Opportunities to transition into QA (Quality Assurance) roles or UX research.

Some testers convert their feedback skills into consulting or freelancing roles, assisting businesses with site audits and usability improvements.

Final Thoughts

Making money by testing websites and apps blends convenience with engaging work that embraces the digital world’s pulse. Whether you want a flexible side hustle or a way to dip your toes into tech-related roles, this route offers accessible entry points and steady income streams.

Approach testing with curiosity, commitment, and professionalism. Over time, you’ll refine your insight, grow your network in the usability community, and maybe even help shape the digital products millions rely on daily. It’s a win-win — your feedback improves user experiences globally while rewarding you for your time and effort.

Ready to get started? Explore the testing platforms, prep your devices, and start giving your voice to the websites and apps shaping the future. The next big bug fix could come from you!
