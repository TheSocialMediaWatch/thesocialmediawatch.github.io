How to Get Your Music on Pandora


Getting your music on Pandora can be a huge step toward increasing your exposure and reaching a wider audience. As one of the most popular music streaming services, Pandora offers a platform where millions of users listen to personalized radio stations and discover new artists every day. If you are a musician or a music producer, it is essential to understand how you can get your tracks on Pandora and start sharing your sound with the world. This post will guide you through the process of submitting your music to Pandora and give you the tools you need to succeed.

Understanding Pandora’s Music Submission Process

Pandora is unique in that it allows music to be submitted both by independent artists and through music distribution services. Unlike some streaming platforms where anyone can upload their tracks directly, Pandora’s submission process involves more steps. This is because Pandora uses a curated model to ensure that the content they offer to users is of high quality and matches their listener’s preferences.

Step 1: Get Your Music Ready

Before submitting your music to Pandora, it’s crucial to ensure your tracks are professionally recorded, mixed, and mastered. The quality of your music plays a significant role in its acceptance and performance on the platform. Make sure that:

  • Your tracks are mixed and mastered to industry standards.
  • You have high-quality audio files (preferably in WAV format).
  • Your music is properly labeled with accurate metadata, including song title, artist name, genre, album name, and release date.

It’s also important that you have the necessary rights to the music you are submitting. This includes being the rightful owner of the content or having permission to distribute it. Pandora requires that all content uploaded to their platform is properly licensed and free from copyright infringement.

Step 2: Create a Pandora AMP Account

Pandora’s Artist Marketing Platform (AMP) is the main platform for submitting music to Pandora. It allows artists to manage their music, promote their tracks, and track listener engagement. To get started, you need to create an AMP account.

  • Visit the Pandora AMP website.
  • Sign up with your email address, and create a password.
  • Once you’ve completed the sign-up process, you’ll need to provide details about your music, including links to your social media profiles, and a description of your music career.

Creating an AMP account is essential for managing your presence on Pandora. With this account, you’ll be able to submit your music, monitor performance metrics, and interact with your fans.

Step 3: Use a Music Distributor

Pandora does not allow direct uploads from independent artists. Instead, you’ll need to use a music distribution service to get your tracks onto Pandora. There are several well-known distribution platforms that can submit your music to Pandora, such as:

  • TuneCore: One of the most popular music distribution platforms. TuneCore distributes your music to a variety of streaming services, including Pandora.
  • CD Baby: Another reputable platform that allows artists to submit their music to Pandora. CD Baby also offers additional services like sync licensing and physical distribution.
  • DistroKid: Known for its quick distribution services, DistroKid is a favorite among independent artists. It submits your music to Pandora and many other platforms, making it a convenient option for those looking for an easy submission process.

These platforms ensure that your music gets onto Pandora as well as other streaming services. The distribution services generally require you to pay a fee or percentage of the royalties from your streams. Be sure to compare the services and choose the one that best suits your needs.

Step 4: Upload Your Music

Once you have signed up with a music distributor, you can begin uploading your music. Here’s how to do it:

  • Log in to your distribution service account.
  • Upload your music: You will need to provide your track files (typically in WAV or MP3 format), album artwork, and track details (song title, artist name, genre, and release date).
  • Choose Pandora: Make sure to select Pandora as one of the platforms where you want your music distributed.

The distributor will then take care of sending your music to Pandora, as well as to other streaming platforms. The process typically takes anywhere from a few days to a few weeks, depending on the distributor and the platforms involved.

Step 5: Wait for Approval

Once your music has been submitted, Pandora will review your tracks. This process may take some time, as Pandora curates its content carefully to ensure quality and relevance to its users. The approval process typically involves checking:

  • The quality of the music.
  • The accuracy of metadata.
  • Whether the music fits Pandora’s genre and style preferences.

While waiting for approval, you can check the status of your submission through your music distributor or Pandora AMP account. If your tracks are approved, they will appear on Pandora’s catalog, ready to be streamed by listeners.

Step 6: Promote Your Music on Pandora

After your music is live on Pandora, it’s time to promote it to gain visibility. Pandora’s AMP platform offers several promotional tools that you can use to get your music in front of more listeners. These tools include:

  • Pandora Stories: This feature allows artists to share personal stories, behind-the-scenes content, and more. It’s an excellent way to connect with your audience and promote your music.
  • Promoted Tracks: You can pay to have your songs promoted directly to Pandora’s users. These promoted tracks are placed in personalized stations and playlists, increasing their chances of being discovered.
  • Fan Engagement: Pandora’s AMP platform allows you to track listener engagement. You can use this data to improve your promotional strategies, ensuring that your fans are getting the best experience.

Additionally, use your social media platforms, email newsletters, and other channels to let your audience know that your music is now available on Pandora. Encourage your fans to follow your station and share your tracks with others.

Step 7: Monitor Your Music’s Performance

Once your music is live on Pandora, it’s crucial to monitor its performance regularly. Pandora provides valuable metrics through its AMP platform, such as:

  • Streams: The number of times your tracks have been played.
  • Thumbs Up/Thumbs Down: The feedback from listeners on your tracks.
  • Listener Demographics: Information about the age, gender, and location of listeners.

These insights can help you understand your audience better and optimize your promotional strategies. You can use the data to focus on improving areas where your music may not be performing as well, and double down on what’s working.

Tips for Success on Pandora

Getting your music on Pandora is just the beginning. To make the most out of this opportunity and gain a loyal fan base, consider the following tips:

  1. Engage with your fans: Building a connection with your audience is key to long-term success. Respond to fan messages, share exclusive content, and keep your listeners engaged.
  2. Stay consistent: Regularly release new music and update your Pandora station. Consistency keeps your audience coming back for more.
  3. Collaborate with other artists: Pandora is a platform where many independent artists thrive. Collaborating with other artists can help you tap into new audiences and grow your following.
  4. Submit to Pandora’s curated playlists: Pandora has a variety of curated playlists based on genre, mood, and activity. Submitting your music to these playlists increases its chances of being featured and reaching a wider audience.

Final Thoughts

Getting your music on Pandora can be a rewarding step in your music career. By following these steps and utilizing Pandora’s tools effectively, you can increase your visibility, connect with new listeners, and grow your fanbase. Remember, the music industry is highly competitive, and consistency, engagement, and quality are key to standing out. Take the time to perfect your music, submit it to the right platforms, and promote it effectively to make the most out of your presence on Pandora.
