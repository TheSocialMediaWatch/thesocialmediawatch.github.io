How to Make Your Own Vegan Cheese at Home


Making your own vegan cheese at home can be an incredibly rewarding experience. Not only does it allow you to experiment with flavors and textures, but it also gives you complete control over the ingredients, ensuring that everything is fresh, healthy, and exactly how you like it. Whether you’re a long-time vegan, lactose-intolerant, or just looking to try something new, crafting your own dairy-free cheese is an enjoyable and practical endeavor. In this guide, we will take you through the process step by step, helping you create vegan cheeses that are creamy, tangy, and full of flavor.

What You Need to Know About Vegan Cheese

Before diving into the actual recipes, it’s important to understand that vegan cheese is made from plant-based ingredients, making it completely free from dairy. Unlike traditional cheese, which is made from animal milk, vegan cheese typically relies on nuts, seeds, and plant-based ingredients like soy, coconut, and cashews. Vegan cheese can be creamy, hard, or even crumbly, mimicking the texture and taste of its dairy counterparts. Some of the key elements in making vegan cheese include:

  • Base Ingredients: These typically include nuts (cashews, almonds), seeds (sunflower, pumpkin), and legumes. These provide the creaminess and flavor base.
  • Acidic Component: To mimic the tangy bite of dairy cheese, an acidic element is needed. Ingredients like lemon juice, vinegar, or probiotic cultures can help achieve this.
  • Thickening Agents: Agar-agar, arrowroot powder, and tapioca starch are often used to give vegan cheese its texture.
  • Flavor Enhancers: Nutritional yeast, miso, garlic, onion, and spices are great for enhancing the depth of flavor and achieving that signature “cheesy” taste.

Vegan cheese recipes can vary widely in complexity, but with a little patience and the right ingredients, you can make anything from soft spreads to hard cheeses suitable for grating.

Key Ingredients for Vegan Cheese

  1. Cashews: Often used in creamy vegan cheeses, cashews provide a rich, buttery texture and are perfect for making soft cheeses. They are easy to blend into smooth creams, which is why they are a favorite for recipes like vegan ricotta, cream cheese, and even mozzarella.

  2. Almonds: Almonds have a slightly firmer texture compared to cashews but are also widely used in vegan cheese recipes. Almond-based cheeses tend to be a bit more granular in texture, making them perfect for creating feta-style or crumbly cheeses.

  3. Coconut Oil: This is an essential ingredient for achieving the creamy and fatty texture that cheese is known for. Coconut oil helps solidify the cheese at room temperature and adds a rich mouthfeel.

  4. Nutritional Yeast: Nutritional yeast is a powerhouse ingredient that provides a cheesy, umami flavor to your vegan cheese. It’s full of B-vitamins and is often used in combination with other flavorings to mimic the taste of dairy cheese.

  5. Lemon Juice or Apple Cider Vinegar: To give your vegan cheese the tangy taste associated with dairy cheese, lemon juice or apple cider vinegar is essential. They help to add acidity to the cheese, making it more complex and giving it the sharp flavor often found in cheddar or gouda.

  6. Agar-Agar or Tapioca Starch: These thickeners are used to help vegan cheeses set and become firm. Agar-agar, which is derived from seaweed, is especially popular in vegan cheese recipes that require a solid, sliceable cheese. Tapioca starch, on the other hand, creates a stretchy texture perfect for mozzarella-style cheese.

  7. Probiotic Cultures: To make your vegan cheese tangy and slightly fermented, probiotic cultures like vegan yogurt or rejuvelac (a fermented liquid made from sprouted grains) can be used. This is ideal for making aged cheeses, or those with a complex, sour flavor profile.

Step-by-Step Guide to Making Vegan Cheese

1. Cashew-Based Vegan Cheese (Soft and Creamy)

Cashew cheese is one of the simplest and most versatile vegan cheese options to make at home. It’s incredibly creamy and can be used as a spread, dip, or sauce. Here’s how to make it:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw cashews (soaked in water for 4-6 hours or overnight)
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 garlic clove (optional)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup water (or more, depending on desired consistency)

Instructions:

  1. Drain and rinse the soaked cashews.
  2. Add the cashews, lemon juice, nutritional yeast, garlic, and salt to a high-speed blender or food processor.
  3. Blend until smooth, adding water little by little until you achieve a creamy, spreadable consistency.
  4. Taste and adjust the seasoning, adding more salt, nutritional yeast, or lemon juice as needed.
  5. Transfer to a container and refrigerate for at least an hour before serving to let the flavors meld.

This cashew-based cheese can be stored in the refrigerator for up to a week and is perfect for sandwiches, crackers, and even as a creamy pasta sauce.

2. Vegan Cream Cheese

If you love the tangy and smooth texture of cream cheese, you can easily make a plant-based version using cashews. This version has a mild tang from the lemon and vinegar, making it perfect for bagels or dips.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw cashews (soaked)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil (melted)
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Drain the cashews and place them in a blender or food processor.
  2. Add the lemon juice, apple cider vinegar, coconut oil, and salt.
  3. Blend until smooth, scraping down the sides as necessary.
  4. Taste and adjust the flavor with additional salt, lemon juice, or vinegar if desired.
  5. Transfer the cream cheese to a jar or container and refrigerate for a few hours before serving.

3. Vegan Cheddar Cheese (Firm and Sliceable)

For a more traditional, firm cheese that can be sliced and grated, vegan cheddar is a great option. This recipe uses agar-agar to help the cheese solidify and form a firm texture.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup soaked cashews
  • 1/2 cup carrots (steamed)
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric (for color)
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 1 tablespoon agar-agar powder

Instructions:

  1. In a blender, combine the soaked cashews, steamed carrots, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, apple cider vinegar, turmeric, garlic powder, and salt. Blend until smooth.
  2. In a small saucepan, combine the water and agar-agar powder. Heat over medium heat, stirring constantly until the agar dissolves and the mixture begins to thicken.
  3. Add the cashew mixture to the saucepan and continue to cook, stirring, for about 5-7 minutes, until the mixture thickens and becomes creamy.
  4. Pour the mixture into a mold or container lined with parchment paper.
  5. Refrigerate for 4-6 hours until the cheese has set and solidified.
  6. Once set, remove from the mold, slice, and enjoy!

This vegan cheddar cheese is perfect for sandwiches, burgers, and even for grating over pasta or salads.

4. Vegan Mozzarella (Stretchy and Melty)

Vegan mozzarella is perfect for pizza and lasagna. It’s stretchy, melty, and holds up well when baked.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup cashews (soaked)
  • 1/4 cup tapioca starch
  • 1/4 cup nutritional yeast
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups water

Instructions:

  1. Blend the soaked cashews, tapioca starch, nutritional yeast, lemon juice, salt, and water until smooth.
  2. Pour the mixture into a saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly. The mixture will start to thicken and become stretchy.
  3. Once it has thickened to a gooey consistency, remove from heat and transfer to a mold.
  4. Refrigerate for about 1-2 hours to allow it to firm up.
  5. Use it as a mozzarella substitute for pizza or any other dishes that require melty cheese.

Tips for Success in Vegan Cheese Making

  • Soaking nuts: Soaking nuts, especially cashews, softens them and makes blending easier, ensuring a smoother consistency in the final cheese.
  • Acidity: The tanginess of cheese comes from acids. Don’t skimp on lemon juice, vinegar, or probiotic cultures. These are key to mimicking the flavor profile of dairy cheese.
  • Experiment with flavors: Add fresh herbs, spices, and smoked paprika to create unique flavor profiles. Garlic, onion, and mustard powder can give your vegan cheese a robust flavor.

Storing Your Vegan Cheese

Homemade vegan cheese can be stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week. For firmer cheeses, wrapping them in parchment paper or wax paper before placing them in a container can help maintain their shape. For soft cheeses, a simple container with a tight-fitting lid works best.

Conclusion

Making your own vegan cheese at home is not only a fun and creative process, but it also allows you to customize flavors, textures, and ingredients to suit your personal preferences. Whether you’re making a creamy spread or a firm, sliceable block, there are endless possibilities for crafting plant-based cheeses that are just as delicious as their dairy counterparts. With a few basic ingredients and some practice, you can enjoy homemade vegan cheese that’s fresh, flavorful, and entirely free of dairy.
