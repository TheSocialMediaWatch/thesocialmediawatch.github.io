The Best Hunting Resources for New Hunters


Hunting is one of the oldest and most exhilarating activities that connect people to nature. It requires patience, skill, and knowledge of the environment. For new hunters, the journey can be both exciting and overwhelming. There’s so much to learn—from the basics of hunting techniques to the finer details of animal behavior. Fortunately, there are numerous resources available to guide beginners through the process. Whether you’re preparing for your first hunting season or you’ve just picked up the sport, these resources will help you build the foundation you need to become a successful hunter.

1. Hunting Education Courses

The most important step for a new hunter is education. Understanding the fundamentals of hunting can make a huge difference in the field. Many states require new hunters to complete a hunter safety course, which provides an introduction to hunting regulations, ethics, and firearm safety. Even if your state doesn’t require one, it’s highly beneficial to take a course.

Online Courses

  • Hunter Ed: Hunter Ed offers an online certification course that’s widely accepted across North America. The courses cover everything from firearm safety to understanding hunting laws.
  • National Shooting Sports Foundation (NSSF): This organization provides resources and training on firearm safety and hunting fundamentals. The NSSF also partners with hunting organizations to promote education and awareness.

In-Person Classes

  • State Wildlife Agencies: Many states offer free or low-cost hunter safety courses that are either classroom-based or field-based. Check with your local wildlife agency for more information.
  • Local Outdoor Clubs and Organizations: Outdoor organizations, such as Ducks Unlimited or the National Wild Turkey Federation, frequently host hunter safety classes and hands-on workshops.

2. Books and Guides

Books remain one of the most comprehensive resources for new hunters. They offer in-depth information that you can reference at your own pace, making them an invaluable asset for beginners.

Recommended Books for New Hunters

  • “The Complete Guide to Hunting, Butchering, and Cooking Wild Game” by Steven Rinella: Steven Rinella is a renowned hunter and outdoorsman, and this book is a fantastic resource for anyone interested in the hunting lifestyle. It covers a wide range of topics, from choosing the right gear to field dressing and preparing your harvest.
  • “Hunting for Food” by Ray Mears: This book focuses on hunting and foraging techniques. Ray Mears offers tips and insights that go beyond just killing game, giving readers the skills to survive in the wild.
  • “The MeatEater Fish and Game Cookbook” by Steven Rinella: If you’re interested in preparing your harvest, this cookbook is packed with recipes and advice on cooking wild game.

Books can also be found in local libraries or bookstores, many of which feature guides specific to the type of hunting you plan to pursue—whether it’s big game, waterfowl, or upland bird hunting.

3. Hunting Forums and Online Communities

Hunting forums are an excellent way for beginners to learn from more experienced hunters. These communities often share tips, gear reviews, hunting stories, and advice that can be invaluable when you’re starting out. Participating in these forums can help you network with other hunters and stay updated on hunting news, trends, and regulations.

Popular Hunting Forums

  • TheHuntersGuide.com: This is a great forum for discussing hunting techniques, tactics, and stories. You can also find information on state-specific laws and regulations.
  • HuntingNet.com: This is one of the largest hunting communities online. It features discussions about equipment, hunting strategies, and personal experiences.
  • ArcheryTalk.com: For those interested in bow hunting, this forum is dedicated to archery enthusiasts. Whether you’re looking for gear advice or tips on improving your aim, you’ll find plenty of discussions.

These forums also often feature specialized sections where you can connect with hunters who focus on specific regions or game species, which is especially helpful for new hunters trying to learn the ropes.

4. YouTube Channels

For visual learners, YouTube is an outstanding resource. There are countless hunting-related channels that cover a variety of topics—from gear reviews and hunting tips to cooking wild game and ethical hunting practices. Watching experienced hunters in action can teach you techniques that books and online resources sometimes miss.

Must-Subscribe Hunting Channels

  • MeatEater: Founded by Steven Rinella, the MeatEater channel is dedicated to educating hunters on the benefits of ethical hunting and conservation. The channel is packed with entertaining and informative content.
  • The Hunting Public: This channel focuses on public land hunting and offers a wide range of tutorials and tips for hunters, especially beginners.
  • Field & Stream: This channel, associated with the famous hunting and fishing magazine, provides practical advice on all things hunting, from gear to tactics.

YouTube also gives you the chance to watch live hunts, where you can learn firsthand how experienced hunters approach different situations. You can also engage with the content creators through comments or by reaching out with questions.

5. Podcasts

Podcasts have become a popular way to consume information on the go, and the hunting world is no exception. There are several podcasts aimed at new hunters, offering a wide range of tips, stories, and insights.

Recommended Hunting Podcasts

  • The MeatEater Podcast: This podcast is hosted by Steven Rinella and is packed with useful hunting advice. Topics range from field tactics and gear recommendations to the importance of conservation.
  • Hunt Talk Radio: Hosted by Randy Newberg, this podcast focuses on public land hunting in the United States. It’s ideal for new hunters who want to understand the challenges and opportunities of hunting on public land.
  • The Hunting Collective: This podcast explores everything from hunting culture to food preparation. It’s a great resource for hunters who want to connect with the broader hunting community.

Podcasts allow you to listen while commuting, hiking, or performing other activities, making it easier to absorb useful information even when you’re not able to sit down with a book or computer.

6. Hunting Gear Reviews and Buying Guides

Choosing the right gear is critical to a successful hunting experience. Whether you’re looking for your first hunting rifle, bow, or boots, knowing what to look for can be confusing. Fortunately, many websites and resources offer detailed reviews and buying guides that can help you make informed decisions.

Websites for Gear Reviews

  • Outdoor Life: This website regularly publishes buying guides and product reviews for hunting gear, from rifles to sleeping bags. It’s an excellent resource for new hunters looking for equipment recommendations.
  • GearJunkie: GearJunkie covers a wide variety of outdoor gear and includes detailed reviews on hunting equipment. They offer expert opinions on everything from tents to trail cameras.
  • The Truth About Guns: If you’re focused on firearms for hunting, this site provides honest reviews and insights into various hunting rifles, shotguns, and ammunition.

These sites also often include seasonal recommendations, which are particularly useful for hunters who are just starting to understand what gear is necessary for different hunting environments and conditions.

7. Hunting Blogs

Hunting blogs are another great source of information for new hunters. They often cover a broad range of topics, including hunting techniques, gear reviews, stories, and advice from seasoned hunters. Many hunting blogs also focus on specific types of hunting, so you can find blogs tailored to your particular interests.

Popular Hunting Blogs

  • The Hunting Life: This blog covers a wide array of hunting topics, from tips for beginners to expert advice on everything from gear to field dressing. The site also offers a great community for hunters to exchange ideas and experiences.
  • Backcountry Hunters & Anglers Blog: A blog that advocates for public lands, ethical hunting, and conservation. It’s perfect for beginners who want to understand the bigger picture of hunting and its role in wildlife management.
  • OutdoorHub: OutdoorHub offers a combination of hunting news, gear reviews, and useful advice for hunters at all levels. It’s a great resource for learning about new hunting technologies and innovations.

Reading hunting blogs will allow you to stay up to date with the latest hunting trends and learn directly from hunters who are actively practicing the sport.

8. Conservation and Wildlife Management Resources

Hunting is not just about harvesting animals; it’s also about conservation and maintaining the health of wildlife populations. Understanding the role that hunters play in wildlife management is crucial. Several organizations and resources focus on educating hunters about their responsibilities in conservation.

Key Conservation Resources

  • National Wildlife Federation (NWF): NWF offers resources and educational materials for hunters interested in conservation. They highlight the importance of sustainable hunting practices and wildlife protection.
  • Rocky Mountain Elk Foundation (RMEF): The RMEF is dedicated to the conservation of elk and their habitats. They offer plenty of educational content for hunters interested in elk hunting and wildlife conservation.
  • Safari Club International (SCI): SCI is a global organization that promotes hunting and wildlife conservation. Their resources help hunters understand ethical hunting practices and the importance of habitat preservation.

By learning about conservation, new hunters can better appreciate the impact their actions have on the environment and the species they pursue.

Conclusion

Becoming a proficient hunter takes time, but the resources available today make it easier than ever to get started. Whether you choose to take a hunter safety course, read books, join online communities, or watch tutorials, there are a wealth of tools and platforms to help guide your journey. As a new hunter, the most important thing is to stay committed to learning and practicing, as hunting is as much about knowledge and experience as it is about skill. Equip yourself with the right information, and your hunting experiences will be both successful and rewarding.
