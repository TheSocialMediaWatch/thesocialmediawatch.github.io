10 Must-Watch Cartoons for Adults in 2024


In the vibrant world of animation, cartoons are no longer just the realm of children. The genre has evolved significantly, catering to adults with complex narratives, dark humor, and thought-provoking themes. As we dive into 2024, a fresh batch of animated series has emerged, captivating audiences with their unique storytelling and artistic styles. Here’s a look at ten must-watch cartoons for adults this year that promise to entertain, challenge, and perhaps even inspire.

1. Rick and Morty (Season 7)

The cult classic continues its cosmic adventures with Season 7, pushing the boundaries of absurdity and intellect. Following the misadventures of the eccentric scientist Rick Sanchez and his good-hearted but fretful grandson Morty, this season promises even more interdimensional escapades. The show is known for its sharp wit, complex character development, and philosophical undertones, making it a staple for any adult animation fan.

Why Watch?

The series skillfully balances humor with deeper themes about family, existence, and the human condition, ensuring a viewing experience that is both entertaining and intellectually stimulating.

2. Big Mouth (Season 7)

Returning for its seventh season, Big Mouth explores the awkwardness of adolescence through the lens of puberty and all its challenges. With its mix of raunchy humor and heartfelt moments, the show remains relevant for adults who can relate to the trials of growing up. The series features a vibrant cast of characters, including hormone monsters and anxiety gremlins, who personify the struggles of teenage life.

Why Watch?

Big Mouth not only delivers laughs but also sparks important conversations about mental health, sexuality, and self-acceptance, making it both a comedic and educational experience.

3. Undone (Season 3)

This groundbreaking series combines live-action with rotoscope animation, creating a visually stunning narrative that explores the boundaries of reality and perception. Following Alma, a young woman who discovers she can manipulate time after a near-death experience, Undone delves into themes of trauma, mental illness, and familial relationships.

Why Watch?

With its innovative animation style and thought-provoking plot, Undone is a unique viewing experience that challenges conventional storytelling, making it a must-watch for those seeking depth in their animation.

4. Solar Opposites (Season 4)

From one of the co-creators of Rick and Morty, Solar Opposites follows a family of aliens attempting to understand life on Earth. The series balances humor with social commentary, exploring themes of assimilation, culture clash, and family dynamics. As the characters navigate their new environment, viewers are treated to a delightful mix of absurdity and heart.

Why Watch?

The clever writing and relatable character arcs make Solar Opposites a standout series that resonates with adult audiences while providing plenty of laughs.

5. The Boondocks (Reboot)

After a long hiatus, The Boondocks returns with fresh episodes that tackle contemporary societal issues through the lens of satire. The show, originally based on Aaron McGruder’s comic strip, remains unapologetically bold in its commentary on race, culture, and politics in America.

Why Watch?

With its sharp humor and relevant themes, the reboot of The Boondocks promises to engage audiences in conversations about societal norms and injustices, all while delivering the iconic wit the series is known for.

6. Tuca & Bertie (Season 3)

This vibrant series about the friendship between two bird-women, Tuca and Bertie, offers a whimsical yet poignant exploration of adulthood. The show addresses topics such as mental health, relationships, and self-discovery with a unique blend of humor and artistry.

Why Watch?

Tuca & Bertie stands out for its colorful animation and relatable storytelling, making it an essential watch for anyone navigating the complexities of adult life.

7. Futurama (Revival)

The beloved series Futurama makes its triumphant return with new episodes that continue the adventures of Fry, Leela, and the rest of the Planet Express crew. Known for its clever satire and imaginative world-building, the revival promises to deliver more of the humor and heart that fans have come to love.

Why Watch?

With its sharp social commentary and beloved characters, the revival of Futurama is a nostalgic yet fresh take on the animated sitcom, appealing to both long-time fans and new viewers alike.

8. Paradise PD (Season 4)

This irreverent animated series about a dysfunctional police department returns for its fourth season, bringing even more outrageous humor and chaotic antics. Known for its over-the-top jokes and absurd scenarios, Paradise PD is not for the faint of heart but offers a hilarious take on law enforcement and small-town life.

Why Watch?

If you’re in the mood for some unapologetic, raucous comedy that doesn’t shy away from the ridiculous, Paradise PD is sure to deliver plenty of laughs.

9. The Legend of Vox Machina (Season 2)

Based on the popular web series Critical Role, this animated fantasy series follows a group of adventurers as they navigate a world filled with magic, monsters, and mayhem. The second season promises to delve deeper into the characters’ backstories while providing epic battles and heartfelt moments.

Why Watch?

The Legend of Vox Machina combines stunning animation with rich storytelling, making it a treat for both fans of fantasy and newcomers alike. The character development and engaging plots ensure that viewers remain invested throughout.

10. Duncanville (Season 4)

This animated series follows the life of Duncan, a teenage boy navigating the trials of adolescence alongside his quirky family and friends. The show combines humor and heartfelt moments, showcasing the ups and downs of growing up in a relatable way.

Why Watch?

With its unique animation style and relatable characters, Duncanville captures the essence of teenage life, making it a perfect pick for adults who enjoy a blend of comedy and nostalgia.

Conclusion

As we venture into 2024, the landscape of adult animation continues to evolve, offering a diverse array of series that cater to a variety of tastes and interests. From the absurdity of Rick and Morty to the heartfelt storytelling of Tuca & Bertie, these cartoons showcase the creativity and depth that the genre can achieve. Whether you’re seeking laughs, insights, or a combination of both, this list of must-watch cartoons for adults is sure to provide plenty of entertainment. So grab your snacks, settle in, and prepare to enjoy the animated wonders that await you this year!
