Best Fantasy Book Series to Dive Into


Fantasy literature has long captured the hearts of readers with its boundless imagination, epic quests, and enchanting worlds. Whether you’re a seasoned fan or a newcomer to the genre, there are countless captivating series that promise to take you on unforgettable journeys. If you’re looking for new worlds to explore, here are some of the best fantasy book series that will transport you into realms of magic, mystery, and adventure.

1. The Lord of the Rings Trilogy by J.R.R. Tolkien

No list of the best fantasy book series would be complete without J.R.R. Tolkien’s iconic The Lord of the Rings. This epic tale of a young hobbit, Frodo Baggins, tasked with destroying a powerful and dangerous ring, has set the standard for high fantasy. Tolkien’s world-building is unparalleled, creating Middle-earth—a land filled with elves, dwarfs, orcs, and men. The series is not only a thrilling adventure but also a deep exploration of friendship, courage, and the battle between good and evil.

Why Read It?

Tolkien’s influence on modern fantasy cannot be overstated. The themes of friendship, sacrifice, and the power of hope resonate with readers of all ages. Moreover, the richly developed cultures, languages, and histories make Middle-earth feel like a living, breathing world.

Books in the Series:

  • The Fellowship of the Ring
  • The Two Towers
  • The Return of the King

2. A Song of Ice and Fire by George R.R. Martin

If you’re looking for a darker, more politically charged fantasy series, A Song of Ice and Fire by George R.R. Martin is the perfect choice. This series, which inspired the hit HBO show Game of Thrones, is set in the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos. The story follows the struggles for power among noble families, the threat of an ancient evil returning, and the survival of individuals caught in a complex web of politics and betrayal.

Why Read It?

George R.R. Martin’s ability to weave multiple storylines and complex characters is one of the defining features of this series. There are no clear-cut heroes or villains—everyone has shades of gray, making each character unpredictable. The intricacies of the political intrigue, combined with the constant threat of warfare, make for a compelling read.

Books in the Series:

  • A Game of Thrones
  • A Clash of Kings
  • A Storm of Swords
  • A Feast for Crows
  • A Dance with Dragons
  • Upcoming The Winds of Winter (anticipated)
  • Upcoming A Dream of Spring (anticipated)

3. The Wheel of Time by Robert Jordan

The Wheel of Time series, which was later completed by Brandon Sanderson after Robert Jordan’s passing, is one of the longest and most intricate fantasy series ever written. Spanning fourteen books, the series is set in a world where time is cyclical, and an ancient prophecy foretells the coming of the Dragon Reborn—an individual who will either save the world or destroy it.

Why Read It?

The expansive world-building and the depth of its characters are what make The Wheel of Time such an enduring series. Jordan’s intricate magic system, complex politics, and philosophical musings on fate and free will are all hallmarks of the series. With the addition of Sanderson’s finishing touch, the series maintains its momentum, offering readers a thrilling, albeit challenging, journey.

Books in the Series:

  • The Eye of the World
  • The Great Hunt
  • The Dragon Reborn
  • The Shadow Rising
  • The Fires of Heaven
  • The Lord of Chaos
  • A Crown of Swords
  • The Path of Daggers
  • Winter’s Heart
  • Crossroads of Twilight
  • Knife of Dreams
  • The Gathering Storm
  • Towers of Midnight
  • A Memory of Light

4. Mistborn by Brandon Sanderson

Brandon Sanderson’s Mistborn series is a brilliant mix of high fantasy, heist, and magic. Set in a world where ash falls from the sky and mist dominates the night, the series follows Vin, a street urchin who discovers she has magical powers linked to metals. As she becomes part of a group of rebels trying to overthrow an oppressive empire, Vin must learn to harness her abilities and confront the terrifying dark forces that threaten her world.

Why Read It?

Sanderson’s unique magic system, based on metals and alloys, is one of the standout features of Mistborn. The series also excels at plotting, pacing, and character development, especially in terms of how Vin grows from a wary, distrustful girl into a powerful force for change. The combination of mystery, action, and philosophical themes makes this series incredibly engaging.

Books in the Series:

  • Mistborn: The Final Empire
  • Mistborn: The Well of Ascension
  • Mistborn: The Hero of Ages

5. The Kingkiller Chronicle by Patrick Rothfuss

For those who appreciate lyrical writing and deeply personal stories, The Kingkiller Chronicle by Patrick Rothfuss offers a captivating blend of fantasy and introspection. The series follows Kvothe, a gifted young man who grows to become a legendary figure. The narrative structure alternates between Kvothe’s present-day life as a wandering musician and his recounting of his past, detailing how he became the subject of myth and legend.

Why Read It?

The beauty of Rothfuss’s writing style and his ability to create emotionally complex characters make The Kingkiller Chronicle stand out. Kvothe’s journey from a curious and talented boy to a man of myth is fascinating. The books also explore themes of knowledge, loss, and the pursuit of personal goals in a world full of magic and mystery.

Books in the Series:

  • The Name of the Wind
  • The Wise Man’s Fear
  • Upcoming The Doors of Stone (anticipated)

6. The Broken Earth Trilogy by N.K. Jemisin

The Broken Earth Trilogy by N.K. Jemisin is a modern classic that challenges traditional fantasy tropes. The series takes place in a world plagued by apocalyptic seismic events known as “seasons.” The story is told from the perspective of Essun, a woman who is thrust into a world of chaos after her family is murdered and her daughter is taken. What follows is an exploration of power, survival, and the complex relationship between oppression and rebellion.

Why Read It?

Jemisin’s work is groundbreaking, as it addresses social issues such as race, power, and oppression while telling a captivating fantasy story. The world-building is richly layered, and the characters are multifaceted and fully realized. The complex plot, coupled with Jemisin’s poetic and thought-provoking prose, makes this trilogy essential for anyone interested in contemporary fantasy.

Books in the Series:

  • The Fifth Season
  • The Obelisk Gate
  • The Stone Sky

7. The Stormlight Archive by Brandon Sanderson

Brandon Sanderson’s The Stormlight Archive is another masterpiece that has taken the fantasy world by storm. The series is set on the storm-ravaged world of Roshar, where magical powers are granted to those who can bond with ancient spren. The central narrative follows multiple characters, each of whom plays a key role in the unfolding battle between ancient forces.

Why Read It?

The Stormlight Archive is a true epic, with grand world-building, an intricate magic system, and multiple interconnected storylines. Sanderson’s skill at developing complex characters and philosophical themes shines through in this series. It’s perfect for readers who enjoy long, immersive fantasy adventures.

Books in the Series:

  • The Way of Kings
  • Words of Radiance
  • Edgedancer (novella)
  • Dawnshard (novella)
  • Rhythm of War

8. The Inheritance Trilogy by N.K. Jemisin

Before Jemisin’s success with The Broken Earth Trilogy, she crafted The Inheritance Trilogy, a brilliant series set in a world where gods walk among mortals, and political intrigue runs deep. The story follows Yeine Darr, who is thrust into a dangerous power struggle after her mother’s murder and the revelation that she is the heir to a powerful and cursed family.

Why Read It?

This series blends mythology, power struggles, and complex characters in a world where gods and mortals are not easily distinguishable. Jemisin’s exploration of power dynamics, identity, and family make this series compelling and relevant, while her poetic prose keeps the reader hooked from start to finish.

Books in the Series:

  • The Hundred Thousand Kingdoms
  • The Broken Kingdoms
  • The Kingdom of Gods

9. The Farseer Trilogy by Robin Hobb

If you’re looking for a series with deeply personal stakes and intricate character development, Robin Hobb’s The Farseer Trilogy is an excellent choice. The story follows FitzChivalry Farseer, a young royal bastard who is trained as an assassin and tasked with protecting the realm from dark forces. The series explores themes of loyalty, duty, and the complexities of relationships, making it a rich and emotionally charged read.

Why Read It?

Robin Hobb is known for creating emotionally complex characters, and Fitz is no exception. The series delves into themes of destiny, loyalty, and sacrifice, making it a deeply personal journey for readers. The world-building is also impressive, with a unique magic system and a fascinating political structure.

Books in the Series:

  • Assassin’s Apprentice
  • Royal Assassin
  • Assassin’s Quest

10. The Magicians Trilogy by Lev Grossman

Lev Grossman’s The Magicians trilogy is often described as a darker, more adult version of Harry Potter. The series follows Quentin Coldwater, a disillusioned teenager who discovers that the magical world he once believed to be fictional is real. As he navigates the highs and lows of magical education, relationships, and adulthood, Quentin must confront his own personal demons and the true nature of power.

Why Read It?

If you’re looking for a more mature and introspective take on magic, The Magicians is the perfect choice. Grossman explores the darker side of fantasy, including themes of depression, disillusionment, and the consequences of seeking power. The series is a compelling mix of magic, coming-of-age, and existential questioning.

Books in the Series:

  • The Magicians
  • The Magician King
  • The Magician’s Land

Conclusion

Fantasy literature offers an escape into worlds full of magic, danger, and intrigue, where anything is possible. Whether you’re drawn to epic battles, complex characters, or philosophical musings, there’s a fantasy series for you. From the classic adventures of Tolkien to the modern twists of Jemisin and Sanderson, these ten series are among the best the genre has to offer. So, grab a book, open your mind, and embark on a journey into the fantastic!
