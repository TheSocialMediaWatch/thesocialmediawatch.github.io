The Most Underrated Games You Need to Play


In a world saturated with blockbuster releases and hyped-up titles, some truly exceptional games quietly slip under the radar. They don’t boast massive marketing budgets or flood your timeline with flashy trailers, yet they offer unforgettable experiences that can resonate deeply with players. These underrated gems often bring unique storytelling, innovative gameplay mechanics, or artistic brilliance that rivals or even surpasses mainstream hits.

If you’re looking to enrich your gaming library and dive into lesser-known adventures that deserve more shine, this comprehensive guide highlights some of the most underrated games across various genres and platforms. Whether you crave emotional impact, challenging gameplay, or fresh ideas, these titles are primed to surprise and captivate you.

1. Outer Wilds

At first glance, Outer Wilds might appear as another space exploration game, but it cleverly blends a time-loop mechanic with mystery and profound storytelling. Players explore a small solar system stuck in a 22-minute time loop, tasked with uncovering secrets before the cycle resets.

What makes Outer Wilds exceptional is its organic story development, driven by player curiosity rather than guided objectives. Each celestial body tells its own story — from ancient ruins to volatile planets — requiring keen observation and experimental gameplay. Its atmospheric sound design and clever puzzles make each discovery feel like unearthing hidden treasure.

Despite critical acclaim in some circles, Outer Wilds remains underplayed by many due to niche appeal and a quiet launch. Players who cherish open-world exploration and narrative depth should not miss this mind-bending journey through time and space.

2. A Plague Tale: Innocence

Set in the plague-ridden France of the 14th century, A Plague Tale: Innocence is a haunting, story-driven action-adventure game. The game follows siblings Amicia and Hugo as they navigate a world devastated by disease, hostile soldiers, and burgeoning rat infestations.

The emotional weight of the sibling bond and the anxiety-inducing survival moments lift this title far beyond typical stealth or action fare. The subtle blend of historical context with supernatural elements creates a stark contrast that deeply engages players.

Despite receiving positive reviews, A Plague Tale seems to lack the broad audience it deserves, hidden behind the shadow of more prominent titles in the genre. It’s a masterclass in atmosphere-building, storytelling, and stealth mechanics, perfect for players seeking heartfelt narratives.

3. Hellblade: Senua’s Sacrifice

Hellblade: Senua’s Sacrifice stands as a striking example of video games as art. The experience is an intimate portrayal of psychosis, wrapped in a dark fantasy action-adventure. Following Senua, a Pict warrior battling inner demons and external enemies, the game explores themes rarely touched upon in mainstream titles.

The developers worked closely with mental health experts to craft an authentic experience portraying psychosis with empathy and nuance. The audio design, blending whispers and hallucinations, pulls players directly into Senua’s fractured mind.

While it received critical praise, many gamers overlook Hellblade due to its intense themes and relatively short length. However, the game’s impact lingers, offering an unforgettable narrative journey paired with responsive combat mechanics and atmospheric visuals.

4. Kentucky Route Zero

If you’re drawn to games that focus on storytelling over action, Kentucky Route Zero offers a uniquely surreal narrative experience. It’s an episodic adventure game set in a magical realist version of rural Kentucky, following a truck driver on a mysterious route through secret highways and strange encounters.

The game’s poetic dialogue and striking visual style create a dreamlike atmosphere that lingers in the mind long after play sessions. Unlike conventional adventure games dependent on puzzles, this title prioritizes player reflection and exploration of themes such as debt, family, and folklore.

Its subtle pacing and atmospheric storytelling have captured a small but devoted audience. For those open to experimental narrative games, Kentucky Route Zero is an evocative journey into the surreal.

5. Sable

Exploration games are often measured by their sprawling worlds and abundant collectibles, but Sable redefines the genre with a minimalist aesthetic and a strong sense of discovery. The player controls Sable, a young nomad embarking on a rite of passage through a vast desert littered with ancient ruins, alien landscapes, and remnants of long-lost civilizations.

The visual style is inspired by graphic novels and line art, giving the game a distinctive look that stands out amid photorealistic counterparts. Movement and traversal, using a hoverbike across dunes and settlements, provide a fluid and calming experience.

Sable’s strength lies in its world-building and the subtle stories woven into environmental details and characters. Its quiet charm and meditative tone often fly under the radar, but those who try it tend to find something truly special.

6. Return of the Obra Dinn

From the mind of Lucas Pope, Return of the Obra Dinn is a puzzle game unlike any other. Set aboard an East India Company ship in the early 1800s, players must piece together the fate of the missing crew using a magical pocket watch that shows the exact moment of death.

The art style mimics 1-bit computer graphics, a stunning choice that makes the game visually striking and readable. The deductive gameplay requires intense observation and logical thinking to match faces, causes of death, and events, making it deeply rewarding to complete.

Despite winning awards, Return of the Obra Dinn remains an underplayed masterpiece due to its unconventional visuals and methodical pacing. Puzzle enthusiasts and mystery lovers will find it an intellectually stimulating challenge.

7. Sunless Sea

For fans of rich writing and Lovecraftian horror, Sunless Sea offers a deep rogue-like experience focusing on exploration, survival, and storytelling. Players captain a steamship sailing an underground ocean dotted with islands, monsters, and mysteries.

The game emphasizes narrative choice, with branching storylines and atmospheric lore that make each voyage unique yet perilous. Resource management, crew morale, and encounter outcomes add layers of tension and replayability.

Its steep learning curve and unforgiving nature likely contribute to Sunless Sea’s underrated status. However, those with patience and a taste for dark storytelling will find it an addictive and memorable adventure.

8. Katana ZERO

If you’re craving a fast, stylish action platformer with a gripping story, Katana ZERO delivers both with flair. The game features tight, one-hit-kill combat combined with a narrative inflected by time manipulation and psychological thriller elements.

Players slice through levels using a katana and special abilities, replaying segments to perfect their moves while unraveling a layered story about memory, trauma, and identity.

Its pixel art style and synthwave soundtrack complement the game’s neon-noir mood. Despite glowing reviews, Katana ZERO remains somewhat underappreciated in the indie action genre, perfect for players seeking a challenging yet story-rich gameplay experience.

9. Oxenfree

Oxenfree is a supernatural thriller adventure game that expertly captures adolescent dialogue and eerie atmospheres. The story revolves around a group of friends who accidentally open a ghostly rift during an island party.

The game’s unique continuous dialogue system keeps conversations natural and reactive, while its unsettling story and haunting soundtrack build a compelling narrative experience.

Oxenfree did well in indie circles but deserves wider recognition for its innovative approach to storytelling and its ability to deliver tension without relying on jump scares. Its emotional depth and engaging mystery make it worth exploring.

10. Hollow Knight

While Hollow Knight has gained a strong cult following, it remains surprisingly underrated compared to larger platformers and metroidvania games. It boasts an epic, interconnected world filled with beautifully hand-drawn art, challenging combat, and atmospheric music.

Exploring the abandoned kingdom of Hallownest reveals lore through environmental storytelling and cryptic NPC encounters. The game strikes a fine balance between punishing difficulty and rewarding exploration.

Players who dive into Hollow Knight discover an expansive and immersive experience that redefines indie metroidvanias, proving that sometimes hidden indie games can stand shoulder to shoulder with AAA titles.

Why These Games Matter

The titles listed above share a common thread — they each offer something beyond the typical, whether it’s through inventive storytelling, artistic vision, or gameplay innovation. In a market where big budgets and loud marketing often dictate exposure, these games prove that creativity and heartfelt craft still thrive all around.

Playing underrated games not only enriches your gaming experience but supports the diverse voices and creators who push the boundaries of what games can be. So next time you’re hunting for your next game, consider stepping off the beaten path. These hidden gems may just become your new favorites.

Final Thoughts

Exploring underrated games expands your horizons and breathing fresh life into your gaming rotation. From the emotional depth of A Plague Tale to the mind-bending puzzle mystery of Return of the Obra Dinn, the choices here offer something unique for a wide variety of players.

Don’t be afraid to embrace the unknown. These games showcase that some of the most powerful and memorable gaming experiences can come from the quiet corners of the industry — waiting for players bold enough to seek them out.

Whether you’re a seasoned gamer looking for something new or an enthusiast eager to discover hidden treasures, immersing yourself in these underrated titles guarantees rewarding adventures that go beyond the mainstream. Happy gaming!
