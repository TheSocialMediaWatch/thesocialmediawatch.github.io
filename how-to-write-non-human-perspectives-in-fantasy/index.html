How to Write Non-Human Perspectives in Fantasy


Fantasy literature thrives on the extraordinary—worlds beyond our own, creatures that defy natural laws, magic systems that bend reality. A powerful way to deepen your fantasy writing is by exploring perspectives beyond human eyes. Writing non-human viewpoints isn’t just a gimmick; it’s a gateway to fresh storytelling avenues, immersive world-building, and profound thematic exploration.

In this article, we’ll dive deep into the art and craft of writing non-human perspectives in fantasy. Whether you’re drawing from the viewpoint of an elf, a dragon, a sentient tree, or an alien lifeform, understanding how to convincingly convey a truly non-human mind can elevate your story to unforgettable heights.

Understanding the Challenge: Thinking Beyond Humanity

The crux of writing a non-human perspective lies in stepping outside the mental, emotional, and sensory framework that defines human experience. Characters of other species often possess:

  • Different modes of perception (e.g., echolocation, infrared sight, pheromone detection)
  • Alternative biological needs and drives
  • Varied cognitive patterns, possibly more primal, alien, or abstract
  • Cultural and social norms unlike anything human
  • Distinct relationships to time, space, and environment

Successfully writing from these vantage points means not just changing surface details but fundamentally reimagining consciousness itself.

Ask yourself: What does existence feel like for this creature? Which sensations dominate? How does it solve problems? How does it communicate? How does it view other beings in the world?

1. Choose the Right Non-Human Entity for Your Narrative

First, consider the impact of your chosen viewpoint on the story’s themes and plot. Different non-human perspectives serve different narrative purposes:

  • Animals or beasts might highlight instinct, survival, or the clash between civilization and nature.
  • Mythical creatures such as dragons or unicorns can embody elemental forces or moral archetypes.
  • Aliens often challenge human assumptions about identity, society, and perception.
  • Elementals or spirits allow exploration of metaphysical existence or nature’s mysteries.
  • Artificial intelligences or constructs provoke questions about consciousness and what it means to be alive.

Each option brings unique challenges and advantages for storytelling.

2. Research and Build Believable Biology and Ecology

The physical form of your creature significantly informs its worldview. A being that flies, burrows, breathes fire, or communicates via bioluminescence perceives and interacts with the world very differently than humans.

Tips:

  • Develop a plausible biological background, even if fantastical.
  • Consider the creature’s senses and how they shape reality.
  • Think about its diet, predators, mates, habitat, and lifespan.
  • Reflect these traits in behavior, priorities, and interaction styles.

For example, a nocturnal predator might have a vastly different relationship to light and shadow, or an aquatic creature must conceptualize three-dimensional movement in ways terrestrial beings cannot.

3. Adjust the Mental and Emotional Framework

Humans are emotionally complex but also culturally conditioned. Non-human perspectives can differ in emotional range, logic, and reasoning patterns:

  • Does the entity experience emotions as intense as humans, or are they more subdued or alien?
  • Is its cognition abstract, instinctual, communal, or highly individualistic?
  • How does it prioritize survival, curiosity, attachment, or honor?
  • How does it understand concepts like death, truth, or justice?

Creating an internally coherent psychological profile enriches authenticity.

4. Reimagine Language and Communication

Language characterizes thought, identity, and culture. To portray a non-human point of view, consider how it communicates—through speech, telepathy, gesture, pheromones, or other mechanisms. This affects:

  • Internal monologue and narration tone
  • How interactions with others occur
  • How emotions and thoughts are expressed

You don’t need to invent entirely new languages (though that can deepen immersion), but tweaking vocabulary, syntax, and metaphor to fit the creature’s mode of thought enhances realism.

5. Use Sensory Details to Convey a Different Experience of Reality

A non-human perspective provides a perfect opportunity for vivid sensory descriptions unlike those based on human experience. The goal isn’t just to swap one sense for another but to immerse the reader in the creature’s unique sensory world.

For instance:

  • A wolf-like creature’s focus on smell and hearing over sight
  • A giant insect’s perception of umwelt (the surrounding sensory environment)
  • An elemental’s connection with temperature or vibrations

These details immerse readers in a new dimension and enhance storytelling texture.

6. Maintain a Balance Between Alienness and Relatability

One of the trickiest parts of writing non-human perspectives is walking the fine line between alien and accessible. If the character’s cognition is too foreign, the reader may struggle to connect; if too human, the perspective may feel shallow or unconvincing.

Hints to strike this balance:

  • Ground the story in universal emotions or themes—even if expressed differently.
  • Use familiar narrative structures, but filter them through alien viewpoints.
  • Provide occasional touchstones that anchor the reader emotionally.

Remember, the goal is to invite readers to inhabit another mind, not to confuse or alienate them.

7. Incorporate Worldbuilding Through Perspective

Non-human viewpoints enrich worldbuilding naturally. Their perceptions and beliefs reveal cultural practices, ecological relationships, and mythologies unique to your fantasy realm.

Examples:

  • How an elven society’s view of time reflects their immortality
  • The role of a dragon’s territorial instincts in shaping political structures
  • A sentient tree’s millennial perspective on human activity

Such perspectives deepen the setting and integrate character and world seamlessly.

8. Experiment with Narrative Structure and Voice

Non-human characters may narrate stories differently. Consider variations in tense, fragmented or cyclical time, telepathic or communal narrations, or even a lack of linear storytelling.

Playing with narrative form can reflect the character’s mental wiring and enrich the reading experience. Just ensure clarity isn’t sacrificed for novelty.

9. Avoid Stereotypes and Clichés

Fantasy often defaults to stock representations—the noble elf, the ruthless orc, the wise dragon. To truly engage readers, deepen these portrayals with nuanced characterization.

  • Challenge expected traits with unique backstories or motivations.
  • Show internal conflicts or contradictions.
  • Flesh out non-human cultures as complex societies.

This makes your non-human perspective fresh, surprising, and memorable.

10. Practice and Revise with Feedback

Writing authentic non-human perspectives is a skill honed over time:

  • Write short scenes from your creature’s viewpoint to test voice and language.
  • Read widely, especially authors who excel at alien or animal perspectives (e.g., Octavia Butler, Jeff VanderMeer).
  • Seek beta readers who can flag confusing or inconsistent passages.
  • Revise to strengthen voice and clarity.

Iterate until the narrative feels both believable and compelling.

Conclusion: The Power of Non-Human Perspectives

Embracing non-human points of view opens up boundless creative horizons. It challenges writers to explore consciousness, reimagine reality, and build immersive fantasy worlds that resonate deeply.

The technique goes beyond fantasy tropes—it’s an invitation to rethink what it means to perceive, feel, and exist. By carefully crafting biology, psychology, language, and narrative style, you can bring vivid, authentic non-human voices to life on the page.

Whether you’re crafting an epic saga seen through the eyes of a dragon, an intimate tale from a fairy’s viewpoint, or a cosmic odyssey narrated by alien minds, mastering non-human perspectives enriches your storytelling with originality and depth.

Happy writing!
