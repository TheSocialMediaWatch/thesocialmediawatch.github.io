Hunting Gear for Dove Hunting: Must-Have Accessories


When it comes to dove hunting, having the right gear can make all the difference between a successful and enjoyable hunt and a frustrating experience. While the equipment needed for dove hunting may seem straightforward, there are some must-have accessories that can enhance your hunting experience and increase your chances of a successful outing. In this guide, we’ll explore the essential gear that every dove hunter should consider adding to their arsenal.

1. Shotgun

The most crucial piece of equipment for dove hunting is, of course, the shotgun. When choosing a shotgun for dove hunting, consider a 12 or 20-gauge shotgun with a modified or improved cylinder choke. This setup provides a good balance between power and pattern spread, making it ideal for taking down fast-flying doves. Additionally, ensure that the shotgun is comfortable to shoulder and carry for extended periods in the field.

2. Ammunition

Selecting the right ammunition is critical for a successful dove hunt. Opt for light loads such as 7.5, 8, or 9 shot, as these loads are effective for taking down doves without damaging the meat. It’s also essential to practice shooting with the chosen ammunition to become familiar with its pattern and performance.

3. Shooting Glasses

Protecting your eyes during a hunt is paramount, and shooting glasses provide both eye protection and enhanced visibility. Look for glasses with polarized lenses to reduce glare and improve your ability to spot doves against the sky. Additionally, shooting glasses can protect your eyes from any debris or branches that may be encountered in the field.

4. Camouflage Clothing

Blend into your surroundings with appropriate camouflage clothing. Opt for lightweight, breathable attire that matches the terrain where you’ll be hunting. Camouflage helps break up your outline, making it more difficult for doves to detect your presence, increasing your chances of a successful hunt.

5. Decoys

Using dove decoys can attract passing doves to your hunting area, increasing your chances of a successful hunt. Consider using both stationary decoys and motorized decoys to add movement and realism to your setup. Strategically placing decoys can lure doves into range, providing you with more opportunities to take a shot.

6. Hunting Vest

A well-designed hunting vest can keep your essential gear organized and easily accessible. Look for a vest with ample pockets for storing extra ammunition, water, snacks, and other necessities. Additionally, a padded shoulder area can provide comfort and reduce recoil when taking shots.

7. Shooting Stool or Chair

Dove hunting often involves extended periods of waiting for the perfect shot. A lightweight, portable shooting stool or chair can provide comfort during long hours in the field. Look for a stool or chair that is easy to carry and set up, allowing you to stay comfortable and focused while awaiting your quarry.

8. Water and Snacks

Staying hydrated and nourished is crucial during any hunting expedition. Pack plenty of water and high-energy snacks to keep you fueled and focused throughout the day. Consider lightweight, non-perishable options that are easy to carry in your hunting vest or pack.

9. Game Bag

A game bag is essential for carrying harvested doves and keeping them in good condition. Look for a bag with breathable material to prevent spoilage and enough space to accommodate your anticipated harvest. Additionally, a game bag with adjustable straps can be worn comfortably while moving through the hunting area.

10. First Aid Kit

Accidents can happen in the field, so it’s essential to have a basic first aid kit on hand. Pack essentials such as bandages, antiseptic wipes, pain relievers, and any necessary personal medications. Being prepared for minor injuries ensures that you can address any mishaps quickly and continue enjoying your hunt.

By equipping yourself with these must-have accessories for dove hunting, you can enhance your hunting experience while increasing your chances of a successful outing. Remember to prioritize safety, follow local hunting regulations, and practice ethical hunting behavior at all times. With the right gear and preparation, dove hunting can be a fulfilling and rewarding outdoor pursuit.
