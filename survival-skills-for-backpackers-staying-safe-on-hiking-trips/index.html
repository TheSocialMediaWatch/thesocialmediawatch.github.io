Survival Skills for Backpackers: Staying Safe on Hiking Trips


Hiking is a thrilling and adventurous activity that allows you to explore the great outdoors and connect with nature. However, it’s important to remember that the wilderness can be unpredictable, and being prepared with essential survival skills is crucial for your safety. In this blog post, we will discuss some vital survival skills for backpackers to ensure a safe and enjoyable hiking trip.

1. Navigation

One of the most important skills for backpackers is the ability to navigate through unfamiliar terrain. Always carry a map and a compass, and make sure you know how to use them. Familiarize yourself with the trail you will be hiking, and pay attention to landmarks along the way. If possible, invest in a GPS device or a hiking app for added safety and convenience.

2. Emergency Shelter

In case of unexpected weather changes or an emergency situation, having the knowledge and tools to build an emergency shelter can be a lifesaver. Carry a lightweight tarp or a space blanket that can provide protection from rain, wind, and cold temperatures. Learn basic shelter-building techniques and practice setting up a shelter before your hiking trip.

3. Water Purification

Access to clean drinking water is essential for survival. While hiking, it’s important to carry enough water to stay hydrated, but it’s also crucial to know how to purify water from natural sources. Invest in a water filter or carry water purification tablets to eliminate harmful bacteria and viruses. Boiling water is also an effective method of purification.

4. Fire Building

Fire not only provides warmth but also acts as a signal for rescuers and helps deter wildlife. Learn how to build a fire using natural materials such as dry leaves, twigs, and branches. Carry waterproof matches or a lighter, and practice fire-building techniques in a controlled environment before your hiking trip. Always remember to follow fire safety guidelines and check if campfires are allowed in the area.

5. First Aid

Accidents can happen anywhere, and having basic first aid knowledge can make a significant difference in emergency situations. Carry a well-stocked first aid kit that includes bandages, antiseptic ointment, pain relievers, and any necessary medications. Familiarize yourself with basic first aid techniques, such as treating cuts, sprains, and insect bites.

6. Food and Foraging

While it’s important to pack enough food for your hiking trip, unexpected situations may arise where you find yourself running out of supplies. Learn about edible plants and berries that are safe to consume in the wilderness. However, be cautious and make sure to properly identify plants before eating them. It’s always better to carry extra food rather than relying solely on foraging.

7. Wildlife Awareness

Encountering wildlife is an exciting part of hiking, but it’s important to respect their habitat and ensure your safety. Research the wildlife present in the area you will be hiking and learn how to react in different situations. Keep a safe distance and never approach or feed wild animals. Store your food securely to avoid attracting wildlife to your campsite.

8. Leave No Trace

As responsible backpackers, it’s crucial to minimize our impact on the environment. Follow the principles of Leave No Trace, which include packing out all trash, disposing of human waste properly, and respecting wildlife and vegetation. By practicing Leave No Trace, we can preserve the natural beauty of hiking trails for future generations to enjoy.

In conclusion, survival skills are essential for backpackers to ensure their safety and well-being while hiking. By being prepared and knowledgeable about navigation, emergency shelter, water purification, fire building, first aid, food and foraging, wildlife awareness, and practicing Leave No Trace principles, you can confidently embark on your hiking trips and enjoy the wonders of nature. Remember, safety should always be your top priority. Happy hiking!
