Crafting Classic Whiskey Cocktails: Old Fashioned, Manhattan, and More


The art of crafting classic whiskey cocktails has been around for centuries. From the Old Fashioned to the Manhattan, these iconic drinks have stood the test of time, offering a rich taste of history with every sip. In this post, we’ll delve into the world of classic whiskey cocktails, exploring the ins and outs of two timeless favorites: the Old Fashioned and the Manhattan. We’ll also discuss the key elements that make these cocktails true classics and reveal a few more lesser-known gems for you to try.

The Old Fashioned

The Old Fashioned is one of the most well-known and enduring whiskey cocktails, dating back to the 19th century. While its origins are debated, the cocktail is simple and straightforward, listing only four ingredients: whiskey, sugar, bitters, and a slice of orange.

To craft a perfect Old Fashioned, follow these steps:

  1. Prepare a glass: Take a rocks glass and gently coat the rim with orange twist-infused simple syrup, ensuring an even coverage.

  2. Muddle ingredients: Add a couple of dashes of bitters and two dashes of simple syrup to the glass. Muddle gently to mix and soften the flavors.

  3. Add whiskey: Pour in two ounces of your preferred bourbon or rye whiskey. Stir gently to create a uniform, honey-like texture.

  4. Garnish and serve: Express an orange peel over the glass, and either eat it or discard it, depending on your preference. Fill the glass with ice, stir once more, and enjoy.

The Manhattan

The Manhattan, dating back to the late 19th century, is another timeless whiskey cocktail. This elegant drink is a blend of whiskey, vermouth, and a dash of bitters, garnished with a cherry or twist of lemon peel.

To master the Manhattan:

  1. Prepare a glass: Fill a mixing glass with ice and add two ounces of rye whiskey and one ounce of sweet vermouth.

  2. Add flavor: Stir in two dashes of angostura bitters, stirring gently to fully incorporate the flavors.

  3. Strain and garnish: Strain the mixture into a chilled lowball glass and garnish it with a maraschino cherry or lemon twist, depending on your preference.

  4. Serve chilled: Enjoy your Manhattan chilled to perfection.

More Classic Whiskey Cocktails

While the Old Fashioned and Manhattan remain wildly popular, several other classic whiskey cocktails are perfect for the discerning drinker. Among them are:

  • The Whiskey Sour, a lively drink combining whiskey, lemon juice, and sweetener, often garnished with a cherry and a lemon twist.
  • The Sazerac, New Orleans’ famous cousin to the Old Fashioned, made with rye whiskey, absinthe, and bitters.
  • The Rob Roy, essentially a Manhattan made with Scotch whiskey instead of rye.

With time-honored classics like the Old Fashioned and Manhattan, as well as additional iconic cocktails, whiskey remains an enduring spirit worthy of exploration and savoring. If you’re looking for another level of sophistication, the classics are the perfect place to start.
