The Best Stand-Up Specials for a Good Laugh


Laughter is the best medicine, and when life gets stressful, there’s nothing like a good stand-up comedy special to lighten the mood. Stand-up comedy offers something for everyone – whether it’s biting satire, observational humor, or silly absurdity. The beauty of a stand-up special is its simplicity; just a talented comedian on stage, delivering jokes that connect us through shared experiences. With so many specials available today, it can be hard to know where to start. So, if you’re in need of a good laugh, here’s a list of some of the best stand-up specials that are sure to bring a smile to your face.

1. Dave Chappelle: Sticks & Stones (2019)

Dave Chappelle is one of the most iconic figures in stand-up comedy today. His 2019 special Sticks & Stones earned both praise and controversy, but it’s undeniable that this special delivers one of the most unique stand-up performances of the decade. Chappelle’s knack for pushing boundaries while also making deep social commentary is on full display in this special.

From dissecting the #MeToo movement to tackling cancel culture and politics, Chappelle doesn’t shy away from the tough topics. However, it’s his ability to weave these difficult conversations into punchlines that makes his comedy resonate. If you’re looking for a stand-up special that is both thought-provoking and hilarious, Sticks & Stones is a must-watch.

2. Hannah Gadsby: Nanette (2018)

When Hannah Gadsby performed Nanette, it completely redefined what stand-up comedy could be. This special doesn’t follow the typical structure of jokes and punchlines. Instead, Gadsby uses the format to tell a deeply personal story about her life, the struggles of being a woman in the comedy world, and the pain she’s endured. But it’s not all dark – Gadsby’s wit and sharp humor still shine through.

Nanette is a groundbreaking special because it challenges the conventional boundaries of comedy. It blurs the lines between comedy and drama, making us laugh, think, and feel deeply. Gadsby’s ability to flip the script on what audiences expect from stand-up comedy makes Nanette an unforgettable experience.

3. John Mulaney: Kid Gorgeous (2018)

John Mulaney’s Kid Gorgeous is one of the most consistently hilarious stand-up specials of recent years. Known for his impeccable timing and clever storytelling, Mulaney takes audiences on a journey through his childhood, his experiences as a writer on Saturday Night Live, and his awkwardness as an adult. His ability to deliver punchlines with such ease and charm makes this special a crowd-pleaser.

One of the highlights of Kid Gorgeous is Mulaney’s story about his encounter with a group of children during a school assembly. The way he sets up the joke and delivers the punchline is nothing short of comedic genius. Whether he’s recounting stories from his past or observing everyday absurdities, Mulaney’s wit is sharp, making this special a must-watch for anyone who appreciates smart, observational humor.

4. Ali Wong: Baby Cobra (2016)

Ali Wong burst onto the comedy scene with Baby Cobra, her hilarious special that showcases her unique voice in the stand-up world. What makes Baby Cobra so special is Wong’s ability to combine her fierce, unapologetic humor with her experience as a woman of color navigating pregnancy and motherhood. She doesn’t hold back, offering a fresh perspective on the challenges and absurdities of life, all while delivering laughs in her own distinct style.

Whether she’s poking fun at her pregnancy cravings or offering a no-holds-barred take on the expectations of women, Wong’s comedy is bold, honest, and uproarious. Baby Cobra is a standout special that perfectly captures the essence of Wong’s comedic talent, making it a must-see for comedy fans looking for something different.

5. Chris Rock: Tamborine (2018)

Chris Rock is a legend in the world of stand-up comedy, and his 2018 special Tamborine proves why. In this special, Rock delves into personal topics like his divorce, his experiences with parenting, and his thoughts on race and society. What’s striking about Tamborine is Rock’s vulnerability. While he’s known for his sharp, observational humor, here he mixes in moments of introspection, making this special feel more like a reflection on his life and career.

There’s a raw honesty in Rock’s delivery that makes Tamborine one of his most powerful specials. His trademark style of humor – both clever and biting – remains intact, but the emotional depth he brings to the stage adds a new layer of complexity to his comedy.

6. Bo Burnham: Inside (2021)

Bo Burnham’s Inside isn’t just a stand-up special – it’s a deep dive into Burnham’s mind during the COVID-19 pandemic. Created entirely by Burnham himself in a single room, this special blends comedy with music, surrealism, and a touch of existential dread. The special is both funny and deeply unsettling at times, as Burnham explores his struggles with isolation, mental health, and the overwhelming nature of modern life.

Inside is a bold departure from traditional stand-up. Burnham’s combination of comedy, music, and visual storytelling makes it an experience rather than just a special. Whether you’re laughing at his quirky songs or feeling a pang of discomfort at the honesty behind his humor, Inside is an unforgettable and unique take on the stand-up genre.

7. Ricky Gervais: Humanity (2018)

Ricky Gervais is known for his edgy, no-holds-barred approach to comedy, and Humanity is a perfect example of that. In this special, Gervais takes on the absurdities of fame, human nature, and social media, with his signature brand of irreverence and dark humor. His style is confrontational, but it’s also insightful, as he holds up a mirror to society and pokes fun at its flaws.

Gervais is a master of observational comedy, and his ability to tackle sensitive subjects like religion and politics with a sense of irreverence makes Humanity both provocative and hilarious. If you enjoy comedy that challenges societal norms while making you laugh out loud, this special is definitely worth watching.

8. Tiffany Haddish: Black Mitzvah (2019)

Tiffany Haddish’s Black Mitzvah is a celebration of her success, culture, and life experiences. In this special, Haddish shares her journey from a difficult childhood to becoming one of the most successful comedians in Hollywood. Her raw, unfiltered humor shines through as she talks about everything from her experiences as a woman of color to the challenges of navigating the entertainment industry.

Black Mitzvah is filled with joy, energy, and an infectious sense of humor that’s impossible to resist. Haddish’s storytelling ability and charisma make this special a fun ride from start to finish. If you’re looking for a stand-up special that combines personal anecdotes with bold humor, Black Mitzvah delivers.

9. Kevin Hart: Irresponsible (2019)

Kevin Hart has built a reputation as one of the biggest names in comedy, and his 2019 special Irresponsible continues to showcase his larger-than-life personality and comedic timing. In this special, Hart takes a look at his own mistakes, from his infidelity scandal to the absurdities of his daily life. As always, Hart’s delivery is energetic and filled with self-deprecating humor, making it easy to relate to even as he laughs at his own misfortunes.

What makes Irresponsible so enjoyable is Hart’s ability to turn his personal struggles into a source of comedy. His charismatic stage presence and natural storytelling ability ensure that Irresponsible remains engaging throughout, making it one of his best specials.

10. Eddie Murphy: Delirious (1983)

No list of the best stand-up specials would be complete without mentioning Eddie Murphy’s Delirious. This 1983 special is a classic, and it remains one of the most influential stand-up performances of all time. Murphy’s energy, charisma, and fearless approach to comedy revolutionized the genre, and Delirious set the standard for future comedians to follow.

From his hilarious impersonations of his family members to his unforgettable jokes about everything from relationships to pop culture, Delirious is filled with iconic moments. While some of the material may feel dated by today’s standards, Murphy’s delivery and timing still hold up, making Delirious a must-watch for any comedy fan.

Conclusion

Stand-up comedy has a unique way of bringing people together through laughter. The comedians on this list have mastered the art of making us laugh, but also of making us think, feel, and reflect. Whether you’re in the mood for sharp, socially conscious humor or lighthearted observational comedy, there’s something for everyone. So, the next time you need a good laugh, queue up one of these stand-up specials and let the comedy work its magic.
