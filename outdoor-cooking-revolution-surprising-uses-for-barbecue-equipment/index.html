Outdoor Cooking Revolution: Surprising Uses for Barbecue Equipment


Summer days are simply not complete without a good ol’ barbecue party with friends and family. But what if I told you that barbecue equipment isn’t just limited to the standard outdoor grilling? Imagine breaking out of the box and discovering new, innovative ways to use your barbecue tools. Prepare to embark on a journey through the mouthwatering, surprising uses for barbecue equipment.

  1. Grill - The All-Round Survivor

Firstly, let’s talk about that sturdy grill. While it’s seemingly made for barbecuing meats and vegetables, there are some unforeseen ways to utilize it. One of these makes use of its ability to even out heat, perfect for rescuing your morning eggs that got cold while toasting your bread. Fire up your grill, place it on low heat, toast your bread as before, and let your eggs cook beside it. Poaching your eggs just got easier!

  1. BBQ Skewers - Uncommon Culinary Tools

Who would have thought that BBQ skewers would have such a variety of uses? Yep, it’s not just about threading meats or vegetables. Put a little ingenuity into action and you’ve got an all-new cooking gadget.

Trying to cool down a piping hot dish? Simply toss some skewers into the pot, and let the food sit on top of it. The higher surface area allows heat to distribute evenly, and the result is a perfectly chilled meal without any lumps.

Tried cutting an avocado or papaya only to find it too squishy to hold steady? Skewer them and hold steady to more easily slice them without the mess.

  1. Pizza Oven Transformation

Can’t wait for the weekend to enjoy a good slice of homemade pizza outdoors? Turns out, your grill can become a personal brick oven in no time. Pause the anticipation and let your grill dreams come true.

Coat your grill grates with high-temperature oil to minimize the sticking risk. Preheat the grill to around 500°F (260°C), and build a small fire on one side for a precise temperature control. Use a pizza stone for even cooking and the perfect finishing outcome, you’ll get the crispy crust you crave.

  1. Garlic Peeler - DIY Style

If you’re the type who despises peeling garlic, here’s your saving grace. Talk about the underdog story; your BBQ skewer is stepping up to the challenge. Given its sturdy and pointed nature, it’s the perfect tool for a DIY garlic peeler.

All you have to do is lay down a few cloves or more, in a straight line, on a cutting board. Using the back of a heavy knife or the flat side of a cleaver, press down firmly and quickly along the length of the skewer. Watch as your garlic peels effortlessly in its skin, and discard the skin only for a flawlessly peeled garlic experience.

  1. Great Smoker Experiment

Who said the smoker is only meant for slow cooking large cuts of meat? The versatility lies within the technique. Enter the world of smoking fruits and vegetables. Herbal smokers, fruit-infused cocktails, vegetable smokers – yes, it’s all yours. Low and slow cooking your chosen veggies will add a dimension of smoky flavor that pairs wonderfully across different dishes. Consider adding dehydrated vegetables to a spice rub for an extra kick or sprinkling smoked nuts over desserts for a rich, smoky flavor.

  1. Ice Bucket Secret

Summer days may heat up, but that doesn’t mean the herbs on your windowsill will die from neglect. In fact, it’s the perfect time to let the flavors infuse. Grab your trusty stainless steel bucket with a lid, stuff it with ice, your herbs, and a towel, seal it up and leave thriving outdoors.

Those once-strong flavors don’t have to suffer in the name of balmy nights. Once infusing is complete, simply blend the herbs with a bit of olive oil for a tantalizing, homemade herb-infused oil perfect for dishes, dips, or drizzling over your next mesa.

  1. Crafty Teapot Technique

Skip the stove, and bring your hot water or tea blend to a boil in your kettle or pot with a spout. Once boiling, swap it out for your trusted BBQ kettle - just don’t forget to place a trivet or heat diffuser underneath to prevent the heat damage to your table. With this setup, your kettle or teapot can enjoy the even, radiant heat of the grill to boil water, steep tea, or warm soup for your outdoor tea party.

  1. Disposable BBQ to Portable Oven

Fed up with going all the way back inside just to flip the burger? Introduce a portable oven into the barbecue mix. Upcycling a disposable BBQ, transfer it onto a rack or inverted wok over your direct heat for temperature contrast. Surprise your guests with a quick & easily assembled portable grill island.

The outdoor cooking revolution isn’t about adding (or spending) more. Instead, it’s experimenting with the resources you have, and finding inventive ways in making the most of them. Let’s hope these uses for your barbecue equipment inspire you to venture into new culinary adventures and start a new tradition.
