Unboxing New Talent: Emerging Music Acts to Watch in 2023


As we dive headfirst into the new year, it’s the perfect time to keep our eyes peeled for emerging music talent. The music scene is ever-evolving, full of surprises, and constantly delivering first-rate music, and 2023 is no exception. Here are ten up-and-coming artists and bands to keep on your radar this year.

1. Big Thief

Even though this indie rock band from Brooklyn has been around for a bit, their second and third albums released in 2019 and 2021 respectively really put them on the map. They’ve garnered acclaim for their emotive songwriting and ethereal harmonies.

2. Arlo Parks

This London-based artist has been at the cusp of super-stardom for a while now. With her poetic lyrics and jazzy vocal abilities, Arlo Parks’ 2020 debut album ‘Collapsed in Sunbeams’ wowed both critics and fans alike. She’s been proclaimed by many to be the voice of her generation, and this is just the beginning.

3. Wet Leg

This Isle of Wight duo have crafted hilarious, relatable, and catchy indie-pop songs that have been making waves both in the UK and beyond. From their sultry harmonies to whimsical lyrics, Wet Leg’s 2022 album is sure to be one of the most anticipated releases of the year.

4. Beabadoobee

Born in Manila, raised in London, Bea Kristi — known by her stage name Beabadoobee — is a key figure in the lo-fi indie scene. With a sound drawing from ’90s indie pop and punk, her latest album ‘Beatopia’ is a melodic, poetic dive into the kaleidoscope of growing up.

5. Clairo

This Atlanta-born and Massachusetts-raised artist first gained popularity through her lo-fi videos on YouTube. Clairo’s soft melodies and introspective lyrics showcase an innate ability to connect with her listeners, making her presence felt far beyond her bedroom-pop origins.

6. Sleaford Mods

Hailing from Nottingham, Sleaford Mods are a band who once again prove that authenticity and political critique can live in the same heart as punk energy. With their fourth and fifth albums released back-to-back in 2014, they’ve kept the same raw intensity, delivering harsh, blunt critiques of British society.

7. ioCOOP

iocoop is a collaboration between long-time friends and amazing musicians Nakai and Rina. Their unique fusion of traditional Japanese elements and trap production captures the feel and sound of bubblegum pop and hip-hop. This fresh take is both contagious and uniquely Japanese.

8. Tobe Nwigwe

Nigerian-American rapper Tobe Nwigwe’s scene is distinct – thought-provoking, spiritual, and full of wisdom. His music is a compelling mix of rap, rhythm, and afrobeats that stretches the boundaries of what is deemed “hip-hop”.

9. Limp Wrist

Limp Wrist is a queercore band from Los Angeles, and their brand of hardcore punk is a powerful voice in contemporary queer culture. They’ve been around since 2002, but their music is as relevant and as important as ever, advocating for equality and acceptance beyond labels.

10. Ley Lines

An exciting addition to the neo-psychedelia scene, Ley Lines brings a refreshing sound to guitar-oriented dream-pop. Their self-titled debut album released in 2021 is a swirling, unique blend of influences, from Cocteau Twins to My Bloody Valentine.

This new batch of artists and bands represents just part of the exciting journey that is waiting for us in the field of music in 2023. Keep an ear out, because there’s no telling who might become the next big sensation that changes the game.

From indie rock and lo-fi to punk and hip-hop, this new crop of artists is carving unique and fascinating paths. Who among them will leave their indelible mark on the musical world this year? Only time (and our ears) can tell!
