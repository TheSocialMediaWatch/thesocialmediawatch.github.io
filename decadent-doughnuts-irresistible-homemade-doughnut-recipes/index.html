Decadent Doughnuts: Irresistible Homemade Doughnut Recipes


Who can resist the allure of a freshly baked doughnut? The soft, pillowy texture, the delectable flavors, and the mouthwatering glazes make them an irresistible treat for both kids and adults alike. While store-bought doughnuts are convenient, there’s something truly special about making your own at home. In this blog post, I’ll share with you some decadent and foolproof homemade doughnut recipes that will elevate your baking game to new heights.

Classic Glazed Doughnuts

Let’s start with the classic glazed doughnut – a timeless favorite that never fails to impress. To make these heavenly treats, you’ll need:

  • 3 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the glaze:

  • 2 cups of powdered sugar
  • 1/4 cup of whole milk
  • 1 teaspoon of vanilla extract

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt. In a separate bowl, combine the milk, eggs, and melted butter. Slowly pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until a soft dough forms.

  2. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for 1 hour or until doubled in size.

  3. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and roll it out to a 1/2-inch thickness. Use a doughnut cutter or a round cookie cutter to cut out doughnut shapes. Place the doughnuts on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper, cover with a kitchen towel, and let them rise for another 30 minutes.

  4. Meanwhile, heat vegetable oil in a deep pot or fryer to 375°F (190°C). Carefully drop a few doughnuts at a time into the hot oil and fry for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown. Remove the doughnuts with a slotted spoon or tongs and place them on a wire rack to cool.

  5. To make the glaze, whisk together powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla extract until smooth. Dip each cooled doughnut into the glaze, allowing any excess to drip off. Place the glazed doughnuts back on the wire rack and let the glaze set for a few minutes before serving.

Chocolate-Filled Doughnuts

For the chocolate lovers out there, these chocolate-filled doughnuts are a dream come true. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 3 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the filling:

  • 1 cup of semisweet chocolate chips
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream

  1. Follow the same steps as the classic glazed doughnuts to prepare the dough.

  2. While the dough is rising, prepare the chocolate filling. In a microwave-safe bowl, heat the chocolate chips and heavy cream in 30-second intervals, stirring each time until smooth. Let the filling cool to room temperature.

  3. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and roll it out to a 1/2-inch thickness. Use a doughnut cutter or a round cookie cutter to cut out doughnut shapes. Place a teaspoon of chocolate filling in the center of half of the doughnut shapes. Top with another doughnut shape and pinch the edges together to seal.

  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pot or fryer to 375°F (190°C). Fry the filled doughnuts for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown. Remove from the oil and let them cool on a wire rack.

  5. Dust the chocolate-filled doughnuts with powdered sugar or drizzle with melted chocolate for an extra indulgence. Serve them warm for a gooey, chocolatey experience that will leave everyone craving for more.

Cinnamon Sugar Doughnuts

Last but not least, we have the comforting and aromatic cinnamon sugar doughnuts. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • 3 1/2 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons of instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup of unsalted butter, melted
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

For the cinnamon sugar coating:

  • 1 cup of granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons of ground cinnamon

  1. Repeat the initial steps for making the dough as mentioned in the previous recipes.

  2. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and roll it out to a 1/2-inch thickness. Use a doughnut cutter or a round cookie cutter to cut out doughnut shapes.

  3. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pot or fryer to 375°F (190°C). Fry the doughnuts for about 2-3 minutes per side, or until golden brown. Remove from the oil and let them cool on a wire rack.

  4. While the doughnuts are still warm, toss them in a mixture of granulated sugar and ground cinnamon until coated evenly.

These homemade doughnut recipes will ensure that you never have to settle for average store-bought doughnuts again. Whether you prefer the classic glazed, the chocolate-filled, or the cinnamon sugar variety, these recipes will help you create the most decadent and irresistible doughnuts right in your own kitchen. So, put on your apron, roll up your sleeves, and let the doughnut-making adventure begin!
