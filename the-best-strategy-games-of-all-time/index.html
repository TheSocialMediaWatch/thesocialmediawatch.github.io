The Best Strategy Games of All Time


Strategy games are timeless. They challenge the mind, require careful planning, and demand creative thinking. From military simulations to city-building experiences, these games offer something for every type of strategist. Whether you are a seasoned veteran or just stepping into the world of strategic gameplay, there is a game that will captivate you and sharpen your skills.

In this post, we will explore the best strategy games of all time, covering a range of genres including real-time strategy (RTS), turn-based strategy (TBS), and grand strategy games. These titles have set the standard for strategy gaming, pushing the boundaries of what games can achieve in terms of complexity, depth, and player interaction.

1. StarCraft II (Real-Time Strategy)

StarCraft II is often considered the pinnacle of real-time strategy games. Released by Blizzard Entertainment in 2010, this game takes place in a futuristic universe where three distinct factions—the Terrans, Zerg, and Protoss—vie for control of the galaxy. What makes StarCraft II exceptional is its precise balance, fast-paced gameplay, and strategic depth.

Each faction in StarCraft II has its own unique playstyle, requiring players to master different tactics and strategies. The game’s competitive multiplayer scene has remained vibrant for over a decade, with professional players still battling it out in tournaments around the world.

StarCraft II combines elements of resource management, tactical combat, and strategic planning, making it a game that rewards both quick thinking and long-term planning. Whether playing in the single-player campaign or competing in online multiplayer matches, this game challenges your ability to adapt to changing circumstances and outsmart your opponent.

2. Civilization VI (Turn-Based Strategy)

If you’re looking for a grand strategy experience, look no further than Civilization VI. Developed by Firaxis Games, this turn-based strategy game allows players to build and lead a civilization from the dawn of history to the modern era. Civilization VI is part of the long-running Civilization series, which has captivated gamers for decades with its deep, complex gameplay.

In Civilization VI, players must manage everything from military conquest to technological advancement, diplomacy, and cultural development. Each civilization has its own unique abilities and units, offering countless strategies to explore. The game’s expansive map, combined with the ability to create alliances or wage wars, ensures that no two playthroughs are ever the same.

The most engaging aspect of Civilization VI is its emphasis on decision-making. Every action you take has long-term consequences, and the path to victory is rarely straightforward. Will you dominate through military might, build a cultural empire, or lead your people to scientific victory? The choice is yours, but the journey will always be challenging and rewarding.

3. XCOM: Enemy Unknown (Turn-Based Strategy)

For fans of tactical combat and squad-based strategy, XCOM: Enemy Unknown is a must-play title. This reboot of the classic XCOM series, developed by Firaxis Games, offers a fresh take on the genre with modern visuals and refined mechanics.

In XCOM: Enemy Unknown, players take on the role of commander of a secret military organization tasked with defending Earth from an alien invasion. The game features a blend of base management, strategic decision-making, and intense tactical combat. Players must carefully manage their resources, recruit soldiers, and research new technologies while engaging in turn-based battles against alien forces.

The game’s permadeath mechanic adds a layer of tension and emotional weight to every mission. When one of your soldiers falls in battle, they are gone for good, and the consequences can be devastating. This creates an atmosphere of constant pressure, making every decision feel meaningful and impactful.

4. Total War: Shogun 2 (Real-Time/Turn-Based Strategy)

The Total War series is known for its large-scale battles, historical accuracy, and deep strategic elements, and Shogun 2 is widely regarded as one of the best entries in the franchise. Set in feudal Japan, Shogun 2 combines turn-based strategy with real-time tactical combat, allowing players to control a clan vying for supremacy in a war-torn country.

On the strategic map, players manage resources, recruit armies, and engage in diplomacy with rival clans. When armies clash, the game shifts to real-time battles where you control units on the battlefield. The blend of grand strategy and tactical combat gives Shogun 2 a unique and satisfying rhythm, and the game’s historical setting adds an extra layer of depth and immersion.

The AI in Shogun 2 is challenging, and the game’s emphasis on diplomacy, resource management, and military tactics means that each playthrough offers a different experience. Whether you’re seeking to crush your enemies in battle or form alliances and slowly build your power, Shogun 2 provides a wide range of strategies to explore.

5. Age of Empires II (Real-Time Strategy)

Released in 1999, Age of Empires II is a timeless classic that continues to be beloved by strategy gamers to this day. This real-time strategy game takes place during the Middle Ages, where players control one of several civilizations and work to build an empire through resource gathering, military conquest, and technological advancement.

What sets Age of Empires II apart is its emphasis on historical accuracy and diverse gameplay options. Players can choose from a variety of civilizations, each with its own unique units and technologies. The game’s campaign mode offers a series of historical scenarios, while the multiplayer mode provides countless hours of competitive gameplay.

The game’s simple yet effective mechanics make it accessible to newcomers, while its strategic depth ensures that it remains challenging for veterans. Whether playing against AI opponents or facing off against other players online, Age of Empires II remains one of the most influential strategy games of all time.

6. Company of Heroes (Real-Time Strategy)

Set during World War II, Company of Heroes is a real-time strategy game that combines tactical combat with a compelling story. Developed by Relic Entertainment, the game puts players in control of a squad of soldiers as they fight through historical battles in the European theater of war.

Unlike many other RTS games, Company of Heroes focuses on cover-based tactics, squad-based combat, and resource management. The game’s destructible environments and detailed line-of-sight mechanics make every battle dynamic and immersive. Players must carefully position their units, control key objectives, and use terrain to their advantage in order to achieve victory.

The game’s single-player campaign is gripping, and the multiplayer mode offers an engaging competitive experience. Company of Heroes is a standout in the RTS genre because of its focus on tactical combat and its ability to make every skirmish feel intense and meaningful.

7. Hearts of Iron IV (Grand Strategy)

For players who enjoy the complexity of grand strategy games, Hearts of Iron IV is one of the best options available. Set during World War II, this game allows players to take control of any nation and guide them through the war’s various stages. The game focuses on political maneuvering, resource management, and military strategy, offering a deep and intricate experience.

Hearts of Iron IV stands out for its attention to historical detail and the wide range of strategic choices available. Players must manage everything from research and production to military deployment and diplomatic relations. The game’s focus on the military aspect of warfare is complemented by the ability to shape political alliances, influence global events, and alter the course of history.

The game’s learning curve can be steep, but its depth and replayability make it incredibly rewarding for dedicated players. Whether you’re leading a major power like the United States or a smaller nation, Hearts of Iron IV offers a level of complexity and freedom that few games in the genre can match.

8. Europa Universalis IV (Grand Strategy)

Another standout in the grand strategy genre is Europa Universalis IV. Developed by Paradox Interactive, this game spans several centuries of history, allowing players to control a nation and guide it through wars, diplomacy, and economic growth from the Middle Ages to the early modern period.

Europa Universalis IV is known for its deep and intricate systems, including diplomacy, trade, religion, and military tactics. Players must manage not only their military forces but also their economy, alliances, and internal politics. The game’s sandbox approach allows players to create their own path to success, whether through conquest, diplomacy, or cultural influence.

The complexity of Europa Universalis IV makes it a challenging but highly rewarding experience. The game’s intricate mechanics and attention to detail give players the freedom to experiment with different strategies and approaches, making it one of the most beloved grand strategy games of all time.

9. The Banner Saga (Turn-Based Strategy)

The Banner Saga is a turn-based strategy game that blends tactical combat with a gripping narrative. Set in a Viking-inspired world, the game follows a group of survivors as they journey across a harsh and unforgiving landscape. Players must manage their resources, make tough decisions, and engage in tactical battles with an emphasis on positioning and resource management.

What sets The Banner Saga apart is its story-driven gameplay. The decisions players make throughout the game have a direct impact on the story, shaping the fate of their characters and the world around them. The game’s beautiful hand-drawn art style and atmospheric soundtrack further enhance the experience, making it a standout in the turn-based strategy genre.

With its deep narrative and challenging combat, The Banner Saga offers a unique and engaging experience for fans of strategy games.

10. Command & Conquer: Red Alert 2 (Real-Time Strategy)

Command & Conquer: Red Alert 2 is one of the most iconic RTS games of all time. Released in 2000, the game takes place in an alternate history where the Allies and Soviets are engaged in a fierce battle for control of the world. Red Alert 2 is known for its fast-paced gameplay, memorable units, and over-the-top action.

The game’s campaign mode is filled with cinematic sequences and intense missions, while the multiplayer mode offers fast and competitive gameplay. The game’s emphasis on base building, resource gathering, and tactical combat makes it a classic in the RTS genre.

Whether playing the single-player campaign or competing online, Command & Conquer: Red Alert 2 remains a beloved title that defined a generation of strategy gamers.

Strategy games have evolved significantly over the years, but the best titles in the genre continue to challenge players with complex mechanics, deep gameplay, and strategic depth. From managing entire civilizations to leading armies into battle, these games offer a wide variety of experiences for all types of strategists. Whether you’re a fan of real-time strategy, turn-based gameplay, or grand strategy, the games listed above represent the best of what the genre has to offer.

So, what are you waiting for? Dive into one of these legendary strategy games and start crafting your path to victory!
