Best Road Trips in the USA: Scenic Routes You Can't Miss


When it comes to exploring the vast and diverse landscapes of the United States, hitting the open road ranks high among the most exhilarating ways to travel. The USA offers a countless variety of routes, each weaving through stunning scenery, charming towns, and historic landmarks. Whether you’re craving coastal views, mountain adventures, desert solitude, or cultural immersion, the country’s road trips promise unforgettable journeys that go far beyond the destination.

Let’s embark on a road trip odyssey, diving into some of the most breathtaking and iconic scenic drives that capture the spirit of adventure and the beauty of the American landscape.

Pacific Coast Highway (California, Oregon, Washington)

Arguably one of the most famous road trips in the country, the Pacific Coast Highway, also known as Highway 1, stretches along the rugged west coast, starting roughly in Southern California and continuing north through Oregon and Washington.

Highlights

  • Big Sur, California: Dramatic cliffs dropping into the Pacific Ocean. The Bixby Creek Bridge and McWay Falls are must-see spots.
  • San Francisco Bay Area: Drive across the Golden Gate Bridge, explore vibrant neighborhoods, and savor fresh seafood at Fisherman’s Wharf.
  • Oregon Coast: Wildlife sightings like sea otters and whales walking among charming coastal towns such as Cannon Beach.
  • Olympic National Park, Washington: A mix of coastline, temperate rainforests, and majestic mountains.

Why It’s Special

The Pacific Coast Highway blends picturesque ocean views with quaint beach towns, state parks, and rich cultural experiences. The constantly changing landscape keeps even the most seasoned traveler engaged.

Blue Ridge Parkway (Virginia to North Carolina)

Known as “America’s Favorite Drive,” the Blue Ridge Parkway is renowned for its serene woodland escapes and panoramic mountain vistas. It stretches over 469 miles connecting Shenandoah National Park in Virginia to Great Smoky Mountains National Park in North Carolina.

Highlights

  • Peak Fall Foliage: The parkway is famous for breathtaking autumn colors that carpet the forests in vibrant reds, oranges, and yellows.
  • Hiking Opportunities: Trails like Humpback Rocks and Linville Falls allow you to explore nature up close.
  • Cultural Stops: Small mountain towns such as Asheville offer vibrant arts and culinary scenes.

Why It’s Special

The Blue Ridge Parkway is ideal for travelers looking to unwind amidst nature, enjoy slower-paced travel with scenic overlooks, and indulge in Appalachian culture and cuisine.

Route 66 (Illinois to California)

Ah, the iconic Route 66, also known as the “Main Street of America.” Although largely replaced by the interstate system, many sections of historic Route 66 are still drivable and offer a nostalgic glimpse into America’s past.

Highlights

  • Classic Americana: Vintage diners, motels, neon signs, and quirky roadside attractions.
  • Chicago: Start with urban energy, architectural marvels, and lakefront views.
  • Midwestern Heartland: Rolling prairies, old-school towns, and Route 66 museums highlight the road’s heritage.
  • Southwestern Landscapes: Monument Valley and desert vistas welcome you in New Mexico and Arizona.
  • California’s Inland Empire: Finish near Los Angeles with its bustling city life.

Why It’s Special

Traveling Route 66 is like flipping through an American history book, reviving the spirit of 20th-century road travel wrapped in charm and kitsch.

Going-to-the-Sun Road (Montana)

Snaking through Glacier National Park, the Going-to-the-Sun Road is a spectacular mountain drive that offers jaw-dropping views of glaciers, alpine meadows, and towering peaks.

Highlights

  • Logan Pass: The highest point accessible by car; often home to mountain goats and marmots.
  • St. Mary Lake and Wild Goose Island: Iconic photo spots famous for their crystal-clear waters and rugged beauty.
  • Waterfalls: Invisible Falls and Bird Woman Falls punctuate the route with refreshing cascades.

Why It’s Special

This road trip is perfect for lovers of soaring mountain landscapes and pristine wilderness. It’s a spring-to-fall destination, as winter snows close the road part of the year.

Natchez Trace Parkway (Mississippi to Tennessee)

The Natchez Trace Parkway is a serene 444-mile drive steeped in history and natural beauty, linking Natchez, Mississippi, to Nashville, Tennessee.

Highlights

  • Historical Sites: Ancient Native American mounds, Civil War landmarks, and interpretive centers.
  • Wildlife Viewing: Deer often roam freely beside the road.
  • Quiet Campgrounds: Experiences of restful forest camping without the hustle of busy areas.

Why It’s Special

It offers a slower-paced, reflective journey combining American history with lush landscapes, perfect for travelers seeking a peaceful escape.

The Loneliest Road (Nevada - U.S. Route 50)

Nevada’s stretch of U.S. Route 50 earned its nickname for one key reason: vast expanses with virtually no towns for miles, making it a true solitude-filled trek.

Highlights

  • Wildlife: Keep an eye out for wild horses and desert foxes.
  • Stunning Desert Scenery: Rolling mountain ranges and wide-open valleys.
  • Historic Mining Towns: Ghost towns provide a glimpse into Nevada’s wild west past.

Why It’s Special

Travelers who crave solitude and stark natural beauty will find Route 50 unforgettable — an adventure into the heart of America’s desert west.

Skyline Drive (Virginia)

Winding through Shenandoah National Park, Skyline Drive is a 105-mile road perfect for leisurely drives through the Blue Ridge Mountains.

Highlights

  • Overlooks: More than 70 scenic overlooks including the breathtaking Hawksbill Mountain.
  • Wildlife Watching: Black bears, deer, and a variety of birds inhabit the park.
  • Seasonal Splendor: Spring wildflowers, summer greenery, vibrant fall colors, and winter snow-covered peaks.

Why It’s Special

Skylines Drive offers nature lovers a taste of all four seasons with well-maintained roads and easy access from Washington, D.C., making it great for a quick getaway.

Million Dollar Highway (Colorado)

Stretching from Ouray to Silverton, this stretch of U.S. Route 550 through the San Juan Mountains is famous for dramatic switchbacks, towering cliffs, and staggering elevation changes.

Highlights

  • Red Mountain Pass: Hairpin curves with dizzying views.
  • Historic Mining Towns: Old western charm meets modern outdoor adventure.
  • Winter Sports: Nearby ski resorts make it a year-round destination.

Why It’s Special

With its thrilling roads and mountainous panoramas, this drive is perfect for those who enjoy a dash of adrenaline with their sightseeing.

Great River Road (Mississippi River)

Following the course of the mighty Mississippi River from Minnesota to Louisiana, the Great River Road is a patchwork of highways revealing the cultural and natural heritage of America’s heartland.

Highlights

  • River Towns: Explore Minneapolis, St. Louis, Memphis, and New Orleans.
  • Cultural Richness: Blues music birthplace, southern cuisine, and historical museums.
  • Natural Beauty: Riverfront parks, wetlands, and bluffs provide a changing backdrop.

Why It’s Special

This road trip celebrates America’s historical and cultural arteries, offering diverse experiences along one of the nation’s most iconic waterways.

Tips for Planning Your Ultimate Scenic Road Trip

  • Map Your Route: Whether you want to focus on a single scenic highway or mix several routes into a longer expedition, planning your trip in advance helps uncover hidden gems.
  • Timing Matters: Some routes like Going-to-the-Sun Road are seasonal; fall is spectacular for many mountain roads; summer suits coastal drives.
  • Pack Smart: Bring a camera, comfortable clothes, snacks, and an offline map app. Cell service can be spotty on remote roads.
  • Local Eats: Stop in small towns for local food—the best discoveries often happen around dinner tables.
  • Stay Flexible: Road trips are about the journey. Allow time to explore unexpected detours or stay longer in places that capture your heart.

Conclusion

The USA’s diverse geography makes it a playground for road warriors seeking both iconic and off-the-beaten-path adventures. From the foggy Pacific Coast to the rolling southern hills, from the rugged Rockies to the quiet river valleys, these scenic routes invite travelers to experience the vastness and variety of America’s landscape firsthand.

So next time you crave a road trip, pick a route, turn on your favorite tunes, and let the open road lead you to breathtaking views and unforgettable memories.

Safe travels!
