Empowering Reads: 15 Books Every Woman Should Read


As women, we have a unique journey filled with triumphs, challenges, and moments of self-discovery. Books have the power to inspire, educate, and empower us, providing a window into different perspectives and helping us navigate the complexities of life. In this blog post, we have compiled a list of 15 empowering reads that every woman should read. So, grab a cup of tea, find a cozy spot, and get ready to be inspired!

1. “Becoming” by Michelle Obama

In her memoir, former First Lady Michelle Obama shares her journey from a young girl on the South Side of Chicago to becoming one of the most influential women in the world. Her story is a testament to the power of determination, resilience, and the importance of staying true to oneself.

2. “The Handmaid’s Tale” by Margaret Atwood

Margaret Atwood’s dystopian novel explores a world where women’s rights have been stripped away. Through the eyes of Offred, a Handmaid, we witness her struggle for freedom and the resilience of the human spirit. This book serves as a reminder of the importance of fighting for equality and the power of individual agency.

3. “Lean In” by Sheryl Sandberg

Sheryl Sandberg, Facebook’s COO, tackles the issue of gender inequality in the workplace in her book “Lean In.” She offers practical advice and insights to empower women to pursue their ambitions and break through barriers. This book is a must-read for any woman looking to navigate the complexities of the modern workplace.

4. “Wild” by Cheryl Strayed

In “Wild,” Cheryl Strayed chronicles her transformative journey hiking the Pacific Crest Trail. This memoir is a testament to the power of perseverance, self-discovery, and the healing nature of the great outdoors. It reminds us that sometimes the most empowering journeys are the ones we take within ourselves.

5. “The Color Purple” by Alice Walker

Alice Walker’s Pulitzer Prize-winning novel, “The Color Purple,” explores themes of race, gender, and identity. Through the eyes of Celie, we witness her resilience and ultimate triumph over adversity. This book is a powerful reminder of the strength and resilience within every woman.

6. “I Am Malala” by Malala Yousafzai

Malala Yousafzai’s memoir, “I Am Malala,” shares her courageous fight for girls’ education in Pakistan. Her story is a testament to the power of education, the importance of standing up for what you believe in, and the ability to create change even in the face of adversity.

7. “The Joy Luck Club” by Amy Tan

Amy Tan’s “The Joy Luck Club” explores the complex relationships between mothers and daughters, bridging the gap between different generations and cultures. It delves into the themes of identity, heritage, and the strength that comes from embracing one’s roots.

8. “Half the Sky” by Nicholas D. Kristof and Sheryl WuDunn

In “Half the Sky,” Nicholas D. Kristof and Sheryl WuDunn shed light on the oppression and marginalization of women around the world. Through powerful stories, they inspire readers to take action, highlighting the importance of empowering women globally.

9. “Bad Feminist” by Roxane Gay

In “Bad Feminist,” Roxane Gay explores the complexities of feminism, challenging traditional notions and embracing imperfections. Her collection of essays offers a fresh perspective on feminism and encourages women to embrace their own unique experiences and beliefs.

10. “Educated” by Tara Westover

Tara Westover’s memoir, “Educated,” chronicles her journey from growing up in a strict and abusive household to pursuing an education and finding her own voice. This book is a powerful reminder of the transformative power of education and the strength that comes from breaking free from societal expectations.

11. “The Power of Now” by Eckhart Tolle

In “The Power of Now,” Eckhart Tolle explores the concept of living in the present moment and finding inner peace. This book is a powerful tool for women looking to embrace mindfulness, let go of past traumas, and unlock their true potential.

12. “Hidden Figures” by Margot Lee Shetterly

Margot Lee Shetterly’s “Hidden Figures” sheds light on the incredible contributions of African-American women mathematicians at NASA during the Space Race. This book celebrates the power of intellect, perseverance, and the importance of representation in all fields.

13. “Big Magic” by Elizabeth Gilbert

In “Big Magic,” Elizabeth Gilbert explores the creative process and encourages women to embrace their innate creativity. This book serves as a reminder that every woman has a unique gift to share with the world and that pursuing creative endeavors can be a transformative and empowering experience.

14. “Bossypants” by Tina Fey

Tina Fey’s memoir, “Bossypants,” offers a humorous and honest look at her journey in the male-dominated world of comedy. Through her witty anecdotes and insights, she challenges gender stereotypes and encourages women to embrace their own unique voices.

15. “We Should All Be Feminists” by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie’s essay “We Should All Be Feminists” explores the importance of gender equality and dismantling societal expectations. Her powerful words serve as a call to action for women to embrace their own power and advocate for change.

These 15 empowering reads offer a diverse range of perspectives, stories, and insights that every woman can benefit from. Whether you’re seeking inspiration, guidance, or simply a captivating story, these books are sure to leave a lasting impact. So, go ahead and embark on your reading journey, and remember, the power to empower yourself lies within the pages of a book. Happy reading!
