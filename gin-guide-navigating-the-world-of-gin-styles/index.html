Gin Guide: Navigating the World of Gin Styles


Gin, the beloved spirit with a rich history and diverse range of styles, has captured the hearts of many cocktail enthusiasts around the globe. From classic London Dry to the innovative New Western styles, there is a gin for every palate. In this comprehensive guide, we delve into the fascinating world of gin styles, exploring their unique characteristics and the perfect serves that showcase their flavors.

London Dry Gin

Characteristics: London Dry Gin is a timeless classic known for its juniper-forward profile. It is a crisp and dry style that forms the base of many traditional gin cocktails. London Dry gins are typically made using a mix of botanicals, with juniper as the dominant flavor.

Perfect Serve: To truly appreciate the essence of London Dry Gin, enjoy it in a classic Gin and Tonic. Fill a glass with ice, add a measure of gin, top up with tonic water, and garnish with a wedge of lime. This simple serve allows the juniper notes to shine.

Plymouth Gin

Characteristics: Plymouth Gin hails from the historic naval town of Plymouth, England. It is a slightly sweeter and less juniper-forward gin compared to London Dry. Plymouth Gin is known for its smooth and aromatic profile, making it a favorite among gin connoisseurs.

Perfect Serve: For a taste of history, savor Plymouth Gin in a classic Martini. Stir gin with dry vermouth, strain into a chilled cocktail glass, and garnish with a lemon twist or olive. The elegant simplicity of this serve highlights the gin’s botanicals.

Old Tom Gin

Characteristics: Old Tom Gin is a bridge between the traditional London Dry and the sweeter Dutch Genever styles. It is slightly sweetened, making it a versatile choice for both classic and modern cocktails. Old Tom Gin adds depth and complexity to mixed drinks.

Perfect Serve: Experience the allure of Old Tom Gin in a refreshing Tom Collins. Combine gin with lemon juice, simple syrup, and soda water over ice, and garnish with a lemon slice and cherry. This effervescent cocktail showcases the gin’s balanced sweetness.

New Western Dry Gin

Characteristics: New Western Dry Gin is a contemporary style that pushes the boundaries of traditional gin. It features bold and unconventional botanicals beyond juniper, leading to innovative flavor profiles. New Western gins offer a modern twist on the classic spirit.

Perfect Serve: Embrace the creativity of New Western Dry Gin in a unique cocktail creation. Experiment with fresh ingredients like herbs, fruits, or spices to craft a personalized gin cocktail that reflects your taste preferences. The versatility of New Western gins encourages exploration and innovation.

Navy Strength Gin

Characteristics: Navy Strength Gin is a robust and intense style originally distilled for the British Navy. It has a higher alcohol content, usually around 57-58% ABV, imparting a powerful flavor profile. Navy Strength gins are bold and full-bodied, ideal for cocktails that can stand up to their strength.

Perfect Serve: For a cocktail with a kick, try Navy Strength Gin in a classic Negroni. Mix equal parts gin, Campari, and sweet vermouth over ice, stir well, and garnish with an orange twist. The strong flavors of the gin complement the bitterness of this iconic cocktail.

In conclusion, the world of gin styles offers a spectrum of flavors and characteristics to suit every taste preference. Whether you prefer the timeless allure of London Dry or the bold innovation of New Western, there is a gin style waiting to be explored. Elevate your cocktail experience by discovering the diverse world of gin and savoring the artistry of this beloved spirit.

Remember, the key to enjoying gin is to savor it responsibly and appreciate the craftsmanship that goes into each bottle. Cheers to the journey of exploring gin styles and creating memorable cocktail moments!

In this blog post, we’ve covered various gin styles, their unique characteristics, and ideal serves to help you navigate the diverse world of gin. Whether you’re a seasoned gin enthusiast or a curious newcomer, there’s always something new to discover in the realm of gin. Cheers to your next gin adventure!
