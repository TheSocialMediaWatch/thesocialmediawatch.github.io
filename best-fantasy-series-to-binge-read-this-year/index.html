Best Fantasy Series to Binge-Read This Year


In the vast realm of literature, fantasy series hold a special place for readers who crave immersive worlds, complex characters, and epic adventures. Whether you’re new to the genre or a seasoned reader looking for your next binge-worthy saga, this guide dives deep into some of the best fantasy series that promise to captivate your imagination and keep you turning pages well into the night.

Why Fantasy Series Are Perfect for Binge-Reading

Fantasy series offer more than just stories—they create entire universes filled with magic, mythology, politics, and mystery. When you get hooked on a good series, the continuity and evolving plots make binge-reading an enticing experience. The layered narratives and rich character development give readers not just a story, but an ongoing journey. These series often span multiple books, offering extensive worldbuilding and elaborate plotlines that reward dedicated reading.

Now, let’s explore some standout fantasy series that are perfect for devouring in one go.

1. The Stormlight Archive by Brandon Sanderson

Brandon Sanderson’s Stormlight Archive is often hailed as a modern epic fantasy masterpiece. Set in the storm-wracked world of Roshar, this series combines intricate magic systems, enormous political intrigue, and characters whose personal struggles resonate deeply.

  • Why binge-read? Each book is massive and packed with twists, making it a satisfying long-term read. Sanderson’s detailed worldbuilding and profound exploration of themes like leadership, honor, and mental health will keep you hooked.
  • Books to start with: The Way of Kings kicks off the series with a rich introduction to Roshar and its key players.
  • What you’ll enjoy: Complex characters, inventive magic, and epic battles, all woven into an immersive storyline that unfolds across multiple perspectives.

2. The First Law Trilogy by Joe Abercrombie

Fans of gritty, dark fantasy will find a perfect match in Joe Abercrombie’s First Law Trilogy. Known for its morally ambiguous characters and sharp wit, this series throws traditional heroic tropes out the window.

  • Why binge-read? Abercrombie’s fast-paced narrative and engaging dialogue make this trilogy addictive. The blend of dark humor and brutal realism adds an exciting edge.
  • Books to start with: The Blade Itself sets the tone with a mix of political intrigue, dark magic, and unpredictable characters.
  • What you’ll enjoy: Antiheroes, morally complex storylines, and a narrative that continuously subverts expectations.

3. The Broken Empire Trilogy by Mark Lawrence

If you’re looking for a fantasy series with a deeply flawed protagonist on a quest for power, The Broken Empire trilogy is an excellent choice. Mark Lawrence’s writing is dark, poetic, and reflective, diving into the psyche of a ruthless prince.

  • Why binge-read? The raw intensity, combined with philosophical undertones, creates a compelling break from traditional fantasy.
  • Books to start with: Prince of Thorns introduces readers to the protagonist’s dark journey.
  • What you’ll enjoy: A compelling antihero, brutal worldbuilding, and a story that challenges the boundaries of the genre.

4. The Kingkiller Chronicle by Patrick Rothfuss

Patrick Rothfuss’s Kingkiller Chronicle is a lyrical and character-driven tale about a gifted young man becoming the most legendary figure in a richly imagined world. The series blends magic, music, and storytelling in a unique narrative style.

  • Why binge-read? Rothfuss’s prose captivates with its beauty and depth. Each page is infused with emotional weight, making the experience as enchanting as the story itself.
  • Books to start with: The Name of the Wind is widely praised for its immersive storytelling.
  • What you’ll enjoy: Poetry-esque writing, a deeply personal story, and a magical academic setting.

5. Malazan Book of the Fallen by Steven Erikson

For readers seeking sprawling, complex epic fantasy, Steven Erikson’s Malazan Book of the Fallen is a monumental series. It’s renowned for its vast scope, intricate plotlines, and a large ensemble of characters.

  • Why binge-read? The dense and layered storytelling rewards patient readers with a richly woven tapestry of magic, history, and mythology.
  • Books to start with: Gardens of the Moon is the gateway into the multifaceted world of Malazan.
  • What you’ll enjoy: Epic battles, deep philosophical themes, and an expansive, elaborate world.

6. The Lies of Locke Lamora by Scott Lynch

Scott Lynch’s Gentleman Bastard series, starting with The Lies of Locke Lamora, is a clever and witty fantasy romp through a city reminiscent of Venice, filled with cons, intrigue, and unexpected magic.

  • Why binge-read? The dialogue sparkles with humor while the plot twists keep you on your toes. Perfect for those who like smart, fast-paced fantasy.
  • Books to start with: Begin with The Lies of Locke Lamora to meet the charming and cunning thieves.
  • What you’ll enjoy: Clever heists, vibrant characters, and an imaginative city setting.

7. The Witcher Series by Andrzej Sapkowski

Before its television adaptation skyrocketed in popularity, The Witcher book series was already a beloved staple of dark fantasy. Polish author Andrzej Sapkowski’s mix of folklore, monster hunting, and complex morality embodies the darker side of fantasy.

  • Why binge-read? Short stories and novels blend seamlessly, offering quick reads and deep lore alike.
  • Books to start with: Start with The Last Wish, a collection of short stories introducing Geralt of Rivia.
  • What you’ll enjoy: Grisly folklore, sharp political commentary, and a morally ambiguous protagonist.

8. Shadow and Bone Trilogy by Leigh Bardugo

Leigh Bardugo’s Shadow and Bone trilogy is a modern fantasy series that introduces the Grisha universe, where magic users can manipulate elements and energy. The books are fast-paced and rich with imaginative magic and compelling characters.

  • Why binge-read? Perfect for readers looking for lush worldbuilding with romance, action, and intrigue.
  • Books to start with: Shadow and Bone introduces readers to the Shadow Fold and its dangerous magic.
  • What you’ll enjoy: A unique magic system, engaging character arcs, and a mix of light and dark tones.

9. Wheel of Time by Robert Jordan (and Brandon Sanderson)

With a sprawling narrative over fourteen books, The Wheel of Time series is a cornerstone of epic high fantasy. Starting with Robert Jordan and finished with Brandon Sanderson, this series is rich in mythology and deeply developed characters.

  • Why binge-read? The length and scope make it ideal for readers eager for an immersive saga.
  • Books to start with: The Eye of the World introduces the complex world and its prophecy-driven story.
  • What you’ll enjoy: A vast mythology, intricate worldbuilding, and a slow-burning but rewarding narrative.

10. The Poppy War Trilogy by R.F. Kuang

R.F. Kuang’s Poppy War trilogy is a brutal and thought-provoking fantasy inspired by Chinese history and mythology. It explores trauma, addiction, and power through intense storytelling.

  • Why binge-read? The series is emotional and gripping, with a gritty and realistic portrayal of war and its consequences.
  • Books to start with: The Poppy War kicks off with an orphan girl summoned to a prestigious military academy.
  • What you’ll enjoy: Historical influences, dark magic, and difficult moral choices.

How to Choose the Right Fantasy Series for You

With so many richly created worlds to choose from, selecting a series can feel daunting. Here are a few tips to guide your decision:

  • Consider your reading preferences: Do you prefer light-hearted adventure, dark and gritty narratives, or deeply philosophical themes? Each series caters to different tastes.
  • Length and commitment: Some series are sprawling epics (Malazan, Wheel of Time), while others are tight trilogies (The Broken Empire, Poppy War).
  • Style and tone: From lyrical prose (Kingkiller Chronicle) to sharp wit and humor (Gentleman Bastard), the writing style can greatly influence your enjoyment.
  • Magic and worldbuilding: Some readers love detailed magic systems and elaborate worlds, while others prefer fast-paced plots with less emphasis on lore.

Final Thoughts

Diving into a fantasy series is like embarking on a grand adventure—one filled with discovery, wonder, and emotional connection. Whether you lean towards sweeping epics, gritty antiheroes, or intricate magic systems, this year offers a treasure trove of fantastic worlds waiting to be explored. Binge-reading these series is not only a journey through pages but also a chance to lose yourself in vibrant stories that linger long after the last chapter.

Prepare your reading nook, stock up on your favorite snacks, and get ready to be transported to realms where anything is possible. Happy reading!
