What to Do in Paris: A 3-Day Itinerary


Paris, the City of Light, is a magnetic blend of art, history, culture, and culinary delights. Its timeless beauty makes it a dream destination for travelers all over the world. Exploring Paris in three days might seem like a whirlwind, but with a carefully planned itinerary, it’s entirely possible to capture its essence and soak in its charm. This guide will take you through an immersive 72-hour journey across Paris, balancing iconic landmarks, hidden gems, local experiences, and mouthwatering cuisine.

Day 1: Classic Paris — A Walk Through History and Iconic Sights

Morning: Eiffel Tower and Champ de Mars

Start your Paris adventure with a visit to the legendary Eiffel Tower. Arriving early helps you beat large crowds and enjoy serene views from the top. Take your time absorbing the panoramic vistas over the Seine River and city rooftops; it’s an unforgettable way to get your bearings.

After descending, enjoy a peaceful stroll around Champ de Mars, the expansive park that stretches from the tower to École Militaire. This green space is perfect for people-watching or a quaint breakfast picnic if you grabbed pastries from a nearby bakery.

Midday: Seine River Cruise and Musée d’Orsay

Walk towards the Seine’s banks and hop on a Seine River cruise. Gliding beneath the city’s historic bridges offers a dreamy perspective of Paris’s charm, from the Louvre to Notre-Dame. A guided cruise helps unveil the history behind each landmark as you quietly cruise through the heart of the city.

Once you disembark, make your way to the Musée d’Orsay, housed in a former railway station. This museum hosts some of the world’s finest collections of Impressionist and Post-Impressionist masterpieces, featuring artists like Monet, Van Gogh, Degas, and Renoir. Spending a couple of hours here sharpens your sense of the artistic movements that shaped much of modern art.

Afternoon: Tuileries Garden and Palais Royal

From Musée d’Orsay, cross the Seine over the Pont Royal to reach the Tuileries Garden. These gardens provide a tranquil escape with fountains, sculptures, and shaded walkways. It’s an ideal place to rest your feet and sip coffee at one of the garden’s cafes.

Next, wander over to the nearby Palais Royal and its iconic black-and-white striped columns, designed by Daniel Buren. Beyond the whimsical art installation, this palace’s courtyard and gardens reflect centuries of Parisian elegance and quiet sophistication.

Evening: Dinner in Le Marais

For your evening meal, head to Le Marais, one of Paris’s oldest neighborhoods, now a hotspot for trendy cafes and restaurants. The cobbled streets and historic architecture create an irresistible atmosphere as you sample French cuisine—whether savoring escargots, duck confit, or classic coq au vin.

After dinner, enjoy a leisurely stroll along the Seine’s banks or explore some of the lively bars and cafés in Le Marais for a taste of Parisian nightlife.

Day 2: Art, Markets, and Montmartre’s Magic

Morning: Morning Market at Rue Cler and Sainte-Chapelle

Begin your day at the vibrant Rue Cler Market, a charming pedestrian street lined with vendors selling fresh produce, cheese, bread, and flowers. Grab a flaky croissant and fresh juice, or shop for picnic supplies to enjoy later.

Then, head to the Sainte-Chapelle, a jewel of Gothic architecture. This chapel’s soaring stained glass windows flood the space with kaleidoscopic light—truly a spiritual and visual experience.

Midday: Notre-Dame Cathedral and Latin Quarter

Just a short walk away lies the famous Notre-Dame Cathedral. Although partially under restoration, the exterior is still breathtaking. Nearby, explore the Latin Quarter—a lively blend of ancient streets, bookshops, and cafes buzzing with intellectual and artistic energy.

For lunch, choose a quaint bistro in this area to enjoy classic French fare such as onion soup or steak frites.

Afternoon: Louvre Museum Exploration

No trip to Paris is complete without a visit to the Louvre Museum. Given its vastness, prioritize highlights like the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and the Winged Victory of Samothrace. Booking tickets online in advance ensures you skip long lines. If you have a keen interest in art history, consider a guided tour to dive deeper into the stories behind the masterpieces.

If you prefer a lighter art dose, explore the museum’s stunning Cour Carrée and the glass pyramid entrance.

Evening: Montmartre and Sacré-Cœur

As the sun begins to set, take the metro or a charming uphill walk to Montmartre, the bohemian heart of Paris. Visit the Sacré-Cœur Basilica, whose white domes stand out against the sky, and enjoy sweeping city views from its steps.

Wander the cobbled streets where artists like Picasso and Dali once lived. You can also find local artists selling their work in the famous Place du Tertre. For dinner, choose a cozy cafe serving hearty French dishes and perhaps enjoy a glass of wine while soaking up the artistic ambiance.

Day 3: Hidden Corners, Gardens, and Culinary Delights

Morning: Luxembourg Gardens and Saint-Germain-des-Prés

Start your day with a tranquil walk in the Luxembourg Gardens, where perfectly trimmed lawns, statues, and fountains provide calm away from the city buzz. Grab a coffee at a nearby café and watch locals practicing tai chi, playing chess, or simply reading on benches.

Next, venture to Saint-Germain-des-Prés, an iconic literary and cultural district. Visit the historic Café de Flore or Les Deux Magots, former haunts of writers like Hemingway and Sartre, for a café au lait and people-watching.

Midday: Exploring Canal Saint-Martin and Lunch

Travel towards Canal Saint-Martin, a less-touristy but wonderfully charming area full of boutiques, bookstores, and street art. The tree-lined canal banks are perfect for a casual stroll or a picnic alongside artisanal bakeries and bustling markets.

Stop for lunch at one of the canal’s trendy eateries—expect fresh, inventive French fusion dishes or simple, refined classics paired with natural wines.

Afternoon: Pompidou Center and Le Village Saint-Paul

Post lunch, visit the Centre Pompidou, known for its radical modern architecture and extensive collection of contemporary art. Its rooftop terrace offers a striking view of Paris’s skyline.

Afterwards, explore Le Village Saint-Paul, a cluster of quaint courtyards lined with antique shops, art galleries, and flower stalls. This neighborhood is Parisian charm at its finest—great for finding unique souvenirs or simply losing yourself in its tranquil ambiance.

Evening: Dinner and Night Walk Along the Seine

For your final evening, dine at a traditional Parisian brasserie to indulge in comfort classics like boeuf bourguignon or a seafood platter. Opt for a place with outdoor seating to enjoy the city lights.

Cap off your journey by taking a slow, reflective walk along the Seine River at night. The illuminated bridges, the reflections of landmarks like the Eiffel Tower sparkling from afar, and the soft murmur of the water provide the perfect farewell to Paris.

Final Thoughts on a 3-Day Paris Experience

Three days in Paris offers a rich sampling of the city’s historic grandeur, artistic treasures, and enchanting neighborhoods. From iconic landmarks to vibrant local scenes, this itinerary balances must-see sights with moments of quiet discovery. Whether you’re savoring a buttery croissant in a hidden cafe or gazing over Paris from Montmartre, the city’s magic is palpable at every turn.

The key to making the most of your stay is to embrace the pace of Paris life—drifting between landmark visits and relaxed strolls, tasting the regional flavors, and soaking in the city’s unique blend of nostalgia and modernity. These three days will leave you with a profound appreciation for why Paris continues to hold an enduring place in travelers’ hearts worldwide. Bon voyage!
