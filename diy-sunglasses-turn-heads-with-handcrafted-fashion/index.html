DIY Sunglasses: Turn Heads with Handcrafted Fashion


Are you tired of wearing the same old sunglasses that everyone else on the street seems to have? Do you want to make a bold, unique fashion statement that will leave people asking where you got your amazing eyewear? Look no further! Here’s an exciting DIY project that will allow you to turn heads, turn your creativity loose, and reduce your carbon footprint, all at the same time.

Imagine holding in your hands a pair of sunglasses that you’ve designed yourself. You can mix and match different shapes, colors, and patterns, making each pair as one-of-a-kind as you are. So, let’s explore this thrilling do-it-yourself journey and embrace the art of crafting sunglasses.

Brilliant Inspiration and the Perfect Beginning

Before you dive into the designing and crafting process, it’s essential to understand the basics of sunglasses-making and the eyewear market. Research the most popular styles, trends, and designers to get a sense of what’s appealing in the fashion world. Visit galleries, museums, and even browse through your family’s old eyewear collections to find inspiration.

Another critical aspect of sunglasses making is understanding the different lens materials and treatments available. High-quality lenses not only provide great sun protection but also add a touch of luxury to your design. Polarized, UV protected, and scratch-resistant coated lenses are just a few options to look into for optimal vision and durability.

Once you garner a solid understanding of what’s trending and delve into the components used in quality sunglasses, you’ll be ready to embark on your DIY journey!

Design and Sketch Your Custom Sunglasses

With inspiration in place, it’s time to unleash your creativity! Start designing your sunglasses by considering aspects such as shape, color scheme, and the total look you want to achieve.

Experiment with different shapes, like round, cat-eye, aviator, or even one-of-a-kind inverted shapes to create an eyewear piece that nobody else has. Try out unique color combinations and use patterns to accent your design. The options are endless - the only limits are your imagination and craft supplies!

Once you’ve designed your perfect sunglasses, document your creation by sketching them out. This step will not only help you visualize your vision but also ensure that you can easily revisit your design at a later stage in the project.

The Materials You’ll Need

Before jumping into construction, gather all the necessary materials to bring your design to life. Here’s a list of supplies you’ll likely need:

  1. Two plastic or metal temple pieces (The “arms” of your sunglasses)
  2. Frames made from metal, plastic, or a combination of both
  3. Lens plastic, such as TR90 or polycarbonate, available in different colors and tints
  4. Nose pads and screws or rivets (if you choose metal frames)
  5. Adjustable nose pads (optional)
  6. Protective coating (polarized, UV protected, or scratch-resistant)
  7. Various tools for cutting, shaping, and assembling your sunglasses (like pliers, cutters, and files)

Note: Check if any specific tools are recommended for your preferred frame material.

The Crafting Process

With your materials gathered and your design on paper, let’s dive into the actual crafting process.

  1. Cut the Frames: Use a jeweler’s saw, or a similar tool based on your chosen material, to carefully cut out your frame shapes according to your design.

  2. Shape and Assemble the Frame: For plastic frames, you can use a heat gun or a hydraulic press to bend and mold the frame pieces to your desired shape. Metal frames can be shaped using hammers, files, and pliers, followed by assembling them together using screws or rivets.

  3. Cut the Lenses: Carefully cut your lens plastic to the desired shape and size. Ensure they align with your frame.

  4. Assemble the Lenses and Temples: Attach the lenses to the frame by using screws, rivets, or any specific attachment method depending on your choice of materials. Attach the temple pieces to both sides of your frame.

  5. Add Nose Pads and Final Touches: Attach nose pads if your design calls for them and make any final adjustments to ensure the sunglasses fit comfortably and are the correct size.

  6. Protect Your Lenses: Lastly, apply any lens treatments or coatings that you intend to use, such as polarized or UV protection for the best vision and eye care.

Share Your Creation

Congratulations! You’ve just designed, crafted, and assembled your unique pair of handcrafted sunglasses. When you wear your custom pair, not only will you stand out in a crowd, but you’ll also have the satisfaction of knowing that no two pairs are alike.

Don’t keep your newly handmade sunglasses to yourself - share the joy of creativity with friends and family, or even start a small business selling your unique designs. Whatever you choose to do, remember that your DIY sunglasses are a testament to the power of imagination and the confidence that comes with being your own fashion designer.

Embrace your newfound eyewear expertise, and continue experimenting with different designs, shapes, and materials. With enough practice and patience, crafting sunglasses can be turned into an art form, and your repertoire will grow to include an array of unique and fashionable sunglasses.

Happy crafting!
