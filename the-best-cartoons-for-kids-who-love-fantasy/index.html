The Best Cartoons for Kids Who Love Fantasy


Fantasy cartoons open up realms of imagination, transporting children beyond their everyday experiences to worlds filled with magic, mythical creatures, and epic adventures. For kids enchanted by dragons, wizards, enchanted forests, and heroic quests, the right cartoon can ignite creativity and foster a love for storytelling in a way few other mediums can. But with so many titles available, which fantasy cartoons truly stand out and captivate young minds? This article explores some of the best cartoons for kids who love fantasy, diving into their unique qualities and why kids fall in love with them.

Why Fantasy Cartoons Are Perfect for Kids

Before listing the top picks, it’s worth understanding why fantasy-themed animations hold such appeal for children. Fantasy cartoons do more than entertain—they invite kids to explore the unknown, challenge their imagination, and develop empathy through vivid storytelling.

  • Encouragement of creativity: Magical worlds encourage children to picture possibilities beyond reality, developing inventive thinking.
  • Strong moral lessons: Many fantasy stories revolve around good vs. evil, bravery, friendship, and perseverance—concepts easily absorbed by younger viewers.
  • Emotional engagement: The fantastical setting provides a safe space for kids to navigate complex feelings reflected in the characters’ journeys.
  • Cognitive development: Following intricate plotlines and understanding character motivations help improve attention and comprehension.

Recognizing these benefits helps identify cartoons that offer not just fun, but meaningful experiences. The following series excel in delivering immersive fantasy worlds catering to young adventurers.

Top Fantasy Cartoons for Kids

1. Avatar: The Last Airbender

One of the most beloved animated series of the past two decades, Avatar: The Last Airbender presents a rich fantasy universe inspired by multiple cultures and martial arts. Kids follow Aang, a young boy who can bend the elements of water, earth, fire, and air, as he seeks to bring balance to a world ravaged by war.

  • Why kids love it: The story balances epic battles and humor with age-appropriate emotional depth.
  • Fantasy elements: Elemental magic, mystical creatures, ancient prophecies.
  • Appeal beyond kids: Complex characters and layered storytelling make this show enjoyable for the whole family.

Its success led to a sequel, The Legend of Korra, continuing the legacy with a fresh heroine and new adventures.

2. My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic

Though primarily a show about friendship and kindness, this series sweeps children into the enchanted land of Equestria, where unicorns, pegasi, and earth ponies go on magical quests and learn valuable lessons.

  • Why kids love it: Colorful characters, catchy musical numbers, and heartwarming stories.
  • Fantasy elements: Magical powers, enchanted realms, mythical creatures.
  • Impact: Emphasizes empathy, teamwork, and acceptance, making fantasy relatable.

Its fantasy setting fosters fascination with magical concepts while offering positivity and fun.

3. Dragon Prince

With a gripping narrative set in a world of magic, elves, dragons, and war, Dragon Prince has quickly become a fan favorite. The story follows two human princes and an elven assassin as they form an unlikely alliance to bring peace.

  • Why kids love it: Intrigue, magic battles, and moral complexity.
  • Fantasy elements: Dragons, magical runes, ancient lore.
  • Unique features: Richly designed world-building with diverse cultures and magical systems.

While some themes are complex, it remains accessible to older kids enchanted by fantasy and adventure.

4. Trollhunters: Tales of Arcadia

Created by Guillermo del Toro, Trollhunters introduces children to a secret world where trolls, goblins, and mythical creatures exist hidden beneath their own town. When a teenager becomes the Trollhunter, he takes on the duty of protecting both humans and trolls.

  • Why kids love it: Exciting battles, relatable characters, and humor.
  • Fantasy elements: Troll kingdoms, enchanted weapons, dark magic.
  • Storytelling strength: Mixes lighthearted teen drama with epic fantasy quests.

It is the first installment in the Tales of Arcadia trilogy, followed by 3Below and Wizards, expanding the universe.

5. Steven Universe

Though more sci-fi than classical fantasy, Steven Universe features magical gems, shape-shifting, and ancient cosmic battles blended with heartfelt life lessons. It weaves fantasy into real-world challenges about identity, friendship, and acceptance.

  • Why kids love it: Colorful animation, catchy songs, and genuine emotional storytelling.
  • Fantasy elements: Magical powers, mystical artifacts, cosmic mythology.
  • Cultural impact: Praised for diversity, inclusivity, and nuanced characters.

Its blend of everyday life and fantasy makes it especially resonant for kids who cherish magical tales with heart.

6. The Owl House

This recent hit tells the story of Luz, a human girl who stumbles into a magical world full of witches, demons, and fantastical creatures, determined to become a witch herself despite not having magic.

  • Why kids love it: Humor, unique characters, and inventive magic systems.
  • Fantasy elements: Witchcraft, magical familiars, cursed objects.
  • Modern twist: Combines classic fantasy tropes with contemporary themes of identity and belonging.

The Owl House uniquely blends fantasy with a coming-of-age story, making it relatable and enchanting.

7. Castlevania

Though geared toward older kids and teens due to darker themes, Castlevania is a gothic fantasy tale filled with vampires, demons, and heroic battles. Its mature storytelling and impressive animation appeal to fantasy fans seeking a more intense adventure.

  • Why older kids love it: Complex plots, rich mythology, challenging moral questions.
  • Fantasy elements: Vampires, dark magic, medieval lore.
  • Caution note: Not suitable for young children due to violence.

It’s a prime example of fantasy animation broadening its scope for different age groups.

What to Look For When Choosing Fantasy Cartoons

Selecting the right cartoon depends on your child’s age, interests, and emotional maturity. Here are some tips to help guide your choice:

  • Age appropriateness: Check the show’s rating and themes to ensure content is suitable.
  • Complexity of story: Younger children thrive on simple, clear narratives while older kids enjoy layered storytelling.
  • Type of fantasy world: Some kids prefer lighthearted magical lands; others crave epic quests and mythic struggles.
  • Character diversity: Look for shows featuring a range of personalities and backgrounds that reflect your child’s worldview.
  • Lesson and values: Balance entertainment with positive messages about courage, kindness, and resilience.

By prioritizing these factors, parents can find fantasy cartoons that both entertain and enrich.

How Fantasy Cartoons Inspire Children Beyond the Screen

Beyond the immediate thrill of watching, fantasy cartoons often spark interests that extend well beyond the TV screen.

  • Creative play: Kids may invent their own stories, dressing up as favorite characters or acting out scenes.
  • Reading habits: Inspired by cartoon lore, children often seek out books or comics expanding those worlds.
  • Artistic expression: Drawing and crafting characters and settings allow kids to personalize their fantasy experience.
  • Social connection: Shared fandoms promote friendships and collaborative projects among peers.

These shows often serve as a gateway to a lifelong passion for fantasy and creativity.

Final Thoughts

For kids who dream of dragons, magic spells, enchanted forests, and heroic journeys, the landscape of fantasy cartoons offers a treasure trove of adventures. From the elemental battles of Avatar: The Last Airbender to the whimsical friendships of My Little Pony, and the dark mysteries of Castlevania for older viewers, there’s a fantasy world to capture every child’s heart.

By thoughtfully choosing fantasy cartoons that align with a child’s interests and maturity, parents can provide not only hours of fun but also meaningful stories that nurture imagination, empathy, and bravery. The magic of these animated tales empowers kids to believe that with courage and kindness, they too can be heroes of their own stories—no matter how fantastical.

Whether your child is just discovering the allure of fantasy or is already a devoted fan, these cartoons stand out as some of the best ways to bring magical stories to life. Pop some popcorn, settle in, and embark on a fantastical journey—there has never been a better time to dive into the wondrous world of animated fantasy.
