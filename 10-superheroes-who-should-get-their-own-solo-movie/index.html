10 Superheroes Who Should Get Their Own Solo Movie


The superhero genre has exploded in popularity over the past two decades, with cinematic universes sprawling almost endlessly. While iconic characters like Spider-Man, Iron Man, and Wonder Woman have enjoyed their solo outings, numerous other compelling heroes remain unexplored in dedicated films. These unsung champions possess rich backstories, unique powers, and powerful narratives that deserve to be showcased on the big screen. Here’s a deep dive into 10 superheroes who should get their own solo movie — each promising fresh storytelling and renewed excitement within the genre.

1. Ms. Marvel (Kamala Khan)

Kamala Khan, also known as Ms. Marvel, represents a new generation of superheroes bringing vital diversity and relatability to the genre. As a Pakistani-American teenager from Jersey City, Kamala’s journey blends the superhero narrative with cultural identity, family dynamics, and adolescent struggles. Her shape-shifting abilities offer visually engaging action sequences and her vibrant personality provides heart and humor.

A solo movie for Ms. Marvel would not only attract younger audiences but also introduce a meaningful storyline about acceptance and empowerment. The character’s youthful optimism paired with modern challenges creates fertile ground for a fresh and inspiring film.

2. Moon Knight (Marc Spector)

Moon Knight enters the scene as Marvel’s darker, grittier vigilante, shrouded in mysticism and psychological complexity. A former mercenary burdened with dissociative identity disorder, Moon Knight serves as a conduit for exploring mental health through a superhero lens. His association with the Egyptian god Khonshu imbues the character with supernatural elements that differentiate him from typical street-level heroes.

A standalone Moon Knight movie could dive deep into themes of identity, faith, and redemption while delivering intense, stylized action and supernatural thrills. Its noir-ish tone would attract adult audiences craving mature storytelling.

3. She-Hulk (Jennifer Walters)

Jennifer Walters is a unique blend of lawyer, cousin to Bruce Banner, and titular She-Hulk. Her story offers a fresh take on the superhero trope by mixing legal drama, comedy, and superhuman strength. Unlike the typical Hulk, Jennifer retains her intelligence and personality when transformed, resulting in a character with tremendous depth and charisma.

A She-Hulk solo movie could capitalize on witty dialogue, courtroom antics, and impressive combat scenes. The combination of humor and action would appeal broadly, potentially blending genres in ways rarely seen in superhero films.

4. Blue Beetle (Jaime Reyes)

Jaime Reyes, the latest Blue Beetle, represents youthful enthusiasm combined with alien technology. His scarab armor grants him a vast array of powers, including energy blasts, flight, and enhanced durability, making for exciting visual effects. Moreover, Jaime navigates the challenges of growing up in a Texan community, balancing family, friendship, and superhero duties.

A solo Blue Beetle movie could explore cultural nuances within the Latino community while delivering sci-fi action and heartfelt storytelling. It’s an opportunity to spotlight lesser-known characters with blockbuster potential.

5. Squirrel Girl (Doreen Green)

Squirrel Girl is something of a cult favorite in the comic world, famous for her quirky humor and surprisingly formidable combat abilities. Despite her cute and whimsical appearance, she consistently outsmarts and defeats more traditionally “powerful” villains. This contradictory blend of lightheartedness with genuine heroism offers an entertaining and refreshing twist on superhero tales.

A Squirrel Girl solo movie could break the mold by embracing absurdity and parody without sacrificing heart. It’s the perfect vehicle for combining comedy with action and can resonate with fans craving an offbeat take on the genre.

6. America Chavez (Miss America)

America Chavez is an incredibly powerful hero with the ability to punch star-shaped portals through dimensions, allowing for interdimensional travel. Beyond her powers, her background as a Latina LGBTQ+ character from the Utopian Parallel makes her story rich with themes of identity, belonging, and the meaning of family.

A film centered around America Chavez would bring fresh perspectives to superhero cinema while unleashing visually dazzling sequences across multiple universes. Her confident, independent personality and heroic spirit make her a compelling protagonist for a solo adventure.

7. Nova (Sam Alexander)

As a member of the Nova Corps, Sam Alexander brings cosmic scale and youthful zeal to the Marvel Universe. Nova’s powers, granted by a symbiotic helmet, include super strength, flight, and energy manipulation, making him an intergalactic hero akin to a spacefaring Iron Man. His teenage persona caught between earthly life and cosmic responsibility offers rich storytelling opportunities.

A Nova solo movie could tap into space opera elements, combining thrilling sci-fi exploration with coming-of-age drama. The potential for stunning visuals and character growth is immense in this uncharted territory of Marvel’s cosmic universe.

8. Kate Bishop (Hawkeye)

Kate Bishop is the skilled archer who inherits the mantle of Hawkeye, carrying forward the legacy while adding her own flair. Her mastery of archery combined with street smarts and a touch of sass make her a standout character. Her role in the Young Avengers and mentorship under Clint Barton provide fertile ground for deep character development.

A Kate Bishop solo film could blend action-packed archery sequences with witty banter and heartfelt moments. Her relatable, grounded personality appeals to audiences hungry for grounded heroes with tangible flaws and aspirations.

9. The Phantom

Even though the Phantom hails from classic comic strips, this masked hero remains a treasure trove of untapped cinematic potential. The Phantom, known as the “Ghost Who Walks,” operates from the jungle of Bangalla, upholding justice through generational legacy rather than superpowers. His mythic quality and mysterious nature would translate well to a film with adventure and suspense.

A solo movie for The Phantom could evoke the adventurous spirit of classic serials like Indiana Jones, combining swashbuckling action with mythic storytelling. His aura of mystery and heroic ideals would resonate with both nostalgia seekers and new fans.

10. Mister Miracle (Scott Free)

Mister Miracle blends cosmic mythology with a deeply human story of escape and love. A master escape artist trained in the New Gods’ world, Scott Free wrestles with trauma and destiny while fighting dark forces on Earth and beyond. His combination of high-concept mythology and intimate drama sets him apart within the superhero pantheon.

A solo Mister Miracle movie could explore themes of freedom, resilience, and purpose through breathtaking escapology and visually imaginative worlds. The mix of emotional depth and fantastical spectacle offers a rare and captivating narrative voice.

Conclusion

While the superhero genre continues to dominate box offices worldwide, there remains a vast sea of fascinating characters deserving of their own cinematic spotlight. These 10 superheroes each bring something unique — whether it’s cultural representation, psychological complexity, cosmic scale, or quirky charm. Developing their stories into solo movies would enrich the superhero film landscape, providing audiences with fresh perspectives and innovative storytelling.

Bringing these heroes to life on screen promises not only thrilling entertainment but an expanded narrative universe where once-overlooked characters can truly shine. The time is ripe to embrace these extraordinary individuals whose solo adventures could redefine and refresh the genre for years to come.
