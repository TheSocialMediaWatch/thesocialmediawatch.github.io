The Best Music Podcasts for Aspiring Artists


For emerging musicians and aspiring artists, knowledge is power. Beyond mastering your instrument or perfecting your vocal style, understanding the music industry, marketing strategies, songwriting techniques, and the stories behind successful artists can make all the difference in carving a sustainable career. Where better to absorb this vast wealth of insight than through music podcasts? They offer intimate, behind-the-scenes conversations, expert advice, and fresh inspiration—all accessible anywhere, anytime.

Here’s a comprehensive exploration of the best music podcasts for aspiring artists, designed to enrich your craft, boost your career, and connect you with the pulse of the music world.

Why Music Podcasts Are Essential for Aspiring Artists

Podcasts have revolutionized the way creatives learn and network. For musicians trying to break into the industry, they offer several advantages:

  • Inside Industry Insights: Hearing directly from producers, label executives, and artists grants unfiltered knowledge that’s hard to find elsewhere.
  • Songwriting and Production Tips: Many podcasts focus on the nuts and bolts of musical craft, giving practical advice that you can apply immediately.
  • Inspiration and Motivation: Stories of struggle, creative process, and triumph open a window into the human side of music, reminding listeners that they’re not alone on their journey.
  • Marketing and Career Growth Strategies: Building a fanbase and standing out require more than just talent; many podcasts delve into branding, social media, and business tactics.
  • Community Connection: Podcasts often have followings of like-minded artists and provide a feeling of belonging to a creative tribe.

Curated List of Top Music Podcasts for Aspiring Artists

1. Song Exploder

Undeniably one of the most impactful music podcasts for creators, Song Exploder dissects individual songs through interviews with the musicians, producers, and engineers involved. Listeners get to hear the isolated layers of each track while the artists reveal their creative choices, inspirations, and challenges.

For aspiring artists, this podcast is a treasure trove of songwriting and production techniques dissected in an intimate and accessible format. Each episode offers lessons in narrative structure, arrangement, and how subtle sound decisions can dramatically affect the emotional impact of a song.

2. Switched on Pop

Hosted by musicologist Nate Sloan and songwriter Charlie Harding, this podcast blends music theory with pop culture analysis. It’s brilliant for musicians wanting to understand why certain songs catch on and the mechanics behind genre trends and hit formulas.

The dual perspective—academic and practical—helps aspiring artists elevate their songwriting while maintaining commercial appeal. Plus, the hosts often feature discussions on emerging artists and shifts in the musical landscape, crucial for staying ahead.

3. And The Writer Is…

If songwriting is your priority, And The Writer Is… offers deep dives into the writing room of celebrated songwriters. Host Ross Golan interviews hitmakers who provide candid insights into the creative process, collaboration, and the emotional core of writing songs.

Aspiring songwriters will appreciate the unvarnished discussions about the triumphs and setbacks that define music creation, along with tips on lyricism, melody crafting, and navigating co-writing relationships.

4. The Music Biz Weekly

Running a career in music requires business savvy, and The Music Biz Weekly podcast focuses on the entrepreneurial and technical skills behind success. Topics include digital distribution, royalty collection, marketing, and legal issues affecting artists today.

For emerging musicians seeking to create a sustainable career, this podcast breaks down complex industry mechanics with clarity and actionable advice, often featuring expert guests from different sectors of music business.

5. ARTISTdirect Insider

On ARTISTdirect Insider, host Gina Tolleson interviews a wide array of artists, producers, and music executives. The episodes provide perspective on creative inspiration, breaking through industry barriers, and learning the ropes of music marketing.

Listeners get exposed to various genres and artist journeys, opening their minds to different pathways in music careers. The conversations are encouraging and often provide humor and empathy alongside practical wisdom.

6. Broken Record

Hosted by the legendary producer Rick Rubin alongside Malcolm Gladwell, Bruce Headlam, and Justin Richmond, Broken Record is a rare glimpse into the art and craft of music making. Its guest list is filled with iconic and emerging figures, each episode a masterclass in creative philosophy and studio culture.

For aspiring artists, this podcast provides both inspiration and instruction, offering stories behind landmark albums and insights into the artist-producer dynamic.

7. Music Marketing Manifesto

This show is laser-focused on one big challenge for artists—growing an audience. Music Marketing Manifesto provides hands-on strategies to harness social media, playlists, email marketing, and more to build a loyal fanbase.

The practical tips are designed for artists at all levels, promoting thoughtful and authentic engagement with audiences rather than spammy tactics. This is an indispensable resource for musicians who want to take control of their promotional efforts.

8. The DIY Musician Podcast

Perfect for artists who want to keep full ownership of their work while maximizing reach, The DIY Musician Podcast shares stories from successful independent musicians. It offers actionable insights on recording, distribution, fan engagement, and monetization.

It’s both motivational and tactical—encouraging self-reliance and creativity in every aspect of an artist’s career. From home studio tips to crowdfunding advice, this podcast covers it all.

9. Songcraft

Songcraft peels back the layers of songwriting through detailed interviews and storytelling episodes. Host Joe Brock tracks the evolution of songs from concept to final mix, with guests sharing their influences and personal journeys.

Aspiring musicians get inspired to push their own creative boundaries while learning about the technical and emotional stages of music creation.

10. The Musician’s Guide to the Music Industry

Doubling as an educational resource, this podcast dives into contracts, copyrights, licensing, and other often opaque music business topics. Host Jason Corey explains complex concepts in accessible language to empower artists to negotiate better deals and protect their work.

Understanding these foundational aspects is crucial for long-term success and avoiding common pitfalls.

How to Use Music Podcasts to Fuel Your Artistic Growth

Listening to podcasts is just the start. To get maximum value:

  • Take Notes: Jot down insights, actionable tips, and recommended tools or resources mentioned.
  • Apply Consistently: Dedicate time each week to implement strategies or try new songwriting techniques.
  • Share and Discuss: Join communities or forums to discuss episodes, network, and exchange feedback with other artists.
  • Reflect on Stories: Use inspiration from artist journeys to motivate your own work during tough moments.
  • Diversify Listening: Mix podcasts focusing on craft, business, and marketing for a well-rounded perspective.

Final Thoughts

Music podcasts provide a rich, multifaceted resource for aspiring artists. Whether you want to sharpen your songwriting, understand the industry better, or find creative encouragement, there’s a podcast tailored for your needs.

Embrace this modern classroom and community. Your next breakthrough idea might just come from the stories, lessons, and conversations waiting in your favorite podcast episode.

Turn on your headphones, tune in, and level up your musical journey.
