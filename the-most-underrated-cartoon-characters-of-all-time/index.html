The Most Underrated Cartoon Characters of All Time


Cartoons have an undeniable place in our hearts. From childhood memories to binge-watching your favorite animated series, cartoon characters have a way of becoming an essential part of popular culture. Whether they’re goofy sidekicks, quirky antagonists, or quietly profound protagonists, these characters often bring more to the table than we initially give them credit for.

While some characters, like Homer Simpson, Bugs Bunny, and Scooby-Doo, have rightfully earned their place in the spotlight, there are many others who deserve more recognition. These are the unsung heroes, the ones who may not always steal the show but still leave a lasting impact on their audiences. Let’s dive into some of the most underrated cartoon characters of all time, who have yet to receive the credit they truly deserve.

1. Brain from Pinky and the Brain

For many, “Pinky and the Brain” was a staple of 90s television, with the duo of Pinky and Brain often stealing the spotlight. However, while Pinky’s goofy antics and hilarious quips are fondly remembered, Brain’s intelligence, ambition, and determination are frequently overshadowed. The show’s premise revolves around Brain, a highly intelligent, yet perpetually defeated, genetically modified mouse who seeks to take over the world.

Brain is often seen as the straight man in contrast to Pinky’s chaotic nature, but beneath his stern demeanor lies a character of depth. His quest for world domination isn’t just about power; it’s a testament to his undying ambition and his struggle against constant failure. He might not be as overtly funny as Pinky, but his sophisticated humor and the tragic element of his relentless desire to conquer the world make him an underrated character. His character arc highlights perseverance in the face of inevitable defeat, something that resonates with many fans on a deeper level.

2. Daria Morgendorffer from Daria

Daria Morgendorffer, the titular character from the animated series Daria, stands as one of the most underappreciated characters in the world of cartoons. In a world where most animated protagonists are colorful, loud, and often exaggerated, Daria’s quiet, dry, and sarcastic personality stands out. She’s the epitome of the cynical teen, observing the world around her with sharp wit and a healthy dose of skepticism.

Daria’s charm lies in her relatability. She’s the character that many of us can see parts of ourselves in—the one who refuses to conform to societal expectations and isn’t afraid to call out the absurdities of life. Although Daria was popular in the late 90s and early 2000s, the character’s complexity and nuanced outlook on the world have led to her being often overlooked in conversations about animated icons. Her understated intelligence and deadpan humor make her a timeless character who speaks to generations of introverts, misfits, and thinkers.

3. Fry from Futurama

Fry from Futurama is often overlooked as a simple, dim-witted character, but there’s far more to him than meets the eye. As the main protagonist of the series, Fry is a time-displaced pizza delivery boy who wakes up in the future after being frozen for 1,000 years. Initially, Fry may seem like just another goofy character, but his development over the course of the show is often understated.

Fry is the kind of character that people can’t help but love because of his optimism in the face of absurdity. His deep emotional connections with his friends and his surprising moments of insight often add a layer of unexpected depth to the show. Unlike other more traditionally “heroic” characters in animated series, Fry’s humanity shines through in his moments of vulnerability, making him one of the most underrated characters in animated television.

4. Ben Tennyson from Ben 10

Ben 10 was one of the most popular action-packed animated series of the mid-2000s, but Ben Tennyson, the series’ protagonist, often gets lost in the shuffle of all the aliens he transforms into. On the surface, Ben seems like your typical overconfident kid who loves to show off. However, as the series progresses, we see a character who matures, learns responsibility, and deals with the consequences of his actions.

Ben Tennyson’s evolution from a bratty, impulsive kid to a more thoughtful and capable young adult is one of the most impressive character arcs in modern animated television. His journey represents growth and self-discovery, and his responsibility as the wielder of the Omnitrix presents a heavy emotional burden. While Ben 10 is often remembered for its thrilling action sequences and imaginative alien designs, it’s Ben’s internal struggle and character growth that make him a standout, albeit underrated, character.

5. Mabel Pines from Gravity Falls

Mabel Pines from Gravity Falls is one of the most exuberant, colorful characters in modern animation, and yet, her depth and complexity are often overlooked. As the twin sister of Dipper Pines, Mabel’s boundless optimism, creativity, and tendency to act without thinking make her a fan favorite. On the surface, she may seem like the goofy, carefree character who contrasts with her more serious brother, but Mabel is much more than that.

Throughout the series, Mabel faces her own set of challenges. From the ups and downs of her friendships to her personal growth as a young adult, Mabel’s journey is one of self-expression, learning to navigate complex relationships, and understanding the importance of being true to herself. Despite her bubbly personality, Mabel’s struggles with growing up and finding her place in the world are deeply relatable and deserving of recognition. She’s not just a comic relief character but a vibrant and multifaceted protagonist who continues to resonate with audiences.

6. Daphne Blake from Scooby-Doo

Daphne Blake is often the forgotten member of the Scooby-Doo gang, overshadowed by the more prominent personalities of Scooby, Shaggy, Fred, and Velma. For many viewers, Daphne is just the “danger-prone” damsel in distress, but this characterization couldn’t be further from the truth. Over the years, Daphne has evolved into a much stronger and more capable character, especially in recent iterations of the show.

In many of the newer Scooby-Doo series, Daphne has been given more depth. She’s not only brave and resourceful, but she also often takes the lead in solving mysteries and showing leadership in times of crisis. Daphne’s character arc shows that intelligence and courage are not defined by traditional gender roles. She’s not just a pretty face or a love interest; she’s an integral part of the team, and her contributions to solving mysteries are often just as important as Velma’s intellectual insights or Fred’s leadership.

7. Mushu from Mulan

While Mulan is often celebrated as a powerful Disney heroine, her sidekick Mushu is often underrated. Mushu is a small, comedic dragon who, despite being tiny and seemingly insignificant, becomes one of Mulan’s most loyal allies in her journey to becoming a warrior. He provides much-needed comic relief in an otherwise serious film, but his role in Mulan’s development is critical.

Mushu’s growth throughout the film, from a mischievous character to one that genuinely supports Mulan’s success, is a subtle yet important aspect of the movie. His relationship with Mulan adds an emotional layer to the story, highlighting themes of loyalty, personal growth, and finding strength in unexpected places. Mushu is more than just a comedic sidekick—he’s a vital part of Mulan’s story, and his selflessness by the end of the movie further cements him as one of the most underrated characters in Disney history.

8. BMO from Adventure Time

Adventure Time is known for its bizarre and often surreal world, but one character that consistently flies under the radar is BMO. BMO, a small living video game console, provides moments of innocence, humor, and warmth throughout the series. While characters like Finn and Jake are the central focus of the show, BMO’s quirky personality and surprising moments of wisdom make them one of the most underrated characters.

BMO embodies the spirit of Adventure Time—a mix of innocence, existentialism, and strange humor. Despite being a machine, BMO often serves as the voice of reason or offers a fresh perspective on the situations surrounding them. Their genderless, childlike nature allows them to be both entertaining and deeply relatable, and their unique perspective on the world makes them one of the most endearing characters on the show.

Conclusion

Cartoons have a unique ability to bring characters to life in ways that are both entertaining and emotionally resonant. While some characters have become household names, others remain underappreciated, despite their depth and contribution to the stories they inhabit. The characters listed above may not always take center stage, but their nuanced personalities, compelling storylines, and emotional growth make them deserving of more recognition. Whether they’re the quiet observer, the misunderstood hero, or the comic relief that holds everything together, these underrated cartoon characters are vital parts of their respective shows and should be celebrated for the impact they’ve had on the world of animation.
