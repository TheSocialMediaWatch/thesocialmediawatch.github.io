10 Comedians You Didn't Know Were Also Writers


Comedians often captivate audiences with their clever observations, razor-sharp wit, and impeccable timing. But behind the spotlight, many comedians bring their talents to the page, crafting compelling narratives, penning hit shows, or scripting stand-up material for others. Some of your favorite comedians have been quietly flexing their writing muscles, contributing to the entertainment world in ways you might not expect. Let’s explore ten comedians who have a knack for writing and might surprise you with their versatility.

1. Mike Birbiglia

Mike Birbiglia is widely recognized for his storytelling-driven comedy specials, but his impressive talent as a writer often gets overlooked. Birbiglia’s ability to translate his comedic voice into literary work is showcased in his memoirs, like Sleepwalk with Me and The New One. These books delve into deeply personal topics with his signature mix of humor and honesty.

Beyond his books, Birbiglia’s writing credits extend to films like Don’t Think Twice, which he wrote and directed. The movie explores the dynamics of an improv troupe, reflecting Birbiglia’s insight into collaboration and comedy. With his work blending heartfelt emotion and meticulous humor, Birbiglia is a perfect example of a comedian whose writing shines both onstage and off.

2. Tina Fey

Known for her cutting humor and memorable impersonations on Saturday Night Live, Tina Fey is also a powerhouse in the world of writing. Fey didn’t just perform on SNL — she was also the show’s first female head writer, where she helped create iconic sketches and characters. Her skills as a writer are also evident in the critically acclaimed sitcom 30 Rock, which she created, wrote, and starred in.

Fey’s memoir, Bossypants, combines hilarious anecdotes with keen reflections on her career, cementing her reputation as a gifted author. Whether she’s penning jokes for television or translating her wit to books, Tina Fey balances humor with sharp storytelling.

3. Donald Glover

Before becoming Childish Gambino, Donald Glover started his Hollywood journey as part of the writing team for 30 Rock while still in college. Although Glover is widely celebrated for his stand-up, music, and acting, his contributions as a writer often fly under the radar.

Glover’s success on the page doesn’t stop with sketch comedy; he created and wrote the groundbreaking series Atlanta. The show blends satire, surrealism, and social commentary like few others, showcasing Glover’s ability to weave impactful narratives. His multidimensional career stands as a testament to his creative brilliance, both as a performer and a writer.

4. Phoebe Waller-Bridge

Phoebe Waller-Bridge made waves with her widely acclaimed series Fleabag, which she wrote and starred in. While audiences know her for her biting comedic timing and relatable performances, her writing skills are equally (if not more) brilliant.

From crafting the powerful scripts of Killing Eve (Season 1) to working on the latest Indiana Jones installment, Waller-Bridge effortlessly navigates between humor and drama. Her knack for introspective storytelling proves that she’s a comedian who brilliantly translates her talent into innovative and provocative writing.

5. John Mulaney

John Mulaney may be a comedy phenomenon thanks to his stand-up specials and self-deprecating humor, but his writing career is just as noteworthy. Mulaney cut his teeth as a writer on Saturday Night Live, co-creating some of the show’s most memorable sketches, including the beloved “Stefon” character performed by Bill Hader.

Mulaney also co-wrote several specials and projects beyond SNL, contributing to his reputation as a comedic mastermind. Whether he’s crafting observational jokes for the stage or helping to shape hilarious television moments, he consistently proves himself as a sharp and versatile writer.

6. Mindy Kaling

As a stand-up comedian and actress, Mindy Kaling delivers charm and humor in every performance. But her talents as a writer are equally dazzling. Kaling started gaining attention while writing for The Office, where she was also a standout cast member playing Kelly Kapoor.

Her writing ingenuity shines through in The Mindy Project, a sitcom she created, starred in, and frequently wrote for. Kaling has also authored several books, such as Is Everyone Hanging Out Without Me? (And Other Concerns), blending humor and heartfelt observations in her signature voice. Her ability to create smart, relatable comedy across multiple mediums has cemented her legacy as both a comedian and a writer.

7. Ali Wong

Ali Wong’s stand-up specials, including Baby Cobra and Hard Knock Wife, showcase her unapologetic humor and bold stage presence. But Wong is also a seasoned writer, known for blending humor and personal experiences into her work offstage.

She’s been part of the writing teams for popular television shows, such as Fresh Off the Boat. Beyond that, Wong’s debut book, Dear Girls: Intimate Tales, Untold Secrets & Advice for Living Your Best Life, further highlights her ability to craft compelling and hilarious narratives. Whether wielding a microphone or a pen, Wong’s talents are undeniable.

8. Hannibal Buress

Hannibal Buress is a master of deadpan comedy, but his unique humor doesn’t end with his stand-up routines. Buress has contributed as a writer to numerous projects, including his role in shaping Broad City, one of the most celebrated sitcoms of recent years.

Buress’s affinity for smart, offbeat humor translates seamlessly to the page, giving shows a distinctive comedic edge that reflects his style. Beyond television, he’s also involved in scriptwriting and creative projects, proving that his talents go far beyond the stage.

9. Mae Martin

Mae Martin, beloved for their intelligent and quirky comedy specials, is also carving out acclaim as a writer. Martin co-created, wrote, and starred in the Netflix series Feel Good, which blends humor with deeply personal themes like identity, addiction, and relationships.

Their writing style is raw, relatable, and full of heart — a skill honed not only through screenwriting but also in authoring the book Can Everyone Please Calm Down?. Martin’s work demonstrates an impressive ability to weave honesty and humor while pushing boundaries in traditional storytelling.

10. Bo Burnham

Bo Burnham’s comedy combines music, sharp social commentary, and emotional insight — and these skills make him a standout writer across multiple formats. Burnham wrote and directed Eighth Grade, earning widespread praise for crafting an authentic coming-of-age story that resonated across generations.

In addition to his work in film, Burnham’s comedy specials, such as Inside, showcase his ability to create introspective, lyrical, and philosophical pieces that blur the line between stand-up and poetry. His writing reflects a profound ability to analyze human behavior while delivering laughs, proving Burnham has a rare combination of talents.

From sitcoms to memoirs, stand-up specials to screenplays, these ten comedians showcase how humor can thrive in written form. Their ability to transition seamlessly between performing and writing demonstrates the creative depth inherent in comedy. Whether crafting punchlines or building worlds, these comedians prove that the art of laughter extends far beyond the stage. So next time you laugh at a joke or cheer for a show, think of the writers behind the scenes—and the comedians who use their comedic genius to leave an impact off-camera.
