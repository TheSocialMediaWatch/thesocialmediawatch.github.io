The Best Paranormal Documentaries You Must Watch


The world of the unknown has always fascinated humans, whether it be the mysteries of the universe or the eerie happenings that defy logic and reason. Paranormal phenomena have intrigued people for centuries, sparking both fear and curiosity in equal measure. In the realm of documentaries, there are numerous films that delve into this world, offering fascinating insights and chilling accounts of strange occurrences. From ghost sightings to UFO encounters, these documentaries provide a front-row seat to the unexplained. If you’re looking to explore the unknown and experience spine-chilling tales, here are some of the best paranormal documentaries you absolutely must watch.

1. The Phenomenon (2020)

Released in 2020, The Phenomenon stands out as one of the most comprehensive UFO documentaries to date. Directed by James Fox, this documentary takes an in-depth look at the history of UFO sightings and government cover-ups. Featuring interviews with former military personnel, government officials, and witnesses, The Phenomenon explores the most compelling and unexplained UFO encounters in recent history.

The film not only sheds light on the shocking evidence that has surfaced over the years but also examines the cultural and governmental response to these phenomena. From military pilots to civilians, the accounts of strange encounters are documented with startling clarity. The Phenomenon is a must-watch for anyone intrigued by the possibility of extraterrestrial life and the government’s hidden involvement in UFO research.

2. Cropsey (2009)

Cropsey is a chilling documentary that blends true crime with paranormal investigation. Directed by Barbara Brancaccio and Joshua Zeman, this film explores the urban legend of Cropsey, a boogeyman figure from Staten Island. However, this tale takes a dark turn as the filmmakers uncover disturbing connections to real-life events. The legend of Cropsey, once thought to be a simple scare tactic used by parents to keep their children in line, becomes intertwined with the unsolved disappearances of several children from the 1970s and 1980s.

Through interviews and archival footage, Cropsey presents an eerie investigation that suggests the legend may have been more grounded in reality than initially thought. This documentary captures the terrifying intersection of myth and reality, making it an essential watch for fans of true crime and the paranormal alike.

3. The Last Dance (2020)

While The Last Dance is primarily a sports documentary about Michael Jordan and the Chicago Bulls, it features a fascinating moment that could be classified as paranormal. In the final episode of the series, Jordan discusses the mysterious “flu game” where he played in the 1997 NBA Finals despite being severely ill. Many believe that the illness was more than just the flu—it was a mysterious poisoning linked to paranormal forces.

This moment, while not the main focus of the documentary, adds a chilling element to an otherwise uplifting sports story. It’s an intriguing reminder that sometimes the line between reality and the paranormal can be blurrier than we think, even in the world of professional sports.

4. The Devil and Father Amorth (2017)

If you’ve ever wondered about the true nature of exorcism and possession, The Devil and Father Amorth provides an extraordinary look. Directed by the legendary filmmaker William Friedkin, who is best known for directing The Exorcist, this documentary takes viewers inside a real exorcism performed by Father Gabriele Amorth, a renowned exorcist in the Vatican.

In the film, Friedkin documents Father Amorth’s work with a woman who claims to be possessed by the devil. The haunting footage of the exorcism, paired with Father Amorth’s insights into the process, provides a rare and chilling look at the world of possession and exorcism. This documentary will make you question the existence of dark forces and leave you with an unsettling feeling long after the credits roll.

5. Tales of the Red Caboose (2017)

For those who are intrigued by ghost stories rooted in American history, Tales of the Red Caboose is a captivating documentary that explores the legend of a haunted caboose in Kansas. The train car is said to be home to the restless spirit of a young woman who tragically died aboard the train years ago. The filmmakers interview locals who claim to have witnessed ghostly phenomena, including strange sounds and apparitions, inside the caboose.

The documentary weaves historical facts with modern-day ghostly encounters, creating a story that is both eerie and educational. It’s a perfect example of how paranormal documentaries can take viewers on a journey to uncover hidden legends and ghost stories that have been passed down through generations.

6. The Nightmare (2015)

The Nightmare by Rodney Ascher is a documentary that delves into the terrifying world of sleep paralysis. Featuring interviews with several individuals who have experienced sleep paralysis, this film explores the common threads that tie their experiences together, including feelings of intense fear, a sense of suffocation, and sightings of shadowy figures.

What makes The Nightmare so unnerving is its exploration of the mind’s ability to conjure terrifying images during sleep. The documentary suggests that sleep paralysis may not just be a sleep disorder but could also be tied to paranormal experiences, with some believing that these shadow figures are manifestations of demonic forces. The Nightmare offers a unique and thought-provoking perspective on the paranormal, making it an essential watch for anyone intrigued by the darker corners of the human psyche.

7. My Amityville Horror (2012)

The story of the Amityville Horror is one of the most infamous paranormal tales in modern history. In My Amityville Horror, the documentary focuses on Daniel Lutz, the eldest son of the Lutz family, who lived in the infamous house where paranormal activity was said to occur. Unlike many retellings of the Amityville story, this documentary gives Daniel Lutz the platform to recount his own terrifying experiences and explain how they affected him throughout his life.

The film is a raw, personal account of the haunting, offering a unique perspective on the events that made the Amityville house a name known worldwide. Whether you believe in the paranormal or not, My Amityville Horror provides a haunting and emotional story that will stay with you long after viewing.

8. Room 237 (2012)

Stanley Kubrick’s The Shining is widely regarded as one of the greatest horror films of all time, but what if there was more to the movie than just a simple ghost story? Room 237 is a fascinating documentary that examines the many theories surrounding the hidden meanings and conspiracies within The Shining. From the alleged portrayal of the Holocaust to suggestions that Kubrick secretly filmed the Apollo 11 moon landing, Room 237 delves into the many interpretations of the film’s eerie imagery.

Although the documentary focuses on a film, it touches on the paranormal and unexplained, with some of the theories presented suggesting that Kubrick’s movie was more than just fiction. If you’re a fan of The Shining and enjoy unraveling complex theories, Room 237 is a must-watch.

9. The House in Between (2020)

If you’re drawn to stories of haunted houses and the paranormal, The House in Between is a documentary that will captivate you. Directed by Mike Nichols, the film tells the true story of a home in Mississippi that has been the site of bizarre and unexplained phenomena for years. The owners of the house have reported strange sounds, objects moving on their own, and an overwhelming sense of dread whenever they’re inside.

The documentary features interviews with paranormal investigators who attempt to uncover the truth behind these eerie occurrences. The story is both compelling and unsettling, making The House in Between an essential watch for anyone interested in haunted houses and ghost stories.

10. Evil Genius: The True Story of America’s Most Diabolical Bank Heist (2018)

While not a traditional paranormal documentary, Evil Genius explores the strange and mysterious events surrounding the infamous “pizza bomber” case. This true crime documentary delves into the bizarre story of a bank robbery gone wrong, where a man was forced to rob a bank with a bomb strapped to his neck. The story takes unexpected twists, including involvement with a potential cult and unexplained motives, making it a fascinating and eerie watch.

What makes Evil Genius so captivating is how it blends elements of the unknown, combining true crime with the sense of a larger conspiracy, as if dark forces were at play behind the scenes. While the paranormal angle might not be as direct as other documentaries, the strange events and twists throughout the case will leave you questioning the forces at work.

In conclusion, the world of paranormal documentaries is filled with films that leave us questioning reality, offering new perspectives on the unexplained. Whether you’re fascinated by UFO sightings, haunted houses, or mysterious true crime cases, these documentaries provide a chilling and compelling look into the unknown. So, grab some popcorn, dim the lights, and prepare yourself for a journey into the realm of the paranormal with these unforgettable films.
