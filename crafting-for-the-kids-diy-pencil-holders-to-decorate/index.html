Crafting for the Kids: DIY Pencil Holders to Decorate


Are you looking for an easy and creative project to undertake with your kids over a cozy weekend or a chilly weekday afternoon? Look no further! Today, we will explore the world of DIY pencil holders that can be decorated to your heart’s content. This delightful activity will not only encourage creativity and unleash imagination in your little ones but also result in practical storage solutions for their stationery. Let’s dive right in to see how you can craft these customizable pencil holders for your children and watch their twinkling eyes as they decorate their masterpieces.

Gathering Supplies

Before we venture forth into the exciting realm of crafting, there must be an organized gathering of what we will need. The reliability of your art station will dictate the quality of your end-results. Here’s what you’ll require:

  • Cardboard
  • Scissors
  • Glue or strong adhesive
  • Decorative materials (paint, markers, colored paper, stickers, glitter, etc.)
  • A few old newspapers for the crafting surface

An entire pantry of paints or a caddy filled with markers is not mandatory, but pick what excites and sparks inquisitiveness in your kids. Alternatively, opt for items you already have lying around the house. Remember, crafting is all about freedom of expression and exploration.

Simple Steps for Crafting Your Pencil Holders

In no time at all, you can transform humble cardboard tubes from paper towels or wrapping paper into designer pencil holders fit for a young artist’s desk. Follow our easy-peasy step-by-step guide below to create these adorable DIY pencil holders.

Step 1: Preparing the Base

Empty cardboard tubes from your kitchen rolls or paper towels will serve as your pencil holders’ bases. Encourage your kids to wrap the entire tube using strips of colorful duct tape or construction paper. This not only gives them a background to work on but also strengthens the cardboard, making it quite sturdy.

Step 2: Creating Handles

Cut a strip of cardboard which is both long and wide enough to wrap around one end of the tube. Make a slit a little above the middle of your strip to insert and secure the pencil holder’s end. Glue the strip to the tube so it looks like the wishbone in a turkey, gently stretching the sides to form two handles. Add another layer of glue or adhesive over the entire joined area for added reinforcement.

Step 3: Decorating Time!

Now, the real fun begins. Set up a work station with your carton of craft materials. Let your children go wild by painting, drawing, or gluing on patterns or pictures of their favorite characters. They can doodle a personal message or a secretive code. The only limit here is their imagination. And remember: there is no right or wrong in this creative outlet.

Some ideas to help get your kids started include:

  • Creating a galaxy by spraying on ministars with glitter glue
  • Decorating the pencil holder in the theme of a favorite book, movie, or superhero
  • Using stamps to make prints of animals, plants, or even their own handprints!

The sky’s the limit, and who knows what delightful masterpieces might unfold?

Step 4: Securing the Decorations

After all that effort, it would be a bummer if your decorations slipped off the tube during playtime. Ensure the artwork doesn’t magic away by generously applying a layer of clear, glossy glue. If available, Mod Podge or a PVA glue spray can do the trick nicely. Let it dry thoroughly, and voila! You have a durable creation that’s sure to put a smile on their face every time they review it.

Step 5: Finalizing and Staging

Let your creations dry completely, preferably overnight. Once your children have successfully transformed the plain cardboard tubes into treasure, it’s time to place them on display. The stage is set!

Instruct your kids to arrange their artwork-filled pencil holders on their desks, shelves, or organize them in their craft boxes. This simple project will add a unique touch and a burst of colors to their learning space.

The Added Perks

Besides the excitement and fun brought to your home, this DIY pencil holder project has numerous added perks.

  • Educational: The process alters from cutting to gluing, to tactile experiences like handling paint, and experimenting with different textures. This repetitive and seemingly monotonous task strengthens hand-eye coordination and fine motor skills.
  • Creative Development: Allowing them to make uninhibited choices and decisions encourages creativity and self-expression.
  • Sensory Enjoyment: Kids love to explore different materials with touch, sight, and smell. Crafting provides a sensory-rich environment that caters to such exploratory instincts.

Conclusion

Despite the bustling world of technology, the charm of hands-on DIY crafting with the kids isn’t fading away. Assaulted by digital screens, this tangible activity provides a welcome reprieve for your children. It combines practical learning and creative self-expression into an activity that parents and kids can enjoy together.

From an exciting, colorful result – a personalized pencil holder, emerge countless lessons in patience, persistence, and a newfound appreciation for the art of crafting. What’s not to love?

Embark on this captivating journey with your kids. Happy crafting!
