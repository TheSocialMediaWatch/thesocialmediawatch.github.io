The Power of Empathy: 15 Life-Changing Books That Foster Compassion


Empathy is a superpower that very few people possess. It’s the ability to feel and understand what others are feeling, regardless of their situation. It often goes hand in hand with compassion, which is the desire to alleviate the suffering and pain we witness. In the fast-paced world we live in, it can be challenging to maintain connections with others and understand their emotions. On top of this, today’s environment is constantly pushing us into survivalist or self-centered mindsets. Here, books can serve as invaluable guides to expanding our understanding and practicing empathy.

In this blog post, we curate favorite reads that will not just soothe your soul and open up a deeper understanding of others but also transform the way you connect with the world. Each of these life-changing books fosters empathy by guiding you through varying perspectives and experiences, encouraging you to put yourself in someone else’s shoes.

  1. To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee

Harper Lee’s classic novel portrays empathy through the eyes of a child attending the Jim Crow-era South, highlighting the importance of understanding and equality. Scout and Jem Finch learn about injustice andacial prejudice, ultimately making them more empathetic individuals.

  1. The bell jar by Sylvia Plath

This semi-autobiographical novel by Sylvia Plath beautifully conveys the crippling impact of mental illness and societal expectations. We walk through the eyes of Esther Greenwood, and we experience her inner turmoil, helping us to better appreciate those struggling with mental health issues.

  1. The Color Purple by Alice Walker

This touching novel set in the early 20th-century American South portrays the resilience of the human spirit. This book offers us an intimate look into the struggles of a young Black woman named Celie, her journey to self-awareness, and finding love and understanding.

  1. Becoming by Michelle Obama

In her memoir, Mrs. Obama shares personal stories about her life, from childhood to the White House. It is not only a chronicle about her personal growth but also an enlightening journey into the world of public service and diplomacy.

  1. The Body Keeps the Score by Bessel van der Kolk

This comprehensive guide to the science of trauma and healing by psychologist Bessel van der Kolk offers an in-depth understanding of the impact trauma has on the human mind and body. By reading this, readers gain empathy towards survivors and develop a deeper comprehension of mental and emotional suffering.

  1. Wild Swans: Three Daughters of China by Jung Chang

Wild Swans chronicles four generations of a Chinese family through the story of three women, spanning from the end of the Manchu dynasty to present-day China. This compelling history provides readers with a greater understanding of the cultural, societal, and political changes that shaped modern China.

  1. The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks by Rebecca Skloot

Reporting the story of Henrietta Lacks and the “HeLa” cells taken from her without her knowledge, Rebecca Skloot sheds light not only on medical achievement and controversy but also the struggles of the Lacks family, creating an acute awareness of the human cost behind scientific progress.

  1. The Spirit Catches You and You Fall Down by Anne Fadiman

The Spirit Catches You and You Fall Down tells the story of the Hmong people from Laos and their experiences in the American medical system. The book fosters empathy and understanding by exploring cultural differences, misunderstandings, and the impact on a Hmong family.

  1. Educated by Tara Westover

Born into a survivalist family in rural Idaho, Tara Westover’s memoir takes readers through her journey of self-discovery and independence. Educated offers insights into the obstacles and privileges of education and the power of empathy in relationships.

  1. A Man Called Ove by Fredrik Backman

A Man Called Ove follows the life of a grumpy, isolated retiree who finds empathy in the most unexpected places — within himself and others. The novel shows how mutual understanding can transcend social boundaries and make life-changing connections.

  1. An American Marriage by Tayari Jones

In this gripping novel, author Tayari Jones explores the tumultuous life of a newly married couple, Roy and Celestial, when Roy is unjustly imprisoned. Jones empowers empathy as she illuminates the harsh realities faced by the prison system.

  1. The Sympathizer by Viet Thanh Nguyen

The Sympathizer is a compelling tale of a Vietnamese CIA agent navigating the complexities of life during and after the Vietnam war. By delving into politics, race, and personal identity, Nguyen provides an astute perspective that fosters empathy and understanding.

  1. Between the World and Me by Ta-Nehisi Coates

A powerful narrative written as a letter to his son, Between the World and Me by Ta-Nehisi Coates explores the reality of growing up black in America and combats the injustice of the societal issues that directly affect the Black community.

  1. The New Jim Crow by Michelle Alexander

Michelle Alexander’s The New Jim Crow offers a compelling argument on the persistence of racism through the mass incarceration system. By understanding this system, readers gain empathy and a greater outlook on improving current race relations.

  1. Florence Nightingale: The Woman and Her Legend by Mark Bostridge

We dive into the life of the legendary and pioneering nurse, Florence Nightingale, through Mark Bostridge’s biography. This book not only offers a clearer understanding of her accomplishments but also presents an empathetic view of the humanitarian and activist she truly was.

By embracing empathy through reading, we foster growth in self-awareness and become invaluable contributors to a more compassionate world. These books serve as powerful reminders of the potential for empathy to create meaningful connections and understanding with people across diverse cultures, backgrounds, and life experiences. Give them a read and embark on this eye-opening journey yourself.
