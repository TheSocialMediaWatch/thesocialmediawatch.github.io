How to Make Your Own Decorative Pillows: A DIY Guide


Creating your own decorative pillows is one of the simplest ways to refresh any living space, infuse personality into a room, and showcase your creativity. Whether you’re looking to add a burst of color to your couch, a dash of texture to your bed, or a personalized accent to a favorite chair, making throw pillows yourself offers infinite customization possibilities. This guide will walk you through every step, from brainstorming design ideas and gathering materials, to sewing and embellishing your unique decorative pillows. By the end, not only will you have brand new home accents, you’ll also gain the satisfaction that comes from making something with your own two hands.

Why Make Your Own Decorative Pillows?

Before diving into the how-to, let’s explore why DIY pillows are so popular among home décor enthusiasts. Store-bought pillows tend to be pricey, especially when you want quality fabric or unique designs. Designing your own gives you complete creative control: choose your favorite fabrics, add trims and embellishments, play with nonstandard shapes, and tailor each pillow to perfectly suit your décor style.

Additionally, custom pillows serve as thoughtful handmade gifts and can even be a stepping stone for starting a small home décor business. They’re quick projects that don’t require a huge investment in equipment or expertise, making them perfect for both beginners and seasoned crafters.

Step 1: Choose Your Style and Design

Start by deciding what style you want for your decorative pillows. Here are a few factors to consider:

Shape: While square pillows are classic, consider experimenting with rectangles (lumbar pillow), circles, bolsters, or novelty shapes (like hearts or stars).

Size: Measure your sofa, chairs, or bed to determine optimal pillow dimensions. Common throw pillow sizes are 16x16, 18x18, and 20x20 inches.

Color and Pattern: Do you want your pillows to pop or blend in with existing décor? Solid colors, bold prints, florals, stripes, geometrics—the options are endless. Mixing patterns can create a modern, eclectic look.

Texture: Velvet, linen, cotton, faux fur, knit—different materials can alter the entire mood of your space.

Embellishments: Pompoms, tassels, embroidery, applique, trims, and buttons let you personalize with flair.

Before picking specifics, browse décor magazines, Pinterest, or fabric store displays for inspiration.

Step 2: Gather Your Materials and Tools

Here’s a list of supplies you’ll need to make your own throw pillows:

  • Fabric: Home décor fabric, quilting cotton, canvas, velvet, linen, or any strong, washable fabric. For one 18-inch pillow, you’ll need about half a yard.
  • Pillow insert or stuffing: Purchase a ready-made pillow form or use polyester fiberfill.
  • Thread: Chosen to match or contrast with your fabric.
  • Scissors or rotary cutter
  • Measuring tape or ruler
  • Sewing pins or clips
  • Sewing machine (optional for no-sew methods)
  • Hand-sewing needle
  • Marking chalk or fabric pen
  • Iron and ironing board
  • Trims: (optional) Pompom fringe, piping, tassels, buttons, embroidery thread.
  • Zipper, buttons, or Velcro: (if you want the cover removable for washing)

Step 3: Cutting Your Fabric

Once you’ve settled on a design and washed your fabric (to prevent shrinkage later), it’s time to cut:

  1. Measure your pillow: For a snug cover, cut your fabric to the exact dimensions of your pillow insert plus a half-inch seam allowance on all sides. For example, for an 18x18 inch pillow, cut two 19x19 inch squares.

  2. Mark and cut: Use measuring tape and fabric chalk to outline your square (or whatever shape you’ve chosen) on the wrong side of the fabric. Double-check measurements before cutting.

  3. Cut both front and back: You can use the same fabric for both sides or mix it up (like a print on the front, solid on the back).

Step 4: Sew the Pillow Cover

Envelope Closure (Beginner-Friendly and No Zipper Required):

The envelope pillow cover is an easy, functional design that makes changing covers a breeze.

  1. For an 18x18 inch pillow:

    • Cut one 19x19 inch front piece
    • Cut two back pieces: each 19 inches wide, but only 13 inches tall

  2. Hem the edges: Fold one long edge of each back piece over by half an inch, press with an iron, fold over again, and sew a hem. These will form the presentable opening.

  3. Layer the fabric: Lay the front piece right side up. Place the two back pieces, right side down, so the hemmed edges overlap in the center.

  4. Pin and sew: Pin all around the edges. Sew a half-inch seam around the perimeter.

  5. Trim corners and turn: Snip the corners diagonally, being careful not to cut through the stitch line. Turn the cover right side out, push out corners with a point turner or blunt chopstick, and press flat with an iron.

  6. Stuff your pillow: Slip the pillow form through the envelope opening. That’s it!

Basic Pillow with Zipper Closure:

If you want a sleeker, more professional look, try a zipper closure.

  1. Pin zipper in place: Lay fabric right sides together. Place a zipper (about 2 inches shorter than the edge) face down at one edge and pin.

  2. Sew: Sew zipper in place using a zipper foot attachment on your sewing machine.

  3. Assemble the cover: Pin fabric right sides together, ensuring the zipper is open halfway for turning later. Sew around the remaining three edges.

  4. Turn out: Open zipper all the way, turn the pillow cover right side out, and stuff with your insert.

Step 5: No-Sew Pillow Covers

Not a fan of sewing? No problem! Here’s a simple no-sew pillow option using fabric glue or fusible webbing:

  1. Cut two pieces of fabric to size (add a 1-inch seam allowance).
  2. Apply fabric glue or iron-on hemming tape to three sides, leaving one side open.
  3. Let set or press with an iron (follow glue/webbing instructions).
  4. Turn pilllow cover right side out.
  5. Insert pillow form and glue last side shut, or use Velcro for a removable closure.

Step 6: Embellish Your Pillow

Once your pillow cover is assembled, have fun adding trims or other details. Here are some possibilities:

Piping:

Sew piping into the seams before assembling the cover for a tailored look.

Tassels or Pompoms:

Attach to the corners for playful flair with a few hand stitches.

Embroidery or Applique:

Hand or machine stitch designs on the front piece before assembling.

Buttons:

Add oversized buttons, either functional (as closure) or purely decorative.

Fabric Paint or Dye:

Personalize with stencils, hand-painting, ombre dip-dye, or tie-dye effects.

Stay creative—your design options are truly limitless.

Step 7: Finishing Touches and Care Tips

Press seams: For a crisp, professional appearance, press all seams flat as the final step.
Check fit: If the cover looks loose, turn inside out and sew slightly inside the first seam line for a tighter fit.
Care: Always pre-wash fabric before sewing to reduce shrinkage. For removable covers, launder as recommended for your chosen material.

Creative Pillow Ideas to Try

  1. Seasonal pillows: Switch fabrics and motifs for holidays—autumn leaves, winter snowflakes, spring florals, or summer coastal themes.
  2. Personalized gifts: Use stenciled names, fun phrases, or embroidered monograms for birthdays or housewarmings.
  3. Kids’ rooms: Mini pillows in animal shapes or bright colors make fun additions to children’s spaces.
  4. Eco-friendly pillows: Upcycle an old sweater or repurpose a vintage tablecloth for a one-of-a-kind piece.
  5. Patchwork or quilted: Piece together scrap fabrics for a boho, homespun vibe.

Troubleshooting Common Issues

  • Pillow insert bunches in the corner? Fluff the insert and poke it into each corner with your fingers or a knitting needle.
  • Seams look uneven? Double-check your measurements and use a seam guide on your sewing machine.
  • Fabric puckers? Use the correct needle and thread type for your fabric; iron seams flat after sewing.
  • Cover fits too loosely? Sew a second seam inside the first to tighten fit.

Remember: Practice makes perfect, and even small imperfections add handmade charm.

Sustainable and Budget-Friendly Tips

  • Upcycle: Repurpose old shirts, jeans, tablecloths, or curtains into new pillow covers.
  • Buy remnant fabric: Check fabric stores for remnant sections and end-of-bolt discounts.
  • Reuse pillow inserts: Give old pillows new life by washing and reusing the insert with a fresh cover.

Final Thoughts

Homemade decorative pillows bring more than just color and comfort to a room—they tell a unique story, reflecting your personality and style. Whether you craft a single eye-catching accent or whip up a coordinated stack, you’ll enjoy both the process and the final results. Experiment with new techniques, play with bold colors, or stick to serene neutrals—there’s no wrong way to tackle this quick, rewarding DIY project.

Ready to brighten up your space? Gather your favorite fabrics, let your creativity flow, and start creating custom pillows perfect for your home. You might just discover that one pillow leads to another—and soon, you’ll wonder how you ever settled for store-bought.
