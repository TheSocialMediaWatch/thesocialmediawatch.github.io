10 Satirical News Websites You Should Bookmark


In a world where headlines often feel stranger than fiction, satirical news websites offer a humorous reprieve for those looking to escape the seriousness of daily events. These sites cleverly craft content that teeters between absurdity and social commentary, offering laughs while often shedding light on societal truths. Whether you’re in need of witty parody or biting critique, these ten satirical sites prove there’s no shortage of creativity when it comes to poking fun at the news cycle. Here are 10 satirical news websites you should bookmark today.

1. The Onion

Arguably the most well-known satirical news outlet, The Onion has been delivering sharp, tongue-in-cheek articles since 1988. With headlines ranging from outrageously absurd to hilariously relatable, The Onion is a masterclass in parody. Whether it’s political commentary or mock advice columns, their content never fails to deliver. They’ve even fooled major networks and politicians into thinking their satire is real, which just underscores how spot-on their humor is.

Why bookmark it?

The Onion excels at blending sharp humor with timely topics. It’s perfect for anyone who loves satire that’s both laugh-out-loud funny and subtly insightful.

2. ClickHole

Spun off from The Onion, ClickHole takes aim at the internet culture of mindless viral content. From clickbait-style headlines like “You Won’t Believe What This Walrus Did To Preserve Its Legacy” to completely outlandish quizzes, ClickHole mimics the tone and substance of viral websites with uncanny precision. The result? Satirical articles that make you laugh and question why you ever fell for similar content in the first place.

Why bookmark it?

If you’re someone who gets bombarded with clickbait headlines daily, ClickHole offers an exaggerated—but wildly entertaining—reminder to think twice before clicking.

3. Reductress

Calling all fans of satirical content with a feminist twist: Reductress has you covered. This website parodies the often problematic world of women’s magazines and lifestyle blogs, delivering hilarious, pointed headlines with a progressive edge. Articles like “How to Convince Yourself a Man Who’s Nice to You Must Be Your Soulmate” or “Accessories That Say ‘I’m Not Here to Make Friends’” reflect a sharp critique of societal expectations placed on women.

Why bookmark it?

The humor is refreshing, relatable, and unapologetically feminist. Reductress smartly mixes biting satire with wit that feels like a friend laughing with you at life’s absurdities.

4. The Beaverton

For those who love satire with a Canadian twist, The Beaverton is your go-to site. While it covers global topics, its humor leans heavily into Canadian culture and politics. Articles like “Toronto Man Shocked to Learn He’s Not Actually Liberal After Visiting Alberta” or “Canadians Enthusiastically Returning to Seasonal Hobby of Complaining About Winter” find hilarity in small, uniquely Canadian truths.

Why bookmark it?

The Beaverton will make you laugh while subtly educating readers about the nuances of Canadian life and global matters—all with razor-sharp satire.

5. The Daily Mash

Straight from the UK, The Daily Mash combines British wit with cutting humor about politics, lifestyle, and everything in between. Their headlines, such as “Middle-Class Man Knows He Shouldn’t But Really Loves Greggs” or “Nation Wonders When Their Lives Turned Into Black Mirror Episode,” deliver a uniquely British take on satire with universal appeal.

Why bookmark it?

The Daily Mash’s quick wit, coupled with culturally specific humor and universal themes, ensures laughter for readers across the globe.

6. Waterford Whispers News

If Irish humor is your cup of tea—or pint of ale—look no further than Waterford Whispers News. This Dublin-based satirical outlet doesn’t hold back, addressing political absurdities, societal trends, and global happenings with hilarious flair. The satire is distinctly Irish but speaks to anyone who loves sharp, irreverent comedy.

Why bookmark it?

Its unique voice and perspective add a fresh flavor to the world of satire, keeping readers entertained while subtly informed.

7. Babylon Bee

Babylon Bee has carved its niche as a satirical news site with a focus on conservative culture and religious humor. The site targets absurdities in faith-based communities, politics, and pop culture. Headlines like “Maintenance Man Asks God To Change That One Light Bulb Way Up High” blend humor with commentary on modern Christianity and evangelicalism.

Why bookmark it?

Babylon Bee offers a different lens on satire, appealing especially to those interested in faith-based humor intertwined with comedy.

8. The Poke

Self-described as the “UK’s leading purveyors of topical hilarity,” The Poke features clever takes on everything from banal reality TV moments to shockingly tone-deaf celebrity comments. Unlike other sites, The Poke often features a mix of original content and curated viral memes that enhance its comedic output.

Why bookmark it?

If you’re a fan of quick humor combined with the best of internet culture, The Poke is ideal for basking in laughs while scrolling.

9. Duffel Blog

For those looking for satire targeted at the military world, Duffel Blog has earned its stripes. Known as “The American Military’s Most Trusted News Source,” this site humorously critiques military bureaucracy, operations, and culture. The blending of insider knowledge and clever comedy makes Duffel Blog a favorite among veterans and civilians alike.

Why bookmark it?

Duffel Blog lets you laugh at the often-ridiculous side of military life while providing surprisingly nuanced takes on pressing issues.

10. The Civilian

Rounding out the list is The Civilian, New Zealand’s answer to satire. While its coverage of New Zealand politics and culture takes center stage, The Civilian also dips into global topics with hilarious headlines like “Government Admits Housing Crisis Was Just Elaborate Test of New Zealanders’ Resilience.”

Why bookmark it?

For a mix of cleverness and culturally specific humor, The Civilian delivers unique satire for readers in New Zealand and beyond.

The Role of Satire in Informing Through Humor

Though these websites primarily aim to entertain, their role in modern media is often underestimated. Satirical articles don’t just make us laugh—they hold up a mirror to society, politics, and culture, encouraging critical thinking while exposing absurdities. A well-crafted joke can sometimes convey truths better than a thousand earnest headlines.

While bookmarking these ten websites may not replace your usual news consumption, it will ensure your humor quota remains fully stacked. Whether it’s politics, pop culture, or societal quirks, satirical news offers a way to laugh while gaining perspective. Dive in, scroll away, and enjoy the clever world of fake news crafted to highlight life’s oddities.
