The Ghosts of the Myrtles Plantation: Haunted History of the South


Nestled in the heart of St. Francisville, Louisiana, lies the Myrtles Plantation, a majestic and captivating piece of Southern history. But behind its beautiful facade lies a dark and haunting past. Known as one of the most haunted houses in America, the Myrtles Plantation has attracted visitors from all over the world, eager to uncover the secrets of its ghostly inhabitants.

History of the Myrtles Plantation

Originally built in 1796 by General David Bradford, the Myrtles Plantation has witnessed centuries of history and tragedy. It was later acquired by Judge Clark Woodruff, whose family would become the center of the plantation’s most chilling tales. The Woodruff family endured numerous deaths and misfortunes, which many believe have left their mark on the property.

The Ghosts of the Myrtles Plantation

Chloe, the Mischievous Slave Girl

One of the most famous spirits believed to haunt the Myrtles Plantation is Chloe, a slave girl who was said to have been involved in a scandalous affair with Judge Woodruff. According to legend, Chloe eavesdropped on the family’s conversations and was caught. In fear of being punished, she resorted to poisoning the family. However, her plan went awry, and she ended up killing Judge Woodruff’s wife and two of their children.

The Ghosts of the Woodruff Children

The spirits of the Woodruff children, who tragically lost their lives at the hands of Chloe’s poisoning, are said to roam the halls of the Myrtles Plantation. Visitors have reported hearing the sound of children’s laughter and footsteps, as well as witnessing the apparitions of young boys and girls playing in the rooms.

The Lady in Green

Another prominent ghostly figure at the Myrtles Plantation is the Lady in Green, believed to be the spirit of a former plantation owner. She has been spotted wandering through the gardens and hallways, often appearing in photographs taken by unsuspecting visitors. Some speculate that she may be searching for her lost love or seeking redemption for the sins committed during her lifetime.

Other Haunting Phenomena

Aside from the main ghostly inhabitants, the Myrtles Plantation is also known for other paranormal activities. Guests have reported unexplained footsteps, mysterious voices, and objects moving on their own. Some have even claimed to have been touched or pushed by unseen forces, leaving them with an undeniable sense of dread.

Visiting the Myrtles Plantation

For those brave enough to explore the haunted history of the Myrtles Plantation, guided tours are available throughout the year. Visitors can immerse themselves in the chilling stories and witness firsthand the paranormal phenomena that have intrigued and terrified people for generations. Whether you are a skeptic or a believer, the Myrtles Plantation promises an unforgettable experience that will leave you questioning the boundaries between the living and the dead.

Conclusion

The Myrtles Plantation stands as a testament to the rich and haunting history of the American South. Its ghostly residents and eerie tales continue to captivate and intrigue visitors, making it a must-visit destination for those seeking a thrilling paranormal adventure. So, if you dare to venture into the world of the supernatural, unlock the secrets of the Myrtles Plantation and prepare to be haunted by the ghosts of the past.
