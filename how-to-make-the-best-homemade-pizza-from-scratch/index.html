How to Make the Best Homemade Pizza from Scratch


Who doesn’t love a good homemade pizza? The aroma of freshly baked dough, the melted cheese, and the savory toppings – it’s a culinary experience that never fails to satisfy. Making your own pizza from scratch may seem daunting at first, but with the right guidance, you can create a mouthwatering masterpiece right in your own kitchen. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the step-by-step process of making the best homemade pizza from scratch, from preparing the dough to choosing the perfect toppings.

Ingredients

For the Pizza Dough

  • 3 1/2 cups (440g) all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/3 cups (300ml) warm water
  • 2 1/4 teaspoons (7g) active dry yeast
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

For the Pizza Sauce

  • 1 can (400g) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the Toppings

  • Fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • Pepperoni, sliced
  • Mushrooms, sliced
  • Bell peppers, diced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

Step 1: Prepare the Dough

  1. In a small bowl, mix the warm water and sugar. Sprinkle the yeast on top and let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and pour in the yeast mixture and olive oil.
  3. Stir the mixture until a dough forms. Turn the dough onto a floured surface and knead for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-1.5 hours until doubled in size.

Step 2: Make the Pizza Sauce

  1. In a saucepan, heat a tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  2. Pour in the crushed tomatoes, dried oregano, dried basil, salt, and pepper. Simmer the sauce for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens.

Step 3: Assemble the Pizza

  1. Preheat your oven to the highest temperature it can go, usually around 500°F (260°C).
  2. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and divide it into two equal portions. Roll out each portion on a floured surface to your desired thickness.
  3. Transfer the dough to a lightly greased pizza pan or baking sheet.
  4. Spread a generous layer of the pizza sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  5. Arrange the sliced mozzarella, pepperoni, mushrooms, and bell peppers on top of the sauce. Finish with a sprinkle of grated Parmesan cheese.

Step 4: Bake the Pizza

  1. Place the assembled pizza in the preheated oven and bake for 12-15 minutes until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  2. Remove the pizza from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before slicing.
  3. Garnish with fresh basil leaves and a drizzle of olive oil before serving.

Tips for the Perfect Homemade Pizza

  • Use a pizza stone for a crispy crust.
  • Experiment with different toppings such as caramelized onions, prosciutto, or arugula for a gourmet twist.
  • For a thinner crust, roll the dough out even thinner before baking.
  • Let the dough rest in the refrigerator for 24-48 hours for enhanced flavor and texture.

With these simple steps and a little practice, you can enjoy a delicious homemade pizza that rivals the best pizzerias. The beauty of making pizza from scratch is that you have complete control over the ingredients and flavors, allowing you to personalize each pie to your liking. So, gather your family and friends, roll up your sleeves, and embark on a pizza-making adventure that will leave everyone craving for more. Happy baking!
