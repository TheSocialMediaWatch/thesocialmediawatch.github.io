The Top 5 Running Drills for Improving Your Performance


Running is more than just putting one foot in front of the other. Whether you’re a seasoned athlete chasing a personal best or a casual runner looking to improve endurance and speed, refining your technique is crucial for reaching your goals. The secret to boosting your running performance lies not only in logging miles but also in incorporating targeted running drills that enhance strength, coordination, and efficiency.

In this comprehensive guide, we’re diving deep into the top 5 running drills designed to elevate your form, power, and speed. These exercises cater to all levels and can be seamlessly integrated into your training sessions to help you become a stronger, faster, and more resilient runner.

1. A-Skips: Mastering Coordination and Knee Drive

The A-Skip drill is a classic favorite among coaches for good reason. It targets coordination, rhythm, and optimal knee lift, all of which are essential for an efficient stride.

How to Perform A-Skips:

  • Start standing tall, engaging your core.
  • Drive one knee up toward your chest while hopping on the opposite foot.
  • Simultaneously, pump your arms in synchronization with your legs.
  • Land softly on the ball of your foot, absorbing impact with your ankle and calf.
  • Maintain an upright posture and focus on a quick, fluid motion.
  • Repeat for 20-30 meters at a controlled pace.

Benefits of A-Skips:

  • Improves neuromuscular coordination.
  • Boosts hip flexor strength.
  • Enhances sprint mechanics by promoting triple flexion (ankle, knee, hip).
  • Develops better ground contact time, which is key for faster running.

Incorporate A-skips into your warm-ups or technique days. Performing them regularly builds muscle memory for proper form, even at high speeds.

2. Butt Kicks: Activating the Hamstrings and Promoting Leg Recovery

Butt kicks are more than a fun sprinting game; they’re a powerful drill to activate your hamstrings and promote quicker leg recovery during stride cycles.

Technique Breakdown:

  • Stand tall and begin jogging forward.
  • Drive your heels upward to touch or come close to your glutes.
  • Make sure your knees stay pointing down and don’t flare out.
  • Keep your posture aligned—avoid leaning forward or backward.
  • Swing your arms naturally in opposition to your legs.
  • Cover 20-30 meters with controlled, rhythmic pace.

Why Butt Kicks Matter:

  • Strengthen and engage hamstring muscles critical for propulsion.
  • Improve leg turnover by training faster limb repositioning.
  • Promote elasticity and flexibility of the posterior chain.
  • Aid in improving stride frequency, a determinant of speed.

This drill is particularly useful during warm-ups or post-run cooldowns to reduce hamstring tightness and injury risk.

3. High Knees: Enhancing Speed and Cardiovascular Endurance

High knees elevate your heart rate while drilling proper knee lift and ankle drive, key components for explosive running.

Steps to Execute:

  • Begin jogging in place or moving forward.
  • Lift your knees as high as possible toward your chest with quick, sharp movements.
  • Maintain an active arm drive that complements leg movement.
  • Keep your torso upright and core tight.
  • Land on the balls of your feet for minimal ground contact.
  • Perform sets of 20-30 seconds with rest intervals for best results.

Advantages of High Knees:

  • Builds power in hip flexors and calves.
  • Conditions cardiovascular systems alongside technique enhancement.
  • Improves stride rate and overall agility.
  • Reinforces proper running posture and alignment.

Add high knees to your dynamic warm-up routine or speed intervals to stimulate fast-twitch muscle fibers and elevate pace.

4. Bounding: Cultivating Explosiveness and Stride Length

Bounding, akin to exaggerated running strides, is a plyometric drill that emphasizes power and stride length, improving running economy and speed.

How To Bound Correctly:

  • Start with a light jog, then begin taking large, exaggerated strides.
  • Push off forcefully from the ball of your foot, propelling your body forward and upward.
  • Extend your leg fully for maximum reach while swinging the opposite arm vigorously.
  • Land softly on the opposite foot, immediately transitioning into the next bound.
  • Keep your torso steady to avoid excessive lateral motion.
  • Target 20-40 meters at a controlled speed.

Why Bounding Is Effective:

  • Enhances leg power through plyometric loading.
  • Trains elastic energy buildup in tendons and muscles, aiding running efficiency.
  • Develops balance and coordination.
  • Encourages proper long-stride mechanics without overstriding and risking injury.

Incorporate bounding into speed sessions or strength days. It complements hill sprints beautifully for runners preparing for races requiring explosive bursts.

5. Strides: Sharpening Speed and Relaxation

Strides are short, controlled sprints typically used for sharpening speed and technique without undue fatigue. They offer a perfect blend of speed work and form practice.

Executing Perfect Strides:

  • Choose a flat area or track and mark 60 to 100 meters distance.
  • Start at an easy jog then gradually accelerate to about 85-95% of maximal effort halfway through.
  • Focus on relaxed shoulders, quick turnover, and balanced arm drive.
  • Maintain a consistent form without tension in your face or hands.
  • Decelerate smoothly after crossing the mark.
  • Perform 4-6 strides with ample recovery (walking or slow jogging) between reps.

Benefits of Incorporating Strides:

  • Enhances neuromuscular coordination at faster paces.
  • Trains the body to run efficiently at near-maximal velocities.
  • Helps maintain good running mechanics under speed-induced strain.
  • Stimulates mental sharpness and race readiness.

Including strides 2-3 times a week can have a substantial impact on your overall running quality and confidence when increasing pace.

Putting It All Together: Designing Your Running Drill Routine

A well-rounded training regimen capitalizes on the synergy between various drills. Here’s how you can integrate these five drills into a weekly program:

  • Warm-up: Begin every session with A-skips and butt kicks to activate major muscle groups and perfect movement patterns.
  • Speed session: Use high knees and strides to enhance turnover and speed.
  • Strength/power day: Employ bounding to develop explosiveness, especially when combined with hill workouts.
  • Cooldown: Light butt kicks and gentle bounding can assist recovery and maintain mobility.

Sample Weekly Plan:

DayDrills Focus
MondayA-skips, Butt Kicks, Light Jogging
WednesdayHigh Knees, Strides
FridayBounding, Hills, Strides
SundayRecovery Run with Butt Kicks & A-Skips

Adjust intensity and volume based on your fitness level and upcoming race goals. The key is consistent, deliberate practice that builds mechanical efficiency and minimizes injury risk.

Final Thoughts

Incorporating these top 5 running drills can transform your performance by improving strength, coordination, and speed mechanics. More than just adding variety, these drills challenge your body to move with precision and power — unlocking potential that simple miles alone cannot achieve.

Whether you’re chasing a marathon PR or just aiming to feel more comfortable on your daily runs, mastering these drills will sharpen your technique and boost your confidence. Try to weave these exercises seamlessly into your training cycle, and watch your running evolve from good to exceptional.

Striding toward personal bests doesn’t happen by chance; it happens by design. Let these drills be your blueprint.

By engaging regularly in these targeted running drills, you’ll build a more resilient and efficient running form over time. Happy running!
