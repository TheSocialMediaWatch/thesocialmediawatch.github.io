Top 5 Road Trip Routes in the United States


The United States is a vast and diverse country, home to a wealth of stunning landscapes, fascinating landmarks, and vibrant cities, making it one of the best destinations for an unforgettable road trip. Whether you’re a fan of scenic coastal drives, dramatic desert landscapes, or winding mountain roads, the U.S. offers something for every type of traveler.

From the rolling hills of the Midwest to the rugged beauty of the Pacific Coast, the following five road trip routes showcase the best of America’s natural beauty and cultural heritage. Pack your bags, fuel up, and hit the road for an adventure you’ll never forget.

1. Pacific Coast Highway (California)

The Pacific Coast Highway (PCH), also known as California State Route 1, is one of the most iconic road trips in the United States. Stretching over 650 miles from Southern California to the northernmost points of the state, this coastal route offers some of the most breathtaking views in the world.

Highlights of the Pacific Coast Highway

  • Big Sur: This section of the highway is famous for its dramatic cliffs, lush forests, and panoramic ocean views. The drive through Big Sur is an experience in itself, with stops like McWay Falls and Bixby Creek Bridge providing jaw-dropping photo opportunities.

  • Santa Barbara: Known as the “American Riviera,” Santa Barbara boasts beautiful Mediterranean architecture and a stunning coastline. It’s a great spot to take a break and enjoy fine dining and wine tasting.

  • San Francisco: The iconic Golden Gate Bridge, Alcatraz Island, and Fisherman’s Wharf are just a few of the many attractions you can explore in San Francisco, which sits at the northern end of the PCH.

  • Monterey and Carmel-by-the-Sea: These charming coastal towns are famous for their rich history, art galleries, and the world-renowned Monterey Bay Aquarium.

Along the way, you’ll find plenty of opportunities to explore hidden beaches, hike through redwood forests, and indulge in the fresh seafood that defines the region.

Best Time to Go

The best time to drive the Pacific Coast Highway is during the spring or fall, when the weather is mild, and the crowds are smaller than in the summer months.

2. Route 66 (Chicago to Santa Monica)

Known as the “Main Street of America,” Route 66 is an iconic route that has been immortalized in pop culture, history, and literature. Originally opened in 1926, this historic route stretches from Chicago to Santa Monica, California, and covers over 2,400 miles through eight states.

Highlights of Route 66

  • Chicago: Starting at the eastern terminus, you can explore the Windy City’s impressive skyline, iconic architecture, and diverse food scene. Don’t miss out on the deep-dish pizza or a stroll through Millennium Park.

  • St. Louis, Missouri: Home to the famous Gateway Arch, St. Louis offers a blend of history and modern culture. While in town, be sure to visit the Missouri Botanical Garden and the National Blues Museum.

  • Route 66 Museums: Along the way, you’ll find quirky museums dedicated to the history of the highway, including the Route 66 Museum in Clinton, Oklahoma, and the Route 66 Hall of Fame and Museum in Pontiac, Illinois.

  • Santa Monica: End your journey at the famous Santa Monica Pier, where you can relax on the beach or enjoy the iconic Ferris wheel.

Best Time to Go

Route 66 can be traveled year-round, but the spring and fall seasons offer the most pleasant weather. Summer temperatures can be harsh, particularly in the desert regions of Arizona and New Mexico.

3. The Great River Road (Mississippi River)

The Great River Road is a scenic byway that follows the Mississippi River for more than 2,000 miles, from Minnesota down to Louisiana. This route offers a unique blend of American history, charming small towns, and scenic river vistas.

Highlights of the Great River Road

  • Minneapolis, Minnesota: Start your journey in the vibrant city of Minneapolis, known for its beautiful parks, lakes, and museums. The city is also home to the iconic Stone Arch Bridge that spans the Mississippi River.

  • Dubuque, Iowa: A charming town located along the river, Dubuque offers stunning views of the Mississippi, as well as attractions like the National Mississippi River Museum and Aquarium.

  • St. Louis, Missouri: The Gateway to the West, St. Louis offers numerous riverfront parks, historic sites, and cultural attractions. The Mississippi River runs right through the city, offering views of the mighty river and the Gateway Arch.

  • New Orleans, Louisiana: End your journey in New Orleans, a city known for its lively music scene, delicious Creole cuisine, and rich cultural heritage. The Mississippi River plays a key role in the city’s identity, and a cruise along the river is a great way to wrap up your trip.

Best Time to Go

The best time to take a road trip along the Great River Road is during the spring or fall when the weather is mild and the landscape is at its most vibrant. Summer months can be hot and humid, especially in the southern regions.

4. Blue Ridge Parkway (Virginia and North Carolina)

The Blue Ridge Parkway is a 469-mile stretch of scenic road that runs through the Appalachian Highlands, connecting the Great Smoky Mountains National Park in North Carolina to Shenandoah National Park in Virginia. Known for its stunning mountain views, dense forests, and picturesque valleys, this is one of the most scenic drives in the United States.

Highlights of the Blue Ridge Parkway

  • Shenandoah National Park: Start your journey in Virginia at the northern terminus of the Parkway. Shenandoah National Park offers over 200,000 acres of protected land, including hundreds of miles of hiking trails and breathtaking views of the Blue Ridge Mountains.

  • Lover’s Leap: This viewpoint in Virginia offers a panoramic vista of the Blue Ridge Mountains and is a popular stop for travelers. The legend behind the name adds to the charm of the location.

  • Great Smoky Mountains: The southern terminus of the Parkway takes you to the Great Smoky Mountains, one of the most biologically diverse national parks in the country. You’ll encounter a variety of wildlife, dense forests, and waterfalls, making it a hiker’s paradise.

  • Mabry Mill: This historic mill in Virginia is one of the most photographed spots along the Parkway. Visitors can learn about traditional Appalachian crafts and even sample some delicious buckwheat pancakes.

Best Time to Go

The Blue Ridge Parkway is best visited in the fall when the foliage turns to vivid shades of red, orange, and yellow. Spring is also a great time to visit, as the wildflowers bloom and the weather is mild.

5. The Desert Loop (Arizona)

Arizona is home to some of the most striking desert landscapes in the world, and the Desert Loop offers a unique way to explore the state’s vast and rugged beauty. This road trip takes you through the heart of the Sonoran Desert, with plenty of stops to marvel at the state’s natural wonders.

Highlights of the Desert Loop

  • Sedona: Known for its stunning red rock formations and vortex sites, Sedona is a must-see destination. The town is also famous for its vibrant arts scene and luxurious resorts, making it a great place to relax and take in the scenery.

  • Monument Valley: This iconic landscape, featured in numerous Western films, is one of Arizona’s most famous natural landmarks. The towering sandstone buttes and mesas set against the desert sky create an unforgettable view.

  • Grand Canyon National Park: No road trip through Arizona would be complete without a stop at the Grand Canyon. This UNESCO World Heritage site is one of the most awe-inspiring natural wonders on Earth. Whether you choose to hike, take a scenic drive, or simply enjoy the view from the rim, the Grand Canyon will leave a lasting impression.

  • Saguaro National Park: End your journey in Tucson, home to the famous saguaro cactus. The park’s desert landscape, dotted with these towering cacti, offers a unique and peaceful setting to reflect on your adventure.

Best Time to Go

The Desert Loop is best visited in the cooler months, typically between October and April. Summer temperatures in Arizona can be extremely high, especially in the desert regions.

Conclusion

Whether you’re drawn to the stunning coastal cliffs of California, the historical charm of Route 66, or the rugged beauty of the American Southwest, these five road trips offer something for every type of traveler. Each route provides its own unique experiences and a chance to explore the diverse landscapes and cultures of the United States.

A road trip is more than just a way to get from one destination to another; it’s an opportunity to immerse yourself in the beauty of the open road and the countless wonders that lie just beyond the horizon. So, buckle up, plan your route, and get ready for an adventure that will stay with you long after you’ve reached your destination. Happy travels!
