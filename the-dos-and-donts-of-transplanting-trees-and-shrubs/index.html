The Dos and Don'ts of Transplanting Trees and Shrubs


Transplanting trees and shrubs can be a rewarding and beneficial practice for your garden or landscape. Whether you are moving a small shrub to a new location or transplanting a large tree, proper technique and care are essential for ensuring the plant’s survival and continued growth. In this guide, we will explore the dos and don’ts of transplanting trees and shrubs to help you achieve success in your gardening endeavors.

The Dos

1. Plan Ahead

Before starting the transplanting process, it’s crucial to plan ahead. Consider the best time of year for the specific plant, the new location’s soil and light conditions, and any potential obstacles such as underground utilities or nearby structures. Proper planning will set the stage for a successful transplant.

2. Prepare the New Site

Prepare the new planting site before digging up the tree or shrub. Ensure that the soil is well-draining and enriched with organic matter. Dig a hole that is wider than the plant’s root ball and of appropriate depth to accommodate the plant’s roots comfortably.

3. Water the Plant

Water the plant thoroughly a day or two before the transplant. Hydrated plants are better equipped to handle the stress of being uprooted and moved to a new location. Adequate moisture will also help hold the soil together during the transplant.

4. Dig Carefully

When digging up the tree or shrub, take care to preserve as much of the root system as possible. Use a sharp spade to cut a circle around the plant, staying at a safe distance from the trunk or main stem. Dig deep enough to capture the majority of the roots without causing damage.

5. Support the Plant

After transplanting, provide support to the plant if necessary. Staking may be needed for larger trees to help them establish their roots in the new location. Be sure to use soft ties to avoid damaging the bark or branches.

6. Mulch and Water

Apply a layer of organic mulch around the base of the transplanted tree or shrub to retain moisture and regulate soil temperature. Water the plant regularly, especially during the first year after transplanting, to promote healthy growth and establishment in its new home.

The Don’ts

1. Transplant During Extreme Weather

Avoid transplanting trees and shrubs during extreme weather conditions, such as periods of intense heat or cold. The added stress of environmental extremes can hinder the plant’s ability to recover from the transplant shock.

2. Neglect Root Pruning

Neglecting to prune the roots before transplanting can lead to root-bound plants that struggle to establish themselves in the new location. Gently pruning the roots will encourage the growth of new feeder roots and promote better nutrient absorption.

3. Rush the Process

Transplanting trees and shrubs is not a task to be rushed. Take the time to prepare the new site, dig carefully, and provide the necessary aftercare. Rushing the process can result in transplant shock and decreased chances of survival for the plant.

4. Forget to Water

After transplanting, it’s essential to maintain proper watering practices. Neglecting to water the plant adequately can lead to stress and dehydration, hindering its ability to adapt to the new environment.

5. Over-fertilize

Avoid over-fertilizing newly transplanted trees and shrubs. Excessive fertilizer can burn the roots and cause further stress to the plant. Instead, focus on providing a balanced and gentle feeding regimen once the plant has acclimated to its new surroundings.

6. Disturb the Root Ball

When transplanting, avoid unnecessary disturbance to the root ball. Rough handling or excessive shaking can damage the delicate root system and impede the plant’s ability to re-establish itself.

Transplanting trees and shrubs is a delicate process that requires careful planning and execution. By following these dos and don’ts, you can increase the likelihood of successful transplantation and ensure the continued health and vitality of your garden or landscape plants.

Remember, each plant species may have specific requirements for transplanting, so it’s essential to research the individual needs of the plants you intend to move. With the right approach and attention to detail, you can enjoy the beauty and benefits of transplanted trees and shrubs in your outdoor space.
