The Best Literary Podcasts You Should Listen to Right Now


If you’re passionate about books, literature, and storytelling, podcasts offer one of the most convenient and enriching ways to dive deeper into the world of words. Whether you’re commuting, exercising, or simply relaxing at home, these literary podcasts provide intimate discussions, author interviews, book recommendations, and explorations of writing craft that can transform your appreciation for literature. Here, we explore some of the very best podcasts that every book lover should have on their playlist.

Why Literary Podcasts?

Podcasts dedicated to literature open a window to diverse viewpoints and genres, connecting listeners with authors, critics, and fellow readers. Unlike static book reviews or printed essays, these audio experiences add personality and immediacy to conversations about books, culture, and the nuances of storytelling. They foster communities of readers while making literature accessible beyond the printed page.

Whether you’re hunting for classic literature discussions, contemporary fiction talks, or deep dives into poetry, memoirs, and literary theory, there’s something available that will satisfy your intellectual curiosity and love for narrative.

1. LeVar Burton Reads

For many, LeVar Burton Reads is a cherished escape into the world of short stories. Hosted by the actor and former “Reading Rainbow” host LeVar Burton, this podcast is a masterclass in storytelling. Each episode features Burton reading a thoughtfully curated short story spanning genres such as speculative fiction, science fiction, fantasy, and literary fiction.

What sets this podcast apart is LeVar’s warm and inviting narration, which breathes life into each narrative, encouraging listeners to savor the emotion and craft behind every story. The selections often spotlight emerging writers and lesser-known gems, making it a treasure trove for discovering new voices.

Who should listen: Fans of short stories, narrative-driven content, and immersive audio storytelling.

2. The New York Times Book Review Podcast

The New York Times Book Review Podcast offers incisive, up-to-date discussions revolving around new book releases and literary trends. Hosted by the magazine’s editors and contributors, this weekly show dives into the books making headlines and literary waves.

What’s compelling about this podcast is its blend of author interviews, book recommendations, and sharp critical analysis. The diversity of genres covered ranges from literary fiction and memoirs to political nonfiction, making it a comprehensive guide to the contemporary literary landscape.

Who should listen: Those seeking informed and timely updates on new books and literary culture.

3. Book Riot - The Podcast

If you’re looking for a lively, witty, and approachable conversation about books, Book Riot nails it. This podcast, featuring hosts from the popular Book Riot website, covers a wide spectrum of book-related topics — from lighthearted book debates and quirky literary news to discussions about diversity in publishing.

The hosts bring a charming, conversational quality that makes everything from writing craft to book club picks feel both entertaining and accessible. They also spotlight underrepresented authors, offering listeners fresh perspectives and recommendations that might otherwise go unnoticed.

Who should listen: Readers who enjoy a casual, fun approach to literature with diverse voices and frequent topical discussions.

4. The Paris Review Podcast

Rooted in the prestigious literary magazine, The Paris Review Podcast offers an elegant and sophisticated dive into the art of writing. Episodes frequently feature readings from authors, interviews with literary figures, and reflections on the craft behind some of the world’s most admired books.

The podcast distinguishes itself with its contemplative tone, deep literary roots, and focus on both classic and contemporary literature. It’s a brilliant way to hear firsthand from groundbreaking authors and immerse oneself in the nuanced art of storytelling.

Who should listen: Literature enthusiasts fascinated by writing craft, author insights, and literary history.

5. What Should I Read Next?

Hosted by bestselling author Anne Bogel, What Should I Read Next? is a gem for anyone feeling overwhelmed by book options or looking for curated, personalized reading advice. Each episode features a guest describing their reading preferences and experiences, which Anne translates into recommendations tailored uniquely for that listener’s taste.

The podcast cleverly balances deep dives into literary preferences with genuine conversation, often uncovering unexpected titles. It’s like having your own personal book concierge guiding you through the complex world of reading.

Who should listen: Readers in search of tailored book recommendations guided by thoughtful dialogue.

6. Literary Friction

With a name evoking thoughtful clash and critical dialogue, Literary Friction is a podcast combining lively debate and interviews with authors and critics. Hosted by the Byron Writers Festival, the podcast journeys through the pressing questions and controversies in contemporary literature.

Discussions touch on diverse topics such as literary politics, book censorship, cultural representation, and the evolving role of the author. Combined with author insights and book recommendations, the show offers a dynamic intellectual engagement with literature’s place in society.

Who should listen: Those who enjoy literary criticism with a socio-political edge and critical debate.

7. The Moth

While not a traditional literary podcast, The Moth deserves mention for its deep connection to storytelling as a craft. Each episode features real people sharing true personal stories live on stage, often told with a raw and emotional honesty that literature aspires to evoke.

The power of The Moth lies in its human connection and narrative immediacy, making it inspiring for writers and readers alike. It reminds listeners that storytelling is not just in books but alive in everyday life.

Who should listen: Story lovers who value authentic, personal narratives and storytelling technique.

8. The History of Literature

For those who want to understand literary works in a broader historical and cultural context, The History of Literature offers a sweeping educational journey through literature’s evolution. Each episode explores works, authors, and movements from classical antiquity into modern times.

The podcast balances scholarly analysis with accessibility, making it perfect for curious readers who want to enhance their appreciation by learning the backstories that shape thematic trends and literary styles throughout periods.

Who should listen: Listeners who appreciate literary history and enjoy context-rich content.

9. Books on the Nightstand

Though it concluded in 2016, Books on the Nightstand remains a beloved podcast in the literary community. Hosted by experienced book enthusiasts, the show offered a rich catalog of book discussions, community engagement, and bookish humor.

Its influence continues, and older episodes remain valuable for discovering a huge range of literary recommendations and thoughtful conversations about reading habits.

Who should listen: Newcomers curious about literary podcast roots and timeless book talk.

10. Between the Covers

Hosted by David Naimon, Between the Covers delivers intimate and in-depth conversations with contemporary authors. The interviews illuminate the creative processes, inspirations, and challenges facing writers today.

This podcast is perfect for those intrigued by what happens behind the scenes of books — how stories come to life, author motivations, and the intricacies of literary careers.

Who should listen: Dedicated readers and aspiring writers seeking artistic insight and author perspectives.

Tips for Making the Most of Literary Podcasts

  1. Create a Listening Schedule: Incorporate podcasts into daily activities — commute, errands, or evening relaxation — to consistently engage with literary content.

  2. Join Online Book Communities: Many podcasts have corresponding social media groups or forums for listener discussions, enhancing connection and discovery.

  3. Take Notes: Jotting down book titles or authors mentioned can help you build your own reading list.

  4. Explore Variety: Mix and match podcasts based on mood or genre interest to maintain freshness and inspiration.

Final Thoughts

Literary podcasts enrich the reading experience by delivering diverse voices, insightful commentary, and storytelling in an intimate audio format. Whether you seek lighthearted book banter, rigorous literary analysis, or profound author interviews, there’s a podcast on this list tailored for you.

By integrating these shows into your routine, you’ll deepen your relationship with books and discover an ever-expanding literary world beyond just the pages of a novel. Ready your headphones for a journey through stories, ideas, and the boundless power of the written word. Happy listening!
