How to Earn Money by Testing Websites and Apps


In an increasingly digital world, businesses are constantly seeking ways to improve user experiences on their websites and applications. One of the most effective ways to achieve this is through user testing. If you have a keen eye for detail and a desire to help improve digital products, you can turn this skill into a source of income by testing websites and apps. This blog post will guide you through the process of earning money by becoming a website and app tester, providing insights into how to get started, the skills you need, and the platforms that can connect you with clients.

What is Website and App Testing?

Website and app testing involves evaluating the usability, functionality, and overall user experience of digital products. As a tester, your job is to identify issues, suggest improvements, and provide feedback on your experience navigating the platform. This could include anything from checking for broken links and slow loading times to assessing the intuitiveness of the interface and the effectiveness of the content.

Businesses use this feedback to make informed decisions about design changes, feature enhancements, and overall user engagement strategies. As a tester, your insights are invaluable, and your contributions can directly influence the success of a product.

Why Test Websites and Apps?

  1. Flexible Work Hours: One of the most appealing aspects of being a website and app tester is the flexibility it offers. You can choose your own hours and work from anywhere, making it an ideal side gig for students, stay-at-home parents, or anyone looking to earn extra income on their terms.

  2. Diverse Projects: Each testing project presents a unique challenge. You might be testing a mobile app for a gaming company one day and a complex e-commerce website the next. This variety keeps the work interesting and allows you to continuously learn about different industries and technologies.

  3. Skill Development: Testing websites and apps can help you develop valuable skills, such as critical thinking, analytical abilities, and effective communication. You will also become more adept at understanding user behavior and the principles of user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) design.

  4. Monetary Rewards: Many companies are willing to pay testers for their time and feedback. Depending on the platform you choose and the complexity of the testing required, you can earn anywhere from $10 to $100 or more per test.

How to Get Started as a Website and App Tester

1. Understand the Requirements

Before diving into website and app testing, it’s essential to understand what is expected of you. Most testing platforms require you to:

  • Have a reliable internet connection
  • Use a computer or mobile device
  • Provide basic demographic information
  • Complete a screener test to assess your capabilities

2. Develop Necessary Skills

While you don’t need to be a tech expert to become a tester, having a foundational understanding of usability principles, web and mobile technologies, and good communication skills will greatly enhance your effectiveness. Consider taking online courses or reading articles about UX design, user research, and usability testing.

3. Create a Professional Profile

Many testing platforms require you to create a profile that showcases your skills, experience, and demographic information. Be honest and thorough while filling out this profile, as it will help match you with relevant projects. Highlight any previous experience in user testing, customer service, or related fields.

4. Sign Up for Testing Platforms

There are several reputable platforms where you can sign up as a website and app tester. Here are a few popular options:

  • UserTesting: One of the leading platforms in user testing, UserTesting connects testers with companies looking for feedback. You will complete tests by recording your screen and voice as you navigate a website or app, providing real-time insights into your experience.

  • TryMyUI: This platform allows you to take tests that involve navigating websites and completing specific tasks. You will provide video and written feedback, and you can earn money for each completed test.

  • Userlytics: Userlytics offers usability testing for websites, mobile apps, and prototypes. As a tester, you’ll complete tasks and submit feedback while being recorded. You can get paid per test completed.

  • Testbirds: This European platform provides opportunities for testers to evaluate websites and apps while earning money. You can participate in both remote usability tests and in-field testing.

  • Validately: Validately focuses on user research and testing, allowing you to interact with real users and gather valuable feedback. You can earn money by participating in both live and recorded tests.

5. Prepare for Testing Sessions

Once you’ve signed up for a platform, it’s time to prepare for your first testing session. Here are some tips to ensure a successful testing experience:

  • Read Instructions Carefully: Before starting a test, make sure you understand the objectives and tasks you need to complete. This will help you provide relevant feedback.

  • Be Honest and Constructive: Your feedback should be honest and constructive. Highlight both positive aspects and areas for improvement.

  • Think Aloud: Many testing platforms will ask you to think aloud as you navigate the website or app. This means verbalizing your thoughts, feelings, and reactions. This feedback is invaluable for developers.

  • Take Your Time: Don’t rush through the test. Take your time to explore the site or app thoroughly, and ensure you provide comprehensive feedback.

6. Submit Feedback and Get Paid

After completing a test, you will usually need to submit your feedback through the platform’s interface. This may involve answering specific questions or providing a summary of your experience. Once your feedback is reviewed, you will receive payment, which can typically be transferred to your bank account or through platforms like PayPal.

Tips for Maximizing Your Earnings

1. Test Regularly

The more tests you complete, the more money you can earn. Set aside dedicated time each week to focus on testing opportunities. This will help you establish a routine and increase your overall earnings.

2. Diversify Your Platforms

Don’t limit yourself to just one testing platform. Sign up for multiple services to increase the number of available projects. This diversification will help you find more consistent work and expand your testing experience.

3. Build a Reputation

Many platforms allow testers to build a reputation based on the quality of their feedback. Aim to provide high-quality, thoughtful feedback to earn positive ratings. This will increase your chances of being selected for more lucrative projects in the future.

4. Stay Updated on Trends

The digital landscape is ever-evolving. Keeping abreast of the latest trends in web and app development, UX design, and usability testing will make you a more effective tester. Consider subscribing to relevant blogs, attending webinars, and participating in online communities.

5. Network with Other Testers

Join online forums and social media groups for testers to share experiences, tips, and job opportunities. Networking can help you learn from others and discover new platforms or testing gigs.

Conclusion

Earning money by testing websites and apps is a flexible and rewarding endeavor that allows you to contribute to the digital landscape while enhancing your skills and income. With the right approach, you can turn this opportunity into a lucrative side hustle or even a full-time job. By understanding the requirements, developing your skills, and actively seeking out testing opportunities, you can successfully navigate the world of user testing and make a meaningful impact on the products you evaluate. Start your journey today, and watch as your feedback shapes the future of digital experiences.
