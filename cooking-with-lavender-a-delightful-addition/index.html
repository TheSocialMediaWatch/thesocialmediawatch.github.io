Cooking with Lavender: A Delightful Addition


Imagine a sunny kitchen, the smell of freshly baked cookies wafts through the air, and you breathe in deeply, noticing a hint of floral scent that gently dances with the sweetness of the butter. This aroma doesn’t come from an exotic flower shipped from around the world but from the kitchen pantry staple - lavender. Now, you may have come across lavender in soaps, perfumes, or sachets but have you explored its culinary applications? If not, let me take you on a delightful journey into the world of cooking with lavender.

Lavender is More Than Just a Fragrance

Derived from the Latin word ’lavare,’ meaning ’to wash,’ lavender represents cleanliness and calmness. The purple blossoms of the lavender plant, scientifically known as Lavandula, are edible and come in three primary varieties - lavandin, English lavender, and Spanish lavender. Each type carries a slightly different flavor profile, and their subtle flowery taste adds an air of refinement to the food you prepare. The fragrant, subtly sweet, and herbaceous essence of lavender, when incorporated into a dish, casts a sense of tranquility and brings a heightened quality to everyday cooking.

Delving into Lavender-Cooking History

Cooking with lavender is not solely a modern culinary trend. In fact, it dates back to Roman and Greek times when lavender was prized for its protective properties. Lavender was often added to dishes to prevent food spoilage and, as time passed, it became integrating into European cuisine. Over the centuries, lavender recipes have been handed down through generations with many households possessing cherished family recipes that incorporate this fragrance into everything from savory dishes to sweet confections.

Savory or Sweet, Lavender Improves the Palate

Using lavender in savory dishes can add an underlying bouquet that subtly enhances the overall flavor profile of a meal. You can incorporate the fragrant herb into marinades, dressings, and even rubs for meat. A touch of lavender added to roasted vegetables or in a cheese-based pasta dish, such as Fettuccine Alfredo, gently elevates the flavors with an aromatic nuance. For savory soups and stews, lavender proves to be an excellent addition in small quantities, lending a unique and comforting depth to the dish.

Transitioning to the sweet side, lavender lightens and complements the sweetness of desserts. Lavender and sugar pair delicately, making baked goods, like cookies or scones, simply irresistible. Infusing honey or syrup with lavender blossoms lends an added layer of fragrance to sweeten ice tea, polenta or pancakes. While adding lavender to your dessert creations, it is essential to remember that less is indeed more; too much lavender can overwhelm and provide an undesirable taste.

A Floral Twist to Your Beverages

Believe it or not, beverages also undergo a refreshing transformation when infused with lavender. An iced lavender-lemonade, with fresh mint, offers an aromatic twist on a classic beverage, resulting in a fizzy and slightly floral refreshment. Recipes requiring a lavender twist may also suggest adding it to Homemade Herbal Soda, creating a delightful and bubbly concoction. Alternatively, a floral note on your morning coffee or steeping lavender in a hot cup of Earl Grey tea provides a calming aroma that can be felt with every sip.

Selecting and Preparing Lavender for Cooking

When cooking with lavender, it is vital to consider the quality of lavender you’re using. It is best to select edible culinary-grade lavender, as the non-culinary varieties could cause digestive upsets due to a camphor-like taste. Lavender should also be fresh and dry. To use lavender in cooking, carefully harvest and dry the flowers, and store them in an airtight container until they’re ready for use. With a toasted nutty flavor, lavender, fortunately, doesn’t lose its aromatic constituents until exposed to high heat. Subsequently, you can either add lavender to the recipe at the beginning of the cooking process or use it to infuse oils or sugars that can be saved for future use.

A Safe Haven for Cooking with Lavender

Lastly, Lavender is a gentle herb that doesn’t overtake the foundation of the dish. Like a silent supporter, it quietly celebrates the flavors of every recipe. Nevertheless, when trying a new herb like lavender, newbies may wonder if it’s suitable for consumption. The great thing about lavender is its inclination towards safety. Most people, even those with sensitive systems, can indulge in the comforts of lavender-based dishes without fear of adverse reactions. Just as the soothing aroma of lavender comforts our senses, prepared with care, it binds our taste buds in a serene culinary experience.

In pursuing culinary adventures with lavender, the possibilities are vast and delicious. With a subtle profile and a gentle floral kiss, lavender makes everyday food memorable, providing a delightful alternative or twist to traditional recipes. With the depth of aroma in your hands, lavender can transform a standard dish into one that lingers in memory and desire. So this time when beeswax wraps, lavender sachets, and scented soaps aren’t enough, step into the kitchen and let lavender’s magic caress your taste buds as you discover the world of cooking with lavender.
