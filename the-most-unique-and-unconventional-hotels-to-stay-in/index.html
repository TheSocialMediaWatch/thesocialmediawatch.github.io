The Most Unique and Unconventional Hotels to Stay In


In the world of travel, traditional hotels offer comfort and convenience, but for those seeking something out of the ordinary, there’s an entire universe of unconventional hotels that can provide an experience far beyond the typical stay. From floating hotels on the water to treehouses perched high in the canopy, these unconventional accommodations offer travelers an unforgettable way to sleep under the stars—or in some cases, above the clouds.

Let’s explore some of the most unique and eccentric hotels in the world, where the stay is as much about the experience as it is about the place itself.

1. The Mirrorcube Hotel – Sweden

Nestled in the heart of the Swedish wilderness, the Mirrorcube Hotel is a perfect example of blending nature with modern design. The hotel is literally a cube-shaped cabin suspended in the trees, covered with one-way mirrored glass that reflects the surrounding forest. This stunning visual effect makes the structure nearly invisible from the outside, creating an intimate connection with nature.

Inside, guests find a sleek, minimalist design featuring floor-to-ceiling windows offering panoramic views of the forest below. The entire experience is designed to immerse you in the serene beauty of the Swedish wilderness while offering the ultimate in comfort. For nature lovers and those seeking solitude, the Mirrorcube provides a unique way to stay among the trees without disturbing the environment.

2. Icehotel – Sweden

Sweden is home to not just one, but several unique hotels, and the Icehotel in Jukkasjärvi is a standout. Built entirely from ice and snow, this iconic hotel is reconstructed every year, making each season’s design unique. The walls, furniture, and even the chandeliers are sculpted from ice, and guests sleep in rooms where the temperature never rises above freezing.

While the cold may sound daunting, the Icehotel provides thermal sleeping bags and fur-lined beds to ensure comfort. Guests can enjoy an ice-cold cocktail at the ice bar or even take part in ice sculpting workshops. If you’re looking for an unforgettable experience—where you can enjoy the art and beauty of ice while staying in an environment that’s completely one-of-a-kind—the Icehotel offers just that.

3. The Dog Bark Park Inn – USA

For animal lovers, the Dog Bark Park Inn in Cottonwood, Idaho, is a quirky and charming place to stay. This hotel is housed inside a giant beagle-shaped building, and it’s one of the most unusual places you’ll find in the United States. Guests stay in rooms located in the dog’s belly, surrounded by wooden carvings of dogs in every corner.

The Inn is part of a dog-themed park that includes an art gallery with wooden dog carvings, which guests can also purchase. The owners, Dennis and Frances, are passionate about their craft, and the whole place feels like a celebration of all things dog. For those who love whimsy and dogs, this hotel offers a fun and memorable way to spend the night.

4. The Giraffe Manor – Kenya

A stay at the Giraffe Manor in Nairobi, Kenya, offers something truly extraordinary—an encounter with giraffes. Located on a private sanctuary, this charming manor is home to a herd of endangered Rothschild giraffes, who are known to stick their long necks through the windows to say hello to guests. The giraffes are friendly and love to interact with visitors, making this one of the most unique and personal hotel experiences.

The manor itself exudes old-world charm with its colonial-style architecture, elegant rooms, and beautiful gardens. Guests can enjoy gourmet meals while being serenaded by the giraffes, who roam freely throughout the estate. Staying here not only offers luxury and tranquility, but also the rare opportunity to connect with these majestic animals in an intimate setting.

5. Propeller Island City Lodge – Germany

Berlin is known for its creative culture, and the Propeller Island City Lodge is a reflection of that spirit. This unconventional hotel features 30 rooms, each one completely unique, and some of them are downright bizarre. For example, one room features a bed suspended from the ceiling, while another is filled with mirrors and features a floating table.

The hotel was designed by artist Lars Stroschen, who wanted to challenge traditional hotel concepts and create a space that encouraged guests to think differently about their surroundings. With its artistic and avant-garde vibe, the Propeller Island City Lodge is ideal for travelers seeking a hotel that pushes boundaries.

6. The Hobbit House – New Zealand

If you’ve ever wanted to live like a hobbit, the Hobbit House in New Zealand offers an experience that’s straight out of a fantasy novel. This charming, hobbit-style accommodation is built into the side of a hill, complete with round doors and windows, grassy roofs, and cozy interiors. Located in the picturesque countryside, the Hobbit House offers stunning views of the surrounding landscape and a peaceful retreat into nature.

Inside, the décor is warm and inviting, with rustic wooden furniture, handmade crafts, and plenty of cozy touches. This is the perfect place for fans of J.R.R. Tolkien’s work or anyone looking for a whimsical getaway in the heart of New Zealand’s natural beauty.

7. The Whitepod Eco Resort – Switzerland

For eco-conscious travelers, the Whitepod Eco Resort in the Swiss Alps offers an innovative and environmentally sustainable way to stay. This resort features a series of geodesic dome pods that are designed to blend into the landscape and minimize their environmental impact. Each pod is equipped with luxurious amenities and offers stunning views of the snow-capped mountains.

The resort is entirely self-sufficient, relying on solar energy and other sustainable practices to ensure that the natural surroundings are preserved. Guests can enjoy activities such as skiing, hiking, and wildlife watching while staying in a setting that’s both luxurious and eco-friendly. If you’re looking to get closer to nature without compromising on comfort, the Whitepod Eco Resort is the perfect place.

8. The Underwater Room – Zanzibar

For an experience unlike any other, The Underwater Room in Zanzibar offers the opportunity to stay beneath the surface of the Indian Ocean. This one-of-a-kind hotel room is located at the Manta Resort and is entirely submerged, with panoramic windows offering views of the surrounding marine life. Guests can watch fish and other sea creatures swim by while enjoying the comforts of a luxury suite.

The Underwater Room is the epitome of a “living in the moment” experience, allowing guests to connect with the ocean in a way that few people ever will. The resort offers a range of activities, from snorkeling and diving to kayaking, but it’s the night-time experience of sleeping under the sea that truly makes this hotel a bucket-list destination.

9. The Caves – Jamaica

Located on the cliffs of Negril, Jamaica, The Caves hotel offers an incredibly unique stay, with rooms carved directly into the limestone rock. The hotel’s 12 cottages and suites offer breathtaking views of the Caribbean Sea, and many of the rooms feature private terraces that overlook the water.

One of the most unique aspects of this hotel is its proximity to the sea. Some rooms have direct access to caves where guests can swim and explore. The hotel also features a private cave bar, where you can sip on tropical cocktails while listening to the sound of the waves crashing against the shore. For those seeking a blend of adventure, romance, and stunning natural beauty, The Caves offers a truly unforgettable stay.

10. Airstream Trailer Hotel – USA

For travelers seeking nostalgia and adventure, the Airstream Trailer Hotel in various locations across the United States offers a quirky and retro way to experience the open road. These vintage Airstream trailers are parked in scenic locations, such as the desert or the beach, and they provide a unique blend of old-school Americana and modern comfort.

Inside, the trailers are equipped with all the amenities needed for a comfortable stay, including air conditioning, modern bathrooms, and full kitchens. Staying in one of these iconic trailers offers a taste of vintage travel combined with the modern luxuries that make for a cozy and unforgettable experience. Whether you’re in California or New York, the Airstream Trailer Hotel promises a nostalgic trip back in time with all the comfort of today.

11. Fogo Island Inn – Canada

Situated off the coast of Newfoundland in Canada, Fogo Island Inn is a stunning example of how architecture and design can transform the experience of staying in a remote location. This modern, minimalist hotel is perched on stilts above the rugged coastline, offering sweeping views of the Atlantic Ocean.

The building itself is a work of art, featuring floor-to-ceiling windows, wood-panelled interiors, and a design that blends seamlessly with the island’s natural surroundings. Fogo Island is also known for its rich cultural heritage, and the inn’s community-based programs help support local artists and craftspeople. For those looking to explore both natural beauty and local culture in an environment of unparalleled luxury, Fogo Island Inn is an ideal destination.

12. Kakslauttanen Arctic Resort – Finland

If you’ve ever dreamt of sleeping under the Northern Lights, Kakslauttanen Arctic Resort in Finland offers a magical opportunity to do so. This unique resort features glass igloos that provide unobstructed views of the night sky, perfect for viewing the Aurora Borealis in winter.

In addition to the glass igloos, the resort offers traditional log cabins and snow igloos, allowing guests to enjoy the beauty of the Arctic Circle in whatever style they prefer. With activities like dog sledding, reindeer safaris, and snowmobiling available, Kakslauttanen is a perfect destination for those looking to experience the Arctic in a way that’s both thrilling and cozy.

Conclusion

Staying at one of these unconventional hotels is more than just a place to sleep—it’s an experience that transforms your entire trip. Whether you’re sleeping beneath the stars in a treehouse, surrounded by art in an avant-garde hotel, or floating on the ocean in an underwater room, these unique hotels are the perfect way to turn your vacation into an unforgettable adventure. Each one offers its own story, setting, and experiences, giving you the opportunity to make memories that last a lifetime.

Next time you plan your getaway, consider staying in one of these extraordinary hotels, where the journey itself is just as exciting as the destination.
