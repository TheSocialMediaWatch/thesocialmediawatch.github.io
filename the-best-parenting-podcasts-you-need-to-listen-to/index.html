The Best Parenting Podcasts You Need to Listen to


Parenting is one of life’s greatest challenges, but it can also be one of the most rewarding experiences. Whether you’re navigating the early years of childhood, tackling the teenage years, or trying to balance your personal life with being a parent, there’s no one-size-fits-all guide to raising kids. However, there’s a wealth of wisdom, advice, and support from parenting podcasts that can make your journey a little easier.

In today’s fast-paced world, podcasts have become a go-to resource for busy parents who need reliable advice, uplifting stories, and expert insights. With so many parenting podcasts available, it can be hard to know where to start. To help you out, we’ve compiled a list of the best parenting podcasts that offer valuable lessons, helpful tips, and relatable experiences.

1. The Longest Shortest Time

If you’re a new parent or about to become one, The Longest Shortest Time is the perfect podcast to start with. Hosted by Hillary Frank, this podcast covers a range of topics from pregnancy and newborn care to the ups and downs of early childhood. With a blend of humor, heartfelt stories, and expert advice, it provides support and reassurance during those sleep-deprived early years.

Episodes tackle everything from baby sleep techniques to the emotional toll of parenting. The beauty of this podcast lies in its diversity, as it often features guest experts, parents from all walks of life, and a variety of perspectives on the challenges of raising children. For any new parent, this podcast is a comforting and informative resource.

2. The Mom Hour

Hosted by Meagan Francis and Sarah Powers, The Mom Hour is a podcast designed for moms at all stages of motherhood. Whether you’re a stay-at-home mom or balancing work and parenting, the hosts cover everything from time management and self-care to emotional well-being and motherhood humor.

What makes The Mom Hour stand out is its focus on real, relatable conversations. The hosts share their own parenting stories, both the good and the bad, and provide practical tips for navigating everything from potty training to teenage rebellion. If you need a mix of lighthearted banter and actionable advice, this podcast is a great choice.

3. Parenting On Purpose

For parents looking to connect deeper with their children, Parenting On Purpose is an excellent podcast to explore. Hosted by Dr. Kim and Brad, this podcast emphasizes intentional parenting and offers a faith-based approach to raising children. It provides listeners with practical tools to nurture their child’s emotional, mental, and spiritual growth.

This podcast isn’t just about discipline or managing behavior; it’s about fostering strong, meaningful connections with your kids. The hosts talk about everything from how to create a positive home environment to strategies for developing empathy and strong communication skills. Parenting On Purpose is ideal for parents who want to align their parenting approach with their values and create a loving, supportive environment for their children.

4. The Struggle Bus

The Struggle Bus is an insightful, hilarious podcast hosted by Katharine Heller and Sally Tamarkin. While not exclusively focused on parenting, this podcast covers a wide variety of topics that are incredibly relatable for parents. The hosts take listener questions about life, relationships, and parenting, offering candid advice with humor and empathy.

Whether you’re dealing with work-life balance, managing stress, or just need a good laugh, The Struggle Bus is a great reminder that everyone is struggling in one way or another. It’s a lighthearted yet supportive listen for parents who want to feel understood and not alone in their challenges.

5. How To Talk To Kids About Anything

If you’re constantly searching for better ways to communicate with your kids, How To Talk To Kids About Anything is the podcast for you. Hosted by Dr. Robyn Silverman, a child development expert, this show gives parents practical tools for talking to kids about a range of topics—from school stress and friendships to emotional intelligence and mental health.

Dr. Silverman brings a wealth of knowledge to the table, breaking down complex concepts into bite-sized, actionable advice that’s easy to implement. The show also includes interviews with experts in child psychology, education, and mental health, making it an invaluable resource for any parent looking to improve their communication skills with their children.

6. Raising Good Humans

In today’s fast-paced world, raising kind, empathetic children is more important than ever. Raising Good Humans, hosted by Dr. Aliza Pressman, a child development expert, is all about raising emotionally intelligent kids. The podcast covers a wide range of topics such as positive discipline, self-regulation, and creating a culture of respect in your home.

One of the standout features of this podcast is Dr. Pressman’s ability to make child development research accessible and applicable to everyday parenting situations. Each episode provides practical strategies for fostering positive behaviors, managing challenging emotions, and promoting kindness in your children. If you’re focused on helping your kids develop a strong moral compass, this podcast will provide you with the tools to do so.

7. The Parenting Junkie

The Parenting Junkie, hosted by Avital Schreiber-Levy, focuses on mindful and peaceful parenting. If you’re looking to cultivate a home environment that is calm, nurturing, and free from the usual stress and chaos that often accompanies raising children, this podcast will be a game-changer.

Schreiber-Levy dives into topics like gentle discipline, the benefits of unschooling, and the importance of connection over correction. The podcast encourages parents to embrace a more mindful, intentional approach to parenting, focusing on building a peaceful relationship with your children while still maintaining healthy boundaries. For those interested in a more holistic, slow-paced approach to raising kids, The Parenting Junkie is a must-listen.

8. The Playful Parenting Podcast

As parents, we often hear about the importance of play in a child’s development, but The Playful Parenting Podcast takes this concept to a deeper level. Hosted by Larry Cohen, a psychologist and author of Playful Parenting, this podcast is dedicated to helping parents understand the importance of play and how to incorporate it into everyday interactions with their children.

Each episode focuses on how to use play to strengthen the parent-child bond, promote emotional development, and address behavioral issues. Whether you’re dealing with a toddler’s tantrums or trying to manage your child’s emotions as they grow older, The Playful Parenting Podcast provides creative solutions rooted in play and connection. If you’re looking for a fun, light-hearted approach to discipline and development, this podcast is a perfect fit.

9. The Happy Mama Movement

Motherhood can be overwhelming, and it’s easy to lose sight of your own well-being while taking care of your family. The Happy Mama Movement, hosted by Anna Seewald, is dedicated to helping moms prioritize their mental health and find joy in the chaos of parenting. The podcast combines self-care, parenting advice, and personal stories to help moms cultivate happiness and resilience.

Seewald’s approach focuses on the importance of self-compassion, mindfulness, and positive mindset. By listening to this podcast, you’ll gain tools to help you manage stress, avoid burnout, and rediscover your sense of self amidst the demands of parenting. It’s an empowering resource for mothers looking to maintain a healthy work-life balance and take care of their own well-being.

10. The Dad Edge Podcast

Parenting isn’t just a mother’s job, and The Dad Edge Podcast is an excellent resource for fathers looking for support and guidance. Hosted by Larry Hagner, this podcast focuses on helping dads become better partners, fathers, and individuals. It provides a mix of expert interviews, personal stories, and practical advice on topics such as parenting, marriage, health, and leadership.

With episodes ranging from discussions on effective communication to tips on how to handle work-life balance, The Dad Edge Podcast encourages fathers to lead by example and take an active role in their families. It’s an invaluable resource for fathers who want to strengthen their relationships with their children while also focusing on personal growth.

11. The Child Repair Guide

If you’re interested in understanding your child’s behaviors and finding solutions to common parenting challenges, The Child Repair Guide hosted by Dr. Steve Silvestro offers expert advice on everything from childhood anxiety to discipline strategies. With a focus on child psychology and practical advice for parents, this podcast aims to empower parents with the knowledge they need to address a wide range of behavioral issues.

Dr. Silvestro’s experience as a pediatrician makes this podcast unique, as he approaches parenting from both a medical and psychological perspective. He provides evidence-based strategies and expert guidance to help parents navigate challenging behaviors, health concerns, and emotional development.

12. Unruffled

If you want to learn more about gentle parenting, Unruffled is an essential podcast. Hosted by Janet Lansbury, a well-known advocate of respectful parenting, this podcast offers advice based on the principles of RIE (Resources for Infant Educarers). Lansbury discusses how to respect your child’s autonomy, the importance of boundaries, and how to address difficult behaviors without resorting to punishment.

The episodes dive deep into topics such as tantrums, sibling rivalry, and how to support your child’s emotional growth in a way that promotes their independence and self-esteem. For parents looking to create a peaceful and empathetic home environment, Unruffled is an excellent resource.

Conclusion

Parenting is a journey full of challenges and rewards, and sometimes, all you need is a little guidance and support along the way. These parenting podcasts provide a wealth of knowledge, expert advice, and relatable stories that can help you navigate the ups and downs of raising children. Whether you’re looking for practical tips on discipline, communication strategies, or simply a dose of encouragement, these podcasts have something for every parent.

So grab your headphones, press play, and let these experts help you become the best version of yourself as a parent.
