DIY Camping Gear: Creating Your Own Accessories for a Truly Custom Experience


When you think of camping, what comes to mind? Tents, a roaring campfire, s’mores, and the great outdoors? While these quintessential camping elements are undoubtedly part of the experience, undoubtedly, they are often accompanied by camping accessories that can make or break your camping trip.

An overpriced, low-quality accessory can easily put a damper on an otherwise enchanting escapade. In many cases, though, there’s a solution: creating your camp gear. Not only does this empower you to build a more customized camping experience, but it also prevents you from having to spend exorbitantly on expensive camping accessories. Here are a few ideas to get you started:

  1. Customized Sleeping Bags

Sleeping bags can be costly, but at the same time, it could be uncomfortable to sleep on the ground. Your answer? Make your own!

First, gather some warm fabric material. Wool, fleece, or even materials often found at your local thrift store can work. Window sheets provide a cheap and insulating option!

Next, consider a few seam-free sleeping bag patterns you can follow easily to create your personalized sleeping bag. Nobody wants unwanted zippers digging into their back while sleeping outside.

  1. Portable Camping Stands

Tables, benches, and stands can make a huge difference on a camping trip. Instead of forking over a fortune for a portable table, consider this DIY alternative.

Start your project by scouting out folding wooden stands from a low-cost source. You can even use wooden pallets found free in various places. Don’t forget nails or screws and some time to assemble your custom camping table!

  1. Innovative Containers for Camping

Containers definitely come in handy during camping, especially for storing food or keeping gear dry. Rather than investing heavily in premade containers, explore plastic bins and pallets.

Transforming wooden pallets can create astounding camping containers. Sand your pallets down and provide gripping surfaces using strong contact paper or linoleum. Your custom, waterproof, and durable pallet-container is just around the corner.

  1. Custom-Artized Lanterns

Food Illuminate your camp with unique lanterns that can keep you company in times of darkness. You can use simple 5-gallon buckets with a gallon cut-out for the opening.

Use plastic VHO Q60 or 90 LED bulbs to illuminate your camp. But be aware, waterproof lanterns in any DIY gear are vital. Get and waterproof your DIY lantern before the trip!

  1. DIY Hammocks

Consider camping in trees using hammocks, particularly during hot weather. Investing in a hammock can be costly. However, an affordable solution lies in the construction of your own.

You need suspension materials strategically placed to provide support for your hammock. Experiment with rope, paracord, or tree straps for extending suspension and ensuring an excellent hammock experience.

  1. Customized Cooking Utensils

Turn cheap wooden spoons, spatulas, and stirrers into unique cooking tools. Sand pleasantly and paint with non-reactive, heat-resistant colors. Customize your utensils with your initials or favorite camping trip memories!

Conclusion

DIY camping gear provides you with numerous advantages. It initiates the creative aspect in you, enhances your camping experiences, and reduces costs vastly. Incorporating these six DIY tips in your next adventure can lead to the most memorable journey that’s truly uniquely customized. Happy camping!
