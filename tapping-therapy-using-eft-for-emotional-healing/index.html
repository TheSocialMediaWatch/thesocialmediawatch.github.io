Tapping Therapy: Using EFT for Emotional Healing


Emotional healing is a crucial aspect of our overall well-being. While traditional therapy methods have proven effective for many individuals, alternative approaches like Tapping Therapy provide a unique and powerful technique for addressing emotional challenges. In this blog post, we will explore the concept of Tapping Therapy, also known as Emotional Freedom Techniques (EFT), and how it can be used for emotional healing.

What is Tapping Therapy?

Tapping Therapy is a holistic healing modality that combines elements of psychology, traditional Chinese medicine, and energy healing. It involves the gentle tapping of specific acupressure points on the body while focusing on specific emotional issues or physical symptoms. This tapping process is believed to rebalance the body’s energy system, thereby alleviating emotional distress and promoting overall well-being.

How does Tapping Therapy work?

Tapping Therapy is based on the idea that negative emotions and physical discomfort are caused by disruptions in the body’s energy system. By tapping on specific acupressure points, Tapping Therapy aims to restore the flow of energy and release emotional blockages.

The process of Tapping Therapy typically involves the following steps:

  1. Identifying the issue: The first step in Tapping Therapy is to identify the specific emotional issue or physical symptom you want to address. It could be anything from stress and anxiety to chronic pain or trauma.

  2. Rate the intensity: Once you have identified the issue, you rate its intensity on a scale of 0 to 10, with 10 being the most intense. This helps you gauge the effectiveness of the technique as you progress.

  3. Setup statement: As you begin tapping, you repeat a setup statement that acknowledges the issue and affirms self-acceptance. For example, “Even though I feel anxious, I deeply and completely love and accept myself.”

  4. Tapping sequence: Next, you tap on specific acupressure points on the body while repeating a reminder phrase that acknowledges the issue. The acupressure points used in Tapping Therapy include the top of the head, eyebrow, side of the eye, under the eye, under the nose, chin, collarbone, under the arm, and the side of the hand.

  5. Reassess the intensity: After completing a round of tapping, you reassess the intensity of the issue. Ideally, the intensity should decrease. If not, you repeat the process, adjusting the setup statement and reminder phrase as needed.

  6. Repeat until resolved: You continue tapping and reassessing until the intensity of the issue reaches a low or zero level. This indicates that the emotional distress has been effectively addressed.

Benefits of Tapping Therapy

Tapping Therapy has gained popularity for its ability to provide relief from a wide range of emotional issues and physical symptoms. Some of the key benefits of Tapping Therapy include:

  1. Stress and anxiety reduction: Tapping Therapy can help alleviate stress and anxiety by releasing emotional blockages and promoting a sense of calm and relaxation.

  2. Pain management: Many individuals have found Tapping Therapy effective in managing chronic pain and reducing physical discomfort.

  3. Emotional healing: Tapping Therapy can support emotional healing by addressing past traumas, negative beliefs, and limiting patterns of behavior.

  4. Improved self-esteem: By addressing underlying emotional issues and promoting self-acceptance, Tapping Therapy can boost self-esteem and enhance overall well-being.

  5. Enhanced performance: Tapping Therapy has been used by athletes, performers, and professionals to overcome performance anxiety and enhance focus and concentration.

Is Tapping Therapy right for you?

While Tapping Therapy has shown promising results for many individuals, it is important to remember that it may not be suitable for everyone. If you have any underlying medical conditions or concerns, it is recommended to consult with a qualified healthcare professional before incorporating Tapping Therapy into your wellness routine.

In conclusion, Tapping Therapy, also known as Emotional Freedom Techniques (EFT), offers a unique and effective approach to emotional healing. By combining elements of psychology, traditional Chinese medicine, and energy healing, Tapping Therapy aims to rebalance the body’s energy system and alleviate emotional distress. Whether you are dealing with stress, anxiety, chronic pain, or past traumas, Tapping Therapy may provide the relief and healing you seek. Give it a try and experience the transformative power of this gentle and empowering technique.
