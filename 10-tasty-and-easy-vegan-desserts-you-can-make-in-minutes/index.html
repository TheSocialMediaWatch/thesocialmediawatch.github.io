10 Tasty and Easy Vegan Desserts You Can Make in Minutes


If you’re craving something sweet but want to stick to a plant-based lifestyle, look no further! Vegan desserts can be just as delicious and indulgent as traditional ones, without using any animal-derived ingredients. Whether you’re new to veganism or simply looking to add more plant-based sweets to your diet, here are 10 mouth-watering, easy-to-make vegan desserts you can prepare in no time.

1. Chocolate Avocado Mousse

For a rich and creamy dessert that’s packed with healthy fats, try making chocolate avocado mousse. This dessert is incredibly smooth and decadent, yet surprisingly simple to prepare. All you need are ripe avocados, cocoa powder, maple syrup, and a bit of vanilla extract. Blend the ingredients together until smooth, and chill for about 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. The result is a luscious, chocolatey treat that’s perfect for any occasion.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 2 tbsp maple syrup (or your preferred sweetener)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • A pinch of sea salt

Directions:

  1. Scoop out the flesh of the avocados and place them in a food processor.
  2. Add cocoa powder, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and sea salt.
  3. Process until smooth and creamy.
  4. Chill in the fridge for at least 10 minutes before serving.
  5. Serve topped with fresh berries or a sprinkle of cocoa powder.

2. Vegan Peanut Butter Cookies

If you’re a fan of peanut butter, these vegan peanut butter cookies will hit the spot! They’re chewy, sweet, and only require a few ingredients. The best part is that you can whip them up in under 15 minutes. The key to these cookies’ texture is the perfect ratio of peanut butter and flour, which results in a satisfying bite. You don’t need eggs or dairy, and they’re absolutely delicious!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup peanut butter (creamy or chunky)
  • 1/2 cup maple syrup or agave nectar
  • 1/2 cup oat flour or whole wheat flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • A pinch of salt

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix together peanut butter, maple syrup, oat flour, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Roll the dough into small balls and place them on a baking sheet.
  4. Flatten each cookie with a fork, creating a crisscross pattern.
  5. Bake for 10 minutes or until golden brown.
  6. Let them cool on a wire rack before enjoying.

3. No-Bake Chocolate Coconut Bars

These no-bake chocolate coconut bars are the ultimate easy dessert. The combination of chocolate and coconut is irresistibly sweet and satisfying. You don’t need to turn on the oven for this treat; just melt the chocolate and mix it with shredded coconut for a quick dessert that will wow your guests.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup shredded coconut
  • 1/2 cup almond butter or peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips

Directions:

  1. In a bowl, mix shredded coconut, almond butter, maple syrup, and vanilla extract.
  2. Line a baking pan with parchment paper and press the mixture into the pan evenly.
  3. Melt the dark chocolate chips in a microwave-safe bowl and pour it over the coconut mixture.
  4. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to set.
  5. Cut into bars and enjoy!

4. Vegan Banana Ice Cream

Banana ice cream is a game-changer for anyone craving a cold, creamy dessert. This simple dessert requires just one ingredient – ripe bananas. The bananas get blended into a smooth, ice cream-like texture when frozen, making them the perfect healthy alternative to traditional ice cream. You can customize it with add-ins like berries, chocolate chips, or a drizzle of peanut butter for extra flavor.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe bananas

Directions:

  1. Peel and slice the bananas into small pieces.
  2. Freeze the banana slices for at least 2 hours.
  3. Once frozen, blend the banana slices in a food processor until they become smooth and creamy.
  4. Serve immediately for soft-serve consistency or freeze for a firmer texture.
  5. Top with your favorite toppings like chopped nuts or fruit.

5. Vegan Chia Pudding

Chia pudding is a simple, nutritious dessert that’s perfect for busy days when you want something sweet but don’t have much time to prepare. Chia seeds absorb liquid and create a creamy, pudding-like texture when left to sit for a few hours. This vegan dessert can be made ahead of time and topped with fresh fruit, nuts, or granola for a more decadent treat.

Ingredients:

  • 3 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 cup almond milk (or any plant-based milk)
  • 1 tbsp maple syrup
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Fresh fruit for topping

Directions:

  1. In a jar or bowl, combine chia seeds, almond milk, maple syrup, and vanilla extract.
  2. Stir well and cover the container.
  3. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
  4. Top with fresh fruit, granola, or shredded coconut before serving.

6. Vegan Lemon Bars

These vegan lemon bars are zesty, tangy, and absolutely refreshing. The lemon flavor shines through beautifully in the filling, while the crust is crumbly and just sweet enough to balance out the tartness. Perfect for a summer picnic or a casual snack, these bars are quick and easy to make, with minimal ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut flour
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 tsp lemon zest
  • 1/2 cup coconut oil
  • 1/4 tsp turmeric (for color)

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Mix almond flour, coconut flour, maple syrup, and melted coconut oil in a bowl to form the crust dough.
  3. Press the dough into the bottom of a greased baking pan.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk together lemon juice, lemon zest, maple syrup, and turmeric to form the filling.
  5. Pour the filling over the crust and bake for 25-30 minutes until the edges are golden.
  6. Let cool and cut into squares before serving.

7. Vegan Apple Crumble

Apple crumble is a classic dessert that’s naturally vegan when made with plant-based butter. The combination of baked apples and a crumbly topping makes for a comforting dessert that you can enjoy year-round. You can even add a scoop of vegan vanilla ice cream to make it extra indulgent.

Ingredients:

  • 4 apples, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/4 cup coconut sugar
  • 1/4 cup plant-based butter
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. Arrange the sliced apples in a greased baking dish.
  3. In a bowl, mix oats, almond flour, coconut sugar, cinnamon, and melted plant-based butter to form the crumble topping.
  4. Sprinkle the topping evenly over the apples.
  5. Bake for 30-35 minutes until the topping is golden and the apples are tender.
  6. Serve warm with a scoop of vegan ice cream.

8. Vegan Chocolate-Dipped Strawberries

Chocolate-dipped strawberries are a simple yet elegant treat that’s always a crowd-pleaser. Using dark chocolate, these strawberries are sweet and rich, with a slight bitterness from the chocolate that balances out the natural sweetness of the fruit. This dessert is perfect for parties or romantic occasions.

Ingredients:

  • 10 fresh strawberries
  • 1/2 cup dark chocolate chips
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil (optional)

Directions:

  1. Melt the dark chocolate chips in a microwave-safe bowl with coconut oil if using.
  2. Dip each strawberry into the melted chocolate, coating it evenly.
  3. Place the dipped strawberries on parchment paper and let the chocolate harden in the fridge for about 10-15 minutes.
  4. Serve chilled and enjoy the simple decadence of chocolate-dipped strawberries.

9. Vegan Coconut Macaroons

Vegan coconut macaroons are crispy on the outside and chewy on the inside, making them a perfect treat for coconut lovers. With just a handful of ingredients, you can easily prepare these macaroons in under 30 minutes. They’re perfect for a holiday treat or a sweet snack throughout the week.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups shredded coconut
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1/4 cup almond flour
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • A pinch of sea salt

Directions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix shredded coconut, almond flour, maple syrup, vanilla extract, and sea salt.
  3. Form the mixture into small mounds and place them on a baking sheet.
  4. Bake for 12-15 minutes until the edges are golden brown.
  5. Let cool before serving.

10. Vegan Chocolate Pudding

Rich, velvety, and indulgent, vegan chocolate pudding is a classic dessert that’s easy to make and perfect for any chocolate lover. You can prepare it in just a few minutes, and the best part is that it’s completely dairy-free and egg-free, making it the ideal dessert for vegans and those with dietary restrictions.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup almond milk
  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1/4 cup cocoa powder
  • 1/4 cup maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch

Directions:

  1. In a saucepan, combine almond milk, coconut milk, cocoa powder, maple syrup, and cornstarch.
  2. Cook over medium heat, whisking constantly, until the pudding thickens.
  3. Pour into serving dishes and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to set.
  4. Serve chilled with a dollop of whipped coconut cream or fresh fruit.

These 10 easy and delicious vegan desserts are perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth without compromising your plant-based lifestyle. Whether you’re in the mood for something chocolatey, fruity, or creamy, there’s a dessert here for everyone. Try out these recipes today, and treat yourself to a guilt-free indulgence!
