Delicious and Easy Mexican Recipes to Try Tonight


If you’re craving vibrant, flavorful, and easy-to-make dishes that will transport your taste buds straight to the heart of Mexico, then you’ve come to the right place! Mexican cuisine is known for its rich, diverse, and comforting flavors, making it a go-to choice for any meal. Whether you’re a seasoned chef or someone who just loves trying new recipes, these Mexican dishes are guaranteed to satisfy your hunger and spice up your evening.

1. Tacos: A Timeless Favorite

Tacos are a staple in Mexican cuisine, and they can be as simple or as complex as you like. Whether you’re making them with grilled chicken, beef, pork, or a vegetarian filling, tacos are always a crowd-pleaser. The beauty of tacos lies in their versatility – you can customize them with an array of toppings to suit your preferences.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef (or any meat of choice)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 1 cup water
  • Soft or hard taco shells
  • Toppings (shredded lettuce, cheese, sour cream, salsa, avocado, cilantro, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until soft.
  2. Add the ground beef and cook until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  3. Sprinkle in the taco seasoning, then add the water. Stir to combine and let it simmer for about 5 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  4. Warm the taco shells in the oven or microwave.
  5. Assemble the tacos by filling the shells with the beef mixture and topping with your favorite toppings like shredded lettuce, cheese, salsa, sour cream, and avocado.

Tacos are incredibly easy to prepare and can be made in under 30 minutes. This dish is perfect for a weeknight dinner, and you can adjust the filling and toppings to suit your tastes.

2. Chicken Enchiladas: Comfort in Every Bite

Enchiladas are one of the most iconic dishes in Mexican cuisine. They’re comforting, cheesy, and packed with flavor. While the cooking time may take a bit longer than tacos, the result is worth every minute. You can make the enchiladas with chicken, beef, or even a vegetarian filling like beans and cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups cooked shredded chicken
  • 10 corn tortillas
  • 1 can red enchilada sauce
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup diced onions
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C).
  2. In a skillet, lightly heat the corn tortillas for a few seconds on each side to make them pliable.
  3. Spread a bit of enchilada sauce on the bottom of a baking dish.
  4. Take each tortilla and fill it with shredded chicken and a little diced onion. Roll the tortilla up and place it seam-side down in the baking dish.
  5. Once all the tortillas are in the dish, pour the remaining enchilada sauce over them and sprinkle with shredded cheese.
  6. Bake the enchiladas for about 20 minutes or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Serve with a dollop of sour cream and garnish with fresh cilantro.

These chicken enchiladas are rich, comforting, and filled with layers of flavor. They’re perfect for a family dinner or a gathering with friends.

3. Guacamole: The Ultimate Mexican Dip

No Mexican meal is complete without a bowl of fresh guacamole. This creamy, zesty dip is perfect for pairing with chips or as a topping for tacos and burritos. Making guacamole at home is simple, and the ingredients are easy to find.

Ingredients:

  • 3 ripe avocados
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, diced
  • 1 jalapeño, seeds removed and finely chopped (optional for heat)
  • 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Cut the avocados in half and remove the pit. Scoop the flesh into a bowl and mash it with a fork until smooth but still chunky.
  2. Add the chopped onion, tomato, jalapeño (if using), lime juice, salt, and pepper. Mix well.
  3. Garnish with fresh cilantro.
  4. Serve immediately with tortilla chips or as a topping for your tacos, burritos, or nachos.

Guacamole is a fresh, zesty dip that brings out the best of Mexican flavors. It’s easy to make, and its creamy texture pairs perfectly with crunchy chips or anything else you decide to dip it into.

4. Mexican Street Corn (Elote): A Flavor Explosion

Mexican street corn, or “elote,” is a popular street food found all over Mexico. It’s sweet, savory, spicy, and creamy all at once. The combination of grilled corn with a rich coating of mayonnaise, cheese, chili powder, and lime creates an irresistible flavor explosion.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ears of corn, husked
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup crumbled cotija cheese (or feta if you can’t find cotija)
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 1/4 tsp smoked paprika (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Grill the corn on medium heat, turning occasionally, until it’s lightly charred on all sides, about 10-12 minutes.
  2. While the corn is grilling, mix the mayonnaise, sour cream, lime juice, chili powder, smoked paprika (if using), and a pinch of salt in a small bowl.
  3. Once the corn is done, brush the mayo-sour cream mixture all over the corn, making sure it’s well-coated.
  4. Sprinkle with cotija cheese and a little extra chili powder for added spice.
  5. Serve the elote with lime wedges on the side.

Mexican street corn is a fantastic side dish or snack that’s full of flavor. It’s the perfect combination of smoky, creamy, and tangy that makes it irresistible.

5. Beef Burritos: Hearty and Satisfying

Burritos are a classic Mexican dish that can be filled with almost anything – rice, beans, cheese, meats, vegetables, or a combination of them all. These beef burritos are packed with savory beef, beans, and cheese, making them hearty and filling.

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 can refried beans
  • 1 packet taco seasoning
  • 4 large flour tortillas
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar or Mexican blend)
  • 1 cup cooked rice (optional)
  • Salsa for topping

Instructions:

  1. In a skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  2. Add the taco seasoning and a little water (according to the seasoning packet instructions) and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  3. Warm the flour tortillas in the microwave or on a skillet for a few seconds.
  4. To assemble the burritos, spread a layer of refried beans on the tortilla, then add a generous scoop of the beef mixture.
  5. Sprinkle cheese and top with cooked rice if using.
  6. Roll the burrito by folding in the sides and rolling it up tightly.
  7. Heat the burrito in a skillet for a minute or two to make it crispy, if desired.
  8. Serve with salsa and sour cream.

Beef burritos are a great option when you’re looking for a filling and delicious meal that’s easy to make. These burritos can also be customized with your favorite fillings and toppings.

6. Mexican Rice: The Perfect Side Dish

Every Mexican meal needs a great side dish, and Mexican rice is a classic option. This fluffy, flavorful rice is often served alongside tacos, burritos, or enchiladas. It’s simple to prepare and adds a wonderful depth of flavor to your meal.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes with green chilies
  • 1 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the diced onion and garlic and sauté until soft and fragrant.
  2. Add the rice to the skillet and cook, stirring constantly, until the rice is lightly browned.
  3. Stir in the diced tomatoes (with juices) and chicken broth. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let the rice simmer for about 20 minutes, or until the liquid is absorbed and the rice is tender.
  5. Fluff the rice with a fork, season with salt and pepper, and garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

Mexican rice is the perfect accompaniment to any Mexican meal. It’s fluffy, flavorful, and pairs beautifully with meats, beans, and salsas.

7. Churros: A Sweet Ending

No Mexican meal would be complete without a sweet treat, and churros are the ultimate dessert to finish off your dinner. These crispy, cinnamon-sugar-coated doughnuts are easy to make and incredibly delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup sugar (for coating)
  • 1 tbsp cinnamon

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring the water, sugar, and butter to a boil.
  2. Once the butter has melted, stir in the flour and cook for about 2-3 minutes until the dough pulls away from the sides of the pan.
  3. Remove the pan from the heat and let the dough cool for a few minutes. Then, beat in the eggs one at a time, followed by the vanilla extract.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  5. Transfer the dough into a piping bag with a star-shaped nozzle. Pipe strips of dough into the hot oil, cutting them into your desired length.
  6. Fry the churros until golden brown, then remove them from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  7. In a separate bowl, mix the sugar and cinnamon. Roll the churros in the cinnamon-sugar mixture while they’re still warm.
  8. Serve with chocolate sauce or dulce de leche for dipping.

Churros are the perfect way to end any Mexican meal. They’re crispy on the outside, soft on the inside, and coated with sweet cinnamon sugar.

These delicious and easy Mexican recipes are perfect for spicing up your dinner routine. Whether you’re in the mood for something savory like tacos or enchiladas or you’re craving a sweet treat like churros, these dishes offer something for everyone. So gather your ingredients, get in the kitchen, and enjoy a festive Mexican meal tonight!
