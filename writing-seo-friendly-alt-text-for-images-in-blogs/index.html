Writing SEO-Friendly Alt Text for Images in Blogs


Have you ever encountered a blog post with images, yet there are no descriptions or captions to help you understand their contents? Indeed, this scenario occurs frequently, and inserting appropriate alt text can enhance usability, accessibility, and search engine optimization. This post will guide you on writing compelling and effective SEO-friendly alt text for your blog images.

Why Use Alt Text?

Traditionally, search engines only understand the text on a webpage. However, with images, they struggle to determine if an image is relevant to the content without any context. Alt text, also known as alternative text, offers context for search engines and visually impaired users using screen readers. Thus, a high-quality alt text describes the image’s contents and accurately conveys its significance to the webpage’s topic.

Identifying the Right Terms for Alt Text:

It’s essential to use descriptive terms when writing alt text. Consider the keywords you would use when optimizing the webpage and incorporate them into the description. These terms will help search engines connect the image to the subject while enhancing your site’s search engine rankings. To identify the right terms, consider the following:

  1. Contextual terms: Choose words that are relevant to the post’s theme and subject matter.
  2. Image-related terms: Identify words that describe the visual elements or graphic nature of the image.
  3. User-friendly language: Use clear, concise, and simple terms that anyone can understand.

Length of Alt Text:

Google advises that adequate alt text should be “descriptive” and “a sensible and necessary” part of the page. Be cautious, however, not to overdo it with unnecessary wordiness. A well-written alt text should be approximately one sentence in length, with no more than 125 characters.

Best Practices for Writing Effective Alt Text:

  1. Use the “img” tag: Attribute your images with the “img” tag, specifying the desired alt text within the “alt” attribute.
<img src="image.jpg" alt="A vibrant image of a lion roaming through the savannah">

  1. Be specific about the image: Incorporate labels, descriptors, and modifiers to provide more context about the image.

  2. Avoid generic terms: Instead of writing “a tree,” elaborate with “a towering oak tree in autumn.”

  3. Concentrate on auditory descriptions: If applicable, describe sounds in the scene or effect of objects, like “the sound of ocean waves crashing against the rocks.”

  4. Highlight important visual elements: For infographics, diagrams, or image-heavy content, describe the main visual elements and the relationships between them.

  5. Consider the context: If you’re unsure what to include in alt text, think about what you would tell someone on the phone or in person when describing an image.

  6. Avoid overusing keywords: Remember that stuffing irrelevant keywords in the alt text isn’t helpful. Instead, use terms naturally and contextually. Strive for quality over quantity.

Evolution and Review of Alt Text:

Ensuring your alt text remains up-to-date and relevant to your content is crucial. Frequent review and update of the descriptions will help keep your content relevant to changes in both the site’s design and the expectations of users.

Conclusion:

Alt text enhances your blog’s accessibility and user experience while providing search engines crucial information about your content. Writing quality alt text, though simple in concept, takes practice to achieve reader-friendliness, accuracy, and contextual relevance. Keep these tips in mind and embark on your journey to optimizing images through effective alt text.
