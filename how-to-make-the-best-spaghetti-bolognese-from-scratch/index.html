How to Make the Best Spaghetti Bolognese from Scratch


Spaghetti Bolognese is a classic Italian dish beloved worldwide for its rich flavor, comforting textures, and satisfying combination of pasta and meaty sauce. Making this dish from scratch elevates it to new heights, allowing you to control the ingredients and flavors to create a dish that’s both authentic and personalized. While there are many variations of this beloved recipe, making a traditional Bolognese sauce from scratch requires patience, attention to detail, and the right ingredients. Here’s how you can craft the best Spaghetti Bolognese from scratch.

Ingredients You’ll Need

Before diving into the cooking process, it’s important to gather the right ingredients for an authentic and flavorful Bolognese sauce. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Olive oil: Used for sautéing the vegetables and meat. Olive oil adds a lovely depth of flavor.
  • Onion: One medium onion, finely chopped, adds sweetness and depth to the sauce.
  • Carrot: A finely grated or chopped carrot brings a subtle sweetness that complements the richness of the sauce.
  • Celery: A couple of stalks of celery, finely chopped, add a refreshing and slightly earthy flavor.
  • Garlic: A couple of cloves, minced, for an aromatic base.
  • Ground beef: Use lean ground beef or a combination of beef and pork for a richer, more flavorful sauce.
  • Tomato paste: Adds concentrated tomato flavor and thickness to the sauce.
  • Crushed tomatoes: A can of good-quality crushed tomatoes provides the base for the sauce.
  • Red wine: A splash of red wine enhances the sauce’s depth and complexity.
  • Beef broth: Adds richness and helps balance the acidity of the tomatoes.
  • Milk or cream: A small amount of milk or cream makes the sauce velvety and smooth, cutting through the acidity of the tomatoes.
  • Herbs and seasonings: Fresh thyme, rosemary, bay leaves, salt, and black pepper are essential for seasoning.
  • Spaghetti: Fresh or dried pasta, traditionally spaghetti, is the perfect vehicle for this sauce.

The Secret to a Perfect Bolognese Sauce: Building Layers of Flavor

The key to an amazing Bolognese sauce lies in building layers of flavor. The process might take a little time, but it’s well worth the effort. Slow-cooking the sauce allows the flavors to meld together, creating a rich and complex taste.

Step 1: Sauté the Vegetables

Start by heating a generous amount of olive oil in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the finely chopped onion, carrot, and celery. These vegetables, collectively known as soffritto, form the aromatic base of the sauce. Cook the vegetables for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until they soften and start to caramelize slightly. This step is crucial because it develops a deep, sweet flavor that will form the foundation of your Bolognese.

Once the vegetables have softened, add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes until fragrant. Be careful not to burn the garlic, as it can turn bitter.

Step 2: Brown the Meat

Next, add the ground beef (or a mix of beef and pork) to the pot. Break up the meat with a spoon or spatula and cook it until it’s browned and no longer pink. This step is important because browning the meat adds a rich, savory depth to the sauce. Stir the meat occasionally to ensure it cooks evenly.

Once the meat is browned, you may notice some caramelized bits sticking to the bottom of the pot. These bits are packed with flavor, so be sure to scrape them off the bottom of the pot as you stir the meat. This process, called deglazing, helps to incorporate all the delicious flavors into the sauce.

Step 3: Add the Wine

Now it’s time to add the red wine. Pour in a splash of wine (about half a cup) and let it simmer for a few minutes, allowing the alcohol to cook off and the wine to reduce slightly. The wine adds acidity and complexity to the sauce, balancing the richness of the meat and the sweetness of the vegetables.

Step 4: Stir in the Tomato Paste

Next, add a tablespoon or two of tomato paste to the mixture. Tomato paste is concentrated and helps deepen the flavor of the sauce. Stir it in well and cook for about 3 minutes to allow the paste to caramelize and release its flavors. This step adds a rich tomato flavor without the excessive acidity found in fresh tomatoes.

Step 5: Add Crushed Tomatoes and Broth

Once the tomato paste is fully incorporated, add the crushed tomatoes and beef broth. The crushed tomatoes provide the bulk of the sauce, while the broth adds richness and helps thin the sauce to the right consistency. Stir everything together, ensuring that the tomato paste, wine, and meat are well-mixed.

Bring the mixture to a simmer, and then reduce the heat to low. Allow the sauce to cook for at least 1.5 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally. The long cooking time is essential for developing the depth of flavor that makes Bolognese sauce so special.

Step 6: Add the Milk or Cream

About halfway through the cooking time, add a small amount of milk or cream—around 1/4 cup. This helps to mellow the acidity of the tomatoes and creates a smooth, velvety texture. The milk or cream also adds richness to the sauce, making it more indulgent and luxurious.

At this point, you can also add fresh herbs like thyme, rosemary, and a couple of bay leaves. These herbs infuse the sauce with a wonderful, earthy aroma. Don’t forget to season with salt and pepper to taste.

Step 7: Simmer and Stir

Continue to simmer the sauce for another hour or more. The longer you let the sauce cook, the more the flavors will meld and deepen. Keep an eye on the sauce, and if it starts to dry out, add a little more broth or water.

Step 8: Prepare the Spaghetti

While the Bolognese sauce is simmering, it’s time to cook the pasta. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil, then add the spaghetti. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions, or until al dente—firm to the bite.

Once the pasta is ready, drain it well, reserving a small cup of pasta water. The starchy pasta water can be used to adjust the consistency of the sauce if needed.

Step 9: Combine the Sauce and Pasta

Once the Bolognese sauce has finished cooking and the spaghetti is ready, it’s time to bring the two together. You have two options: either mix the sauce directly into the pasta or serve the sauce on top of the pasta. If you prefer the sauce to coat the pasta, you can add a little pasta water to the sauce to help it adhere better.

Toss the pasta with the sauce until it’s well-coated. If you like, you can top the dish with freshly grated Parmesan cheese for an extra burst of flavor.

Tips for the Perfect Bolognese Sauce

  • Patience is key: A true Bolognese sauce requires time to develop its flavors. The longer you cook it, the more delicious it will be.
  • Use quality meat: For the best flavor, use fresh, high-quality ground beef and pork. Some people even recommend using a mix of veal, pork, and beef for a more authentic taste.
  • Don’t skip the wine: The wine adds acidity and depth that are essential to the sauce. Don’t skip this step!
  • Feel free to adjust the seasonings: Depending on your taste, you can add a pinch of nutmeg or a little cinnamon for extra warmth and depth.

Serving Suggestions

Spaghetti Bolognese is perfect as a main course, but it can also be served with a variety of sides to enhance the meal:

  • Garlic bread: Crispy, buttery garlic bread pairs wonderfully with the rich sauce.
  • Salad: A light, refreshing green salad with a tangy vinaigrette helps balance the richness of the dish.
  • Wine: Pair the meal with a glass of red wine, such as Chianti or Cabernet Sauvignon, to complement the flavors of the sauce.

Conclusion

Making the best Spaghetti Bolognese from scratch is an investment in flavor, and the result is a dish that will leave your guests or family members asking for more. By following these steps and paying attention to the details—slow-cooking the sauce, using quality ingredients, and seasoning carefully—you can create a Bolognese sauce that is rich, savory, and utterly satisfying. Whether you’re enjoying it for a weeknight dinner or serving it at a family gathering, this timeless Italian dish is sure to impress.
