The Best Fantasy Books for Fans of Medieval Times


Fantasy literature has always been a beloved genre for readers who enjoy intricate world-building, heroic battles, and sweeping narratives. For those who have an affinity for medieval settings, where knights, castles, dragons, and political intrigue dominate the landscape, there is a treasure trove of captivating books that transport readers to distant, magical realms. Whether you’re seeking epic sagas or intimate tales of bravery, these fantasy books will take you deep into the heart of medieval-inspired worlds. Here are the best fantasy books for fans of medieval times, each brimming with adventure, mystery, and timeless appeal.

1. The Lord of the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien

It’s impossible to discuss medieval-inspired fantasy without mentioning J.R.R. Tolkien’s The Lord of the Rings. This monumental trilogy, set in the richly developed world of Middle-earth, has become the cornerstone of modern fantasy literature. The story of Frodo Baggins’ quest to destroy the One Ring is filled with elements of medieval mythology, including heroic quests, ancient evil, powerful wizards, and noble knights. Tolkien’s intricate world-building, complete with languages, histories, and cultures, creates a tapestry that feels both timeless and deeply connected to medieval traditions.

Middle-earth itself, with its diverse races such as elves, dwarves, and orcs, as well as the epic scale of its conflicts, evokes the medieval periods of history. Fans of medieval times will find plenty to admire in the valor of the warriors, the chivalric themes, and the eternal struggle between good and evil that resonates with the spirit of medieval epics.

2. A Song of Ice and Fire by George R.R. Martin

No list of medieval-inspired fantasy would be complete without mentioning A Song of Ice and Fire, the series that inspired the hit TV show Game of Thrones. George R.R. Martin’s sprawling epic takes place in the fictional continents of Westeros and Essos, where noble houses vie for power, dragons rise from myth, and the very fate of the world hangs in the balance. Martin’s depiction of medieval politics, with its court intrigues, battles for power, and complex characters, is unparalleled in modern fantasy.

The series blends historical inspiration, particularly from the War of the Roses, with the fantastical elements of magic and mythical creatures. The grittiness of Martin’s world, where allegiances are fluid and heroes can easily become villains, captures the harsh realities of medieval life, where honor often clashes with survival.

3. The Broken Empire Trilogy by Mark Lawrence

For fans of darker, more introspective fantasy, The Broken Empire Trilogy by Mark Lawrence is an excellent choice. The series follows Jorg Ancrath, a ruthless and haunted prince, as he embarks on a bloody quest for revenge and power in a fractured, medieval-inspired world. Lawrence’s writing is poetic and brutal, capturing the desolation of a post-apocalyptic world where remnants of a once-great empire now lie in ruins.

The trilogy is deeply philosophical, exploring themes of morality, power, and the consequences of violence. Fans of medieval times will appreciate the grit of Jorg’s journey, as well as the richly imagined landscapes and castles that evoke the feeling of a once-glorious medieval kingdom now falling into decay.

4. The Wheel of Time by Robert Jordan

Another monumental fantasy series, The Wheel of Time by Robert Jordan, is a must-read for any fan of medieval fantasy. Spanning fourteen books, the series takes place in a world where the Wheel of Time spins the threads of fate, and powerful figures known as Aes Sedai wield magical powers. The story focuses on Rand al’Thor, a young man destined to face the Dark One, a malevolent force threatening to remake the world.

While the series includes elements of magic and prophecy, it is steeped in medieval culture, particularly in its social structures, weaponry, and battles. The intricate politics and constant shifting of power among various factions mirror the feudal system of medieval times, and the battles between light and dark feel very much like the epic confrontations of history’s greatest wars. Fans of medieval themes will find much to appreciate in the way Jordan draws upon these historical elements while weaving a story filled with mystery and intrigue.

5. The Farseer Trilogy by Robin Hobb

For readers who enjoy tales of royal intrigue, political maneuvering, and complex character development, Robin Hobb’s Farseer Trilogy is a compelling choice. The series follows FitzChivalry Farseer, a royal bastard trained as an assassin, who finds himself at the center of courtly politics and magic in a medieval-inspired world. Hobb’s writing excels at depicting the emotional turmoil of her characters, particularly Fitz, who struggles with his place in a world that views him as an outsider.

The world-building in the Farseer Trilogy is rich, with a well-defined political system, social stratification, and magical traditions that give the story an authentic medieval feel. The plot is driven by both personal conflict and larger, world-altering events, blending intimate character moments with epic battles and courtly intrigue.

6. The Chronicles of Thomas Covenant by Stephen R. Donaldson

Stephen R. Donaldson’s The Chronicles of Thomas Covenant is another classic in the genre of medieval-inspired fantasy. The series follows Thomas Covenant, a modern man who is transported to a fantastical world where he is hailed as the savior of a land on the brink of destruction. However, Covenant struggles with his role as the hero, as he is skeptical of the magical world and reluctant to accept his destiny.

The world Donaldson creates is both fantastical and medieval, with knights, wizards, and mythical creatures living alongside a rich history and culture. The conflict between good and evil is central to the series, but it is also deeply personal, exploring the moral complexities of heroism, fate, and responsibility. The Thomas Covenant series is ideal for readers who enjoy medieval fantasy with a philosophical and psychological twist.

7. The First Law Trilogy by Joe Abercrombie

Joe Abercrombie’s The First Law Trilogy is perfect for readers who crave a gritty, no-holds-barred approach to medieval fantasy. Set in a world where brutal wars rage and characters are often morally ambiguous, the series follows a diverse cast of characters, including a barbarian, a torturer, and a reluctant hero, as they navigate the political and social upheaval of their time.

Abercrombie’s world is steeped in medieval violence, with brutal battles, dark magic, and a sense of hopelessness that permeates every page. His characters are far from traditional heroes; they are flawed, often making morally questionable choices, which gives the series a sense of realism rarely seen in traditional medieval fantasy. Fans of medieval times will appreciate Abercrombie’s unflinching portrayal of a world where survival often comes at the cost of one’s humanity.

8. The Inheritance Trilogy by N.K. Jemisin

N.K. Jemisin’s The Inheritance Trilogy blends elements of medieval power struggles with intricate world-building and divine politics. The story is set in a world where gods are enslaved by mortals, and the protagonist, Yeine Darr, finds herself caught in a deadly power play between rival factions of gods and humans. The series explores themes of power, family, and destiny, with a strong emphasis on political intrigue and personal sacrifice.

The world of The Inheritance Trilogy is both lush and brutal, with its portrayal of feudal power structures and divine manipulation evoking the dark, complex realities of medieval kingdoms. Jemisin’s writing is rich with symbolism and layered storytelling, making this a compelling read for fans of medieval fantasy who enjoy books with emotional depth and philosophical undertones.

9. The Black Prism by Brent Weeks

Brent Weeks’ The Lightbringer Series, beginning with The Black Prism, offers a unique take on medieval-inspired fantasy with its use of magic based on light and color. In this world, magic is tied to the manipulation of light, creating a vibrant and visually stunning system of powers that feel both fresh and rooted in medieval concepts of magic.

At the heart of the series is Gavin Guile, the world’s most powerful man, who is also burdened with the responsibilities of ruling and protecting his kingdom. The world-building is vast, with intricate political systems, warring factions, and a highly structured society reminiscent of medieval feudalism. Fans of medieval fantasy will appreciate the intense world-building, the moral dilemmas, and the sweeping scale of the conflicts, all set within a universe that feels both familiar and original.

10. The Sword of Shannara Trilogy by Terry Brooks

Terry Brooks’ The Sword of Shannara Trilogy is a seminal work in the realm of medieval fantasy. The first book, The Sword of Shannara, follows a young hero, Shea Ohmsford, who embarks on a quest to destroy a powerful weapon that could bring about the end of the world. The story features many of the classic tropes of medieval fantasy, including an unlikely hero, a powerful magical object, and an ancient evil.

While the series draws heavily from J.R.R. Tolkien’s work, it also creates a unique world filled with its own mythologies, histories, and races. For fans of medieval times, The Sword of Shannara offers a nostalgic return to the epic quests and battles that define the genre, with a story that will appeal to both new readers and long-time fantasy fans.

Conclusion

For fans of medieval times, fantasy literature offers a wealth of stories filled with knights, dragons, and political intrigue. Whether you’re looking for epic battles, complex characters, or worlds rich with history and magic, the books listed here will transport you to worlds inspired by the medieval past. From the sprawling epics of The Lord of the Rings to the darker, more intimate tales of The Broken Empire, there is something for every reader who loves the allure of medieval fantasy. Dive into these worlds, and prepare to be captivated by the magic, adventure, and timeless stories they have to offer.
