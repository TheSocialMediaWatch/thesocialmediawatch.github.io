The Classics That Famous Actors Can't Stop Talking About


Have you ever wondered which classic films and novels have had the most significant impact on the world’s most renowned actors and directors? As much as they are idolized by fans for their exceptional talent and skills, actors too, are often enthusiasts who have been greatly influenced by classic works of literature, theater, and cinema. Today, let’s delve into a fascinating exploration of the classics that famous actors can’t stop talking about.

When asked about his influences, at the peak of his career, Hollywood star, Gary Oldman, cited the great Peter Sellers as his role model and expressed admiration towards Charlie Chaplin and Buster Keaton. These icons of the silent era had a formidable impact on Oldman’s approach to acting, character development, and physical comedy.

Oprah Winfrey’s love for literature is no secret. She’s often been seen gushing about “To Kill a Mockingbird” by Harper Lee. This poignant narrative of a young girl growing up in the racially segregated South, exploring themes of equality, morality, and empathy, has left an indelible mark on the legendary talk-show host’s life. Similarly, Jennifer Lawrence was known to adore Harper Lee’s work, having performed a reading of “To Kill a Mockingbird” when she was just 14 years old.

The tragic beauty of the world described in “The Great Gatsby” by F. Scott Fitzgerald is why Leonardo DiCaprio has constantly spoken about his adoration for the story. One of the leading actors of our time, DiCaprio’s strong connection to the character of Jay Gatsby brings to life one of the most iconic characters in modern literature. When Meryl Streep speaks of her favorite book, she won’t hesitate to mention “The Odyssey” by the legendary Homer. Streep finds great inspiration in the epic tale of a hero’s journey and his struggle against the challenges that test human endurance and spirit.

For Halle Berry, who once dreamt of becoming a casting director, “High Fidelity” by Nick Hornby remains the go-to book. The novel explores the life of a recovering romantic-turned-record-shop-owner, and has been a source of anecdotal inspiration for the talented actress.={<O:0}

Outside Hollywood, the influence of classic cinema has seeped in across borders. Indian cinema’s finest actress, Waheeda Rehman, can often be found speaking fondly of “Gone with the Wind.” Released in 1939, the movie’s grandeur and the character depth presented by the story captivated the iconic Bollywood star.

Speaking of depth, it would be remiss not to mention the impact that the works ofliterature have had on one of the finest actor-directors of the modern cinema, Joaquin Phoenix. Phoenix cites Fyodor Dostoevsky’s “Crime and Punishment” as one of the most inspiring novels of all time. This exploration of moral philosophy and the psychological gambit that shapes the protagonist’s journey, deeply resonates with the artist’s infatuation for in-depth character analysis.

As we revisit these timeless stories and captivating films, we can see that they have transcended generations, carrying on their shoulders the mark of eternity by ceaselessly enchanting the greatest talents of our times. These classics have continued to influence countless actors and play an essential role in shaping the cinematic experience that we know and love today.

Curious to find out which classic works have had a lasting impact on other famous actors and actresses you admire? No need to search far and wide, just revisit their interviews to find your answer. The literary and cinematic greats will always continue to captivate and influence those who feel, think, imagine, and ignite expression on a canvas, on a small or big screen.
