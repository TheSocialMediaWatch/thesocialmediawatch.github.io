DIY Jewelry Holder: Keep Accessories Tidy without the Clutter


Jewelry and accessories can sometimes become tangled and cluttered, making it difficult to find the perfect piece for any occasion. To maintain order in your space and allow easy access to your favorite items, consider crafting a custom DIY jewelry holder that not only keeps your pieces organized but is also stylish and functional. In this post, we’ll explore different ways to create a one-of-a-kind jewelry holder using commonly found materials and a variety of techniques. Let’s get started!

Choosing your base The first step in designing your DIY jewelry holder is selecting the foundation. To give you a place to start, let’s explore two popular choices:

  1. Wooden plaque: A wooden plaque or panel is an ideal foundation, as it is easy to work with and can be painted or decorated to achieve any desired aesthetic. You can choose from a variety of sizes and thicknesses, depending on the size of your jewelry collection and available space.

  2. Cork bulletin board: For those who prefer something eco-friendly, a cork bulletin board makes for an excellent base. Cork is naturally flexible, allowing you to firmly secure your accessories without damaging them. Plus, it provides a neutral backdrop, making it easy to complement your decor with decorative elements.

Now that you have your base, it’s time to consider the best way to display your jewelry.

Displaying your jewelry The beauty of a DIY jewelry holder is the creative freedom it offers, allowing you to develop a customized organization system suited to your unique collection. Here are a few ideas to help kickstart your imagination:

  1. Bead curtain: Attach fishing line or a bead curtain to your base, allowing each strand to have enough tension to securely hold your accessories. This design is perfect for bead necklaces, charm bracelets, and rings, as well as decorative tinsel wire and studs.

  2. Pegboard system: Cut a piece of pegboard to your desired size and secure it to your base. Various peg shapes and sizes can be used to accommodate your specific necklace lengths, earrings, and rings. Hole punches and mounting strips can also be used to create additional hooks for rings or earrings.

  3. Magazine sleeves: Purchase cardstock or fabric magazine sleeves and staple or glue them to your base. This creates discreet pockets that can comfortably house smaller jewelry boxes, rings, or bracelets. You can customize the sleeves with photographs, adhesive vinyl, or stencils to coordinate with your current décor.

Adding a touch of style To transform your DIY jewelry holder into a stunning decorative piece, consider these three elegant enhancements:

  1. Paint and stencils: Give your base a fresh coat of paint in your favorite color or pattern. Once the paint has dried, use stencils or freehanded designs to create unique patterns that showcase your personal style.

  2. Adhesive embellishments: Enhance your jewelry holder with decals, adhesive gems, or glitter to create an eye-catching accessory station. These decorative elements add the perfect finishing touches while also serving to complement the theme of your space.

  3. Lighting: Add a fairy light garland or a low-intensity LED strip along the bottom of your jewelry holder to create a stunning and practical ambiance. Soft, warm light emphasizes the beauty of your accessories while making organizing in the evening a relaxing experience.

Organizing your space Arranging your jewelry on your new holder can be an overwhelming task. Begin by organizing your jewelry by type and preferably by color. Keep necklaces separated and tangle-free by looping them over their designated hooks or hanging them on dowel rods. Group bracelets in clear zippered bags or adjustable wall pockets, and secure earring pairs together or store them in small, clear shoeboxes with labels.

DIY jewelry holders help transform a cluttered and chaotic collection into an organized and visually appealing accessory station. Whether you choose a wooden plaque or a cork bulletin board, the options for customization are endless. By experimenting with different materials, designing your perfect hooks and holders, and enhancing your creation with personal touches, you can create an organized and stylish accessory display that suits your unique sense of style.

As you embark on your jewelry organization journey, remember that the most important components of this project are practicality and creativity. Embrace your creativity and have fun designing your customized display!

Happy creating!
