The Best Craft Beer and Liquor Pairings for Your Next BBQ


When it comes to BBQs, there’s something about the combination of perfectly grilled meats, smoky flavors, and the right drink that takes the experience to a whole new level. Whether you’re hosting a backyard BBQ or attending a friendly gathering, the right craft beer or liquor pairing can elevate the meal and leave a lasting impression. In this guide, we’ll dive into the best craft beer and liquor pairings for your next BBQ, offering suggestions for different types of meats, sides, and even dessert.

Why Pairing Matters

Pairing craft beer and liquor with BBQ isn’t just about picking what you like best — it’s about finding drinks that enhance the flavors of your food. The smoky, savory, and sometimes spicy notes of grilled meats can be complemented by specific drink styles that either contrast with or highlight those flavors. The right drink pairing can help cut through the richness of fatty cuts of meat or bring out the complexity in a flavorful sauce.

Key Considerations for Pairing

  • Flavor intensity: Consider the intensity of both the beer or liquor and the food. Bold flavors like smoky brisket or spicy ribs often pair better with strong, full-bodied drinks.
  • Acidity and sweetness: Sweetness in drinks can balance out spicy or tangy BBQ sauces, while acidic drinks can cut through the fat of grilled meats.
  • Carbonation: Carbonated beverages like beer help cleanse your palate between bites, while smoother spirits like whiskey can bring warmth and depth to the experience.

Best Craft Beer Pairings for BBQ

1. Pilsner with Grilled Chicken or Sausages

A crisp, refreshing Pilsner is an excellent choice when serving lighter grilled meats like chicken or sausages. The light maltiness and slight hop bitterness of a Pilsner won’t overpower the flavor of the meat, making it a perfect balance. Whether you’re grilling simple herb-marinated chicken or a flavorful sausage link, the carbonation of the Pilsner helps cleanse your palate after every bite, preparing you for the next.

Why it works: The delicate flavors of grilled chicken or sausages are complemented by the clean, crisp finish of the Pilsner. This pairing offers a refreshing contrast to the slightly charred skin of the meat.

2. American IPA with BBQ Ribs or Pulled Pork

For a bolder BBQ experience, an American IPA is a fantastic choice. The IPA’s hoppy bitterness and strong citrus or pine notes pair beautifully with the bold, savory flavors of BBQ ribs or pulled pork. If your ribs are coated in a tangy, vinegar-based sauce, the IPA’s bitterness helps cut through the richness, balancing the sweet, savory flavors of the meat.

Why it works: The high carbonation and bitter finish of an IPA help to refresh the palate after each bite, ensuring the smoky flavors of the BBQ shine through. The hop profile of the beer also enhances the spices used in rubs or marinades.

3. Stout with Beef Brisket or Steak

A rich, dark stout is a fantastic match for hearty cuts of beef like brisket or steak. The maltiness and roasty flavors of a stout are a perfect complement to the deep, smoky flavors of slow-cooked brisket or a well-grilled steak. If your steak is seasoned with a charred crust or accompanied by a rich sauce like béarnaise or steak sauce, the stout will intensify the overall experience.

Why it works: The stout’s roasted notes and smooth texture pair beautifully with the umami-rich flavors of beef, providing a full-bodied and satisfying drinking experience. The beer’s slight sweetness also pairs well with caramelized crusts and sauces.

4. Wheat Beer with Grilled Veggies or Fish

When grilling vegetables like zucchini, bell peppers, or mushrooms, a wheat beer is an ideal match. Its light and slightly fruity profile pairs well with the freshness of grilled vegetables, while the slight yeastiness complements grilled fish like salmon or tilapia. The wheat beer’s slight spiciness and refreshing finish can balance out the natural sweetness of the vegetables, especially if they’re glazed with a sweet marinade.

Why it works: Wheat beer’s light body and slightly fruity, spicy flavors act as a perfect match for the charred yet fresh nature of vegetables and seafood. The beer helps cleanse the palate between bites without overpowering the dish.

5. Amber Ale with Grilled Burgers or BBQ Chicken Wings

Amber ales are an excellent all-around choice when you’re grilling hearty, flavorful foods like burgers or BBQ chicken wings. The malt-forward profile of an amber ale provides a perfect balance to the savory flavors of grilled meats, while its moderate hop bitterness ensures that it doesn’t overpower the food. The amber ale’s slightly sweet caramel notes pair well with the smoky, charred flavor of burgers and wings.

Why it works: The malt sweetness of the amber ale enhances the richness of the beef in a burger or the smoky tang of BBQ chicken wings. The beer’s moderate bitterness keeps the flavors fresh, preventing the dish from feeling too heavy.

Best Liquor Pairings for BBQ

1. Bourbon with Grilled Pork Chops or Baby Back Ribs

Bourbon is a classic choice for pairing with BBQ, thanks to its rich, slightly sweet, and smoky flavors. If you’re serving grilled pork chops or baby back ribs, the caramel and vanilla notes in bourbon pair beautifully with the sweet, smoky, and savory flavors of the meat. Whether you’re serving your pork chops with a smoky BBQ glaze or just a dry rub, bourbon complements those flavors and enhances the overall experience.

Why it works: The sweetness of bourbon balances the savory and smoky flavors in pork, while its warming qualities add an extra layer of depth to each bite. A sip of bourbon between bites of rich, smoky BBQ creates a full-bodied and satisfying experience.

2. Tequila with Grilled Fish Tacos or Shrimp Skewers

If you’re serving fish tacos or grilled shrimp skewers, tequila is an excellent choice. The crisp, slightly peppery flavors of a high-quality blanco or reposado tequila pair perfectly with the freshness of grilled seafood. Tequila’s slight herbal and citrus notes enhance the lightness of the fish, while the alcohol’s bite can cut through the richness of creamy sauces or guacamole often served with tacos.

Why it works: The agave-forward flavor of tequila works harmoniously with the bright, fresh flavors of seafood, while the acidity in the drink complements the tanginess of any citrus garnishes or sauces. A tequila cocktail like a margarita can also bring a refreshing contrast to spicy, grilled shrimp.

3. Rye Whiskey with Grilled Steaks or BBQ Brisket

For those who prefer something with a little more spice, rye whiskey is a great pairing for grilled steaks or BBQ brisket. Rye’s peppery, slightly dry finish pairs well with the bold, smoky flavors of beef, enhancing the richness without overpowering the dish. Whether you’re grilling a thick steak or slow-cooking brisket, rye whiskey’s complex flavors help balance the meat’s intensity.

Why it works: Rye whiskey’s spiciness and dryness contrast beautifully with the fatty, smoky richness of beef, creating a harmonious balance that’s both warming and refreshing. The slightly herbal and fruity notes in rye whiskey also complement the spices in rubs or marinades.

4. Rum with Grilled Pineapple or Jerk Chicken

Rum is a fantastic match for BBQ dishes that incorporate sweetness, such as grilled pineapple or jerk chicken. The tropical flavors of rum, particularly spiced or dark rum, enhance the sweetness of grilled fruits or the bold, spiced flavors in jerk seasoning. A rum cocktail, such as a mojito or a daiquiri, adds a refreshing, citrusy touch to the meal, making it the perfect drink for a summer BBQ.

Why it works: The warm, spicy flavors of rum complement the smoky sweetness of grilled pineapple, while its tropical notes bring out the spices in jerk chicken. Rum’s versatility also means it pairs well with both sweet and savory BBQ dishes.

5. Gin with Grilled Veggie Skewers or Herb-Marinated Chicken

Gin’s herbal, botanical flavors make it a perfect match for grilled veggies or herb-marinated chicken. The subtle juniper and citrus notes in gin enhance the freshness of the grilled vegetables, while the alcohol’s smooth, dry finish balances out the richness of the chicken. A classic gin and tonic or a gin-based cocktail can serve as a refreshing contrast to the smoky, savory flavors of the BBQ.

Why it works: The herbaceous and citrusy notes of gin bring out the natural flavors of grilled vegetables and chicken, while the refreshing nature of the drink ensures that the meal doesn’t feel too heavy. Gin’s versatility makes it a great option for lighter BBQ dishes that feature fresh herbs or delicate marinades.

Conclusion

When planning your next BBQ, consider how the craft beer and liquor you choose can enhance your food experience. From crisp and refreshing pilsners for grilled chicken to bold, smoky bourbon for BBQ ribs, there’s a drink pairing for every dish. Experiment with different flavors, textures, and combinations to find what works best for your palate, and don’t be afraid to try new drinks alongside your favorite BBQ recipes.

Remember, the best pairings will bring out the best in both your food and your drink. Enjoy your BBQ, and raise a glass to good company, great food, and the perfect pairing!
