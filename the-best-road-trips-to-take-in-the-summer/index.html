The Best Road Trips to Take in the Summer


Summer is the perfect time to embark on a road trip, as the long days, warm weather, and beautiful landscapes make for an unforgettable adventure. Whether you prefer coastal drives, mountain escapades, or traversing iconic highways, summer road trips offer the freedom to explore new places and create lasting memories. If you’re searching for the ultimate road trip ideas for the upcoming summer, look no further. This guide will take you through the best routes that will satisfy every type of traveler.

1. Pacific Coast Highway, California

Overview:

The Pacific Coast Highway (PCH), also known as California State Route 1, is arguably one of the most iconic road trips in the United States. This coastal drive offers jaw-dropping views of the Pacific Ocean, cliffside vistas, sandy beaches, and charming coastal towns. Starting from the northern part of California, near San Francisco, and stretching all the way to Southern California, the Pacific Coast Highway has become a must-do road trip for anyone visiting the West Coast.

Highlights:

  • Big Sur: One of the most dramatic stretches of the Pacific Coast Highway, Big Sur features rugged cliffs, secluded beaches, and lush forests. Stop at McWay Falls or hike the Bixby Creek Bridge for stunning photo opportunities.
  • Santa Barbara: Known as the “American Riviera,” Santa Barbara boasts Mediterranean-style architecture, vineyards, and plenty of sunshine. Take a walk along Stearns Wharf or visit the Santa Barbara Mission.
  • Monterey Bay: Home to one of the world’s most famous aquariums, the Monterey Bay Aquarium, this area also offers beautiful beaches, the scenic 17-Mile Drive, and the historic Cannery Row.

Why it’s perfect for summer:

The PCH provides a cool ocean breeze that offers a reprieve from the heat during summer months. With a combination of charming towns and unparalleled natural beauty, this road trip is a summer dream come true.

2. Route 66, Illinois to California

Overview:

Known as the “Main Street of America,” Route 66 holds a nostalgic and historic significance. Stretching from Chicago, Illinois, to Santa Monica, California, this legendary route takes you through small towns, quirky attractions, and iconic Americana. Whether you’re a history buff or simply looking to experience a true American road trip, Route 66 delivers an experience like no other.

Highlights:

  • Chicago, Illinois: Start your adventure in the vibrant city of Chicago. Visit Millennium Park, the Art Institute of Chicago, or take a cruise on Lake Michigan for a breathtaking view of the city’s skyline.
  • St. Louis, Missouri: Don’t miss the famous Gateway Arch, a national monument that represents the westward expansion of the United States. St. Louis also boasts plenty of parks, museums, and delicious BBQ.
  • Winslow, Arizona: For fans of the Eagles, stop in Winslow to stand on the corner made famous in the song “Take It Easy.” The town has become a popular roadside attraction.
  • Santa Monica, California: End your journey at the iconic Santa Monica Pier, with its Ferris wheel and a wide range of entertainment options.

Why it’s perfect for summer:

Route 66 provides a journey back in time, offering a combination of history, culture, and nostalgia. The summer heat along the route can be intense, but the many small towns offer places to cool off and enjoy some local hospitality.

3. Blue Ridge Parkway, Virginia to North Carolina

Overview:

The Blue Ridge Parkway is a scenic drive that stretches 469 miles through the Appalachian Highlands, connecting the Great Smoky Mountains National Park to Shenandoah National Park. Known for its stunning mountain views, the Parkway is an excellent choice for those looking for a peaceful and serene road trip with plenty of opportunities for hiking, wildlife viewing, and outdoor exploration.

Highlights:

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park: The drive through this park offers some of the most picturesque mountain views in the U.S., with opportunities to see a variety of wildlife, including black bears, deer, and wild turkeys.
  • Linville Falls: A beautiful waterfall located along the Parkway, it is a must-see for nature lovers. A short hike will take you to an overlook with breathtaking views of the falls.
  • Blowing Rock, North Carolina: A charming town where you can explore unique shops, restaurants, and outdoor activities. The Blowing Rock itself is a natural rock formation that offers sweeping views of the surrounding landscape.

Why it’s perfect for summer:

The Blue Ridge Parkway is perfect for those who enjoy cooler temperatures and lush green landscapes. During the summer months, the mountains provide a refreshing escape from the heat, and the drive offers plenty of places to stop and enjoy the natural beauty.

4. Great River Road, Minnesota to Louisiana

Overview:

The Great River Road is a historic route that follows the course of the Mississippi River, stretching from the headwaters in Minnesota to the mouth in Louisiana. This road trip offers a unique blend of riverfront views, charming towns, and cultural experiences. Along the way, you’ll find a variety of attractions, from historic river towns to state parks and scenic byways.

Highlights:

  • Minneapolis, Minnesota: Start your journey in the largest city in Minnesota, known for its parks, museums, and vibrant arts scene. Don’t miss the famous Minnehaha Falls or the Walker Art Center.
  • St. Louis, Missouri: The Gateway Arch is a major highlight of this section, but the city also offers a vibrant food scene, with famous dishes like toasted ravioli and gooey butter cake.
  • New Orleans, Louisiana: End your journey in the cultural hub of New Orleans, known for its jazz music, historic French Quarter, and delicious Creole and Cajun cuisine.

Why it’s perfect for summer:

The Great River Road offers a slower pace and plenty of scenic views. Summer is the ideal time to experience the lively culture and festivals along the Mississippi River. From the bustling streets of New Orleans to the serene riverside towns, this road trip combines history, nature, and vibrant culture.

5. Alaska Highway, Canada to Alaska

Overview:

For those seeking adventure in the wild, the Alaska Highway is one of the most thrilling road trips you can take. Starting in Dawson Creek, British Columbia, and ending in Delta Junction, Alaska, this route takes you through the rugged wilderness of northern Canada and into Alaska. The drive offers dramatic landscapes, wildlife sightings, and the chance to explore some of the most remote areas of North America.

Highlights:

  • Liard River Hot Springs: A perfect spot to relax in natural hot springs while enjoying the surrounding wilderness. The hot springs are a popular stop along the highway, offering a peaceful retreat.
  • Yukon: Home to stunning mountain views and crystal-clear lakes, the Yukon offers some of the most pristine wilderness in North America. Stop at the historic town of Whitehorse, the capital of the Yukon Territory, or venture into Kluane National Park.
  • Fairbanks, Alaska: Known for its rich history and outdoor activities, Fairbanks is a great place to experience the beauty of the Alaskan wilderness. You can take part in a dog sledding adventure or see the stunning Northern Lights.

Why it’s perfect for summer:

The Alaska Highway is best experienced during the summer when the days are long, and the landscape is vibrant with wildflowers and wildlife. It’s a journey for the adventurous spirit, offering a chance to escape the hustle and bustle of everyday life and immerse yourself in nature.

6. Going-to-the-Sun Road, Montana

Overview:

One of the most spectacular mountain drives in the U.S., Going-to-the-Sun Road cuts through the heart of Glacier National Park in Montana. This 50-mile road takes you over the Continental Divide, offering sweeping views of snow-capped peaks, alpine meadows, and crystal-clear lakes. The road is only open during the summer months due to heavy snowfall in the winter, making it a seasonal must-do for travelers.

Highlights:

  • Logan Pass: The highest point along the road, offering panoramic views of the surrounding mountains and valleys. A visitor center is located here, and there are plenty of hiking opportunities.
  • Lake McDonald: A stunning lake located along the western edge of the park, known for its clear, blue waters and surrounding mountain views.
  • St. Mary Lake: Another breathtaking lake located on the eastern side of Glacier National Park, offering a tranquil setting for picnicking or simply soaking in the natural beauty.

Why it’s perfect for summer:

The summer months offer the best chance to fully experience the park, with wildflowers in bloom, pristine lakes, and snow-capped peaks all set against a perfect blue sky. Going-to-the-Sun Road offers an unparalleled experience for those who appreciate nature and outdoor adventure.

Conclusion

Summer road trips are the ultimate way to explore new places, experience different cultures, and create lifelong memories. Whether you choose the winding curves of the Pacific Coast Highway, the historic charm of Route 66, or the wilderness beauty of the Alaska Highway, each of these routes offers a unique and unforgettable adventure. So pack your bags, fill up your tank, and hit the open road—your next great summer adventure awaits!
