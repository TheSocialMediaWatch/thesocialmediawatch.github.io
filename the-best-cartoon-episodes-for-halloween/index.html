The Best Cartoon Episodes for Halloween


Are you ready for some spine-tingling, hair-raising laughs? Halloween is just around the corner, and what better way to celebrate than by indulging in some classic cartoon episodes that will give you goosebumps? From spooky to downright terrifying, here we present the best cartoon episodes to watch this Halloween.

  1. The Simpsons: “Treehouse of Horror” The annual “Treehouse of Horror” episode has been a much-anticipated Halloween treat for Simpsons lovers since 1990. Each year, three original stories are delivered in an anthology format, with many paying homage to horror icons such as Edgar Allan Poe and Stephen King. With numerous episodes spanning more than two decades, it’s impossible to select one particular favorite. You’ll have to binge-watch them all!

  2. Scooby-Doo: “A Night Lingers on Haunted Highway” Scooby-Doo and the gang unearth a ghostly mystery on Haunted Highway, a former prison turned rest stop in the midst of a Howliday house hunt. The wit, the puns, and the eerie atmosphere make this cartoon episode a must-watch Halloween classic. An unmasking is inevitable, but the journey gets most frightfully fun!

  3. Gravity Falls: “A Reptilian Abomination” Gravity Falls has earned its spooky reputation in just two seasons due to creator Alex Hirsch’s extraordinary storytelling and meticulous design details. “A Reptilian Abomination” tackles the legend of Old Nick on the’iweb and delivers an atmospheric episode that leaves you shivering and grabbing for the remote. A perfect watch with kids, just use a puppet to help them understand “the man behind the legend.”

  4. South Park: “Hell on Earth” In “Hell on Earth,” South Park’s boys, Stan, Kyle, Cartman, and Kenny find themselves in a real-life version of Jiggery Pokery after getting caught by Satan himself. It’s non-stop, action-packed mayhem as they attempt to escape the Halloween underworld. This episode has it all: blood, guts, and priceless lines like “Biggy Smalljams.”

  5. Tom and Jerry: “The Carved Cha-Cha” Tom and Jerry’s “The Carved Cha-Cha” features an eerie old mansion, a stuffy ex-puppet master, and a rather creepy assortment of marionettes. The musical score perfectly captures the Halloween atmosphere, and the chase scenes are as intense as ever. In true Tom and Jerry fashion, the ending is a plot twist that will leave you convinced to never skip stones in the park.

Unleash the fright and laughter this Halloween. Power through the eerie laughter, indulge in some pulpy treats, and memorably enjoy these classic cartoon episodes!

Other Episodes to Consider:

  • “It’s the Great Pumpkin, Charlie Brown!” – A Halloween tale that doesn’t involve supernatural beings or scary escapades, but it manages to convey the essence of the evening.
  • “Halloween with the Addams Family” – A light costume-change, twists, and turns with the famous deadpan Addams Family<|reserved_special_token_260|>
  • Check local listings for special Halloween-themed episodes featuring your favorite contemporary series.

Remember to set an appropriate mood and, most importantly, enjoy the thrills and chills with friends or family! Happy haunting!
