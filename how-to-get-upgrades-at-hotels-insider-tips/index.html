How to Get Upgrades at Hotels: Insider Tips


Are you someone who loves to add a touch of luxury to your hotel stay without breaking the bank? Getting a free upgrade at a hotel can elevate your experience and make your trip even more memorable. While there’s no guaranteed way to secure an upgrade, there are some insider tips and tricks that could increase your chances of enjoying a better room or additional perks during your stay. Here are some savvy strategies to keep in mind the next time you check into a hotel:

1. Timing Is Key

Arriving at the hotel at the right time can significantly impact your chances of receiving an upgrade. Avoid peak check-in times when the front desk is busiest, such as late afternoons or early evenings. Opt for a mid-morning or late evening arrival when the staff may have more time to consider upgrade requests.

2. Join the Loyalty Program

Many hotels offer loyalty programs that reward frequent guests with perks like room upgrades, complimentary breakfast, or late checkout. Signing up for these programs is usually free and can increase your chances of being recognized as a valued guest.

3. Engage in Polite Conversation

A friendly and polite demeanor can go a long way in securing a hotel upgrade. Strike up a conversation with the front desk staff, inquire about their day, and express your excitement about your stay. Building a rapport with the staff may make them more inclined to offer you a complimentary upgrade.

4. Special Occasions

If you’re celebrating a special occasion like a birthday, anniversary, or honeymoon, don’t hesitate to mention it during check-in. Hotels often strive to make these moments extra special for guests and may be more willing to upgrade you as a gesture of goodwill.

5. Utilize Social Media

Before your stay, engage with the hotel’s social media accounts by liking, commenting, or sharing their posts. Some hotels monitor social media activity and may surprise you with an upgrade as a token of appreciation for your online support.

6. Book Directly with the Hotel

Booking your room directly through the hotel’s website or reservation line can sometimes work in your favor. Hotels may prioritize direct bookings over third-party reservations when it comes to upgrades and special requests.

7. Dress the Part

While you don’t need to dress to the nines, presenting yourself neatly and professionally can create a positive impression. Hotels are more likely to upgrade guests who appear respectful and well-presented.

8. Express Your Preferences

If you have specific preferences such as a room with a view, a higher floor, or a king-sized bed, don’t hesitate to communicate this to the hotel staff. While they may not always be able to accommodate your request, expressing your preferences can sometimes lead to a favorable upgrade.

9. Be Flexible

Sometimes, hotels may not have the room availability to offer an upgrade in the category you desire. In such cases, be open to alternative upgrades or perks like access to the executive lounge, complimentary spa services, or dining credits.

10. Tip Generously

While tipping should never be seen as a bribe for an upgrade, a generous tip at check-in or during your stay can show your appreciation for excellent service. It may also leave a positive impression that could work in your favor.

By incorporating these insider tips into your hotel booking strategy, you may increase your chances of snagging that coveted room upgrade and enjoy a more luxurious stay. Remember, while upgrades are never guaranteed, a positive attitude and a bit of strategic planning can certainly work in your favor. Happy travels!
