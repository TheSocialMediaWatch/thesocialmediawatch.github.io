How to Find Time to Read in Your Busy Schedule


In today’s fast-paced world, finding time to read can feel like a daunting task. With the constant juggle of work, family obligations, social commitments, and personal goals, it seems there is little room left for indulging in the pages of a good book. However, reading offers numerous benefits, from expanding knowledge to improving mental health and cognitive function. So, how can we carve out precious time to read in our already-packed schedules?

In this blog post, we’ll explore practical tips, strategies, and mindset shifts to help you find time to read, no matter how busy life may seem.

1. Reframe Your Mindset: Prioritize Reading as a Form of Self-Care

Many of us view reading as a luxury or an activity that must be squeezed in during rare moments of free time. To make reading a consistent part of your life, you need to shift how you perceive it. Rather than thinking of reading as something optional or frivolous, embrace it as a form of self-care. Just like exercising, eating healthy, or meditating, reading nourishes your mind and spirit. It’s not an indulgence—it’s an investment in your well-being.

When you prioritize reading in this way, you’ll be more likely to create space for it. Reframe your thinking from “I don’t have time to read” to “I make time for the things that nourish my mind and help me grow.”

2. Set a Daily Reading Goal

A practical and highly effective way to integrate reading into your daily routine is by setting a specific reading goal. It doesn’t have to be a monumental goal like reading a book a week (though if you’re up for the challenge, go for it!). Start small and realistic. Commit to reading just 10–15 minutes a day.

Setting this goal helps create a habit. Think about how easy it is to scroll through your phone for 10 minutes—why not use that same time to read a chapter or a few pages of a book? Over time, you can increase your goal as you begin to enjoy the routine and see reading as a normal part of your day.

3. Make Use of Short, Available Time Gaps

If you feel like your schedule is completely packed, consider those short pockets of time throughout the day when you aren’t doing anything else. This could be during your commute, waiting in line, or while you’re on a lunch break. Even if these moments only last 5–10 minutes, you can still make progress in your book.

For instance, if you take public transportation, bring a book or e-reader with you. If you’re stuck waiting at an appointment or in line, instead of mindlessly scrolling through social media, pull out your book. These small moments add up, and you’ll be surprised how much you can read when you leverage these brief snippets of time.

4. Utilize Audiobooks During Multitasking Moments

Audiobooks are a game changer for anyone with a busy schedule. If you have an hour-long commute or find yourself doing tasks that don’t require much cognitive effort, such as cleaning, cooking, or walking the dog, why not listen to an audiobook during these moments?

With audiobooks, you can still immerse yourself in literature while going about your daily activities. This allows you to “read” during times when it would otherwise be difficult to sit down and open a physical book. You can listen on your smartphone or use a dedicated audiobook player, and many apps also allow you to adjust playback speed to suit your pace.

5. Incorporate Reading Into Your Morning or Evening Routine

Your morning or evening routine is a great time to incorporate reading. If you’re someone who already has a structured morning routine, try adding a 15-minute reading session as part of your ritual. This could mean waking up 15 minutes earlier to enjoy a quiet time with your book, sipping coffee, and setting a peaceful tone for the day ahead.

Alternatively, if you’re more of a night owl, dedicate a portion of your evening to reading before bed. This will not only help you unwind but also set a calm atmosphere to end your day. Instead of scrolling through your phone, which can disrupt your sleep, choose a good book to help you relax and drift into a restful slumber.

6. Create a Dedicated Reading Space

Sometimes, simply making space for reading can help you make time for it. Consider creating a designated reading nook in your home. It doesn’t have to be elaborate—just a cozy chair or corner with good lighting and a comfy throw blanket could make a big difference. Having a dedicated space for reading not only helps you mentally prepare for reading but also makes the process feel more intentional and enjoyable.

When you walk into that space, you’ll automatically associate it with relaxation and focus, making it easier to immerse yourself in your book.

7. Combine Socializing and Reading

You don’t have to choose between socializing and reading. You can combine the two in a way that’s both enjoyable and productive. For example, joining a book club can help you stay committed to reading while also connecting with others. You can share insights, discuss characters, and deepen your understanding of the material through conversation.

If formal book clubs aren’t your style, consider starting a mini book club with friends or colleagues, where you all read the same book and chat about it during casual get-togethers. Reading can become a social activity, and you’ll have a community of people to motivate you to stick with it.

8. Opt for Shorter Books or Read in Bite-Sized Portions

If you’re struggling to find time to read longer books, try switching to shorter ones or breaking down longer reads into manageable chunks. Many books are around 150–200 pages, which can be completed in a few hours. Choose books that fit your lifestyle and reading speed.

Alternatively, you can read in small bursts. It’s not about how long you read; it’s about consistency. Reading a few pages during each sitting will eventually add up. The key is to create a habit of reading, regardless of how much you read each time.

9. Switch to E-Books for Convenience

Physical books are wonderful, but carrying them around can be a hassle, especially when you’re on the go. Consider switching to e-books or using an e-reader app on your phone. This way, you’ll always have your book with you, whether you’re waiting at a coffee shop, sitting in a doctor’s office, or traveling.

Many e-reader apps also offer customization options such as adjusting font size, changing background color, and even using a built-in dictionary. This makes reading more convenient and tailored to your preferences, which can make it easier to squeeze in more reading time during your day.

10. Use Technology to Your Advantage

In addition to audiobooks and e-readers, there are plenty of other technological tools that can help you make time for reading. Apps like Blinkist offer summaries of books in 15 minutes or less, which is perfect for busy individuals who want to get the key takeaways from a book but don’t have time to read the full version.

Additionally, there are digital reading apps that allow you to download books and read offline, such as Kindle or Google Books. With these, you can access a huge library of books right from your smartphone or tablet, making it easy to read whenever you have a moment.

11. Read What You Enjoy—No Pressure

Lastly, it’s important to remember that reading should be enjoyable, not a task to tick off a list. If you’re forcing yourself to read a genre or a book that doesn’t excite you, it will only feel like a chore, making it harder to find time for it. Choose books that you’re genuinely excited about, whether they’re fiction, self-help, biographies, or business books. When you’re passionate about the content, you’ll naturally make more time for it.

Reading is a personal journey, and there’s no one “right” way to do it. Explore different genres, authors, and formats until you find what truly sparks your interest. The more fun and engaging your reading material, the easier it will be to make time for it.

Conclusion: Start Today

Incorporating reading into your busy schedule doesn’t have to be overwhelming. By using some of the strategies outlined above, you can easily find time to read every day, no matter how packed your life may seem. Start small, build a consistent habit, and remember to enjoy the process. Whether you read for knowledge, relaxation, or inspiration, making time to read will enrich your life in countless ways.

The key is to make reading a priority. You have the power to find time for it, and once you do, you’ll reap the many rewards that come with being a reader. So pick up that book, start your reading habit today, and discover the joy of reading on your terms.
