Upcycled Vintage Book Crafts: Literary DIY Treasures


Are you a book lover who is looking for unique ways to give old books a new lease on life? Look no further than upcycled vintage book crafts! These literary DIY treasures not only allow you to repurpose old books but also create beautiful and functional items that showcase your love for literature. In this blog post, we will explore some innovative and creative ideas for upcycling vintage books.

Book Page Wreath

One of the simplest yet stunning upcycled book crafts is a book page wreath. With just a few basic supplies, you can transform old book pages into a beautiful and eye-catching wreath that adds a touch of literary charm to any space. To make a book page wreath, you will need:

  • Vintage book pages
  • Styrofoam wreath form
  • Hot glue gun
  • Ribbon or twine for hanging

Begin by cutting the book pages into strips of equal width. Then, using a hot glue gun, carefully wrap each strip around the wreath form, securing it in place. Continue this process until the entire wreath form is covered. Finally, attach a ribbon or twine to the back of the wreath for hanging. Hang your book page wreath on a door, wall, or above a fireplace for a unique and literary touch.

Book Page Coasters

If you’re looking for a practical yet stylish way to protect your furniture, why not make book page coasters? These coasters not only serve their purpose but also add a touch of vintage charm to your living space. To make book page coasters, gather the following materials:

  • Vintage book pages
  • Clear adhesive contact paper
  • Scissors or craft knife
  • Mod Podge or clear sealant

Start by cutting the book pages into square or circular shapes, depending on your preference. Next, cut squares of adhesive contact paper slightly larger than each book page. Carefully peel off the backing of the contact paper and place a book page on top, ensuring there are no wrinkles or air bubbles. Trim any excess contact paper. Finally, apply a layer of Mod Podge or clear sealant to protect the book page and give it a glossy finish. Once dry, your book page coasters are ready to use!

Book Planter

For the green thumbs and book lovers out there, a book planter is the perfect way to combine your passions. Transform an old hardcover book into a unique and whimsical planter that will add a touch of nature to any room. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Vintage hardcover book
  • Craft knife
  • Plastic planter or container
  • Potting soil
  • Small plant or succulent

To create a book planter, start by selecting a vintage hardcover book that you no longer need. Open the book to the first few pages and use a craft knife to carefully cut out a rectangular shape, leaving a border around the edges. Remove the cut pages to create a hollow space. Line the hollow space with a plastic planter or container to prevent water damage. Fill the planter with potting soil and plant a small plant or succulent of your choice. Place your book planter on a windowsill or shelf and watch as your literary DIY treasure grows.

Conclusion

Upcycled vintage book crafts offer a creative and eco-friendly way to breathe new life into old books. From book page wreaths to book page coasters and book planters, there are endless possibilities for turning your beloved books into unique and functional pieces of art. So, grab some vintage books and let your imagination run wild as you embark on your literary DIY adventure. Happy crafting!
