Strategies for Online Educators: Building Connections with Students


online educators, especially during these unprecedented times, play a pivotal role in shaping the future by creating a conducive and engaging environment for learning. In the digital realm where virtual classrooms have become the new normal, nurturing connections with students assumes even more significance. The following strategies can come in handy for educators to build stronger bonds with learners and foster a positive learning environment.

  1. Be approachable and foster a sense of community

One of the most crucial aspects of being an effective educator is to create an environment where students feel valued and respected. Even in the digital medium, the look and feel of a welcoming classroom can be created. Make sure your virtual background reflects a comforting and informal atmosphere, and always introduce yourself with a warm smile. Encourage students to present their queries and opinions calmly, without hesitation. Be inclusive and make it amply clear that you value student participation.

  1. Get to know students at a personal level

Building connections means knowing your students beyond their academic strengths and achievements. Taking a genuine interest in their hobbies, interests, and life events is a powerful way to convey empathy and show them you care. Create time slots where students can talk about anything they like, without any academic agenda. It helps to humanize the teaching-learning relationship and enhances a sense of camaraderie.

  1. Maintain consistent communication

Staying connected regularly through emails, forums, or messaging platforms can significantly reduce learning barriers and keep students on track. As an educator, listen to their problems and provide timely solutions. If you see a student struggling with a particular topic or concept, reach out and offer help. Consistent communication can transform an abstract relationship into a supportive and reliable bond.

  1. Utilize formative assessments effectively

Incorporating formative assessments allows you to gauge students’ learning progress and tailor your teaching methods accordingly. It not only helps improve the quality of instruction but also strengthens student-teacher bonding. Whether it is through quizzes, surveys or informal discussions, utilizing formative assessments effectively provides valuable insights and enables an educator to address students’ learning needs.

  1. Continuously improve your teaching methods

Embrace feedback and continuously enhance your teaching methodologies. Adapt new teaching techniques, incorporate technology and leverage the power of digital platforms. Be open to experimentation, and don’t shy away from taking calculated risks. Show your students that you genuinely strive to deliver the best learning outcomes.

  1. Finally, bring positivity to every interaction

Your students’ attitude towards learning greatly depends on the type of rapport you build with them. Stay positive and display enthusiasm towards your subject. Use positive affirmations and bring a can-do attitude to every dialogue. Encouragement and praise, in equal measure, can go a long way in fostering stronger connections.

In conclusion, building relationships with students is not only fundamental for effective teaching but also crucial for student success. Digital classrooms require intentional efforts to establish meaningful connections, and the strategies above provide a starting point. Remember, good teaching goes beyond imparting knowledge; it’s about creating a supportive and engaging learning environment that lasts a lifetime.
