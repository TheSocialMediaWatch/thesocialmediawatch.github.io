Why Adulting Feels Like a Comedy Show (And How to Survive It)


Adulting. If you’ve ever found yourself standing in the middle of a grocery store, unsure of what to pick between whole wheat bread and white bread, you’re not alone. Or maybe you’ve spent an hour staring at your phone screen trying to figure out if you can afford rent or need to consider downsizing your lifestyle (again). The reality is that adulting often feels like a never-ending sitcom. There’s a chaotic mix of stress, humor, failure, and moments of triumph, and it seems like the universe is watching, waiting for your next big screw-up. In fact, it’s almost like someone wrote your life script with the same absurdities of a comedy show.

This post dives deep into why adulting feels like an ongoing comedy show and, more importantly, how to survive it without losing your mind. Buckle up, it’s going to be a hilarious (and maybe slightly tearful) ride.

1. The “I Have No Idea What I’m Doing” Phase

When you first enter the world of adulthood, there’s this expectation that you should have it all together. You should know how to pay taxes, cook a perfect steak, balance your budget, and still have time to make it to the gym. But let’s face it: we’re all winging it.

You might feel like you’re auditioning for a role in an absurd sitcom where the main character, despite their best efforts, constantly ends up in situations they have no business being in. You might find yourself at the DMV trying to understand why you need both proof of residency and your social security number to change your address. Or you’re watching YouTube tutorials on how to unclog your toilet because, surprise, you weren’t trained for this level of responsibility.

The beauty of this phase, though, is that you’re not alone. Everyone feels like they’re just making it up as they go along. The only difference is that in a comedy show, the protagonist eventually figures it out. In real life, sometimes you just hope no one notices that you’re Googling “How to change a flat tire.”

2. Bills, Bills, Bills

Adulting involves a magical time when you realize just how much of your paycheck disappears into the abyss of bills. Rent, utilities, phone plans, and of course, those mysterious subscription services you signed up for three years ago and forgot about. The first time you sit down to look at your budget is like the moment in a comedy show when the character opens their bank account to realize they have only $3.72 left after spending the entire month eating ramen noodles and taking public transit.

And don’t even get me started on student loans. Those loan repayment plans feel like they’re straight out of a slapstick comedy where you keep dodging the giant pie of financial responsibility, only for it to land right in your face.

But here’s the kicker: there’s humor in this madness too. The absurdity of it all. You’ve been told your entire life that adulthood means financial independence, but now you’re looking for creative ways to survive off instant noodles and coffee. And yet, in the grand scheme of things, we’re all just trying to figure out how to make sure we don’t end up living with our parents forever.

3. The Realization That Cooking Isn’t Always Fun

As a kid, you probably dreamed of the day when you could eat whatever you wanted, whenever you wanted. No more asking for permission, no more vegetables (or so you thought). But when you finally step into the kitchen as an adult, it hits you: cooking is actually a lot of work.

The first time you try to make a meal from scratch, you might find yourself in a hilarious situation where your rice turns into a sticky mess, your chicken is mysteriously undercooked, and the kitchen looks like a crime scene of splattered sauce. You might even joke with your friends about how, as a kid, you thought cooking was all fun and games, but now it’s just a struggle for survival.

The thing is, cooking doesn’t have to be a grand, complicated affair. Embrace the fact that your culinary skills might involve burning toast or ruining the first batch of cookies. Eventually, you’ll learn how to make a quick, delicious meal without needing a five-star recipe, and that’s when you truly “adult.”

4. Why Social Media Feels Like a Joke

Social media is, in many ways, the perfect backdrop for adulting comedy. You scroll through your feed and see everyone’s “perfect” lives, their vacations, their work successes, and their beautiful homes. And there you are, in your sweatpants, eating cereal at 10 PM because you forgot to make dinner (again).

There’s a strange disconnect between what people post and what’s happening behind the scenes. You see someone share their engagement photos, but you didn’t see them have the meltdown the night before, wondering whether they should send that “I love you” text or if it would be too soon. Or you hear about someone buying their first home, but you didn’t witness their 20-minute internal struggle over whether they should splurge on the new couch or save for retirement.

It’s easy to get caught up in the comparison game, but the truth is, social media is just a highlight reel. And that reel, much like a sitcom, is full of moments that are exaggerated for entertainment. The behind-the-scenes moments—whether it’s crying over spilled milk or accidentally sending a text to the wrong person—are the parts that make adulting so ridiculously relatable.

5. Relationships Are a Comedy of Errors

Adulting also means navigating relationships, whether it’s with family, friends, or a romantic partner. And let’s be real—relationships can be a series of miscommunications and awkward moments. When you’re in a new relationship, it might feel like every date is a scene from a romantic comedy, with some moments that are ridiculously sweet and others that are just plain embarrassing.

You might say something completely nonsensical, like explaining the plot of a movie you’ve seen 50 times but still can’t remember the ending to, or forgetting important dates like anniversaries or birthdays. And yet, these awkward moments are often the foundation of strong relationships. The weirdness and unpredictability of it all is what makes life interesting and keeps the comedy alive.

6. The Misadventures of Job Hunting and Career Development

Entering the job market is another milestone in adulting that feels like a comedy sketch gone wrong. It’s a series of trial and error, where you send out countless resumes, receive rejections, and wonder why no one wants to hire you even though you have a college degree and an impressive list of internships. The first time you get a job offer, you might celebrate like you’ve just won the lottery, only to find out that your new boss sends 100 emails a day, and your office chair is mysteriously uncomfortable.

And then there’s the awkwardness of the first day at your new job. You might show up early, only to find out the office is still locked. Or you might accidentally wear the wrong color shirt to a company meeting and feel like the odd one out. All of these moments are the punchlines of the adulting comedy show, where you’re constantly improvising your way through new situations.

7. How to Survive the Comedy Show of Adulting

Surviving adulting is not about trying to be perfect—it’s about embracing the ridiculousness of it all. Here are a few tips on how to navigate the chaos without losing your sense of humor:

1. Embrace the Chaos

Life is messy. Adulting is unpredictable. And sometimes, the best thing you can do is laugh at the absurdity of it all. When you accidentally spill coffee all over your favorite shirt or miss an important deadline, instead of stressing out, try to find humor in the situation. Remember, everyone is going through it.

2. Seek Support

You don’t have to go through this journey alone. Whether it’s friends, family, or even online communities, sharing your struggles and laughing about them with others can make adulting feel a little less overwhelming. Plus, you’ll likely find that others are dealing with the same challenges.

3. Give Yourself Grace

No one has it all figured out, no matter how much it may seem like they do. Give yourself grace and room to make mistakes. The most important part of adulting is learning and growing. Don’t expect perfection—just aim for progress.

4. Find Balance

While adulting can feel like an endless series of responsibilities and tasks, it’s important to find balance. Schedule time for self-care, hobbies, and moments of fun. A life without laughter is like a sitcom without punchlines.

5. Laugh at the Failures

The mess-ups, the embarrassing moments, the mishaps—they’re all part of the journey. Laugh at them. The moments you think are failures will eventually turn into funny stories you tell over dinner. So why not enjoy the ride while it lasts?

Conclusion: Adulting as a Comedy Show

At the end of the day, adulting doesn’t have to be as serious as it sometimes feels. Yes, there are moments of responsibility, stress, and hard work, but there are also plenty of moments of humor, absurdity, and unexpected joy. The key is to embrace the comedy of it all. Life is a series of trials, errors, and successes. If you can laugh at the mistakes and appreciate the progress, you’re doing adulting right.

So, when you find yourself in a moment of utter chaos—whether you’re struggling to cook dinner, trying to pay your bills, or navigating an awkward situation—just remember: this is the comedy show of life, and you’re the star. Keep laughing, keep learning, and most importantly, keep surviving.
