Survival Knots: Essential Techniques for Every Situation


When it comes to survival situations, having the right knowledge and skills can mean the difference between life and death. One crucial skill that every adventurer, hiker, or outdoor enthusiast should have in their arsenal is the ability to tie various survival knots. These knots can be incredibly useful in a wide range of situations, from building shelters and securing gear to creating traps and performing first aid.

The Bowline Knot

Let’s start with one of the most versatile and essential survival knots: the bowline knot. This knot creates a secure loop that won’t slip or tighten under pressure. It’s perfect for creating a fixed loop at the end of a rope or for securing items such as tents or tarps.

To tie a bowline knot, follow these steps:

  1. Create a small loop near the end of the rope, with the free end passing over the standing part of the rope.
  2. Pass the free end through the loop.
  3. Bring the free end around the standing part of the rope and back down through the loop.
  4. Tighten the knot by pulling the standing part of the rope, ensuring that the loop remains fixed.

The Clove Hitch

Another essential knot to have in your survival toolkit is the clove hitch. This knot is quick to tie and untie, making it ideal for securing items to poles or trees. It’s commonly used in camping, climbing, and boating scenarios.

To tie a clove hitch, follow these steps:

  1. Pass the rope around the pole or object you want to secure.
  2. Cross the working end of the rope over the standing part.
  3. Cross the working end over the standing part again, creating an X shape.
  4. Pass the working end under the standing part and through the X shape.
  5. Tighten the knot by pulling both ends of the rope.

The Square Knot

The square knot, also known as the reef knot, is a basic knot that is used to join two ropes together. It’s an essential knot for building shelters, creating clotheslines, or securing bundles of firewood.

To tie a square knot, follow these steps:

  1. Take two ropes and lay them parallel to each other.
  2. Cross the right rope over the left rope.
  3. Bring the right rope under the left rope and pass it through the loop created.
  4. Cross the left rope over the right rope.
  5. Bring the left rope under the right rope and pass it through the loop created.
  6. Tighten the knot by pulling both ends of the rope simultaneously.

The Taut-Line Hitch

The taut-line hitch is a versatile knot that allows you to easily adjust the tension of a line, making it perfect for securing tents or tarps. It’s a great knot to know when you need to secure a line in a way that can be easily adjusted as needed.

To tie a taut-line hitch, follow these steps:

  1. Wrap the rope around the object you want to secure.
  2. Cross the working end of the rope over the standing part.
  3. Bring the working end under the standing part and through the loop created.
  4. Wrap the working end around the standing part, moving away from the object.
  5. Pass the working end through the loop again.
  6. Tighten the knot by sliding it up or down the standing part of the rope.

These are just a few examples of the essential survival knots that can be incredibly useful in various situations. Remember, practice makes perfect, so take the time to learn and practice these knots until you can tie them with ease. The more familiar you become with these knots, the more confident you’ll feel in your ability to handle unexpected situations in the great outdoors.

Stay safe, and happy knot tying!
