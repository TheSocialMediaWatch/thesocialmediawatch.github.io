If You're a Visual Learner, Here's How to Get The Most Out of Your Language Studies


Are you a visual learner? Do you find it easier to understand and remember information when it’s presented visually? Well, you’re not alone! Many people are visual learners who might struggle with traditional language learning methods. But don’t worry – there are plenty of ways to make language studies more enjoyable and effective for you.

1. Use Visual Aids

Instructions and content presented visually can make all the difference when it comes to learning a new language. Here are some visual aids you can use to enhance your language learning experience:

Flashcards

Flashcards are perfect for visual learners because they allow you to see words and their meanings, along with any necessary pronunciation guides or example sentences. Customize your flashcards with vibrant colors, pictures, and patterns to make them even more memorable.

Vocabulary Walls

Creating a vocabulary wall with words, phrases, or idioms you’re learning can be a great way to keep them at the top of your mind. You can even use colored index cards, or create flashcards with images to make your wall visually engaging.

Infographics

Language learning infographics are an excellent resource to help you visualize grammar rules, verb conjugations, and more. You can find them online or create your own using tools like Canva or Piktochart.

2. Watch Videos

Watching videos of native speakers speaking the language you’re learning can be incredibly helpful for your understanding of pronunciation, tone, and pacing. It’s also engaging and entertaining! Some helpful resources include:

YouTube

YouTube is a goldmine for language learners. Channels like FluentU, News in Slow French, and many others offer video content that exposes you to native speakers and various aspects of the language.

Language Learning Platforms

Subscription-based platforms like Rosetta Stone, Babbel, and Duolingo offer video lessons that help you visualize various aspects of the language and practice pronunciation.

Podcasts

Listening to podcasts can be a great way to immerse yourself in the language visually, while also sharpening your listening skills. Look for podcasts that target beginners or include cultural information so you don’t get lost.

3. Use Apps

Language learning apps are designed with visual learners in mind. Here are some popular choices:

Memrise

Memrise uses pictures and videos to teach you new words, phrases, and even pronunciation. The spaced repetition system ensures that you will be able to remember the information.

HelloTalk

HelloTalk allows you to connect with native speakers from around the globe. You can practice speaking through text, voice, or video chat, and even request specific lessons or phrases. The platform’s visual interface makes it easy to keep track of your conversations and progress.

Anki

Anki is a flashcard app that uses a spaced repetition system to help you retain information. With Anki’s support for images and audio, it allows you to create custom flashcards to learn any language visually.

4. Create Your Language Learning Visual Journal

Keeping a visual journal dedicated to your language studies is a great way to make your learning process even more enjoyable. Some elements to include are:

  • Vocabulary and grammar charts
  • Custom flashcards with pictures and example sentences
  • Notes from video lessons, podcasts, or conversations
  • Cultural tidbits and insights

This visual journal can evolve over time and become a valuable resource as you progress in your language learning journey.

5. Don’t Forget the Power of Drawings

Drawings can help to visualize abstract concepts, making them more accessible to understand. When learning a new language, consider the following drawing techniques:

  • Word Associations: Draw a picture that represents the word and include other related vocabulary words.
  • Mnemonic Devices: Create humorous or memorable characters to help associate with difficult-to-remember phrases.
  • Storytelling: Draw a series of frames telling a story in the language you are learning, which could include dialogue balloons with key phrases to translate.

Finally, remember to practice patience and persistence, because learning a new language is a journey. By incorporating visual aids and strategies into your study routine, you will find the process becomes more enjoyable and engaging. Cheers to the world of endless possibilities that awaits you!
