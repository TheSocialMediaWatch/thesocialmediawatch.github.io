The Best Hunting Accessories You Didn't Know You Needed


When it comes to hunting, the right gear can make all the difference between a successful trip and a missed opportunity. While most hunters are familiar with the basics like rifles, scopes, and camo, there are countless accessories that can elevate your experience. These often overlooked items can improve your comfort, enhance your performance, and even help you stay safe. In this post, we’ll explore some of the best hunting accessories you might not even know you needed, but once you try them, you’ll wonder how you ever hunted without them.

1. Rangefinders: Precision in Every Shot

A rangefinder is one of those accessories that often gets overlooked by casual hunters, but it’s an invaluable tool for any serious shooter. Understanding the exact distance to your target allows for much more accurate shots, especially at longer ranges. These devices use lasers to measure the distance between you and the target, ensuring that your scope adjustments are precise.

Not all rangefinders are created equal. Some models feature ballistic calculators that account for wind, angle, and even temperature, providing you with real-time adjustments for more accurate shooting. The most advanced models can sync with your smartphone to give you further data analysis, which can be a game-changer during long-range hunts.

A rangefinder is a must-have accessory for hunters aiming to maximize accuracy, particularly when hunting large game or in open fields where distances can be difficult to gauge.

2. Trail Cameras: Capture Every Moment

For hunters who want to get an edge over their game, trail cameras are a game-changer. These cameras are designed to be set up along well-trodden animal paths or near feeding grounds, allowing hunters to monitor wildlife activity 24/7. This gives you a clear idea of when and where animals are most active, helping you plan your hunt more effectively.

Modern trail cameras are equipped with infrared sensors, motion detection, and high-definition video, enabling you to capture quality images and videos even at night. Many cameras can now send real-time updates to your smartphone, so you can monitor activity without needing to check the camera manually. This can save you time and reduce your chances of spooking the game.

Trail cameras are particularly useful for deer hunters, but they can be helpful for tracking almost any type of game. They allow you to gather valuable intel on your prey’s habits, leading to more successful hunts.

3. Hunting Knives: Precision and Durability

A good hunting knife is one of the most essential tools for any hunter. Whether you need to field dress a deer or cut through tough brush, having a reliable, sharp knife can make all the difference. But not all knives are made equal—what you need is a knife that combines sharpness, strength, and versatility.

Look for a blade that’s made from high-carbon steel, which holds an edge longer and is easier to sharpen in the field. A full-tang design is also preferred for maximum strength. Many hunters choose folding knives for convenience, but a fixed-blade knife offers unmatched durability and performance.

A hunting knife isn’t just about skinning and cleaning; it can also come in handy for a variety of tasks, such as cutting rope, preparing food, or even as a self-defense tool. A high-quality hunting knife can last for generations, becoming a trusted companion on every hunting trip.

4. Hunting Boots: Comfort and Protection

While hunting may involve a lot of sitting and waiting, it often requires a lot of walking as well. Whether you’re trekking through rough terrain, crossing streams, or walking miles in pursuit of game, your feet need to be well taken care of. The right pair of hunting boots can prevent blisters, provide insulation, and offer support during long hours in the field.

When choosing hunting boots, consider the weather and terrain conditions you’ll be facing. Waterproof boots are essential for wet environments, while insulated boots are crucial for cold weather hunts. For warmer conditions, lightweight and breathable boots will keep you comfortable without overheating.

Look for boots that provide ankle support to prevent injuries while trekking through uneven terrain. A durable sole with good traction is important to ensure stability when climbing or walking over slippery surfaces.

5. Game Calls: Mastering the Art of Attraction

A game call is an essential tool for hunters looking to attract specific animals to their location. These devices mimic the sounds of animals, whether it’s the call of a deer, the gobble of a turkey, or the howl of a predator. Game calls can be used to lure animals into range, confuse their senses, or even help you communicate with other hunters.

There are a variety of game calls available for different species, and some are designed to mimic specific calls that animals respond to in particular seasons. A good call can be the difference between an empty day in the woods and a successful hunt.

The best game calls are durable, easy to use, and can produce authentic sounds that mimic real animal calls. Practicing with your call before you go out hunting is crucial; the more realistic your imitation, the better your chances of attracting the game.

6. Scent Control Products: Stay Invisible to Your Prey

Deer and other game animals have an extraordinary sense of smell, which means even the slightest trace of human scent can alert them to your presence. Scent control products are an essential accessory for any hunter who wants to stay undetected.

There are several types of scent control products, from scent-blocking clothing to sprays and wipes designed to neutralize human odors. Using these products in combination with scent-free soaps and deodorants can make a significant difference in your ability to approach game without being detected.

Additionally, scent control products like scent drags or scent wafers can help you create an artificial trail, leading your prey directly to your location. These products are especially useful for deer hunters, but they can also be helpful for other types of hunting.

7. Hunting Chairs: Comfort on Long Hunts

Spending hours sitting in a tree stand or in a blind can be grueling if you don’t have the right seating. Hunting chairs are specifically designed to provide comfort and support during long periods of sitting, ensuring that you stay alert and ready to take a shot when the moment arises.

Look for a chair that offers adjustable height, padded seats, and backrests for maximum comfort. Many modern hunting chairs are lightweight and foldable, making them easy to carry into the field. Some models even come with built-in cup holders and storage pouches, allowing you to keep your essentials close by.

A comfortable chair can be the difference between a painful, restless hunt and a successful, enjoyable experience. It’s worth investing in a high-quality chair to ensure that you remain comfortable and focused during your hunt.

8. Portable Power Banks: Stay Charged in the Wilderness

In today’s tech-driven world, staying connected or having access to a GPS system during a hunt can be invaluable. A portable power bank ensures that your devices stay charged, whether it’s your smartphone, GPS unit, or camera. There’s nothing worse than having your devices run out of power in the middle of a hunt, leaving you without the ability to navigate or track your prey.

When choosing a power bank, look for one that is rugged and weatherproof, as hunting trips often involve exposure to the elements. The best power banks have a high mAh rating, which means they can charge your devices multiple times before needing a recharge themselves.

Having a portable power bank gives you peace of mind, knowing that you’re always prepared for whatever comes your way during a hunt.

9. Headlamps and Flashlights: Lighting the Way

Whether you’re trekking through the woods before dawn or tracking your game into the evening, having reliable lighting is essential. A headlamp or flashlight can help you navigate in the dark, light your path, and even field dress your kill once the sun sets.

Headlamps offer the advantage of keeping your hands free, allowing you to work or move about with ease while still having sufficient lighting. Many modern headlamps come with adjustable brightness settings and red or green light options, which are less likely to spook animals.

Flashlights, on the other hand, are perfect for illuminating larger areas or for when you need to focus on a specific area. Look for flashlights that are waterproof and shock-resistant, as these qualities are essential when hunting in rugged environments.

10. Multi-tools: Versatility in the Field

A multi-tool is a must-have accessory for any hunter. These compact, all-in-one devices combine several tools into one, including pliers, knives, screwdrivers, and other essential implements. Whether you need to fix your equipment, cut rope, or perform other field tasks, a multi-tool can save you from carrying a heavy toolbox.

Choose a multi-tool that is durable, lightweight, and easy to use. Some models even feature specialized tools like bone saws or fish scalers, which can come in handy during specific hunts. A high-quality multi-tool ensures that you’re prepared for almost any situation, from equipment malfunctions to survival scenarios.

Final Thoughts

When it comes to hunting, having the right gear can make all the difference. While basic equipment like rifles and camo are essential, many of the best hunting accessories go unnoticed. By investing in tools like rangefinders, trail cameras, and scent control products, you can enhance your hunting experience, improve your accuracy, and stay safe in the field. Whether you’re a seasoned hunter or a beginner, these accessories are game-changers that will take your skills to the next level. So, don’t overlook the smaller tools—sometimes, it’s the little things that make the biggest impact.
