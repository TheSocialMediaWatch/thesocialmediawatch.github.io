How to Make Your Own Homemade Tortillas


Making your own homemade tortillas is a rewarding experience that brings the authentic taste and texture of traditional Mexican cuisine right to your kitchen. Whether you’re preparing tacos, burritos, quesadillas, or just want a soft, warm tortilla to accompany a meal, homemade tortillas can take your dishes to a whole new level. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of making soft, pliable tortillas from scratch. It’s easier than you might think and will undoubtedly elevate your cooking game.

Ingredients You Will Need

To make delicious homemade tortillas, you only need a handful of simple ingredients:

  • 2 cups of all-purpose flour – This will serve as the base for your tortilla dough.
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt – Salt is essential for enhancing the flavor of the tortillas.
  • 1 tablespoon of baking powder – Baking powder will help the tortillas puff up slightly, creating a softer texture.
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil – This will contribute to the softness and texture of your tortillas.
  • 3/4 cup of warm water – The water helps to bring the dough together and provides the necessary moisture.

These ingredients are enough to make about 8 to 10 tortillas, but you can easily adjust the quantities depending on how many people you’re serving.

Tools You Will Need

While the ingredients are simple, you’ll need a few basic tools to prepare the dough and cook the tortillas:

  • Mixing bowl – A large bowl to combine all your ingredients.
  • Rolling pin – A rolling pin will help you roll out the dough into thin, even circles.
  • Cast-iron skillet or griddle – A flat, heavy pan is perfect for cooking your tortillas, as it ensures even heating and a beautiful char.
  • Kitchen towel – This is used to keep the cooked tortillas warm and soft while you finish cooking the rest.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Combine the Dry Ingredients

In your mixing bowl, start by adding the 2 cups of flour. Sprinkle in the salt and baking powder, then whisk the dry ingredients together to ensure they’re evenly distributed. This step is important because it ensures that the baking powder is evenly spread throughout the flour, which helps each tortilla rise evenly.

Step 2: Add the Oil

Next, add 1/4 cup of vegetable oil to the flour mixture. Use your hands or a fork to work the oil into the flour until it resembles a coarse meal or breadcrumb texture. This step ensures that the tortillas will have a nice, soft texture once cooked.

Step 3: Gradually Add the Water

Slowly pour in the warm water, a little at a time, stirring the dough as you go. The warm water helps to activate the flour and makes it easier to form the dough. You may not need all the water, or you may need a little more, so add it gradually until the dough comes together into a ball. The dough should be soft, but not too sticky. If it’s too sticky, add a small amount of flour; if it’s too dry, add a bit more water.

Step 4: Knead the Dough

Once the dough has come together, turn it out onto a clean, lightly floured surface. Knead the dough for about 4 to 5 minutes, until it’s smooth and elastic. Don’t rush this step—kneading helps to activate the gluten in the flour, which gives your tortillas structure and chew.

Step 5: Let the Dough Rest

After kneading, form the dough into a ball and cover it with a damp kitchen towel. Let the dough rest for at least 20 to 30 minutes. This resting period allows the gluten to relax, which will make it easier to roll out the dough and help prevent the tortillas from shrinking when cooked.

Step 6: Divide the Dough into Balls

Once the dough has rested, divide it into small, even-sized portions. Typically, you’ll want to aim for balls that are about the size of a golf ball, but you can make them larger or smaller depending on the size of tortilla you want.

Step 7: Roll Out the Dough

Take one of the dough balls and flatten it slightly with your fingers to form a small disc. Using a rolling pin, roll the dough out into a thin circle about 6 to 8 inches in diameter. Try to roll it as evenly as possible, but don’t worry if your tortilla isn’t a perfect circle—it’s the taste that matters most!

If the dough sticks to the surface or rolling pin, sprinkle a small amount of flour on the surface and continue rolling. Just be cautious not to use too much flour, as it can affect the texture of your tortillas.

Step 8: Heat the Skillet

While you’re rolling out the dough, heat a cast-iron skillet or griddle over medium-high heat. You want it to be very hot but not smoking. A well-heated skillet will ensure that the tortillas cook quickly and evenly.

Step 9: Cook the Tortillas

Once the skillet is hot, carefully place the rolled-out tortilla into the pan. Let it cook for about 30 seconds to 1 minute, or until you start to see bubbles forming on the surface. Using tongs or a spatula, flip the tortilla over and cook the other side for another 30 seconds to 1 minute. The tortilla should develop light golden brown spots on both sides.

Don’t overcook the tortillas—if you cook them too long, they can become hard and crunchy. The goal is to achieve a soft, pliable texture with just a little bit of golden brown color.

Step 10: Keep the Tortillas Warm

Once the tortilla is cooked, remove it from the skillet and place it on a clean kitchen towel. Fold the towel over the tortilla to keep it warm and soft. Repeat this process with the remaining dough balls, stacking the cooked tortillas in the towel.

Tips for Perfect Homemade Tortillas

  • Don’t skip the resting period: Allowing the dough to rest is crucial for soft tortillas. It helps the gluten relax, making it easier to roll out the dough and creating a more tender result.

  • Roll evenly: Try to roll your tortillas as evenly as possible to ensure that they cook uniformly. If they are too thick in spots, they won’t cook as well, and if they are too thin in places, they may tear.

  • Control the heat: Make sure your skillet is hot before adding the tortilla. If the heat is too low, the tortilla will take too long to cook and may become tough. If the heat is too high, the tortillas may burn before they fully cook through.

  • Use warm water: Warm water helps to activate the flour and makes it easier to form a smooth dough. It also allows the dough to absorb the water more efficiently.

  • Experiment with flavor: Once you’ve mastered the basic tortilla recipe, consider adding flavors or variations. For instance, try using whole wheat flour for a nuttier taste, or incorporate herbs like cilantro or garlic for a savory twist.

How to Store Homemade Tortillas

If you happen to have any leftover tortillas (though they’re so delicious, they probably won’t last long!), you can store them for later use. To store homemade tortillas, allow them to cool completely, then stack them and wrap them tightly in plastic wrap or foil. You can keep them in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or freeze them for up to 3 months. If freezing, separate each tortilla with parchment paper to prevent them from sticking together.

When you’re ready to enjoy them, simply reheat the tortillas on a hot skillet for a few seconds on each side.

Conclusion

Homemade tortillas are an easy and satisfying way to add authentic flavor to your meals. With just a few simple ingredients and some time spent perfecting your technique, you can enjoy soft, fresh tortillas that are a far cry from store-bought versions. Whether you’re making tacos for Taco Tuesday or preparing a batch of homemade burritos, the effort you put into making your own tortillas will undoubtedly be worth it.

So, next time you’re in the kitchen, give homemade tortillas a try. You’ll be amazed at how simple it is to make your own, and your taste buds will thank you for it.
