Beats That Bite: How to Use Music to BOOST Your Workout


Music has the power to elevate our moods, evoke emotions and heighten our experiences. But did you know it can also be harnessed to enhance your workouts? In this article, we will delve into the ways music can kick your fitness routine into high gear and offer tips on how to find the perfect playlist for your workout.

First, let’s explore the scientific reasons behind this phenomenon. Music stimulates the brain regions connected to motivation and movement, causing us to exercise longer and harder. Studies have shown that people who exercise while listening to music tend to feel less exhausted and exert more effort than those who work out in silence.

Furthermore, music can help distract us from discomfort during workouts, create a sense of rhythm for coordination exercises, and even improve our posture. Additionally, listening to fast-paced music during cardio exercises can increase our heart rate, making the workout feel more challenging.

So, now you know the power of music, how do you create the perfect workout playlist? Here are a few guidelines to get you started:

  1. Music Genres: Experiment with different genres to find the one that works best for you. For many, fast-paced pop, rock, or hip-hop may pump up the adrenaline. Others may thrive on the steady rhythm of jazz or classical compositions. Don’t be afraid to explore new sounds.

  2. Beat Per Minute (BPM): BPM is an essential factor to consider in your workout music. Generally, a higher BPM suggests faster-paced music, which enhances cardio workouts. For a more intense aerobic session, songs with a BPM of over 120 might be the way to go. Conversely, lower BPM music may be better for strength-building and yoga routines.

  3. Song Length: Shorter songs are ideal for quick bursts of activity, such as HIIT workouts. On the flip side, longer tracks make great background music for extended stretches, runs, or any exercise that allows for continuous motion without needing to change tracks frequently.

  4. Emotional Connection: Choose songs that you find invigorating or uplifting. Music that resonates with your feelings and personal experiences can have a more profound impact on your workouts.

  5. Diversity: While it’s tempting to fill a playlist with one artist or genre, you’ll find it more stimulating to incorporate a variety of artists, genres, and sounds, catering to differing moods and workout styles.

  6. Personalization: Since everyone’s unique preferences and goals differ, it’s wise to create a personalized playlist. You’d likely have a different playlist for running, strength training, or even cycling. Be flexible in switching playlists when you change up your workout routines.

  7. Progression: As you grow more confident in your workouts, upgrade your music progression, too. This may involve trying new genres or increasing the BPMs in your tracks.

Lastly, don’t forget the importance of saving your playlist on a mobile device so you have access to it during your workouts. There’s nothing more frustrating than attempting to work out with music only to be met with an unresponsive device or playback issues.

In conclusion, music is a vital component of an effective workout. Its transcendent power to inspire, motivate, and engage can lift you to greater heights in your fitness pursuits. To make the most of this dynamic impact, tailor your playlist to your preferences, workout types, and the unique blend of energy and focus needed for each session. Remember, the right music can be the difference between an ordinary workout and a victorious one.

Now get out there and show those beats who’s boss! Enjoy your high-intensity workouts accompanied by your personalized music-laden arsenal. Cheers to a more energized, engaging, and ultimately, successful workout journey.
