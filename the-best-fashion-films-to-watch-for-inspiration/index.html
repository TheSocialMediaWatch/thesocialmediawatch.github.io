The Best Fashion Films to Watch for Inspiration


Fashion films have become an essential part of the fashion industry, offering a unique and visually captivating way to showcase clothing, style, and the creative vision behind fashion collections. These films not only entertain but also provide inspiration for designers, stylists, and fashion enthusiasts alike. If you’re looking for a dose of fashion inspiration, here are some of the best fashion films that are worth watching.

1. “The Devil Wears Prada” (2006)

Based on the best-selling novel by Lauren Weisberger, “The Devil Wears Prada” takes you behind the scenes of the cutthroat fashion industry. This film follows the story of Andy Sachs, played by Anne Hathaway, as she navigates her way through the demanding world of fashion journalism under the formidable editor-in-chief, Miranda Priestly, portrayed by Meryl Streep. The film offers a glamorous glimpse into the world of high fashion and the challenges faced by those striving to make their mark in the industry.

2. “Coco Before Chanel” (2009)

For those seeking inspiration from the iconic designer herself, “Coco Before Chanel” is a must-watch. This biographical drama delves into the early life of Gabrielle “Coco” Chanel, played by Audrey Tautou, and her journey to becoming one of the most influential figures in the history of fashion. The film beautifully captures the essence of Chanel’s timeless style and her determination to challenge societal norms through her revolutionary designs.

3. “Phantom Thread” (2017)

Set in the 1950s London fashion scene, “Phantom Thread” follows the life of renowned dressmaker Reynolds Woodcock, played by Daniel Day-Lewis. Directed by Paul Thomas Anderson, this film explores the intricate relationship between Woodcock and his muse, Alma, portrayed by Vicky Krieps. The exquisite costumes and attention to detail in the film provide a masterclass in craftsmanship and the complexities of the creative process.

4. “The September Issue” (2009)

“The September Issue” offers a fascinating glimpse into the making of the highly anticipated September issue of Vogue magazine, known for its influence on the fashion industry. Directed by R.J. Cutler, this documentary follows the editor-in-chief, Anna Wintour, and her team as they navigate the challenges and creative decisions involved in producing a fashion bible. The film showcases the power and influence of fashion magazines and the intricate work that goes into shaping trends and setting the tone for the industry.

5. “Yves Saint Laurent” (2014)

This biographical drama tells the story of iconic fashion designer Yves Saint Laurent, portrayed by Pierre Niney. The film chronicles Saint Laurent’s rise to fame, his creative genius, and the challenges he faced both personally and professionally. The stunning costumes and meticulous attention to detail make this film a visual feast for fashion enthusiasts and provide an intimate look into the mind of a fashion visionary.

6. “Funny Face” (1957)

A classic musical romantic comedy, “Funny Face” stars Audrey Hepburn as a bookshop clerk turned fashion model who catches the eye of a renowned fashion photographer, played by Fred Astaire. The film showcases the glamour of the fashion world, with stunning outfits designed by Hubert de Givenchy. The iconic dance sequences and Hepburn’s timeless elegance make this film a delightful watch for those seeking inspiration from the golden age of fashion.

7. “The Neon Demon” (2016)

For a darker take on the fashion industry, “The Neon Demon” explores the cutthroat world of modeling in Los Angeles. Directed by Nicolas Winding Refn, this psychological horror film follows aspiring model Jesse, played by Elle Fanning, as she navigates the treacherous waters of beauty and obsession. While the film may not be for the faint of heart, it offers a thought-provoking commentary on the extreme pressures faced by those in the fashion industry.

Conclusion

Fashion films provide a unique and visually captivating way to immerse yourself in the world of fashion and find inspiration for your own style or creative endeavors. Whether you’re in the mood for a glamorous Hollywood portrayal of the fashion industry or a thought-provoking exploration of the challenges faced by fashion designers, these films are sure to leave you inspired and eager to dive deeper into the world of fashion. So grab some popcorn, cozy up on the couch, and let these fashion films transport you into a world of style and creativity.
