10 Creative Ways to Use Mason Jars in Your Crafts


Mason jars are versatile, timeless, and can be used in a variety of craft projects. Whether you’re a seasoned crafter or a beginner, mason jars offer endless opportunities for creativity. They have become a staple in many DIY projects due to their simplicity, durability, and charm. In this post, we will explore 10 creative ways to incorporate mason jars into your crafts, adding a touch of rustic elegance to your home, garden, or gifts.

1. Mason Jar Terrariums

Terrariums are a beautiful and unique way to bring greenery into your space, and mason jars are the perfect vessels for creating these little indoor gardens. All you need is a clear mason jar, some small plants (like succulents or air plants), decorative stones, pebbles, and a bit of soil. The clear glass of the mason jar allows you to view the layers of plants and stones, creating an aesthetically pleasing display.

To make your own terrarium, start by layering the bottom of the jar with small pebbles to allow drainage. Then, add a thin layer of charcoal (to keep moisture in check) followed by soil. Plant your greenery, and add some decorative accents like moss or small figurines. A mason jar terrarium makes a perfect centerpiece for a coffee table or a thoughtful gift for nature lovers.

2. Mason Jar Candle Holders

Create rustic and charming candle holders using mason jars for a cozy, ambient atmosphere. Whether you’re hosting a dinner party or just relaxing at home, mason jar candle holders add a warm glow to any setting. You can easily personalize them with different colors, textures, and embellishments.

To make a mason jar candle holder, simply place a tea light or small candle inside the jar. If you want to get creative, add some decorative touches. For instance, wrap twine around the jar, glue lace around the rim, or even paint the jar in a soft color for a more personalized look. You can also use mason jars in a group to create a charming centerpiece for special events like weddings or holiday gatherings.

3. Mason Jar Soap Dispenser

Transform a simple mason jar into a stylish soap dispenser. This is a practical and unique way to use mason jars in your bathroom or kitchen. All you need is a pump dispenser top, which you can find at most craft stores, and a mason jar that matches your home’s décor.

Simply screw the pump lid onto the jar, fill it with your favorite hand soap or dish soap, and place it next to your sink. This not only adds a touch of farmhouse style but also helps reduce waste if you use refillable soap containers. To make it even more personalized, you can decorate the jar with paint, fabric, or stencils to match the rest of your décor.

4. Mason Jar Flower Vases

Mason jars make delightful flower vases that can be used for both fresh and artificial flowers. Their simple design adds a rustic touch to any bouquet, making them perfect for weddings, events, or even just everyday décor. A mason jar flower vase is an easy and elegant way to display flowers in your home.

You can keep it simple by using the jar as is, or you can personalize it by painting the jar or adding decorative twine or lace. Another creative idea is to use a mason jar as a centerpiece by grouping several jars together with different flower types. This works well for garden parties or outdoor gatherings. Mason jar flower vases are also a great gift idea, especially when paired with a beautiful bunch of blooms.

5. Mason Jar Organizer

Whether you need a way to keep your office supplies in order or a system for your craft tools, mason jars can serve as an efficient and attractive storage solution. Mason jars come in various sizes, making them perfect for organizing small items like pens, pencils, paintbrushes, screws, buttons, or even spices in the kitchen.

To create your own mason jar organizer, simply gather a few jars and arrange them in a tray or mount them on a wooden board. You can label the jars for easy identification, or even decorate them to add a personal touch to your workspace. This mason jar organizer is not only practical but also an aesthetic addition to any room in your home or office.

6. Mason Jar Snow Globes

Mason jars are an excellent medium for creating DIY snow globes, a nostalgic and magical craft perfect for the winter months or for holiday décor. With a mason jar snow globe, you can create a little winter wonderland, no matter the season. The beauty of a mason jar snow globe lies in its ability to showcase tiny, intricate scenes within the jar.

To create your own snow globe, start by gluing a small figurine or scene to the inside of the lid of the mason jar. Fill the jar with water, add a few tablespoons of glitter (to mimic snow), and screw the lid back on tightly. Shake the jar, and watch the snow swirl around the figurines. You can also personalize the outside of the jar with paint or stickers to match the holiday theme.

7. Mason Jar Drink Dispensers

For outdoor gatherings, BBQs, or parties, a mason jar drink dispenser is both practical and stylish. These dispensers make it easy to serve lemonade, iced tea, or even punch to guests. With a large mason jar and a spigot, you can easily turn a mason jar into a functional drink dispenser.

To create a drink dispenser, all you need is a large mason jar with a spigot, which you can purchase online or at most craft stores. Fill the jar with your favorite beverage, and place it on a sturdy stand or table. For an added touch, garnish the drink with fresh fruits like lemons or berries. This DIY mason jar drink dispenser will be a hit at your next summer party or event.

8. Mason Jar Planters

If you have a green thumb, using mason jars as planters is a wonderful way to add a touch of nature to your home or garden. These small, charming planters are ideal for growing herbs, succulents, or small flowers. The transparent glass of the mason jar also allows you to see the roots of the plants, adding a visually interesting element to your gardening project.

To create a mason jar planter, simply fill the jar with soil, add your plant or seeds, and place it in a sunny spot. You can also decorate the outside of the jar by painting it or wrapping it with twine for added style. If you’re growing herbs in the kitchen, label each jar with the herb’s name to create an organized herb garden that looks both beautiful and functional.

9. Mason Jar Jewelry Holder

Another creative way to use mason jars in your crafts is by turning them into jewelry holders. This is an excellent way to display and organize your necklaces, bracelets, earrings, or rings. With a bit of creativity, you can make a mason jar jewelry holder that will keep your accessories neat and easy to find.

To make a jewelry holder, place a mason jar on a decorative stand or tray. You can use a larger jar to store bracelets and rings, while smaller jars can hold earrings or necklaces. For an added touch, glue small hooks to the lid of the jar to hang necklaces or other pieces. You can also paint or decorate the jar to suit your style.

10. Mason Jar Gift Packaging

When it comes to gift giving, mason jars make fantastic packaging options. Instead of using traditional gift bags or boxes, consider using a mason jar to package your gifts. Whether it’s for a birthday, holiday, or special occasion, mason jars can hold small gifts like candies, homemade cookies, or small trinkets, making the presentation unique and memorable.

To make the gift extra special, add a personalized touch. You can paint the jar, tie a ribbon around the lid, or attach a handwritten note. A mason jar filled with homemade treats, like cookies or jam, makes an excellent personalized gift. The recipient will love both the thoughtful contents and the charming packaging.

Final Thoughts

Mason jars are more than just practical containers; they are the perfect foundation for creative and stylish craft projects. From home décor to organization, mason jars can be transformed into beautiful and functional items that add charm to any space. Whether you’re making terrariums, flower vases, or unique gift packaging, there are endless possibilities to explore with mason jars.

So, grab a few mason jars, unleash your creativity, and start crafting! With their timeless appeal and endless potential, these little glass jars can help you make something truly special. Happy crafting!
