How to Start Your Own Fantasy Writing Group


Do you find yourself lost in the magical worlds of Tolkien, Rowling, or Martin? Do you have a passion for creating your own fantastical realms, filled with dragons, wizards, and epic quests? If so, you might be interested in starting your own fantasy writing group. Writing groups can provide invaluable support, feedback, and inspiration for aspiring authors. In this guide, we’ll explore the steps to take in order to establish your own fantasy writing group and nurture a community of like-minded writers.

Step 1: Define Your Group’s Focus

The first step in starting a fantasy writing group is to define the focus and goals of the group. Are you primarily interested in epic fantasy, urban fantasy, or a mix of sub-genres? Will the group be open to all levels of writing experience, or will it cater to more seasoned writers? By clearly defining the focus of your group, you can attract writers who share your interests and ensure that the group’s discussions and activities align with its purpose.

Step 2: Find Like-Minded Writers

Once you have a clear vision for your writing group, the next step is to find like-minded writers who are eager to join. You can start by reaching out to local writing communities, posting in online forums, or leveraging social media to connect with potential members. Consider attending local book clubs, writing workshops, or fantasy conventions to network with writers who share your passion for fantasy fiction. Building a strong foundation of enthusiastic members is crucial for the long-term success of your group.

Step 3: Establish Group Guidelines

In order to maintain a productive and harmonious writing group, it’s essential to establish clear guidelines for participation. This may include guidelines for providing feedback, respecting each other’s work, and adhering to meeting schedules. By setting clear expectations from the outset, you can create a positive and supportive environment where writers feel comfortable sharing their work and receiving constructive criticism.

Step 4: Plan Regular Meetings and Activities

Consistency is key when it comes to maintaining an active writing group. Plan regular meetings, whether they are in-person gatherings or virtual meetups, to ensure that members have a consistent opportunity to share their work and engage in discussions. Consider incorporating writing prompts, critique sessions, and group writing exercises to keep the creative energy flowing and encourage members to explore new ideas and techniques.

Step 5: Foster a Supportive Community

Beyond providing a platform for sharing and critiquing writing, a successful writing group should foster a sense of community and support among its members. Encourage open communication, celebrate each other’s successes, and offer assistance to fellow writers who may be struggling with their creative endeavors. Building a supportive community can inspire writers to push their boundaries and grow in their craft.

Step 6: Expand Your Reach

As your fantasy writing group gains momentum, consider expanding your reach by organizing public readings, collaborative projects, or even publishing anthologies featuring the works of group members. This not only provides exposure for the group and its members but also contributes to the wider fantasy literary community.

Starting a fantasy writing group can be a rewarding endeavor for both aspiring and established writers. By following these steps and nurturing a community of passionate fantasy writers, you can create a space where creativity thrives and imaginations soar. Embrace the magic of storytelling and embark on this journey to build a vibrant fantasy writing community!

In conclusion, starting your own fantasy writing group can be an exciting and fulfilling venture. By defining your group’s focus, finding like-minded writers, establishing clear guidelines, planning regular activities, fostering a supportive community, and expanding your reach, you can lay the groundwork for a thriving fantasy writing group. So gather your quills, summon your muses, and embark on this enchanting journey of fantasy storytelling with fellow writers who share your love for the extraordinary.
