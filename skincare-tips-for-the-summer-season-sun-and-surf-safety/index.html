Skincare Tips for the Summer Season: Sun and Surf Safety


Summer, the season of sun, sand, and surf, is finally here! As we soak up the warm rays and enjoy outdoor activities, it’s essential to know how to protect our skin from the harsh ultraviolet (UV) radiation that can lead to sunburns, premature aging, and skin cancer. In this post, we’ll explore some valuable tips for maintaining healthy and beautiful skin during the summer months.

  1. Practice Sun Safety The most fundamental aspect of summertime skincare is practicing sun safety. There are a few critical steps to follow:
  • Apply broad-spectrum sunscreen with at least SPF 30 at least 30 minutes before going outside. Reapply after swimming or sweating, and every two hours.
  • Seek shade, especially between 10 AM and 4 PM when the sun’s rays are strongest.
  • Wear protective clothing, such as long-sleeved shirts, wide-brimmed hats, and sunglasses.
  • Avoid indoor tanning, and protect others from overexposure by tanning only in a tanning bed with a certified operator.

  1. Choose the Right Sunscreen Chemical-free sunscreens are growing in popularity, and for a good reason. They are less irritating and more effective than their chemical-based counterparts. Still, it’s crucial to find a product with an accurate and reliable formulation, as misleading labels can give a false sense of security.

  2. Hydrate and Moisturize Remember to hydrate from the inside by drinking plenty of water and other hydrating beverages. Dehydrated skin can feel tight and uncomfortable, making it more prone to irritation and even aging. Additionally, apply a moisturizer before applying sunscreen, as hydrated skin is less likely to absorb sunscreen unevenly, leading to missed spots and Sunburns.

  3. Treat Sunburns Promptly If you experience a sunburn, treat it quickly to minimize the appearance and duration of the burn. Take a cool shower or bath, apply a soothing aloe vera gel or hydrocortisone cream (prescription strength if necessary), and avoid scents and hot showers. To reduce inflammation and promote healing, also consider taking over-the-counter pain relief medication or consuming natural remedies like chamomile tea or papaya enzymes.

  4. Protecting Your Skin in the Surf The surf might look inviting, but the sun reflecting off the water can intensify ultraviolet rays without you even realizing. Apply extra sunscreen and wear a cap or a rash guard while swimming.

  5. Adapting Your Skincare Routine for the Season Adjust your skincare routine based on the season for optimal performance. Exfoliate more during summer to discourage clogged pores and reduce the risk of breakouts. For oily or acne-prone skin, switch to a lighter moisturizer or use oil-controlling products. Periodically update your skincare lineup to assess what works best for your skin type and needs.

Finally, remember that wearing sunscreen is a life-long habit. Even on cloudy days or during winter, excessive sun exposure can cause long-term damage. By taking these precautions and being mindful of our skin, we can enjoy summers safely, preserving our skin’s vitality for years to come.

Stay sun smart and happy during the summer months!
