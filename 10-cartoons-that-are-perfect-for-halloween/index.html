10 Cartoons That Are Perfect for Halloween


As the leaves turn golden and the air gets crisp, Halloween approaches with its promise of spooky fun, costume parties, and plenty of candy. One of the best ways to get into the Halloween spirit is through cartoons that capture the essence of the season. Here’s a roundup of ten animated shows and films that are perfect for Halloween viewing, whether you’re a kid or just a kid at heart.

1. It’s the Great Pumpkin, Charlie Brown

No Halloween list would be complete without mentioning this timeless classic. First airing in 1966, this animated special follows Charlie Brown and his friends as they prepare for Halloween. Linus is determined to wait for the Great Pumpkin, a mythical figure who rises from the pumpkin patch to deliver toys to good children. The charming storytelling, paired with the iconic Peanuts characters, makes it a must-watch every Halloween season. It’s a heartfelt tale that captures the essence of hope and friendship, making it suitable for audiences of all ages.

2. Scooby-Doo, Where Are You!

The original Scooby-Doo series debuted in 1969 and has been a Halloween staple ever since. The gang—Fred, Daphne, Velma, Shaggy, and of course, Scooby-Doo—solves mysteries involving supposedly supernatural creatures. Each episode is filled with spooky settings, clever plots, and a healthy dose of humor. The series has spawned numerous spin-offs and movies, but the original captures that classic Halloween feel with its mix of mystery and mild scares, making it perfect for family viewing.

3. The Nightmare Before Christmas

Though it straddles both Halloween and Christmas, Tim Burton’s “The Nightmare Before Christmas” has become a quintessential Halloween film. The story follows Jack Skellington, the King of Halloween Town, who discovers Christmas Town and becomes obsessed with bringing Christmas to his home. With its unique animation style, memorable songs, and a blend of spooky and whimsical, this film captures the spirit of Halloween while also exploring themes of identity and belonging. It’s a visual feast that’s perfect for family movie night.

4. Courage the Cowardly Dog

This cult classic series aired from 1996 to 2002 and is known for its unique blend of horror and humor. Courage, a timid dog, lives with his elderly owners in a remote farmhouse and often encounters bizarre and terrifying creatures. The show is filled with dark, surreal humor and unsettling storylines, making it ideal for older kids and adults who appreciate a good scare. The animation style and storytelling create an eerie atmosphere, perfect for Halloween.

5. Hocus Pocus: The Animated Series

While the original “Hocus Pocus” film is beloved, the animated series takes the story of the Sanderson sisters—Winifred, Sarah, and Mary—into a new direction. Set to premiere soon, this series promises to blend humor, magic, and mischief as the sisters navigate modern-day challenges. The allure of Halloween and the misadventures of the iconic witches make it an exciting addition to the Halloween cartoon lineup, appealing to both new fans and those who grew up with the original film.

6. The Addams Family

The Addams Family has been a beloved franchise since it first appeared as a comic strip in the late 1930s. The animated series captures the dark comedy and eccentric charm of the family, featuring Gomez, Morticia, Wednesday, Pugsley, Uncle Fester, and the rest of the gang. Their spooky yet lovable nature makes them perfect for Halloween viewing. The show’s mix of humor and macabre elements creates a fun atmosphere for the season.

7. Monster High

This animated series and film franchise targets a younger audience, combining themes of friendship, acceptance, and self-expression with a spooky twist. Set in a high school for monsters, the series features characters inspired by classic horror figures, including Draculaura, Frankie Stein, and Cleo de Nile. The vibrant animation style and engaging storylines make it a fun choice for kids looking to celebrate Halloween while learning about the importance of being true to oneself.

8. Casper the Friendly Ghost

Casper has been enchanting audiences since the 1940s, and his animated adventures remain popular today. Known as the friendly ghost, Casper’s stories often revolve around his desire to make friends, despite his ghostly nature. The series blends light-hearted humor with gentle scares, making it suitable for younger viewers. Casper’s charming personality and heartwarming tales are perfect for setting a light-hearted Halloween mood.

9. The Simpsons: Treehouse of Horror

For fans of adult animation, “The Simpsons” annual “Treehouse of Horror” episodes are a Halloween tradition. These specials feature three short stories that parody horror films and classic tales, all infused with the show’s signature humor. From spooky twists on familiar stories to original frightful tales, these episodes are perfect for older audiences looking for a good laugh alongside their scares. The clever writing and satirical approach make it a standout in Halloween programming.

10. Over the Garden Wall

This unique miniseries, which aired in 2014, is a beautiful blend of adventure, folklore, and the supernatural. It follows two half-brothers, Wirt and Greg, as they navigate a mysterious forest called the Unknown. The series is steeped in a haunting atmosphere, with elements of Americana and fairy tales that create an enchanting yet spooky vibe. Its stunning animation and poignant storytelling make it perfect for Halloween, appealing to both children and adults who appreciate a more artistic approach to the season.

Conclusion

As Halloween approaches, these ten cartoons provide a delightful mix of spookiness, humor, and heartwarming tales. Whether you’re looking for something classic or a fresh take on Halloween themes, these animated shows and films offer something for everyone. Gather your friends and family, grab some popcorn, and enjoy a night of Halloween-themed cartoons that will entertain and delight viewers of all ages. Happy Halloween!
