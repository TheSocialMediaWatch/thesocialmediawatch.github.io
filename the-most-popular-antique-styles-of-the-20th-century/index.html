The Most Popular Antique Styles of the 20th Century


The 20th century was a transformative period for art and design, marked by the rise and fall of numerous iconic styles that continue to influence the world of antique collecting and interior design today. From the elegant lines of Art Deco to the playful irreverence of Mid-Century Modern, each style has its unique charm and character, showcasing the creative innovation and experimentation that defined an era. The Roaring Twenties: Art Deco Revival In the aftermath of World War I, the world was looking for a fresh start, and what could be more exhilarating than a return to the glamour and opulence of the Roaring Twenties? Art Deco emerged as a beacon of style and sophistication, drawing inspiration from ancient Egyptian and Mayan civilizations, as well as the machine age aesthetics of industrialization. Ornate details, geometric patterns, and metallic accents gave birth to an era of unapologetic luxury, as captured in the iconic designs of society clubs and movie palaces. Standing the test of time, Art Deco continues to captivate collectors with its ornate beauty and flashy appeal. Meanwhile, in the realm of furniture, the Charleston-style settee and the Parkdale bench captivated with their curved lines and rosewood accents, reflecting the shifting tide of societal values and aspirations. The Rise of Minimalism: Bauhaus and Streamline As the century progressed, the world was beset by financial downturns and economic struggles, prompting a fundamental shift in design philosophy. Bauhaus, founded in Germany, emerged as a force of minimalist innovation, emphasizing clean lines, simplicity, and functionality. Embracing the artistic ideals of democracy and social change, Bauhaus envisioned design as a democratizing force, empowering people to create their own economic revolutions. This cultural shift gained full force with Streamline Moderne, an art deco sub-style that strove for purity and travel to far-off, exotic places.
