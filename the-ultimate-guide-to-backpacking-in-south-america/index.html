The Ultimate Guide to Backpacking in South America


South America offers one of the most diverse and thrilling backpacking experiences on the planet. From the soaring peaks of the Andes to the lush Amazon rainforest, from vibrant cities pulsating with culture to remote villages steeped in tradition, this continent is a playground for adventurous souls. If you’re contemplating a backpacking journey through South America, this comprehensive guide will equip you with essential knowledge, practical tips, and inspiring ideas to make your travel unforgettable.

Why Backpack South America?

South America stands out as a backpacker’s dream for several reasons:

  • Staggering natural diversity: Explore glaciers in Patagonia, deserts in Atacama, and tropical jungles in the Amazon basin.
  • Rich historical heritage: Uncover the mysteries of ancient civilizations like the Incas and other indigenous cultures.
  • Vibrant cultural encounters: Experience local festivals, music, dance, and cuisine that showcase a complex cultural mosaic.
  • Affordability: Compared to other continents, traveling here with a backpack is often budget-friendly.
  • A range of adventure activities: Trekking, surfing, paragliding, wildlife watching, and more are readily accessible.

Preparations Before You Go

Choosing Your Route

South America’s vastness means you’ll want to segment your trip into manageable parts or themes:

  • Andean Highlights: Peru, Bolivia, Ecuador, and Colombia offer Andean landscapes, indigenous cultures, and iconic sites like Machu Picchu and the Uyuni Salt Flats.
  • Patagonia and the Southern Cone: Chile and Argentina are famed for their wild, windswept landscapes—perfect for trekking.
  • Amazon Basin: The world’s largest rainforest stretches across Brazil, Peru, Colombia, and beyond, ready for immersive jungle experiences.
  • Caribbean Coastline & Cities: Colombia and Venezuela offer tropical beaches and vibrant urban scenes.

Many backpackers start in Colombia or Peru, traveling southward, while others prefer to explore Brazil’s southern cities, then push north into the jungle.

Visas and Vaccinations

Most countries require either no visa or easy tourist visas for visitors from major nations. However, it’s wise to:

  • Check entry requirements for each country in your itinerary.
  • Get vaccinations for yellow fever, typhoid, hepatitis A and B, and tetanus as recommended.
  • Bring anti-malarial medication if you plan to stay in jungle areas.

Packing Essentials

Backpacking South America means packing light but strategically:

  • Durable backpack: Aim for a 40-50L backpack for mobility.
  • Clothing layers: Temperatures vary drastically by region and elevation.
  • Sturdy hiking boots.
  • Rain jacket: The wet season can bring heavy showers.
  • Travel documents: Passport, visas, copies of health records.
  • Portable charger and adapters (mostly Type C and L plugs).
  • Basic first aid kit.
  • Reusable water bottle with a filter.
  • Spanish phrasebook or app: Spanish is the dominant language in most countries, Portuguese in Brazil.

Budgeting and Money

South America tends to be more budget-friendly than Europe or North America. Daily expenses can vary depending on style:

  • Street food, hostels, and local buses can keep daily costs very low.
  • Tourist hotspots or luxury activities will cost more.
  • Using local currency is almost always better than dollars or euros when paying locally.
  • ATM use is widespread but withdrawing larger amounts at once reduces fees.
  • Have a backup credit card and some cash safely stored.

Must-Visit Destinations and Experiences

Peru: Gateway to Ancient Wonders and Nature

Machu Picchu, the striking Incan city perched high in the clouds, is a bucket-list destination for backpackers. Whether you trek the famous Inca Trail or take the train, this site leaves a lasting impression.

Other great highlights in Peru include:

  • Cusco: Charming colonial architecture meets indigenous culture.
  • The Sacred Valley: Picturesque villages and lesser-known ruins.
  • Lake Titicaca: The highest navigable lake in the world, with floating reed islands.
  • Nazca Lines: Mysterious giant geoglyphs best seen from a small plane.

Peru is also a food lover’s paradise with dishes like ceviche and lomo saltado widely available.

Bolivia: Land of Extremes and Mystical Landscapes

Bolivia offers some of the most breathtaking and surreal landscapes:

  • Uyuni Salt Flats: The world’s largest, an enormous white salt desert creating otherworldly reflections.
  • La Paz: The world’s highest administrative capital, bustling with markets and rich culture.
  • The Death Road: Adventurous cyclists take on this thrilling downhill route through cloud forests.
  • Lake Titicaca (Bolivian side): Less commercialized, perfect for authentic cultural interactions.

Travel in Bolivia can be challenging (high altitude, rough roads), but the rewards are immense.

Argentina: The Elegance and Wilderness Blend

Argentina has something for every backpacker:

  • Buenos Aires: Tango dancing, great steak, vibrant nightlife.
  • Patagonia: Iconic trekking spots such as El Chaltén, Perito Moreno glacier, and Torres del Paine.
  • Mendoza: Wine country for relaxing and sampling Malbecs.
  • Iguazu Falls: Spectacular waterfalls on the border with Brazil.

Argentina’s vast size means public transport can be slow; consider internal flights to save time.

Chile: Diverse Landscapes from Desert to Ice

Chile offers an incredible geographical contrast:

  • Atacama Desert: The driest desert on Earth, with lunar landscapes and stargazing opportunities.
  • Patagonia (Chilean side): Torres del Paine National Park is a must for trekkers.
  • Santiago: A modern city nestled against the Andes, with buzzing food scenes and nearby ski resorts.
  • Easter Island: For the enigmatic Moai statues, a remote cultural experience.

Colombia: Emerging Backpacker Hotspot

Colombia has bounced back as a highlight for adventurous travelers seeking history, coffee, beaches, and urban culture:

  • Cartagena: Colonial walled city with Caribbean charm.
  • Medellín: From troubled past to cultural renaissance.
  • Coffee region: Scenic landscapes, coffee farm tours.
  • Tayrona National Park: Beautiful beaches and hiking.

Ecuador: Compact and Complete

Ecuador packs a punch in terms of variety:

  • Quito: Historic old town with impressive colonial architecture.
  • Galápagos Islands: A dream for wildlife lovers, though can be pricey.
  • Cotopaxi National Park: Trek volcanoes and explore paramo landscapes.

The Amazon Rainforest Adventure

Regardless of which country you enter from, the Amazon region offers unparalleled biodiversity:

  • Jungle lodges accessible from Iquitos (Peru), Manaus (Brazil), and Leticia (Colombia).
  • Guided tours include wildlife spotting, night walks, and indigenous community visits.
  • Sustainable tourism initiatives encourage responsible travel.

Safety Tips for Backpackers

While South America can be very rewarding, it also requires vigilance and smart travel strategies:

  • Stay aware of your surroundings, especially in overcrowded places like buses and markets.
  • Use reputable transportation options; avoid hitchhiking.
  • Keep valuables secure; money belts or hidden pouches are practical.
  • Avoid walking alone in unfamiliar or poorly-lit areas at night.
  • Carry photocopies of important documents.
  • Trust your instincts; if something feels off, leave.

Transportation: Getting Around South America

South America’s vast distances mean travel times can be long. Options include:

  • Buses: The backbone of land travel, connecting nearly every town. Luxury buses with reclining seats are common.
  • Flights: Low-cost airlines make flights between major cities affordable and save precious time.
  • Trains: Limited but iconic routes like Peru’s train to Machu Picchu.
  • Boats and ferries: In places like the Amazon and Lake Titicaca.

Booking in advance can secure the best prices, especially in high season.

Accommodations: From Hostels to Eco-Lodges

Backpackers will find a wide range of options:

  • Hostels: Social, affordable, and great for networking with other travelers.
  • Guesthouses or family stays: Often more intimate and culturally immersive.
  • Eco-lodges: For jungle or nature stays with a focus on sustainability.
  • Budget hotels: Cheap but comfortable options especially in cities.

Booking platforms and word-of-mouth reviews help find gems that match your style.

Food and Drink: Exploring South America’s Culinary Delights

Food is a fundamental part of any travel experience in South America:

  • Street food stalls and markets offer local dishes like empanadas, arepas, and grilled meats.
  • Signature dishes:
    • Peru: Ceviche, anticuchos, quinoa-based dishes.
    • Argentina: Asado (barbecue), dulce de leche sweets.
    • Brazil: Feijoada (black bean stew), açaí bowls.
    • Colombia: Bandeja paisa, arepa.
  • Fresh fruit juices and exotic fruits like guava, passionfruit, and lucuma.
  • Safe tap water isn’t always guaranteed; bottled or filtered water is advisable.

Language: Bridging the Communication Gap

Spanish dominates in most countries, with Portuguese spoken in Brazil. Learning basic phrases will enhance your experience and interactions. Key words to know include greetings, numbers, directions, and phrases for ordering food, asking prices, or requesting help.

Many locals in tourist areas speak some English, but venturing off beaten paths requires preparation.

Responsible Backpacking in South America

Respect for indigenous communities, conservation efforts, and sustainable tourism are important priorities. Support local businesses, minimize waste, and be considerate of cultural norms. Participating in community-led tours helps preserve heritage and provides equitable economic benefits.

Final Thoughts

Backpacking in South America is a journey filled with awe-inspiring landscapes, fascinating cultures, and unforgettable adventures. Preparation and an open mind will transform this trip into a life-changing experience. Wherever you roam—through the towering Andes, tropical Amazon, or vibrant cities—you’ll find stories, friendships, and moments to treasure forever.

Ready to pack your bags? South America awaits with its breathtaking allure and open arms for the curious traveler. Happy trekking!
