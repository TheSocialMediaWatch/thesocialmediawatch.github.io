How to Use Survival Gear in an Emergency


When disaster strikes, whether it’s a natural disaster, a remote outdoor adventure gone awry, or an unforeseen emergency, survival gear becomes your most valuable asset. Knowing how to effectively use this equipment can mean the difference between life and death. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll walk you through the essential survival gear, how to use it properly, and the critical steps to follow in an emergency to increase your chances of survival.

1. Preparing for the Worst: Why Survival Gear is Essential

Emergencies happen when we least expect them. From getting lost on a hike to facing the aftermath of a natural disaster like an earthquake or a flood, being prepared with survival gear is essential. Having the right tools at your disposal can help you maintain warmth, find water, navigate your way out, and signal for help.

Before we dive into how to use specific survival gear, it’s important to have an understanding of the basics:

  • Stay Calm: In any emergency, panic can cloud your judgment. The first step is always to remain as calm as possible.
  • Assess the Situation: Identify your immediate needs. Are you injured? Are you exposed to harsh weather conditions? Do you have access to food and water?
  • Prioritize: Address your needs in order of importance, focusing on life-threatening concerns first, such as water, warmth, and shelter.

Survival gear can provide the tools you need for these priorities, but knowing how to use each piece correctly is vital.

2. Essential Survival Gear and How to Use It

a) Fire Starter: Creating Heat and Light

One of the first things you’ll need to do in an emergency situation is create a fire. Whether it’s to stay warm, purify water, or signal for help, fire is a vital survival tool.

How to Use a Fire Starter

  • Fire Starter Kit: Many survival kits come with fire starters, which could include magnesium blocks, flint and steel, or matches.
  • Magnesium Block: Scrape off some magnesium shavings using a knife and strike it with a flint or steel. This creates a spark that ignites the magnesium, producing a fire.
  • Flint and Steel: Strike the steel against the flint to create sparks. Aim the sparks onto dry tinder such as dry leaves, grass, or bark to start a fire.
  • Matches: If you have waterproof matches, use them by striking them against the matchbox. Ensure you have enough dry kindling around to keep the fire going.

b) Water Filtration System: Clean Drinking Water

Water is essential for survival, and finding a clean, reliable water source is one of your top priorities. In an emergency, a water filtration system can help ensure you stay hydrated and avoid waterborne diseases.

How to Use a Water Filtration System

  • Portable Water Filters: These typically come as straws or pump systems. To use, simply submerge the straw in the water source and suck through it. For a pump system, attach the filter to the water source and pump water into a container.
  • Chemical Water Purification Tablets: If a filter is unavailable, use iodine or chlorine tablets. Drop the tablet into a liter of water, wait 30 minutes to an hour, and your water will be safe to drink.
  • Boiling Water: If you don’t have purification tablets or a filter, boiling water for at least 5 minutes will kill most pathogens.

c) Multi-tool: Versatile Functionality

A multi-tool is one of the most versatile pieces of survival gear. It combines a variety of functions into one compact device, including a knife, pliers, screwdrivers, and more.

How to Use a Multi-tool

  • Knife Blade: The knife can be used for cutting through rope, preparing food, or even self-defense. Keep the blade sharp and be mindful of safety when using it.
  • Pliers: These are useful for fixing equipment, bending wires, or extracting objects. They provide extra grip in situations where your hands can’t manage.
  • Screwdrivers: A multi-tool’s screwdriver function can help in situations where you need to repair or disassemble objects, such as tents or shelter materials.

d) First Aid Kit: Treating Injuries and Illnesses

Injuries are a common occurrence in emergency situations, so having a first aid kit is a must. It will allow you to address cuts, scrapes, sprains, and other injuries until professional medical help is available.

How to Use a First Aid Kit

  • Bandages and Gauze: Use sterile bandages or gauze to stop bleeding. Apply direct pressure to the wound and cover it with a bandage. Ensure it is snug but not too tight to prevent further injury.
  • Antiseptic Wipes: Clean wounds with antiseptic wipes to prevent infection. Apply a small amount of antiseptic ointment if available before covering with a bandage.
  • Pain Relief: If you have pain-relieving medication in your first aid kit, take it according to the instructions. Always be cautious of dosage and potential side effects.

e) Emergency Shelter: Protecting Yourself from the Elements

Shelter is one of your most immediate needs in a survival situation, especially in cold or extreme weather conditions. An emergency shelter can help protect you from hypothermia, sun exposure, and wind chill.

How to Use an Emergency Shelter

  • Space Blankets: A lightweight space blanket can be used to reflect body heat back towards you. Simply wrap it around your body or use it as a shelter to reduce heat loss.
  • Tarps: A tarp can be used to create a makeshift shelter. Tie it between trees or over a structure to protect yourself from rain or the sun.
  • Tent: If you have a compact, lightweight tent in your survival gear, set it up to create a secure, dry space for resting. Make sure to keep it anchored properly in windy conditions.

f) Compass and Map: Navigating Your Way

If you are lost or need to find your way to safety, a compass and map are crucial tools. They can help you navigate to the nearest town, rescue point, or safe shelter.

How to Use a Compass and Map

  • Compass: Hold the compass flat in your hand and rotate the dial to align it with the magnetic north. Using the map, align the compass with your desired direction of travel, and follow the path indicated.
  • Map: Study the map before heading out to familiarize yourself with landmarks, trails, and topography. Use the compass to determine your direction and distance to key points on the map.

g) Signaling Devices: Calling for Help

In a dire situation where you need to signal rescuers, having the right signaling devices can make a huge difference. A whistle, mirror, or signal flare can help others locate you.

How to Use Signaling Devices

  • Whistle: Blow the whistle in a series of three short bursts to indicate distress. This is a universal distress signal, recognized by rescuers worldwide.
  • Signal Mirror: Use the mirror to reflect sunlight in specific patterns to attract the attention of aerial search teams. Aim the mirror towards the sun and flash signals towards the sky.
  • Signal Flares: If you have flares, light one and wave it above your head to signal for help. Flares can be seen from a great distance, especially at night.

h) Knife: Essential Tool for Survival

A good survival knife is one of the most versatile tools you can have in your emergency gear. It can help you with food preparation, shelter building, and self-defense.

How to Use a Survival Knife

  • Cutting and Preparing Food: A sharp survival knife can be used to cut through meat, vegetables, and fruits. It can also be used for chopping wood for fire or shelter construction.
  • Building Shelters: You can use a knife to carve wood, cut rope, or create stakes for building a shelter.
  • Self-defense: In an emergency, a survival knife can serve as a means of protection. Always be cautious when using it for self-defense, as it requires precision and control.

i) Rope: For Tying and Securing

Rope is incredibly versatile in an emergency. It can be used to tie things down, create shelter, or even fashion a makeshift stretcher for an injured person.

How to Use Rope

  • Tying Shelter Materials: Use rope to secure a tarp, build a shelter, or create a hammock for sleeping.
  • Securing Equipment: If you’re in a boat, raft, or other transport, rope can help secure equipment to prevent it from being lost in a storm or flood.
  • First Aid: In some cases, you can use rope to make a tourniquet to stop severe bleeding or create a sling for an injured arm or leg.

j) Food and Energy Bars: Sustenance in a Crisis

Food is often the last thing on your mind when an emergency strikes, but you’ll eventually need to eat to maintain energy. Having high-calorie, compact energy bars or freeze-dried food is essential for prolonged survival situations.

How to Use Food and Energy Bars

  • Energy Bars: Eat energy bars in small portions to maintain your strength. They are high in calories, providing essential nutrients.
  • Freeze-Dried Meals: If you have freeze-dried meals, all you need to do is add hot water to rehydrate and eat. This type of food is lightweight and easy to store in a survival kit.

3. Final Thoughts: Mastering Survival Gear Usage

Knowing how to properly use your survival gear is one of the most important skills to master when preparing for the unexpected. While you may never face a life-threatening situation, it’s always best to be prepared. Having the right survival gear, and more importantly, knowing how to use it effectively, can increase your chances of staying safe and securing help when needed.

Remember, preparation is key. Take the time to practice with your gear, familiarize yourself with the components of your survival kit, and learn essential survival skills before disaster strikes. By being knowledgeable and ready, you’ll have the confidence to navigate any emergency with calmness and clarity, ultimately giving you the best chance to survive.
