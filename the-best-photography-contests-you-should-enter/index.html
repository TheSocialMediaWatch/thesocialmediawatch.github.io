The Best Photography Contests You Should Enter


Photography is an art form that combines technical skill, creativity, and a unique perspective to capture moments that speak volumes. Participating in photography contests is one of the most effective ways to showcase your talent, gain recognition, and potentially jumpstart your professional journey. With hundreds of contests available worldwide, finding the right ones that match your style and level can be daunting. To help you make an informed decision, here’s a comprehensive guide to some of the best photography contests you should consider entering.

Why Enter Photography Contests?

Before diving into the list, it’s worth exploring why photographers—whether amateurs or professionals—should consider entering contests:

  • Exposure and Recognition: Winning or even being shortlisted can significantly raise your profile.
  • Portfolio Building: Contests provide an opportunity to diversify and strengthen your portfolio.
  • Networking Opportunities: Many contests connect photographers with industry leaders, potential clients, and peers.
  • Monetary Rewards and Prizes: Some contests offer cash prizes, gear, or publishing opportunities.
  • Creative Challenge: They motivate photographers to think outside of their usual style and push artistic boundaries.

Curated List of Outstanding Photography Contests

1. World Photography Awards

Arguably one of the most prestigious global photography competitions, the World Photography Awards cover a broad range of categories, from wildlife and landscapes to portraits and street photography. The contest is highly competitive but offers incredible exposure, including publication in their annual book and exhibitions worldwide.

  • Entry Fee: Varies by category.
  • Prize: Cash awards, global exhibitions, equipment sponsorships.
  • Notables: Separate professional and amateur categories.

2. Sony World Photography Awards

Organized by the World Photography Organisation, this contest combines prestige with accessibility, featuring professional, open, youth, and student competitions. It’s renowned for celebrating contemporary photography from emerging and established artists worldwide.

  • Entry Fee: Most categories are free.
  • Prize: Cash prizes, Sony camera gear, global showcasing.
  • Special Feature: Sony Talent program for young photographers.

3. National Geographic Photo Contest

As one of the most iconic brands in photography and exploration, National Geographic’s annual photo contest attracts high-quality submissions from around the globe. Their theme is often related to nature, culture, and adventure, encouraging photographers to tell powerful stories through their images.

  • Entry Fee: Free.
  • Prize: Publication on National Geographic platforms, editorial assignments.
  • Impact: Exceptional for wildlife and travel photographers.

4. LensCulture Photography Awards

LensCulture champions photographers who offer fresh perspectives and groundbreaking visual narratives. Their annual awards include a diverse range of categories such as documentary, street, and experimental photography. It’s a great platform for photographers seeking exposure and feedback from a global community.

  • Entry Fee: Modest entry fees per category.
  • Prize: Monetary awards, publication, mentoring opportunities.
  • Special Feature: LensCulture also hosts workshops and exhibitions.

5. International Photo Awards (IPA)

A competition designed for both professionals and enthusiasts, the IPA offers categories spanning advertising, fine art, nature, and more. Winners gain significant industry recognition, and the contest is known for its stringent judging process.

  • Entry Fee: Moderate, varies with category.
  • Prize: Cash, inclusion in the IPA Annual book, global press exposure.
  • Timeline: Opens annually with early bird discounts.

6. Comedy Wildlife Photography Awards

For those who enjoy capturing wildlife’s quirkiest moments, this contest combines humor with conservation awareness. It’s a niche but highly popular event that rewards creativity and timing in nature photography.

  • Entry Fee: Free or low cost.
  • Prize: Cash and prizes supporting conservation efforts.
  • Viral Potential: Images frequently receive widespread media coverage.

7. Magnum Photography Awards

Magnum Photos, a legendary photo agency, organizes this contest to discover and support talented photographers in documentary and fine arts photography. Winning means joining a circle of some of the world’s most esteemed photographers.

  • Entry Fee: Higher than average.
  • Prize: Cash, publication, possible agency representation.
  • Reputation: Esteemed judges and rigorous standards.

8. Fine Art Photography Awards

Focused on artistic creativity, this contest emphasizes conceptual and fine art imagery. It celebrates originality and craftsmanship, making it ideal for photographers working in still life, abstract, or fine art genres.

  • Entry Fee: Moderate.
  • Prize: Cash rewards, global exhibitions.
  • Audience: Fine art collectors, curators, and galleries.

9. Hasselblad Masters Award

Recognized as one of the top accolades in photography, the Hasselblad Masters is for accomplished image makers across various styles and industries including fashion, landscape, portraiture, and commercial photography.

  • Entry Fee: Applications tend to be invitation-based or selective.
  • Prize: High-end Hasselblad cameras, global recognition.
  • Exclusive: Entry is often by prior achievement.

10. Monochrome Awards

Dedicated to black-and-white photography enthusiasts, this contest welcomes a range of categories from street to portraiture and beyond. It’s unique in its focus on monochrome aesthetics and techniques.

  • Entry Fee: Reasonable.
  • Prize: Cash prizes, exhibitions, and publication.
  • Community: Strong niche following.

Tips for Maximizing Your Success in Photography Contests

Entering contests is more than just submitting your best photo. Here’s how you can optimize your chances:

  • Understand the Theme and Rules: Carefully read submission guidelines and themes, ensuring your images fit the contest’s requirements.
  • Select Your Best Work: Quality over quantity; often a single, outstanding photo is better than several average ones.
  • Tell a Story: Judges and audiences appreciate a compelling narrative behind the shot.
  • Post-Processing: Edit tastefully; make sure your work enhances the story without appearing overdone.
  • Keep Track of Deadlines: Missing submission windows is a common mistake.
  • Prepare for Licensing Terms: Some contests require specific usage rights, so review agreements carefully.
  • Seek Feedback Before Submitting: Join photography communities or get constructive critiques to refine your work.

Emerging Contests and Online Platforms to Watch

For photographers just starting out or seeking alternative opportunities, these platforms provide excellent stages:

  • EyeEm Photography Awards: Leveraging an app-based submission system, EyeEm allows photographers to enter their work with ease and connect to a global marketplace.
  • ViewBug Contests: Hosting frequent themed competitions with varying difficulty levels, it’s great for beginners.
  • Photocrowd: A platform blending contests with community-driven feedback and peer scoring.
  • GuruShots: A gamified photography challenge platform, perfect for honing your skills and gaining exposure interactively.

Final Thoughts

Photography contests can be transformative experiences that inspire creativity, open doors professionally, and provide valuable validation. While the competition is often fierce, the benefits of entering far outweigh the risks. Whether you are drawn to the grandeur of the World Photography Awards or the playful snapshots of the Comedy Wildlife Photography Awards, there’s a contest out there suited to your style and ambition.

Remember, the true essence isn’t only winning but the journey—exploring new ideas, improving your technique, and sharing your unique vision with the world. So grab your camera, start shooting, and dive into the dynamic world of photography contests to make your mark. Happy clicking!
