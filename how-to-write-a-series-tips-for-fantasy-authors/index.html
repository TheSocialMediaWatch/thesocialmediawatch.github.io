How to Write a Series: Tips for Fantasy Authors


Writing a series in the fantasy genre can be an exciting and daunting task. As a fantasy author, creating a captivating and immersive world that spans multiple books requires careful planning, world-building, and character development. Whether you’re embarking on your first fantasy series or looking to enhance your approach, here are some valuable tips to help you navigate the intricate process of crafting a compelling fantasy series.

1. Establish a Rich and Coherent World

One of the hallmarks of a successful fantasy series is a rich and coherent world that feels alive and immersive. When creating your fantasy world, consider the geography, history, cultures, and magical systems that make it unique. Be mindful of consistency in your world-building, ensuring that the rules and details remain coherent throughout the series. By creating a vivid and believable world, you will draw readers into the enchanting realm you’ve envisioned.

2. Develop Multi-Dimensional Characters

In a fantasy series, characters are the heart of the story. Develop multi-dimensional characters with diverse backgrounds, motivations, and arcs that evolve over the course of the series. Ensure that your characters face internal and external conflicts, and allow them to grow and change as the story progresses. Readers should feel deeply connected to your characters, rooting for their triumphs and empathizing with their struggles.

3. Outline a Clear Story Arc for the Series

Before diving into the writing process, outline a clear story arc that spans the entire series. While each book may have its own plot and resolution, it’s crucial to have an overarching narrative that ties the books together. Consider the pacing, themes, and major events that will unfold across the series, providing a sense of continuity and purpose to the story.

4. Balance Continuity and Standalone Appeal

Striking a balance between continuity and standalone appeal is essential in a fantasy series. Each book should contribute to the larger narrative while offering a self-contained story that engages readers on its own merit. Integrate subtle callbacks, recurring motifs, and unresolved plot threads to maintain continuity, while ensuring that each installment offers a satisfying reading experience independently.

5. Embrace the Element of Surprise

Fantasy readers often crave the element of surprise and unpredictability. Introduce unexpected plot twists, moral dilemmas, and complex revelations that challenge readers’ assumptions and keep them eagerly turning the pages. Infuse your series with moments of awe, wonder, and poignant revelations that leave a lasting impact on your audience.

6. Cultivate a Sense of Myth and Legend

Fantasy series thrive on the allure of myth and legend. Incorporate ancient prophecies, mythical creatures, and enigmatic folklore that add depth and mystique to your world. By weaving a tapestry of legends and lore, you can imbue your series with a sense of timelessness and wonder that captures the imagination of your readers.

7. Maintain Consistent Tone and Voice

Consistency in tone and voice is paramount in a fantasy series. Whether your narrative leans towards epic, dark, whimsical, or gritty, ensure that the tone remains consistent throughout the series. Your writing style should become a signature element that resonates with your audience and distinguishes your series within the fantasy genre.

8. Pay Attention to Reader Feedback

As you embark on the journey of writing a fantasy series, pay attention to reader feedback and engage with your audience. Take note of their reactions to characters, plot developments, and world-building elements. Constructive feedback can offer valuable insights that may enrich your storytelling and strengthen the connection between you and your readers.

9. Foster a Sense of Anticipation

Throughout your series, strive to cultivate a sense of anticipation and intrigue. Plant seeds of foreshadowing, mysteries, and unresolved conflicts that compel readers to eagerly await the next installment. By nurturing a sense of anticipation, you can sustain readers’ interest and anticipation for the unfolding narrative.

10. Stay True to Your Vision

Above all, stay true to your creative vision and passion for storytelling. Writing a fantasy series is a labor of love that demands dedication, resilience, and unwavering commitment to your craft. Embrace the journey with enthusiasm, and let your imagination soar as you breathe life into the enchanting world and characters you’ve envisioned.

In conclusion, writing a fantasy series is a monumental undertaking that offers boundless opportunities for creativity and storytelling. By establishing a rich world, crafting multi-dimensional characters, and weaving an epic narrative, fantasy authors can captivate readers and leave a lasting impression. Embrace the magic of storytelling, and embark on the extraordinary adventure of writing a compelling fantasy series.
