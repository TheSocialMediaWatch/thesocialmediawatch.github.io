How to Choose the Best Backpack for Your Outdoor Adventures


When you set out for an outdoor adventure, whether it’s a day hike, a multi-day trek, or a camping trip, choosing the right backpack is crucial. The perfect backpack will not only carry your gear comfortably but also ensure that you have access to everything you need while keeping you light on your feet. A poor choice can leave you struggling with discomfort or disorganization, which can quickly turn a fun adventure into a frustrating experience.

In this comprehensive guide, we will walk you through the key factors to consider when choosing the best backpack for your outdoor adventures. From size and fit to materials and features, we’ll break down everything you need to know so you can make an informed decision that will enhance your outdoor experience.

1. Understand the Different Types of Outdoor Backpacks

Backpacks come in a variety of shapes and sizes, each designed for specific types of outdoor activities. Understanding the different categories of backpacks will help you narrow down your options. Here are some of the most common types:

Daypacks

Daypacks are small, lightweight backpacks designed for short hikes or single-day trips. Typically ranging from 15 to 30 liters in capacity, these backpacks provide enough space for essential items like water, snacks, a first aid kit, a jacket, and other lightweight gear. Daypacks are ideal for quick outdoor excursions when you don’t need to carry a lot of equipment.

Hiking Backpacks

Hiking backpacks come in a range of sizes, typically from 30 to 50 liters, and are built to carry more gear for longer hikes. These backpacks often feature more advanced suspension systems, making them more comfortable for longer wear. They can accommodate extra clothing, food, water, and other hiking essentials like trekking poles, maps, and navigation tools. Hiking backpacks are a great choice for moderate to extended treks.

Overnight and Multi-Day Backpacks

These larger backpacks, usually between 50 to 75 liters, are designed for extended trips where you need to carry more gear, including a sleeping bag, tent, cooking equipment, and extra clothing. Overnight and multi-day backpacks often come with additional compartments for better organization and may include features like hydration reservoirs, gear loops, and trekking pole attachments. The added size comes with extra weight, but the comfort and capacity are ideal for longer adventures.

Hydration Packs

Hydration packs are smaller, specialized backpacks that are designed to carry a water reservoir along with a small amount of gear. These packs typically range from 1 to 10 liters in capacity and are ideal for activities like running, cycling, or short hikes where hydration is the top priority. If you want easy access to water on the go without carrying a bulky bottle, a hydration pack is an excellent option.

Technical Packs

For more specialized outdoor activities such as climbing, skiing, or mountaineering, technical packs are specifically designed to carry specialized gear. These backpacks come with reinforced areas to carry ice axes, ropes, skis, helmets, and other equipment. Depending on the activity, technical packs can be smaller or larger, but their primary purpose is to help you organize and carry activity-specific gear securely.

2. Consider Backpack Capacity

The capacity of a backpack determines how much gear it can carry. Choosing the right size depends on the duration and nature of your adventure. Here’s a general breakdown of how much gear can fit in different capacities:

  • 15-30 liters: Ideal for day hikes or short outings where you only need to carry essentials like water, snacks, and a light jacket.
  • 30-50 liters: Suitable for overnight trips, weekend hikes, or day-long activities that require more space for extra food, clothing, or equipment.
  • 50-75 liters: Best for multi-day backpacking trips or extended outdoor adventures where you’ll need room for a sleeping bag, tent, stove, extra layers, and other gear.
  • 75+ liters: These backpacks are built for expeditions or through-hikes, allowing you to carry heavy loads over long distances, including specialized gear and supplies.

It’s important to balance the size of the backpack with what you actually need to carry. A larger pack than necessary can become cumbersome and increase your load unnecessarily, while a too-small pack will force you to leave essential gear behind.

3. Prioritize Fit and Comfort

Even the best backpack won’t be useful if it doesn’t fit properly. A well-fitting backpack will reduce strain on your back, shoulders, and hips, improving your overall comfort during your outdoor adventure.

Frame Type

Backpacks generally come in two types of frames:

  • Internal Frame: Most modern hiking and backpacking backpacks feature an internal frame. This frame is hidden within the backpack and provides support and structure. Internal frames allow for a snug, body-hugging fit, improving stability and comfort. They are ideal for carrying heavier loads and are more common in multi-day backpacks.
  • External Frame: External-frame backpacks have a visible frame that is attached to the outside of the pack. These backpacks offer better ventilation, as air can circulate between the frame and your back. They are typically used for carrying heavy loads over long distances, but they can feel bulky and less maneuverable than internal-frame packs.

Hip Belt and Shoulder Straps

A good backpack will have padded shoulder straps and a wide, supportive hip belt. The shoulder straps should be adjustable so you can fine-tune the fit. The hip belt is crucial because it helps distribute weight from your shoulders to your hips, reducing strain on your upper body.

When selecting a backpack, make sure to adjust the straps properly and test how it feels with weight inside. The pack should sit comfortably on your back, with the weight evenly distributed between your shoulders and hips.

Torso Length

Backpacks are designed to fit different torso lengths, so make sure to choose a pack that is tailored to your body. To find your torso length, measure the distance from the top of your hip bones to the base of your neck. Many backpacks have adjustable torso lengths, but some may be sized as “small,” “medium,” or “large.” Getting the right size ensures that the load is properly balanced and reduces discomfort during long hikes.

4. Evaluate the Backpack Material and Durability

The material of the backpack is critical for its durability and weather resistance. You want a backpack that can withstand the wear and tear of outdoor adventures while protecting your gear from the elements.

Common Backpack Materials

  • Nylon: Lightweight and durable, nylon is one of the most common materials used for outdoor backpacks. It is resistant to abrasion and relatively water-resistant, making it a great choice for general hiking and camping.
  • Polyester: Slightly heavier than nylon, polyester is durable and often used for cheaper backpacks. It tends to hold up well to wear and tear but may not be as water-resistant as nylon.
  • Cordura: A type of nylon that is known for its exceptional durability. Cordura is resistant to tears, abrasions, and scuffs, making it ideal for heavy-duty outdoor adventures.
  • Ripstop Fabric: Ripstop is a type of fabric that includes a reinforcing thread pattern that prevents small tears from spreading. It’s often used in backpacks that are designed to endure tough environments, such as climbing or mountaineering backpacks.

Weather Resistance

Weatherproofing is a key consideration, especially for extended trips where rain or snow is a possibility. Many backpacks come with a water-resistant coating or an integrated rain cover to protect your gear. Look for backpacks that have weatherproof zippers, sealed seams, and durable water-repellent coatings for added protection in inclement weather.

5. Think About Features and Organization

Different backpacks come with a variety of features designed to make your outdoor adventure easier and more efficient. Here are some important features to consider:

Hydration Reservoir Sleeve

A hydration reservoir sleeve is an essential feature for many hikers, especially those on longer trips. It allows you to carry a water bladder (hydration reservoir) and access water through a hose, without having to stop and fumble with bottles.

Compression Straps

Compression straps are used to tighten the backpack and compress your load. This feature can help balance the weight and reduce shifting while you move, making your backpack more stable and comfortable.

Side Pockets and Mesh Pockets

Side pockets are handy for storing smaller items like water bottles, snacks, or a map. Mesh pockets, often found on the sides or front of the backpack, are great for stashing wet gear like a jacket or gloves.

Top Lid or Flap

The top lid of a backpack is a useful feature that provides an additional storage compartment, which is perfect for storing items you need quick access to, like a headlamp, rain jacket, or first-aid kit.

Ventilation System

Long hikes in hot weather can lead to excessive sweating and discomfort, so some backpacks come equipped with a ventilation system that keeps your back cool by allowing air to flow between the pack and your body. Look for packs with mesh panels or ventilated back panels to improve airflow.

Accessibility

Make sure that the backpack provides easy access to your gear. Some backpacks feature a top-loading design, while others have a front or side zipper that allows you to open the bag and access your gear without having to unpack everything. Consider how you will be using the pack and what kind of access will be most convenient for your adventure.

6. Consider Your Budget

Outdoor backpacks vary greatly in price, and while it’s tempting to go for the cheapest option, investing in a quality pack will pay off in terms of comfort, durability, and performance. High-quality backpacks often feature superior materials, better suspension systems, and more thoughtful design, ensuring that you have a more enjoyable experience on the trail.

However, there are plenty of good backpacks available at different price points. Set a budget that works for you and choose the best pack within that range. Keep in mind that durability and comfort are worth prioritizing, as these factors will ultimately make or break your experience.

7. Test Before You Buy

If possible, always try on the backpack before purchasing it. Many outdoor retailers offer opportunities to test the fit of the backpack by loading it with weight and walking around the store. This is your chance to ensure the straps fit correctly, the backpack sits comfortably on your hips, and that the overall fit feels right for your body.

If you’re buying online, check the return policy and make sure you can exchange the pack if it doesn’t work for you.

Choosing the best backpack for your outdoor adventures requires careful consideration of your needs, body type, and the activities you’ll be doing. By understanding the different types of backpacks, their features, and how to choose the right fit, you can find the ideal pack that will keep you comfortable and organized, ensuring your adventure is a success. Happy trails!
