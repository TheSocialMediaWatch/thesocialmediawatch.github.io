Top 10 Luxury Hotels You Must Visit in 2025


In 2025, the world of luxury hospitality is taking it up a notch, offering experiences that go beyond simply lavish accommodations. Whether you’re seeking a private retreat in the heart of nature, a contemporary urban sanctuary, or a beachfront paradise, the top luxury hotels for 2025 are set to redefine what it means to indulge in five-star service, world-class amenities, and unforgettable experiences. Here’s a guide to the most luxurious hotels you need to add to your bucket list.

1. The St. Regis Maldives Vommuli Resort, Maldives

The St. Regis Maldives Vommuli Resort continues to stand out as a top destination for travelers looking for ultimate luxury in the Indian Ocean. Situated on the picturesque island of Vommuli, this resort combines natural beauty with world-class facilities. The resort features overwater and beach villas, each designed with lavish materials and breathtaking views. Guests can indulge in the resort’s butler service, private pools, and exquisite dining experiences that fuse international flavors with local Maldivian influences.

Notable highlights include the Iridium Spa, offering a wide range of holistic treatments, and the resort’s signature wine cellar, which boasts an impressive collection of fine wines. For those looking for adventure, the resort offers diving and snorkeling excursions, and you can even charter a private yacht for a sunset cruise.

2. Aman Tokyo, Japan

Aman Tokyo, nestled in the heart of Japan’s capital, is a perfect blend of serenity and sophistication. This sleek, contemporary hotel is housed in a 38-story skyscraper, offering panoramic views of the city’s skyline and Mount Fuji. The design of the hotel marries modern architectural elegance with traditional Japanese elements, creating a calming atmosphere that contrasts with the busy metropolis below.

Guests can experience the renowned Aman hospitality, with bespoke services, personalized itineraries, and private access to exclusive cultural experiences, such as private tea ceremonies and guided temple tours. The Aman Spa is a haven for relaxation, offering traditional Japanese healing therapies alongside luxurious treatments. Dining at Aman Tokyo is a treat with a wide selection of Japanese delicacies and international dishes prepared by world-class chefs.

3. Four Seasons Resort Bali at Sayan, Indonesia

For those who seek both adventure and relaxation, Four Seasons Resort Bali at Sayan offers the best of both worlds. Located in the heart of Bali’s lush Ayung River Valley, this resort is a true oasis. The stunning surroundings are complemented by luxury villas and suites that feature private plunge pools, open-air showers, and expansive terraces overlooking the jungle.

This eco-conscious resort is dedicated to sustainability, offering organic dining experiences at the resort’s signature restaurants and providing guests with access to organic farms where they can learn about local agriculture. The resort also offers a variety of cultural experiences, from Balinese dance performances to cooking classes with local chefs. Spa treatments, wellness programs, and yoga sessions are available to enhance your relaxation.

4. The Ritz-Carlton, Kyoto, Japan

For an unmatched blend of luxury, history, and culture, The Ritz-Carlton, Kyoto, stands as one of the best places to stay in Japan’s ancient capital. Set on the banks of the Kamogawa River, this hotel is a short distance from iconic Kyoto landmarks like Kinkaku-ji and Gion District. The interiors of the hotel reflect Japan’s rich heritage, with elements of Zen-inspired minimalism and traditional Kyoto-style architecture.

Guests can unwind in spacious rooms with views of the river, private gardens, or the Higashiyama Mountains. The Ritz-Carlton Kyoto also offers a wide range of dining options, including sophisticated kaiseki cuisine, teppanyaki, and fine Italian dining. The hotel’s luxurious spa offers traditional Japanese wellness treatments, and the service is unparalleled, with bespoke experiences tailored to each guest’s preferences.

5. The Dorchester, London, United Kingdom

For a quintessentially British luxury experience, The Dorchester in London is an iconic institution. This five-star hotel has been a favorite of royalty, celebrities, and dignitaries for decades, thanks to its unparalleled service, prime location, and historic charm. Situated in the heart of Mayfair, the hotel provides easy access to London’s top attractions, including Buckingham Palace, Hyde Park, and the high-end shopping of Oxford Street.

The Dorchester is renowned for its elegant rooms and suites, many of which offer spectacular views of Hyde Park. Guests can enjoy Michelin-starred dining at Alain Ducasse at The Dorchester, experience a traditional afternoon tea, or unwind in the hotel’s spa and wellness center. The hotel’s impeccable service includes personalized butler assistance, ensuring that your stay is nothing short of extraordinary.

6. Bulgari Resort, Dubai, United Arab Emirates

Dubai is known for its extravagant architecture and luxurious hotels, and the Bulgari Resort is no exception. Situated on Jumeirah Bay Island, this resort blends contemporary design with Italian elegance, offering guests a private retreat with stunning views of the Arabian Gulf. The resort features spacious villas, suites, and private residences, each showcasing the finest craftsmanship and luxurious amenities.

Guests can indulge in the Bulgari Spa, which offers a range of treatments using the brand’s exclusive products. Dining options include the Michelin-starred Il Ristorante, offering authentic Italian cuisine with a modern twist, and the relaxed beachside restaurant, which specializes in Mediterranean-inspired dishes. The resort also boasts a private beach, yacht club, and exclusive boutiques, ensuring a complete luxury experience.

7. The Brando, Tetiaroa, French Polynesia

For the ultimate in privacy and eco-luxury, The Brando is a must-visit destination. Located on the remote Tetiaroa Atoll, a private island once owned by Marlon Brando, this resort offers an exclusive escape in one of the most beautiful and pristine environments on earth. The resort’s villas, each with its own private pool, are designed to blend seamlessly with the island’s lush natural surroundings.

Sustainability is at the heart of The Brando, with the resort focusing on preserving the local ecosystem while providing luxury accommodations and services. Guests can enjoy a range of water activities, from snorkeling and diving to paddleboarding and kayaking. The resort also features a world-class spa, offering Polynesian-inspired treatments and wellness programs. Dining options include French and Polynesian fusion cuisine, served in idyllic beachfront settings.

8. Rosewood Hong Kong, Hong Kong

Set against the dramatic backdrop of Victoria Harbour, Rosewood Hong Kong offers a sophisticated blend of luxury, modern design, and impeccable service. Located in the vibrant Tsim Sha Tsui district, the hotel is within walking distance of the city’s best shopping, dining, and entertainment venues. The hotel’s rooms and suites are some of the most spacious in the city, with floor-to-ceiling windows offering panoramic views of the harbor and skyline.

The Rosewood Hong Kong features a stunning rooftop bar, which is perfect for enjoying a sunset cocktail while taking in the views of Hong Kong’s skyline. The hotel also offers a world-class spa, a fitness center with state-of-the-art equipment, and a selection of fine dining restaurants, including French-inspired cuisine and contemporary Cantonese dishes.

9. Mandarin Oriental, Marrakech, Morocco

Marrakech is one of the most vibrant and culturally rich cities in the world, and the Mandarin Oriental in Morocco offers a luxurious and serene oasis in the midst of the bustling city. Set within expansive gardens and featuring traditional Moroccan architecture, this hotel combines contemporary design with local craftsmanship to create a truly unique atmosphere.

The hotel’s spacious villas and suites come with private pools and gardens, providing guests with the ultimate in privacy and relaxation. The Mandarin Oriental also boasts a beautiful spa, where you can experience traditional Moroccan hammams, as well as a range of treatments inspired by ancient beauty rituals. Dining options include Moroccan and Mediterranean cuisine, with a focus on fresh, locally sourced ingredients.

10. Singita Kruger National Park, South Africa

For an unforgettable African safari experience, Singita Kruger National Park offers one of the most luxurious and exclusive stays in the world. Located in the heart of South Africa’s famous Kruger National Park, this lodge offers the perfect combination of luxury, adventure, and wildlife. The private concession ensures an intimate experience, away from the crowds, and offers unparalleled access to some of the best safari experiences in the world.

The lodge’s suites and villas are designed to immerse guests in the surrounding natural beauty while providing all the comforts of a five-star hotel. Guests can enjoy guided safari tours, wildlife photography, and birdwatching, as well as indulge in exceptional dining and spa experiences. The lodge also supports conservation initiatives, helping to preserve the unique wildlife and environment of the region.

These ten luxury hotels are just the beginning of the exciting new world of high-end travel in 2025. Whether you’re looking to experience unparalleled relaxation in a remote paradise, immerse yourself in a vibrant cultural setting, or indulge in a cosmopolitan retreat, these destinations offer something for every discerning traveler. As the world of luxury hospitality continues to evolve, these hotels represent the pinnacle of what’s possible in terms of service, design, and unique experiences.
