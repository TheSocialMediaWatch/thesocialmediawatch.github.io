Cartoons That Are Actually Great for Adults


Cartoons often evoke memories of childhood, bright colors, silly characters, and carefree stories. But what if I told you that there are cartoons that are not just for kids? Over the years, animated series have evolved into sophisticated, witty, and thought-provoking art forms that are just as appealing to adults. From deep philosophical undertones to sharp humor, these shows tackle complex themes and provide engaging narratives that appeal to an older audience. Whether you’re a fan of dark comedy, social commentary, or surreal plots, there’s something in the world of adult animation for everyone.

The Rise of Adult Animation

Adult animation has come a long way since its early days. While cartoons like The Flintstones or The Simpsons in their early years were seen as a new frontier for television, it was the growth of cable TV and the internet that helped adult animation truly flourish. Today, animated shows no longer have to rely on slapstick humor or basic storytelling to engage their audience. Instead, they can explore deep themes such as mental health, politics, relationships, and the absurdities of modern life—all wrapped in a comedic or dramatic package.

In this post, we will explore some of the most influential and entertaining cartoons that are perfect for adult audiences, proving that animation is not just for children anymore.

1. BoJack Horseman

A Dark Comedy That Tackles Life’s Toughest Questions

BoJack Horseman is a show that may seem, at first glance, like just another quirky adult cartoon. However, it quickly proves to be so much more. The series follows BoJack, a washed-up 1990s TV star who happens to be an anthropomorphic horse. Over its six-season run, BoJack Horseman masterfully blends humor, tragedy, and social commentary, dealing with addiction, mental illness, and the quest for redemption.

The show’s surreal, dark comedy is combined with poignant moments that touch on universal issues like self-doubt and the impact of fame. While it certainly isn’t for the faint of heart, BoJack Horseman is one of the most emotionally intelligent cartoons to date. It uses animation to explore the messiness of human existence in ways few live-action dramas dare to.

Why Adults Love It:

  • Realistic Depictions of Mental Health: The show doesn’t shy away from depicting struggles like depression, addiction, and existential crises.
  • Layered Humor: The humor is both clever and absurd, with rapid-fire jokes that provide a lighthearted balance to its darker themes.
  • Philosophical Undertones: BoJack Horseman explores ideas about self-worth, the nature of success, and the possibility of redemption.

2. Rick and Morty

A Mind-Bending Mix of Sci-Fi and Philosophy

If you’re into mind-bending sci-fi and absurd humor, Rick and Morty is a must-watch. The series centers on Rick Sanchez, a genius but wildly reckless scientist, and his easily manipulated grandson, Morty. Together, they embark on interdimensional adventures that are as chaotic as they are thought-provoking.

At its core, Rick and Morty explores themes of nihilism, free will, and the absurdity of the universe. It questions the meaning of life and the consequences of unchecked intellectualism, all while making you laugh uncontrollably. The animation is filled with vibrant, inventive worlds, bizarre creatures, and fast-paced action, but the real appeal lies in its philosophical musings about the human condition.

Why Adults Love It:

  • Intellectual Humor: The show is packed with references to scientific theories, philosophy, and pop culture.
  • Existential Themes: Rick and Morty pushes viewers to confront uncomfortable questions about life, purpose, and the universe.
  • Surreal, Absurd Worldbuilding: The sci-fi elements and dimensions make every episode feel fresh and unpredictable.

3. Futurama

A Perfect Blend of Comedy and Social Commentary

Set in the future, Futurama follows the adventures of Philip J. Fry, a slacker who is accidentally frozen for 1,000 years and wakes up in a futuristic society. Alongside an eclectic crew of characters, including a robot with a drinking problem and a cyclopean captain, Fry navigates a world filled with bizarre technological advancements, alien species, and plenty of social satire.

Futurama is famous for its witty humor, clever parodies, and surprisingly emotional moments. Although it leans heavily into absurdity and satire, the show also explores serious issues like the ethics of artificial intelligence, environmentalism, and the nature of humanity.

Why Adults Love It:

  • Clever Social Commentary: The series takes on modern issues like corporate greed, environmental degradation, and the consequences of unchecked technology.
  • Emotional Depth: Despite its comedic surface, Futurama is known for some heart-wrenching and thought-provoking moments that resonate deeply.
  • Timeless Humor: The combination of quirky characters, wordplay, and visual gags make it a delight for adult audiences.

4. The Venture Bros.

A Satirical Take on Adventure and Superheroes

The Venture Bros. is a parody of superhero tropes and classic adventure stories. The series follows the Venture family—Dr. Rusty Venture, his two sons, and their bodyguard Brock Samson—as they navigate a world filled with secret organizations, mad scientists, and eccentric villains.

At its core, The Venture Bros. is an exploration of what happens when the heroic idealism of adventure stories clashes with the gritty, flawed reality of adult life. It’s both a loving tribute to and a sharp critique of the superhero genre, blending dark humor with real emotional stakes. The series delves into themes of identity, failure, and the consequences of living in the shadow of greatness.

Why Adults Love It:

  • Complex Characters: Each character in The Venture Bros. has depth and struggles with their own personal issues, making them relatable to adult audiences.
  • Adult Themes: The show explores themes like parental expectations, existential crises, and the disillusionment of adulthood.
  • Smart Parody: It satirizes superhero culture in a way that is both hilarious and thought-provoking.

5. South Park

Pushing Boundaries with Humor and Social Critique

South Park is one of the longest-running and most controversial adult cartoons out there. Known for its crude humor and fearless approach to sensitive topics, the show follows four boys—Stan, Kyle, Cartman, and Kenny—as they navigate the absurdities of life in a small Colorado town.

While its early years were focused on shock value and pushing the limits of taste, South Park has evolved into a sharp political and social satire. It addresses everything from celebrity culture to global politics with a unique blend of humor and critical analysis. The show’s rapid response to current events, often producing episodes within a week of a major news story, has made it an important voice in contemporary media.

Why Adults Love It:

  • Unapologetically Bold: The show doesn’t shy away from controversy, making it perfect for those who enjoy pushing boundaries.
  • Social and Political Commentary: South Park provides sharp, satirical takes on modern issues like politics, religion, and pop culture.
  • Dark Humor: The show blends juvenile humor with intelligent commentary, making it both entertaining and thought-provoking.

6. Archer

A Spy Comedy with Adult Humor and Sharp Writing

Archer is a sharp, fast-paced animated series that blends espionage with adult humor. The show follows Sterling Archer, a suave but inept secret agent, as he navigates the chaotic world of espionage, all while dealing with his dysfunctional colleagues at ISIS (International Secret Intelligence Service).

The show is known for its witty dialogue, pop culture references, and the over-the-top personalities of its characters. Archer is a parody of spy thrillers, but it’s also a smart, adult comedy that touches on relationships, workplace dynamics, and the absurdity of modern life.

Why Adults Love It:

  • Quick-Witted Humor: The dialogue in Archer is fast-paced and full of clever one-liners, making it a delight for fans of sharp comedy.
  • Complex Relationships: The show delves into the complicated relationships between its characters, adding depth to its humor.
  • Spy Parody: If you enjoy spy thrillers but want something more irreverent and lighthearted, Archer is the perfect mix.

7. Big Mouth

An Honest, and Often Hilarious, Look at Puberty

Big Mouth is an animated series that takes a brutally honest and comedic look at the awkward, confusing, and often hilarious aspects of puberty. The show follows a group of pre-teens as they navigate the trials and tribulations of growing up, with plenty of supernatural elements thrown in for good measure.

With characters like the “Hormone Monster,” Big Mouth provides an unfiltered, often crude, but hilarious portrayal of adolescence. It’s a series that doesn’t shy away from awkward conversations about sex, body changes, and personal insecurities, all while maintaining a sharp sense of humor.

Why Adults Love It:

  • Honest Portrayal of Puberty: Big Mouth dives into the uncomfortable and often taboo aspects of growing up, making it relatable for many.
  • Bold Humor: The show doesn’t hold back on jokes that push boundaries, appealing to adults who appreciate irreverent comedy.
  • Nostalgia: For those who remember their own awkward teenage years, Big Mouth offers a funny and exaggerated look at puberty.

8. Tuca & Bertie

A Feminist and Surreal Exploration of Friendship

Tuca & Bertie is an animated series created by Lisa Hanawalt, who also worked on BoJack Horseman. The show follows the lives of two anthropomorphic birds—Tuca, a carefree and wild toucan, and Bertie, an anxious and more grounded songbird. The series explores themes of female friendship, mental health, and the complexities of modern womanhood, all while delivering absurd, surreal humor.

While it’s visually striking and quirky, Tuca & Bertie is also an honest portrayal of the challenges women face in their personal and professional lives. Its feminist themes, combined with its bold animation and unique storytelling, make it a standout in the world of adult animation.

Why Adults Love It:

  • Feminist Themes: The show explores the ups and downs of female friendship, mental health, and the challenges of being a woman in today’s world.
  • Bold, Surreal Humor: The animation is colorful, strange, and filled with unexpected twists, making it visually stunning and unpredictable.
  • Deep Emotional Moments: Beneath the humor, the series also delves into real-life struggles, making it both funny and meaningful.

Conclusion

As we’ve seen, adult cartoons have come a long way from simple slapstick humor and over-the-top antics. These shows are now exploring deeper, more meaningful themes and providing audiences with rich, layered stories. Whether you’re into dark comedy, philosophical musings, or satirical takes on modern life, there’s an adult cartoon out there for you.

So next time you’re looking for something to watch, remember that animated shows are no longer just for kids. They can be just as profound, thought-provoking, and hilarious as any live-action series, making them the perfect entertainment for adults who want more than just fluff and filler.
