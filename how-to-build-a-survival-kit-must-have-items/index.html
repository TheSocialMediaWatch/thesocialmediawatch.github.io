How to Build a Survival Kit: Must-Have Items


Building a survival kit is one of the most important steps in preparing for emergencies, whether you’re heading into the wilderness, preparing for natural disasters, or simply want to be ready for unexpected situations. A well-crafted survival kit can be the difference between life and death in a critical moment. In this guide, we’ll break down essential items that should be included in any survival kit to ensure you’re prepared for whatever comes your way.

1. Water Filtration and Storage

Water is the most critical element for survival. The human body can survive for weeks without food but only a few days without water. Ensuring that you have clean water to drink should be a top priority when assembling your survival kit.

Water Filtration System

A portable water filter or water purification tablets are essential for ensuring that you can safely drink water from natural sources. Many survival experts recommend having a high-quality, compact water filter like a LifeStraw or Sawyer Mini Water Filter, which can remove bacteria and protozoa from water, making it safe to consume.

Water Storage

You should also include a means of storing clean water. A collapsible water container or durable plastic bottles can help you carry and store water for immediate use. Always try to have at least one gallon of water per person per day for a 72-hour survival period.

2. First Aid Kit

Accidents happen, and in a survival situation, access to medical care may not be immediately available. A well-stocked first aid kit is an absolute must. It should include, at a minimum:

  • Bandages of various sizes: for cuts, abrasions, and blisters
  • Antiseptic wipes or solution: to clean wounds and prevent infection
  • Sterile gauze pads and adhesive tape: for larger wounds or abrasions
  • Antibiotic ointment: to reduce the risk of infection
  • Pain relief medication: such as aspirin, ibuprofen, or acetaminophen
  • Tweezers: for removing splinters or thorns
  • Thermometer: to monitor body temperature in case of illness
  • Elastic bandage (like an Ace bandage): for sprains or strains
  • Instant cold packs: to reduce swelling or injuries
  • Burn gel or cream: to treat burns and scalds

While these items are essential, it’s also important to customize your first aid kit based on specific needs, such as any personal medications or special health concerns.

3. Fire Starting Tools

Fire is crucial for warmth, cooking, and sterilization. Without a reliable fire-starting method, survival in harsh conditions becomes much more difficult.

Fire Starter Options

  • Waterproof Matches: These are reliable even in wet conditions. Store them in a waterproof container to keep them dry.
  • Lighter: A standard disposable lighter is compact and can light many fires.
  • Fire Steel or Ferro Rod: This is a reliable, long-lasting fire starter that works even in extreme weather conditions.
  • Cotton Balls and Vaseline: These make excellent tinder. By soaking cotton balls in Vaseline, you create a fire starter that burns for several minutes, making it easier to start a fire with minimal resources.

Fire Safety

When packing your fire-starting tools, ensure you have a small, portable container for storing tinder. It’s also a good idea to carry a fire blanket or fire extinguisher to help control any accidental fires.

4. Food Supply

While water is more immediately essential than food, having a supply of ready-to-eat meals can make the difference in how long you can survive if stranded. The food in your survival kit should be lightweight, easy to store, and have a long shelf life.

MREs (Meals Ready-to-Eat)

MREs are ideal because they are designed to be compact, calorie-dense, and easy to prepare. MREs are self-contained meals that include everything needed for consumption, including utensils, a flameless ration heater (for heating the food), and dessert or snacks. Each MRE can provide a full meal, so having a few days’ worth of MREs in your survival kit ensures that you’ll have sustenance.

Emergency Food Bars

Another good option for your survival kit is emergency food bars. These bars are designed to be high in calories, with a shelf life of up to five years. They are small, lightweight, and easy to carry, making them ideal for survival kits.

Freeze-Dried Food

Freeze-dried meals are another excellent choice, especially for longer-term survival situations. These meals are lightweight, easy to prepare by adding hot water, and have a long shelf life.

5. Shelter and Warmth

In a survival situation, shelter and warmth are necessary to prevent hypothermia, particularly in cold climates. Depending on the environment, there are a few options for shelter and keeping warm.

Emergency Mylar Blanket

A lightweight, compact emergency blanket made from reflective Mylar can help retain body heat and protect you from wind and rain. These blankets are often used in survival kits because they are effective in reducing heat loss.

Tent or Tarp

A small, lightweight, and weather-resistant tarp or emergency shelter tent can provide protection from the elements. These items are usually waterproof and easy to set up, offering immediate relief in rain or snow.

Sleeping Bag

For cold weather survival, a high-quality sleeping bag that can withstand low temperatures is essential. Even if you’re not facing extreme conditions, a good sleeping bag ensures you remain warm during the night.

Hand Warmers

Small, disposable hand warmers can provide extra heat in your clothing or sleeping bag, making it easier to manage low temperatures. They can be activated and placed in pockets, gloves, or boots.

6. Tools and Multi-Use Equipment

Tools are essential for a variety of tasks in a survival scenario, from building a shelter to preparing food and creating fire. Below are some basic tools to include in your survival kit.

Multi-Tool

A high-quality multi-tool, like a Swiss Army knife or a Leatherman, is a must-have for any survival situation. These compact tools usually include blades, screwdrivers, can openers, pliers, and more. With just one tool, you can complete a wide range of tasks.

Folding Knife

A durable folding knife is another essential tool for self-defense, food preparation, or cutting materials for shelter. Choose a reliable, high-quality knife that fits comfortably in your hand and can be easily deployed.

Paracord

Paracord is versatile and can be used in numerous ways, such as securing tents, making traps, creating fishing lines, or even as a clothesline. A 100-foot coil of paracord takes up very little space in your kit but has countless uses.

Duct Tape

Duct tape is incredibly versatile and can be used to repair equipment, seal leaks, or create makeshift bandages. A small roll of duct tape should be included in every survival kit.

Compass

A compass is a valuable tool for navigation, particularly in the wilderness. Even if you’re familiar with the terrain, a compass can help you avoid disorientation and ensure you’re heading in the right direction.

7. Communication Devices

Staying in touch or signaling for help is essential in any survival situation. Without proper communication, you could remain stranded longer than necessary. Here are some options to consider:

Whistle

A loud, high-pitched whistle is one of the easiest ways to attract attention from a distance. Carrying a whistle that can be heard from far away can significantly increase your chances of being located.

Signal Mirror

A signal mirror is another simple yet effective way to signal for help. By reflecting sunlight, a mirror can be used to send a distress signal over long distances, especially in open areas.

Emergency Radio

An emergency radio with NOAA weather alerts can provide crucial information during a crisis, including weather warnings and evacuation instructions. Consider one that is hand-cranked or solar-powered, so you don’t rely on batteries.

8. Personal Items and Comfort

In addition to the essential survival gear, it’s also important to consider your comfort and personal well-being. Here are a few personal items you might want to include:

Extra Clothing

Include a change of clothes, especially socks and underwear. Make sure you have clothing that is appropriate for the weather, such as warm layers for cold climates and breathable, moisture-wicking fabrics for hot climates.

Sunglasses

Protecting your eyes from the sun’s glare is important, particularly in areas where sunlight is intense or if you’re spending a lot of time in snow or sand. A pair of durable sunglasses with UV protection is a good addition to your kit.

Toilet Paper or Wet Wipes

Hygiene is an important consideration, especially in long-term survival situations. Carrying toilet paper, wet wipes, or a small bottle of hand sanitizer will help you maintain cleanliness and prevent infections.

Cash

It’s always wise to have a small amount of cash in your survival kit. If you are in a situation where electronic payment systems are down, cash could be critical for purchasing supplies or gaining access to help.

Conclusion

Building a comprehensive survival kit requires careful planning and consideration of the specific environment you may find yourself in. From water filtration to shelter, first aid, tools, and communication devices, every item in your survival kit should serve a purpose in ensuring your safety and well-being.

By taking the time to gather and maintain these items, you increase your chances of surviving an emergency, whether you’re facing a natural disaster, lost in the wilderness, or in any unexpected situation. Don’t wait for a disaster to strike—start assembling your survival kit today, and ensure that you’re ready for anything life throws your way.
