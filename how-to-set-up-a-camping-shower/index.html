How to Set Up a Camping Shower


Camping is an adventure that allows you to connect with nature, but sometimes it can feel a bit uncomfortable, especially when it comes to cleanliness. After a long day of hiking, swimming, or simply being out in the wilderness, a refreshing shower can make all the difference. Setting up a camping shower may seem like a hassle, but with the right tools and knowledge, it can be a straightforward process that ensures you stay fresh and clean during your outdoor excursions.

In this comprehensive guide, we will walk you through everything you need to know about setting up a camping shower, from choosing the right shower system to creating a private space and maintaining hygiene in the wilderness. Whether you’re planning a weekend trip or a longer adventure, this guide will ensure you enjoy the benefits of a proper shower while camping.

1. Why You Need a Camping Shower

The first question you might be asking is, why bother with a camping shower at all? Camping is supposed to be about embracing the outdoors, so why do you need modern luxuries like a shower?

While it’s true that camping allows you to escape from your daily routine, staying clean is crucial for comfort, health, and safety. A shower can help you:

  • Feel refreshed: After a long day of outdoor activities, a shower can help you wash off sweat, dirt, and grime, leaving you feeling rejuvenated.
  • Prevent skin irritation and infections: Sweating and exposure to dirt can lead to rashes or infections, especially in sensitive areas. A shower helps minimize this risk.
  • Maintain hygiene: Keeping clean reduces the chance of attracting insects and pests that are drawn to sweat and body odors. It also helps prevent body odor, which can become uncomfortable in close quarters.

In addition to the practical benefits, a shower can enhance your overall camping experience, making your adventure more enjoyable and less stressful.

2. Types of Camping Showers

Before setting up your camping shower, it’s important to choose the right type for your needs. There are several options available, each with its own pros and cons. Here’s a look at some of the most popular types:

2.1 Solar Showers

Solar camping showers are one of the most common options for outdoor enthusiasts. These showers typically consist of a large bag made of durable material that holds water, and they rely on the sun to heat the water.

Advantages:

  • Environmentally friendly: Solar showers use no electricity or fuel.
  • Portable: They are lightweight and easy to carry.
  • Affordable: Generally cheaper than other options.

Disadvantages:

  • Dependence on sunlight: Solar showers only work when there is enough sunlight to heat the water, so they may not be reliable on cloudy days or in colder weather.
  • Limited water supply: Solar showers typically have a limited capacity (usually around 5-10 gallons), so you may need to refill the bag if you want to take a longer shower.

2.2 Battery-Powered Showers

Battery-powered camping showers, also known as portable shower systems, use a pump to provide water pressure, making them more like a traditional shower. You fill a container with water, and the pump delivers it to a showerhead.

Advantages:

  • Convenient and efficient: These showers provide steady water pressure, similar to what you’d experience at home.
  • No need for sunlight: Battery-powered showers can work in any weather, making them more reliable than solar showers.
  • Variety of sizes: Available in different sizes and capacities, allowing you to choose based on your needs.

Disadvantages:

  • Heavier and bulkier: These showers tend to be more substantial than solar showers and may require more storage space.
  • Need for batteries: You’ll need to ensure that you have charged batteries or access to power to keep the shower running.

2.3 Gravity Showers

Gravity-fed showers rely on the force of gravity to deliver water. These showers typically consist of a bag or container suspended above the user, and water flows downward when the valve is opened. They are similar to solar showers but work without requiring direct sunlight.

Advantages:

  • Simplicity: Gravity showers are easy to set up and operate, requiring no pumps or complicated mechanisms.
  • Quiet operation: Since there’s no motor or pump, gravity showers operate silently.
  • Low maintenance: With fewer moving parts, gravity showers require less maintenance than battery-powered systems.

Disadvantages:

  • Limited water pressure: Gravity-fed showers often have lower water pressure compared to battery-powered models, which may make for a less satisfying shower experience.
  • Dependence on elevation: You need to hang the water bag or container from a height to generate water flow, so finding a suitable place to set it up can be tricky.

2.4 Shower Tents and Privacy Shelters

Regardless of the shower type you choose, you’ll need a private space to set it up. A shower tent or privacy shelter is a lightweight, portable structure designed to give you the privacy you need while showering outdoors.

Advantages:

  • Privacy: A shower tent allows you to shower without feeling exposed, making the experience more comfortable.
  • Convenient: Many shower tents come with features like mesh windows for ventilation, extra storage for toiletries, and drainage floors to keep water from pooling inside.
  • Quick setup: Shower tents are easy to set up and take down, offering a fast solution for privacy.

Disadvantages:

  • Space requirements: Depending on the size of the tent, it may take up more room in your campsite.
  • Weather concerns: Shower tents may not hold up well in extremely windy or rainy conditions, so make sure you choose a sturdy one.

3. Setting Up Your Camping Shower

Now that you have an idea of which type of shower system is best for you, let’s go over how to set it up in a way that ensures comfort, convenience, and hygiene during your camping trip.

3.1 Select the Right Location

The first step in setting up your shower is to find the right location. Choose a spot that is:

  • Away from camp: To avoid splashing water near your tents or cooking area, set up your shower a little further from your main campsite.
  • Near a water source: If possible, choose a location near a river, lake, or stream, as this will make it easier to refill your shower system.
  • On flat ground: A level surface ensures that water drains properly and helps prevent the shower system from tipping over.

3.2 Set Up Your Shower System

Depending on the type of shower you’ve chosen, the setup will vary:

  • Solar shower: Hang the shower bag from a tree branch or a suitable structure that’s at least 6-8 feet above the ground. Fill the bag with water, ensuring the opening is sealed tightly, and leave it in the sun to warm up for at least an hour before use.
  • Battery-powered shower: Set up your water container and connect the pump to the showerhead. Make sure the pump is fully charged or has fresh batteries. Place the water container on a stable surface that is higher than your showerhead to ensure the pump works effectively.
  • Gravity shower: Hang the water bag from a high point, such as a tree branch or shower pole. Open the valve and allow gravity to push the water down through the hose and showerhead.

3.3 Create a Private Shower Area

While some camping showers come with built-in privacy, others will require a separate shower tent. If you don’t have a shower tent, you can:

  • Use a tarp or large piece of fabric to create a makeshift shower enclosure.
  • Hang a sheet or blanket around a tree to block the view.
  • Choose a secluded area surrounded by natural foliage for a more discreet option.

3.4 Add Extra Comforts

If you’re looking for a more comfortable shower experience, consider adding a few extras:

  • Non-slip mat: Place a mat or towel at the base of your shower area to provide traction and comfort while you shower.
  • Shower caddy: Use a small caddy or bag to store soap, shampoo, and other toiletries.
  • Towel: Have a quick-dry towel on hand to dry off after your shower.

3.5 Drainage and Clean-Up

Make sure the water from your shower drains properly. If you’re using a solar or gravity-fed shower, the water will naturally flow down into the ground. However, you’ll want to avoid polluting the environment with soap and other hygiene products, so use biodegradable soap whenever possible.

If your campsite requires it, consider setting up a small drainage system using a shower mat or catchment tray to direct the water away from sensitive areas.

4. Maintaining Hygiene While Camping

Taking a shower is just one aspect of maintaining hygiene while camping. Here are a few additional tips to ensure you stay clean and comfortable during your adventure:

  • Use biodegradable products: Always choose eco-friendly, biodegradable soap and shampoo to minimize your environmental impact.
  • Hand hygiene: Bring along hand sanitizer or hand wipes for quick clean-ups when soap and water aren’t available.
  • Personal hygiene: Don’t forget about other hygiene habits, such as brushing your teeth, washing your hands before eating, and changing into fresh clothes.

5. Final Thoughts

Setting up a camping shower may seem like a complicated task, but with the right equipment and planning, it can enhance your overall camping experience. Whether you opt for a solar shower, a battery-powered system, or a simple gravity-fed shower, having the ability to wash off after a long day in the wild is a luxury that makes your camping trip more comfortable and enjoyable.

Now that you have all the knowledge you need, it’s time to hit the great outdoors, set up your camping shower, and make your next adventure a truly refreshing one. Happy camping!
