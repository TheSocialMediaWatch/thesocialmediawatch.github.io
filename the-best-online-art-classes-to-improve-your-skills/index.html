The Best Online Art Classes to Improve Your Skills


For many creative individuals, art serves as an outlet for self-expression, exploration, and professional growth. Whether you’re a beginner looking to grasp the basics or an experienced artist searching for ways to refine your craft, online art classes offer a flexible and convenient way to elevate your artistic abilities. With countless options available today, finding the best classes to match your goals and expertise can be overwhelming. This guide will highlight some of the most effective online art courses and platforms, enabling you to master new techniques, expand your skill set, and nurture your passion for art.

Why Choose Online Art Classes?

The popularity of online education has soared in recent years, and platforms offering art instruction are no exception. Virtual art classes provide numerous benefits:

  1. Flexible Learning: Learn at your own pace, revisiting lessons when needed, without the constraints of a rigid schedule.
  2. Access to Professionals: Many online classes are taught by renowned artists, illustrators, and animators who share their expertise.
  3. Diverse Subjects: From digital art and animation to oil painting and sketching, online platforms offer courses spanning every niche.
  4. Cost-Effective: Compared to traditional art school tuition, the cost of many online art classes is significantly lower, making quality education more accessible.
  5. Global Community: You’ll connect with instructors and fellow students from around the world, enriching your artistic journey with different perspectives.

Whether you aim to start small or take advanced lessons, online art classes can be tailored to your personal needs.

Top Online Art Classes and Platforms

1. Domestika: Art Courses for Every Skill Level

Domestika is a hub of creativity, offering thousands of courses in everything from watercolor painting to graphic design. Instructors are often industry professionals, meaning you’ll receive high-quality insights and training. Classes feature comprehensive lesson plans, and many come with access to downloadable project files to help enhance your hands-on learning experience.

Domestika excels at beginner-friendly courses, introducing foundational techniques you can build upon. Its courses are also ideal for artists who want to explore new mediums—whether brush lettering, character illustration, or even ceramics.

2. Skillshare: Develop Artistic Techniques with Variety

Skillshare is an online learning platform known for its wide array of creative courses. It covers topics such as drawing, illustration, photography, and animation. With highly engaging projects, Skillshare classes encourage active participation throughout the learning process.

The platform’s subscription model allows access to all classes, so you can explore different areas without committing to a single course. Detailed reviews and personalized recommendations make it easy to discover classes that align with your artistic goals, whether you’re looking to sharpen your skills or explore new creative avenues.

3. Proko: Perfect Your Drawing and Anatomy Skills

Proko is widely respected among budding illustrators and professionals alike for its focused art tutorials. It specializes in figure drawing, anatomy, and portraiture—all essential skills for creating realistic and dynamic artworks.

What sets Proko apart is its practical emphasis: You won’t just learn techniques; you’ll learn how to apply them effectively. With step-by-step instructions, feedback forums, and downloadable reference materials, Proko enables thorough learning at your own pace.

4. CG Spectrum: High-End Animation and Digital Art Training

For budding animators, digital painters, and storytellers, CG Spectrum is one of the most specialized platforms available today. Known for its connections to the film and gaming industries, this platform offers training in 3D modeling, digital sculpting, VFX, game design, and more.

Their courses go beyond beginner levels and cater to advanced artists seeking a career in the entertainment industry. CG Spectrum pairs students with industry mentors to receive one-on-one feedback, which makes mastering complex software and techniques even more effective.

5. Udemy: Affordable Courses with Broad Coverage

Udemy is a popular online learning platform that offers affordable courses for artists of all levels. It has an extensive library of classes covering topics such as painting, digital illustration, graphic design, and art fundamentals.

What makes Udemy stand out is the sheer variety of options available, often presented at discounted rates. Courses are structured into video tutorials, many of which come with lifetime access, so you can move through them at your own convenience. Interactive assignments and quizzes also make learning more engaging.

6. CreativeLive: Explore Multiple Disciplines

CreativeLive specializes in creative courses for learners who want to broaden their horizons. While not exclusively focused on art, its offerings include classes on drawing, calligraphy, mixed media, and more. The platform is particularly notable for its live broadcasts, which allow students to engage with instructors in real time.

CreativeLive also bundles its classes into multi-session series for in-depth explorations of specific topics. Whether you’re interested in sketching techniques or mixed-media projects, the platform offers something for everyone.

7. New Masters Academy: Learn Fine Arts from Experts

New Masters Academy is one of the most comprehensive platforms for serious students of fine arts. This platform provides access to thousands of hours of video lessons taught by master artists, covering everything from charcoal drawing and oil painting to sculptural techniques.

New Masters Academy is a great choice for artists seeking to master traditional methods. It also offers portfolio reviews and advanced critique sessions, which can be especially helpful for those pursuing professional art careers.

Tips for Maximizing Your Online Learning Experience

Taking online art classes requires self-discipline and focus. To ensure you gain the most value from your experience, consider the following tips:

  1. Set Clear Goals: Define what you want to achieve before enrolling in a class. Are you looking to explore a new medium, refine your techniques, or build an art portfolio? Focus on courses aligned with your objectives.

  2. Develop a Routine: Treat online learning as you would an in-person class. Set aside dedicated time for lessons, practice, and reviewing materials.

  3. Engage with Communities: Many platforms feature discussion boards or group projects—take advantage of these opportunities to share feedback and learn from peers.

  4. Practice Consistently: Improvement in art comes from consistent effort. Supplement tutorials with your own creative projects to refine your skills.

  5. Experiment with Mediums: Don’t limit yourself to one subject. Experimentation often leads to unexpected inspiration and growth.

Should You Invest in Paid Art Classes?

Many platforms offer free introductory art lessons, but investing in paid courses can deepen your learning. Paid programs often include additional perks such as personalized feedback, downloadable resources, assignments, and lifetime access. If you’re serious about improving your art skills, selecting a well-reviewed paid course can be worth the cost.

Final Thoughts

Online art classes are transforming the way artists learn, offering accessible, high-quality instruction for individuals of all skill levels. Whether you’re improving your drawing skills, diving into animation, or refining your painting techniques, choosing the right course can make a substantial difference in your progress. Explore the platforms mentioned, establish your artistic goals, and commit to consistent learning to unlock your full creative potential. The world of online art education is vast—take that first step and watch your talents flourish.
