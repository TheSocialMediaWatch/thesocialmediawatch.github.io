The Best Liquors for Making Classic Highballs


The highball is a quintessential cocktail that epitomizes simplicity and elegance. With just two primary ingredients—liquor and a mixer—it’s a drink that can be tailored to suit any palate. From the classic whiskey highball to refreshing gin and tonics, the versatility of highballs allows them to shine in a multitude of settings. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the best liquors for making classic highballs, providing insights on flavor profiles, mixing techniques, and serving suggestions to elevate your home bartending game.

Understanding the Highball

Before diving into the best liquors for highballs, it’s essential to understand what defines this beloved cocktail. A highball typically consists of a spirit served with a larger proportion of a non-alcoholic mixer, traditionally served over ice in a tall glass. The beauty of a highball lies in its refreshing nature and the ability to highlight the distinctive qualities of the liquor used.

The Essence of a Great Highball

When crafting a highball, the key is to maintain a balance between the liquor and the mixer. The choice of liquor plays a pivotal role in determining the drink’s overall flavor, while the mixer enhances and complements those flavors. A good highball should be refreshing, easy to drink, and, most importantly, a true expression of the chosen spirit.

Exploring the Best Liquors for Highballs

1. Whiskey

Bourbon Highball

Bourbon is a fantastic choice for a highball, bringing rich notes of caramel, vanilla, and oak to the mix. A classic bourbon highball is made by mixing bourbon with ginger ale or club soda. The sweetness of the bourbon pairs beautifully with the crispness of ginger ale, making it a refreshing option for warm weather.

Mixing Tip: Use a high-quality bourbon like Buffalo Trace or Woodford Reserve for a richer flavor profile. Serve over ice with a twist of lemon for added brightness.

Rye Whiskey Highball

Rye whiskey, known for its spiciness and bold character, makes for an invigorating highball. Pairing rye with club soda allows the peppery notes to shine through, creating a drink that is both refreshing and complex.

Mixing Tip: Opt for a rye whiskey like Bulleit Rye or Sazerac Rye. Garnish with a lemon twist to enhance the aromatic qualities of the drink.

2. Gin

Classic Gin and Tonic

The gin and tonic is perhaps the most iconic highball, celebrated for its refreshing qualities and botanical complexity. The choice of gin is crucial here; a London dry gin will provide a crisp, juniper-forward flavor, while a more contemporary gin may introduce floral or citrus notes.

Mixing Tip: Use a high-quality gin such as Tanqueray or Bombay Sapphire. Pair with a premium tonic water to enhance the overall experience, and garnish with fresh lime or cucumber slices.

Gin Fizz

While technically a variation, the gin fizz deserves mention as a highball favorite. By combining gin, lemon juice, and soda water, you create a bubbly, citrus-forward drink that’s perfect for any occasion.

Mixing Tip: Experiment with different gins to find your favorite combination. Adding a splash of elderflower liqueur can elevate the flavor profile even further.

3. Vodka

Vodka Highball

A vodka highball is one of the most versatile options, allowing for numerous mixer combinations. Vodka’s neutral flavor makes it a blank canvas perfect for pairing with a variety of mixers, from ginger beer to flavored sparkling waters.

Mixing Tip: Use a quality vodka like Grey Goose or Tito’s Handmade Vodka. For a twist, mix with soda water and a splash of cranberry juice for a refreshing flavor.

Moscow Mule

While often served in a copper mug, the Moscow mule is a highball that features vodka, ginger beer, and lime juice. The combination of spicy ginger beer and tart lime makes for a zesty drink that packs a punch.

Mixing Tip: Choose a good ginger beer, like Fever-Tree or Bundaberg, for authentic flavor. Fresh mint leaves make a great garnish.

4. Rum

Classic Rum Highball

Rum offers a tropical twist to the highball category, making it perfect for summer sipping. Mixing rum with club soda or tonic brings out the spirit’s natural sweetness and underlying notes of molasses or fruit.

Mixing Tip: Use a high-quality white rum like Bacardi or Mount Gay. Garnish with a lime wedge to enhance the drink’s tropical flair.

Dark and Stormy

A variation of a rum highball, the Dark and Stormy features dark rum and ginger beer. The rich, caramel notes of the dark rum create a delicious contrast with the spicy ginger beer, making it a bold choice.

Mixing Tip: Use a dark rum such as Gosling’s or Kraken. Squeeze in fresh lime juice for added zest.

5. Tequila

Tequila Highball

Tequila is often overlooked in the highball category, but it can be a refreshing and exciting choice. Pairing tequila with soda water or grapefruit soda creates a light and zesty drink that’s perfect for warm climates.

Mixing Tip: Choose a quality blanco tequila like Espolon or Don Julio. Garnish with a slice of grapefruit for a refreshing twist.

Paloma

The paloma, made with tequila and grapefruit soda, is another highball variation that’s worth trying. The tartness of grapefruit balances perfectly with the smoothness of tequila, creating a delightful drink.

Mixing Tip: Use fresh grapefruit juice and a splash of soda for a more authentic flavor. Salt the rim for an added touch.

Enhancing Your Highball Experience

Garnishes Matter

While the base ingredients are crucial, garnishes can elevate your highball experience to new heights. Fresh herbs, citrus peels, and even spices can add aroma and visual appeal. Here are a few garnish ideas:

  • Citrus: Lemon, lime, or orange wedges enhance brightness.
  • Herbs: Mint or basil can add a refreshing aroma.
  • Spices: A sprinkle of nutmeg or cinnamon can introduce warmth.

Glassware Selection

The right glassware can significantly impact the drinking experience. Highballs are typically served in tall glasses, allowing for ample ice and mixer. Consider using highball glasses, Collins glasses, or even rocks glasses, depending on your preference and the drink’s presentation.

Ice Matters

Quality ice is vital for creating the perfect highball. Larger ice cubes melt more slowly, keeping your drink chilled without diluting it too quickly. Consider investing in an ice maker or mold that produces larger cubes for a more sophisticated touch.

Conclusion

Crafting the perfect highball is an art that combines the right liquor, mixer, and garnishes. Whether you prefer the smoky notes of bourbon, the botanical essence of gin, or the tropical flair of rum, there’s a highball for every taste. By experimenting with different spirits and mixers, you can create a signature highball that reflects your personal style.

So, gather your ingredients, invite your friends, and enjoy the refreshing simplicity of classic highballs. Cheers to flavorful concoctions that celebrate the essence of fine spirits!
