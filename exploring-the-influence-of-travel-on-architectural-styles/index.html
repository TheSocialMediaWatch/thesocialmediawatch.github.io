Exploring the Influence of Travel on Architectural Styles


As the world becomes increasingly interconnected, architectural styles are constantly evolving and being influenced by various cultures and traditions. When great buildings of different styles meet, they create new bridges between civilizations and transform architectural landscapes. This phenomenon of architectural influence through travel has been occurring throughout history, with each era bringing its distinct characteristics to the table.

The Roman Colosseum impacted the design of many stadiums and amphitheaters around the world. Gothic cathedrals from Europe inspired ornate details in buildings in the Americas and beyond. Even Islamic architecture has had a significant influence on the design of many structures worldwide, especially in the areas where Islamic and European cultures have interacted.

One of the most crucial ways in which architects have been exposed to other cultures is through travel. Traveling to a different part of the world not only exposes an architect to varied architectural styles but also allows them to gain valuable first-hand experience of the unique geographical conditions, cultural preferences, and climate. By incorporating these factors into their designs, architects create innovative structures that cater to the region’s specific environment, climate, and society.

An excellent example of how travel influences architecture is the work of 20th-century architect Oscar Niemeyer. Born in Brazil, Niemeyer traveled extensively in Europe, particularly to visit Le Corbusier’s landmarks in France, which significantly shaped his style. Oscar Niemeyer’s fusion of modernist principles with the grandeur of Brazil’s natural landscape and the country’s unique cultural elements created his signature style, which has since inspired a whole generation of architects.

Another architect who drew inspiration from travel was Lutyens, a British architect born in the late 19th century. He was famously influenced by his travels in Greece, as well as Hindu and Islamic architecture. This exposure led him to create his unique brand of architecturally rich designs that have become synonymous with neo-classical architecture.

Traveling not only affects individual architects but also influences architectural styles on a national and global scale. An excellent example of this influence is the Hassan Farsi-designed Azadi Tower in Tehran, Iran. The design was inspired by global trends and Persian history and became the symbol of a modern Iranian identity, which reflects the influence of contemporary architecture and Persian culture.

Lastly, traveling can also inspire architects to explore and experiment with sustainable architecture. With the increasing awareness of the impact of buildings on the environment, architects are traveling to find answers and inspiration from diverse communities that have developed sustainable solutions precisely because of the resources available to them.

In summary, travel is a critical component in the development of architectural styles. Whether it’s individual architects being inspired by their travels or the adoption of global trends, travel has the power to drive innovation and creativity in architecture. As we continue to connect as a global community, we can expect to see new and exciting architectural styles being shaped by this ever-growing interconnected world. The next unique architectural wonder may just be inspired by your next adventure.
