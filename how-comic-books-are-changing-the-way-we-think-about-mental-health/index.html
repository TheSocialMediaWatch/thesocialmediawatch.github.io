Conclusion

The changing portrayal of mental health in comics is not just a trend—it is a revolution in how we understand and discuss psychological well-being. By breaking down barriers, challenging stereotypes, and offering nuanced portrayals of mental illness, comics are creating a more empathetic and informed conversation around mental health. Whether through the lens of superheroes or ordinary people navigating their struggles, comics are showing us that mental health is a universal issue, deserving of attention, compassion, and support.

As more creators continue to explore these themes, and as comics remain an accessible and engaging medium for audiences of all ages, we can expect this transformation to continue. In the process, comics are not only changing how we think about mental health—they are changing how we live with it. Through stories, illustrations, and emotional narratives, comics are offering readers the opportunity to heal, reflect, and ultimately find hope.
