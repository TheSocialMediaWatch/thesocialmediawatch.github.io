The Intriguing History of Tesla and His Lost Inventions: A Review of his Groundbreaking Work


Nestled in their infancy, the world of electrical engineering and the marvelous potential of power distribution across great distances presented a golden opportunity for ambitious individuals. One such unforgettable figure was Nikola Tesla, a visionary scientist with an unparalleled portfolio of world-transforming ideas.

Born in 1856 in what is now modern-day Croatia, Tesla’s universe began to unravel as he pursued a degree in engineering at the Austrian Polytechnic in Graz. He then embarked on one of the most exciting journeys of human imagination and comprehension, earning him an esteemed reputation as a master inventor and electrical engineer.

During his forty-year career, Tesla registered over 300 patents worldwide - some commercially successful, others less so or never realized. While his enduring legacy is primarily rooted in his influential AC motor and transformer designs that propel the industrial, commercial, and household use of electricity, a myriad of his pioneering innovations remain relatively unknown. In this post, we will review these lesser-known, lost inventions that redefined technology landscapes and laid crucial groundwork for future inventions.

Embarking on our discourse, Tesla’s early work on the fundamentals of alternating current (AC) transforms the understanding of electrical power transmission. As a direct competitor to Thomas Edison’s incompatible direct current (DC) system, Tesla’s insistence on poly-phase AC systems revolutionized long-distance power distribution. Consequently, AC power overtook DC, reshaping our global energy landscape.

Meanwhile, Tesla’s most unorthodox brainstorm was the Tesla Coil. This gothic-inspired transformer, a harbinger of vintage sci-fi, materialized wireless energy transmission. Though never commercialized due to insurmountable practical barriers at the time, the Tesla Coil paved the way for future endeavors into resonance and wireless communication. Our modern Bluetooth or Wi-Fi technology, creditably, has Tesla to thank.

Tesla’s eccentric genius extended beyond the etheric realms. He devised “teleautomation,” predicting a future of cars controlled electronically, foreshadowing the advent of the self-driving vehicles now on the cusp of mainstream adoption.

Clock/calendar synchronization remains a routine task for today’s computers; however, in Tesla’s time, timekeeping devices often ran independently. Leveraging his prowess in electromechanics, Tesla invented an accurate, self-starting-wave generator that harmonized quartz oscillators, positioning him as a pioneer in timing technologies.

Lastly, Tesla’s less-celebrated physician-assisting inventions hold use in healthcare today. His oscillating current Tesla treatment device sought less invasive means to deploy electric oscillation for therapeutic improvement. This precursor to contemporary stinging devices, or “electro-therapy,” invigorates a field in pursuit of healing with renewable energy forms over traditional invasive procedures.

Tesla’s life is an inspiring testament to the human spirit’s indomitable capacity to innovate and defy the odds. Regardless of whether these inventions came to fruition or not, Tesla’s lost inventions provide a vibrant backdrop for our modern advances in energy distribution and technology.

By exploring these lost Tesla inventions, we are reminded that significant shifts in technology often remain hidden in the shadows of history. Moreover, as museums and inventors are brought together to restore these rare Tesla embodiments, profound lessons for future inventions beckon. For curious engine minds, delving into Tesla’s lost inventions uncovers the vast reservoirs of uncharted knowledge buried deep within the earth, stars, and between the organizing principles of energy and matter. As we stand on the cusp of the fourth industrial revolution, understanding our history fosters today’s innovation with a renewed appreciation for Tesla’s lifetime of prophetic work.
