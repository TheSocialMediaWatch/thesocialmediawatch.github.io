The Best Wine Pairings for Steak and Grilled Meats


When it comes to pairing wine with steak and grilled meats, the right choice can elevate your dining experience to new heights. Whether you’re grilling burgers in the backyard or indulging in a juicy filet mignon at a fine dining restaurant, selecting the perfect wine to complement the flavors of the meat is an art. This guide will explore the best wine pairings for various types of steak and grilled meats, offering suggestions that will delight your palate and enhance your meal.

Understanding the Basics of Wine Pairing

Before diving into specific wine suggestions, it’s important to understand the basic principles of wine pairing. The key is to match the weight, flavor profile, and texture of the wine with the richness and seasoning of the meat. Red wines tend to be a go-to choice for steak and grilled meats due to their bold flavors and tannins, which help balance the fats and proteins in the meat. However, there are also white wines and rosés that can provide refreshing contrasts, especially for grilled meats that are lighter in flavor.

Here are some fundamental tips for wine pairing:

  • Tannins: Wines with higher tannin content, such as Cabernet Sauvignon or Malbec, are excellent for pairing with fattier cuts of meat. Tannins help cut through the richness and provide a balanced mouthfeel.
  • Acidity: Wines with higher acidity, like Pinot Noir or Zinfandel, complement meats with a tangy marinade or grilled meats with charred flavors.
  • Body: Full-bodied wines work best with rich, meaty cuts like ribeye, while lighter-bodied wines, like Merlot or Chardonnay, are a great match for leaner cuts like flank steak or grilled chicken.

Now, let’s explore the best wines for different types of steak and grilled meats.

Best Wine Pairings for Steak

Ribeye Steak and Cabernet Sauvignon

Ribeye steak, known for its marbling and rich flavor, pairs beautifully with full-bodied, tannin-rich wines like Cabernet Sauvignon. The bold flavors and tannic structure of Cabernet help cut through the fattiness of the ribeye, balancing the richness while enhancing the deep, savory flavors of the meat. The wine’s dark fruit flavors, such as blackcurrant and plum, complement the umami notes in the steak.

Other Great Choices for Ribeye:

  • Malbec: With its velvety texture and ripe fruit profile, Malbec offers a great contrast to the fattiness of ribeye, making it a versatile option for grilled meats.
  • Syrah/Shiraz: Known for its spicy notes and full-bodied structure, Syrah (or Shiraz, depending on the region) is another excellent choice for pairing with ribeye steak, particularly if the meat is seasoned with bold spices.

Filet Mignon and Pinot Noir

Filet mignon is a tender, lean cut of steak that pairs well with wines that offer a balance of acidity and subtlety. Pinot Noir is an ideal match due to its light to medium body, bright acidity, and delicate fruit profile. The wine’s red berry flavors, such as cherry and raspberry, highlight the tenderness and mild flavor of the filet, without overpowering it. Additionally, the wine’s acidity cuts through the natural richness of the steak, providing a clean finish.

Other Great Choices for Filet Mignon:

  • Merlot: Soft tannins and smooth fruit flavors make Merlot an excellent alternative for pairing with filet mignon, especially when the steak is served with a lighter sauce.
  • Chardonnay: If you prefer white wine, a full-bodied, oaked Chardonnay can be a delightful match for filet mignon, as the wine’s buttery texture and slight oak influence complement the steak’s tenderness.

New York Strip and Zinfandel

The New York Strip is a flavorful cut of beef with a firm texture and a moderate amount of fat. A wine like Zinfandel is perfect for this steak, as its bold fruit flavors and spicy undertones complement the meat’s savory richness. The wine’s slight peppery finish enhances the grilled crust on the steak, while the ripe fruitiness balances the natural flavors of the meat.

Other Great Choices for New York Strip:

  • Cabernet Sauvignon: Another classic pairing, Cabernet Sauvignon’s tannic structure and dark fruit flavors make it a great companion to New York Strip, especially when the steak is seasoned with black pepper or other bold spices.
  • Syrah: If you’re looking for something a little different, Syrah offers a spicy and smoky profile that works well with the charred crust of New York Strip.

T-Bone Steak and Chianti

The T-bone steak, with its combination of tender filet mignon and flavorful strip steak, benefits from a wine that can balance both cuts. Chianti, with its high acidity and medium body, is a perfect pairing. The wine’s bright cherry and herbal flavors complement the lighter filet portion while the acidity helps cut through the fattiness of the strip portion.

Other Great Choices for T-Bone:

  • Sangiovese: A close relative to Chianti, Sangiovese has similar characteristics with bright acidity and cherry flavors, making it an excellent choice for T-bone steak.
  • Barbera: Known for its lively acidity, Barbera is another wine that pairs well with the varying textures and flavors of T-bone steak.

Best Wine Pairings for Grilled Meats

Grilled Chicken and Chardonnay

Grilled chicken, especially when seasoned with herbs or served with a tangy sauce, pairs wonderfully with Chardonnay. A medium-bodied Chardonnay, especially one that has been lightly oaked, complements the smokiness from the grill while offering flavors of citrus, apple, and buttery textures. The acidity in Chardonnay also works well with grilled chicken, providing balance and a clean finish.

Other Great Choices for Grilled Chicken:

  • Sauvignon Blanc: If you prefer a more citrus-forward wine, Sauvignon Blanc offers bright acidity and refreshing flavors that work wonderfully with grilled chicken, particularly when it’s marinated with lemon or herbs.
  • Pinot Grigio: A light and crisp wine like Pinot Grigio is another great match for grilled chicken, especially when the meat is served with a light salad or fresh vegetables.

Grilled Pork and Zinfandel

Grilled pork, especially when it’s seasoned with spices or glazed with a sweet sauce, pairs beautifully with Zinfandel. The bold, fruity nature of Zinfandel complements the sweetness of the pork glaze and enhances the smoky flavors from the grill. The wine’s spice notes add an extra layer of complexity to the grilled meat.

Other Great Choices for Grilled Pork:

  • Grenache: A fruit-forward wine with soft tannins, Grenache offers a slightly spicy kick that pairs well with the savory-sweet profile of grilled pork.
  • Malbec: If the pork is grilled with a charred, smoky flavor, Malbec’s rich fruit flavors and smooth texture will enhance the experience.

Grilled Lamb and Bordeaux Blend

Grilled lamb, with its distinct flavor and slightly gamey profile, requires a wine that can stand up to its boldness. A Bordeaux blend, particularly one dominated by Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot, is an excellent pairing. The tannins in the wine help balance the richness of the lamb, while the dark fruit and earthy notes enhance the meat’s savory qualities.

Other Great Choices for Grilled Lamb:

  • Syrah/Shiraz: Known for its peppery and smoky flavors, Syrah is another great match for grilled lamb, especially when the lamb is seasoned with herbs like rosemary or thyme.
  • Tempranillo: A Spanish red like Tempranillo offers a balance of acidity and tannins, along with flavors of dark fruit and tobacco, which pair well with the richness of grilled lamb.

Grilled Sausages and Beaujolais

Grilled sausages, whether they are pork, chicken, or beef, benefit from a wine that has fruitiness and acidity without being too heavy. Beaujolais, made from the Gamay grape, offers a light body, refreshing acidity, and juicy berry flavors that complement the savory, sometimes spicy, flavors of grilled sausages.

Other Great Choices for Grilled Sausages:

  • Pinot Noir: A lighter red wine like Pinot Noir, with its subtle red fruit and earthy undertones, is a fantastic match for grilled sausages, particularly when served with mustard or tangy condiments.
  • Côtes du Rhône: A versatile red wine with flavors of red fruit and spice, Côtes du Rhône pairs well with sausages, especially if they’re grilled with herbs or spices.

Conclusion

Pairing wine with steak and grilled meats is a fun and rewarding experience that can greatly enhance the flavors of both the food and the wine. By understanding the characteristics of the wine and the meat, you can create a harmonious balance that will delight your taste buds. Whether you prefer a bold red like Cabernet Sauvignon for a hearty ribeye or a lighter wine like Pinot Noir to accompany a delicate filet mignon, there’s a perfect wine for every cut of meat and every grilling style. Experiment with different pairings to discover new favorites and take your steak and grilled meat dishes to the next level.
