The Green-To-Green Guide: Transitioning from Lawn to Garden


Ah, summer days spent lounging on a lush green lawn, basking in the warm sun, and watching a game of frisbee soar through the air – it’s a quintessential scene from countless childhood memories. But as we grow older, and our perspectives mature, we begin to realize the need for a more holistic, sustainable approach to landscaping our surroundings. This is where transitioning from a traditional lawn to a thriving, diverse garden comes into play. The Green-To-Green Guide will walk you step-by-step through this transformative journey, as we replace our green carpet with a verdant oasis teeming with life, generosity, and joy.

1. Understand the Why

The primary driver behind this movement is the desire to create a more sustainable, environmentally friendly landscape that requires less water, maintenance, and chemicals – thereby benefiting both our health and Earth’s delicate ecosystem. A traditional lawn is primarily made up of monoculture grass, which requires regular mowing, watering, and chemical treatments to keep it healthy and thriving. In contrast, a sustainable garden is a living diverse ecosystem, rich in flora and fauna, with a reduced need for human intervention.

2. Analyzing Your Lawn’s Situation

The process starts by assessing your existing lawn. Are there bald spots, diseases attacking the grass, or bare patches due to overuse? Have a good look and make a note of these indicators, as they will shape the decisions you make when embarking on your green transformation.

3. Planning Your Garden

The next step is to dream big and plan your garden. Brainstorm the various native plants, shrubs, and trees you want to include. To create a diverse habitat, select species that provide food, cover, and resting spaces for wildlife. A garden rich in biodiversity will also help attract beneficial insects, birds, and other woodland creatures, creating lasting memories for your family and friends.

4. Breaking the Grass Monoculture

To transition from a lawn to a garden, you’ll need to weaken the grass’s hold on your landscape. Begin by patch-testing an area not easily visible and apply a solution of 50% white vinegar and 50% water. Allow it to sit for several days and observe any alterations in the lawn’s appearance. You may also choose to remove the existing grass manually or through smothering techniques.

5. Tilling or Sheet Mulching

Tilling or sheet mulching breaking down the existing grass and soil is highly recommended. Tilling will turn the soil upside down, enabling new seeds and plant materials to embed and settle better. Sheet mulching, also known as lasagna gardening, involves layering natural materials such as newspapers, cardboard, manure, compost, leaves, mulch, and topsoil to enrich the soil. This method retains soil moisture, suppresses weeds, and nurtures microbiological activity, which in turn encourages the growth of healthy plant life.

6. Planting and Care

Now that your lawn is transformed into a fertile bed, it’s time to plant your chosen flora. Start small and select native plants that are well-suited to your local climate and soil conditions. Dig holes, position, and carefully plant your specimens. Don’t forget to water them during their first year and monitor the progress of these new inhabitants. Remember to identify invasive species if necessary and remove them immediately to prevent unwanted competitors in your garden.

7. Preserve, Maintain, and Rejoice

Over time, your garden will mature and flourish, creating a beautiful, symbiotic environment teeming with biodiversity. Regularly clear out any invasive weeds, prune plants, and fallen leaves. Observe. Learn. Enjoy. Share your experiences with friends and family, creating a generational connection as you watch the transformation unfold.

By undertaking the Green-To-Green transition, you’re not just creating a sustainable garden – you’re also fostering a healthier, more resilient community and helping to create connections for generations to come. The Green-To-Green Guide is truly an adventure, not just into the heart of your backyard oasis, but also a journey towards a more sustainable and environmentally-conscious future. So, go ahead, say goodbye to your grass, and let nature flourish in a verdant, diversified garden paradise!
