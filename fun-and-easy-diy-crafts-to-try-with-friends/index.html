Fun and Easy DIY Crafts to Try with Friends


Crafting with friends is one of the most joyful and rewarding ways to spend time together. Not only does it spark creativity and collaboration, but it also strengthens bonds and leaves you with tangible keepsakes. Whether you’re a seasoned crafter or a beginner looking for simple projects, there’s a world of fun DIY crafts that you can dive into without needing a ton of materials or experience.

In this comprehensive post, we’ll explore a variety of engaging and easy DIY crafts perfect for friend groups. These projects combine creativity, laughter, and the satisfaction of making something beautiful or useful. Let’s jump right in and discover some fantastic ideas!

Why Crafting with Friends Is So Rewarding

Crafting collectively offers much more than just creating art; it’s about connection and shared experiences. When friends come together to craft, it:

  • Encourages teamwork: Many projects require coordination, turning crafting into a fun group challenge.
  • Creates lasting memories: The stories behind the craft often become as cherished as the craft itself.
  • Boosts creativity: Sharing ideas can spark inspiration and new techniques.
  • Provides relaxation: Crafting is calming, helping reduce stress and fostering mindfulness.

With these benefits in mind, here are some simple and enjoyable DIY crafts perfectly suited for groups.

1. Customized Friendship Bracelets

Friendship bracelets are classic, meaningful, and incredibly easy to make. They don’t require expensive supplies, and the techniques can range from simple braiding to intricate knotting.

Materials Needed:

  • Embroidery floss or colorful yarn
  • Scissors
  • Tape or clipboard (for securing your bracelet while working)

How to Make:

  1. Choose 3-5 colors of thread and cut equal lengths (about 24 inches each).
  2. Tie a knot at the top to hold all strands together.
  3. Use braiding or simple knotting patterns like the chevron to create your bracelet.
  4. Once the desired length is reached, tie a knot to secure it.
  5. Share your bracelet with a friend or keep it as a personal memento.

This craft is portable and perfect for casual hangouts or long conversations.

2. Personalized Terrariums

Creating miniature gardens inside glass containers is not only aesthetically pleasing but also introduces a bit of nature indoors.

Materials Needed:

  • Clear glass jars or old light bulbs (with ends removed carefully)
  • Small pebbles or gravel
  • Activated charcoal (to keep the environment fresh)
  • Potting soil
  • Small succulents or air plants
  • Decorative elements (tiny figurines, moss, colored sand)

How to Make:

  1. Start by placing a layer of pebbles in the bottom of the container for drainage.
  2. Add a thin layer of activated charcoal to prevent odors.
  3. Pour in the potting soil to fill about one-third of the container.
  4. Carefully position your plants and add decorative elements.
  5. Mist lightly with water, but don’t saturate.
  6. Place your terrarium in indirect sunlight.

Working on terrariums together allows friends to share design tips and admire each other’s styles.

3. Hand-Painted Tote Bags

Turn plain canvas totes into personal works of art. This is a practical craft since everyone can use a stylish tote bag for shopping or everyday use.

Materials Needed:

  • Plain canvas tote bags
  • Fabric paint or permanent markers
  • Paintbrushes, stencils, or stamps
  • Cardboard (to place inside the bag while painting)

How to Make:

  1. Insert cardboard inside the tote to prevent paint from bleeding through.
  2. Sketch your design lightly with pencil, if desired.
  3. Use fabric paints or markers to decorate. Abstract patterns, quotes, or cute drawings work well.
  4. Allow the paint to dry fully before using the bag.

This project is great for groups because people can exchange ideas and even swap bags as gifts after painting.

4. DIY Scented Candles

Making candles is easier than you might think, and the ability to customize fragrances makes this a rewarding group activity.

Materials Needed:

  • Candle wax (soy wax recommended for easy melting)
  • Candle wicks
  • Mason jars or old jars
  • Essential oils or candle fragrance oils
  • Crayons (optional, for coloring)
  • Double boiler or microwave-safe container

How to Make:

  1. Melt the wax gently using a double boiler or microwave.
  2. Add a few drops of your chosen fragrance oil and stir.
  3. If you want colored candles, add small pieces of crayon for color.
  4. Place the wick in the jar, securing it with a toothpick or pencil to keep it centered.
  5. Pour the melted wax carefully into the jar.
  6. Let the candles cool and harden completely before trimming the wick.

Friends can experiment with different scent combinations, creating unique candles to take home.

5. DIY Photo Collage Wall Art

This craft is perfect for groups who want to showcase shared memories in a creative way.

Materials Needed:

  • A large blank canvas or sturdy poster board
  • Printed photos (can be polaroids or prints)
  • Mod Podge or other glue medium
  • Paintbrush for applying glue
  • Stickers, glitter, or decorative paper (optional)

How to Make:

  1. Lay out photos on the canvas in the desired arrangement.
  2. Apply a thin layer of Mod Podge on the canvas where each photo will go.
  3. Press photos firmly and smooth out any bubbles.
  4. Once all photos are glued, brush another layer of Mod Podge over the entire surface to seal.
  5. Add any decorative touches around the photos.

This project not only creates a lovely piece of art but also sparks storytelling as friends reminisce over pictures.

6. DIY Marbleized Paper

Marbleizing paper is a stunning craft that allows you to create swirled, colorful patterns using simple materials.

Materials Needed:

  • Shaving cream
  • Food coloring or liquid watercolors
  • Baking tray or shallow dish
  • Cardstock or thick paper
  • Spatula or ruler
  • Toothpicks or skewers

How to Make:

  1. Spray shaving cream into a baking tray to form a thick layer.
  2. Drop food coloring randomly across the surface.
  3. Swirl the colors gently with a toothpick to create a marbled effect.
  4. Press a piece of cardstock down onto the shaving cream and lift.
  5. Use a spatula or ruler to scrape off the excess shaving cream, revealing the marbled pattern.
  6. Let dry completely before handling.

This craft is incredibly fun in groups as everyone gets unique results and can compare colorful creations.

7. Painted Rocks

This simple and relaxing craft lets you turn ordinary stones into tiny pieces of art. Painted rocks can be gifts, decorations, or even part of a community rock hunt.

Materials Needed:

  • Smooth stones or pebbles
  • Acrylic paint or paint pens
  • Sealant spray or Mod Podge to protect your design

How to Make:

  1. Clean your rocks to remove dirt and let them dry.
  2. Paint your design using whatever inspires you—animals, patterns, inspirational quotes.
  3. Let the paint dry fully.
  4. Apply a clear sealant to make your rocks weather-resistant.

This project is fantastic for all skill levels and allows for endless creativity.

8. Handmade Greeting Cards

Who doesn’t love receiving personalized cards? Making your own greeting cards is a heartfelt way to celebrate special occasions.

Materials Needed:

  • Blank card stock or thick paper folded into cards
  • Colored pencils, markers, stamps
  • Stickers, ribbons, buttons
  • Glue or double-sided tape
  • Stencils or rubber stamps (optional)

How to Make:

  1. Design your card’s front with drawings, stamps, or pasted decorations.
  2. Write thoughtful messages or poems inside.
  3. Embellish with ribbons or buttons for texture.
  4. Let everything dry properly before gifting.

Card making is a gentle, conversational craft with lots of potential for personal expression.

Tips for Making Your Group Crafting Session a Success

  • Set up a comfortable workspace with enough room for everyone.
  • Prepare supplies ahead to avoid interruptions.
  • Encourage sharing of tools and ideas to foster collaboration.
  • Celebrate mistakes as part of the creative process—sometimes accidents lead to beautiful surprises.
  • Take breaks for snacks and chats to keep the energy positive.

Final Thoughts

Crafting with friends is an enriching way to have fun, express creativity, and build lasting memories. The projects listed above offer something for every taste and skill level—from quick and easy fun to more involved processes that challenge your artistic side. Next time you’re looking for a way to spend quality time with friends, choose one of these crafts and enjoy the charm of handmade creativity together.

No matter what you pick, the best part will always be the shared smiles, laughter, and the artifacts of your collective imagination. So grab your friends, gather some supplies, and start crafting your next memorable adventure!
