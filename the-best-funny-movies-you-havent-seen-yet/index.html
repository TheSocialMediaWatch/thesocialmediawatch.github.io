The Best Funny Movies You Haven't Seen Yet


If you’re tired of watching the same old comedies and are looking for fresh, hilarious films to tickle your funny bone, you’re in the right place. While mainstream comedies dominate the box office, there’s a treasure trove of lesser-known films that promise to deliver the laughs. These underrated gems have flown under the radar for many moviegoers, but they offer unique humor that can leave you in stitches. Whether you’re a fan of dark humor, witty dialogue, or quirky characters, these funny movies will keep you entertained from start to finish.

1. The Lobster (2015)

Director: Yorgos Lanthimos
Starring: Colin Farrell, Rachel Weisz, Olivia Colman

If you’re in the mood for an offbeat and absurd comedy, The Lobster should be at the top of your list. This dark comedy takes place in a dystopian society where single people are sent to a hotel to find a partner within 45 days. If they fail, they are transformed into an animal of their choosing. The film is quirky, surreal, and offers a biting commentary on the absurdity of modern relationships. Its dry humor and deadpan delivery make it an unforgettable experience, even if it’s not what you’d typically expect from a comedy.

2. What We Do in the Shadows (2014)

Directors: Jemaine Clement, Taika Waititi
Starring: Jemaine Clement, Taika Waititi, Jonathan Brugh

What We Do in the Shadows is a mockumentary that follows the lives of four vampires living together in modern-day New Zealand. This hilarious film explores their daily struggles with everything from household chores to maintaining their ancient customs while trying to fit into contemporary society. With a sharp sense of humor and brilliant performances from Clement and Waititi, this film has become a cult classic. If you love dry, quirky humor with an absurd premise, this one is a must-see.

3. In the Loop (2009)

Director: Armando Iannucci
Starring: Peter Capaldi, Tom Hollander, James Gandolfini

In the Loop is a sharp political satire that delivers comedy in the form of quick-witted dialogue and biting humor. The film follows British and American government officials as they navigate the lead-up to a war in the Middle East. The razor-sharp dialogue and absurd political maneuvers make for a hilarious, if at times uncomfortable, watch. With a cast that includes James Gandolfini and Peter Capaldi, the film’s humor is intelligent and at times painfully awkward, giving it a unique and memorable edge.

4. The Death of Stalin (2017)

Director: Armando Iannucci
Starring: Steve Buscemi, Simon Russell Beale, Jeffrey Tambor

If you’re a fan of dark humor and historical satire, The Death of Stalin offers a hilarious yet chilling take on the aftermath of Joseph Stalin’s death. The film showcases the chaos and absurdity that ensues as Soviet officials scramble to assume power. Despite the dark historical backdrop, the film’s clever writing, sharp wit, and stellar performances from an all-star cast, including Steve Buscemi, make it a laugh-out-loud comedy with plenty of moments that feel just as uncomfortable as they are funny.

5. A Serious Man (2009)

Directors: Joel and Ethan Coen
Starring: Michael Stuhlbarg, Richard Kind, Fred Melamed

The Coen Brothers have built a reputation for their dark comedies, and A Serious Man is no exception. Set in 1960s Minnesota, the film follows Larry Gopnik, a mild-mannered physics professor whose life unravels in increasingly absurd ways. It’s a movie that blends existential humor with the strange randomness of life. With its blend of absurdity, dry wit, and deeper philosophical themes, it’s a film that will make you laugh while leaving you questioning the meaning of life itself.

6. The Nice Guys (2016)

Director: Shane Black
Starring: Russell Crowe, Ryan Gosling, Angourie Rice

The Nice Guys is an action-comedy set in 1970s Los Angeles, following a private eye (Ryan Gosling) and a tough enforcer (Russell Crowe) as they team up to solve a mysterious case involving a missing girl and a dead porn star. The chemistry between Gosling and Crowe is hilarious, with their contrasting personalities and the film’s fast-paced, witty script making for a fun and thrilling ride. Shane Black’s signature blend of comedy and action is evident throughout, making this film a standout in the genre.

7. The Farewell (2019)

Director: Lulu Wang
Starring: Awkwafina, Tzi Ma, Diana Lin

The Farewell is a heartwarming and humorous exploration of family dynamics and cultural expectations. The film centers around Billi, a Chinese-American woman who returns to China under the guise of a wedding to spend time with her beloved grandmother, who is unaware that she has been diagnosed with terminal cancer. The film expertly balances humor and emotional depth, with Awkwafina’s standout performance adding to the charm. The gentle, yet humorous portrayal of family bonds makes it a truly touching and funny film that’s not to be missed.

8. The Grand Budapest Hotel (2014)

Director: Wes Anderson
Starring: Ralph Fiennes, Tony Revolori, Saoirse Ronan

Wes Anderson’s signature style shines in The Grand Budapest Hotel, a visually stunning film set in a fictional Eastern European country during the early 20th century. The story follows the adventures of a hotel concierge (Ralph Fiennes) and his protégé as they get caught up in a robbery and the aftermath. With its quirky humor, whimsical set design, and Anderson’s meticulous attention to detail, this film is a comedy masterpiece. The unique characters and zany antics make it a must-see for anyone who enjoys witty, stylish films with plenty of heart.

9. Hunt for the Wilderpeople (2016)

Director: Taika Waititi
Starring: Julian Dennison, Sam Neill, Rima Te Wiata

Hunt for the Wilderpeople is a quirky adventure-comedy about a rebellious teenager and his foster uncle who go on the run in the New Zealand wilderness after a misunderstanding. The film is filled with heart, humor, and charming performances, particularly from Sam Neill and Julian Dennison. Taika Waititi’s direction brings a perfect blend of absurdity and emotional depth, making it a film that’s both laugh-out-loud funny and surprisingly touching.

10. Sorry to Bother You (2018)

Director: Boots Riley
Starring: Lakeith Stanfield, Tessa Thompson, Armie Hammer

Sorry to Bother You is a surreal and satirical comedy that follows Cassius Green, a young African American man who discovers that using his “white voice” helps him climb the corporate ladder in a telemarketing job. The film tackles issues like capitalism, race, and identity while delivering its message with a heavy dose of dark humor and absurdity. The bold visuals and unconventional storytelling make it a unique and memorable film that will keep you laughing and thinking long after it’s over.

11. The Royal Tenenbaums (2001)

Director: Wes Anderson
Starring: Gene Hackman, Anjelica Huston, Ben Stiller

Another Wes Anderson classic, The Royal Tenenbaums follows the dysfunctional Tenenbaum family as they navigate life’s many challenges. The film is both tragic and hilarious, with a talented cast delivering memorable performances. The quirky characters and deadpan humor make for an unforgettable viewing experience, and the film’s style and mood remain iconic in the world of comedy.

12. The Big Lebowski (1998)

Directors: Joel and Ethan Coen
Starring: Jeff Bridges, John Goodman, Steve Buscemi

The Big Lebowski has become a cult favorite for a reason. This absurdist comedy follows Jeff Bridges’ character, “The Dude,” as he gets entangled in a bizarre kidnapping case after being mistaken for a wealthy man with the same name. With its offbeat characters, witty dialogue, and unforgettable performances, it’s no wonder this film has continued to captivate audiences for decades. If you’re looking for a film that blends humor with a unique story, The Big Lebowski is one you won’t want to miss.

13. Moonrise Kingdom (2012)

Director: Wes Anderson
Starring: Jared Gilman, Kara Hayward, Bill Murray

Moonrise Kingdom is another of Anderson’s distinctive films that balances offbeat humor with emotional depth. The film tells the story of two young lovers who run away together, causing a search party to be formed in their small New England town. The quirky characters and whimsical tone give it a unique charm, while the film’s tender moments add a layer of heart to the comedy. Anderson’s direction ensures that this film is both visually stunning and hilariously entertaining.

These are just a few of the many funny, unconventional movies out there that have yet to gain the recognition they deserve. Each film offers a fresh take on humor, pushing the boundaries of what comedy can be. So the next time you’re looking for a laugh, skip the usual mainstream fare and dive into these hidden gems. You might just find your new favorite film.
