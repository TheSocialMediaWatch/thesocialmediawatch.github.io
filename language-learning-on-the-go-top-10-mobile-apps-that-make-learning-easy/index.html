Language Learning on the Go: Top 10 Mobile Apps that Make Learning Easy


Our fast-paced world often leaves little room for lengthy language lessons. The good news is that we have plenty of on-the-go options at our fingertips, and investing in a mobile app is now one of the most convenient ways to develop your skills in a new language. While the language-learning industry is teeming with great apps, here are ten of the best that will make learning easy, fun, and most importantly – accessible wherever life takes you.

  1. Duolingo

Duolingo’s bright and colorful user interface disguises a language learning app that offers serious substance. This free app uses gamification to keep you motivated, with bite-sized lessons that chip away at the goal of fluency effortlessly. Duolingo covers 30+ languages with native speaker pronunciation ensuring pronunciation accuracy.

  1. Busuu

Busuu takes a more structured approach, offering a comprehensive course for each language, which is broken down into initial basics and progressive levels. This app contains a broad range of languages, but what sets it apart is the face-to-face element with native speakers through the ‘Conversation’ feature.

  1. Memrise

Homepage Header Image: Memrise

A great choice for vocabulary building, Memrise is a giant library of videos recorded by native speakers – they’re engaging, humorous, and incredibly useful. While Memrise does focus on memorization, it combines this with storytelling and puns to avoid making learning feel like a chore.

  1. Babbel

Babbel’s interactive lessons help you build vocabulary and pronunciation, while teaching you to form sentences and express yourself correctly. Babbel offers courses specifically designed by experts to guide you through real-life scenarios.

  1. Mondly

Homepage Header Image: Mondly

Don’t underestimate Mondly; it packs a punch when it comes to effective language learning. It presents lessons like a futuristic simulation where you converse with an artificial intelligence character. This app helps in recognizing and understanding responsive speech too.

  1. HelloTalk

Connect with native speakers all over the world with HelloTalk – a social media platform for language learners. Also, you can text, voice-message, and even video-chat with your language exchange partners.

  1. Rosetta Stone

This well-known language learning app offers full immersion through its dynamic features. Its TruAccent® speech recognition technology ensures your pronunciation is accurate, while its ‘Heart Rate Variability’ feature helps alleviate stress during learning.

  1. Rosa Language

Homepage Header Image: Duolingo

Rosa Language is a great tool for practical language learning, with its rich-text conversations and phrasebook inclusive of commonly-used phrases and vocabulary which correspond to each language level.

  1. FluentU

For language learners who feel visual learning resonates more with them, FluentU is the app to turn to. The app offers real-world videos like music videos, movie trailers, news, and inspiring talks that have been turned into personalized language learning lessons.

  1. Mango Languages

Lastly, Mango Languages focuses on practical conversation skills while incorporating the element of cultural context. It’s a great tool for travelers, offering authentic conversations and expressions which would be relevant during your trip abroad.

With so many incredible language learning apps at our disposal, there’s no excuse for struggling with communication in a foreign language. While each app takes a unique approach, they’re all here to guide you on the journey to fluency – wherever and whenever suits you. Whether you’re aiming for a quick study session during a morning commute or needle-in-the-haystack hunting for a word translation while browsing in Paris, the answer is always there in your pocket, just a tap away. Learn, explore, converse, and revel in the beauty of multi-language proficiencies – all of this and more you’ll experience by using these top mobile apps for language learning.
