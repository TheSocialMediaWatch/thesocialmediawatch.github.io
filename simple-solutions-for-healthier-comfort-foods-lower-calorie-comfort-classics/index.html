Simple Solutions for Healthier Comfort Foods: Lower-Calorie Comfort Classics


Comfort foods have a unique way of bringing warmth and satisfaction to our taste buds, especially during trying times. However, these delicious dishes often pack a calorie punch that may not align with our health goals. Fear not, for they can still be enjoyed without compromising on taste. Here, we dive into the world of lower-calorie comfort classics that will nourish your soul and make your taste buds dance with joy.

Quarentine Chili

Comfort food does not have to mean loading up on meat and beans in a sugary sauce. You can easily create a homemade chili that is packed full of hearty flavors and fresh vegetables. A recipe that replaces ground beef with lean turkey or extra-lean ground sirloin helps cut back on calories but still offers the savory taste that we crave. You can further trim calories by reducing the amount of cheese on top and using a low-fat sour cream instead.

Servings: 8

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. extra-lean ground sirloin
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 3 bell peppers, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cans kidney beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cans crushed tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, cook sirloin over medium heat, breaking it into small pieces as it cooks. Once fully cooked, drain off any excess fat.
  2. Add onions, bell peppers, and garlic to the pot and cook until softened.
  3. Stir in kidney beans, crushed tomatoes, chili powder, cumin, and salt and pepper. Reduce heat and let it simmer for at least 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Enjoy your homemade, lower-calorie comfort classic while knowing you’re making healthier choices without sacrificing taste.

Skinny Shepherd’s Pie

Shepherd’s pie is a classic dish that can be made lighter while retaining its lush taste. This dish gets a lower-calorie makeover with lean ground turkey instead of ground beef and a baked sweet potato topping. The result is a delicious and comforting meal without the guilt.

Servings: 6

Ingredients:

  • 1 lb. extra-lean ground turkey
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 large sweet potato
  • 1/4 cup nonfat milk

Instructions:

  1. Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F.
  2. In a large skillet, cook ground turkey, onion, and garlic over medium heat. Once the turkey is cooked through, add in the peas and season with salt and pepper.
  3. While the turkey mixture is cooking, bake the sweet potato in the oven for 45 minutes to an hour, then remove and let it cool.
  4. Once cooled, scoop the flesh from the sweet potato and mix in non-fat milk and a dash of cinnamon to create a smooth and creamy mash.
  5. Pour the turkey mixture into a baking dish and top with the sweet potato mix. Return it to the oven and bake for 20 minutes or until the top is slightly golden.

Enjoy this lighter version of a classic dish, which is sure to satisfy your cravings while staying within your healthier food choices.

Light Corn Chowder

Corn chowder is a welcoming hug in a bowl during the colder months. But traditional recipes are laden with heavy cream and butter, packing on the calories. You can make a lighter version by replacing the cream with low-sodium chicken broth and using non-fat milk to keep the creamy texture. This version still delivers all the warmth, comfort, and deliciousness of the traditional dish, but with fewer calories and fat.

Servings: 8

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices whole grain bread
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 4 cups corn kernels
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup non-fat milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Dash of paprika for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Begin by drying out the bread slices and then crumbling them into a small bowl. Set aside.
  2. In a large pot, cook onions and garlic until the onions are soft and translucent.
  3. Add in the cubed potatoes and chicken broth, bring to a boil, and then simmer until the potatoes are tender.
  4. Add the corn and continue to simmer for 10 more minutes.
  5. Use an immersion blender or transfer the mixture to a blender and blend until smooth. Be cautious when blending hot liquids.
  6. Stir in non-fat milk and season with salt and pepper.
  7. Serve the chowder topped with the crumbled bread to provide a satisfying crunch.

Don’t let the worry of added calories stop you from enjoying your favorite comfort foods. With a little creative ingenuity and low-calorie substitutions, you can savor those hearty dishes that warm your soul during these challenging times. Now get your apron on and start cooking. Bon appétit!
