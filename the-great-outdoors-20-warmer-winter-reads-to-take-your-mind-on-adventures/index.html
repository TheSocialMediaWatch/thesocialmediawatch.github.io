The Great Outdoors: 20 Warmer Winter Reads to Take Your Mind on Adventures


Winter can either be a season to hibernate indoors or a chance to embrace the great outdoors. As the world around us embraces the icy chill, snuggling up with a good book can transport us to warmer climes and inspire our senses to experience nature’s lavish wonders. So, as temperatures drop and days grow shorter, let’s explore 20 warmer winter reads that will fill our hearts with adventure and our minds with stories of love for the natural world.

  1. The Alchemist by Paulo Coelho – Venture into the mysteries of life and the deserts of the Sahara as Santiago, the shepherd boy with a dream, embarks on a journey of self-discovery.

  2. The Underdogs: The Story of the Mexican Revolution by Carlos Fuentes – Immerse yourself in the rich history and landscape of Mexico through the eyes of rebel General Enrique Corona and his lieutenant, Felipe Muriel.

  3. A Walk in the Woods by Bill Bryson – Join Bill Bryson and his friend Katz as they hike the Appalachian Trail, with witty commentary and accounts of their misadventures on the nation’s most beloved walking path.

  4. Into the Wild by Jon Krakauer – Delve into the mind of a free spirit as Christopher McCandless abandons civilization and embarks on a life-changing adventure in the Alaskan wilderness.

  5. The Innocent by Ian McEwan – Enjoy sunshine, scenery, and romance against the backdrop of the Spanish countryside in this tale of a love affair that brings together two strangers amidst war and revolution.

  6. The Joy of Less: A Simple Guide to Declutter, Organize, and Zin by Francine Jay – Uncover the beauty of minimalism and learn to appreciate the simple joys of nature as you declutter your life and find solace in a simpler existence.

  7. The Ocean at the End of the Lane by Neil Gaiman – Follow a middle-aged man as he relives his childhood memories in a small English village and encounters magical occurrences and friendships that defined his past.

  8. The Bean Trees by Barbara Kingsolver – Discover the vibrant landscapes of rural Kentucky, Arizona, and the small lives of the characters who inhabit these states in this moving tale of resilience and friendship.

  9. The Midnight Tunnel: The Mysterious Death of Percival Morrison by Elisabeth Stortigua – Unravel the mysteries behind the dark history of San Diego’s Balboa Park, as this elegantly crafted true crime story unveils the darker side of man’s relationship with nature.

  10. The Secret Garden by Frances Hodgson Burnett – Take a stroll into one of the most magical and charming settings in children’s literature as Mary Lennox discovers the beauty and healing power of nature in her secret garden.

  11. A Natural History of Dragons: A Memoir by Lady Trent by Marie Brennan – Embark on a passionate journey through the Victorian world of paleontology, exploration, and romance as Isabella, Lady Trent, chronicles her adventures seeking out the extinct dragons she once so desperately sought to understand.

  12. The Thing About Life Is That One Day You’ll Be Dead by David Rakoff – Discover the humorous musings and experiences of David Rakoff as he ponders the mysteries of aging, beauty, and self-awareness against the backdrop of nature’s changing seasons.

  13. The Snow Child by Eowyn Ivey – Experience the enchantment of an Alaskan frontier tale as a child is discovered in the snow and a couple faces the secrets, heartache, and wonder of motherhood and love.

  14. The Unchangeable Spots of Leopards by Delia Ephron – Follow an aspiring writer named Faye as she navigates the unpredictable world of love, loss, and self-discovery amidst the backdrop of breathtaking landscapes and beautiful friendships.

  15. Land’s End: A Walk in Provence by Keith Bellows – Join National Geographic photographer Keith Bellows on a journey through the storied countryside of Provence, France, or as the locals say, “où le vent souffle d’or,” or “where the wind blows gold.”

  16. A Thousand Miles in the Sky: Stories by Karl Ove Knausgaard – Discover the Norwegian countryside and the poignant stories of childhood and self-discovery written by a memoirist who knows the value of reveling in beauty and silence.

  17. The Places in Between by IT Finn – Experience the harsh beauty and stark desolation of Afghanistan as IT Finn treks through the country after the Taliban’s fall and observes its people, traditions, and resilience firsthand.

  18. Sailing Alone Around the World by Joshua Slocum – Set sail and follow the lone sailor who, in 1895, became the first person to sail solo around the world as he weaves a tale of adventure and determination in stunning seascapes.

  19. The Yonahlossee Riding Camp for Girls by Kimberly Cutter – Unravel the secrets and tragedy in the lives of five guests at a girls’ riding camp nestled in the North Carolina Blue Ridge Mountains during the summer of 1930.

  20. The Tiger’s Wife by Téa Obreht – Follow a physician navigating the aftermath of her father’s death as she weaves a tale of myth, history, and her family’s past in Yugoslavia through the enduring legend of the Deathless Man.

Let these warmer winter reads inspire your own adventures, whether they’re daydreams or reality. With pencils, paper, and perhaps a seat by the fire, capture the essence of these vivid landscapes and compelling characters in your heart and have your own adventures at any time of the year. As author Poirot once said, “There is nothing as wonderful as the freedom of the open road. Not even sky.” Let these books be your window to the great outdoors, wherever you may be. Happy reading!
