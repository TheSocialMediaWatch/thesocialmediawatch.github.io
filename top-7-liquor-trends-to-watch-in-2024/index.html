Top 7 Liquor Trends to Watch in 2024


As we step into a new year, the world of spirits is on the cusp of significant changes. From innovative production methods to shifting consumer preferences, the liquor landscape is evolving at a rapid pace. Here are the top 7 liquor trends to watch in 2024:

  1. Sustainable Spirits As consumers become increasingly environmentally conscious, the spirits industry is responding with sustainable practices. Many distilleries are now using eco-friendly packaging, reducing their carbon footprint, and sourcing ingredients from environmentally responsible suppliers. This trend is not just a marketing gimmick; it's a reflection of the industry's commitment to a greener future.
  2. Plant-Based Mixers The rise of plant-based diets has also influenced the way we consume liquor. Plant-based mixers, made from natural ingredients like fruits, herbs, and spices, are gaining popularity. These mixers not only cater to the growing demand for vegan and vegetarian options but also offer a healthier alternative to traditional cocktail mixers.
  3. Japanese Whiskey Japanese whiskey has been gaining traction in recent years, and its popularity shows no signs of slowing down. With its smooth, complex flavor profile and rich history, Japanese whiskey is becoming a staple in many bars and restaurants. Expect to see more Japanese whiskey releases in 2024, as well as increased innovation in production methods.
  4. Low- and No-ABV Options The low- and no-ABV movement is gaining momentum, driven by health-conscious consumers who want to enjoy the social aspect of drinking without the high alcohol content. This trend is not just limited to beer and wine; low- and no-ABV spirits, like gin and vodka, are also on the rise.
  5. Flavored Whiskey Flavored whiskey has been a staple in the spirits industry for years, but 2024 is expected to see a surge in new and innovative flavors. From citrus-based whiskeys to more adventurous flavors like honey and coffee, the possibilities are endless. This trend is not just limited to traditional whiskey flavors; flavored whiskey is also being applied to other spirits like rum and tequila.
  6. Canned Cocktails Canned cocktails have been a game-changer in the industry, offering a convenient and portable way to enjoy a drink. With the rise of outdoor activities and social gatherings, canned cocktails are becoming increasingly popular. Expect to see more premium canned cocktail brands hit the market in 2024, with a focus on high-quality ingredients and innovative flavors.
  7. Collaborations and Limited Releases The spirits industry is becoming increasingly collaborative, with distilleries partnering with influencers, breweries, and other brands to create unique and limited-release products. These collaborations not only offer consumers something new and exciting but also help to drive innovation and creativity in the industry. Conclusion The world of liquor is constantly evolving, and 2024 is shaping up to be an exciting year for the spirits industry. From sustainable practices to innovative production methods, there's no denying that the industry is on the cusp of significant changes. Whether you're a seasoned spirits enthusiast or just starting to explore the world of liquor, these trends are sure to make a splash in 2024. Sources
