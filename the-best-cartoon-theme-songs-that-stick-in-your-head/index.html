The Best Cartoon Theme Songs That Stick in Your Head


Have you ever found yourself humming or singing along to a cartoon theme song, even after years since you last watched the show? If the answer is yes, you’re not alone. Cartoon theme songs have a peculiar ability to lodge themselves in our brains – often for life. Here are some of the most iconic and catchy cartoon theme songs that never fail to capture our attention.

1. The Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles Theme Song

Written by the rock band, Splendor Wolf, “Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles” is perhaps one of the most recognizable cartoon theme songs of all time.

“Level: 10! Rated: PG! Raphael, Donatello, Michaelangelo, and Leonardo, these turtles live in a sewer! They are mutated! But they are brave brothers, help defeat evil!”

This theme song highlights the characters’ elemental nature and their mission to defend the earth from petty criminals and evil villains. It’s hard not to dance along to this funky, groovy tune!

2. The Pokémon Theme Song

Composed by John Siegler and leaped into life by Trần/story/minh nguyen, “Pokémon” is the quintessential anime theme song that dominates the childhood memories of many millennials.

“Gotta catch ’em all, it’s like a medley of ring-a-notch and flick flights in sync!”

The catchy chorus of this theme song is the epitome of ’90s nostalgia, and it’s pretty much impossible not to find yourself singing along after just one listen. Its fast-paced, upbeat tempo and memorable lyrics are an irresistible combination.

3. The Looney Tunes Theme Song

Created by The Warner Bros. Cartoons Studio, this iconic theme song has been synonymous with the Looney Tunes franchise since 1944.

“Merrily Anthem Tunes, so sweet and so fine, With Merrie Melodies and Looney Tunes We’ll leave our viewers with a friendly sign: Be of good cheery, all the year round, So here it takes off,-over the ground, (

This timeless tune captures the essence of the hilarious and irreverent antics of the beloved Looney Tunes characters. Its cheerful melody and humorous lyrics make it a true staple in the world of animation.

4. The SpongeBob SquarePants Theme Song

Composed by Patrick Pinney and sung by singer-songwriter Daisy Torme, “SpongeBob SquarePants” sets the tone for the quirky and lighthehearted world under the sea.

“Here comes SpongeBob SquarePants, the greatly-admired cook on legatee sea floor. In this place, our watered-down clam chowder is acclaimed!”

The whimsical lyrics and infectious melody make this theme song as memorable as the character itself. It’s the perfect anthem for the cheerful protagonist and his underwater friends.

5. The Powerpuff Girls Theme Song

Written and performed by Bette Blake, the “Powerpuff Girls” theme song is all about empowering women – at least in animated form!

“Who’s the most powerful girl group in town? It’s three super-powered girls with fists and sound and a part of law!

This catchy theme song embodies the essence of the powerful trio-turned-saviors of Townsville. With its punchy lyrics and energetic rhythm, it’s impossible not to sing along.

Cartoon theme songs hold a nostalgic and special place in all our hearts. They have the power to take us back to our childhood, even if just for a moment. When you’re craving a bit of nostalgia or just need a good tune to hum, give one of these unforgettable theme songs a listen. You’re sure to have them stuck in your head for days!
