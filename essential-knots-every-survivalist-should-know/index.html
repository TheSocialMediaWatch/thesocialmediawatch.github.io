Essential Knots Every Survivalist Should Know


As a survivalist, knowing how to tie essential knots is a fundamental skill that can make a significant difference in challenging situations. Whether you’re camping in the wilderness, exploring uncharted territories, or simply preparing for unforeseen emergencies, mastering these knots will give you the confidence and capability to handle various tasks and ensure your safety. In this blog post, we will introduce you to six essential knots every survivalist should know.

1. Square Knot

The square knot, also known as the reef knot, is a versatile knot that is useful for joining two ropes of equal diameter. It’s commonly used for securing packages, tying bandages, or creating a makeshift clothesline. To tie a square knot, follow these steps:

  • Cross the right end of the rope over the left.
  • Pass the right end under the left and pull it through.
  • Cross the left end over the right.
  • Pass the left end under the right and pull it through.

2. Bowline Knot

The bowline knot is known for its strength and reliability. It creates a loop that won’t slip or tighten under pressure, making it ideal for securing shelters, hoisting items, or creating a rescue harness. Follow these steps to tie a bowline knot:

  • Create a small loop near the end of the rope, leaving a long tail.
  • Pass the tail of the rope through the loop.
  • Bring the tail around the standing part of the rope.
  • Pass the tail back through the loop.

3. Clove Hitch

The clove hitch is a simple and effective knot used for securing objects to poles or posts. It’s particularly handy for setting up tarps, building shelters, or hanging items securely. To tie a clove hitch, follow these steps:

  • Wrap the rope around the pole or post.
  • Cross the working end of the rope over the standing part.
  • Pass the working end of the rope under the standing part.
  • Bring the working end over the standing part and tuck it under the second wrap.

4. Taut-Line Hitch

The taut-line hitch is an adjustable knot that can be easily tensioned or loosened. It’s perfect for securing tents, guylines, or creating a makeshift clothesline. Follow these steps to tie a taut-line hitch:

  • Wrap the rope around a fixed object.
  • Cross the rope over itself, creating a loop.
  • Pass the end of the rope through the loop.
  • Wrap the rope around itself, going away from the fixed object.
  • Pass the end of the rope through the loop again.
  • Adjust the tension as needed by sliding the knot up or down.

5. Sheet Bend

The sheet bend is an excellent knot for joining two ropes of different diameters. It’s commonly used for creating a longer rope, rescuing someone from a dangerous situation, or crafting improvised tools. To tie a sheet bend, follow these steps:

  • Form a loop with the thicker rope.
  • Pass the thinner rope through the loop.
  • Wrap the thinner rope around itself and the thicker rope.
  • Pass the thinner rope through the loop again.
  • Tighten the knot by pulling both ends.

6. Figure Eight Knot

The figure eight knot is a simple and reliable stopper knot that prevents ropes from unraveling. It’s essential for keeping ropes secure, creating handholds, or attaching gear to your backpack. Follow these steps to tie a figure eight knot:

  • Form a loop near the end of the rope.
  • Pass the end of the rope over and behind the standing part.
  • Bring the end of the rope up and through the loop.
  • Tighten the knot by pulling both ends.

By familiarizing yourself with these essential knots, you’ll have the necessary tools to tackle various survival situations. Practice tying them regularly to build muscle memory and ensure you can execute them quickly and efficiently when needed. Remember, in survival situations, the right knot can be the difference between success and failure. Stay prepared, stay safe!
