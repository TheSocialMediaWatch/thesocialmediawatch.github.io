Cartoon Movie Releases to Look Out for in 2025


Animation fans are in for a thrilling year, as 2025 is shaping up to become a landmark in animated cinema. With sequels to beloved franchises, original stories from top studios, and innovative ideas from rising animation houses, big screens are set for a line-up that promises excitement, nostalgia, and brand-new worlds to explore. Whether you’re a parent seeking the next family outing or a lifelong animation enthusiast eager for cutting-edge artistry, there’s something magical coming. Here’s a deep dive into the most anticipated cartoon movie releases to circle on your calendar for 2025.

The Evolution of Animated Films

Before jumping into the titles, let’s take a moment to appreciate just how far animated features have come. Twenty years ago, 2D animation still dominated multiplexes. Digital platforms had yet to disrupt how stories were told. Today, we see studios blending 2D and 3D seamlessly, leveraging AI tools for background design and character motion, and catering to a global audience with diverse tastes.

As a result, the 2025 roster isn’t just a series of crowd-pleasers—it’s a snapshot of an industry in transformation, pushing creative boundaries.

Sequels and Spin-Offs from Beloved Franchises

1. Inside Out 2

Pixar’s catalogue is stuffed with classics, but few have struck the emotional chord of Inside Out. The 2015 original took viewers on a journey through the mind of Riley, guided by her emotions. Next year, Inside Out 2 returns with a grown-up Riley lending itself to new emotions and more complex scenarios. Expect older fans to return, eager to see how Joy, Sadness, Anger, Fear, and Disgust navigate adolescence and even adulthood. If you loved the studio’s unique blend of heart and imagination the first time around, this sequel promises even deeper storytelling.

2. Shrek 5

Shrek left ogre-sized footprints on the world of animated movies. While Shrek Forever After seemed to wrap things up, fan hunger for more never truly faded. 2025 will see the return of Shrek, Fiona, Donkey, and their fairy tale friends. While official plot details are under wraps, DreamWorks has hinted at fresh characters and storylines reflecting today’s cultural climate—all wrapped up in the satire and physical comedy that made the originals smash hits.

3. Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse 3

The Spider-Verse took animation to exhilarating new places with its frenetic mix of visual styles and heartfelt storytelling. The trilogy’s concluding chapter swings into theaters in 2025, promising Miles Morales, Gwen Stacy, and an ever-expanding cast of Spider-heroes. The franchise’s hallmark? Dazzling animation that looks straight out of a comic, whip-smart humor, and emotional stakes that land with precision. It’s not just a superhero story—it’s an exploration of identity, friendship, and destiny.

Dazzling Original Animated Movies

4. Moana 2

Disney isn’t letting go of the oceanic magic just yet. Moana 2 will bring fans back to the South Pacific, following Moana’s adventures beyond Motunui. Though the plot is tightly guarded, early hints suggest new mythological lands, spectacular songs, and iconic characters both old and new. The musical numbers alone are bound to become instant classics, and the vibrant visuals promise to push animation technology further than ever.

5. Utopia

Emerging animation powerhouse Lightbox Entertainment takes on its most ambitious project yet with Utopia. Billed as a cinematic meditation on harmony and conflict in a magical city inhabited by talking animals, the film draws on classic allegories while spinning a stand-out original tale. Expect cutting-edge animation, nuanced characters, and a blend of adventure and comedy that will appeal to all ages.

6. Chronicles of the Skylands

A wholly original sci-fi adventure, Chronicles of the Skylands comes from Blue Sky Studio’s new wave of releases. With airships, floating islands, and a story that weaves together friendship and the fight for home, early concept art and teaser trailers have already set the internet buzzing. The film’s ambitious visual style and world-building promise a true feast for the eyes.

Continuing Traditions and Breaking New Ground

7. Trolls World Tour 3

The Trolls’ infectious music and riotous color palettes will return in Trolls World Tour 3. Poppy, Branch, and their friends are set for another musical adventure, this time exploring genres and cultures that haven’t hit the franchise before. DreamWorks knows its audience: expect toe-tapping new songs, social media-savvy jokes, and eye candy that aims straight for the dopamine centers of viewers young and old.

8. Sonic the Hedgehog: Emerald Quest

While not a pure cartoon, Sonic the Hedgehog: Emerald Quest will blend live-action and animation to a greater degree than its predecessors, with more of Sonic’s world entering the “real” one. Fans are hyped for high-speed chases, crackling banter, and nods to decades of video game history.

Anime: The Global Influence Grows

9. My Hero Academia: The Final Stand

Anime has exploded in global popularity, and My Hero Academia stands at the forefront. The upcoming theatrical release, The Final Stand, promises the exhilarating battles, sharp animation, and heartfelt stories that fans crave. With the manga near its dramatic conclusion, the movie’s release is perfectly timed to give longtime viewers a spectacle to remember.

10. Studio Ghibli’s Secret Project

It’s always big news when Studio Ghibli unveils a new title. Details about the studio’s upcoming 2025 feature are predictably sparse, but whispers of a fantasy epic, hand-drawn artistry, and a return to Miyazaki’s favorite themes—nature, childhood, and wonder—have fans marking their calendars in ink.

Noteworthy Mentions

  • Kung Fu Panda 5: Po kicks his way back into theaters, aiming for new heights of animated martial arts.
  • Frozen III: Anna and Elsa’s journey continues, with early concept work suggesting new lands and challenges.
  • The Loud House: Off to College: Nickelodeon’s noisy family tackles growing up—expect laughter and heartfelt moments.
  • The Fairly OddParents: Wishful Thinking: A fresh, full-length venture into Dimmsdale, blending nostalgia and modern humor.

The Technology Behind the Magic

2025’s animated films aren’t just about slapstick and song. Behind the scenes, several trends are emerging:

  • Hybrid Animation: Films like Sonic and Spider-Verse showcase how 2D and 3D, digital and hand-drawn can combine in unique ways.
  • AI in Animation: Studios employ AI to streamline backgrounds and character movements, creating smoother, more dynamic scenes.
  • Interactive Engagement: Social media-inspired features and connected world-building tie in with apps and games, making the movie experience more immersive.

These advances help artists focus energy on inventiveness and emotion, which are at the heart of every great cartoon.

Why 2025 Will Be a Milestone Year

Three reasons stand out. First, animation in 2025 is more inclusive and culturally rich than ever, reflecting the world’s diversity in both characters and creators. Second, the blend of nostalgia and bold innovation means you never quite know what to expect—but you’re almost always delighted. Third, access keeps expanding: global releases and streaming bring these visual stories to more people faster.

Key Takeaways for Animation Fans

Whether you’re jazzed for massive franchises returning or curious to see what new worlds studios conjure, 2025 is a gold mine for animated movie lovers. Pencil in premiere dates, grab that bucket of popcorn, and get ready for a year that will charm, inspire, and maybe even change the course of animation itself.

With theaters and platforms alike catching the latest wave, every month brings the promise of a new adventure. Animation’s future is bright, and 2025 is the proof.

Stay tuned—screens, big and small, will be alive with color, music, and the stories that remind us why cartoon movies matter at any age. 2025 won’t just be another year at the cinema. For fans of cartoons, it’s the start of a new era.
