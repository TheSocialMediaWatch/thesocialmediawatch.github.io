How to Mix Patterns and Prints for a Fashionable Look


When it comes to fashion, mixing patterns and prints can be a bold and exciting way to create a unique and eye-catching look. However, it can also be a bit intimidating for those who are not familiar with the art of pattern mixing. Fear not! With the right tips and tricks, you can master the art of mixing patterns and prints like a pro. In this guide, we’ll explore the dos and don’ts of pattern mixing, as well as some creative ideas to help you elevate your fashion game.

Understanding the Basics

Before diving into the world of pattern mixing, it’s important to understand the basic principles. The key to successful pattern mixing lies in finding the right balance between different prints. Start by choosing a dominant pattern as the focal point of your outfit, and then complement it with smaller, coordinating prints. For example, pair a bold floral top with a subtle striped skirt, or mix a classic polka dot blouse with a geometric patterned scarf. The goal is to create visual interest without overwhelming the eye.

Embracing Contrast

Contrast is essential when it comes to pattern mixing. Experiment with contrasting colors, sizes, and textures to create a dynamic and visually appealing ensemble. For instance, pair a large, graphic print with a smaller, more intricate pattern to achieve a well-balanced look. Don’t be afraid to mix unexpected combinations – a classic plaid with a modern abstract print, or a delicate floral with a bold animal print. Embracing contrast is the key to making your outfit stand out.

Playing with Scale

Playing with the scale of patterns is another effective way to create a harmonious and stylish look. Mix large-scale prints with smaller, more intricate patterns to add depth and dimension to your outfit. For example, pair a wide-striped shirt with a dainty floral skirt, or combine a bold chevron print with a subtle polka dot accessory. By playing with scale, you can create a visually interesting outfit that is both modern and sophisticated.

Keeping it Cohesive

While mixing patterns and prints is all about creativity and experimentation, it’s important to keep your overall look cohesive. Choose patterns that share a common color palette or theme to ensure that your outfit appears harmonious and well-coordinated. For example, if you’re mixing a floral print with a geometric pattern, make sure that they share at least one common color to tie the look together. Keeping it cohesive will help you achieve a polished and put-together appearance.

Adding Statement Pieces

To take your pattern mixing to the next level, consider incorporating statement pieces into your ensemble. A boldly patterned blazer, a vibrant printed scarf, or a pair of eye-catching patterned shoes can instantly elevate your look and add an extra element of interest. These statement pieces can serve as the focal point of your outfit, around which you can build the rest of your look. Don’t be afraid to experiment and have fun with your fashion choices.

Final Thoughts

Mixing patterns and prints is a fun and creative way to express your personal style and make a fashion statement. By understanding the basics of pattern mixing, embracing contrast, playing with scale, keeping it cohesive, and adding statement pieces, you can confidently step into the world of pattern mixing with style and flair. Remember, fashion is all about self-expression, so don’t be afraid to push the boundaries and create looks that are uniquely you.

So, go ahead and unleash your creativity, experiment with different combinations, and have fun with your fashion choices. With these tips in mind, you’ll be well on your way to mastering the art of mixing patterns and prints for a truly fashionable look.
