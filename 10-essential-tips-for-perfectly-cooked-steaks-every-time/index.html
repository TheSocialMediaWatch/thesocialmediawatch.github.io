10 Essential Tips for Perfectly Cooked Steaks Every Time


A well-cooked steak is an experience that can uplift anyone’s dining experience to new heights. While getting the perfect steak might seem like a difficult task, it’s feasible with a little practice and understanding of the essential cooking techniques. Here are ten handy tips that will make sure your steak is perfectly cooked every time.

1. Selecting the Right Cut

Not all steaks are the same, as the quality of the meat plays an Integral role in achieving the desired flavor and texture. Opt for a high-quality cut such as tenderloin, ribeye or New York strip. It’s a good idea to visit your local butcher, as they can guide you to the best cuts available.

2. Properly Resting

Contrary to popular belief, leaving the steak to ‘rest’ after cooking is crucial to the overall outcome. When the steak is removed from the heat source, all the juicy juices are driven back into the meat due to the insulating effect of the overlying layers. This process typically takes around 20-30 minutes.

3. Proper Preheat

Preheating your grill or pan is essential to ensure even cooking. The best way to do this is to heat your grill or pan for about 5 minutes after adding a layer of oil.

4. Temperature Mastery

Knowing how to gauge the required temperature is vital when cooking your steak. A high heat cooks quickly, creating that desirable sear while leaving the inside rare, while medium heat cooks the steak more evenly, making it more popular among steak lovers.

5. Rubbing with Salt and Pepper

An aromatic rub of pure rock salt and cracked black pepper can bring out the natural flavors of the steak. Simply let the steaks sit for 10-15 minutes after applying the rub. Remember, less is more when it comes to seasoning.

6. Optimal Thickness

Choose thick cuts of steak, typically around 2 inches. This results in a better overall cooking experience, as it allows for even cooking while retaining flavor.

7. Avoid Overcooking

Steaks can quickly move from delightful to rubbery if overcooked. Always estimate your steak’s readiness and remove it from heat a minute earlier than your desired doneness.

8. Add Delicious Extras

Add a mix of chopped rosemary and thyme with olive oil and salt for a subtle, delightful flavor to your steak. This works wonders in enhancing your dish’s overall profile.

9. Specific Cuts for Specific Cookers

A juicy porterhouse or T-bone steak is ideal for indoor grilling, while larger cuts like a tomahawk steak work best on outdoor grills.

10. Practice Consistency

Consistency is key when cooking steaks. As you gain experience, ensure every steak follows the same steps, and your diners will enjoy superbly cooked meals every time.

With passion and persistence, this culinary mastery can be achieved in no time. Remember every tip stands equal, and their marriage is just as important as each individual step in delivering a perfect steak. So go ahead, fire up your grill, and get ready to delight your taste buds.
