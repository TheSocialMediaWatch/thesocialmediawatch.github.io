Home Security Tips for New Homeowners


Owning a home is a significant accomplishment, and with this achievement comes the responsibility of securing the place you’ve worked so hard for. If you are new to homeownership, it’s essential to understand the importance of implementing home security measures to protect your investment and family. Here are some crucial home security tips to consider as you settle into your new space:

  1. Change the locks: One of the first things to do once you’ve moved into your new home is to change the locks on your doors and windows. Many homeowners don’t realize that old keys can provide easy access to their homes. You never know who might possess a copied key, so it’s best to start fresh.

  2. Secure sliding doors and windows: Invest in sliding door locks and window locks to prevent intruders from simply sliding or lifting open your sliding doors or windows. These simple yet effective measures add an extra layer of protection to your home.

  3. Use a security system: A home security system not only wards off potential intruders but also provides peace of mind for homeowners. There are many affordable options available, such as DIY security systems, alarms, and cameras. Choose a system that best suits your needs and preferences. Ensure you have working smoke detectors and carbon monoxide detectors as well.

  4. Make your home look occupied: Leave lights on when you’re away from home, and create a routine with timers to turn lights on and off. Stop mail and newspapers, or ask a neighbor to pick them up for you, to give the impression that you’re home. A television or radio can be left running as a deterrent as well.

  5. Trim shrubs and enhance exterior lighting: Maintain a clear line of sight around your property by trimming hedges and tall shrubs near entry points to your home. Proper exterior lighting deters intruders and increases visibility around your property.

  6. Be cautious with personal information: Be cautious with sharing information on social media that might indicate you are away from home. Wait to post vacation pictures until you return home. Avoid sharing details about your daily routines, such as when you leave for work or when you’re expected home.

  7. Get to know your neighbors: Building a strong relationship with your neighbors strengthens your community and provides extra eyes in the case of suspicious activity. Don’t hesitate to call on them for assistance in an emergency.

  8. Know where to seek help: Always know the location of the nearest police or emergency services. Memorize the phone number for emergencies, and keep it in a safe place. Familiarize yourself with your homeowners insurance policy in case of theft or damage.

  9. Store valuable items securely: Do not leave valuable items, such as electronics and jewelry, on display or near windows. Store them safely in a locked safe or cabinet.

  10. Create a family emergency plan: In case of a home emergency, such as a fire or natural disaster, have a plan in place, including an evacuation route, a designated meeting spot, and emergency contact information for all family members. Practice this plan regularly to ensure everyone is familiar with the process.

Securing your new home should never be overlooked, as it plays a significant role in providing a sense of safety and freedom in your new space. Prioritize implementing these home security tips to protect your family, your investment, and your peace of mind.
