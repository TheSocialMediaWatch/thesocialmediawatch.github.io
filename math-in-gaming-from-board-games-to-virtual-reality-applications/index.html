Math in Gaming: From Board Games to Virtual Reality Applications


Mathematics is an indispensable part of our lives, completing puzzles, organizing our daily tasks, and providing the backbone for various scientific discussions. It’s not just confined to academia and research; it has also found its way into the entertainment industry in recent years. Particularly, it has profoundly influenced gaming, transforming the way games are designed, played, and perceived. Let’s delve into the fascinating realm of math in gaming where board games, video games, and virtual reality applications intertwine, foster innovation and challenge our cognitive abilities.

Early board games like Chess and Go are notable examples where math plays a critical role. These games are centuries old, their rules have been meticulously designed, and they teach strategic thinking and planning. Chess even has its representation in mathematics through game theory, where players analyze each other’s moves, probabilities, and predict the outcomes. Similarly, Go, the ancient Chinese board game, encompasses an intricate system where numbers don’t merely represent pieces but carve a labyrinth of abstract possibilities. Here, mathematicians rejoice as they discover new patterns and hidden beauty in these games. In recent years, modern board games such as Settlers of Catan, Ticket to Ride, and Gloomhaven have taken an even deeper plunge into the realm of mathematics; introducing resource management, simultaneous planning, and probabilistic analysis to strategy games.

The digital evolution of board games has pushed the mathematical boundaries further, allowing complex algorithms to power game engines that simulate the game’s rules, AI opponents that think and learn like humans, and game world dynamics that resemble real-life problems. For instance, Monopoly has an odds calculation in its latest versions, which provides an authentic simulation of the highs and lows of the stock market. Tetris, on the other hand, leverages its iconic tetromino mechanics to tackle mathematical concepts such as polyomino tiling and combinatorics.

Where board games meet mathematics, video games elevate the experience to another level. “Elevating” is indeed an apt term, as most video games are inherently based on escalation; escalating in level difficulty, in complexity, in interactions, and eventually, in dynamics. The birth of video games saw trigonometry applied to realistic physics engines, with projectiles flying and characters running, rolling, jumping, all thanks to vectors and rotation matrices. Over time, mathematicians found new ways to make games more interactive and engaging. For instance, in popular video games like Minecraft or Cities: Skylines, players apply mathematical concepts of geometry, topology, and AI as they build, craft, plan, and strategize.

Moreover, gameplay elements such as probabilities and statistics are core to many video games. In games such as Poker and Blackjack, players develop a deep understanding of randomness, probabilities, and expected outcomes to navigate them successfully. In a more applied role, games designers often use emerging fields such as procedural geometry and generative algorithms to power vast open worlds and non-linear storytelling experiences.

With the rise of virtual reality (VR), the realm of mathematical gaming is set to expand further. In a VR environment, players can exist in a mathematically designed world, immersing themselves completely into the gameplay. Rhythm-based games such as Beat Saber and auditory games such as Audiosurf embody the perfect blend of math and VR, where player’s time, rhythm, and spatial awareness intricately tie with the game’s algorithmic core. More complex applications of VR, such as surgical simulations or flight training programs, are poised to heavily rely on math for an authentic representation of real-life scenarios.

In conclusion, our ever-evolving gaming world is a fascinating spectacle where the world of math unfolds in diverse and thought-provoking ways. From board games that refine strategic thinking to VR applications that put us in the thick of mathematical realities, the link between gaming and math is a beautiful sight to behold, ensuring that the convergence of these fields stays at the forefront of 21st-century entertainment and education.
