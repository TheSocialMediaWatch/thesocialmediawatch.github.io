Discover the World of Cryptozoology: 15 Enthralling Books For the Curious


Cryptozoology is the study of mysterious and unexplained creatures that some believe to exist but have yet to be proven scientifically. This shadowy realm of investigation often delves into myths, legends, and fascinating tales of beasts that lurk in the darker corners of our world, some for countless generations. And as our demand grows for adventure and the extraordinary, so too does our interest in the strange, unknown creatures that populate the curious world of cryptozoology. If you’re fascinated by these mythical beasts and legends, perhaps it’s time you started exploring these 15 enigmatic books, each exposing the intriguing world of cryptozoology as you’ve never seen it before.

1. “The Encyclopedia of Loch Ness Monsters” by Dr. Roger M. Caras

Who could fail to have a fascination with the enigmatic Loch Ness Monster, Nessie? Dr. Roger M. Caras reveals numerous theories, stories, and historical accounts of the Loch Ness Monster in this comprehensive tome, making it an ideal introduction to the world of cryptozoology.

2. “Mysterious America: The Revised Edition” by Loren Coleman

Prepare to be swept away on a whirlwind tour of unexplained phenomena and folklore as Loren Coleman takes you on a mesmerizing journey across the United States. With this book, you’ll develop a deeper understanding of cryptozoology, as well as the culture and history of the regions that house these mysterious creatures.

3. “Bigfoot! The True Story of Apocalypse Network’s Quest for the Creature” by JC Harris

Discover what really happens behind-the-scenes in the world of Bigfoot filming, hunting, and research with JC Harris’s novel, “Bigfoot! The True Story of Apocalypse Network’s Quest for the Creature.” With thrilling accounts and real events that took place behind the scenes of the hit show, this book delves deep into the world of the elusive Sasquatch.

4. “Monsters of Maryland: The True Stories Behind 50 of Maryland’s Cryptids, Ghosts, and Mysteries” by John K. Kapiche

Explore the mysteries of Maryland with John K. Kapiche in this intriguing volume, full of tales of ghosts, cryptids, and strange occurrences that hark back to Maryland’s past. Perfect for any cryptozoologist who wishes to explore the world of mythical monsters in their very own backyard.

5. “Sky Creatures: Investigating Birds, Beasts, and the Flying Monster Mysteries” by Colby Rodowsky and Loren Coleman

The sky may be the limit in this book about winged beasts and mysterious birds that have captured the public’s imagination for centuries. The boys of Colby Rodowsky and Loren Coleman disseminate an elaborate explanation about the unknown beasts of the sky, piquing your interest in cryptozoology like never before.

6. “The Big Book of Monsters” by Paul B. Coleman

Bring out the child in you with “The Big Book of Monsters” by Paul B. Coleman, a stellar catalog of unique and monstrous legends from around the globe. The vivid illustrations and engaging descriptions only add to the enthralling nature of these beasts.

7. “The Field Guide to Lake Monsters, Sea Serpents, and Cigar-Shaped Craft” by Mark A. Hall

No exploration of the world of cryptozoology would be complete without delving into the mysteries of water-dwelling creatures such as the Champ of Lake Champlain or Scotland’s own Loch Ness Monster. Mark A. Hall provides a sizzling guide filled with detailed illustrations that shed light on these evasive aquatic creatures.

8. “The Book of Dead Authors: A Tribute to the Master’s of British Gothic Fiction” by Chris (Malkin) Smith

Prepare to be captivated by tales of vampires, werewolves, curses, and the macabre as Chris (Malkin) Smith takes you on a spellbinding journey through the dark, gothic world of some of the most famous authors in British fiction. “The Book of Dead Authors” provides a captivating blend of wonder and terror as it explores the supernatural.

9. “The Cryptozoology Encyclopedia: Complete Coverage of Encountered Creatures” by Michel Raynal

With “The Cryptozoology Encyclopedia,” author Michel Raynal has compiled a treasure trove of data on mythical creatures, covering an extensive range of fascinating and bizarre cryptids that have captured peoples’ imaginations across the globe.

10. “Giant Cryptids: Real Life Legends of the World” by Chad Vernon

Chad Vernon brings forth the evidence in his chilling book “Giant Cryptids,” exposing the stories behind the world’s most elusive and gigantic creatures. Full of integrated diagrams and maps, this thrilling read gives every cryptozoologist a taste of the legendary giants that roam the earth.

11. “The Lake Michigan Monster: On the Trail of a Man-Eating Fish” by Dennis Hackin

The Lake Michigan Monster is no ordinary sea creature; Dennis Hackin uncovers its terrifying secrets in “The Lake Michigan Monster,” tracing the history of this repulsive beast that hunters and scientists have sought for decades without success.

12. “A Guide to the Loch Ness Monster” by J.M. Bose

Delve deep into the legend of Scotland’s own Loch Ness Monster with J.M. Bose as your guide. “A Guide to the Loch Ness Monster” not only gives you an all-encompassing look at the world’s most famous lake but also offers valuable insights into the search for the elusive beast.

13. “Sea Serpent Sightings: Real Encounters With Millennia Old Creatures” by Stan Gordon

Stan Gordon’s “Sea Serpent Sightings” is a gripping voyage exploring the fascination with sea serpents that has persisted throughout human history. With intriguing interviews, photographs and detailed analyses, this book highlights the persistent belief in these mysterious creatures lurking beneath our oceans.

14. “Bigfoot!: The True Story of Apocalypto Network’s Quest for the Creature” by JC Harris

Discover the spine-tingling, behind-the-scenes account of the Apocalypto Network’s hunt for the elusive Sasquatch in JC Harris’ chilling novel, “Bigfoot!: The True Story of Apocalypto Network’s Quest for the Creature.” Including first-hand interviews and exclusive details, this book is a thrilling read that reveals the world of Bigfoot like never before.

15. “In the Field: A Guide to Nonfiction Writing” by Gene Wolff

Lastly, no book on cryptozoology would be complete without addressing the art of nonfiction writing itself. With dazzling instruction and invaluable advice, Gene Wolff’s “In the Field: A Guide to Nonfiction Writing” offers readers insights into attaining that genuine captivating voice when delving into the extraordinary world of cryptozoology.

Explore the curious world of cryptozoology with these highly-recommended books. These 15 enthralling reads will both captivate and challenge you like never before, taking you on a journey into the shadows where reality becomes the stuff of legends.
