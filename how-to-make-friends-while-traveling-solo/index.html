How to Make Friends While Traveling Solo


Traveling solo is one of the most liberating and enriching experiences you can have. There’s an unmatched sense of freedom in determining your own itinerary, pacing, and destinations. Yet, the prospect of exploring new places without a familiar companion can feel daunting, especially when it comes to forging connections and building friendships on the road. Making friends while traveling solo not only enriches your experience but can also provide support, local insights, and shared memories that last a lifetime. Here’s a comprehensive guide on how to connect with others effortlessly while navigating new countries, cities, and cultures on your own.

Step 1: Embrace Open-Mindedness and Positivity

The foundation of making friends anywhere — especially when traveling solo — is an open and welcoming mindset. Trying to connect with strangers requires a willingness to be vulnerable, curious, and adaptable. When you approach new people with genuine interest and kindness, it encourages reciprocal openness.

  • Stay Curious: Ask questions about local customs, favorite places, or experiences. People naturally like to share their stories.
  • Give Off Open Body Language: Smile, maintain relaxed posture, and avoid crossing arms. Small cues signal friendliness.
  • Show Enthusiasm: Share your travel dreams and enthusiasm. Energy is contagious.

Step 2: Select Accommodations That Encourage Socializing

Where you stay can greatly influence your ease of meeting others. Certain types of lodging encourage interaction over isolation.

  • Hostels: Known for communal living, hostels often have lounges, kitchens, and organized events. They tend to attract like-minded travelers eager to socialize.
  • Guesthouses and B&Bs: Smaller, family-run places may provide personal introductions to other guests or locals.
  • Shared Apartments or Co-Living Spaces: These often attract digital nomads or longer-term travelers who welcome casual meetups.

When booking, review past guest comments to see if the place fosters a friendly, social atmosphere. Consider places with group activities or communal dining.

Step 3: Leverage Social Apps and Online Communities

The digital age has bridged the gap for travelers seeking instant connections. A variety of apps and websites exist to connect solo travelers, locals, and expats.

  • Meetup: Join groups focused on local events, hobbies, or cultural activities. It’s perfect for finding community meetups tailored to your interests.
  • Couchsurfing: Beyond free lodging, Couchsurfing offers “Hangouts” where locals and travelers gather.
  • Travello: A social network for travelers to meet, share tips, and plan meetups.
  • Facebook Groups: Many cities have expat and traveler communities where locals post invite events or casual meetups.
  • Tinder or Bumble BFF: These dating apps often have modes for platonic friendship connection.

Using apps takes a proactive approach, but it allows filtering for safe, compatible, and conveniently located acquaintances.

Step 4: Join Group Tours and Activities

Group activities are an excellent way to bond with people who share your interests and curiosity about a new place.

  • City Walking Tours: Great for meeting fellow travelers while exploring landmarks.
  • Cooking Classes: Shared culinary experiences build camaraderie and provide conversation starters.
  • Adventure Excursions: Kayaking, hiking, or day trips gather small groups with a shared goal.
  • Workshops or Language Classes: Immersing yourself in local culture while meeting others eager to learn.

When booking these experiences, try to join those that encourage interaction rather than large, impersonal tours.

Step 5: Engage with Locals in Everyday Places

While meeting other travelers is helpful, connecting with locals often results in deeper, more authentic friendships and cultural exchanges.

  • Visit Local Cafés and Bars: Frequenting neighborhood hangouts encourages familiarity.
  • Attend Community Events: Festivals, markets, gallery openings, or local celebrations can be an informal way to mingle.
  • Volunteer: Giving time to community projects or NGOs often introduces you to locally committed individuals.
  • Use Language Exchange Meetups: Many cities host events where locals and travelers practice languages together.

Being present in community spaces with a friendly attitude can invite engaging conversations that transcend the superficial.

Step 6: Master the Art of Small Talk and Storytelling

Making friends often starts with casual conversations. Small talk may seem trivial, but it opens doors.

  • Ask Open-Ended Questions: Instead of “Are you from here?” ask “What’s your favorite thing about living in this city?”
  • Share Your Stories: Exchange travel experiences or cultural anecdotes — this makes chats more memorable and personal.
  • Find Common Ground: Shared interests, favorite spots, or travel humor can create an immediate bond.

Listening attentively and responding warmly shows respect and nurtures trust.

Step 7: Be Vulnerable But Safe

Opening up about your solo travel journey — your excitement, challenges, or delights — invites empathy and connection. People generally appreciate honesty and authenticity.

At the same time, trust your instincts. Friendships on the road should be enjoyable and supportive, not forced. Don’t be afraid to politely exit conversations or groups that don’t feel right.

Step 8: Follow Up and Stay in Touch

Friendships require nurturing, especially when formed quickly in transient environments. Swap contact information or social media handles. Make plans to meet again in your current or future destinations if possible.

Maintaining connections post-travel can lead to meaningful lifelong friendships or travel partnerships. Plus, it enhances the excitement of planning your next escape with familiar faces.

Bonus Tips for Making Friends While Traveling Solo

  • Participate in Local Sports or Dance Classes: These encourage teamwork and casual bonding.
  • Attend Workshops or Creative Meetups: Pottery, photography, writing groups attract passionate participants.
  • Use Your Interest as a Bridge: For example, book clubs, hiking enthusiasts, or foodie tours, where conversations flow naturally.
  • Join Co-Working Cafés or Spaces: If you travel for work or long-term stays, these are hubs of sociability.
  • Be Patient and Persistent: Not every attempt leads to friendship, but every effort adds to your experience.

Common Concerns About Making Friends When Traveling Alone

Will I Appear Awkward or Overeager?

Feeling a little awkward is normal. Most travelers share this feeling. Confidence grows each time you step outside your comfort zone. Genuine interest in others shines through more than polished greetings.

What If I Don’t Speak the Local Language Well?

Language barriers can be overcome with smiles, gestures, and universal kindness. Many locals appreciate travelers making the effort, and others may speak enough English or your language to bridge gaps. Plus, learning together can itself be a bonding experience.

What About Safety?

Meeting strangers carries some risk, like in everyday life. Always meet in public places, trust your gut, inform others of your plans, and keep valuables secure. Being solo actually tends to make travelers more alert and cautious.

Closing Thoughts

Making friends while traveling solo is not only possible but profoundly rewarding. Each connection brings new perspectives, joyous companionship, and enriches your solo adventure with shared stories and laughter. It transforms the experience from a solitary journey into a tapestry of human connections spanning the globe. Approach your travel with an open heart, a sense of curiosity, and a spirit eager for friendship — the world is waiting to welcome you.

So next time your solo trip looms on the horizon, remember these approaches. Step out, engage deeply, and watch how new friendships unfold naturally where you least expect them. Safe travels and happy friend-making!
