How to Use Pinterest to Drive Sales for Your Business


Pinterest, with its visually rich platform and active user base, has proven to be an essential tool for businesses looking to boost sales and increase brand visibility. Whether you’re a small business owner or a large enterprise, leveraging Pinterest’s unique features can significantly impact your marketing strategy. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll dive into the ways you can harness Pinterest to drive sales for your business, step by step.

Why Pinterest?

Pinterest is often misunderstood as just a platform for inspiration and hobbies. However, it’s so much more. The platform serves as a virtual catalog, where users pin ideas, products, and services they are considering purchasing. Unlike other social media platforms, Pinterest is highly focused on discovery and intent, making it a perfect environment for businesses to market their products.

  1. A Highly Visual Platform: Pinterest is inherently visual. Users are drawn to eye-catching images and infographics, making it ideal for showcasing products. With over 450 million active users, Pinterest offers a large audience, particularly those who are actively seeking inspiration to make purchases.

  2. User Intent: Unlike platforms like Facebook or Instagram, where users primarily scroll for entertainment, Pinterest users often have purchase intent. They use the platform to find and save ideas for future purchases, which gives businesses a powerful opportunity to meet users exactly where they are in the decision-making process.

  3. Long-term Visibility: Pins have a long lifespan. Unlike posts on other platforms, which get buried in newsfeeds quickly, Pinterest pins can continue to gain traction months or even years after they are initially shared. This extended shelf life makes Pinterest an excellent tool for businesses looking to maintain consistent sales growth.

Setting Up Your Pinterest Business Account

Before you can start driving sales on Pinterest, it’s essential to set up your Pinterest Business Account. Here’s how:

1. Create a Business Profile

  • Sign Up for Pinterest for Business: If you already have a personal account, you can convert it into a business account. This gives you access to valuable analytics and tools designed for businesses. Alternatively, you can sign up for a new business account.

  • Complete Your Profile: Include a professional logo, a compelling description, and a link to your website. Make sure your bio is clear and informative, offering insight into what your business does and what users can expect to find on your profile.

2. Verify Your Website

Verifying your website allows you to connect your Pinterest account with your business website, which is crucial for tracking analytics and enabling features like Rich Pins.

  • How to Verify: Go to the settings of your Pinterest business account and follow the steps to add a meta tag to your website’s HTML or upload an HTML file to your server. Pinterest will confirm your website and give you access to more advanced features.

3. Optimize Your Pinterest Boards

Creating and organizing Pinterest boards is a fundamental part of getting your business discovered. Each board should focus on specific themes, categories, or product lines, making it easy for users to navigate and find relevant content.

  • Create Niche Boards: If you sell multiple products, create individual boards for each category. For example, if you sell clothing, you might have separate boards for “Summer Dresses” and “Winter Outerwear.” This allows users to quickly find what they’re interested in.

  • Use Board Descriptions: Don’t forget to include detailed descriptions for each board. Use relevant keywords to enhance your SEO and make your boards more discoverable.

Content Strategy for Pinterest

To drive sales on Pinterest, you need a consistent and strategic content approach. Here’s how to get started:

1. Use High-Quality Images and Pins

Pinterest is a highly visual platform, so it’s essential to use high-quality images that stand out in the feed. Whether you’re pinning product photos, blog content, or infographics, the images should be crisp, clear, and aesthetically pleasing.

  • Vertical Pins: Vertical pins (2:3 aspect ratio) perform better on Pinterest. These pins take up more space in users’ feeds and are easier to engage with. Make sure your images are designed to fit this format.

  • Incorporate Text Overlays: While the image is the main attraction, adding text to your pins can enhance clarity and encourage clicks. For example, if you’re promoting a sale, add a “20% Off” text overlay to your product images.

2. Create Pinterest-Specific Content

It’s important to tailor your content to the Pinterest audience. Create content specifically designed for Pinterest users, such as tutorials, “how-to” guides, or inspiring images that showcase your product in action.

  • Infographics: Infographics perform incredibly well on Pinterest. They break down complex information into easy-to-digest visuals that can quickly go viral. If your business can offer valuable insights in infographic form, it’s an excellent way to attract users.

  • Tutorial Pins: Step-by-step tutorials or “how-to” content encourages users to engage with your pins. For instance, if you sell crafting supplies, create tutorials showing how to make something using your products.

3. Optimize Your Pin Descriptions

Just like you would optimize content for search engines, it’s essential to optimize your pin descriptions for Pinterest’s search engine. Use keywords naturally in your descriptions to increase discoverability.

  • Be Descriptive: Provide a compelling description of what the pin is about and include relevant keywords. If it’s a product pin, describe its features, benefits, and how it can solve a problem.

  • Include a Call to Action: Encourage users to take action by including a clear call to action (CTA) in your descriptions. Phrases like “Shop Now,” “Learn More,” or “Get the Look” can motivate users to click on your pins and explore your website.

4. Use Rich Pins

Rich Pins are a Pinterest feature that allows businesses to include extra information directly on their pins, such as pricing, availability, and product descriptions. There are four types of Rich Pins: product, recipe, article, and app.

  • Product Pins: These are incredibly useful for driving sales because they include real-time pricing and availability details. Whenever a user sees a product pin, they can immediately see the price and purchase it directly through the link.

  • How to Set Up Rich Pins: After verifying your website, you can apply for Rich Pins by validating the metadata on your website. Once approved, Rich Pins will automatically be applied to your content.

Drive Traffic to Your Website with Pinterest

Pinterest can help drive traffic directly to your website and boost sales. Here are some ways to get more visitors:

1. Link to Product Pages

Whenever you create a pin for a product, make sure to include a direct link to the relevant product page on your website. This is the most direct way to guide Pinterest users from browsing to purchasing.

  • Add Trackable Links: Use UTM parameters or shortened links to track the traffic coming from Pinterest. This helps you measure the effectiveness of your Pinterest marketing efforts.

2. Promote Blog Posts and Content

Pinterest is an excellent platform for promoting blog posts, case studies, or other content that may drive interest in your products. By pinning your blog images and linking them to relevant articles, you can educate your audience while promoting your products.

  • Create Evergreen Content: Focus on creating content that remains relevant for a long time. Evergreen blog posts can continually drive traffic from Pinterest, helping you maintain consistent visibility.

3. Run Pinterest Ads

If you’re looking to accelerate your results, Pinterest offers an advertising platform that allows businesses to create Promoted Pins. These pins appear in users’ feeds and search results, allowing you to reach a wider audience.

  • Target Your Audience: Pinterest Ads allow you to target users based on their interests, demographics, and behaviors. By using targeted ads, you can reach users who are most likely to convert into customers.

4. Leverage Pinterest Analytics

Pinterest offers a robust set of analytics tools that can help you track the performance of your pins. With Pinterest Analytics, you can see which pins are getting the most engagement, which boards are the most popular, and how much traffic you’re driving to your website.

  • Optimize Based on Data: Use the data provided by Pinterest Analytics to tweak your content strategy. Focus on creating more of what works and continually adjust your approach for the best results.

Engaging with Your Pinterest Community

Building a community around your brand on Pinterest can help you establish trust and loyalty. Engage with users by repinning their content, commenting on relevant pins, and collaborating with other businesses in your niche.

  • Respond to Comments: If users comment on your pins, respond with helpful information or thank them for their feedback. Engaging with your community shows that you care about their needs and encourages further interaction.

  • Collaborate with Influencers: Partnering with influencers in your industry can help expose your brand to a larger audience. Look for influencers whose audiences align with your target market and collaborate to create compelling content that drives sales.

Conclusion

Pinterest is a powerful tool that, when used strategically, can significantly drive sales for your business. By creating a visually appealing and optimized Pinterest presence, sharing high-quality content, and engaging with your audience, you can turn this platform into a long-term sales engine for your brand. Remember, consistency is key. With time and effort, Pinterest can become a reliable source of website traffic and conversions, helping your business grow and thrive.
