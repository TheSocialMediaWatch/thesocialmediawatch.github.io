The Best Superhero TV Shows of the 70s


The 1970s was a remarkable era for superhero television shows. As the comic book craze continued to captivate audiences, the small screen became a playground for larger-than-life characters with extraordinary powers. From iconic classics to cult favorites, the superhero TV shows of the 70s left an indelible mark on pop culture. Let’s take a trip down memory lane and explore some of the best superhero TV shows that graced our screens during this golden age.

The Incredible Hulk

One of the most beloved superhero TV shows of the 70s was “The Incredible Hulk.” Premiering in 1977, this series starred Bill Bixby as Dr. David Banner, a physician on a quest to control his inner rage and transform into the green behemoth known as the Hulk, portrayed by Lou Ferrigno. The show’s introspective exploration of the human condition, coupled with intense action sequences, cemented its status as a fan favorite. Viewers were drawn to the poignant narrative of a man grappling with his own inner demons while simultaneously helping those in need.

Wonder Woman

Another standout superhero TV show of the 70s was “Wonder Woman,” featuring Lynda Carter as the titular Amazonian princess. Debuting in 1975, the series followed Wonder Woman’s adventures as she fought for justice and defended the innocent. Carter’s portrayal of the iconic heroine, coupled with the show’s emphasis on empowerment and moral fortitude, resonated deeply with audiences. The vibrant action sequences and compelling storytelling made “Wonder Woman” a timeless classic that continues to inspire generations of fans.

The Six Million Dollar Man

While not a traditional superhero, “The Six Million Dollar Man” deserves a mention for its groundbreaking portrayal of a bionic protagonist. Starring Lee Majors as Steve Austin, a former astronaut enhanced with bionic implants, this series showcased the superhuman capabilities of its protagonist as he embarked on daring missions. The show’s futuristic premise and thrilling action sequences captured the imagination of viewers, solidifying its status as a pioneering entry in the realm of superhero-inspired television.

The Secrets of Isis

“The Secrets of Isis” emerged as a trailblazing superhero TV show that premiered in 1975. Starring Joanna Cameron as Andrea Thomas, a high school science teacher who discovers an ancient amulet that grants her the powers of the Egyptian goddess Isis, the series offered a unique blend of educational content and superhero escapades. Through her alter ego, Isis, Andrea Thomas embarked on daring adventures while imparting moral lessons to her students. The show’s emphasis on heroism and altruism made it a standout in the superhero TV landscape of the 70s.

Electra Woman and Dyna Girl

Rounding out our list is “Electra Woman and Dyna Girl,” a superhero TV show that debuted in 1976. Starring Deidre Hall and Judy Strangis as the titular crime-fighting duo, the series combined campy humor with action-packed exploits as Electra Woman and Dyna Girl battled a rogues’ gallery of villains. With its tongue-in-cheek approach to superhero storytelling and dynamic female leads, the show achieved a cult following that endures to this day.

The 1970s undoubtedly left an indelible legacy in the realm of superhero TV shows. From introspective dramas to action-packed adventures, these series captivated audiences with their larger-than-life characters and timeless themes of heroism and justice. As we look back on this golden age of television, it’s clear that the superhero TV shows of the 70s continue to inspire and entertain, reminding us of the enduring power of extraordinary storytelling.
