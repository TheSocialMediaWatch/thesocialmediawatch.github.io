The Most Challenging Platformer Games You Need to Try


There’s something magical about platformers. Maybe it’s the rush of perfectly nailing a precise jump, the tense satisfaction of memorizing every obstacle, or the old-school glee of seeing the “Game Over” screen and coming back for just one more try. For many, the pure challenge of platformers draws them in, testing reflexes, patience, and determination in equal measure.

If you appreciate difficult games that test your skills, reflexes, and sheer stubbornness, you’re in the right place. This is a tour through the most notoriously challenging platformer games—modern masterpieces and old-school nightmares alike. Whether you’re hungry for pixel-perfect action or inventive difficulty, you’ll find new favorites and old legends on this curated list. Just don’t blame us if you end up rage-quitting (or rage-loving) these hardcore platformer games.

Super Meat Boy

Ask any platformer enthusiast about brutal difficulty, and Super Meat Boy is bound to be among the first names dropped. This indie darling takes classic platforming inspiration and cranks the intensity to eleven. You control Meat Boy on a wild quest to rescue Bandage Girl, dodging saw blades, spikes, and a dazzling assortment of hazards across hundreds of meticulously crafted levels.

The magic of Super Meat Boy isn’t just in the relentless pace or the deviously laid-out levels, but in the snappy controls that make every death your fault—and every victory a triumph. The instant respawn system minimizes frustration, coaxing you back into the fray over and over. It has become a yardstick for challenging platformers, and with good reason. If you haven’t yet experienced the rush (or agony) of finally clearing a level after your 150th try, you’re missing out.

Celeste

On its surface, Celeste is a visually charming, emotionally resonant indie platformer. Beneath that pastel-colored pixel art lies one of the most challenging and rewarding platformers in years. You play as Madeline, who climbs the titular Celeste Mountain in a journey filled with physical and metaphorical obstacles.

Celeste is lauded for its tight controls and ingenious level design, weaving advanced movement mechanics with increasingly fiendish challenges. Every chapter introduces new twists—gravity-defying bubbles, shifting platforms, and deadly spikes that demand lightning-fast reflexes and creative problem-solving. For completionists, the optional B-sides and C-sides (harder remixed levels) push even veterans to their limits.

But perhaps what sets Celeste apart is its ability to make each frustrating setback feel meaningful—not just gameplay-wise, but thematically. Overcoming Celeste’s toughest trials feels like a genuine accomplishment, both in-game and out.

N++

Minimalist visuals, maximalist challenge—N++ takes a simple premise and elevates it into a merciless gauntlet of precision platforming. You control a nimble ninja navigating compact obstacle courses, gathering gold, avoiding deadly obstacles, and sprinting to the exit before time runs out.

Each level demands total mastery of momentum, timing, and wall-jumping, with hazards ranging from homing missiles to mines and laser turrets. The beauty of N++ is in how each death teaches you something new; progress comes through repetition and a gradual, gleeful mastery of its complex, ever-evolving mechanics.

N++ boasts a monumental number of levels—hundreds, literally—plus an absurdly robust level editor and a vibrant custom level community. If you’re looking for pure, distilled challenge with infinite replayability, N++ could be your new obsession.

Cuphead

If you think the 1930s-inspired cartoon art style means Cuphead is charming but easy, think again. This lovingly hand-animated game is famed for its relentless bosses, bullet-hell chaos, and old-school platforming madness.

Playing as Cuphead (and optionally, his brother Mugman), you’ll take on screen-filling behemoths and sidescrolling stages peppered with hazards. Each boss fight is an epic, multi-stage battle requiring memorization, quick reactions, and trigger-finger accuracy. Cuphead’s difficulty is unforgiving but fair; success always feels hard-earned and supremely satisfying.

What’s special about Cuphead is the way it blends classic run-and-gun challenge with jaw-dropping aesthetics. Every failure is softened by the sheer beauty of the game, and every victory feels like taking down a notorious classic cartoon villain. Brace yourself for a wild ride—Cuphead isn’t for the faint of heart.

Hollow Knight

Metroidvanias are often associated with intricate maps and hidden secrets, but Hollow Knight proves that atmosphere and difficulty can be perfectly entwined in the same experience. Set in the haunting, beautiful underground world of Hallownest, Hollow Knight invites players to explore, fight, and survive in some of the most demanding environments in modern gaming.

While it’s not a platformer in the strictest sense, Hollow Knight’s intense platforming sequences, especially in the late-game and optional challenge rooms, are serious tests of skill. The “White Palace” and “Path of Pain” sections in particular are infamous among players—fragile, spike-lined corridors where split-second precision and perfect timing are essential.

Combined with challenging enemy encounters and massive boss fights, Hollow Knight stands as a shining example of how difficulty can be rewarding, pushing players to learn, adapt, and grow—not just as a hero of Hallownest, but as a gamer.

Ori and the Blind Forest & Ori and the Will of the Wisps

Both Ori games are showcases of breathtaking visuals and music, but don’t let their ethereal atmosphere lull you into thinking they’re a gentle stroll through a magical forest. Underneath the beauty lies a hardcore platformer that demands lightning reflexes, careful observation, and creative problem-solving.

The escape sequences—those relentless, do-or-die chases—are the stuff of legend, requiring absolute focus over extended periods without checkpoints. Ori’s movement is fluid, but with that grace comes a need for perfect execution as you dash between spikes, leap over pitfalls, and string together combos to avoid instant doom. The sequel, Ori and the Will of the Wisps, amps up the difficulty further with new mechanics and even more inventive platforming setpieces.

For those who crave a marriage of challenging gameplay and stunning art direction, the Ori series is a must-play.

The End Is Nigh

From the creator of Super Meat Boy, The End Is Nigh offers a different kind of existential platforming pain. You play as Ash, a squishy blob navigating a grotesque, decaying world stuffed with cruel traps and devilish secrets.

Every room is a microcosm of frustration and accomplishment, and the sheer number of spikes, moving hazards, and split-second jumps will test your platforming mettle. Think of The End Is Nigh as the natural evolution of hardcore platforming: there’s no infinite lives here, and the sheer density of content ensures that every victory comes at the cost of much persistence.

For completionists, the game dangles the allure of collecting tumors (yes, really) and uncovering hidden endings, goading you into pushing your skills even further.

VVVVVV

Sometimes, the simplest mechanics yield the deadliest designs. VVVVVV gives you a single power: flip gravity at the press of a button. With only this and your movement, you’re tasked with navigating a hazardous world filled with spikes, platforms, and ingenious traps.

The game’s retro aesthetic and chiptune soundtrack lull you into a false sense of security before unleashing some of the nastiest platforming challenges around. “Veni, Vidi, Vici” is the stuff of speedrunners’ nightmares—a single-screen gauntlet that’s both infamous and beloved.

What makes VVVVVV special isn’t just how deadly it is, but how fair and clean its mechanics are. Every hazard is clearly telegraphed, and every victory feels like you’ve broken a personal record.

Cat Mario (Syobon Action)

If you’ve ever wanted to see the classic Mario formula twisted into nightmare logic, Cat Mario is your fever dream (or possibly, fever nightmare). This Japanese indie title takes the platforming ideas you know and love and turns them upside-down, inside-out, and backwards.

Cat Mario’s world is filled with hidden traps, unpredictable hazards, and troll-level design that exists gleefully to confound your expectations. Platforms that vanish, pipes that explode, coin blocks that kill you—and that’s just the first level. Progress involves trial, error, and a willingness to laugh at your own suffering.

You may rage, you may laugh, and you’ll definitely die—often absurdly—but Cat Mario offers a unique challenge. It’s less about mastering mechanics and more about expecting the unexpected.

Spelunky & Spelunky 2

Procedurally generated, deceptively cute, and famously ruthless, Spelunky’s caverns are a meat grinder for the careless or unprepared. Each run is different: traps are randomized, enemies pop up where you least expect, and the tiniest misstep can end a playthrough in seconds.

What makes Spelunky—and its sequel, Spelunky 2—so engrossing is the blend of tight controls, emergent gameplay, and genuine risk/reward systems. You’ll make snap decisions, learn from failures, and gradually become savvy enough to survive its many dangers. Daily Challenge runs pit you against the world in a single attempt for the high score, adding to the tension and the bragging rights.

If you’re looking for a platformer that emphasizes planning, adaptability, and nerve—where death is permanent and lessons are hard-earned—Spelunky is waiting for you.

Shovel Knight: Plague of Shadows & King of Cards

The Shovel Knight series is beloved for its retro roots and pitch-perfect platforming, but two of its expansions truly dial up the challenge: Plague of Shadows and King of Cards. Both provide fresh takes on the classic levels, reimagined with new protagonists, movement mechanics, and devilishly tricky platforming segments.

Playing as Plague Knight, you deal with explosive jump mechanics that make every leap a daredevil experiment. King Knight, meanwhile, turns the traditional platformer moveset on its head with charge attacks, bounces, and unique traversal. Mastery requires adapting to each character’s quirks and bravely facing the new challenges each campaign brings.

For fans chasing a revitalized retro challenge, both of these stand out as high points even within the excellent Shovel Knight series.

Donkey Kong Country: Tropical Freeze

Nintendo’s platformers are often praised for their accessibility, but Donkey Kong Country: Tropical Freeze is here to remind you that they’ve got old-school teeth. This vibrant, colorful adventure doesn’t pull its punches, especially in later worlds or secret levels.

Incredible level design, tight controls, and a jazzy soundtrack propel you through inventive and sometimes brutal platforming segments—minecart rides, rocket barrels, tightrope climbs, and enemies galore. The secret stages ratchet the difficulty even further, offering veteran players a true test of skill and perseverance.

Few platformers blend challenge and charm so seamlessly, making Tropical Freeze one of the most rewarding games for those who want cheerful frustration with their bananas.

Slime-san

Slime-san might not be as well-known as some heavy hitters, but it’s a standout in the “die-and-retry” platforming scene. You control a green blob trapped inside a gigantic worm, racing through hundreds of twitchy, time-sensitive levels filled with spikes, hazards, and deadly creatures.

The game’s visual style is strikingly simple, almost an abstraction of classic platformers, enabling clarity amidst chaos. Quick respawns and bite-sized levels encourage experimentation and tenacity.

If you’re hunting for a platformer off the beaten path that rewards speed, precision, and a bit of improvisation, Slime-san is a hidden gem worth discovering.

Honorable Mentions

No list could possibly contain all the hardest platformers, but here are a few more to test your nerves, reflexes, and patience:

  • La-Mulana – Puzzle-heavy, trap-filled, and cryptic to the core, perfect for explorers who don’t mind suffering in service of discovery.
  • Jump King – One wrong move sends you plummeting, making every vertical ascent a gamble with your sanity.
  • I Wanna Be The Guy – A fan-made legend in the “masocore” scene, famous for its unpredictable traps and references to old-school gaming pain.

Final Thoughts

Challenging platformer games are more than just a test of patience—they’re a celebration of finely-tuned mechanics, creative level design, and the thrill of overcoming the impossible. The games on this list are infamous for a reason: they push boundaries, demand mastery, and—most of all—offer the sweetest victories to those who refuse to give up.

Whether you’re itching for pixel-perfect jumps, bullet-hell bosses, or wild, unpredictable levels, there’s a platformer here ready to humble you. So pick one up, steel your nerves, and get ready to die, learn, and—eventually—conquer.

Who knows? Maybe the next legendary run will be yours.
