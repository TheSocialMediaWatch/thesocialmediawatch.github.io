The Most Popular Cocktails of 2023: Trends to Watch


As we move further into the 2020s, the world of mixology continues to evolve, with new ingredients, techniques, and ideas emerging at a rapid pace. The year 2023 has seen some fascinating developments in the world of cocktails, blending the old with the new and introducing exciting trends that are reshaping the way we drink. From classic cocktails given a modern twist to the rise of new, experimental flavors, the cocktail scene has something for everyone.

In this post, we’ll explore the most popular cocktails of 2023, the trends influencing them, and what you can expect from the cocktail culture this year. Whether you’re a seasoned cocktail connoisseur or someone just starting to explore the world of drinks, these trends are sure to inspire your next drink order or even a DIY mixology session at home.

1. The Revival of Classic Cocktails

It’s no secret that the classics never go out of style. In 2023, there’s been a resurgence of timeless cocktails like the Old Fashioned, Negroni, and Martini. These cocktails have stood the test of time for a reason — their balance of flavors, simplicity, and sophistication make them perfect choices for any occasion.

The Old Fashioned

The Old Fashioned continues to be a favorite, celebrated for its simplicity and versatility. Made with whiskey, sugar, bitters, and a twist of citrus peel, this cocktail is a great choice for those who enjoy spirit-forward drinks. Bartenders are experimenting with different types of whiskey, such as rye or bourbon, and adding unique twists like smoked ice or flavored bitters to keep things interesting.

The Negroni

The Negroni, a blend of gin, vermouth rosso, and Campari, remains as popular as ever. Its bold, bitter flavor profile has made it a go-to drink for those who like their cocktails with a little more edge. In 2023, we’re seeing the Negroni being reimagined in various forms, such as the Negroni Sbagliato, which swaps gin for sparkling wine, and the White Negroni, which uses gin, Lillet Blanc, and Suze for a lighter, less bitter flavor.

The Martini

The Martini is an iconic cocktail that has evolved over the years. In 2023, it’s not just about gin or vodka — it’s all about the garnish. While the classic Martini remains a staple, bartenders are experimenting with garnishes ranging from olives to citrus peels, pickled vegetables, and even exotic herbs. The dry Martini remains the most popular choice, but variations like the Dirty Martini (with olive brine) and the Vesper Martini (a combination of gin, vodka, and Lillet Blanc) are gaining traction.

2. The Rise of Low-Alcohol and Non-Alcoholic Cocktails

One of the biggest trends in the cocktail world in 2023 is the rise of low-alcohol and non-alcoholic cocktails. As people become more health-conscious and mindful of their alcohol consumption, bartenders are creating flavorful drinks that cater to this demand.

Low-Alcohol Cocktails

Low-alcohol cocktails are gaining popularity as a way to enjoy the ritual of drinking without the high alcohol content. Drinks like the Spritz, made with prosecco, Aperol, and soda water, are the perfect example of a refreshing, easy-drinking cocktail with a lower alcohol content. In 2023, we’re seeing even more low-ABV options, including drinks that use vermouth, sherry, or fortified wines as their base, often paired with sparkling water or light tonic for added complexity without the buzz.

Non-Alcoholic Cocktails

Non-alcoholic cocktails, also known as mocktails, have come a long way since their humble beginnings. In 2023, they are just as creative and flavorful as their alcoholic counterparts. Ingredients like non-alcoholic spirits, fresh herbs, fruit infusions, and exotic syrups are being used to craft drinks that are just as complex and satisfying. Popular options include non-alcoholic versions of classic cocktails like the Mojito, Margarita, and Negroni, as well as entirely new creations like the Ginger Mule, made with ginger beer, lime, and mint.

3. Innovative Ingredients and Flavors

2023 has been a year for experimentation with bold and unexpected ingredients. From exotic fruits and spices to botanical infusions, bartenders are getting creative with the flavors they incorporate into their drinks. This trend is particularly evident in cocktails that showcase complex, layered flavors that surprise and delight the palate.

Botanical and Herbal Infusions

Herbs and botanicals have been making their way into cocktails for a while, but in 2023, they are being used more than ever. Fresh herbs like basil, thyme, and rosemary are being used in both classic and contemporary cocktails, adding an earthy, aromatic quality to drinks. Additionally, herbal liqueurs like Chartreuse, Benedictine, and Amaro are being used to enhance the flavor profiles of drinks, creating a more herbal, complex taste.

Exotic Fruits and Spices

Exotic fruits such as dragon fruit, lychee, and passionfruit are becoming more common in cocktails, adding vibrant colors and tropical flavors. These fruits are often paired with bold spices like chili, ginger, or turmeric to create drinks that are both refreshing and exciting. A drink like a Spicy Mango Margarita, with fresh mango, chili-infused tequila, and a hint of lime, is a perfect example of how these ingredients are being used to create bold, memorable cocktails.

Fermented and Aged Spirits

Fermented ingredients, such as kombucha and kefir, are making their way into cocktails, adding a tangy, effervescent twist to drinks. Aged spirits, particularly rum, whiskey, and tequila, continue to grow in popularity in 2023. The aging process imparts complex flavors that make these spirits perfect for sipping or mixing into cocktails. Bartenders are experimenting with aged versions of classic cocktails, such as the Aged Rum Old Fashioned, to highlight the rich depth of these spirits.

4. Sustainability in Cocktails

As sustainability becomes an increasingly important factor in the food and beverage industry, it’s no surprise that this trend has made its way into the world of cocktails. In 2023, bartenders and bars are making a conscious effort to reduce waste, use locally sourced ingredients, and focus on sustainable practices.

Zero-Waste Cocktails

Zero-waste cocktails have become a major focus in 2023, with bartenders finding ways to use every part of the ingredients they work with. For example, citrus peels and cores, which would typically be discarded, are being repurposed into syrups, garnishes, or even as a base for infusions. Similarly, leftover herbs and vegetable scraps are being used to create unique bitters and garnishes. This not only reduces waste but also leads to the creation of some truly innovative cocktails.

Local and Seasonal Ingredients

More and more bars are focusing on using local and seasonal ingredients to create cocktails. This approach not only supports local farmers and businesses but also ensures that cocktails are made with the freshest ingredients possible. In 2023, we’re seeing bartenders use everything from locally foraged herbs and flowers to seasonal fruits and vegetables in their drinks, creating a connection to the land and the environment.

5. Elevated Experience: Cocktail Pairings and Presentation

2023 is all about providing an elevated drinking experience, not just in terms of the taste of the cocktail but also the atmosphere and presentation. Cocktail pairings, like those seen in fine dining, are becoming more common, with bars offering food pairings designed to complement specific drinks.

Cocktail and Food Pairings

Just as wine is paired with food, cocktails are now being paired with a variety of dishes to enhance both the flavors of the drink and the meal. In 2023, expect to see innovative pairings such as a smoky Mezcal cocktail paired with grilled meats, or a refreshing gin-based drink served alongside fresh seafood. These pairings elevate the overall dining experience, offering a deeper understanding of the flavors in both the food and the cocktail.

Artistic Presentation

Cocktail presentation has become an art form, with bartenders using everything from intricate garnishes and glassware to dramatic smoke or lighting effects to enhance the overall experience. 2023 has seen more emphasis on visual appeal, with cocktails being crafted to look as stunning as they taste. Expect to see drinks served with unique garnishes like edible flowers, dehydrated fruit, and even smoke-infused bubbles to create a sensory experience that goes beyond just the flavor.

6. The Impact of Technology on Cocktails

Technology is making its mark on the cocktail scene in 2023, with innovations that are shaping the way drinks are made, served, and enjoyed. From apps that help you craft the perfect cocktail at home to advanced bar tools and machines that create precision cocktails, technology is helping to push the boundaries of mixology.

High-Tech Bartending Tools

Bartenders are embracing advanced tools, such as carbonation machines, ice makers that create perfectly clear ice, and precision distillation devices, to craft cocktails with precision and flair. These tools allow for more consistent results and allow bartenders to experiment with new techniques and flavors in ways that were previously not possible.

Cocktail Apps and Virtual Bartenders

Technology is also helping to democratize the art of cocktail making. Cocktail apps and virtual bartenders are becoming more popular, providing users with step-by-step instructions on how to mix their favorite drinks at home. These platforms often feature user-generated content, allowing cocktail lovers to share their recipes and tips with others. This has created a more accessible and inclusive cocktail culture, where everyone can learn to make great drinks, no matter their level of experience.

Conclusion

The cocktail world in 2023 is a vibrant and ever-changing landscape, where tradition meets innovation and creativity knows no bounds. From the revival of classic cocktails to the rise of low-alcohol drinks, exotic ingredients, and sustainable practices, this year has seen a wide variety of trends that are reshaping the way we drink. Whether you’re seeking a classic cocktail, a new twist on an old favorite, or something entirely unique, there’s never been a better time to explore the diverse world of cocktails. Keep an eye on these trends, as they’re sure to shape the future of mixology for years to come.
