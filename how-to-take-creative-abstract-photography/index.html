How to Take Creative Abstract Photography


Abstract photography is a world where the familiar transforms into the unfamiliar, shapes defy convention, and colors evoke raw emotions. Unlike traditional photography, which aims to represent reality, abstract photography encourages you to explore beyond the obvious and to capture the essence of a subject without giving it away entirely. It invites viewers to interpret images in subjective ways and lets creativity soar unrestricted.

Whether you are an aspiring photographer looking to expand your portfolio or a seasoned creative seeking new ways to express yourself, delving into abstract photography offers a rich playground. In this comprehensive guide, we’ll uncover techniques, tools, and mindsets that empower you to take your abstract photography from ordinary snapshots to compelling works of art.

What is Abstract Photography?

Abstract photography focuses on shape, form, color, texture, and light rather than identifiable subjects. It’s about creating images that may not have immediate recognition but evoke feeling or spark curiosity. When you shoot abstractly, the goal isn’t to showcase what something looks like, but how it can be perceived differently. It strips away narrative and realism, placing emphasis on artistic composition.

Abstract photography can be achieved through many methods: close-ups that render objects unrecognizable, intentional camera movement, playing with light and shadows, or digital manipulation—all of which break free from the typical conventions of photography.

Cultivating the Abstract Mindset

The most critical factor in producing creative abstract photography is cultivating an abstract mindset. This means training yourself to:

  • Look beyond objects’ typical functions or identities.
  • Focus on patterns, textures, and unusual perspectives.
  • Experiment fearlessly with composition, light, and focus.
  • Embrace ambiguity and embrace imperfection.

Building awareness for these elements will help you see the world through a new lens, and gradually your creative intuition will deepen.

Essential Equipment for Abstract Photography

You don’t need the most expensive gear for abstract photography, but certain tools can expand your options and elevate your work:

  • Camera: Any DSLR, mirrorless, or even a quality smartphone camera can work. Manual controls give you greater creative freedom.
  • Lenses: A macro lens is excellent for detailed close-ups that transform subjects into textured abstract forms. Prime lenses with wide apertures allow experimentation with shallow depth of field.
  • Tripod: For deliberate long exposures or controlled movements, having a stable base is invaluable.
  • Filters: Polarizers, neutral densities, or color filters allow manipulation of light, reflections, and tones.
  • Light sources: Natural or artificial lighting setups, including flashlights or colored gels, can reshape how you produce textures and shadows.

Techniques to Create Abstract Images

1. Zooming and Blurring for Dynamic Motion

By using a slow shutter speed on a tripod or handheld, try zooming your lens in or out while the shutter is open. The resulting image produces striking streaks of light and distorted shapes that are vibrant and swirling, perfect for abstract expression.

Alternatively, you can move the whole camera intentionally during an exposure—pan horizontally or vertically to smear the scene into flowing bands of color.

2. Get Close: Macro Abstraction

Get extremely close to textures and surfaces such as rusted metal, peeling paint, or fabric folds. Under magnification, these become unfamiliar landscapes filled with intricate lines and gradients. Focusing on a portion of a texture enhances the mystery.

3. Lighting and Shadows Play

Use directional light to create stark contrasts and unusual shadows that convert simple objects into surreal compositions. Try point lighting or side lighting to exaggerate textures and depth. Play with backlighting to create silhouettes that are both minimalistic and abstract.

Colored lighting gels worn as filters over flash or continuous light sources can add a layer of unorthodox color dynamics.

4. Reflection and Refraction

Reflections on water, glass, or metal surfaces distort reality, creating dreamlike or fractured representations of everyday scenes. Shooting from unusual angles can amplify this effect. Using prisms or crystals in front of the lens also bends and multiplies light, offering kaleidoscopic tricks.

5. Multiple Exposures and Layering

Combine two or more images to create surreal or textured abstracts. This can be done in-camera (if your camera has multiple exposure settings) or during post-processing. Overlaying patterned shots or combining contrasting elements can provide narrative layers without obvious meaning.

6. Minimalist Composition

Embrace simplicity by isolating shapes, colors, and forms against neutral or monochromatic backgrounds. Minimalism directs the viewer’s focus and invites deeper contemplation of the abstract elements.

7. Use of Color Theory

Understanding how colors interact can turn an image from ordinary to emotively powerful. Complementary colors radiate tension and vibrant contrast, while analogous colors soothe with harmony. Employ unexpected color combinations or desaturate parts of your image for emphasis.

8. Emphasize Texture and Detail

Texture injects a tactile sense into abstract images, encouraging sensory engagement beyond the visual. Surfaces like cracked earth, peeling paint, woven fabrics, or frosted glass all lend character. Use angled light to highlight texture or shallow depth of field to isolate details.

Post-Processing to Enhance Abstract Art

Editing plays a crucial role in abstract work, both in refining and manipulating images to bring your creative vision to life.

  • Cropping can intensify abstraction by tightly framing parts of an image and eliminating recognizable context.
  • Adjust contrast and brightness to emphasize shapes and lines or create dramatic silhouettes.
  • Experiment with saturation and color balance to push the mood of the photo; moody blues, fiery reds, or surreal purples can shift emotional tone.
  • Use selective blur or sharpening to highlight or soften important elements in your composition.
  • Apply filters and artistic effects sparingly to introduce texture or surreal qualities.

Software like Adobe Lightroom, Photoshop, or even mobile apps provide robust tools tailored for nuanced editing.

Finding Inspiration and Developing Your Style

Abstract photography thrives on experimentation, so staying inspired and curious is essential. Here’s how to nurture your creative journey:

  • Regularly challenge yourself with new themes (e.g., water, metal, fabric) or techniques.
  • Study the work of abstract artists and photographers for insight into composition and mood.
  • Keep a photography journal to record your ideas, experiments, successes, and failures.
  • Share your images with a creative community for feedback and motivation.

Remember, your unique perspective is your greatest asset. Over time, you’ll discover recurring motifs, color preferences, or compositional approaches that define your signature abstract style.

Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

  • Overly literal images: Avoid images where objects remain clearly identifiable; abstraction relies on ambiguity.
  • Cluttered compositions: Too much detail can overwhelm the viewer. Strive for balance and focus.
  • Lack of intentionality: Abstract photos should appear spontaneous but result from deliberate creative choices.
  • Ignoring technical fundamentals: Even with experimentation, proper exposure and focus retain professionalism and visual impact.

Abstract Photography: Beyond the Frame

Abstract photography isn’t limited to static art—it can be powerful in storytelling, advertising, and design. Its ability to evoke mood, provoke thought, and stimulate imagination makes it prized across creative industries.

Experimenting with abstract concepts can also refine your overall photographic eye, as it encourages close observation of detail, light, and form.

Final Thoughts

Venturing into creative abstract photography transforms how you perceive and capture the world. It invites you to explore the beauty hidden in shapes, colors, and light patterns beyond literal reality. The magic lies in revealing the unseen, crafting mystery, and engaging emotions without words.

So grab your camera, leave preconceptions at the door, and let your imagination roam freely. The abstract realm awaits your vision.

With practice and openness, abstract photography becomes not just a technique, but a language to express the infinite nuances of your artistic soul. Enjoy the journey of discovery!
