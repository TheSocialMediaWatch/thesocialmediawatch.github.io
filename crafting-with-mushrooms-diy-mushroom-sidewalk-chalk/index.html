Crafting with Mushrooms: DIY Mushroom Sidewalk Chalk


Family vacations and summer breaks always remind me of the irresistible allure of sidewalk chalk. From creating detailed pixie worlds to simple shapes and colors, the artistry of chalk on cement has ignited the imagination of children for decades. However, have you ever thought of stepping into the exciting world of crafting using a more unorthodox material – mushrooms? Say hello to DIY Mushroom Sidewalk Chalk!

The Power of Mushrooms

Mushrooms are more than just culinary delicacies or enigmatic forest inhabitants. These fascinating organisms have a myriad of practical uses ranging from medicine to biofuel production. The majority of their application comes from the mycelium, an underground network of threads that make up the majority of the fungal body. Mycelium is an incredibly versatile material that can be molded and shaped into various forms and textures, making it perfect for our DIY chalk experiment.

What You Need

To make your one-of-a-kind mushroom chalk, you’ll need the following:

  1. Mycelium: For our experiment, mushrooms of the Pleurotus species are best. You can either grow your own mycelium at home using a grain medium, purchase it dried or as a mycelium growth board, or, if you’re lucky enough to know a mycelium supplier, source it directly from them.

  2. Chalkdust: Find a variety that is non-toxic and suitable for children. Make sure it is available in your desired colors.

  3. Mixing bowl: You will need one to mix your chalkdust and mycelium.

  4. Blender or food processor: This tool will help you break down the mycelium into smaller bits to mix with the chalkdust.

  5. Refrigerator: Keep your new chalk creations cool to hardness at all times.

  6. Molds: To create your chalk, molds in various shapes can be used. Heart-shaped and star-shaped silicone molds work very well but feel free to channel your creativity.

The Crafting Process

  1. Start by growing or obtaining your mycelium in the desired substrate or board.

  2. While your mycelium matures, prepare the chalkdust by dividing it into separate containers based on the mold colors you plan to use.

  3. Once your mycelium is ready, remove it from the substrate and cut it into small pieces. You can blend or food process the mycelium to get evenly-sized bits. This step is essential for an even distribution of mushroom in your chalk.

  4. Combine the mycelium bits with the chalkdust in your mixing bowl. Stir gently to ensure even distribution. You may need to adjust the ratio of mycelium to chalkdust depending on how “mushroomy” you want your chalk to be. If you’re a-fungi of experimentation, play around with mixing different ratios of mycelium and chalkdust to see what you prefer.

  5. Transfer the chalk mixture to your selected molds. Press it down lightly and level the surface to ensure uniformity.

  6. Refrigerate your molds. The cold will help set the chalk and solidify the mycelium within the chalkdust.

  7. After a few hours, you can remove the chalk forms and let them dry out for 24 hours at room temperature.

  8. Your DIY Mushroom Sidewalk Chalk is now ready to use! Draw attention-grabbing murals, or create playful and thought-provoking artwork with your one-of-a-kind chalk, knowing you are one step closer to blending the wondrous worlds of nature and art.

Conclusion

So, the next time you’re looking for an innovative and engaging activity to do with your friends or family, consider crafting with mushrooms. By transforming your sidewalk chalk experiences with the enigmatic power of fungi, you’re not just creating a work of art or playing a game; you’re encouraging others to ponder the mysteries of nature, all while illustrating the boundless potential of the world of mycology.

And remember, the beauty of this project lies in its experimental nature. So don’t hesitate to combine your love for art with a sprinkle of whimsy, and discover a new facet of the wonderful world of fungi.
