The Top Animated Series for Teens


In the world of animated series, there’s a diverse array of shows that cater to different age groups. Among these, teen-oriented animated series stand out due to their unique blend of humor, adventure, and deeper themes that resonate with young viewers. Whether it’s dealing with the complexities of adolescence, navigating fantastical worlds, or diving into emotional journeys, these animated series engage teens in a way that no other medium can.

In this post, we’ll explore the top animated series for teens, highlighting what makes them stand out and why they are so beloved by their audience.

1. Avatar: The Last Airbender

Perhaps one of the most iconic animated series for teens, Avatar: The Last Airbender remains a cultural touchstone. Set in a world divided into four elemental nations—Water, Earth, Fire, and Air—this series follows Aang, the last Airbender and the Avatar, who must restore balance to the world. The story combines martial arts, magic, and rich character development, making it not just an action-packed adventure, but a tale of personal growth and responsibility.

The deep themes of friendship, identity, and overcoming personal challenges make Avatar: The Last Airbender a perfect watch for teens who are figuring out who they are and what they stand for. The series also introduces important messages about peace, conflict resolution, and the value of understanding different cultures, which resonate deeply with a young audience.

2. Teen Titans Go!

If you’re in the mood for a lighter, more comedic series, Teen Titans Go! is an excellent choice. Based on DC Comics’ Teen Titans, this series takes a more humorous and often irreverent approach to the lives of the beloved superheroes. Unlike other adaptations, Teen Titans Go! emphasizes the goofy side of the characters, turning their daily lives into absurd, often hilarious adventures.

The show’s fast pace and quirky humor make it a perfect pick for teens who are looking for an escape from the more serious aspects of life. While it doesn’t delve into the deep themes that other series might, it still offers a fun way to spend time with characters that many teens grew up with.

3. BoJack Horseman

A groundbreaking series in the world of animated television, BoJack Horseman explores the life of a washed-up actor who happens to be a horse. The show is not just about his comedic misadventures, but it dives deep into complex themes of mental health, addiction, and self-worth. The series is lauded for its exploration of depression, fame, and the existential crises that many teens face as they grow older.

BoJack Horseman is a prime example of an animated series that’s not afraid to tackle difficult and often taboo subjects. The wit and humor that are present throughout the show make it accessible, while its depth ensures that older teens and young adults can relate to its emotional core.

4. Rick and Morty

For teens who love science fiction with a side of dark humor, Rick and Morty is a must-watch. Created by Justin Roiland and Dan Harmon, this series follows the misadventures of a mad scientist, Rick Sanchez, and his good-hearted but easily manipulated grandson, Morty. With its blend of slapstick comedy, intellectual themes, and absurd universes, Rick and Morty appeals to teens who enjoy mind-bending concepts wrapped in humor.

What sets Rick and Morty apart from other animated series is its ability to mix absurd, often surreal adventures with philosophical questions about existence, meaning, and the universe. The show pushes boundaries and challenges societal norms, offering something for teens looking for more than just a straightforward comedy.

5. Big Mouth

Teens are no strangers to the complexities of adolescence, and Big Mouth takes an unapologetic, sometimes uncomfortable look at the awkwardness of growing up. The series follows a group of middle schoolers as they navigate the challenges of puberty with the help of personified hormones, who guide them through their emotional and physical changes. The humor in Big Mouth is raw, honest, and often gross, making it a perfect series for teens who want to laugh about the awkward moments they experience during puberty.

The show does not shy away from discussing difficult topics like sexuality, body image, and mental health. By presenting these issues in a frank and humorous way, Big Mouth provides a platform for teens to process and laugh at the chaos that comes with growing up.

6. The Owl House

For teens who enjoy fantasy and magic, The Owl House offers an enchanting world that mixes supernatural elements with real-life teen issues. The story follows Luz, a girl who stumbles upon a portal to a magical world where she befriends an eccentric witch named Eda. Together with a tiny, powerful sidekick named King, Luz embarks on a journey to become a witch herself.

The Owl House stands out due to its inclusive representation of LGBTQ+ characters, particularly in a genre that often lacks diverse narratives. The series tackles themes of acceptance, self-discovery, and friendship, all while keeping the tone light and adventurous. Teens will find the characters relatable, and the world-building and magical adventures are captivating.

7. Adventure Time

One of the most beloved animated series in recent years, Adventure Time follows the adventures of Finn, a human boy, and his best friend Jake, a shape-shifting dog, as they explore the post-apocalyptic land of Ooo. The series combines surreal humor, touching moments, and deep emotional storylines that appeal to both kids and teens.

What makes Adventure Time unique is its balance between lighthearted fun and serious themes about growing up, love, and the nature of friendship. As the series progresses, it delves into more mature topics, such as loss and the impact of time, making it a show that resonates with teens on a personal level.

8. Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (Movie)

While technically a movie rather than a series, Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse has earned its place as one of the top animated picks for teens. This visually stunning film introduces multiple versions of Spider-Man from different dimensions, with the focus on Miles Morales, a teenager who takes up the mantle of Spider-Man. The film is full of action, but it’s the emotional arc of Miles’ journey that truly makes it stand out.

The movie tackles themes of self-discovery, responsibility, and the pressures that come with being a young superhero. For teens, the film is an empowering reminder that anyone can be a hero, no matter their background or circumstances.

9. Steven Universe

Steven Universe is a show that beautifully combines fantasy, music, and heartfelt storytelling. It follows Steven, a young boy who is part of the Crystal Gems, a group of magical beings tasked with protecting the Earth. Over the course of the series, Steven learns about love, family, and self-identity, all while trying to navigate his role in the world.

The show’s inclusive representation of gender and sexuality makes it an important series for today’s teens. With its focus on acceptance, empathy, and personal growth, Steven Universe speaks to teens who may be grappling with their own identity or those simply looking for a show that champions kindness and understanding.

10. Gravity Falls

Gravity Falls is an animated mystery-comedy series that has gained a loyal following for its clever writing, engaging characters, and underlying supernatural elements. The show follows twins Dipper and Mabel Pines as they spend their summer with their great-uncle Stan in the mysterious town of Gravity Falls. As they uncover bizarre and supernatural occurrences, the twins learn more about themselves and the world around them.

The series balances humor with deeper emotional moments, exploring themes of family, growing up, and the unknown. The strong character development and intriguing mysteries make it a great choice for teens who love to think and engage with more complex stories.

Conclusion

Animated series for teens offer much more than just entertainment; they serve as a reflection of the experiences, challenges, and triumphs of adolescence. Whether it’s through magic, humor, or deep emotional journeys, these shows provide teens with both escapism and valuable life lessons. From light-hearted comedy to intense action and existential drama, there’s something for every teen in the world of animated television. The best part? These shows are as fun as they are meaningful, making them essential viewing for young people navigating the twists and turns of growing up.
