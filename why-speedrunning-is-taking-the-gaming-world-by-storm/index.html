Why Speedrunning Is Taking the Gaming World by Storm


In recent years, the gaming world has seen a unique and fascinating trend rise to prominence—speedrunning. This phenomenon, which revolves around completing a video game in the shortest time possible, has garnered a massive following and has become a central part of online gaming culture. But what is speedrunning, and why is it capturing the attention of so many gamers worldwide? In this post, we will dive into the essence of speedrunning, explore the different categories of speedruns, and understand why this niche has evolved into one of the most exciting aspects of gaming today.

What Is Speedrunning?

At its core, speedrunning is about completing a video game as fast as possible. Players, often referred to as speedrunners, aim to finish a game in the least amount of time, exploiting knowledge of the game mechanics, discovering hidden tricks, and sometimes using glitches or unintended features to speed up their progress.

What sets speedrunning apart from traditional gameplay is the competitive edge. Speedrunners are not simply playing for fun or to explore the narrative of the game—they are racing against the clock, challenging themselves to break personal records and, in many cases, world records. The pursuit of the fastest completion time requires an intense level of dedication, precision, and mastery of the game, making it a unique and compelling pursuit within the gaming community.

The Evolution of Speedrunning

Speedrunning has existed in some form since the early days of video gaming. In the 1980s and 1990s, gamers would often race to finish games like Super Mario Bros. or The Legend of Zelda as quickly as possible. However, the modern speedrunning scene began to take shape in the early 2000s with the advent of online communities and streaming platforms. Websites like SpeedDemosArchive (SDA) were among the first to bring together a community of players dedicated to optimizing their game runs.

As technology evolved, so did speedrunning. The rise of streaming services, particularly Twitch, gave players a platform to showcase their skills to a global audience. Viewers could watch their favorite speedrunners attempt to break records in real-time, creating a more interactive and engaging experience. The growth of social media and video-sharing platforms like YouTube also played a significant role, as gamers could share their runs, discuss techniques, and collaborate with other speedrunners.

One of the key factors contributing to the rise of speedrunning has been the advent of tool-assisted speedruns (TAS). While these runs rely on the use of specialized software to optimize gameplay, they have opened new doors for exploring the boundaries of game mechanics. TAS allows players to create nearly flawless runs, showcasing what is theoretically possible within a game. This level of precision has drawn a lot of attention from both gamers and non-gamers alike.

Categories of Speedrunning

Speedrunning is not a one-size-fits-all endeavor. There are numerous categories that cater to different playstyles, objectives, and rules. The diversity of categories has helped speedrunning expand into a rich and varied subculture.

1. Any% Speedrun

The most popular and straightforward category is the Any% speedrun. In this format, the objective is simply to finish the game as fast as possible, regardless of how much content is completed along the way. The only requirement is that the game must be completed, and the fastest time is the winner. This often leads to the discovery of glitches and exploits that allow players to bypass large portions of the game.

2. 100% Speedrun

In contrast to Any%, the 100% speedrun requires players to complete every possible objective in the game—whether that means collecting every item, unlocking every level, or defeating all bosses. This category is far more demanding, as it requires a comprehensive knowledge of the game and a high level of skill to complete every task as quickly as possible.

3. Glitchless Speedrun

Some players prefer a more purist approach to speedrunning and aim to complete the game without exploiting any glitches. In a Glitchless speedrun, players must navigate the game in a way that aligns with its intended design, relying solely on their skill and knowledge of the game mechanics to reach the finish line.

4. Any% No Major Glitches (NMG)

This category is a variation of the Any% speedrun, with the added rule that certain major glitches—those that fundamentally break the game—are prohibited. While players are still allowed to use minor glitches or sequence breaks, this category emphasizes skillful play without relying on game-breaking exploits.

5. Single-Segment Speedrun

A Single-Segment speedrun involves completing the game in one continuous session without any interruptions or resets. If the player fails or dies, they must restart from the beginning. This adds a level of tension and excitement to the run, as the player must avoid mistakes while maintaining optimal performance throughout the entire game.

6. Race Speedruns

In Race speedruns, two or more players compete against each other in real-time to see who can complete the game the fastest. These races often take place during live-streamed events or speedrunning marathons and add a layer of excitement as players vie for the top spot.

7. Challenge Runs

Challenge runs involve completing a game under self-imposed restrictions, such as playing with only one hand, using specific weapons, or completing the game without taking any damage. These runs often have their own set of rules and offer an added layer of difficulty for players seeking an extra challenge.

Why Speedrunning Is So Popular

The rapid rise of speedrunning can be attributed to a number of factors that make it an engaging and exciting pursuit for both players and spectators alike.

1. The Thrill of Competition

At its core, speedrunning is about competing against the clock and others in the community. Whether it’s trying to break a world record or improving on personal bests, the competitive aspect of speedrunning fuels a sense of excitement and achievement. Many players find themselves drawn to the challenge of pushing their limits and seeing how fast they can complete a game.

2. The Community and Camaraderie

One of the most important aspects of speedrunning is the sense of community that surrounds it. Speedrunners from all over the world come together to share strategies, discuss techniques, and celebrate their successes. Online forums, Discord servers, and streaming platforms like Twitch have allowed speedrunners to build strong bonds and collaborate on runs. Many players also participate in charity events, such as Games Done Quick (GDQ), where speedrunning marathons raise money for various causes. This spirit of camaraderie and altruism has made speedrunning a welcoming and supportive community.

3. The Joy of Discovery

Speedrunning encourages players to explore games in ways they might not have considered otherwise. By diving deep into the mechanics of a game, speedrunners uncover hidden tricks, glitches, and strategies that even the developers may not have anticipated. This sense of discovery keeps the gaming experience fresh and exciting, even for well-worn titles.

4. The Appeal of Replayability

While many games are designed with a single playthrough in mind, speedrunning introduces the concept of replayability in a whole new way. Players repeatedly attempt to improve their times, trying to shave off precious seconds and refine their execution. This constant refinement fosters a sense of mastery and accomplishment that keeps players engaged over long periods.

5. A Gateway to New Audiences

The rise of platforms like Twitch and YouTube has made speedrunning more accessible than ever before. Viewers can watch speedrunners tackle their favorite games in real-time, learn new techniques, and join in the excitement. Speedrunning has become a form of entertainment, with some runs attracting hundreds of thousands of viewers. This exposure has helped introduce speedrunning to a broader audience, turning it into a legitimate form of competitive gaming.

6. The Speedrunning Marathon Phenomenon

One of the most exciting developments in the speedrunning world is the emergence of speedrunning marathons. Events like Games Done Quick have brought together large communities of speedrunners and raised millions of dollars for charity. These marathons showcase the best speedrunners in the world, who attempt to break records while raising awareness for good causes. The combination of high-level gameplay, charitable donations, and a sense of community makes these events a celebration of the speedrunning culture.

The Future of Speedrunning

The future of speedrunning looks incredibly bright. As technology continues to improve, players will gain access to more advanced tools and techniques to push the limits of what’s possible. The rise of virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) could open new avenues for speedrunning, allowing players to navigate fully immersive environments at lightning speeds. Additionally, as gaming continues to evolve, new genres and titles will present fresh opportunities for speedrunning communities to explore.

In conclusion, speedrunning is not just a trend—it’s a movement that has reshaped the gaming world. With its unique blend of competition, community, discovery, and entertainment, speedrunning has captured the hearts of gamers worldwide and shows no signs of slowing down. Whether you’re a seasoned speedrunner or a curious spectator, the world of speedrunning offers something for everyone. It’s a testament to the creativity, dedication, and passion that define the gaming community, and it’s exciting to see where it will go next.
