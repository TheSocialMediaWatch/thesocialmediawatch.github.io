Fishing for Northern Pike: Tips and Tricks for Catching These Ferocious Fish


Are you an avid angler looking to add some excitement to your fishing trips? Look no further than the Northern Pike, a ferocious predator that will test your skills and give you an adrenaline rush like no other. In this blog post, we will dive into the world of Northern Pike fishing and share some valuable tips and tricks to help you become a master angler.

Understanding the Northern Pike

Before we get into the tips and tricks, let’s take a moment to understand our target species - the Northern Pike. These formidable fish are known for their elongated bodies, sharp teeth, and aggressive nature. They can grow up to 4 feet in length and weigh over 20 pounds, making them a challenging catch for any angler.

Choosing the Right Gear

To successfully catch Northern Pike, you need to have the right gear in your arsenal. Here are some essential items you should consider:

  1. Rods and Reels: Opt for a medium to heavy-action spinning or baitcasting rod with a fast or extra-fast tip. Pair it with a reel that has a smooth drag system to handle the powerful runs of a Northern Pike.

  2. Fishing Line: Use a braided fishing line with a high pound test (around 20-30 lbs) to withstand the pike’s sharp teeth and aggressive strikes.

  3. Leaders: Attach a wire leader to prevent the pike from biting through your line. A 12-18 inch leader with a 30-50 lb test is ideal.

  4. Lures: Northern Pike are known to strike at a variety of lures. Experiment with spinnerbaits, spoons, crankbaits, and jerkbaits to see which ones work best in your fishing spot.

Locating the Northern Pike

Now that you have your gear ready, it’s time to find the Northern Pike’s favorite hiding spots. These fish are commonly found in weed beds, rocky areas, and near drop-offs. Look for shallow areas with dense vegetation, submerged logs, or structures like docks and fallen trees. Northern Pike are ambush predators, so they tend to lurk near these areas, waiting to strike their prey.

Techniques for Catching Northern Pike

  1. Casting and Retrieving: Cast your lure near the weed beds or structures and retrieve it with an erratic action to mimic a wounded baitfish. Pike are attracted to the movement and will strike aggressively.

  2. Trolling: If you have a boat, trolling can be an effective technique. Use a combination of shallow and deep-diving lures to cover a larger area and entice the pike to strike.

  3. Dead Bait Fishing: For a more passive approach, you can use dead baitfish as bait. Attach a wire leader to your line and hook the baitfish through the lips or the back. Allow it to sink and wait for the pike to take the bait.

Playing and Landing the Pike

Once you’ve hooked a Northern Pike, the real challenge begins. These fish are known for their powerful runs and acrobatic jumps. Here are a few tips to help you land your catch successfully:

  1. Keep the Line Tight: Maintain a steady pressure on the fish to prevent it from shaking the hook and escaping.

  2. Use a Landing Net: When the pike is near the surface, carefully scoop it up with a landing net to avoid losing your catch.

  3. Handle with Care: Pike have sharp teeth, so use a pair of pliers or a jaw spreader to safely remove the hook. Always support the fish horizontally and return it to the water quickly to ensure its survival.

Conclusion

Fishing for Northern Pike is an exhilarating experience that tests both your skills and your gear. With the right techniques and a bit of patience, you can have a thrilling day on the water and catch these ferocious fish. Remember to respect the environment and practice catch-and-release whenever possible to preserve the population of Northern Pike for future generations.

So grab your gear, head to your favorite fishing spot, and get ready to battle with the Northern Pike. Happy fishing!
