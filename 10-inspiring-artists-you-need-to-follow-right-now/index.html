10 Inspiring Artists You Need to Follow Right Now


In the vast and ever-evolving world of art, discovering fresh and inspiring talent can be both exhilarating and challenging. Whether you’re an artist seeking motivation, an art lover wanting to stay on top of trends, or simply curious about who’s shaping the creative landscape today, here’s a curated list of ten exceptional artists whose work resonates deeply and whose voices deserve your attention now.

1. Maya Lin — The Sculptor of Silence and Space

Known primarily for her minimalist yet profoundly evocative sculptures and installations, Maya Lin’s work explores memory, environment, and the interplay between nature and human impact. Her ability to create spaces that evoke contemplation is unmatched. From large-scale land art to intimate gallery pieces, her artistry invites viewers to pause and reflect on the world around them.

Why Follow Maya Lin?

Her commitment to environmental themes, combined with her innovative use of natural materials, makes her art both beautiful and thought-provoking. Lin’s projects often blur the boundary between landscape and sculpture, inspiring a new way of understanding art’s role in ecological discourse.

2. Kehinde Wiley — A Bold Reimagining of Portraiture

Kehinde Wiley challenges traditional portraiture with his vibrant, majestic paintings that place African American individuals in classical European poses. His work is both a celebration of Black identity and a critique of art history’s Eurocentric narratives.

What Makes Him Stand Out?

Wiley’s rich colors, intricate patterns, and fearless subject matter breathe new life into portraiture. By centering subjects often marginalized in mainstream art, he invites conversations about race, power, and representation.

3. Yayoi Kusama — The Queen of Infinity

Yayoi Kusama’s signature polka dots, nets, and infinity rooms have made her one of the most visually recognizable contemporary artists in the world. Her art merges personal experience with psychedelic visuals, often reflecting her struggles with mental health.

Reasons to Follow Kusama

Her installations are immersive and transformative, creating environments that challenge perceptions of self and universe. Kusama’s prolific output across painting, sculpture, and installation art continues to captivate global audiences with its whimsical and sometimes haunting beauty.

4. Tschabalala Self — Celebrating Identity and Form

Tschabalala Self’s multidisciplinary approach combines painting, textile, and collage to explore Black female identity and the body. Her works are colorful, dynamic, and unapologetically bold.

Why She’s Inspiring

Self challenges stereotypical notions of beauty and representation by crafting figures that are both fragmented and whole, pushing viewers to reconsider ideas around race, gender, and body politics.

5. Ai Weiwei — Art as Activism

Ai Weiwei’s fearless activism through art has positioned him as a key figure in contemporary culture. His large-scale installations, sculptures, and documentaries address human rights, freedom of expression, and political oppression.

What to Watch For

His work is not only aesthetically arresting but also imbued with deep social commentary, encouraging audiences to question authority and engage with pressing global issues.

6. Njideka Akunyili Crosby — Bridging Cultures with Intimacy

Njideka Akunyili Crosby blends painting, collage, and photo transfers to depict the nuances of the African diaspora experience. Her layered compositions explore family, culture, and identity with sensitivity and complexity.

The Draw of Her Art

Her ability to intertwine personal history with broader cultural narratives provides a window into the spaces between homeland and adopted home, fostering empathy and understanding.

7. James Jean — The Master of Surreal Narratives

James Jean’s work seamlessly integrates fantasy, storytelling, and fine art. Known for his ethereal and richly detailed imagery, his paintings draw on mythology, dreams, and pop culture.

Why He’s a Must-Follow

Jean’s artistry invites viewers into alternate realms where the boundaries of reality bend—perfect for those craving imaginative escapism and intricate visual storytelling.

8. Angel Otero — Innovation with Paint

Angel Otero pushes the limits of painting using unconventional techniques, creating works that are simultaneously portraits, landscapes, and abstract compositions. His layered and ceremonially-sourced paint skins offer fresh textures and depth.

The Appeal of Otero’s Technique

Otero’s inventive process and vibrant palette challenge traditional perceptions of what painting can be, making his art an ongoing experiment in perception and materiality.

9. Shantell Martin — Visualizing the Mind’s Flow

Shantell Martin captures the spontaneity of thought with her distinctive black-and-white drawings. Through flowing lines and interconnected patterns, her art is a graphic diary of inner dialogues and social commentary.

What Draws You In

Martin’s work feels intimate and universal simultaneously—a visual metaphor for the constant conversation within ourselves and with others, rendered through simple yet powerful strokes.

10. Zaria Forman — The Art of Climate Awareness

Zaria Forman creates breathtakingly realistic pastel drawings of glaciers, oceans, and landscapes affected by climate change. Her work is both art and activism, aiming to inspire awareness and action around environmental issues.

Why You Should Follow Her

Her detailed and emotive depictions not only highlight nature’s beauty but also starkly illustrate its fragility, transforming environmental data into affective, tangible art.

Staying Connected with Contemporary Visionaries

Following these ten artists offers a diverse cross-section of the art world today—and the chance to engage with themes that are shaping society: identity, politics, environment, and innovation. Each artist invites us to look deeper, rethink assumptions, and experience the world anew through the lens of creative mastery.

For those eager to explore and be inspired, diving into these artists’ works, exhibitions, and social media channels can open doors to fresh perspectives and new creative energy. Whether you create art yourself or simply enjoy absorbing it, these visionaries are igniting conversations and transforming our cultural landscape with every brushstroke, sculpture, and installation.

Take a moment to look closer, think bigger, and get inspired by their daring and dynamic contributions to art today.
