Best Hiking Trails Near Popular Campsites


If you’re an outdoor enthusiast, nothing beats the thrill of a hiking adventure combined with a peaceful camping experience. The great outdoors offers a unique chance to connect with nature, and what better way to immerse yourself in it than by exploring scenic hiking trails near well-loved campsites? Whether you’re a seasoned hiker or just starting out, these trails offer breathtaking views, diverse landscapes, and an unforgettable connection to nature.

In this blog post, we will dive into some of the best hiking trails near popular campsites. From towering peaks to tranquil lakes, these trails are perfect for hikers of all levels, providing the ideal balance of challenging routes and serene spots to relax. Let’s explore these hidden gems where you can enjoy both hiking and camping in harmony.

1. Great Smoky Mountains National Park - Elkmont Campground

The Great Smoky Mountains National Park, known for its mist-covered mountains and rich biodiversity, is home to one of the best hiking experiences in the country. Located near Elkmont Campground, hikers can access a number of trails that offer stunning views of the mountains, forests, and streams.

Elkmont Nature Trail

The Elkmont Nature Trail is a moderate, 2.6-mile loop that takes you through a beautiful old-growth forest. As you walk, you’ll encounter diverse plant life and wildlife, including deer, raccoons, and numerous bird species. The trail is family-friendly and ideal for those looking for a relaxing yet immersive hike.

Alum Cave Trail

For a more challenging hike, the Alum Cave Trail offers a strenuous 4.4-mile round trip with an elevation gain of about 1,000 feet. The trail takes hikers through rhododendron thickets, past historic structures, and offers expansive views from the Alum Cave Bluffs at the end. The bluff’s 80-foot-high rock overhang is a must-see, and the journey is both physically demanding and incredibly rewarding.

2. Yosemite National Park - North Pines Campground

Yosemite National Park, renowned for its iconic granite cliffs, waterfalls, and giant sequoias, offers a variety of trails that cater to both novice and experienced hikers. North Pines Campground, nestled within Yosemite Valley, is an excellent base for exploring the park’s vast hiking network.

Mist Trail to Vernal and Nevada Falls

One of the most famous hikes in Yosemite, the Mist Trail, takes you to two of the park’s most stunning waterfalls: Vernal Falls and Nevada Falls. The 3-mile round trip to Vernal Falls is moderately strenuous, while the full 7-mile loop, which includes Nevada Falls, is a more challenging trek. Along the way, you’ll be treated to misty spray from the falls, which creates a refreshing atmosphere even during the warmer months.

Four-Mile Trail

If you’re looking for a more ambitious adventure, the Four-Mile Trail provides an incredible panoramic view of Yosemite Valley. This challenging 4.8-mile one-way hike ascends 3,200 feet, offering awe-inspiring views of Yosemite Falls, Half Dome, and Glacier Point. It’s a great trail for those seeking to experience the grandeur of Yosemite’s landscape while testing their endurance.

3. Banff National Park - Tunnel Mountain Campground

Banff National Park in Canada is a stunning wilderness of alpine lakes, snow-capped peaks, and dense forests. Tunnel Mountain Campground, located just outside the town of Banff, offers easy access to several fantastic hiking trails that will take you through the heart of the Canadian Rockies.

Tunnel Mountain Trail

The Tunnel Mountain Trail is a short but steep 1.7-mile trail that offers fantastic views of Banff town, the Bow River, and the surrounding mountains. The hike takes about an hour to complete, making it perfect for a morning or evening excursion. While the ascent can be challenging, the panoramic views at the summit make it all worthwhile.

Johnston Canyon Trail

For a more immersive trail, the Johnston Canyon Trail is a relatively easy 5.2-mile round trip that leads to a series of beautiful waterfalls. The trail passes through lush forests and along a narrow canyon, with wooden bridges offering close-up views of the waterfalls and rock formations. It’s a magical experience, especially when the waterfalls are in full flow during spring and early summer.

4. Zion National Park - South Campground

Zion National Park is one of the most popular national parks in the United States, famous for its towering sandstone cliffs, narrow canyons, and desert landscapes. South Campground, located near the park’s main entrance, serves as an excellent base for exploring Zion’s many hiking opportunities.

Angels Landing Trail

One of the most iconic and challenging hikes in the park is the Angels Landing Trail. This 5.4-mile round trip is considered one of the most exhilarating hikes in the U.S., featuring steep switchbacks and narrow ridgelines. The last section involves holding onto chains while navigating along a sheer cliff face. The views from the summit, which overlook the entire Zion Canyon, are absolutely breathtaking. This hike is not for the faint of heart but rewards brave adventurers with unparalleled vistas.

The Narrows

The Narrows is another Zion classic, where hikers wade through the Virgin River while walking between towering canyon walls. The trail can be done as a short hike or as a full 10-mile round trip. The Narrows are most accessible in the summer and early fall when the river is lower and calmer. Hikers should always check conditions before attempting this trail, as flash floods can occur in the canyon.

5. Acadia National Park - Blackwoods Campground

Acadia National Park, located in Maine, offers rugged coastal landscapes, beautiful forests, and an array of wildlife. Blackwoods Campground, situated near the park’s main entrance, provides easy access to some of Acadia’s most picturesque and rewarding hikes.

Cadillac Mountain Summit Trail

Cadillac Mountain is the highest peak in Acadia, and the Cadillac Mountain Summit Trail is the perfect way to reach the top. The 4.4-mile round trip trail ascends over 1,500 feet, offering spectacular views of the surrounding islands, bays, and forests. If you’re visiting in the fall, the changing foliage adds an extra layer of beauty to the landscape.

Jordan Pond Path

For a more leisurely hike, the Jordan Pond Path is a beautiful 3.3-mile loop around the clear, serene waters of Jordan Pond. This relatively easy trail offers a peaceful walk through Acadia’s lush forests, with views of the surrounding mountains. It’s a great option for those looking to unwind after a long day of hiking or camping.

6. Shenandoah National Park - Loft Mountain Campground

Shenandoah National Park, located in Virginia, offers over 200,000 acres of protected land, including scenic views, cascading waterfalls, and dense forests. Loft Mountain Campground is located along the Blue Ridge Parkway and offers easy access to many of the park’s best hiking trails.

Old Rag Mountain

Old Rag Mountain is one of the park’s most popular and challenging hikes, a 9-mile loop that takes you up steep rock scrambles, offering panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. The hike takes about 5 to 7 hours to complete, and it’s best suited for experienced hikers looking for a physically demanding and rewarding experience. The views from the summit are worth every ounce of effort.

Dark Hollow Falls Trail

For a shorter, easier hike, the Dark Hollow Falls Trail offers a 1.4-mile round trip to one of Shenandoah’s most beautiful waterfalls. The trail is steep, but the stunning waterfall at the end makes it all worth it. This trail is ideal for families or those looking for a quick but scenic hike.

7. Joshua Tree National Park - Jumbo Rocks Campground

Joshua Tree National Park, located in southern California, is a land of fascinating desert landscapes, iconic rock formations, and unique flora and fauna. Jumbo Rocks Campground, situated in the heart of the park, is a great spot to camp while exploring the nearby trails.

Hidden Valley Trail

The Hidden Valley Trail is one of Joshua Tree’s most popular hikes, a 1-mile loop that offers a glimpse into the park’s diverse ecosystems. The trail takes you through a sheltered valley, surrounded by massive boulders and desert vegetation. It’s a relatively easy hike, making it perfect for beginners or those looking for a short adventure.

Ryan Mountain Trail

For a more challenging hike, the Ryan Mountain Trail is a 3-mile round trip with an elevation gain of 1,000 feet. From the summit, you’ll be treated to panoramic views of the park’s iconic rock formations and the surrounding desert. The trail is a bit more strenuous but offers one of the best perspectives of Joshua Tree National Park.

Conclusion

The beauty of hiking near popular campsites is that you can enjoy a full outdoor experience — combining scenic hikes with the relaxation and camaraderie of camping. Whether you prefer a tranquil stroll through a forest, an adrenaline-pumping ascent, or a stroll beside a river, there is something for every type of adventurer. So, pack your gear, lace up your boots, and get ready to explore some of the best hiking trails near popular campsites that the world has to offer!
