Hear Their Voices: 20 Outstanding Books by Diverse Authors


Diverse voices hold the power to captivate, inspire, and transform our perspective on the world around us. In this blog post, we are diving deep into a selection of 20 exceptional books by diverse authors, from multiple genres, backgrounds, cultures, and lived experiences.

These groundbreaking works in literature, poetry, and other forms await to be explored by curious minds. They weave stories that reflect the tapestry of our diverse humanity – making our reading experience all the more gratifying.

Nonfiction

1. “The Autobiography of Malcolm X” - Malcolm X and Alex Haley

A richly detailed account of the life of a man transformed from a petty criminal into one of the most powerful voices for the Black Power movement. Weaving together a collection of interviews, personal reflections, and records, this is an empowering, transformative read.

2. “The Immortal Life of Henrietta Lacks” - Rebecca Skloot

Delving into the life of Henrietta Lacks, a woman whose cells have impacted countless medical breakthroughs, this book features touching human stories, hard-hitting science, and a critical look at bioethics.

3. “Between the World and Me” - Ta-Nehisi Coates

In this profound letter to his teenage son, Coates explores the experience of negotiating the existence of race in America, its inherent struggles, and the urgency to speak up.

Fiction

4. “Americanah” - Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

An unflinching exploration of race and identity in America, through the eyes of a young woman transitioning from Nigerian immigrant to American-presenting individual.

5. “The Fishermen” - Chigozie Obioma

This novel follows four brothers in 1990s Nigeria, as they experience the ethereal power of folklore and prophecy, leading to a gripping tale filled with hope, desire, and betrayal.

6. “Saudade: A Love Story” - Patricia Storace

Spanning decades of connection, separation, and longing, this is a beautifully woven story of love, anchored through the complexity of human emotions.

Mystery/Thriller

7. “Creepy Crawly Symphony” - Luridae Lindsay

When odd events occur, it’s up to Detective Cassie Grafton to unravel the mysteries. A thrilling journey waits, with uncertain truths and strange companions lurking in the shadows.

8. “A Song for Olorum” - Tirkeoluwa Arike Akomolafe

When supernatural forces clash with human darkness, a medium-slayer-crossdiff unveils a world of gods, fighting to discover celestial truths in the human world, with stakes higher than ever before.

Fantasy/Sci-Fi

9. “Dark Matter” - Blake Crouch

A mind-bending story of quantum physics, relativity, love, and loss, in which one man’s life takes an extraordinary turn with his wife’s disappearance.

10. “The Round House” - Louise Erdrich

This poignant coming-of-age story, set on a Native American reservation, explores themes of cultural identity, justice, and loss in the wake of a terrible crime.

Poetry

11. “The BreakBeat Poets” - Yosr El Haggar, Mahogany L. Browne, and José Olivarez (Editors)

A fresh, modern collection by poets of color, the BreakBeat Poets manifest new realities and narratives, marrying hip-hop, spoken word, and the power of diverse voices.

12. “Wild Mushrooms” - A. Van Jordan

An intricate exploration of the will and spirit of the human condition, Jordan blends vivid imagery with topical themes, creating a truly imaginative fusion.

Historical Fiction

13. “The Salt Road” - Holly Phillips

Melding reality and the supernatural with masterful skill, Phillips delivers a powerful novel about fate, family, and friendship in a world where witches’ salt can serve as currency.

14. “Pachinko” - Min Jin Lee

A beautifully crafted saga, this book showcases a Korean immigrant family navigating shifting loyalties, rivalries, and strong familial bonds in 20th-century Japan.

Romance

15. “The Prince Saverio’s Bride” - Stephanie Dray

A sweeping historical romance transporting us to the Italian Renaissance, the story reveals the heart-touching love story between a duke’s daughter and a lowly embroiderer.

16. “We refined People: Inside the White Harlem Renaissance” - Heyward Erika M. Brooks

This narrative highlights lesser-known figures, illuminating the Afro-Puerto Rican experience within the Harlem Renaissance, weaving a story of love, culture, and passion.

Children’s/YA

17. “(Big Enough) For A King” - C. B. Hendry

Embracing the complexities of identity and freedom, this enchanting tale for young readers follows the journey of a boy striving to save his village despite his small stature.

18. “The Green Basket” - Meeka Cooper

In this poignant book, a refugee girl discovers the power of empathy and kindness by weaving together two disparate communities through fate and unity.

Multigenre

19. “The House on Mango Street” - Sandra Cisneros

Through the artistically crafted vignettes, Cisneros explores the layers of identity, relationships, and belonging from the perspective of a young Hispanic girl.

20. “Beloved” - Toni Morrison

A haunting narrative illuminating the darkness of slavery’s stark impact on the lives of its survivors, an eloquent account of heart-wrenching love, loss, and tribulation.

Each of these books grapples with some of the most profound and persistent issues that human beings face. The themes of love, despair, the search for identity, belief in the supernatural, and the human desire for connection and understanding are interwoven in the collected works of these diverse authors.

As we embrace these striking literary works by diverse voices, we open not only gates to new worlds but recognition of the beauty that lies within our shared and varied human experiences. Reading can serve as a catalyst for change, empathy, and understanding. This list offers a deliciously appetizing taste of just what diverse books have to offer.

Together, let us invite these diverse voices in, giving them the unique attention, space, love, and respect they deserve. After all, a richer world indeed, is a well-read world.
