The Best Fantasy Novels Featuring Strong Female Leads


Fantasy novels have long been a staple of the literary world, offering readers a chance to escape into a world of magic, adventure, and wonder. But for far too long, these stories have been dominated by male protagonists, leaving female readers feeling left out and underrepresented. Thankfully, in recent years, there has been a surge in the number of fantasy novels featuring strong female leads, offering readers a chance to see themselves reflected in the stories they love.

In this post, we’ll be exploring some of the best fantasy novels featuring strong female leads, from classic tales to modern masterpieces.

1. The Mists of Avalon by Marion Zimmer Bradley

First published in 1983, The Mists of Avalon is a classic fantasy novel that reimagines the legend of King Arthur from the perspective of the women who lived alongside him. The novel follows the story of Morgaine, a powerful sorceress who is determined to destroy the sword Excalibur and bring an end to the patriarchal society that has ruled Camelot for so long. With its richly detailed world-building and complex characters, The Mists of Avalon is a must-read for anyone who loves fantasy and is looking for a strong female lead.

2. The Fifth Season by N.K. Jemisin

The Fifth Season is the first book in N.K. Jemisin’s Broken Earth trilogy, a series that has won widespread critical acclaim for its unique world-building and complex characters. The story follows Essun, a powerful orogene who is on the run from the authorities after her daughter is taken from her. As Essun travels across a world that is still reeling from a catastrophic event known as the Fifth Season, she must use her powers to survive and uncover the truth about her daughter’s disappearance. With its richly detailed world-building and complex characters, The Fifth Season is a must-read for anyone who loves fantasy and is looking for a strong female lead.

3. The Poppy War by R.F. Kuang

The Poppy War is a fantasy novel that is set in a world inspired by Chinese history and mythology. The story follows Rin, a young orphan who enrolls in a prestigious military academy and discovers that she has a unique talent for shamanism. As Rin becomes more powerful, she is drawn into a conflict between rival nations and must use her abilities to survive and uncover the truth about her past. With its unique world-building and complex characters, The Poppy War is a must-read for anyone who loves fantasy and is looking for a strong female lead.

4. The Golem and the Jinni by Helene Wecker

The Golem and the Jinni is a fantasy novel that is set in early 20th-century New York City. The story follows Chava, a golem who is brought to life by a Rabbi and is struggling to understand her place in the world. As Chava navigates the complexities of human society, she meets a jinni named Ahmad, who is also struggling to find his place in the world. With its unique world-building and complex characters, The Golem and the Jinni is a must-read for anyone who loves fantasy and is looking for a strong female lead.

5. The Song of Achilles by Madeline Miller

The Song of Achilles is a fantasy novel that is set in ancient Greece. The story follows Briseis, a Trojan princess who is captured by the Greeks during the Trojan War. As Briseis navigates the complexities of war and politics, she finds herself drawn to Achilles, the greatest warrior of the Greeks. With its unique world-building and complex characters, The Song of Achilles is a must-read for anyone who loves fantasy and is looking for a strong female lead.

6. The Lies of Locke Lamora by Scott Lynch

The Lies of Locke Lamora is a fantasy novel that is set in a fantasy version of Renaissance Italy. The story follows Camorr, a young con artist who is part of a group of thieves known as the Gentlemen Bastards. As Camorr navigates the complexities of the criminal underworld, he finds himself drawn into a conflict between rival gangs and must use his wits and cunning to survive. With its unique world-building and complex characters, The Lies of Locke Lamora is a must-read for anyone who loves fantasy and is looking for a strong female lead.

7. The First Fifteen Lives of Harry August by Claire North

The First Fifteen Lives of Harry August is a fantasy novel that is set in a world where people are reincarnated after they die. The story follows Harry August, a man who is reborn every time he dies and must use his unique abilities to uncover the truth about his past and prevent a catastrophic event from occurring. With its unique world-building and complex characters, The First Fifteen Lives of Harry August is a must-read for anyone who loves fantasy and is looking for a strong female lead.

8. The House of Shattered Wings by Aliette de Bodard

The House of Shattered Wings is a fantasy novel that is set in a post-apocalyptic Paris. The story follows Philippe, a young man who is struggling to survive in a world that is ruled by powerful houses and is haunted by fallen angels. As Philippe navigates the complexities of the city, he finds himself drawn into a conflict between rival houses and must use his wits and cunning to survive. With its unique world-building and complex characters, The House of Shattered Wings is a must-read for anyone who loves fantasy and is looking for a strong female lead.

9. The City of Brass by S.A. Chakraborty

The City of Brass is a fantasy novel that is set in 18th-century Cairo. The story follows Nahri, a young con artist who is part of a group of thieves known as the Daeva. As Nahri navigates the complexities of the city, she finds herself drawn into a conflict between rival factions and must use her wits and cunning to survive. With its unique world-building and complex characters, The City of Brass is a must-read for anyone who loves fantasy and is looking for a strong female lead.

10. The Black Tides of Heaven by J.Y. Yang

The Black Tides of Heaven is a fantasy novel that is set in a world where gods and mortals coexist. The story follows Ming, a young orphan who is part of a group of orphans who are being trained to become assassins. As Ming navigates the complexities of the world, he finds himself drawn into a conflict between rival factions and must use his wits and cunning to survive. With its unique world-building and complex characters, The Black Tides of Heaven is a must-read for anyone who loves fantasy and is looking for a strong female lead.

In conclusion, these fantasy novels featuring strong female leads offer readers a chance to see themselves reflected in the stories they love. With their unique world-building and complex characters, these novels are a must-read for anyone who loves fantasy and is looking for a strong female lead.
