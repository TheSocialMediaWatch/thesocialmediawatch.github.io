The Funniest Internet Memes of the Year: A Compilation


Each year, the internet gives us countless moments of laughter, and memes are undoubtedly the crowned jewels of viral humor. These digital snippets often capture the absurdities of everyday life, pop culture, or trending events, blending them with wit and relatability that connect millions across the globe. As the internet fires on all cylinders, creativity seems to hit new highs with memes. Let’s take a dive into this year’s funniest internet memes—a selection of the most hilarious, share-worthy snapshots of humor that had us all scrolling, reposting, and crying with laughter.

The Rise of Reaction Memes

Reaction memes have long been the backbone of internet humor, and this year they continued their dominance with several noteworthy entries. These memes typically revolve around a single image or short clip paired with captions that embody relatable emotions or experiences. Among some of 2023’s standout reaction memes, one of the most beloved was the “Shocked Pikachu Face” resurgence. Yes, the meme that embodies the art of feigning surprise made another big comeback, proving its timelessness. It re-emerged in tweets and TikToks, often paired with clever captions that dramatized moments of playful ignorance.

Another highlight was the “This You?” meme that thrived on sharp comebacks. Originating from users digging up old embarrassing tweets or photos, the meme evolved to become a staple for playful accountability. A simple screenshot captioned with “This You?” has been enough to cause hilarity on Twitter threads, poking fun at hypocrisy and backtracking in the most entertaining way possible.

Wholesome Humor: Memes That Warmed Hearts

While memes are often associated with relentless sarcasm or biting wit, this year delivered several rounds of wholesome humor that reminded us of the simpler joys of life. Enter “Monkey in a Puffer Jacket,” a meme centered around an undeniably chic monkey wearing a puffy winter coat. The image radiated cozy vibes, and captions turned the monkey into everyone—from your dad complaining about thermostats to fashionistas flaunting their newest winter wardrobes.

Additionally, the “Is It Cake?” meme garnered a lot of love from both baking enthusiasts and people taken over by the absurdity of an internet trend. Inspired by viral videos where cakes were shaped like everyday objects, the meme naturally spiraled into jokes about real-life surprises. Conversations about trust issues somehow reached hysterical new heights, thanks to memes proclaiming that anything—your coffee mug, sneakers, or even avocado toast—might very well be cake. The internet found humor in questioning reality, and we all laughed along.

Pop Culture Gold: Memes Inspired by TV, Movies, and Music

Pop culture has long been fertile ground for meme creation, and 2023 was no exception. This year, television series dominated the meme space, inspiring entire feeds filled with inside jokes, relatable moments, and satirical commentary. One of the biggest contributors to this meme genre was the highly anticipated second season of The White Lotus. With its dramatic twists and bubbling tension between characters, fans had plenty of fodder for meme-making. Who could forget the “Jennifer Coolidge” dialogue excerpts turned into templates for pretending to be rich and clueless? And her repeatedly iconic line, “These gays, they’re trying to murder me,” turned into a meme powerhouse that had many users hilariously overreacting to mundane situations.

Meanwhile, music also played a significant role in fueling meme culture this year. Taylor Swift’s Eras Tour brought about its own subcategory of Swiftie memes. Fans got creative in joking about ticket prices, fighting for seats, and even the emotional rollercoaster of reliving every musical ‘era’ live. TikTok’s algorithm practically served fans a steady stream of humor related to friendship bracelets, setlists, and… crying entirely too much during All Too Well (10 Minute Version).

TikTok’s Creative Explosion

If there’s one platform that shaped meme culture the most this year, it has to be TikTok. Short-form videos offer a creative landscape unlike any other, which means the memes originating here often reach peak absurdity and hilarity. For starters, the “Corn Kid” meme evolved far beyond TikTok and became universally adored. What began as a child enthusiastically talking about his love for corn turned into a musical remix, reaction memes, and even emotional expressions about life’s simple pleasures.

Another viral TikTok trend came in the form of the “NPC Challenge,” which hilariously mimicked the awkward, repetitive actions of video game non-player characters (NPCs). From exaggerated waving to clunky catchphrases, creators turned this challenge into a massive laugh-fest.

Not to be outdone, the platform also gave rise to the “Rizz” meme, a slang term that humorously refers to someone’s charm, flirting skills, or romance-game prowess. Whether it was real-life romantic scenarios or friends jokingly rating each other’s “rizz,” the internet proved that even matters of the heart could be pure comedic gold.

Memes Inspired by Daily Struggles

One of the reasons memes resonate so deeply is their ability to perfectly capture everyday frustrations and struggles, transforming them into hilarious depictions of shared experiences. This year, the “Broke College Student Starter Pack” meme made waves across social media, featuring exaggerated takes on ramen dinners, worn-out apartment furniture, and prayers that financial aid would come through before finals. It was painfully relatable—and yet effortlessly funny.

The “NPC in Real Life” meme also found fertile ground in these themes of mundane everyday life. Pairing clunky, robotic movements with relatable captions became an easy formula for clever jokes that captured how we all float through routine interactions without much thought.

The Unstoppable Power of Animal Memes

If there’s one category of humor that never seems to fade, it’s memes featuring animals. Cats, as usual, led the charge, with “Grumpy Cat’s Snarky Cousin” becoming 2023’s new famous feline. The cat, known for its perpetually unimpressed expression, starred in memes about bad dates, canceled plans, and awkward family reunions.

Not to be outdone, dogs brought their trademark charm to the year’s funniest memes as well. The “Overly Polite Dog” meme—a photo of a dog awkwardly keeping its paws balanced and waiting patiently—was used to comment on everything from good manners to the anxiety of politely declining party invites. Animal memes prove they’re a universal language that continues to unite us all.

Why Memes Matter

Memes are much more than funny pictures on a screen; they represent the pulse of our cultural and emotional climate. There’s a deep connection in finding memes that perfectly encapsulate how we feel or what we’re experiencing. They bring people together, giving us moments of joy, distraction, and camaraderie in a world that grows increasingly chaotic. This year’s funniest memes were a testament to the sheer creativity and humor of internet communities, turning moments of silliness into shared memories.

As the internet evolves, we can only imagine what future years will bring in terms of meme innovation and hilarity. For now, let’s take a moment to celebrate the laughter this year’s memes gave us—a collection of humor that reminded us not to take life too seriously. After all, scrolling endlessly through memes is one guilty pleasure we’re all happy to admit. The funniest memes of the year became our shared inside jokes, our moments of digital connection, and our comedic relief during stressful times.

So, what’s next? The internet continues to churn out unfiltered creativity daily, and no doubt, new memes are already brewing that will have us howling with laughter before the year is over. Until then, keep those meme folders organized and ready.
