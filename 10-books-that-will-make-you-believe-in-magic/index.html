10 Books That Will Make You Believe in Magic


As children, we all believed in the magic of fairy tales and mythical creatures. We would spend hours poring over picture books and chapter books, imagining ourselves as brave knights, clever wizards, and daring adventurers. But as we grew older, the magic began to fade, replaced by the harsh realities of the adult world. Or did it?

There are still many books out there that can transport us back to that magical world of childhood wonder, where anything is possible and the impossible becomes possible. Here are 10 books that will make you believe in magic once again.

1. “The Night Circus” by Erin Morgenstern

In this enchanting novel, two young magicians, Celia and Marco, engage in a high-stakes competition at a mysterious circus that appears at night. The circus is a marvel of magic and illusion, filled with wonders and surprises. As the competition unfolds, the boundaries between reality and fantasy begin to blur, and the reader is left wondering what is real and what is just an illusion.

2. “The Secret Garden” by Frances Hodgson Burnett

This classic novel tells the story of Mary Lennox, a spoiled and lonely girl who discovers a neglected garden and, through her care of it, learns about friendship, hard work, and the healing power of nature. The garden is a symbol of the magic that can be found in the everyday world, if only we take the time to look.

3. “The Phantom Tollbooth” by Norton Juster

This beloved children’s book follows the adventures of Milo, a young boy who discovers a magical tollbooth in his room that transports him to a fantastical world filled with puns, puzzles, and wordplay. Along the way, he learns about the importance of language, mathematics, and the power of imagination.

4. “The Golden Compass” by Philip Pullman

In this epic fantasy series, Lyra Belacqua and her daemon, Pantalaimon, embark on a journey through parallel universes, encountering witches, armored bears, and other magical creatures along the way. The series is a masterclass in world-building and storytelling, with a rich tapestry of characters and plot twists.

5. “The Magicians” by Lev Grossman

This novel follows Quentin Coldwater, a brilliant and awkward young man who discovers that magic is real and that he has been accepted to Brakebills College for Magical Pedagogy. The book is a witty and irreverent take on the traditional fantasy genre, with a cast of complex and nuanced characters.

6. “The Graveyard Book” by Neil Gaiman

In this Newbery Medal-winning novel, Bod, a young boy, is raised by ghosts in a graveyard after his family is murdered. The book is a beautifully written exploration of childhood, identity, and the power of storytelling.

7. “The Last Unicorn” by Peter S. Beagle

This classic fantasy novel tells the story of a beautiful unicorn named Amalthea, who is the last of her kind. The book is a poignant and powerful exploration of identity, morality, and the human condition.

8. “The House in the Cerulean Sea” by TJ Klune

This charming novel follows Linus Baker, a caseworker for the Department in Charge of Magical Youth, as he investigates a mysterious orphanage on a remote island. The book is a heartwarming and humorous exploration of family, identity, and the power of love.

9. “The First Fifteen Lives of Harry August” by Claire North

In this thought-provoking novel, Harry August is reborn every time he dies, remembering everything from his past lives. The book is a fascinating exploration of reincarnation, identity, and the consequences of human action.

10. “The Golem and the Jinni” by Helene Wecker

This beautifully written novel tells the story of Chava, a golem created from clay, and Ahmad, a jinni trapped in a human body, as they navigate the immigrant communities of early 20th-century New York City. The book is a rich and evocative exploration of identity, culture, and the power of storytelling.

These 10 books are just a few examples of the many magical worlds that await us on the pages of literature. So, grab a cup of tea, settle in, and let the magic begin!
