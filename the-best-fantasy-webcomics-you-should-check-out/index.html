The Best Fantasy Webcomics You Should Check Out


Webcomics have become a popular form of entertainment, combining art and storytelling in a unique way. If you’re a fan of fantasy, then you’re in luck! There are some incredible fantasy webcomics out there that deserve your attention. From epic adventures to whimsical tales, these webcomics will transport you to fantastical worlds and captivate your imagination. So without further ado, here are some of the best fantasy webcomics you should check out:

1. “The Wormworld Saga” by Daniel Lieske

“The Wormworld Saga” is a visually stunning webcomic that follows the journey of a young boy named Jonas who discovers a mysterious painting that transports him into a fantastical world. With breathtaking artwork and a compelling storyline, this webcomic will keep you hooked from start to finish.

2. “Lackadaisy” by Tracy J. Butler

Set in the Prohibition era, “Lackadaisy” is a fantasy webcomic that combines anthropomorphic animals with a thrilling crime story. Join the charismatic and enigmatic feline protagonist, Rocky Rickaby, as he navigates the dangerous world of speakeasies and bootlegging. With its unique art style and engaging characters, “Lackadaisy” is a must-read for any fantasy and crime noir enthusiast.

3. “Nimona” by Noelle Stevenson

“Nimona” is a delightful webcomic that tells the story of a shapeshifter named Nimona who becomes the sidekick to a villainous knight. With its blend of humor, action, and heartwarming moments, this webcomic is a joy to read. Noelle Stevenson’s artwork and storytelling are top-notch, making “Nimona” a true gem in the fantasy genre.

4. “Gunnerkrigg Court” by Tom Siddell

“Gunnerkrigg Court” is a webcomic that follows the adventures of Antimony Carver as she attends a mysterious boarding school filled with supernatural beings and ancient secrets. With its rich world-building and complex characters, this webcomic will keep you guessing at every turn. Tom Siddell’s intricate artwork and intricate plot make “Gunnerkrigg Court” a must-read for any fan of fantasy and mystery.

5. “The Phoenix Requiem” by Sarah Ellerton

“The Phoenix Requiem” is a beautifully illustrated fantasy webcomic set in a world where magic and science coexist. Follow the journey of Jonas Faulkner, a young man who becomes entangled in a web of mystery and romance as he searches for a cure for a deadly disease. Sarah Ellerton’s stunning artwork and compelling storytelling make “The Phoenix Requiem” a standout in the fantasy genre.

6. “Ava’s Demon” by Michelle Czajkowski

“Ava’s Demon” is a dark and captivating fantasy webcomic that follows the story of Ava, a young girl haunted by a demon named Wrathia. As Ava and Wrathia form an unlikely alliance, they embark on a quest for redemption and revenge. Michelle Czajkowski’s unique art style and intricate plot make “Ava’s Demon” a must-read for fans of dark fantasy.

7. “The Order of the Stick” by Rich Burlew

“The Order of the Stick” is a humorous and clever webcomic that parodies traditional fantasy tropes. Set in a world of dungeons, dragons, and adventurers, this webcomic follows a group of misfit heroes on their quest to save the world. Rich Burlew’s witty writing and clever storytelling make “The Order of the Stick” a must-read for any fantasy and comedy enthusiast.

These are just a few of the best fantasy webcomics out there, but there are many more waiting to be discovered. Whether you’re a fan of epic adventures, whimsical tales, or dark fantasy, there’s a webcomic out there that will captivate your imagination. So grab your favorite device, find a cozy spot, and dive into the enchanting world of fantasy webcomics. Happy reading!
