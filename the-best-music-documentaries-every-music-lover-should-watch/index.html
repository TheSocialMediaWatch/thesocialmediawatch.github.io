The Best Music Documentaries Every Music Lover Should Watch


Music is not just sound; it’s a narrative, a journey, and an emotional experience that has defined cultures, influenced generations, and shaped personal identities. Whether you’re a rock enthusiast, a classical music lover, or a fan of modern pop, the stories behind the music can be just as fascinating as the songs themselves. Music documentaries provide an immersive way to discover the lives, struggles, and triumphs of musicians and bands, often revealing untold stories and giving fans a deeper understanding of their favorite artists.

In this post, we dive into some of the best music documentaries every music lover should watch. These films not only showcase incredible performances but also offer insight into the struggles, inspirations, and creative processes behind iconic music legends. From gripping biographical accounts to behind-the-scenes glimpses of music history, these documentaries capture the essence of the artists and movements that have left an indelible mark on the world.

1. 20 Feet from Stardom (2013)

Director: Morgan Neville
Available on: Netflix, Amazon Prime, YouTube
Genre: Documentary, Music

“20 Feet from Stardom” focuses on the unsung heroes of the music world: backup singers. While lead singers often steal the limelight, these artists play a pivotal role in shaping the sound of countless songs. This documentary shines a light on legendary backup singers like Darlene Love, Merry Clayton, and Lisa Fischer, offering a deeper look at their experiences, challenges, and unrecognized contributions to music.

The film combines archival footage, intimate interviews, and powerful performances to capture the emotional essence of these singers’ lives, making it a must-watch for anyone interested in the unseen world of music.

2. Amy (2015)

Director: Asif Kapadia
Available on: Netflix, Amazon Prime, YouTube
Genre: Biographical, Music

“Amy” is an intimate and poignant documentary that chronicles the life and tragic death of British singer Amy Winehouse. Through home videos, interviews, and never-before-seen footage, the film provides an in-depth look at Amy’s rise to fame, her struggles with addiction, and her personal demons. The documentary doesn’t just focus on her musical genius but also on the human side of her story, revealing the pressures she faced as a young, talented woman in the public eye.

Amy’s powerful voice and her influence on jazz, soul, and contemporary music are undeniable, and this documentary presents a haunting portrait of her journey to fame and the cost of it. The rawness of the film makes it a must-watch for anyone who appreciates the complexities of being an artist in the spotlight.

3. The Last Waltz (1978)

Director: Martin Scorsese
Available on: Amazon Prime, YouTube
Genre: Concert, Music

When The Band, one of the most influential rock groups of the 60s and 70s, decided to retire, they went out with a spectacular final concert—captured in Martin Scorsese’s “The Last Waltz.” This documentary is a breathtaking look at the final performance of The Band, featuring iconic guest appearances by Bob Dylan, Eric Clapton, Neil Young, and many more.

Beyond the performances, “The Last Waltz” provides an intimate look at the dynamics within The Band, the creative process behind their music, and the highs and lows of their career. With its stunning cinematography and unforgettable performances, it’s not just a concert film but a masterpiece that captures a pivotal moment in rock history.

4. What Happened, Miss Simone? (2015)

Director: Liz Garbus
Available on: Netflix, Amazon Prime, YouTube
Genre: Biographical, Music

Nina Simone was a complex and revolutionary artist, blending jazz, blues, soul, and classical music while addressing themes of race, identity, and civil rights. “What Happened, Miss Simone?” chronicles the life of this legendary artist, using archival footage, interviews, and personal letters to tell the story of her rise to fame, her activism, and her personal struggles.

The film paints a portrait of a woman who was both a musical genius and a deeply conflicted individual, struggling with mental health issues and her place in a world that was often hostile to her. This documentary is a beautiful tribute to Simone’s legacy and is essential viewing for anyone who wants to understand the intersection of music and social change.

5. Stop Making Sense (1984)

Director: Jonathan Demme
Available on: Amazon Prime, YouTube
Genre: Concert, Music

“Stop Making Sense” is the quintessential Talking Heads concert film. Directed by Jonathan Demme, this documentary captures the band’s electrifying performance at Hollywood’s Pantages Theater in 1983. What makes this documentary stand out is its energy and innovative presentation. From the minimalist set design to the unique choreography, the film is a visual and auditory delight, showcasing the band’s eclectic style and David Byrne’s charismatic stage presence.

The documentary is often considered one of the best concert films ever made. Whether you’re a longtime Talking Heads fan or new to their music, “Stop Making Sense” offers a rare glimpse into one of the most influential new wave bands of the 80s.

6. This Is Spinal Tap (1984)

Director: Rob Reiner
Available on: Amazon Prime, YouTube
Genre: Comedy, Music, Mockumentary

While technically a comedy, “This Is Spinal Tap” is a brilliant mockumentary that skewers the absurdities of rock stardom. Directed by Rob Reiner, the film follows the fictional British heavy metal band Spinal Tap as they navigate their troubled American tour. The film satirizes the egos, conflicts, and bizarre moments of rock band life with hilarious accuracy.

Despite being a parody, “This Is Spinal Tap” resonates with anyone who’s familiar with the music industry and its eccentricities. It’s an essential watch for music lovers who enjoy both comedy and a critical look at the absurdities of fame.

7. The Decline of Western Civilization (1981)

Director: Penelope Spheeris
Available on: Amazon Prime, YouTube
Genre: Documentary, Music

Penelope Spheeris’ “The Decline of Western Civilization” is an unflinching look at the Los Angeles punk rock scene in the late 70s and early 80s. The documentary focuses on iconic punk bands like Black Flag, X, and the Germs, capturing the raw energy and rebellious spirit of the scene. It also delves into the culture of punk, addressing the socio-political climate and the music’s relationship with anti-establishment sentiments.

The documentary is gritty, intense, and offers an authentic snapshot of the punk movement, making it a must-watch for anyone interested in the genre and the history of underground music scenes.

8. Muscle Shoals (2013)

Director: Greg “Freddy” Camalier
Available on: Netflix, Amazon Prime, YouTube
Genre: Documentary, Music

“Muscle Shoals” explores the small town in Alabama that became the birthplace of some of the greatest music ever made. The documentary focuses on the FAME Recording Studios and its legendary producer Rick Hall, who helped create timeless hits for artists like Aretha Franklin, The Rolling Stones, and Etta James.

The film showcases the blend of soul, gospel, and rock that defined Muscle Shoals and its impact on the music industry. Through interviews with artists, producers, and musicians, “Muscle Shoals” reveals the magic behind the sound that still resonates today, offering a fascinating look at how place and people shaped music history.

9. Alive Inside (2014)

Director: Michael Rossato-Bennett
Available on: Netflix, Amazon Prime, YouTube
Genre: Documentary, Music

“Alive Inside” is a touching and heartwarming documentary that follows the work of social worker Dan Cohen, who introduces music to elderly patients with dementia and Alzheimer’s. The film captures the profound impact music has on the mind, as patients who are often unresponsive light up when hearing their favorite songs.

The documentary is not only a testament to the healing power of music but also a poignant reminder of the emotional connections that songs can evoke, regardless of age or memory loss. It’s an uplifting and deeply moving exploration of how music can restore joy and humanity in the most challenging circumstances.

10. The Beatles: Eight Days a Week – The Touring Years (2016)

Director: Ron Howard
Available on: Hulu, Amazon Prime, YouTube
Genre: Documentary, Music

Directed by Ron Howard, “The Beatles: Eight Days a Week” focuses on the Beatles’ touring years, providing an in-depth look at their rise to global fame, the pressure of constant performances, and the challenges they faced during their time on the road. Featuring interviews, rare footage, and concert performances, the documentary paints a vivid picture of what it was like to be part of the most famous band in history.

This film is a must-watch for any Beatles fan, offering a deeper understanding of the band’s relationship with their fans, the world, and each other. It also provides insight into how their music evolved during their touring years and how the band’s impact reshaped the landscape of modern music.

11. Rattle and Hum (1988)

Director: Phil Joanou
Available on: Amazon Prime, YouTube
Genre: Documentary, Music

“Rattle and Hum” follows U2 on their 1987 Joshua Tree Tour, blending live performances with behind-the-scenes footage. The documentary offers a deep dive into U2’s creative process, their musical influences, and the cultural and political themes that shaped their music. It also features some of the band’s most iconic performances, including “I Still Haven’t Found What I’m Looking For” and “With or Without You.”

For U2 fans, “Rattle and Hum” offers a rare look into the band’s evolution, from their early days in Ireland to becoming one of the biggest rock bands in the world. It’s a thrilling, immersive experience for anyone who appreciates the power of music to communicate complex emotions and ideas.

Conclusion

Music documentaries have the power to transform the way we perceive music, offering a deeper connection to the artists, their stories, and the cultural significance of their work. Whether you’re looking to learn more about the legends of the past or understand the complexities of the music industry, these documentaries are the perfect way to expand your musical knowledge and experience the emotional journey of the artists you love.

The documentaries listed above provide a wide range of perspectives on different music genres, from rock and jazz to soul and punk. They offer a unique look into the lives of musicians and the creative forces that have shaped the world of music as we know it. So grab some popcorn, turn down the lights, and get ready to dive into the captivating world of music documentaries.
