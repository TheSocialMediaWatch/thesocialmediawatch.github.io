Best Fishing Knots Every Angler Should Know

4 min · 700 words

When it comes to fishing, one of the most crucial skills you need to master is tying knots. A properly tied knot can be the difference between landing that trophy fish or watching it swim away with your bait. In this blog post, we will discuss some of the best fishing knots every angler should know. Whether you’re a beginner or an experienced angler, these knots will come in handy in various fishing situations.

The Improved Clinch Knot

The Improved Clinch Knot is one of the most popular fishing knots and is perfect for attaching a hook, lure, or swivel to your fishing line. It is a simple knot that is easy to tie and provides excellent strength. To tie the Improved Clinch Knot, follow these steps:

  1. Pass the end of the fishing line through the eye of the hook or the lure.
  2. Make five to seven wraps around the standing line.
  3. Pass the end of the line through the small loop formed above the eye of the hook or the lure.
  4. Wet the knot with water or saliva to lubricate it.
  5. Pull the standing line and the tag end simultaneously to tighten the knot.
  6. Trim the tag end close to the knot.

The Palomar Knot

The Palomar Knot is another versatile fishing knot that is known for its strength and reliability. It is ideal for attaching hooks, lures, and swivels to your fishing line, especially when using braided lines. To tie the Palomar Knot, follow these steps:

  1. Double about 6 inches of the fishing line and pass it through the eye of the hook or the lure.
  2. Tie an overhand knot with the doubled line, leaving a loop large enough to pass the hook or the lure through.
  3. Pass the hook or the lure through the loop.
  4. Wet the knot with water or saliva to lubricate it.
  5. Hold the standing line and the tag end, and pull them in opposite directions to tighten the knot.
  6. Trim the tag end close to the knot.

The Blood Knot

The Blood Knot is a popular choice for joining two fishing lines of similar or different diameters. It creates a strong and smooth connection, making it ideal for leaders, tippets, and fly fishing. To tie the Blood Knot, follow these steps:

  1. Overlap the ends of the two fishing lines for about 6 inches.
  2. Take one end and make five to seven wraps around the other line.
  3. Pass the end through the loop created between the two lines.
  4. Repeat steps 2 and 3 with the other end.
  5. Wet the knot with water or saliva to lubricate it.
  6. Pull the standing lines in opposite directions to tighten the knot.
  7. Trim the tag ends close to the knot.

The Uni Knot

The Uni Knot, also known as the Duncan Loop, is a versatile knot that can be used for various applications, including attaching hooks, lures, and swivels to your fishing line. It is easy to tie and provides good strength. To tie the Uni Knot, follow these steps:

  1. Pass the end of the fishing line through the eye of the hook or the lure.
  2. Double about 6 inches of the line and make a loop.
  3. Wrap the end of the line around the double line and the loop for about six turns.
  4. Pass the end of the line through the loop.
  5. Wet the knot with water or saliva to lubricate it.
  6. Hold the tag end and the standing line, and pull them in opposite directions to tighten the knot.
  7. Trim the tag end close to the knot.

The Surgeon’s Knot

The Surgeon’s Knot is a simple and reliable knot that is commonly used to join two fishing lines of similar or different diameters. It is quick to tie and provides good strength. To tie the Surgeon’s Knot, follow these steps:

  1. Overlap the ends of the two fishing lines for about 6 inches.
  2. Tie an overhand knot with both lines, creating a loop.
  3. Pass the ends of the lines through the loop two more times.
  4. Wet the knot with water or saliva to lubricate it.
  5. Pull the standing lines in opposite directions to tighten the knot.
  6. Trim the tag ends close to the knot.

Conclusion

Knowing how to tie the right fishing knots can significantly improve your fishing success. The Improved Clinch Knot, Palomar Knot, Blood Knot, Uni Knot, and Surgeon’s Knot are some of the best fishing knots every angler should know. Practice tying these knots until you can do it with your eyes closed. Remember, a well-tied knot is the foundation of a successful fishing trip. So, make sure to master these knots and enjoy a day on the water with confidence!
