The Best Personal Finance Blogs to Follow


Personal finance is an essential aspect of life that can often feel overwhelming, especially when you’re trying to manage debt, plan for retirement, save for your children’s education, or simply improve your financial literacy. Fortunately, the internet is filled with valuable resources to help guide you on this journey, and personal finance blogs are some of the best places to start.

Whether you’re just getting started with budgeting or you’re looking for advanced strategies to build wealth, these blogs offer actionable advice, expert insights, and inspiration to help you achieve your financial goals. In this post, we’ll explore some of the top personal finance blogs that can help you navigate the complex world of money management.

1. The Penny Hoarder

One of the most popular personal finance blogs out there, The Penny Hoarder is dedicated to helping readers manage their money, save, and earn more. It covers everything from budgeting and debt reduction to side hustles and smart shopping tips. What makes this blog particularly great is its accessibility—it’s perfect for beginners, offering practical advice that doesn’t overwhelm.

The blog regularly features guest posts and stories of real people who have turned their finances around, making it not just informative but also inspirational. It’s a great resource for anyone looking to take control of their finances and improve their financial situation step-by-step.

Key Topics:

  • Budgeting and Saving Money
  • Making Extra Money with Side Hustles
  • Debt Reduction Strategies
  • Personal Finance Success Stories

2. The Financial Panther

The Financial Panther is a personal finance blog that focuses on financial independence, early retirement (FIRE), and side hustles. The blog’s author, a lawyer by trade, shares his journey towards financial freedom, along with tips on how to save aggressively, invest wisely, and create multiple income streams. One of the unique aspects of The Financial Panther is its emphasis on real-world advice, combining the author’s own experiences with practical strategies for others to follow.

If you’re interested in exploring the FIRE movement or looking for side hustle ideas to increase your income, this blog is definitely worth checking out. The posts are detailed and often include data-backed strategies, which makes it appealing to readers who want to dive deep into personal finance topics.

Key Topics:

  • Financial Independence and Early Retirement (FIRE)
  • Side Hustles and Gig Economy Tips
  • Investment Strategies for Beginners and Advanced Investors
  • Debt Payoff Plans

3. The Budget Mom

The Budget Mom is a fantastic resource for anyone struggling to manage their monthly expenses or trying to find a way to start budgeting. The blog, created by Kumiko Love, offers detailed budgeting tools, personal stories about managing money, and a wide range of practical financial tips. Kumiko’s approach to budgeting is holistic, focusing not only on finances but also on creating a healthy relationship with money.

A unique feature of The Budget Mom is the focus on cash envelope budgeting, which has gained popularity among people who prefer using physical money as a way to control their spending. If you’re someone who wants to take a hands-on approach to budgeting, this blog offers a wealth of resources to help you get started.

Key Topics:

  • Cash Envelope Budgeting
  • Saving Money on Groceries and Other Essentials
  • Creating a Debt Repayment Plan
  • Managing Finances for Families

4. Mr. Money Mustache

If you’re someone who dreams of retiring early or living a life of financial independence, Mr. Money Mustache should be on your radar. Founded by Pete Adeney, this blog offers unconventional wisdom on how to cut expenses, increase savings, and build a life that doesn’t revolve around consumerism. Mr. Money Mustache advocates for a minimalist lifestyle, believing that reducing unnecessary spending can fast-track your journey to financial freedom.

The blog includes a wide range of content, from in-depth articles on how to lower your living expenses to specific advice on frugal living. Whether you’re just starting your journey to financial independence or you’re looking for advanced strategies, this blog provides clear, actionable steps to help you achieve your financial goals.

Key Topics:

  • Financial Independence and Early Retirement (FIRE)
  • Frugal Living Tips
  • Cutting Everyday Expenses
  • Living a Simple, Intentional Life

5. Smart Passive Income

Pat Flynn’s Smart Passive Income is a blog dedicated to helping people create passive income streams through various online ventures. If you’re looking to generate income without having to constantly trade time for money, this blog offers excellent advice on how to start and grow online businesses.

Pat Flynn shares his own experiences of building passive income streams, including detailed case studies, tips on creating online courses, and ways to build a personal brand. His approach is grounded in authenticity, and his content is focused on helping others achieve financial freedom by leveraging their skills and knowledge online.

Key Topics:

  • Passive Income Strategies
  • Building an Online Business
  • Online Marketing Tips
  • Personal Branding and Entrepreneurship

6. Frugalwoods

Frugalwoods is a blog that focuses on frugality, simplicity, and the pursuit of financial independence. The blog’s author, Liz Thames, shares her family’s journey from suburban life to a homestead in rural Vermont, demonstrating how a frugal lifestyle can lead to greater financial freedom.

The blog is a great resource for anyone who wants to live a simpler, more intentional life. Liz’s posts cover everything from saving money on groceries to building wealth through investments, and her practical advice is aimed at readers who want to take control of their finances and reduce their expenses.

Key Topics:

  • Frugality and Simple Living
  • Financial Independence
  • Saving Money on Everyday Expenses
  • Creating Wealth Through Smart Investments

7. Money Under 30

As the name suggests, Money Under 30 is a personal finance blog geared toward helping young adults navigate their finances. It offers straightforward advice on budgeting, paying off student loans, saving for retirement, and building credit—all tailored to the needs of individuals in their 20s and early 30s.

The blog is designed to break down complex financial concepts into bite-sized, digestible pieces. Whether you’re trying to get out of debt, buy your first home, or save for the future, Money Under 30 provides a roadmap to financial success with practical tips and easy-to-understand advice.

Key Topics:

  • Budgeting and Managing Expenses
  • Student Loan Repayment Strategies
  • Saving for Retirement (Starting Early)
  • Building Credit and Buying Your First Home

8. Broke Millennial

Broke Millennial, created by Erin Lowry, is a blog dedicated to helping millennials take control of their finances. Erin’s approach is down-to-earth, using humor and relatability to make difficult financial topics more accessible. She covers everything from budgeting and saving to investing and negotiating salaries.

In addition to practical advice, Erin shares personal stories from her own financial journey, which makes the content even more relatable to her audience. If you’re a millennial looking to take control of your financial future, this blog is a great resource.

Key Topics:

  • Financial Independence and Saving Strategies
  • Money Management for Millennials
  • Paying Off Debt (Student Loans, Credit Cards, etc.)
  • Investing and Wealth Building

9. Get Rich Slowly

Get Rich Slowly is a blog that focuses on long-term financial success. Founded by J.D. Roth, this blog offers a variety of content designed to help individuals take small, consistent steps toward achieving financial independence. J.D. emphasizes the importance of building healthy financial habits and making thoughtful decisions when it comes to spending, saving, and investing.

The blog covers a range of topics, including budgeting, debt management, and retirement planning. It also includes in-depth personal finance case studies, which provide valuable insights into the financial journeys of others.

Key Topics:

  • Building Healthy Financial Habits
  • Debt Reduction Plans
  • Retirement and Investment Strategies
  • Personal Finance Case Studies

10. Women Who Money

Women Who Money is a blog dedicated to helping women take control of their finances and achieve financial independence. It covers a wide range of personal finance topics with a particular focus on issues that affect women, such as the gender pay gap, family finances, and balancing work and life goals.

The blog offers a variety of content, including personal finance tips, interviews with financial experts, and resources specifically geared toward helping women gain confidence in their financial decision-making. It’s a must-follow for any woman looking to improve her financial knowledge and achieve her financial goals.

Key Topics:

  • Financial Independence for Women
  • Overcoming the Gender Pay Gap
  • Family and Personal Finance Management
  • Investing and Retirement Planning for Women

Conclusion

Personal finance is a journey, and having the right resources to guide you can make all the difference. Whether you’re trying to save more, pay off debt, or plan for retirement, these personal finance blogs offer valuable advice and actionable tips that can help you achieve your financial goals. From budgeting to investing, financial independence to side hustles, these blogs cover it all, making them excellent resources for anyone looking to take control of their money and build a secure financial future.

By following these blogs, you’ll gain access to expert insights, relatable stories, and strategies that can help you make smarter financial decisions every day. No matter where you are on your financial journey, there’s a blog out there for you that can offer the advice and inspiration you need to succeed.
