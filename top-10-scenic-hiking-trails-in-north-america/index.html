Top 10 Scenic Hiking Trails in North America


North America is a treasure trove for hiking enthusiasts, offering some of the most breathtaking and diverse landscapes found anywhere on the planet. From rugged mountain ranges and ancient forests to vast deserts and dramatic coastlines, the continent is a paradise for those looking to connect with nature on foot. Whether you’re a seasoned hiker yearning for challenging terrain or a casual walker seeking serene vistas, these top 10 scenic hiking trails in North America offer unforgettable experiences that captivate the soul.

1. The Appalachian Trail (Eastern United States)

Spanning over 2,190 miles from Georgia to Maine, the Appalachian Trail is one of the most iconic long-distance hiking trails in the world. It traverses 14 states, winding through lush forests, rolling hills, and picturesque ridges. The trail offers a variety of sights—from the vibrant fall foliage of the Smoky Mountains to the rugged peaks of the White Mountains. Hikers can choose to do sections or strive for thru-hiking the entire trail, immersing themselves in a diverse range of ecosystems and charming mountain towns along the way.

Highlights:

  • Great Smoky Mountains National Park: Thick forests and lush wildlife.
  • Shenandoah National Park: Skyline Drive vistas and rhododendron blooms.
  • Mount Katahdin: The northern terminus with challenging climbs and stunning summit views.

2. Pacific Crest Trail (Western United States)

Stretching over 2,650 miles from Mexico to Canada through California, Oregon, and Washington, the Pacific Crest Trail (PCT) showcases an extraordinary variety of landscapes. From arid deserts and volcanic peaks to alpine lakes and ancient forests, the PCT is nature’s grand showcase. The trail’s remote sections offer solitude and vast, untouched beauty, perfect for those seeking wild adventure.

Highlights:

  • Sierra Nevada Mountains: Iconic spots like Yosemite’s high country and the John Muir Trail.
  • Mount Rainier and North Cascades National Parks: Snow-capped peaks and glacier fields.
  • Mojave Desert: Stark landscapes with colorful sunsets and star-studded skies.

3. Banff National Park – Plain of Six Glaciers Trail (Alberta, Canada)

Set in the heart of the Canadian Rockies, Banff National Park is renowned for its crystal-clear turquoise lakes and soaring peaks. The Plain of Six Glaciers Trail is a moderately strenuous hike that rewards adventurers with panoramic views of glaciers, rugged mountains, and the vibrant waters of Lake Louise. Along the way, hikers pass the historic Tea House, a perfect spot to rest and enjoy world-class alpine scenery.

Highlights:

  • Spectacular views of Victoria Glacier and Mount Lefroy.
  • The opportunity to spot wildlife such as marmots and mountain goats.
  • Photo-worthy vistas of emerald glacial lakes.

4. Zion National Park – Angel’s Landing (Utah, United States)

Angel’s Landing is one of the most exhilarating hikes in North America, offering stunning panoramic views combined with a heartbeat-raising ridge climb. This 5-mile round trip trail in Zion National Park is renowned for its steep, narrow paths and chains to assist hikers along the final ascent. The dramatic sandstone cliffs and the winding Virgin River below make the effort worthwhile for thrill seekers.

Highlights:

  • Sweeping views of Zion Canyon and the Virgin River.
  • Unique sandstone rock formations in red and orange hues.
  • An adrenaline-pumping final climb with chains and sheer drops.

5. Glacier National Park – Highline Trail (Montana, United States)

Located along the continental divide, the Highline Trail offers one of the most scenic hikes in Glacier National Park. This trail takes you along the edge of the Garden Wall, revealing dramatic drops to valleys below and sweeping views of rugged peaks. Wildlife sightings of mountain goats, marmots, and birds are common, adding to the experience.

Highlights:

  • Stunning elevation changes with sweeping views of glaciers and mountain peaks.
  • The chance to see diverse alpine flora and fauna.
  • Connection with other trails within Glacier, creating options for extended adventures.

6. Gros Morne National Park – Western Brook Pond Trail (Newfoundland, Canada)

Gros Morne National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, features dramatic fjords, towering cliffs, and pristine wilderness. The Western Brook Pond Trail leads hikers on a trek to a stunning freshwater fjord surrounded by sheer cliffs upwards of 2,000 feet. The trail is relatively short and accessible but offers incredible views and a boat tour opportunity at the pond’s end.

Highlights:

  • Impressive fjord landscape with tower-like cliffs and pristine waters.
  • Boat tours that offer a different perspective of the towering cliffs.
  • Chance to spot eagles, moose, and other wildlife.

7. Grand Canyon – Rim-to-Rim Trail (Arizona, United States)

For an iconic challenge, hiking the Grand Canyon Rim-to-Rim trail is a dream come true for many trekkers. Traversing from the North Rim to the South Rim or vice versa, this approximately 24-mile journey takes you deep into the geological wonder of the canyon, showcasing vast, colorful rock layers that tell the story of millions of years.

Highlights:

  • Dramatic views of the canyon’s vast scale and geological formations.
  • Diverse ecosystems, from forested rim areas to desert-like inner canyon.
  • The powerful Colorado River at the base, visible in parts of the hike.

8. The West Coast Trail (Vancouver Island, British Columbia, Canada)

Widely regarded as one of the toughest but most rewarding trails in North America, the West Coast Trail winds 47 miles along rugged coastline, dense rainforests, and ancient Indigenous territories. The trail challenges hikers with ladders, log crossings, and slippery roots but offers stunning views of crashing Pacific waves, tidal pools, and marine wildlife.

Highlights:

  • Untouched coastal rainforest and sandy beaches.
  • Opportunity to spot whales, sea lions, and seabirds.
  • Rich cultural history tied to Indigenous peoples in the region.

9. The Lost Coast Trail (California, United States)

The Lost Coast Trail in Northern California leads hikers through some of the state’s most remote and wild coastal landscapes. Stretching roughly 25 miles, the trail follows a rugged shoreline with dramatic cliffs, tide pools, and secluded beaches. It’s ideal for those seeking solitude paired with raw natural beauty.

Highlights:

  • Expansive Pacific Ocean views from towering cliffs.
  • Low crowds and a feeling of true wilderness.
  • A perfect blend of beach hiking and rugged terrain.

10. The Skyline Trail – Jasper National Park (Alberta, Canada)

The Skyline Trail offers a spectacular alpine hiking experience with panoramic mountain views stretching as far as the eye can see. Located in Jasper National Park, the trail is known for its wildflower-filled meadows during summer and alpine lakes reflecting the surrounding peaks.

Highlights:

  • High elevation viewpoints with dramatic mountain panoramas.
  • Opportunities to spot wildlife like elk, bears, and eagles.
  • Diverse ecosystems including forest valleys and alpine tundra.

Conclusion

These top 10 scenic hiking trails in North America offer some of the continent’s most stunning natural wonders. Each trail has its unique character, from the verdant mountains of the East to the rugged coastal wilderness of the West. Whether draining your spirits in the vast emptiness of the desert or soaking in the alpine beauty of glaciers and peaks, these trails promise unforgettable journeys and deep connections with nature.

Before hitting any trail, it’s smart to prepare with proper gear, research trail conditions, and respect local guidelines for preserving these beautiful landscapes. Happy hiking—adventure awaits!
