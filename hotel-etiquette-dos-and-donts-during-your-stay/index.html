Hotel Etiquette: Do's and Don'ts During Your Stay


When it comes to staying at a hotel, there are certain etiquette guidelines that can help ensure a pleasant and enjoyable experience for both you and your fellow guests. From check-in to check-out, here are some do’s and don’ts to keep in mind during your stay.

Do: Respect Quiet Hours

One of the most important things to remember when staying at a hotel is to be considerate of others. Many hotels have designated quiet hours, usually during the evening and early morning. Respect these hours by keeping noise levels down in your room and common areas. Avoid loud conversations, slamming doors, or playing music at high volumes.

Don’t: Be Late for Check-Out

Check-out time is typically in the morning, and it’s important to adhere to this time to allow the hotel staff to prepare the room for the next guest. Being late for check-out can inconvenience both the staff and the incoming guest. Set an alarm and make sure you’re ready to leave the room by the designated time.

Do: Keep Your Room Clean

Maintaining cleanliness in your hotel room is not only respectful to the housekeeping staff but also contributes to a more pleasant stay for yourself. Dispose of trash properly, keep your belongings organized, and report any maintenance issues promptly. Remember to leave the room in the same condition you found it.

Don’t: Abuse Hotel Amenities

While it’s tempting to take advantage of the amenities offered by the hotel, it’s important to use them responsibly. Be mindful of other guests who may also want to enjoy the facilities. Don’t hog the poolside chairs or monopolize the gym equipment for an excessive amount of time. Sharing is caring!

Do: Follow the Dress Code

Some hotels may have specific dress codes for certain areas such as restaurants or lounges. It’s always a good idea to check if there are any dress requirements before entering these spaces. Adhering to the dress code shows respect for the establishment and the other guests.

Don’t: Smoke in Non-Smoking Areas

As smoking policies have become more stringent, most hotels now have designated non-smoking areas. It’s essential to respect these policies and only smoke in designated smoking areas, if available. Smoking in non-smoking areas can not only result in fines but also cause discomfort for other guests.

Do: Tip the Staff

If you receive exceptional service from the hotel staff, it’s customary to leave a tip as a token of appreciation. Tipping the housekeeping staff, concierge, or bellmen is a kind gesture and shows gratitude for their hard work. Remember, tips are not mandatory, but they are always appreciated.

Don’t: Steal or Damage Hotel Property

This should go without saying, but it’s essential to mention: stealing or damaging hotel property is unacceptable. Respect the hotel’s belongings as you would your own. If you accidentally break something, report it to the front desk and offer to cover the cost of the damage.

Do: Be Kind and Courteous

Above all, practicing kindness and courtesy towards the hotel staff and fellow guests will create a positive atmosphere during your stay. A smile, a polite greeting, and a “thank you” can go a long way in making everyone’s experience more enjoyable.

Remember, following these do’s and don’ts of hotel etiquette will not only make your stay more pleasant but also contribute to a harmonious environment for all guests. Happy travels!

Note: This blog post does not endorse any specific hotel or brand. The information provided is general and applicable to most hotels.
