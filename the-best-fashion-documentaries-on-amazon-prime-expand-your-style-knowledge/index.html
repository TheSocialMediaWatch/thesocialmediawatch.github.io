The Best Fashion Documentaries on Amazon Prime: Expand Your Style Knowledge


Fashion is not just about clothes; it’s an art form that reflects our culture, history, and personal expression. If you’re passionate about fashion or simply want to learn more about this fascinating industry, then you’re in luck. Amazon Prime offers a treasure trove of fashion documentaries that will expand your style knowledge and provide valuable insights into the world of fashion. From the iconic designers to the rise of streetwear, these documentaries offer a front-row seat to the captivating world of fashion. So grab your popcorn, sit back, and get ready to be inspired.

1. The September Issue (2009)

Step inside the world of high fashion with The September Issue. Directed by R.J. Cutler, this documentary provides an in-depth look at the making of Vogue’s iconic September 2007 issue, the largest and most important issue of the year. Follow legendary editor-in-chief Anna Wintour and her team as they navigate the fast-paced and glamorous world of fashion, offering a glimpse into the creative process behind one of the most influential fashion magazines in the world.

2. Dior and I (2014)

For a behind-the-scenes look at one of the most prestigious fashion houses, Dior and I is a must-watch. Directed by Frédéric Tcheng, this documentary takes you into the atelier of Christian Dior as newly appointed creative director Raf Simons prepares his first haute couture collection. Witness the intense pressure, meticulous craftsmanship, and artistic vision required to create the iconic designs that define Dior.

3. The First Monday in May (2016)

Ever wondered what goes into curating a blockbuster fashion exhibition? The First Monday in May explores the collaboration between Vogue’s Anna Wintour and the Metropolitan Museum of Art as they organize the 2015 Met Gala and the accompanying exhibition, “China: Through the Looking Glass.” Directed by Andrew Rossi, this documentary offers a fascinating glimpse into the intersection of fashion, art, and culture, showcasing the meticulous planning and artistic vision required to create a truly memorable fashion event.

4. McQueen (2018)

Discover the extraordinary life and career of fashion’s enfant terrible, Alexander McQueen, in McQueen. Directed by Ian Bonhôte and Peter Ettedgui, this documentary delves into the mind of the brilliant designer, exploring his creative process, groundbreaking runway shows, and personal struggles. Through interviews with friends, family, and industry insiders, McQueen provides an intimate portrait of a true fashion icon.

5. The True Cost (2015)

While fashion can be glamorous, it also has a dark side. The True Cost sheds light on the environmental and ethical impact of fast fashion. Directed by Andrew Morgan, this eye-opening documentary takes you on a journey from the runways of Paris to the sweatshops of Bangladesh, revealing the true cost of our insatiable appetite for cheap clothing. Prepare to be moved and inspired to make more conscious fashion choices.

6. Bill Cunningham New York (2010)

If you’re a fan of street style and want to know more about the man behind the lens, then Bill Cunningham New York is a must-watch. Directed by Richard Press, this documentary celebrates the life and work of legendary New York Times fashion photographer Bill Cunningham. Follow Cunningham as he captures the vibrant and ever-evolving street fashion of New York City, offering a unique perspective on style and individuality.

7. Valentino: The Last Emperor (2008)

Step into the world of Italian fashion icon Valentino Garavani in Valentino: The Last Emperor. Directed by Matt Tyrnauer, this documentary provides an intimate look at the final two years of Valentino’s career before his retirement. From the atelier to the red carpet, Valentino: The Last Emperor showcases the dedication, craftsmanship, and timeless elegance that defined Valentino’s legendary career.

8. Iris (2014)

Age is just a number, and no one embodies this better than Iris Apfel. Directed by Albert Maysles, Iris follows the life and style of the irreverent and eccentric fashion icon. With her signature oversized glasses and bold fashion choices, Apfel proves that style has no boundaries and that individuality is the ultimate fashion statement.

Conclusion

Fashion documentaries offer a unique and immersive way to learn about the fashion industry, its history, and the people who have shaped it. Whether you’re a fashion enthusiast, a budding designer, or simply curious about the world of fashion, these documentaries on Amazon Prime will expand your style knowledge and provide a deeper understanding of this captivating industry. So grab your remote, queue up these films, and prepare to be inspired by the artistry, creativity, and passion that defines the world of fashion.
