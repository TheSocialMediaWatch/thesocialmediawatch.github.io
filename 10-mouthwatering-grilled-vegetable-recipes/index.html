10 Mouthwatering Grilled Vegetable Recipes


Are you tired of the same old grilled veggies? Looking to spice up your summer barbecues with some new and exciting flavors? Look no further! We’ve curated a list of 10 mouthwatering grilled vegetable recipes that are sure to impress your family and friends. From smoky and savory to sweet and tangy, these recipes will take your grilled veggies to a whole new level. So fire up that grill and let’s get cooking!

1. Grilled Asparagus with Lemon Garlic Marinade

This simple yet flavorful recipe is perfect for asparagus lovers. Toss the asparagus spears in a marinade of lemon juice, minced garlic, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Let it sit for 15 minutes before grilling over medium heat for about 5 minutes, or until tender-crisp. The tangy lemon and garlic flavors will complement the natural sweetness of the asparagus.

2. Spicy Grilled Zucchini

If you like a little heat, this recipe is for you. Slice zucchini into thick rounds and brush with a mixture of olive oil, chili powder, cumin, salt, and pepper. Grill over medium-high heat for 3-4 minutes on each side until nicely charred. The smoky flavors of the grill combined with the spicy seasoning will leave your taste buds begging for more.

3. Balsamic Glazed Grilled Eggplant

Eggplant lovers, rejoice! This recipe takes grilled eggplant to a whole new level of deliciousness. Slice the eggplant into thick rounds and brush with a mixture of balsamic vinegar, honey, olive oil, salt, and pepper. Grill over medium heat for 4-5 minutes on each side, or until tender and caramelized. The sweet and tangy balsamic glaze will elevate the flavors of the eggplant.

4. Grilled Portobello Mushrooms with Herbed Butter

Portobello mushrooms are a vegetarian’s dream. Clean the mushrooms and brush with a mixture of melted butter, minced garlic, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper. Grill over medium-high heat for 5-6 minutes on each side until juicy and tender. The herbed butter adds a burst of flavor to these meaty mushrooms.

5. Tangy Grilled Bell Peppers

Add some color to your grill with tangy bell peppers. Slice the peppers into thick strips and toss with olive oil, red wine vinegar, minced garlic, salt, and pepper. Grill over medium heat for 3-4 minutes on each side until slightly charred and tender. The tangy marinade will complement the sweetness of the peppers perfectly.

6. Grilled Corn on the Cob with Chili Lime Butter

No summer barbecue is complete without grilled corn on the cob. Peel back the husks, remove the silk, and brush the corn with a mixture of melted butter, chili powder, lime zest, salt, and pepper. Grill over medium-high heat for 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the corn is tender and charred. The chili lime butter adds a burst of flavor to this classic side dish.

7. Grilled Sweet Potatoes with Maple Glaze

Who said grilled veggies can’t be sweet? Slice sweet potatoes into thick rounds and brush with a mixture of maple syrup, melted butter, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Grill over medium heat for 5-6 minutes on each side until caramelized and tender. The maple glaze adds a delightful sweetness to these grilled sweet potatoes.

8. Grilled Brussels Sprouts with Balsamic Reduction

Brussels sprouts get a bad rap, but they can be absolutely delicious when grilled. Toss the Brussels sprouts in olive oil, salt, and pepper, then grill over medium-high heat for 5-6 minutes on each side until charred and tender. Drizzle with a balsamic reduction for a tangy and sweet flavor.

9. Grilled Cauliflower Steaks with Herb Marinade

Cauliflower steaks are a great vegetarian alternative to meat. Slice the cauliflower into thick steaks, brush with a mixture of olive oil, minced garlic, lemon juice, thyme, salt, and pepper, and grill over medium-high heat for 5-6 minutes on each side until nicely charred and tender. The herb marinade infuses the cauliflower with a burst of flavor.

10. Grilled Portobello Burger with Avocado Mayo

Last but not least, we have a mouthwatering grilled portobello burger. Marinate the portobello mushrooms in a mixture of balsamic vinegar, soy sauce, minced garlic, and olive oil for 30 minutes. Grill over medium-high heat for 4-5 minutes on each side until juicy and tender. Serve on a bun with avocado mayo, lettuce, and tomato for a flavorful and satisfying vegetarian burger.

There you have it - 10 mouthwatering grilled vegetable recipes to take your summer barbecues to the next level. Whether you’re a vegetarian or just looking to add some variety to your grill, these recipes are sure to please. So fire up that grill, gather your ingredients, and get ready to indulge in some delicious grilled veggies. Happy grilling!
