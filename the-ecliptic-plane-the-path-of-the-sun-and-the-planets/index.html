The Ecliptic Plane: The Path of the Sun and the Planets


This article discusses the ecliptic plane, which is the path that the Sun and the planets follow as they move through the universe. The ecliptic plane is an elliptical plane that surrounds the Sun and includes all of the planets, including Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

The ecliptic plane is thought to have originated around 4.6 billion years ago, when the solar system was first forming. At that time, the solar system was not yet stable, and the ecliptic plane was influenced by the uneven distribution of mass within the solar system. This uneven distribution of mass caused the ecliptic plane to take on its characteristic shape, which is an ellipse.
