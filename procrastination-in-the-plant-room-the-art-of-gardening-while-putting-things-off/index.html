Procrastination in the Plant Room: The Art of Gardening While Putting Things Off


Ever found yourself hyped about a new hobby, only to realize you’ve spent most of your time scrolling on your phone instead of diving right into it? We’ve all been there; it’s part of our human nature to procrastinate. And if you’re a gardening enthusiast like me, you might find yourself spending hours researching plants and soil instead of actually cultivating them. But let me tell you, procrastination can sometimes lead to surprising discoveries and delightful outcomes. We’ll explore how procrastination in your plant room can be the perfect art form for cultivating patience, self-awareness, and even the perfect garden.

The Dance of Procrastination and Gardening: A Love Story

To understand the significance of procrastination in the plant room, let’s dive into why we procrastinate in the first place. Some call it laziness, but in truth, it’s our brain’s way of protecting us from overwhelm, allowing us to tackle tasks with more energy and focus. The irony here is that a task as calming and rewarding as gardening can easily transform into an endless rabbit hole of ideation. I once spent days trying to figure out the best spot in my garden for a sunflower, only to realize I didn’t even have the patience to wait for it to grow.

A Journey Through Inaction

When you procrastinate, you often embark on an unexpected journey. You discover new plants, gardening techniques, and perhaps even hidden talents. I stumbled upon my knack for propagating succulents while aimlessly scrolling through gardening blogs, but it took months of procrastination before I finally turned to the task. Through this in-action, I learned the art of patience and the delicate balance of nurturing growth – both in plants and in myself.

Unearthing Compassion for Yourself

One of the most valuable, albeit unexpected, gifts of procrastination in the plant room is self-compassion. By recognizing your tendency to procrastinate, you learn to be more gentle with yourself when faced with tasks that seem daunting. My plant room has become a haven for learning self-love as I simultaneously nurture my garden and myself, accepting that perfectionism and productivity don’t always go hand in hand.

Finding Harmony: Combining Procrastination and Gardening

It’s time to embrace the procrastination in your plant room and find harmony between your desire to grow your garden and your penchant for delay. Here are some tips for those of us who love to put things off:

  1. Start Small: Break down your gardening goals into smaller, manageable tasks. This approach will help you tackle those tasks with less procrastination while also allowing you to truly enjoy the process.

  2. Change Your Outlook: Instead of viewing procrastination as time wasted, see it as breathing room for growth. Often, we stumble upon new ideas or creative solutions when we allow space for procrastination.

  3. Lean on Your Community: Connect with fellow plant enthusiasts. Join a local gardening group or online forum. You’ll be amazed at how this sense of community can motivate you to get your hands dirty and turn those procrastination fantasies into a thriving reality.

  4. Practice Mindful Gardening: This means being present with your plants, appreciating and observing their growth, and accepting when they don’t thrive as expected. When you find yourself procrastinating, spend that time in mindful observation, and you’ll find your garden miracles will unfold all the more undeniably.

  5. Give Yourself Permission to Rest: Remember, your plants aren’t going anywhere. Nature has its own pace. So, while it’s tempting to get everything done instantly, know that allowing yourself the time to enjoy the journey will lead to a more enriched, fulfilled garden experience.

Embracing the Procrastination Garden

At the heart of gardening while procrastinating lies the delicate dance between doing and not doing. Through procrastination, you uncover new delights and develop a relationship with your garden that transcends a simple love of plants. This, my friends, is the art of procrastination in the plant room. So, dive in, embrace the in-action and watch your garden thrive, one procrastinated step at a time.
