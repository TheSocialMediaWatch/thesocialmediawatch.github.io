Seasonal Planting: What to Grow Each Season


Seasonal planting is a key aspect of gardening, ensuring that gardens burst into life with spectacular flowers and fruits at every turn. Different regions have different planting seasons depending on climate, but the following recommendations serve as a helpful guide for enthusiastic gardeners.

Spring Planting

The gentle warming of the earth in spring signals the start of the growing season. Early spring is particularly special as it marks the beginning of long summer days, and the promise of abundance is in the air.

  1. Flowers: Spring is the time for flowering plants. Choose from a wide variety of spring flowers like crocuses, daffodils, tulips, and hyacinths to adorn your garden. These early bloomers add delightful colors to the barren landscape.
  2. Vegetables: Hardy vegetables such as lettuce, spinach, carrots, and beets are optimal for planting in the spring. These seedlings will be better protected from pests and harsh weather conditions as they establish themselves.
  3. Herbs: Basil, cilantro, and parsley thrive in mild spring temperatures. Plant these herbs to enhance your culinary endeavors.

Summer Planting

As temperatures rise, gardening gets more interesting. The main focus in summer is to keep up with the rapid growth and ensure the plants get adequate water.

  1. Flowers: Summer is the time when flowers bloom in full glory. Choose perennials like dahlias, salvia, and daylilies that will outlive the season.
  2. Fruits: Summer is an ideal time to plant fruits like berries, melons, and tomatoes. Patience will be rewarded with a bountiful harvest in the fall.
  3. Vegetables: Warm-weather vegetables like tomatoes, peppers, and zucchini can be sown directly into the ground this season. They grow well in the warm soil and sunshine.
  4. Herbs: Herbs that prefer dry conditions, like rosemary and thyme, can be planted in summer.

Autumn Planting

Autumn gardening often gets neglected, but it’s crucial for laying the groundwork for a beautiful garden the following spring. Planting in autumn takes advantage of the remaining heat and moisture in the soil.

  1. Flowers: These are the last to bloom before winter. Flowers like chrysanthemums, router-and-trees, and calendula are perfect for autumn landscapes.
  2. Vegetables: Root vegetables such as beets, carrots, and parsnips grow throughout the cold season. Plant them in the fall so they can develop strong root systems before winter.
  3. Herbs: Autumn is a good time to plant perennials like chives and mint, as they’ll have a chance to establish underground roots before the cold reaches them.

Winter Planting

Although the growing season is less evident in winter, it can be put to good use by planting hardy winter vegetables and evergreens.

  1. Flowers: Decorate your winter landscape with evergreens like pine and holly. They’ll add textures and colors throughout the dreary season.
  2. Vegetables: Winter vegetables like kale, spinach, and chicory can be started in the fall and continue to provide fresh food even in the cold season.
  3. Herbs: Winter herbs like mint, woodruff, and savory can be planted in the fall. These hardy herbs are perfect for seasoning during the festive season.

Seasonal planting is an exciting way to grow and change your garden throughout the year. As temperatures vary, so too can the plants that thrive within the unique microclimate found in your garden. Cheers to experimenting with gardens of all four seasons!
