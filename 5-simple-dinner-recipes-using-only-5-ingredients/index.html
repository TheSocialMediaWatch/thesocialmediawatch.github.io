5 Simple Dinner Recipes Using Only 5 Ingredients


It’s a weeknight. You open your fridge, peer inside, and…not a lot stares back at you. The idea of crafting an elaborate meal just isn’t on tonight’s agenda—but takeout? Again? Not necessary. Sometimes, delicious dinners come from constraint. Limiting your ingredients can force bursts of creativity and, as a happy bonus, keep both shopping and cleanup refreshingly painless.

Dinner doesn’t have to be overcomplicated or require a shopping cart overflowing with specialty ingredients. The following five recipes, each built with only five key staples, cover a range of cravings and cooking styles. You’ll find variety—classic comfort food, light seasonal options, crowd-pleasers, and dishes that feel much fancier than their shopping list implies.

Each recipe is straightforward, uses just five main ingredients (we’re not counting salt, pepper, water, or basic oils), and is designed for real weeknight life. Whether you’re feeding the family, whipping up a solo supper, or craving something special with minimum effort, you’ll have dinner on the table in less time and with less fuss than your last delivery order.

1. Creamy Garlic Parmesan Chicken

Few things hit the spot like chicken with a golden, garlicky crust bathed in a creamy Parmesan sauce. This recipe delivers all the rich comfort food energy without a pot-load of steps or ingredients.

Ingredients:

  • Chicken breasts (boneless, skinless)
  • Garlic cloves
  • Heavy cream
  • Parmesan cheese (freshly grated works best)
  • Baby spinach

Instructions:

  1. Season the chicken breasts with salt and pepper. Heat a drizzle of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat and cook the chicken until golden and cooked through (about 6-7 minutes per side, depending on thickness). Remove to a plate.
  2. In the same pan, add minced garlic. Sauté for 60 seconds until fragrant.
  3. Pour in the heavy cream, scraping up any browned bits. Simmer gently.
  4. Add the Parmesan cheese, stirring until the sauce thickens and is velvety.
  5. Add spinach, stirring until wilted. Return chicken to the pan, spoon sauce over the top, and simmer for another minute.
  6. Serve with your favorite side, or simply mop up the creamy sauce with crusty bread.

Tips for Success and Variations:
For deeper flavor, feel free to add a squeeze of lemon or sprinkle chopped fresh herbs. Serve over pasta, rice, or steamed vegetables for a complete meal.

2. Sheet Pan Lemon-Herb Salmon

Dinner cooked on one pan makes for simple prep and, more importantly, rare quick cleanup. Salmon shines with just a hint of citrus and fresh herbs.

Ingredients:

  • Salmon fillets (skin-on or off, your choice)
  • Fresh lemon
  • Fresh dill (or flat-leaf parsley)
  • Olive oil
  • Small potatoes (baby potatoes or fingerlings work beautifully)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Halve the baby potatoes and toss with olive oil, salt, and pepper on a baking sheet. Roast for 10 minutes.
  2. Remove the sheet, push potatoes to one side, and nestle in the salmon fillets.
  3. Drizzle salmon with olive oil, season lightly, and top with thin slices of lemon and a shower of chopped dill or parsley.
  4. Return to the oven and roast until the salmon is flaky and opaque (another 12-15 minutes) and potatoes are golden and tender.
  5. Squeeze extra lemon juice over the finished dish for a bright, clean flavor.

Tips for Success and Variations:
Mix things up with new potatoes or sweet potato rounds. If fresh herbs aren’t on hand, dried dill or Italian seasoning will do in a pinch. Serve with a crisp salad for extra greens.

3. 5-Ingredient Pasta alla Vodka

Classic Italian flavors in a flash. A rich, silky vodka sauce needs surprisingly little: just good tomatoes, vodka, cream, and Parmesan over your favorite pasta. Don’t worry: most of the alcohol cooks off, leaving a subtle, deep flavor.

Ingredients:

  • Pasta (penne or rigatoni are favorites, but any variety works)
  • Crushed canned tomatoes
  • Heavy cream
  • Vodka
  • Parmesan cheese

Instructions:

  1. Cook pasta in generously salted water until just al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water and drain.
  2. In a large skillet, pour in crushed tomatoes. Simmer a few minutes to reduce slightly.
  3. Add vodka, stirring, and simmer for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Stir in heavy cream and continue simmering until sauce is cohesive and pink.
  5. Toss cooked pasta in the sauce, add reserved pasta water as needed to loosen, and finish with a handful of grated Parmesan cheese.
  6. Serve immediately, adding more cheese if the mood strikes.

Tips for Success and Variations:
Toss in red pepper flakes for heat. If you have fresh basil, it’s a lovely final touch. Round out the meal with a green salad or garlic bread.

4. Turkey & Black Bean Skillet

Hearty, wholesome, and filling—this skillet meal is the kind of thing you’ll return to all year long. Ground turkey delivers lean protein, while black beans add fiber and flavor. With a pop from salsa and a crown of melted cheese, it’s dinner in less than 25 minutes.

Ingredients:

  • Ground turkey
  • Canned black beans (drained and rinsed)
  • Store-bought salsa (medium for kick, mild if sensitive to spice)
  • Shredded cheese (cheddar or Monterey Jack)
  • Bell pepper (any color)

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet, sauté chopped bell pepper in a bit of oil until softened.
  2. Add ground turkey and cook, breaking up with a spoon, until just browned.
  3. Stir in black beans and salsa. Simmer for 5-7 minutes to combine flavors.
  4. Sprinkle cheese over the top, cover, and cook for another couple of minutes until melted and gooey.
  5. Serve straight from the skillet with tortilla chips, over rice, or in warmed tortillas.

Tips for Success and Variations:
Add a spoonful of Greek yogurt or sour cream for creaminess. Mix in corn or chopped jalapeño if you have some on hand. Leftovers make stellar burrito fillings.

5. Caprese-Stuffed Portobello Mushrooms

Looking for a lighter vegetarian main that’s still satisfying? These oversized portobello mushrooms become edible plates, loaded with creamy mozzarella, sweet tomatoes, and fresh basil for that irresistible caprese salad flavor. Just five ingredients—endless payoff.

Ingredients:

  • Large portobello mushroom caps
  • Fresh mozzarella cheese
  • Ripe cherry or grape tomatoes
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Balsamic glaze (store-bought or homemade reduction)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Remove stems from mushrooms and gently scrape out gills with a spoon for more room.
  2. Place mushrooms gill-side up on a baking sheet. Layer with slices of mozzarella and halved cherry tomatoes.
  3. Drizzle lightly with olive oil, sprinkle salt and pepper, and bake until cheese is melting and mushrooms are juicy (about 15 minutes).
  4. Top with torn basil leaves and finish with a generous drizzle of balsamic glaze.
  5. Serve immediately, perhaps with crusty bread or a simple green salad.

Tips for Success and Variations:
Add a sprinkle of crushed red pepper for a kick, or a handful of baby arugula for peppery bite. These are fabulous starters but can hold their own as a main course, especially paired with herbed couscous or quinoa.

Why Five-Ingredient Dinners Work

Minimal ingredient cooking isn’t just about ease—it naturally lets flavors shine through. By focusing on complementary ingredients, you can build richness and complexity without stocking a massive pantry. Recipes like these hit the sweet spot: simple enough for stressed-out evenings, special enough for family or last-minute guests.

With the right five components, you can keep things balanced: protein, vegetables, healthy fats, and plenty of flavor. It’s a reminder that everyday dinner can walk the line between streamlined and sensational.

Smart Swaps & Tips for Success

  • Prioritize flavor: Quality matters more when there are fewer ingredients. Splurge on good Parmesan, ripe tomatoes, or fresh herbs.
  • Use pantry staples wisely: Salt, pepper, water, and basic oils aren’t counted in these recipes, so use them freely for seasoning and cooking.
  • Keep your own favorites: Sub in the protein or veg you love most—swap chicken for fish, or cheddar for mozzarella.
  • Batch cook: Make a double batch for easy lunches or quick dinners later in the week.
  • Play with sauces: Store-bought salsas, pesto, or vinaigrettes can elevate simple dishes to full-flavored meals.

Dinner Can Be This Simple

The next time you’re short on time, patience, or extra ingredients, remember that delicious dinners do not require a long list or endless steps. Try these five-ingredient wonders—chicken in creamy sauce, one-pan salmon, lush vodka pasta, hearty turkey skillet, and caprese-stuffed mushrooms—and discover how choosing simplicity doesn’t mean sacrificing flavor or satisfaction.

Let your fridge and pantry inspire you. Restriction can be the secret ingredient you never knew you needed for creating dinners everyone looks forward to.
