How to Save Money on Hotels Without Compromising Comfort


Choosing a place to stay can often be the most significant hotel expense while traveling. Hotels are a significant part of travel budgets, and therefore, finding ways to save money on hotel bookings is indeed an exciting prospect. This guide elaborates on several strategies that can aid in cutting accommodation costs without compromising on comfort.

  1. Booking in Advance

Bookings in advance can offer substantial savings on hotels. Hotels prefer to fill their rooms with advanced bookings; they can slash rates to do so. Planning in advance helps you observe this drop in room rates. Websites like Orbitz and Expedia often provide advanced-purchase discounts. Sign up for an account, and you can receive instant alerts about such deals. Also, airlines often offer bundled hotel rates as a part of their package sales, which can save significantly.

  1. Utilizing Reward Programs

Reward programs are incentives in themselves. By enrolling, you become part of a select group where points earned from bookings can be redeemed for free hotel stays or room upgrades. Marriott’s Bonvoy and Hilton Honors are notable examples of these hotel chains’ reward programs. Furthermore, websites like Hotels.com’s rewards system can send you promotional codes for discounted rates that you can claim.

  1. Opting for Alternatives

Are hotels, by default, your only choice of accommodation? No, alternatives exist. Services like Airbnb, Vrbo, and Bookabach provide access to the homes of locals. Renting a home can be a cheaper proposition than hotels. Unique rooms, fully-equipped apartments, treehouses, boats, castles, and more await potential visitors.

  1. Being Flexible with Dates

Hotels fill their rooms based on demand. Be ready for your travel dates to be re-routed if flight disruptions occur. Picking lesser-known travel times can bring cost savings. Flights and hotels can be cheaper on weekdays than weekends.

  1. Exploring Off-Season Travel

Measuring success primarily through revenue, the hotel industry understands and benefits by the optimized revenue strategy. Pricing fluctuates depending on time factors. Off-season travel savings are massive and can be between 20% to 70%. Chasing what is likely a server refreshing would not be my first recommendation. Research the peak months and the shoulder periods.

  1. Negotiating with Hotels

Most people assume that hotels have fixed rates. It’s not entirely accurate. Often, direct contact with hotels can result in the hotel booking at a lesser price. Travel booking sites go through intermediaries, with some of their margin feeding in the room rates. Going direct to the hotel can be beneficial.

  1. Taking Advantage of Loyalty Credential When Booking

Few people know that loyalty credentials like AAA or AARP can snag travel discounts. You don’t always have to wait for Black Friday or seasonal travel discounts to enjoy savings. Being a member of these groups can offer such opportunities.

In conclusion, staying at hotels doesn’t necessarily have to be expensive. By considering multiple aspects, it’s indeed possible to save thousands yearly. That next vacation could see you damaging those Amex points to love and laugh on an exotic beach. Happy savings!
