Understanding the Basics of Human Rights

The concept of human rights is deeply ingrained in the history of civilization, tracing back to ancient civilizations and reflective in various religious and cultural practices. At the core of human rights are three major categories: civil, political, and social rights. Civil rights are those rights pertaining to one’s relationship with the state and involve privileges granted to individuals by the government, such as the right to a fair trial, the right to vote, and the right to privacy.
