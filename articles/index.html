Understanding the significance of malolactic fermentation is essential for both wine enthusiasts and aspiring winemakers. What is Malolactic Fermentation? Malolactic fermentation is a secondary fermentation process that occurs after the primary alcoholic fermentation. Unlike the primary fermentation, which is carried out by yeast converting grape sugar into alcohol, malolactic fermentation involves bacteria, specifically Lactic Acid Bacteria (LAB), converting harsh malic acid into softer lactic acid and carbon dioxide.
