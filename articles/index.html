The Triangulum Galaxy, also known as Messier 33 or M33, is a stunning spiral galaxy located in the Local Group, a small cluster of galaxies that includes our own Milky Way. It is situated approximately 3 million light-years away from Earth, making it one of the closest spiral galaxies to us. Despite its proximity, the Triangulum Galaxy remained undiscovered until the late 18th century. A Stellar Sight When gazing up at the night sky, the Triangulum Galaxy is an impressive sight to behold. With an apparent magnitude of 5.7, it is visible to the naked eye under dark skies. However, due to its relatively low surface brightness, it can be challenging to spot in light-polluted areas.
