Compounding is the process by which your money grows exponentially over time, as the returns you earn on your investments are reinvested to generate even more returns. In simple terms, it’s like a snowball effect for your money - the longer it rolls, the larger it gets. Understanding Compounding To understand the power of compounding, let’s take a look at a hypothetical example. Suppose you invest $10,000 in an account that earns an annual interest rate of 5%. In the first year, your investment would grow to $10,500. In the second year, you’d earn interest not only on your initial $10,000 but also on the $500 interest from the first year, resulting in a total of $10,500 compounded by 5%.
