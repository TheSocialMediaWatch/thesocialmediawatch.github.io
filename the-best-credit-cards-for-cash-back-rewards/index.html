The Best Credit Cards for Cash Back Rewards


Credit cards have become an essential financial tool for many individuals, offering a variety of benefits ranging from convenience to security. Among the most sought-after perks is the ability to earn cash back on everyday purchases. Cash back rewards programs allow cardholders to earn a percentage of their spending back, which can be redeemed for statement credits, direct deposits, or gift cards. However, with so many options on the market, choosing the best credit card for cash back rewards can be overwhelming. In this guide, we will explore the top cash back credit cards, helping you find the right one for your needs and maximize your earnings.

What Are Cash Back Rewards?

Cash back rewards refer to a type of credit card benefit where cardholders earn a percentage of their spending back as a rebate. For example, if you spend $100 on a purchase with a 2% cash back credit card, you will earn $2 as a reward. These rewards can add up over time and can be used for a variety of purposes, including reducing your credit card balance, saving for future purchases, or even receiving direct payments.

Cash back credit cards are popular because they are relatively simple to understand and use. They don’t involve complicated reward structures like points or miles and are available to both new and seasoned credit card users.

Factors to Consider When Choosing the Best Cash Back Credit Card

Before diving into our list of the best cash back credit cards, it’s important to consider several key factors that can influence your decision. Understanding your spending habits and how you plan to use the card will help you choose the most beneficial option.

1. Cash Back Rate

The primary benefit of cash back credit cards is, of course, the cash back rate. Most credit cards offer different cash back percentages for various categories, such as groceries, gas, dining, and online shopping. It’s essential to compare these rates and determine which categories align with your spending habits. For example, if you spend a lot on dining out, you may want to look for a card that offers high cash back rewards for restaurants.

2. Annual Fees

Some of the best cash back credit cards come with an annual fee, while others are fee-free. A fee-free card might seem like a better choice, but if the rewards structure of a card with an annual fee outweighs the cost, it might still be worth the investment. Be sure to calculate how much you expect to earn in rewards and compare it to the card’s annual fee to determine if it’s worth it.

3. Sign-Up Bonuses

Many credit cards offer enticing sign-up bonuses to new cardholders. These bonuses can be in the form of a large cash back reward or a points bonus, which you can redeem for cash back. Sign-up bonuses can provide a substantial boost to your rewards early on, but make sure to read the terms and conditions, as these offers may require you to meet specific spending thresholds within a set time period.

4. Foreign Transaction Fees

If you plan to use your credit card while traveling abroad, look for a card with no foreign transaction fees. Many cash back credit cards charge a fee of 3% for transactions made outside the United States, which can add up quickly when traveling. Cards with no foreign transaction fees allow you to use your card abroad without worrying about extra charges.

5. Redemption Options

Cash back rewards typically come with several redemption options. Some cards allow you to redeem your rewards for statement credits, while others offer direct deposits into your bank account or gift cards. Check to see if the redemption process is easy and offers the flexibility you need.

Top Cash Back Credit Cards

Now that you understand the key factors to consider, let’s take a look at some of the top cash back credit cards available. These options offer high cash back rates, generous rewards, and a variety of other benefits to help you make the most of your spending.

1. Chase Freedom Unlimited®

The Chase Freedom Unlimited® is one of the best all-around cash back credit cards available today. It offers an impressive 1.5% cash back on all purchases, with no spending categories to track. Additionally, it provides higher cash back rates for specific categories:

  • 5% cash back on travel purchases made through the Chase Ultimate Rewards® program.
  • 3% cash back on dining and drugstore purchases.

This card also offers a sign-up bonus: earn a $200 cash bonus after spending $500 on purchases in the first 3 months from account opening. The card has no annual fee, making it an excellent option for those looking for straightforward cash back rewards.

2. Blue Cash Preferred® Card from American Express

The Blue Cash Preferred® Card from American Express is ideal for those who spend a lot on groceries. It offers an impressive 6% cash back on up to $6,000 spent at U.S. supermarkets per year (then 1%), making it the top choice for grocery shopping enthusiasts. In addition, cardholders earn:

  • 3% cash back on U.S. gas station and transit purchases.
  • 1% cash back on other purchases.

This card also comes with a generous sign-up offer: earn a $350 statement credit after spending $3,000 on purchases in the first 3 months. The card does have a $95 annual fee, but the high cash back rewards on groceries and gas can quickly offset the cost.

3. Citi® Double Cash Card

The Citi® Double Cash Card is one of the simplest and most rewarding cash back cards available. It offers an industry-leading 2% cash back on every purchase: 1% when you buy and 1% when you pay. This makes it an excellent choice for people who want a straightforward, no-fuss cash back card that offers high returns on every purchase.

There’s no annual fee, and the cash back can be redeemed as a statement credit, check, or direct deposit into your bank account. The Citi® Double Cash Card is a great option for those who don’t want to worry about category restrictions or tracking specific types of purchases.

4. Capital One QuicksilverOne® Cash Rewards Credit Card

If you’re looking for a card with a simple rewards structure and an easy path to earning cash back, the Capital One QuicksilverOne® Cash Rewards Credit Card is an excellent option. It offers unlimited 1.5% cash back on every purchase, with no rotating categories or spending limits.

This card is particularly well-suited for those with average credit, as it has a more lenient approval process compared to some other premium cards. While there is an annual fee of $39, the straightforward rewards and no foreign transaction fees make it a solid choice for those new to credit cards.

5. Discover it® Cash Back

The Discover it® Cash Back card is a great option for individuals who want to earn cash back on rotating categories each quarter. It offers 5% cash back on up to $1,500 in purchases in select categories that change every quarter (activation is required), and 1% cash back on all other purchases. Categories in the past have included things like grocery stores, restaurants, and gas stations.

New cardholders can also earn a unique welcome bonus: Discover will match all the cash back you’ve earned at the end of your first year. This card has no annual fee, making it a solid choice for those looking to earn higher cash back rates in rotating categories.

6. Wells Fargo Active Cash® Card

For those who want a straightforward card with great rewards, the Wells Fargo Active Cash® Card is worth considering. It offers unlimited 2% cash back on all purchases, making it an excellent option for everyday spending. The card also comes with a $200 cash rewards bonus after spending $500 on purchases in the first 3 months.

With no annual fee and no rotating categories, this card is an easy choice for people who want to earn consistent cash back without worrying about tracking spending. The Wells Fargo Active Cash® Card also provides a 0% introductory APR on purchases and qualifying balance transfers for the first 15 months.

7. Bank of America® Customized Cash Rewards Credit Card

The Bank of America® Customized Cash Rewards Credit Card allows cardholders to earn 3% cash back on a category of their choice, such as gas, online shopping, dining, travel, drug stores, or home improvement. You can also earn 2% cash back on grocery store and wholesale club purchases (on the first $2,500 in combined choice category/grocery store/wholesale club purchases each quarter). All other purchases earn 1% cash back.

This card is unique because it offers flexibility in choosing your 3% cash back category, which can help you maximize your rewards based on your spending habits. There’s no annual fee, and the card also offers a $200 online cash rewards bonus after making at least $1,000 in purchases in the first 90 days.

Conclusion

Choosing the best cash back credit card ultimately depends on your spending habits, the benefits that matter most to you, and how much you’re willing to pay in annual fees. Whether you prefer a simple flat-rate rewards structure or a card that allows you to earn higher rewards in specific categories, there is a cash back credit card that can help you make the most of your everyday spending.

By carefully comparing cash back rates, annual fees, sign-up bonuses, and other perks, you can find the card that best aligns with your financial goals. With the right cash back credit card, you can enjoy more rewards and a higher return on your spending, helping you save money and reach your financial objectives faster.
