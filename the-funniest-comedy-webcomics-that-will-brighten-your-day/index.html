The Funniest Comedy Webcomics That Will Brighten Your Day


Looking for a good laugh? Look no further than these hilarious webcomics that are sure to bring a smile to your face. From witty humor to relatable characters, these webcomics cover a wide range of topics and styles, offering something for everyone. Whether you’re a long-time fan of webcomics or just getting started, these selections are guaranteed to brighten your day.

1. The Perry Bible Fellowship

If you appreciate dark, offbeat humor, then The Perry Bible Fellowship is the webcomic for you. Created by Nicholas Gurewitch, this comic features a unique blend of surreal and absurd comedy, often with unexpected twists that will leave you chuckling. With its clever writing and distinctive art style, this webcomic has garnered a dedicated following and numerous awards over the years.

2. xkcd

For those who love science, math, and technology, xkcd is a must-read. Created by Randall Munroe, this webcomic uses stick figure drawings and witty dialogue to explore a wide range of topics, from physics and computer science to everyday absurdities. With its clever and insightful humor, xkcd has become a beloved staple in the webcomics community.

3. Sarah’s Scribbles

If you’re a millennial or Gen Z-er, you’ll likely relate to the humor in Sarah’s Scribbles. Created by Sarah Andersen, this webcomic humorously captures the everyday struggles and awkward moments of young adulthood. With its charming and relatable art style, Sarah’s Scribbles has gained a massive following on social media platforms and has been compiled into several best-selling books.

4. Cyanide & Happiness

A list of funny webcomics wouldn’t be complete without mentioning Cyanide & Happiness. Created by Kris Wilson, Rob DenBleyker, Dave McElfatrick, and Matt Melvin, this webcomic is known for its dark and twisted humor, often delving into the absurd and macabre. With its distinctive stick figure characters and irreverent style, Cyanide & Happiness has amassed a large and dedicated fanbase.

5. The Oatmeal

The Oatmeal, created by Matthew Inman, is a webcomic that combines humor with insightful commentary on various aspects of life, technology, and cats. Known for its quirky art style and humorous take on everyday situations, The Oatmeal has gained widespread popularity and has been praised for its unique blend of comedy and thought-provoking content.

6. Saturday Morning Breakfast Cereal

If you enjoy intellectual humor and thought-provoking comics, Saturday Morning Breakfast Cereal is a must-read. Created by Zach Weinersmith, this webcomic delves into science, philosophy, and the absurdities of life with a clever and often satirical approach. With its unique blend of humor and intellectual wit, Saturday Morning Breakfast Cereal has earned a dedicated following and critical acclaim.

7. Poorly Drawn Lines

Poorly Drawn Lines, created by Reza Farazmand, offers a delightful mix of absurd humor, whimsical characters, and surreal situations. With its charmingly simplistic art style and whimsical storytelling, this webcomic manages to capture the absurdities of modern life in a way that is both relatable and hilarious.

Whether you’re a fan of dark humor, intellectual wit, or relatable everyday situations, these webcomics are guaranteed to brighten your day and leave you in stitches. So, grab your favorite beverage, kick back, and immerse yourself in the wonderful world of webcomics. You’re in for a hilarious and entertaining ride!

There you have it – a collection of some of the funniest and most beloved webcomics that are sure to bring a smile to your face. Whether you’re a long-time fan of webcomics or just getting started, these selections are guaranteed to brighten your day.
