Satire Writing Workshops You Can Join Online


Satire has been a powerful tool in literature and media for centuries, delighting and provoking audiences through humor wrapped in critique. Whether you’re aiming to sharpen your wit, hone your narrative craft, or dive into the subversive world of satirical commentary, joining an online satire writing workshop can be a fantastic way to develop your skills and connect with fellow writers. This post explores some of the best virtual spaces to learn satire writing and what you can expect from these experiences.

Why Satire? Why Now?

In a world buzzing with contradictory headlines and social upheavals, satire holds a unique place. Its ability to poke fun at societal norms, politics, culture, and human folly lets writers influence public perception creatively and critically. Writing satire is as much about sharp observation as it is about timing, tone, and subtlety.

Modern digital platforms have expanded the reach and immediacy of satirical content. Memes, tweets, blogs, and web series often use satire to comment on current events, making the ability to craft witty and insightful satire highly relevant. If you’re looking to write something that’s not only entertaining but thought-provoking, learning the craft through an online workshop can be your stepping stone.

What Can You Expect in an Online Satire Writing Workshop?

A well-structured satire writing workshop offers a mix of theory, analysis, writing exercises, and peer feedback. Some key features include:

  • Understanding Satirical Techniques: From irony and exaggeration to parody and sarcasm, these workshops dissect different satirical methods and explore how they function.

  • Historical and Contemporary Examples: Participants study iconic satire, ranging from Jonathan Swift’s A Modest Proposal to recent viral satirical articles, to learn tone, style, and impact.

  • Writing Prompts and Assignments: Tackling specific exercises designed to stretch your ability to employ satire effectively.

  • Feedback Sessions: Constructive critiques help highlight strengths and areas for improvement while encouraging experimentation.

  • Community Building: Engaging with fellow satirists, sharing work, brainstorming ideas, and supporting each other’s creative journey.

Now, let’s look at some reputable online satire writing workshops you can join to elevate your craft.

Top Online Satire Writing Workshops to Consider

1. The Poetry School: Writing Satire Course

Though originally known for poetry, The Poetry School offers a satirical writing module aimed at developing sharp, socially conscious prose. The flexible schedule allows participants to work at their own pace while receiving personalized tutor feedback.

  • Duration: 6 weeks
  • Delivery: Online via dedicated learning platform
  • Highlights: Focus on social criticism through humor and irony, analysis of classic and modern satires, regular writing tasks.

2. Writing the Satirical Essay – Udemy

Udemy hosts several courses focusing on satire, with this particular course helping aspiring writers build the foundations of satirical essays. It includes video lectures, downloadable resources, and practical assignments.

  • Duration: Self-paced
  • Delivery: Video lessons online
  • Highlights: Step-by-step guidance on crafting satire with clear examples, accessible for beginners and intermediate writers.

3. LitReactor: Satire Writing Workshop

LitReactor offers robust workshops led by published authors specializing in satire and humor. These workshops combine rigorous writing exercises with community forums to encourage ongoing dialogue.

  • Duration: 8 weeks
  • Delivery: Online, via web portal, includes live video discussions
  • Highlights: Focus on narrative satire, integration of humor theory, peer and instructor feedback, opportunity to submit work for publication guidance.

4. MasterClass: David Sedaris Teaches Storytelling and Humor

While not exclusively about satire, David Sedaris’s MasterClass includes substantial content on satirical humor and personal storytelling. Lessons cover how to weave satire into engaging narratives effectively.

  • Duration: 19 video lessons (Variable pacing)
  • Delivery: Streaming online platform
  • Highlights: Learn from a master humorist, practice blending satire with memoir-style writing, keen insights on timing and character voice.

5. Gotham Writers Workshop – Online Satire Course

Gotham Writers Workshop is a well-established name in creative writing education. Their dedicated satire class helps writers recognize the ethics and boundaries of satire while emphasizing creativity and clarity.

  • Duration: 6 weeks
  • Delivery: Live online classes via video conferencing
  • Highlights: Interactive classes, small group sizes, detailed feedback, exploration of satire’s social responsibilities and tools.

How to Choose the Right Workshop for You

Selecting the ideal satire writing workshop requires consideration of several factors:

  • Skill Level: Some courses cater to complete beginners while others are designed for experienced writers looking to refine their satirical voice.

  • Time Commitment: Do you need a flexible, self-paced course or do you prefer scheduled live sessions that hold you accountable?

  • Focus Area: Some workshops concentrate on political satire, while others approach satire in fiction, essays, or humor writing broadly.

  • Instructor Expertise: Learn from published satirists, humorists, or writing coaches with strong records in satirical content.

  • Community and Critique Opportunities: A vibrant writers group that shares meaningful feedback can be enhance growth immensely.

Tips for Making the Most Out of Your Workshop Experience

To get the best bang for your buck:

  1. Engage Actively: Participate in discussions, submit all assignments, and approach feedback with an open mind.

  2. Read Widely: Complement the course with reading different satirical styles across eras and media.

  3. Write Regularly: Practice outside of assignments to build your own voice and experiment with tone and subjects.

  4. Network: Use the community as a resource to exchange ideas, co-write projects, or collaborate on satirical content.

  5. Apply Your Skills Publicly: Try publishing your work on blogs or social media to test its impact and reach.

What Kind of Satire Can You Write After Training?

By completing these workshops, you’ll gain a toolkit that enables you to craft a variety of satirical formats, including:

  • Satirical Essays and Articles: Witty critiques of current events, cultural norms, or political decisions.

  • Short Stories: Using exaggerated characters and scenarios to reveal truths about societal dynamics.

  • Screenplays and Scripts: Satirical dialogues and scenes that work well for video, podcasts, or stage.

  • Comic Strips and Memes: Concise, humorous commentary suited to digital audiences.

  • Social Media Content: Crafting posts that use irony and parody to engage and inform followers.

The Cultural Impact of Satire in Modern Media

Satire today permeates all forms of media – from late-night comedy shows and cartoons to viral twitter accounts and satirical news websites. Participating in a workshop not only builds your technical skills but helps you understand satire’s evolving role in shaping public discourse.

By developing a nuanced approach, satirists walk a tightrope between humor and offense, commentary, and alienation. These workshops teach you how to wield satire responsibly and effectively, aligning humor with insight rather than mere shock value.

Final Thoughts: The Journey of Becoming a Skilled Satirist

Satire writing isn’t just about making others laugh – it’s about provoking thought, flipping perspectives, and inspiring change through clever storytelling. Online satire writing workshops invite you into a world where humor and critique unite to form powerful narratives that resonate deeply.

Whether you want to dip your toes or dive headfirst into the satire ocean, exploring these workshops can be the catalyst for a highly rewarding creative endeavor. The digital landscape offers unprecedented opportunities to share your voice widely — all you need is the skill and confidence to craft your message sharply and smartly.

Ready to sharpen your pen, challenge convention with wit, and join a community passionate about satirical craft? The right online workshop awaits to help you bring your freshest, funniest, and most fearless ideas to life.

Additional Resources for Aspiring Satirists

  • Books: The Satirist: America’s Most Insulting Newspaper by Peter H. Schuck, Satire: A Critical Reintroduction by Dustin Griffin.

  • Websites: The Onion, McSweeney’s Internet Tendency, and Reductress for modern satire inspiration.

  • Podcasts: The Bugle, Wait Wait… Don’t Tell Me! often blend satire and news humor.

Experiment, observe, and most importantly, enjoy the craft of turning society’s quirks into captivating satire!
