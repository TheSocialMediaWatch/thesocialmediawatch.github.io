How to Find Your Fantasy Writing Voice


Writing fantasy is an exhilarating endeavor. It’s a genre filled with limitless possibilities, where the laws of reality are bent, rewritten, or discarded entirely. Yet for all its creative freedom, writing fantasy comes with unique challenges—particularly when it comes to finding your personal writing voice. Voice is the heartbeat of your work; it’s the stylistic essence that makes your writing memorable. In fantasy, your voice does more than tell a story—it shapes how readers escape into your world. But how do you find that elusive writing voice amid a sea of genre expectations, narrative conventions, and diverse inspirations?

Finding your fantasy writing voice is not something that happens overnight. It’s an evolving process rooted in self-discovery, experimentation, and a deep understanding of your craft. Below, we’ll explore practical steps and techniques to help you uncover your unique fantasy voice while honing your ability to craft immersive and captivating worlds.

1. Understand What Writing Voice Means

Before diving into self-discovery, it’s important to clarify what writing voice really is. Voice is not the same as tone or style, though these concepts are intertwined. Think of tone as the mood or emotional stance of your story, and style as the specific techniques you use to shape your prose—such as word choice, sentence structure, and figurative language. Voice, however, is your distinctive fingerprint on the page. It’s that intangible quality that separates Tolkien’s lyrical grandeur from George R.R. Martin’s gritty realism or N.K. Jemisin’s vibrant cultural narratives.

Your fantasy voice should authentically reflect who you are as a writer, while aligning with the visions of the worlds you wish to create. It’s about connecting with your readers in a way that feels fresh, sincere, and deeply rooted in the curiosities and passions that drive your imagination.

2. Discover Your Influences

Every writer starts by emulating others. Whether you draw inspiration from epic fantasy, urban fantasy, historical mythologies, or surrealist tales, your influences will shape the foundation of your voice. Think about the authors whose work excites and resonates with you. Do you admire Brandon Sanderson’s meticulous magic systems, Leigh Bardugo’s lush character dynamics, or Ursula K. Le Guin’s philosophical prose? Are you drawn to sprawling world-building, intimate character arcs, or poetic storytelling?

Rather than imitating your favorite authors outright, study their techniques. What makes their voice compelling? Is it their ability to craft relatable characters, their innovative plots, or their mastery of rhythm and pacing? Incorporate elements that speak to you while exploring ways to make them your own. This process not only helps refine your technical skills but also highlights aspects of writing that resonate most with your creative instincts.

3. Identify Your Passions and Themes

Central to your voice is the core of what drives your storytelling. Fantasy offers endless thematic possibilities, but your work will gain depth and authenticity when rooted in themes that genuinely matter to you. What ideas do you find yourself returning to? Are you fascinated by moral ambiguity, the dynamics of power, the clash between tradition and change, or the resilience of hope? The themes that compel you will infuse your writing with a personal, emotional truth that shapes your voice.

Additionally, think about the elements of fantasy that inspire you most. Are you captivated by ancient prophecies, complex political intrigue, magical creatures, or enigmatic landscapes? Your passions will naturally steer your creative choices—helping you discover the essence of your fantasy voice in the process.

4. Freewrite and Experiment

Voice emerges through practice. To find your voice, write freely without overthinking. Set aside perfectionism and pressure to mimic other writers. Focus instead on exploring what comes naturally to you. Consider experimenting with different points of view, writing styles, or narrative structures. Test out first-person perspectives to emphasize intimacy, or third-person omniscient to achieve epic scope. Write a short story told entirely as a diary entry or a lengthy passage narrated by a mythical creature. Break conventions and explore techniques outside your comfort zone.

Freewriting can feel messy and chaotic, but it’s this very lack of restraint that allows your authentic voice to surface. Keep what resonates and discard what feels forced. Over time, patterns will emerge in your writing that reveal a style or tone that feels uniquely yours.

5. Immerse Yourself in the Craft of World-Building

Fantasy thrives on world-building, and how you construct your worlds can play a huge role in solidifying your voice. Are your worlds steeped in intricate cultural histories? Are they surreal landscapes dictated by emotional logic, or do they adhere to crystalline rules of magic and science? World-building is not simply a list of details; it’s an exercise in revealing how you, as a writer, perceive possibility. Your personal voice will emerge through the way you prioritize elements of your setting and integrate them into your narrative.

While world-building, focus on sensory immersion—how your characters and readers experience the story’s environment. Do you lean toward sumptuous descriptions of the natural world, or are you drawn to jarring contrasts between the mystical and the mundane? Infusing detail with your individual perspective can transform even familiar tropes into something entirely your own.

6. Pay Attention to Dialogue

Dialogue is one of the most revealing aspects of voice. In fantasy, how characters speak often reflects both the world you’ve created and your individual storytelling style. For instance, does your dialogue weave formal, almost theatrical exchanges that emphasize tradition and hierarchy? Or do your characters speak in modern, casual tones that ground fantastical elements in relatable human experiences?

Explore how your characters’ personalities and values shape their speech. Their voices should carry the rhythm, syntax, and nuances of the world they inhabit. The way you approach dialogue—whether through sharp wit, lyrical mysticism, or biting realism—will be a cornerstone of your fantasy voice.

7. Embrace Trial and Error

Finding your voice is not a straight path. You may find early drafts littered with inconsistencies, moments of brilliance alongside passages that ring hollow. Think of this process as calibrating a compass—you’re determining the direction that feels most true to your identity as a writer.

Allow room for failure as you refine your voice. Read your work aloud to hear how it sounds—does it resonate, or does it feel disconnected from the identity you’re aiming for? Share drafts with trusted beta readers for feedback on tone, style, and emotional impact. Pay attention to what aspects of your writing linger with readers, and use their insights to guide your development.

8. Write What You Care About

Perhaps the simplest piece of advice on finding your fantasy writing voice is to write the story you care about most deeply. Voice develops when your writing is infused with passion and sincerity. Whether you’re crafting a story of lost gods, forgotten kingdoms, or ordinary lives touched by magic, the best way to discover your voice is to let your creativity be governed by what you love.

Fantasy invites you to step into the extraordinary, but your voice will shine most brightly if it carries fragments of yourself into those extraordinary worlds. Emotion, curiosity, and dedication are what ultimately shape an unforgettable writing voice.

Conclusion

Finding your fantasy writing voice is a journey, not a destination. It’s shaped by your influences, passions, experimentation, and connection to the genre’s boundless potential. Most importantly, it’s a process that thrives on authenticity—an exploration of how your perspective as a writer transforms the fantastical into something uniquely your own.

Take your time, embrace the freedom of discovery, and stay true to the stories that excite you. Along the way, you’ll uncover a voice that not only captivates readers but also reflects the powerful realms of imagination your work embodies.
