The Best Cartoon Shows of the Decade You Shouldn't Miss


Over the past decade, the landscape of animated television has undergone a remarkable transformation. From deep storytelling to stunning visual design and character development, the best cartoon shows of the last ten years have captivated audiences of all ages. Whether you’re a fan of traditional 2D animation, cutting-edge 3D CGI, or a mix of both, there’s something in the world of animation that caters to your preferences. In this post, we’ll explore the best cartoon shows of the decade that you absolutely shouldn’t miss. From quirky comedies to emotional dramas, these shows have redefined animation and left a lasting impact on pop culture.

1. BoJack Horseman (2014 - 2020)

A critical darling and often hailed as one of the most intelligent animated shows ever made, BoJack Horseman is a blend of dark comedy and profound psychological exploration. The series follows BoJack, a washed-up ’90s TV star who also happens to be a talking horse, as he navigates his struggles with addiction, relationships, and self-worth. Set in a world populated by both humans and anthropomorphic animals, BoJack Horseman tackles a variety of issues, from fame and mental health to the complexities of personal redemption. The show’s stunning animation, witty humor, and deeply emotional storytelling made it a standout in the 2010s.

The series isn’t just funny—it’s heart-wrenching, and it will likely leave you thinking long after the credits roll. If you haven’t seen it yet, BoJack Horseman is definitely one of those must-watch cartoons that’s perfect for adults who crave meaningful animated content.

2. Rick and Morty (2013 - present)

Rick and Morty quickly became a cultural phenomenon, and it’s easy to see why. This irreverent science fiction sitcom, created by Justin Roiland and Dan Harmon, centers around the misadventures of Rick Sanchez, a mad scientist, and his easily impressionable grandson, Morty. Their interdimensional travels lead them to encounter strange alien species, alternate realities, and wild situations that challenge both their intellects and their relationships.

The show is packed with sharp wit, philosophical musings, and endless pop culture references. Its narrative structure is often non-linear, making each episode feel fresh and unpredictable. While the humor is dark and occasionally crude, Rick and Morty stands out for its clever storytelling and its ability to blend the absurd with moments of surprising emotional depth. If you’re looking for a show that pushes the boundaries of animated comedy, this one is a must-watch.

3. Avatar: The Last Airbender (2005 - 2008)

While Avatar: The Last Airbender technically aired in the mid-2000s, it remains one of the most beloved animated series well into the 2010s and beyond. Set in a world where certain individuals can control the four elements—water, earth, fire, and air—Avatar follows the journey of Aang, the last Airbender and the Avatar, who is tasked with bringing balance to the world. Along the way, he is joined by a group of friends, including the fierce Katara, the loyal Sokka, and the brooding Prince Zuko.

What makes Avatar stand out is its exceptional world-building, multi-layered characters, and deeply engaging plotlines. The series blends adventure with spiritual exploration, offering lessons on friendship, destiny, and the nature of conflict. Though aimed at younger audiences, Avatar: The Last Airbender is a series that resonates with viewers of all ages. Its legacy continues through the recently released The Legend of Korra and various comics and books.

4. Steven Universe (2013 - 2020)

Steven Universe broke new ground in animated television with its inclusive storytelling, diverse characters, and heartwarming themes. Created by Rebecca Sugar, the show follows Steven, a young boy who is half-human and half-Gem (a race of magical beings), as he embarks on adventures with the Crystal Gems—three powerful female warriors who protect Earth.

At its core, Steven Universe is a coming-of-age story that explores identity, love, and the complexities of family. Its groundbreaking representation of LGBTQ+ characters and themes of self-acceptance have made it a trailblazer in the animation world. The show also boasts vibrant animation, fantastic music, and an intricate plot that unfolds over time. If you’re looking for a series that is as emotionally resonant as it is visually stunning, Steven Universe is a must-watch.

5. Adventure Time (2010 - 2018)

Adventure Time is a show that, on the surface, may appear to be a whimsical, colorful tale of a boy and his dog embarking on wacky adventures. However, beneath its playful exterior lies a rich, layered story filled with themes of friendship, loss, and self-discovery. Created by Pendleton Ward, the series follows Finn the Human and Jake the Dog as they explore the post-apocalyptic land of Ooo, meeting a variety of bizarre creatures and eccentric characters along the way.

Over its run, Adventure Time evolved into one of the most unique animated series of the decade, with an unforgettable mix of absurd humor and poignant moments. The show’s innovative animation, coupled with its deep emotional resonance, set it apart from other cartoons aimed at younger audiences. If you haven’t seen it yet, Adventure Time is a show that will appeal to viewers of all ages.

6. The Owl House (2020 - 2023)

In the world of animation, few shows have made as big an impact in such a short amount of time as The Owl House. Created by Dana Terrace, this series follows Luz, a teenage girl who stumbles upon a portal to another world and befriends a rebellious witch named Eda and a tiny demon named King. Together, they embark on a journey through the magical Boiling Isles, where Luz learns about magic and discovers her true potential.

What sets The Owl House apart from other cartoons is its deep exploration of identity, mental health, and personal growth. With its diverse characters and strong LGBTQ+ representation, the show has become a favorite among fans looking for inclusive, thought-provoking animation. The series also combines elements of dark fantasy with humor and adventure, making it a perfect balance of light-heartedness and depth.

7. Big Mouth (2017 - present)

If you’re in the mood for a more mature animated series, Big Mouth is a hilarious and unapologetic exploration of adolescence. Created by Nick Kroll, Andrew Goldberg, Mark Levin, and Jennifer Flackett, the show follows a group of pre-teens as they navigate the awkward, embarrassing, and often confusing stages of puberty. The show is filled with raunchy humor and outrageous situations, but it’s also surprisingly thoughtful when it comes to addressing topics like body image, sexuality, and mental health.

While its humor might not be for everyone, Big Mouth stands out for its bold approach to talking about issues that many animated shows shy away from. Its exaggerated characters, including hormone monsters and anxiety mosquitos, provide a unique lens through which to view the challenges of growing up. If you’re looking for a show that doesn’t pull any punches and brings a fresh perspective on adolescence, Big Mouth is definitely worth checking out.

8. Gravity Falls (2012 - 2016)

A sleeper hit among animated series, Gravity Falls is a treasure trove of mystery, adventure, and humor. Created by Alex Hirsch, the show follows twins Dipper and Mabel Pines as they spend the summer with their eccentric great-uncle Stan in the small, strange town of Gravity Falls. As they investigate the town’s bizarre occurrences, they uncover secrets that point to a much larger supernatural conspiracy.

What makes Gravity Falls so special is its ability to blend mystery with heart, humor with horror, and nostalgia with fresh ideas. The show’s attention to detail, hidden clues, and intricate plotlines make it a show that rewards viewers who pay close attention. Additionally, its heartfelt moments and strong character development ensure that it resonates with both kids and adults alike. If you’re a fan of puzzles, adventure, and quirky characters, Gravity Falls is a must-watch.

9. Final Space (2018 - 2021)

Combining space opera with sharp humor and emotional drama, Final Space is an animated series that became a cult favorite over its three-season run. Created by Olan Rogers, the show follows Gary Goodspeed, a bumbling astronaut who forms an unlikely bond with a mysterious alien creature named Mooncake. Together, they embark on a perilous journey through space to stop an evil force known as the Lord Commander.

What sets Final Space apart is its mix of epic sci-fi adventure with deep emotional stakes. The series delves into themes of loneliness, sacrifice, and the search for purpose, all while delivering thrilling action and laughs. Its stunning animation and well-developed characters make it one of the standout animated shows of the decade. If you’re a fan of space adventures with heart, Final Space is definitely a show to add to your list.

10. Tuca & Bertie (2019 - present)

If you’re a fan of quirky, offbeat animation, Tuca & Bertie is a hidden gem that you need to check out. Created by Lisa Hanawalt, the series follows two anthropomorphic birds—Tuca, a free-spirited toucan, and Bertie, a more anxious and reserved songbird—as they navigate life in a vibrant, surreal world. The show tackles adult themes like relationships, mental health, and self-discovery, all while maintaining a unique, surreal style that sets it apart from other animated series.

Though it may have flown under the radar for some, Tuca & Bertie is a bold and refreshing take on the animated genre. With its striking animation, rich character development, and deep exploration of life’s complexities, this show offers something truly special. Whether you’re looking for humor, heart, or a little bit of both, Tuca & Bertie is definitely a series that deserves more recognition.

Conclusion

The past decade has been a golden age for animated television, with a wide range of shows pushing the boundaries of what cartoons can achieve. From dark comedies to heartwarming adventures, the best cartoon shows of the decade have provided both entertainment and meaningful storytelling for audiences of all ages. Whether you’re in the mood for a deep psychological exploration or a light-hearted romp through fantastical worlds, there’s a show for you. So, grab some popcorn and dive into these must-watch animated series—you won’t regret it!
