Cartoons You Should Watch Before You Die


Animation isn’t just for kids. Behind the vibrant colors and zany characters, cartoons are packed with clever writing, powerful storytelling, and moments that stay with you much longer than the credits. Whether you grew up waking up early on Saturdays or caught your favorite series on late-night reruns, these shows are more than entertainment—they’re cultural touchstones.

There is genuine artistry in animation. It’s where childhood nostalgia meets smart humor, social commentary, and breathtaking creativity. In this curated list, you’ll find a journey through the most influential, innovative, and downright delightful cartoons you have to see before you clock out. So grab your remote (or your streaming app), and get ready to dive deep into the best cartoons of all time.

1. Avatar: The Last Airbender

When it comes to epic storytelling, Avatar: The Last Airbender stands tall. This groundbreaking Nickelodeon show redefined what animated series could accomplish. With richly developed characters, a tight narrative arc, action sequences, and a world inspired by East Asian mythology, Avatar is much more than a children’s show—it’s an odyssey.

Why you shouldn’t miss it:

  • Deep themes: War, honor, redemption, and growth are explored through every episode.
  • Unforgettable characters: From Zuko’s redemption arc to Uncle Iroh’s wisdom, these characters grow and evolve in realistic ways.
  • Gorgeous animation: The elemental bending scenes are as breathtaking now as when they first aired.

If you’re new to the series, prepare for laughter, tears, and many moments where you’ll pause just to reflect.

2. Bojack Horseman

On the surface, it’s a comedy about a washed-up sitcom star who just happens to be a horse. Peel back the layers, though, and you find one of the most emotionally raw and philosophical shows ever animated.

Key reasons to watch:

  • Dark humor sharpened by tragedy: Bojack Horseman doesn’t shy away from depression, addiction, and identity crises.
  • Complex characters: There’s real journey here—no one stays static, and actions have consequences.
  • Stunning visual metaphors: It isn’t afraid to use animation in surreal, poetic ways to convey internal turmoil.

This is the cartoon equivalent of a gut punch—and a must-watch for anyone who values meaningful stories.

3. Adventure Time

Cartoon Network’s Adventure Time changed the landscape of modern cartoons. Finn and Jake’s travels through the Land of Ooo mix surreal adventure with unexpectedly deep lore.

Highlights:

  • Absurdist humor and charm: The show bounces between silliness and profound themes.
  • Creative world-building: Each character, from the Ice King to Marceline, is memorable and unique.
  • Genuine emotion: The show’s episodes about friendship, loss, and personal growth frequently cut through the wackiness.

There’s a reason Adventure Time is consistently cited as a cult favorite among both kids and adults.

4. Batman: The Animated Series

Gotham has never looked this moody. With its neo-noir aesthetic and mature narratives, Batman: The Animated Series asserted that cartoons could nail atmosphere and pathos.

Noteworthy aspects:

  • Stylish direction: Its art deco influences and shadowy visuals became iconic.
  • A perfect Joker: Mark Hamill’s legendary voicework is a benchmark for villains.
  • Smart storytelling: This isn’t just a superhero cartoon; it’s episodic drama with tight plotting.

It’s a must-see for comic book fans and anyone who believes animation can do justice to classic American icons.

5. The Simpsons

When pop culture mocks, celebrates, or laments itself, it does so through The Simpsons. For decades, Springfield has been a satirical mirror to American life, politics, and family dysfunction.

Legacy factors:

  • Omnipresent references: Its gags, quotes, and predictions have permeated every facet of pop culture.
  • Generational humor: Both broad slapstick and cerebral jokes land effortlessly.
  • Enduring characters: From Homer’s bumbling to Lisa’s earnestness, the Simpson family remains relatable and fresh.

There’s no animation canon without The Simpsons—it’s a rite of passage for any cartoon aficionado.

6. Rick and Morty

Irreverent, hilarious, and deeply existential, Rick and Morty is the show for anyone who loves their mind-bending sci-fi with a healthy dose of nihilism.

Why it’s a classic:

  • Uncompromising intelligence: References to science, philosophy, and pop culture abound.
  • Creative chaos: Each episode takes bizarre risks—often with jaw-dropping results.
  • Emotional core: Despite its cynicism, there’s vulnerability at the center, delivered in surprisingly tender moments.

If you want a challenge for the brain and the funny bone, look no further.

7. Gravity Falls

Alex Hirsch’s Gravity Falls is what happens when you combine small-town mystery with supernatural intrigue and impeccable comedic timing. Mabel and Dipper Pines’ summer adventures are equal parts hilarious and haunting.

Best features:

  • Ongoing mysteries: Hidden codes, cryptography, and ongoing story arcs reward real engagement.
  • Wholesome humor: Family-friendly but never condescending.
  • Gorgeous animation: The Pacific Northwest vibes are gorgeously rendered.

It’s the rare cartoon that both kids and adults can watch—over and over again.

8. SpongeBob SquarePants

You’d be hard-pressed to find someone who hasn’t absorbed any SpongeBob meme, quote, or song. Beneath Bikini Bottom’s absurdity, there’s a contagious optimism.

Why It Endures:

  • Zany premise: No one expected a sea sponge in pants would change the face of cartoons.
  • All-ages appeal: The surreal humor is layered for both adults and children.
  • Endless quotability: “Is Mayonnaise an instrument?” Classic.

Sit down with a Krabby Patty and embrace the nonsense.

9. Samurai Jack

A minimalist masterpiece, Samurai Jack is art house filmmaking disguised as a Saturday morning cartoon.

What sets it apart:

  • Cinematic visuals: Wide-shot landscapes, evocative silence—it treats each episode like a living painting.
  • Epic quest: The titular samurai’s battle against Aku is classic heroic storytelling.
  • Inventive action sequences: Every sword fight is a study in style and choreography.

Simply put, it raises animation’s bar.

10. Over the Garden Wall

This singular mini-series is a piece of animated poetry. It’s haunting, heartfelt, and beautifully crafted.

Essentials:

  • Folktale atmosphere: Autumn vibes, Americana, and fairy-tale dread combine.
  • Lovingly oddball: The blend of music and tone makes for a one-of-a-kind experience.
  • Compact and powerful: Every episode matters, and the emotional payoff is huge.

If you want to watch something short and unforgettable—start here.

11. Futurama

From the creators of The Simpsons, Futurama is a sci-fi lover’s dream. Packed with clever gags, touching moments, and inventive narratives, there’s nothing else quite like it.

Reasons to tune in:

  • Impossibly smart jokes: Math, physics, and classic film references abound.
  • Wacky ensemble cast: From Bender’s chaos to Leela’s heart, every character shines.
  • Poignant storytelling: Episodes like “Jurassic Bark” and “The Luck of the Fryrish” are tearjerkers.

It’s proof that even robots and mutants can make you cry.

12. Steven Universe

Steven Universe broke ground for queer representation and sensitive storytelling in children’s TV.

Why it resonates:

  • Progressive themes: Love, identity, acceptance—all handled thoughtfully.
  • A stellar soundtrack: The music isn’t just catchy; it’s narratively meaningful.
  • Evolving narrative: Long-term character arcs give emotional heft.

It’s the rare show that makes you feel good watching it—no matter your age.

13. The Flintstones

Before any of the above, there was The Flintstones. Often described as “The Honeymooners with dinosaurs,” it’s the prototype for suburban sitcoms in animation.

Significance:

  • Cultural milestone: First prime-time American animated series.
  • Lasting catchphrases: “Yabba Dabba Doo!” needs no further explanation.
  • Timeless slapstick: Kids and adults alike can jump in and laugh.

This is the stone-age family that started it all.

14. Courage the Cowardly Dog

Equal parts unsettling and hilarious, Courage the Cowardly Dog is Cartoon Network’s ode to weirdness.

Unique strengths:

  • Surreal horror: Scary but always clever, often riffing on urban legends and classic myths.
  • Empathy in odd places: A pink dog with anxiety steals your heart.
  • Inventive villains: There’s nothing stranger than Katz or the Freaky Fred episode.

If you like your cartoons with a side of the surreal, Courage should be on your watchlist.

15. DuckTales (1987 and 2017)

Both versions of DuckTales are worth your time—for slightly different reasons.

Why they matter:

  • Adventurous spirit: Treasure hunts, mysteries, and globetrotting fun.
  • Generational appeal: The reboot gives a modern spin and deeper character arcs.
  • One of the finest theme songs ever created.

Go ahead. Try not to sing along—impossible.

16. Animaniacs

Animaniacs is what happens when animation meets improv theater and classic vaudeville.

Best bits:

  • Fully loaded comedy: Parodies, political jokes, musicals—all in a single episode.
  • Easter eggs galore: Adult viewers always find another hidden gag.
  • The indelible trio: Yakko, Wakko, and Dot are cartoon legends.

This one is an absolute masterclass in comedic timing and writing.

17. Teen Titans

Dark, stylish, and packed with emotional stakes, Teen Titans nailed both superhero spectacle and coming-of-age drama.

Definitive qualities:

  • Serialized storytelling: Solid villains, multi-episode arcs, genuine character development.
  • Laugh-out-loud humor: It balances the serious with goofy one-liners.
  • Iconic opening theme: Admit it. You’re singing it now.

The Titans went where few comic book cartoons dared.

18. South Park

Crude, biting, and unafraid—South Park is still poking at cultural nerve endings decades later.

Reasons to watch:

  • Relentless satire: Current events, celebrities, and politics all get shredded.
  • Unfiltered creativity: Set in motionless construction paper, the animation grew more sophisticated, but the edge stayed sharp.
  • Enduring influence: Whether you love or hate it, you can’t ignore it.

Get ready for social commentary with no brakes.

19. Bob’s Burgers

You could binge Bob’s Burgers just for the delightfully oddball Belcher family.

Lovable qualities:

  • Cozy comedy: There’s charm in every pun, song, and awkward silence.
  • Quirky characters: From Tina’s monotone delight to Linda’s theatrical outbursts.
  • Surprisingly poignant: For all its silliness, each episode hums with warmth.

It’s the sort of show that feels like home.

20. One Piece

Anime fans know the value of persistence. One Piece stands as the marathon cartoon everyone secretly wishes they could finish.

Why embark on this journey?:

  • Expansive world-building: The lore and geography rival any fantasy novel.
  • Underdog characters: Every Straw Hat Pirate comes with dreams, flashbacks, and loyalty.
  • Epic arcs: The payoff for sticking with it? Huge.

If you’re ready for a long adventure, set sail with Luffy and the gang.

The Infinite Why of Cartoons

Why do these cartoons matter so much? Because, at their best, animation elevates storytelling: it gives us room to dream bigger, to laugh a little harder, and to untangle the serious business of growing up—and staying weird.

Some of these series echo with nostalgia. Others help us see the world in new ways, breaking boundaries—and hearts—on the way. Whether you want escapism, enlightenment, or a little bit of both, there’s a cartoon here that will outlast one life, maybe two.

Tally up the shows you’ve seen. Mark the ones you’ve missed. There’s no better time to start watching than now. After all, the end credits are still a way off. Who knows what you’ll find if you hit play?
