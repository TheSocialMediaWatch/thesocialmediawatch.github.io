The Legal Side of Online Privacy for Children: COPPA and Parental Consent


In today’s digital age, children are more connected than ever before. With smartphones, tablets, and computers becoming an integral part of their lives, it is crucial to ensure their online privacy and protection. This is where the Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and parental consent come into play.

What is COPPA?

COPPA is a federal law enacted in 1998 in the United States, designed to protect the online privacy of children under the age of 13. The law requires websites and online services to obtain verifiable parental consent before collecting personal information from children. It also sets guidelines for how this information should be handled and protected.

The Importance of Parental Consent

Parental consent is a critical aspect of COPPA. It ensures that parents have control over their child’s online activities and the collection of their personal information. Websites and online services must obtain consent from a parent or guardian before collecting any personal data from children.

Obtaining verifiable parental consent involves various methods, such as providing a consent form that needs to be signed and returned, using a credit card verification process, or requiring a phone call with a trained operator. These measures are in place to confirm that the person granting consent is indeed a parent or legal guardian.

What is considered personal information?

Under COPPA, personal information includes any individually identifiable information that can be used to identify or contact a specific person. This includes but is not limited to:

  • Full name
  • Home address
  • Email address
  • Telephone number
  • Social security number
  • Geolocation data
  • Persistent identifiers (such as cookies or IP addresses)

Websites and online services must be transparent about the information they collect and how it will be used. They should have clear privacy policies that outline what data is collected, who has access to it, and how it is safeguarded.

The Role of Website Operators and App Developers

Website operators and app developers have a crucial role in complying with COPPA. They must implement proper privacy policies, obtain verifiable parental consent, and provide a safe environment for children to browse and interact online.

Here are some key responsibilities for website operators and app developers:

  1. Privacy Policies: Websites and apps must have clear and concise privacy policies that explain how personal information is collected, used, and protected.

  2. Verifiable Parental Consent: Before collecting any personal information from children, website operators and app developers must obtain verifiable parental consent through appropriate methods.

  3. Data Security: They must implement robust security measures to protect the personal information collected from children. This includes encryption, secure storage, and regular data audits.

  4. Age Verification: Website operators and app developers should put measures in place to ensure that users are accurately providing their age during the registration process.

  5. COPPA Safe Harbor Program: Some organizations participate in the COPPA Safe Harbor Program, which provides additional guidelines and oversight to ensure compliance with the law.

Penalties for Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with COPPA can result in severe penalties. The Federal Trade Commission (FTC) has the authority to impose fines of up to $42,530 per violation. In addition, non-compliant websites and apps may face reputational damage and loss of trust from their users.

Conclusion

Protecting the online privacy of children is a shared responsibility. COPPA and parental consent play a crucial role in ensuring that children’s personal information is safeguarded while they explore the digital world. Website operators, app developers, and parents must work together to create a safe and secure online environment for our youngest internet users.

Remember, as a parent or guardian, it is essential to stay informed about the websites and apps your child uses and to have open conversations about online privacy and safety. By doing so, we can empower our children to make responsible choices and enjoy the benefits of the digital world while protecting their privacy.
