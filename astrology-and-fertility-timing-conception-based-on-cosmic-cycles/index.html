Astrology and Fertility: Timing Conception Based on Cosmic Cycles


Astrology, an ancient practice with roots tracing back to civilizations like Babylonians, Mayans, and Chinese, holds fascination for us even today. Many people seek guidance and insights from astrology regarding crucial life events, such as tying the knot, changing jobs, or even having children. Interestingly, there is a strong belief among astrologers that timing conception based on cosmic cycles can enhance the chances of fertility and even the well-being of the baby. Let’s dive deep into the world of astrological alignment for reproductive success.

Traditional astrology divides the zodiac into four elements: fire, earth, air, and water. These elements combine with three modalities (cardinal, fixed, and mutable) to create the 12 zodiac signs (Aries, Taurus, Gemini, Cancer, Leo, Virgo, Libra, Scorpio, Sagittarius, Capricorn, Aquarius, and Pisces). Each zodiac sign rules specific body parts, according to astrological tradition, including reproductive organs and processes. In astrology, these associations have formed the basis for Timed to Conceive, an art of conceiving during the most favorable astrological alignments.

An Overview of Astrological Fertility

In astrology, both the Sun and Moon’s positions in a person’s natal chart can offer insights into their potential fertility and optimal conception timing. Additionally, the entire cosmic scene, including aspects between planets and during significant astrological transits, can impact fertility. While some of the effects of planetary alignments on fertility may not yet have substantial scientific backing, many people still rely on cosmic cycles to conceive and bring new life into the world.

Aligning Your Astrology To Conception Cycles

Astrologers and traditional practitioners believe that for optimal fertility, conception should occur during a period ruled by a beneficial combination of planets. This often refers to the most harmonious alignments of celestial bodies called “benefics,” which include Venus, Neptune, Pluto, and Jupiter.

Here are some guidelines to determine the best time for conception within your personal astrology:

  1. Check the “planetary hours” – These are 2-hour periods during each day designated by the dominant planet, with each day divided by the East-pointing horas from sunrise (Venus), sunrise plus 2 hours (Mercury), sunrise plus 4 hours (Venus), sunrise plus 6 hours (Mars), up to Arka-Merutunda, which is an hour added to the 24 hours’ cycle (Sun), to calculate these hours, you can use the sunrise and sunset times for your location. The 12-hour system calculates with Lagna (Ascendant) hours and the next 12 hours calculate with planetary Hours. During these periods, planetary energies are assumed to be more favorable for conception and birth.

  2. Consider your ascendant – The rising sign, also known as the ascending sign, in your natal chart influences your physical appearance, initial impressions, and, by some accounts, fertility. The strongest conception periods may coincide with your ascendant being ruled by the most beneficial planets.

  3. Be aware of astrological transits – Keep track of significant transits, especially those related to benefic planets and your personal astrological chart. Often, powerful and favorable transits coincide with ascendant shifts or signify a favorable time to align with positive influences for conception.

Additional Astrological Factors You Might Not Know

While considering the influences of specific celestial bodies, a few other factors that have potential significance in astrology when trying to conceive:

a. Moon phases- Moon’s cycles can be incredibly influential regarding tides, the human reproductive system, and even conception. Much like how the full moon symbolizes the peak of a woman’s fertility cycle, it also signifies peak ovulation. Given that astrologers believe the full moon can increase fertility, you may want to take advantage of this cycle.

b. Retrograde planets- When a planet appears to move backward in the sky, it’s called retrograde. Retrograde periods in astrology are often believed to heighten introspection, urgency, and purpose, increasing the intensity of the energies associated with that planet. For instance, if Venus is retrograde, it could be an ideal time for finding potential partners or deepening existing connections.

Conclusion

Astrology and fertility, both fascinating subjects, can be a powerful combination for those eager to improve their chances of conception. While conventional wisdom may argue against the science behind astrological alignments, many individuals have found inspiration and guidance in timing conception according to certain cosmic cycles.

This approach may not guarantee a successful conception, but there’s no harm in exploring alternative and empowering methods that can give you a sense of control over a situation that can be emotional and unpredictable. So go ahead, observe the skies and the cosmic dance of planets, and tap into the wisdom of an ancient art form to potentially enhance your chances of welcoming a new life into the world. Happy conceiving!
