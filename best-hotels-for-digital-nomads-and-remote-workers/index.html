Best Hotels for Digital Nomads and Remote Workers


For digital nomads and remote workers, the concept of “home” is ever-evolving. As technology enables us to work from virtually anywhere, finding accommodation that supports both productivity and relaxation has become essential. Hotels around the world have begun adapting to meet the needs of travelers who blend work and leisure. These accommodations offer a seamless combination of comfortable living spaces, high-speed internet, and environments that inspire creativity and focus. Whether you’re looking for vibrant co-working spaces, private sanctuaries for deep concentration, or communal areas to network with like-minded individuals, the right hotel can transform your work-travel experience.

Essential Features for Digital Nomads and Remote Workers

Before delving into the best hotels, it’s crucial to define the features that make a location truly suitable for remote work. While individual preferences will always vary, the following elements are universal essentials:

  • Reliable High-Speed Internet: A stable and fast Wi-Fi connection is non-negotiable for video calls, downloading large files, or using cloud-based tools.

  • Comfortable Workspaces: Whether it’s a designated desk in your room, a co-working area, or ergonomic seating in communal spaces, well-designed work environments make a significant difference.

  • Flexible Check-in and Check-out Options: Digital nomads often juggle multiple time zones, necessitating flexibility in their schedules.

  • Central Location with Access to Essentials: Proximity to restaurants, cafes, and supermarkets is important for convenience. Bonus points for hotels near major transportation hubs.

  • Community Atmosphere: For those who thrive on networking and collaboration, properties offering co-living or co-working setups can be ideal.

  • Affordable Long-term Stays: Many remote workers are on flexible schedules and limited budgets, so options for extended stays at discounted rates are highly sought after.

With these features in mind, here’s a curated list of some of the best hotels across the globe that cater to digital nomads and remote workers.

1. Selina, Global Locations

Selina is a brand that has reimagined the concept of hospitality for a new generation of mobile professionals. With vibrant locations spanning everywhere from Costa Rica and Mexico to Portugal and Israel, Selina offers an exceptional fusion of work and play. These hotels come equipped with co-working spaces, fast Wi-Fi throughout the premises, and regular events designed for networking. Rooms range from private accommodations to shared dorms, making Selina accessible to every traveler’s budget.

What sets Selina apart is its community-oriented approach. Whether it’s yoga classes, workshops, or group excursions, there’s always an opportunity to meet others with similar lifestyles. Remote workers will also appreciate the subscription-based “Selina CoLive” program, which provides discounts for those hopping between locations on long-term stays.

2. CitizenM Hotels, Major Global Cities

CitizenM has made a name for itself with its bold, modern design and tech-first approach. Located in major cities such as Amsterdam, New York, and Singapore, these hotels are ideal for digital nomads who prefer cosmopolitan settings. The rooms, although compact, are optimized for both productivity and relaxation, featuring luxurious beds, blackout blinds, and smart room controls via tablet.

The standout feature of CitizenM is its “Living Room” concept: expansive communal spaces designed for work, socializing, or simply relaxing. The high-speed Wi-Fi and ergonomic seating make it a perfect spot for remote workers who draw inspiration from bustling surroundings.

3. Outsite, Co-living Spaces Worldwide

Outsite is a hybrid of co-living and co-working, designed with remote workers in mind. With properties located in dreamy destinations such as Bali, Lisbon, and Santa Cruz, Outsite provides everything a digital nomad needs for an extended stay. Guests enjoy high-speed internet, ergonomic workstations, and a strong emphasis on community through organized meetups and dinners.

Outsite emphasizes environmental sustainability and cultural immersion, offering a more meaningful work-travel lifestyle. It’s an excellent option for those who thrive in serene, aesthetically pleasing environments and value connecting with like-minded individuals.

4. Zoku, Amsterdam and Copenhagen

Zoku redefines the standard hotel experience by blending the functionality of a home with the services of a hotel. Its innovative loft-style rooms are specifically designed for working professionals, featuring multi-purpose furniture, full kitchens, and dedicated workspaces. The hotels boast communal rooftops and shared lounges brimming with natural light, fostering creativity and collaboration.

Zoku’s philosophy revolves around community and well-being. Regular workshops, events, and networking opportunities are offered, making it a great choice for solo travelers looking to expand their professional and social circles. The extensive attention to detail in catering to remote workers has earned Zoku numerous accolades in the hospitality industry.

5. The Bali Bee House, Bali, Indonesia

Bali has become a hotspot for digital nomads over the years, and The Bali Bee House is a testament to the island’s growing coworking culture. Tucked away in a serene environment, this boutique hotel specializes in affordable yet functional spaces for remote workers. Private and shared rooms are available, all featuring rustic decor and access to high-speed Wi-Fi.

It’s not just the accommodation that makes The Bali Bee House stand out—the on-site co-working area is a hive of productivity, complete with comfortable seating and complimentary coffee. Outside of work hours, you can unwind with yoga classes, indulge in local culinary delights, or explore the lush surroundings.

6. Roam, Multiple Locations

Roam is another global brand that combines co-living and co-working with sophistication. Remote workers can choose from breathtaking locations such as Miami, Bali, and Kyoto. Rooms are designed with comfort and productivity in mind, often including en-suite bathrooms and ample desk space.

What’s unique about Roam is its membership model, which allows you to effortlessly move between locations. This flexibility is especially appealing for individuals who combine work with a passion for globetrotting. With beautifully designed communal kitchens, lounges, and workspaces, Roam emphasizes building a sense of community and belonging.

7. The Hoxton, Various Cities

For remote workers who enjoy a touch of luxury, The Hoxton is an excellent choice. Located in trendy neighborhoods in cities like London, Paris, and Chicago, these stylish hotels cater to creative professionals. Rooms are cozy yet functional, offering everything from plush bedding to curated bookshelves.

While the in-room desks are convenient, the real draw is The Hoxton’s open-house policy. Its ground-floor spaces include restaurants, cafes, and lounges brimming with contemporary charm, making them ideal for networking or catching up on work. The hotel frequently hosts events that attract local entrepreneurs and freelancers, providing additional opportunities for connection.

8. Selina Chelsea, New York City

City dwellers seeking inspiration amid the hustle and bustle of New York will find Selina Chelsea an ideal home base. This location offers a co-working space that’s perfect for professionals requiring a productive atmosphere during the day and vibrant nightlife options once the laptop is shut for the evening.

Selina Chelsea blends modern design with a creative community, offering weekly events such as art shows, live music, and wellness activities. Whether you’re in the city on business or carving out time to create your next big project, Selina Chelsea strikes an excellent balance of work and leisure.

9. Swissôtel, Berlin

For those who prioritize comfort and practicality, Swissôtel Berlin strikes a perfect balance. Situated in one of Europe’s most culturally rich cities, this hotel caters to remote workers with its luxurious yet efficient environments. The business-focused rooms come equipped with fast internet and comfortable desks, and their meeting rooms are available for those requiring a more professional setup.

Located in the heart of the city, Swissôtel offers easy access to local dining, markets, and sightseeing opportunities. The property’s gym and spa facilities provide a much-needed escape after long hours of work.

Final Thoughts

The rise of remote work has transformed the travel industry, leading to a surge in accommodations tailored for digital nomads and remote workers. Whether you’re seeking bustling city energy or tranquil rural retreats, these hotels offer a range of options to suit different preferences. By combining functional workspaces with social opportunities and luxurious amenities, these properties create spaces where productivity and leisure go hand in hand.

Finding a hotel that aligns with your work style and travel goals can elevate your remote work experience. Each destination on this list offers unique features and conveniences, ensuring you remain productive while enjoying the freedom of location-independent living. As more people embrace this lifestyle, the hotel industry continues to innovate—offering exciting opportunities for digital nomads to thrive wherever they go.
