10 Spirits You Didn't Know You Could Mix


When it comes to crafting cocktails, the world of spirits is vast and often overwhelming. While many people stick to the classics like vodka, gin, and rum, there is a treasure trove of unique spirits waiting to be explored. Mixing lesser-known spirits can elevate your cocktail game and surprise your palate. Here are ten spirits that you might not have thought to mix, along with some creative cocktail ideas to inspire your next gathering.

1. Pisco

Pisco, a South American brandy primarily produced in Peru and Chile, has a flavor profile that ranges from floral to fruity. This versatile spirit can be used in various cocktails beyond the classic Pisco Sour.

Cocktail Idea: Pisco Punch

Combine 2 oz Pisco, 1 oz fresh pineapple juice, ½ oz lime juice, and ½ oz simple syrup in a shaker with ice. Shake well and strain into a chilled glass. Garnish with a pineapple slice for a refreshing twist.

2. Amaro

Amaro is an Italian herbal liqueur that can range from bitter to sweet. While often consumed neat or on the rocks, it can add depth to cocktails.

Cocktail Idea: Amaro Mule

Mix 1.5 oz of your favorite Amaro with 3 oz ginger beer and a squeeze of lime. Serve over ice in a copper mug, and garnish with a lime wheel for a complex yet refreshing drink.

3. Aquavit

Aquavit, a Scandinavian spirit flavored with caraway, dill, and other herbs, is often overlooked in the cocktail world. Its bold flavors can enhance various mixers.

Cocktail Idea: Aquavit Sour

Shake 2 oz aquavit, 1 oz lemon juice, and ½ oz simple syrup with ice. Strain into a glass and garnish with a lemon twist for a zesty cocktail that showcases aquavit’s unique character.

4. Soju

Soju, a Korean distilled spirit, is known for its smoothness and versatility. It can be a fantastic substitute for vodka in cocktails.

Cocktail Idea: Soju Mojito

Muddle fresh mint leaves and lime wedges in a glass. Add 2 oz soju, 1 oz simple syrup, and top with soda water. Stir gently and garnish with a mint sprig for a refreshing twist on the classic mojito.

5. Mezcal

Mezcal, often confused with tequila, is a smoky agave spirit that can add a rich flavor to cocktails. Its complexity is perfect for adventurous drinkers.

Cocktail Idea: Smoky Paloma

In a shaker, combine 2 oz mezcal, 1 oz grapefruit juice, ½ oz lime juice, and ½ oz agave syrup. Shake and pour over ice in a salt-rimmed glass. Garnish with a grapefruit slice for a smoky twist on the traditional Paloma.

6. Baijiu

Baijiu, a potent Chinese spirit, is made from fermented grain and has a distinctive flavor profile that can be surprising in cocktails.

Cocktail Idea: Baijiu Fizz

Shake 1.5 oz baijiu, 1 oz lemon juice, and ½ oz honey syrup with ice. Strain into a glass and top with club soda. Garnish with a lemon wheel for a bubbly and unique drink.

7. Fernet

Fernet, a type of amaro, is known for its bitter and herbal flavors. While often consumed as a digestif, it can create interesting cocktails.

Cocktail Idea: Fernet & Cola

Simply mix 1.5 oz Fernet with 4 oz cola over ice. Stir gently and garnish with a lemon twist for a refreshing yet bold beverage that’s perfect for late-night gatherings.

8. Grappa

Grappa is an Italian pomace brandy that is often enjoyed as a digestif. However, it can work wonders in cocktails due to its intense flavors.

Cocktail Idea: Grappa Spritz

Combine 2 oz grappa, 3 oz Prosecco, and a splash of soda water in a glass filled with ice. Stir gently and garnish with an orange slice for a refreshing aperitif.

9. Raki

Raki, a traditional Turkish anise-flavored spirit, offers a unique taste that can create exceptional cocktails when paired with the right ingredients.

Cocktail Idea: Raki Sour

Shake 1.5 oz raki, 1 oz lemon juice, and ½ oz simple syrup with ice. Strain into a chilled glass and garnish with a sprig of mint for a fragrant and refreshing drink.

10. Cachaça

Cachaça, Brazil’s national spirit, is similar to rum but made from fresh sugarcane juice. Its sweetness can create delicious cocktails that transport you to the tropics.

Cocktail Idea: Cachaça Caipirinha

Muddle 1 lime cut into wedges with 2 tsp sugar in a glass. Add 2 oz cachaça and fill the glass with ice. Stir well and enjoy a taste of Brazil.

Conclusion

Exploring the world of mixology doesn’t have to be limited to the usual suspects. By incorporating these lesser-known spirits into your cocktails, you can surprise your guests and enhance your drinking experience. Whether you’re hosting a gathering or just looking to try something new, these unique combinations will elevate your cocktail repertoire and delight your taste buds. So, grab a bottle of one of these spirits and start mixing—your next favorite cocktail is just a shake away!
