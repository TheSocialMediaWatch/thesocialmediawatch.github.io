Preserving Antique Lace: Care and Display Tips


Antique lace pieces represent a rich chapter in the history of textile arts. They served as symbols of elegance, beauty, and refinement in various cultural contexts. Preserving lace for future generations requires the knowledge of proper care and display techniques. This comprehensive guide provides insightful tips that will help you protect your antique lace and showcase it in the best light.

Understanding the Nature of Lace

Lace has a delicate structure and composition, primarily made of silk or cotton threads, organized in intricate patterns. Its fragility demands caution and understanding to prevent damage or loss of value. Before we delve into care and display techniques, let’s take a brief look at lace materials and their features.

Lace can be categorized into two distinct types:

  1. Bobbin lace: Also known as bâte de Venise or Dutch lace, this method requires lace-makers to manipulate individual threads with bobbins, creating intricate patterns as the threads are interlaced.

  2. Lace-making machines: In the late 18th century, fulling mills converted silk and cotton fibers into lace, earning the name “Veniseed” or “guipure.” Machine-made lace is often less expensive and less valuable than bobbin lace.

Caring for Your Antique Lace

The preservation of antique lace requires a gentle touch and awareness of its vulnerabilities. Here are some essential tips to maintain the integrity and beauty of your precious lace pieces:

  1. Avoid Excessive Sunlight Exposure: Direct sunlight can cause lace to fade or weaken over time. To protect your pieces, keep them out of direct sunlight and avoid hanging them in places where they are exposed to natural lights for extended periods.

  2. Proper Storage: Keep your lace pieces in a cool, dry, and dark location, preferably in an archival-quality container or box. Avoid wooden boxes or chests, as their enemies can destroy or discolor your lace pieces. Use soft, lined containers that prevent friction and maintains the lace’s shape.

  3. Handle with Care: Always handle antique lace gently, using clean and gloved hands or specialized tissue to lift and support it. Pressing, pulling, or stretching the threads can cause irreversible damage.

  4. Humidity Control: High humidity can cause mold and mildew, damaging your lace pieces. Maintain a moderate humidity level (around 50%) to ward off moisture-related issues.

Displaying Your Antique Lace

A tasteful display of antique lace is equally important as its care and preservation. Here are some tips to showcase your lace pieces:

  1. Ensure Proper Framing: When framing antique lace, use a non-acidic, neutral pH matboard and sealing agent that will prevent or slow down any deterioration. It is equally vital to use UV protective glass to safeguard your lace against ultraviolet rays.

  2. Display Contextually: To emphasize the historical and cultural value of your antique lace pieces, consider incorporating them into relevant settings, such as themed rooms or exhibitions. You might display them alongside books or artworks referencing lace-making history or culture.

  3. Avoid Wall Clutter: While hanging your lace piece, keep the visual environment minimal. Do not overcrowd the space with other artifacts or decorations that may distract from the beauty of your lace.

  4. Proper Lighting: Although sunlight exposure must be avoided for preservation, lamps or other artificial light sources can help accentuate the luster, texture, and intricacy of your lace pieces. Ensure even, subtle, and flattering illumination that brings out the best in your lace.

The beauty of antique lace extends beyond its aesthetic value – it carries narratives of craftsmanship, elegance, and the historical and cultural significance of a bygone era. By following the care and display tips above, you maintain the integrity of your lace pieces and ensure that they are admired for generations to come.
