DIY Space Adventure: Crafting DIY Rocket Ship Bed


So, you want to create a space-themed room that will transport your child (or yourself) to the stars? Who doesn’t, right? An out-of-this-world idea that will leave you both in awe is a DIY rocket ship bed. By following the steps below, you’ll be able to build a stylish, fun, and one-of-a-kind bed fit for an astronaut-in-training.

Gathering Your Materials

Before we launch into this ambitious project, let’s collect the essential materials you’ll need:

  • A large piece of foam insulation board or a bouncy castle “living room” castle room
  • Four sturdy wooden beams or metal tubes (about 30 inches long each)
  • Three more wooden beams or metal tubes (about 20 inches long each)
  • Two large sections of plywood for the base of your rocket ship
  • Two smaller sections of plywood for the top of your rocket ship
  • Black or grey fabric for the “sides” of your rocket ship
  • White fabric for the “shades” of your rocket ship
  • Wheels, axles, and bearings (for wheels to spin freely)
  • Wooden spools for your rocket ship’s bases
  • A drill, saw, fabric glue, glue gun, sponge, paint, paintbrush, and a screwdriver

Designing Your Rocket Ship Bed

Before cutting or pasting anything, it’s essential to sketch out your design. After all, determination is the pillar of success. To create a detailed blueprint, think about the following elements:

  • The dimensions of your rocket ship (length, width, and height)
  • The shape of your rocket ship (cylindrical, cone-shaped, or a mix)
  • The number of wing-like flaps you want on the side
  • The location of windows, control panels, and other fun features

Building the Base

Once your design is ready, it’s time to bring it to life. Start by creating the base. Nail or screw the larger plywood sections together, ensuring stability. Attach the four sturdy beams or metal tubes to the corners of your base, using a drill to create holes and a screwdriver to secure them in place.

Creating the Ship’s Walls

Cut your foam insulation board into the desired shapes and sizes, following your sketch, to create the walls of your rocket ship. Use adhesive foam strips to attach these pieces together, forming a tube-like shape. Next, wrap your rocket ship’s frame with black or grey fabric, cutting it to size as you go. Tuck and secure the edges using fabric glue or a glue gun.

Adding the Rocket Ship’s Interior

Mask off your rocket ship using painter’s tape, and paint the interior a bright white color. Once the paint is dry, attach smaller sections of your plywood to the inside of the foam walls as shelves, play areas, or storage spaces. Don’t forget to add windows and doors!

Constructing the Top

Following your sketch, cut the smaller plywood pieces into the desired shapes for your rocket ship’s top half. Attach them to your build using glue or nails, and cover the whole piece with white fabric, trimming and tucking as necessary.

Adding Wheels and Finishing Touches

Screw wooden spools into your rocket ship’s base for stability, then attach the axles and your chosen wheels. Voila! Your DIY rocket ship bed is now complete.

To give your rocket ship bed the perfect finishing touch, take some time to add decals or paint details like the rocket’s engines, stickers, or inspirational quotes.

In your newly designed space-themed room, your child will fall asleep surrounded by their own cosmic dreamscape, eager to share their adventures with you in the morning.

Still not convinced about this out-of-this-world project? After all, a bedroom is a sacred place of rest and rejuvenation. Creating a rocket ship bed might just do the trick. So, take the plunge and join us on this adventure. The stars await!

Frequently Asked Questions

What if I don’t have a drill or saw?

Don’t worry! You can find affordable options at local hardware stores or even ask your neighbors for a hand. If all else fails, consider taking your project to a professional for assistance.

How much is this project going to cost?

That’s an excellent question! The costs will vary depending on materials, tools, and supplies you choose. On average, you can expect the costs to range anywhere from $200 to $800 or even more for the ultimate rocket ship experience.
