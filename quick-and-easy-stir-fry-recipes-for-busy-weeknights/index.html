Quick and Easy Stir-Fry Recipes for Busy Weeknights


Preparing dinner on busy weeknights can often feel like a daunting task. After a long day at work or taking care of various responsibilities, the last thing we want to do is spend hours in the kitchen. That’s where quick and easy stir-fry recipes come to the rescue! Not only are they delicious and packed with flavor, but they also require minimal effort and time. In this blog post, we will share some mouthwatering stir-fry recipes that you can whip up in no time.

Teriyaki Chicken Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets
  • 1 cup snap peas
  • 1/4 cup teriyaki sauce
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • Sesame seeds, for garnish (optional)
  • Green onions, chopped, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. In a large skillet or wok, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the chicken strips and cook until they are no longer pink in the center. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the bell peppers, broccoli, and snap peas. Cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the vegetables are crisp-tender.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the teriyaki sauce, soy sauce, honey, garlic, and ginger. Pour the sauce into the skillet with the vegetables and stir to combine.
  5. Return the cooked chicken to the skillet and toss everything together until well coated in the sauce.
  6. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the chicken is heated through.
  7. Remove from heat and garnish with sesame seeds and green onions, if desired.
  8. Serve the Teriyaki Chicken Stir-Fry over steamed rice or noodles for a complete and satisfying meal.

Garlic Shrimp and Vegetable Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cup sugar snap peas
  • 1 cup sliced carrots
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • Crushed red pepper flakes, to taste (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the shrimp to the skillet and cook until they turn pink and are opaque. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the bell peppers, sugar snap peas, carrots, and minced garlic. Cook for 3-4 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, and crushed red pepper flakes, if desired.
  5. Pour the sauce into the skillet with the vegetables and stir to combine.
  6. Return the cooked shrimp to the skillet and toss everything together until well coated in the sauce.
  7. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the shrimp are heated through.
  8. Remove from heat and garnish with fresh cilantro, if desired.
  9. Serve the Garlic Shrimp and Vegetable Stir-Fry over steamed rice or noodles for a delightful and satisfying meal.

Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound flank steak, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 3 cups broccoli florets
  • 1/4 cup low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons hoisin sauce
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • Toasted sesame seeds, for garnish (optional)
  • Sliced green onions, for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the sliced flank steak to the skillet and cook until browned on all sides. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the broccoli florets and cook for 3-4 minutes, or until they are crisp-tender.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, cornstarch, brown sugar, garlic, and ginger. Pour the sauce into the skillet with the broccoli and stir to combine.
  5. Return the cooked flank steak to the skillet and toss everything together until well coated in the sauce.
  6. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, or until the beef is heated through.
  7. Remove from heat and garnish with toasted sesame seeds and sliced green onions, if desired.
  8. Serve the Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry over steamed rice or noodles for a satisfying and flavorful meal.

We hope these quick and easy stir-fry recipes help you conquer busy weeknights with delicious and effortless meals. Feel free to experiment with different vegetables, proteins, and sauces to customize your stir-fry creations. Bon appétit!
