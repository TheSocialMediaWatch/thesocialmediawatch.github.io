10 Creative Ways to Use Old T-Shirts in Your Crafts


Chances are, there’s a pile of retired t-shirts lurking at the back of your closet. Maybe their graphics have faded, or their shape isn’t what it used to be. Before you toss them out or turn them into dust rags, consider giving those fabric friends a second chance at usefulness. Old t-shirts are incredibly versatile and perfect for craft projects—plus, upcycling them is a sustainable way to express your creativity. Here are ten inventive ways to transform old shirts into fresh, functional, and fabulous handmade treasures.

1. DIY T-Shirt Yarn

T-shirt yarn (sometimes called “tarn”) is a magical supply for knitters, crochet enthusiasts, and crafters who love playing with fabric textures.

How to make it:
Lay the t-shirt flat, smooth out the wrinkles, and cut across the body beneath the sleeves. Starting at the bottom hem, cut a continuous spiral strip about one inch wide. Gently tug the strip—jersey knits will curl at the edges, turning into a cord-like yarn perfect for braiding, crocheting, or knitting.

Project ideas with t-shirt yarn:

  • Crocheted baskets
  • Rug making
  • Plant hangers
  • Chunky necklaces

Using t-shirt yarn, you can whip up stylish, eco-friendly accessories in no time.

2. Colorful Braided Rugs

Those soft t-shirts are begging to be turned into plush, washable rugs that add flair to any room.

How to make it:
Cut the shirts into long strips and braid them together tightly. Once you have a lengthy braid, coil it into a spiral on a flat surface, stitching the layers together by hand or with a zigzag sewing machine stitch. Alter color combinations for unique patterns.

This project works best with cotton tees in complementary or contrasting colors. The result is a cozy rug full of personality and sentimentality.

3. Upcycled Tote Bags

A reliable tote bag is always handy, and making one from an old t-shirt is fast, easy, and requires zero sewing experience.

How to make a no-sew tote:

  • Cut off the sleeves and neckline to shape handles.
  • Determining the desired bag depth, cut slits across the bottom hem.
  • Tie the resulting fringe tightly for a sturdy, fringed bottom.

For tote bags with a smoother finish, stitch the bottom closed instead. Personalized with fabric paint or patches, these bags make excellent reusable shoppers or beach bags.

4. Cozy Throw Pillows

Transform graphic tees or bold colors you still love into decorative pillow covers.

Easy steps:

  • Lay out your t-shirt and cut two squares matching the size of your pillow insert, using the coolest parts of the shirt.
  • Sew three sides together, insert the pillow, and close it up by hand stitching or using an invisible zipper.

This upcycling project gives you functional, custom décor without a big investment. Bonus: sentimental shirts keep your favorite memories in sight.

5. Homemade T-Shirt Quilts

Whether it’s beloved band tees or sports shirts from your school days, memory quilts are a sentimental staple for upcycled t-shirt projects.

How to make it:
Cut each shirt into large squares or rectangles, stitch them onto fabric backing, and add a layer of batting for warmth. Bind the edges for a professional look. This project requires a bit of patience and sewing experience, but the outcome is a one-of-a-kind keepsake full of nostalgia.

T-shirt quilts are perfect for graduation gifts, dorm bedding, or anyone who wants to wrap themselves in memories.

6. Fashionable Infinity Scarves

Soft, drapey cotton works beautifully for scarves. T-shirts come in endless colors and prints, making these accessories truly unique.

How to make a t-shirt scarf:
Cut across the body of the shirt just under the arms and above the hem. Stretch the loop to encourage curling, then twist and layer it for a stylish infinity scarf. For more volume, knot together loops from multiple shirts.

This project is quick—think ten minutes, tops—and results in stylish, personalized neckwear for any season.

7. Eco-Friendly Produce Bags

Cut back on single-use plastic by creating reusable produce bags from lightweight t-shirts.

Instructions:

  • Cut the shirts into rectangles or squares.
  • Sew three sides together, add handles (or leave them as simple drawstring bags by threading a cord through the top hem).

Mesh or thin, airy shirts work best for produce bags. These help keep fruits and vegetables organized while you’re shopping and storing food at home.

8. Unique Wall Art Canvases

Showcase eye-catching graphics, logos, or patterns from old shirts as custom wall art.

How to do it:

  • Cut around your favorite shirt design with a couple of extra inches for wrapping.
  • Stretch the fabric over a wooden canvas or embroidery hoop and secure it at the back with staples or glue.

Arrange several canvases in a gallery wall style. Oversized logos and quirky prints become bold room accents—no brush required.

9. Pet Toys With Personality

Your pets will love chew toys or tug ropes made from repurposed t-shirts, and you’ll love not spending money on new ones.

How to craft them:
Cut multiple long strips, braid, and knot them into durable tug toys for dogs. For cats, sew a small pillow and fill with catnip, then sew shut.

Washable and made from familiar fabrics, these handmade toys are perfect for furry friends with sensitive noses or tummies.

10. Homemade Cleaning Cloths

Why buy expensive microfiber cloths when you already own the softest cleaning rags possible?

How to make t-shirt rags:
Cut up old shirts into squares or rectangles. Jersey cotton doesn’t fray, so you don’t have to finish the edges. Use these cloths for household cleaning, dusting, polishing, or even as reusable napkins for eco-friendly living.

Keep stacks in the kitchen, bathroom, or garage to significantly reduce your reliance on disposable paper towels.

Tips for Working With T-Shirts in Crafts

  • Pre-wash shirts: Remove residual dyes and softeners for better project results.
  • Select the right shirt type: 100% cotton works best for soft crafts; blends add stretch and durability.
  • Play with color: Mix shades and patterns for visual impact—think rainbow rugs, ombre scarves, or patchwork masterpieces.
  • Don’t worry about flaws: Stains or small holes can be trimmed away or camouflaged in many projects.

Conclusion

Old t-shirts are far more than memory-laden textiles—they’re a canvas for creativity and practical upcycling. Whether you want to decorate your home, accessorize your wardrobe, or keep your cleaning green, there’s a craft project here for you. Grab scissors, gather shirts, and let your imagination transform yesterday’s attire into tomorrow’s treasures.

Give these ideas a try and discover just how rewarding recycling can be—not just for you, but for the planet too. What will you make first?
