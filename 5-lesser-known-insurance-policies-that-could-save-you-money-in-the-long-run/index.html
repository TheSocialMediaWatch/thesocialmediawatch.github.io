5 Lesser-Known Insurance Policies That Could Save You Money in the Long Run


Do you ever feel like you’re missing out on potential savings by not having the right insurance policies? Well, you’re not alone. There are numerous lesser-known insurance policies that could save you money in the long run. In this blog post, we’ll explore five of these policies to help you make informed decisions about your insurance needs.

1. Identity Theft Insurance

Identity theft can cause significant damage to your financial wellbeing. Identity theft insurance can help protect you from financial losses that may occur due to identify theft. While most standard homeowners or renters insurance policies provide some coverage for financial losses resulting from identity theft, standalone policies often offer more comprehensive protection, and the Coverage varies widely.

2. Guaranteed Asset Protection (GAP) Insurance

Guaranteed Asset Protection (GAP) insurance is designed to cover the ‘gap’ between the value of your assets and the amount your insurance will pay out in the event of their loss or damage. This type of insurance is particularly valuable in case of total loss of your assets like theft or accidental damage. Imagine if your car is stolen or destroyed and the insurer only pays out the current market value, GAP insurance would cover the difference.

3. Pet Insurance

Pet insurance may seem like a luxury, but it’s quickly becoming more mainstream as people treat their pets as valued family members. Pet insurance could help you save money in case of unexpected medical bills for your furry loved ones. Various plans are available, and their prices vary based on the level of coverage and the age of your pet.

4. Mortgage Hazard Insurance

Mortgage hazard insurance, also known as mortgage life insurance, pays off the remainder of your mortgage in case of unexpected events like death, disability, or becoming unemployed. The insurance can be especially beneficial if you’re underwater on your mortgage or have a large amount still to pay.

5. Vacation Home Insurance

If you own a vacation home, you likely need specific insurance to cover your secondary property adequately. While your primary home’s insurance policy may include liability coverage, it might not protect you fully if something happens to your vacation home. You’ll need specialized policies to protect your vacation property, which could save you money in case of unexpected events, such as damage or a lawsuit.

Final Thoughts

Taking the time to explore lesser-known insurance policies could save you significant money in the long run. While they may not be as well-known as homeowners or renters insurance, identity theft, GAP, pet, mortgage hazard, and vacation home insurance policies provide critical coverage for various scenarios.

Don’t underestimate the impact these policies could have on your financial wellbeing. Instead, consider the value they may bring and decide whether these policies fit your insurance needs.
