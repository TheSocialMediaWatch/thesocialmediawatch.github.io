The Best Fantasy Video Games of the Decade


Fantasy video games have always held a special place in the hearts of players, offering mesmerizing worlds filled with mythical creatures, epic quests, and deep lore. Over the past decade, the genre has reached new heights, delivering unforgettable experiences that redefine storytelling, gameplay mechanics, and immersion. From sprawling open-world adventures to tightly-crafted narratives, the past ten years saw the emergence of titles that set a benchmark for the fantasy genre. Let’s explore the best fantasy video games of the decade—games that enriched the genre and captivated gamers worldwide.

1. The Enchantment of Open Worlds: The Rise of Iconic Fantasy Universes

Open-world design has transformed how players engage with fantasy games, allowing vast exploration and nuanced narratives that adapt to individual playstyles. Leading this trend, The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim emerged as one of the most influential and celebrated fantasy titles of the decade. Though initially released in 2011, its impact reverberated well into the following years through countless re-releases and an enduring modding community.

Set in the snow-capped region of Skyrim, players were drawn into a living, breathing world filled with dragons, warriors, and timeless lore. Its freedom of choice and endless customization made every playthrough feel entirely unique. The ability to craft your own story in a richly detailed environment ensured Skyrim remained an undisputed titan in fantasy gaming.

Similarly, The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt pushed the boundaries of open-world fantasy storytelling. Released in 2015, it delivered an emotionally gripping tale centered on Geralt of Rivia, a monster hunter searching for his surrogate daughter amidst political intrigue and supernatural threats. The rich narrative layers, complex characters, and morally ambiguous decisions set this title apart as a gold standard for role-playing games. Every quest, whether main or secondary, felt deeply personal, proving that side missions in fantasy games could be more than filler—they could be a vital part of the story experience.

2. Fantasy Games and Emotional Narrative: Unforgettable Stories

Fantasy video games excel at weaving deep, emotional narratives, and some of the best titles of the decade placed story front and center. Dragon Age: Inquisition, released in 2014, made waves by offering players a chance to lead the Inquisition and gather allies to save the world from catastrophic events. Its rich character development, interpersonal relationships, and choice-driven storytelling solidified its place among the decade’s finest.

Another standout was Divinity: Original Sin 2, which brought divine-level storytelling to the fantasy RPG space in 2017. With cooperative gameplay and an intricate, reactive world, it offered players unparalleled freedom to shape their journey. The diverse cast of characters, combined with a detailed narrative that balanced humor, intrigue, and emotional weight, showcased the strength of creativity within the fantasy genre.

Meanwhile, Hollow Knight, with its poignant exploration of the hauntingly beautiful world of Hallownest, proved that fantasy narratives don’t have to adhere to traditional forms. Its artistic design and subtle storytelling allowed players to uncover its mysteries at their own pace, cultivating a sense that exploration itself was part of the narrative experience.

3. The Growth of Dark Fantasy: Challenging Worlds and Gritty Themes

For players seeking grittier landscapes, the last decade delivered a wealth of dark fantasy titles that thrived on challenging mechanics and unforgiving worlds. Dark Souls III, a 2016 masterpiece, represented the pinnacle of FromSoftware’s iconic series. Its punishing yet rewarding gameplay and atmospheric design showcased dark fantasy at its finest. The interconnected environments, cryptic lore, and intense boss fights pulled players into a world brimming with despair but offering triumph for those who persevered.

Building on this legacy, Bloodborne, released in 2015, combined Victorian aesthetics and cosmic horror with dark fantasy. Its fast-paced combat and mysterious, unsettling atmosphere solidified it as one of the standout games of the decade. Players were left gripped by layers of cryptic storytelling that unraveled with each discovery, making it a fascinating and truly unique addition to the genre.

In the same vein, Sekiro: Shadows Die Twice reinvented fantasy by immersing players in a feudal Japan-inspired world filled with mystical creatures and brutal duels. Released in 2019, its intense combat mechanics encouraged precision and strategy, making victory feel deeply satisfying and earned.

4. Indie Innovations in Fantasy Gaming: Reshaping Worldbuilding

Indie developers continued to push the boundaries of creativity, delivering exceptional fantasy games that rivaled big-budget titles. Undertale, released in 2015, stood out for its unique approach to the RPG formula, allowing players to complete the game without resorting to violence. Its quirky characters, profound moral choices, and engaging world challenged conventions and showcased the potential for small-scale innovation within fantasy gaming.

Another indie gem, Hades, arrived in 2020 and set the gaming world ablaze with its addictive rogue-like gameplay and masterful storytelling. Weaving Greek mythology into its fast-paced dungeon-crawling mechanics, players found themselves invested not just in survival but in the relationships and struggles of the characters. The seamless blend of narrative and gameplay became one of its defining hallmarks, earning acclaim across the gaming community.

On a smaller scale, Gris offered an ethereal exploration of loss and hope through its breathtaking visuals and subtle fantasy elements. Its emotionally resonant storytelling complemented its gameplay, transforming the act of progression into a metaphorical journey through grief and healing.

5. The Revival of Multiplayer Fantasy Experiences

Multiplayer gaming in the fantasy genre also evolved significantly over the decade, pushing boundaries with ambitious entries. Final Fantasy XIV, despite a rocky original launch, underwent a revival with A Realm Reborn in 2013 and subsequent expansions like Shadowbringers and Endwalker. The game grew into one of the most successful MMORPGs of all time, offering players a communal experience filled with elaborate raids, exploring breathtaking worlds, and uncovering incredible fantasy narratives together.

Adding new twists to cooperative play, Monster Hunter: World redefined multiplayer fantasy in 2018, letting players team up to hunt sprawling, fantastical creatures. Combining strategy, skill, and teamwork, it created thrilling encounters that encouraged collaboration and immersive exploration. Its deep crafting systems and gorgeous environments made each hunt an unparalleled experience.

On the competitive side, League of Legends stood tall throughout the decade as the defining multiplayer fantasy arena. With a roster of elaborately designed heroes and an ever-expanding in-game world, it remained both a cultural phenomenon and a testament to the fantasy genre’s capacity to captivate audiences in the multiplayer space.

6. The Enduring Appeal of Fantasy: Closing Thoughts

The past decade has been a golden era for fantasy video games, with developers across the globe crafting remarkable experiences that continue shaping how players interact with fictional worlds. Whether it’s through sprawling open environments, meticulously crafted narratives, or boundary-pushing indie creations, the genre has revealed its ability to endlessly evolve while keeping its core appeal: the magic of escapism.

From the Nordic kingdoms of Skyrim to the haunting streets of Bloodborne, fantasy video games have allowed players to step into shoes of heroes, villains, and wanderers alike, engaging with worlds limited only by imagination. As the industry continues to grow and embrace new technologies, one can only anticipate what wonders the next decade will bring to this beloved genre.
