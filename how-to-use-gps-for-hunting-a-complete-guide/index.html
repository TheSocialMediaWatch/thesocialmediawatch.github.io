How to Use GPS for Hunting: A Complete Guide


GPS technology has revolutionized many outdoor activities, including hunting. Whether you’re a seasoned hunter or a beginner, incorporating GPS into your hunting strategy can drastically improve your efficiency, safety, and overall experience. In this guide, we will explore how GPS can enhance your hunting trip, how to use it effectively, and tips for making the most out of this technology in the wild.

What is GPS and Why Should Hunters Use It?

Global Positioning System (GPS) is a satellite-based navigation system that provides accurate location data anywhere on Earth. For hunters, GPS is an invaluable tool for navigation, tracking game, marking spots, and ensuring safety in the wild. By offering precise coordinates and allowing you to map out your hunting routes, GPS helps avoid getting lost and makes your hunting journey more organized.

Types of GPS Devices for Hunting

There are several GPS devices available, and selecting the right one for your hunting needs depends on factors like features, durability, and ease of use. Here are the most common types of GPS devices for hunting:

Handheld GPS Units

Handheld GPS units are compact, portable, and perfect for hunters who want a dedicated device to guide them through the wilderness. These devices come with built-in maps, compass functions, and waypoint marking capabilities. Popular models from brands like Garmin, Magellan, and Bushnell are often equipped with features like long battery life, waterproofing, and enhanced accuracy, making them ideal for outdoor activities such as hunting.

GPS Smartwatches

GPS smartwatches offer the convenience of tracking your location directly on your wrist. These watches are lightweight, easy to use, and can provide live navigation, fitness tracking, and communication features. While they may not have the advanced mapping features of dedicated handheld devices, they offer an excellent balance between portability and functionality for casual hunters.

Smartphone Apps

Many hunters now rely on smartphone apps that offer GPS navigation and hunting-specific features. Apps like OnX Hunt, Gaia GPS, and HuntStand provide detailed topographic maps, tracking capabilities, and features like waypoint marking. The convenience of using your phone as a GPS device makes it an accessible option for many hunters, but keep in mind that battery life and reception may be an issue in remote areas.

GPS Collars for Tracking Game

Another innovative use of GPS in hunting is with GPS collars, which are typically used for tracking dogs. If you are hunting with a trained dog, these collars allow you to monitor the dog’s location, making it easier to track game and stay in control of your hunting team.

How to Use GPS for Hunting: Step-by-Step

1. Pre-Hunt Planning

The key to a successful hunt begins well before you step foot in the woods. GPS is an excellent tool for pre-hunt planning. Here’s how to use GPS to plan your hunt:

  • Study Maps and Terrain: Use your GPS device or smartphone app to view topographic maps of the area you plan to hunt. These maps show elevation changes, water bodies, roads, and other important features that could impact your hunt. Understanding the lay of the land will help you make strategic decisions about where to set up and where game might be most abundant.

  • Mark Key Locations: Before you head out, mark important locations on your GPS, such as entry points, water sources, or areas with high game activity. By pre-marking these spots, you’ll have a better idea of where to focus your efforts once you’re in the field.

  • Route Planning: Plan your route using the GPS. Whether you’re walking through dense forests, navigating by riverbeds, or crossing open fields, plotting your course will prevent you from getting disoriented during the hunt. A well-planned route is critical for ensuring you don’t get lost in unfamiliar terrain.

2. Use GPS to Navigate in the Field

Once you’re in the field, your GPS can help you stay on track and avoid getting lost. Here’s how to make the most of it:

  • Follow Waypoints: By marking waypoints on your GPS during pre-hunt planning, you can easily follow a set route. This is especially important in unfamiliar terrain where trail markers may not be visible. Your GPS will guide you from one waypoint to the next, ensuring you stay on course.

  • Track Your Position: GPS devices continuously update your position on the map, allowing you to keep track of where you are in real time. If you ever feel uncertain about your location, simply check your GPS to confirm you’re on the right path.

  • Set Boundaries: GPS allows you to set virtual boundaries within your hunting area. By marking “no-go zones” or areas where you want to avoid getting too close, you can manage your hunt more efficiently and reduce the risk of crossing onto private property or dangerous terrain.

  • Navigate Back to Camp: One of the most critical functions of GPS is the ability to get back to your starting point, such as your camp or vehicle. If you’re deep in the woods and losing daylight, your GPS will provide directions to return to your base camp safely.

3. Track Game Movement with GPS

Tracking game can be one of the most challenging aspects of hunting. Fortunately, GPS technology offers several ways to track game effectively.

  • Marking Game Sighting Locations: If you spot game in a particular area, mark the exact location on your GPS. You can then track your movement and the game’s movement to determine patterns. This information is helpful when you plan to track the same animal or group of animals at a later time.

  • Using GPS Collars: As mentioned earlier, GPS collars for tracking dogs are an excellent tool for hunters who use trained dogs. These collars allow you to track the dog’s movements in real time, ensuring that you can stay in communication and follow the dog’s progress when chasing game.

  • Plotting Animal Trails: Over time, you can use GPS to mark animal trails. When you repeatedly track game in a certain area, you’ll begin to identify patterns in the way animals move. These trails will be invaluable when you need to predict where game might move next.

4. Safety and Emergency Situations

When you’re hunting in remote areas, safety is paramount. GPS devices can help you stay safe in a variety of situations:

  • Locate Emergency Services: In case of an emergency, a GPS device can provide your precise coordinates, making it easier for rescuers to find you. Some GPS devices come equipped with an emergency SOS feature that alerts authorities to your exact location.

  • Share Your Location: Some GPS apps allow you to share your location with a hunting partner or family member. This is especially helpful if you’re hunting alone and want someone to know where you are in case of an emergency.

  • Avoid Getting Lost: If you venture deep into the woods or unfamiliar terrain, your GPS will help you avoid losing your way. This is particularly important during long hunting trips or when visibility is low due to weather conditions.

5. Post-Hunt Analysis

After the hunt is over, GPS can still play a valuable role in analyzing your experience and improving future hunts.

  • Review Your Routes: By reviewing your tracks, you can gain insights into your movements during the hunt. This can help you identify areas where you spent too much time or places where game activity was abundant. Analyzing these patterns will help you refine your strategies for future hunts.

  • Map Game Locations: If you marked game sightings during your hunt, you can use your GPS to assess the areas where you had the most success. This data can help you make better decisions in future hunts, whether that means returning to the same areas or trying new locations.

  • Evaluate Terrain: GPS can also be useful for evaluating the terrain you hunted. Understanding the geography of the area, such as elevation changes and natural features, can help you better prepare for future hunts.

Tips for Using GPS Effectively in Hunting

To maximize the benefits of GPS, consider the following tips:

  • Keep Your GPS Charged: Make sure your GPS device is fully charged before you head out. Consider carrying extra batteries or a portable charger if you plan to be in the field for extended periods.

  • Learn to Use Your Device: Spend some time learning how to use your GPS before your hunting trip. Familiarize yourself with its features, settings, and how to mark waypoints so that you can use it efficiently in the field.

  • Avoid Over-Reliance on GPS: While GPS is a valuable tool, it’s still important to develop traditional navigation skills, such as reading maps and using a compass. GPS devices can fail or lose signal, so it’s essential to have backup navigation methods in case of emergencies.

  • Check Local Regulations: Some hunting areas may have specific rules about the use of GPS or other electronic devices. Be sure to check the local regulations to ensure you’re in compliance.

Conclusion

GPS has become an indispensable tool for hunters, offering numerous advantages, from navigation and tracking to safety and post-hunt analysis. By understanding how to use GPS effectively, you can enhance your hunting experience and improve your chances of success. Whether you’re hunting in dense forests, open fields, or rugged mountains, GPS provides the accuracy and confidence you need to navigate the wilderness safely and efficiently.
