Existentialist Views on Ethics and Freedom


When exploring the foundations of philosophy, one must not overlook the existentialist movement. Originating in the 19th and 20th centuries, existentialism addresses fundamental questions about human existence, such as: ‘What is the meaning of life?’, ‘How does one achieve fulfillment?’, and ‘What is the nature of personal freedom?’ Today, we will delve into the heart of existentialist thinking and uncover the essence of existentialist views on ethics and freedom.

First and foremost, existentialist philosophy values individual freedom and choice. Abandoning the idea of inherent meaning or purpose in life, existentialists believe that each individual must create their own meaning and seize control of their existence. By recognizing the freedom of choice, however, existentialists assert that one must also accept responsibility for their decisions and confront the inevitable uncertainty of life.

Sartre, a prominent existentialist, expressed the concept of radical freedom in his work, “Being and Nothingness.” He posited that individuals are born into a world with no predetermined purpose, allowing them to shape their identity through free choice. Encountering situations that offer myriad potential actions, individuals must select a path and live with the consequences of their choices. This assertion amplifies the importance of personal responsibility and the need to embrace uncertainty in life.

But what about the moral compass guiding these decisions? Although existentialism shuns overarching moral systems, it does not negate the existence of individual morality. Existentialists argue that moral values are subjective and unique to each person; thus, there is no universal moral code. Instead, individuals must create their own moral framework grounded in their experiences and values. By taking ownership of one’s moral standpoint, a person can derive a sense of identity and purpose from it.

Moreover, existentialism clarifies the link between freedom and morality. As individuals create their moral codes, they simultaneously influence their actions, making choices in accordance with their principles. This connection between freedom and morality proves pivotal in defining the essence of an existentialist approach to life.

In summary, existentialist views on ethics and freedom emphasize the significance of individual choice, responsibility, and the creation of one’s moral compass. By shunning predetermined meanings and embracing uncertainty, existentialists encourage individuals to forge their path through life’s labyrinth, guided by their personal values and free will.

Life may be complex and chaotic, but adopting an existentialist perspective provides a roadmap to navigate its twists and turns. Ultimately, existentialism proves a thought-provoking and compelling philosophical approach that challenges individuals to actively engage with their existence. To embrace the human experience, one must challenge conventional thought, create their path, and confront life’s uncertainties with courage and conviction.

In preparing for this journey, remember - you are not alone. Each step is a choice, and together we empower ourselves with the knowledge and spirit of existentialist thought. So let us venture forth, forging our path through life, enriched by the wisdom and insight of existentialism.
